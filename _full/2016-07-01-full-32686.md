<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini membahas temuan kata – kata tabu dalam Bahasa Inggris dan terjemahannya dalam Bahasa Indonsesia. Dikarenakan masing – masing bahasa memiliki latar belakang budaya yang berbeda, maka penerjemah akan menemukan beberapa perbedaan budaya yang membuat penerjemahan kata – kata tabu menjadi rumit. Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk mengklasifikasikan kata – kata tabu yang muncul dalam bahasa sumber dan mendeskripsikan teknik penerjemahannya sehingga didapat padanan dalam terjemahan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Metode dokumentasi diaplikasikan untuk mengumpulkan data dari film Ted. Selanjutnya, metode deskriptif kualitatif diaplikasikan dalam analisis dan penyajian data. Analisis dari penelitian ini diperoleh dengan cara mengaplikasikan teori yang dikemukakan oleh Jay (1992) tentang tipe – tipe kata tabu, Larson (1998) dan Davoodi (2009) tentang teknik penerjemahan. Kemudian, teori – teori tersebut didukung dengan pendekatan makna konsep oleh Bell (1991).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Terdapat 9 tipe kata tabu yang ditemukan, yaitu cursing, profanity, taboo, obscenity, vulgarity, slang, epithet, insult and slurs and scatology. Sementara blasphemy tidak ditemukan dalam data. Terdapat 6 variasi teknik penerjemahan yang muncul dalam menerjemahkan kata tabu. Secara garis besar teknik tersenut dibagi menjadi dua yaitu literal and idiomatic translations. Secara lebih spesifik, dibagi menjadi empat yaitu, censorship, substitution, taboo for taboo and euphemism. Teknik penerjemahan tersebut diterapkan dengan mempertimbangkan kondisi dan situasi pada saat kata tabu tersebut diucapkan sehingga akan didapatkan padanan dalam terjemahan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kata tabu, teknik penerjemahan, padanan terjemahan</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In society, the use of language can be divided into two forms. There are literal and non- literal languages. Non – literal language is a language which is not</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">to mean what the speaker stated but to imply something else. In non – literal language, the speaker tries to construct the language itself by using parable natural phenomenon which has resembled meaning to the meaning that is intended to inform. Then, one term of non – literal language occur in society as the interaction of language and society itself is taboo words. Taboo words are the words that one often considered offensive, shocking or rude because they refer to sex or a part of body which cannot be stated literally (Fromkin: 1988: 286).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There are many kinds of taboo words found in movie’s dialog, for instance in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Ted</span><span class="font2"> Movie. This movie is watched cross nationalities. Therefore, for the other countries where English is not their mother tongue, the movie will be added translation or known as subtitle into their languages, in this case Indonesian.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Translation is not simply as a process of replacing words from source language (SL) to target language (TL). However, it includes the transfer of the meaning of SL to TL (Larson: 1998). Furthermore, when we talk about the translation of movie or other literary works, we cannot focus only on the form of the target language but we also have to focus on the other sides of the form, like cultural side. In translating taboo words, there are various translation techniques applied in order to gain the equivalent in translation. Those ways can be literal and idiomatic which specifically in term of: taboo for taboo, euphemism, substitution or censorship.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data of taboo words were taken from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Ted</span><span class="font2"> Movie which was an American comedy movie. This movie was directed by Seth MacFarlane, produced by Media Rights Capital and distributed by Universal Pictures in 2012. The translation or subtitle can be found in its DVD.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The method applied in this study was documentation method. The method was supported by some techniques. They were note taking and comparative technique. Note taking was applied in collecting data from subtitle. Afterward the comparative technique was applied in comparing the data from English version to Indonesian subtitle.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Descriptive qualitative method was applied in the analysis of this study. First, the collected data, taboo words from the movie were selected to a certain number. Second, the taboo words were classified based on the theory proposed by Jay (1992). Finally, the taboo words and their translation were analyzed in order to find out the translation technique in reaching translation equivalence based on theories by Larson (1998) and Davoodi (2009). Then, it was supported by Bell’s approaches (1991) of the conceptual meaning in translation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Presenting Analysis</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The result of the analysis was presented using formal and informal methods and it was supported by some techniques. Those were, firstly, the data was classified into the types of taboo words and it was shown informally in the form of sentence in table. Finally, in order to answer the second problem, the translation techniques were presented descriptively by showing it in form of table and also presenting the diagram of the conceptual meaning to prove the equivalent in translation.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of English – Indonesian Translation of Taboo Words in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ted </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Movie</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Taboo Words and Their Translation into Indonesian</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taboo</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Taboo is cultural custom that forbids talking about a certain thing, in general agreement not to discuss something. The function of taboo is to prohibit the behavior of a speaker and preserve social cohesion. Different cultures, in order to preserve social order use taboo words to control individuals within groups. Below is an example of taboo word that was found in the movie:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: Did you just call me a </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">whore</span><span class="font2">?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: Apa kau baru saja meyebutku </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">pelacur</span><span class="font2">?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The taboo word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whore</span><span class="font2"> was uttered to Lori by Tammy who got angry to her. The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whore</span><span class="font2"> in this case was used in order to name the term female prostitute. This term was used since it was prohibited to say prostitute directly. Therefore, the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whore</span><span class="font2"> was uttered in the movie in order to preserve social cohesion.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the data presented above, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whore</span><span class="font2"> was translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> in TL. The source language was translated with the proper expression in the target language since the translation result could support the plot of the story which meant the sense of the meaning was almost the same as the source language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whore</span><span class="font2"> itself in SL indeed means a female prostitute. Nevertheless, the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> in TL is originated from the basic word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lacur</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Lacur</span><span class="font2"> in TL means unlucky or unfortunate thing. The basic word, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lacur</span><span class="font2"> is attached by prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">me–.</span><span class="font2"> As a result, it becomes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">melacur</span><span class="font2"> which has meaning to do unlucky thing or known as selling own self (prostitute). Then, in another term, the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lacur</span><span class="font2"> is attached by agentive prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pe–.</span><span class="font2"> In this case, the meaning of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> becomes someone who does the job as a prostitute or known as a female prostitute.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Translation Techniques of Taboo Words into Indonesian</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Idiomatic Translation</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taboo for Taboo</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In this translation technique, the translator prefers to render the taboo terms into taboo though they know that they are inadmissible to the target society and people. The instance can be seen as follow:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: Plus a </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">hooker</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">took a shit in our apartment.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: Ditambah, seorang </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> membuang kotoran di apartemen kita.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The denotative meaning of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2"> is a player in rugby team, whose job is to pull the ball out the scrum with his foot. If it is looked at the basic word, the meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hook</span><span class="font2"> refers to a plastic or wire for hanging things on. Then, if the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hook</span><span class="font2"> is attached with suffix morpheme </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> which becomes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2">, there will be a change in its meaning. The meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2"> implies the connotative meaning of a person who hooks in somebody or something.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This connotative meaning was translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> in TL. The context of situation when the speech was uttered in the movie’s scene had considered. Therefore, the translation result was still in taboo term. In accordance with the explanation, it can be drawn that the translation technique applied was idiomatic translation since it was not word per word translation. Furthermore, the translation techniques applied was taboo for taboo.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The equivalent in translation of the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> could be seen as hyponymy. The meaning concept of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2"> in the English was translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur.</span><span class="font2"> The term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2"> is considered as more general, which may include the concept of a person who hooks in somebody but not a female prostitute. On the other hand, the concept of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> is more specific as the part of the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hooker</span><span class="font2">. Even though the concept of both words is not quite similar, however it is</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">still equivalence in translation since the meaning of source language can be transferred in the same context of the target language.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">5.2.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Substitution</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This technique of translation tries to change the form of source language to target language’s form but the meaning does not gain enough definition as the meaning in source language. However, the audiences still can understand the meaning that wants to be conveyed. The example of this technique was found in the data, as follow:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: Hey, play chopsticks, you jazzy </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">slut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: Hei, bermain dengan sumpit, kamu </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">pelacur</span><span class="font2"> jazzy.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are nine types of taboo words from ten types of taboo words found from the data. Those are cursing, profanity, taboo, obscenity, vulgarity, slang, epithet, insult and slut, and scatology. One of the ten types is not found in the data is blasphemy. There are no words which are attack religion, since the story of movie itself is not related to religion. Therefore, there is no blasphemy.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The translator attempted to reach the equivalent in translation by applying some translation techniques. There are six variant translation techniques applied in translating taboo words. In general, it is divided into literal and idiomatic translation. Specifically the translation techniques applied are censorship, substitution, taboo for taboo and euphemism. Those translation techniques are applied based on the condition when sentences were uttered and situational feeling of the characters in the movie scene. In translating taboo words, both the conceptual meaning of source and target languages were also considered. All of the techniques applied were intended to have closet equivalent in translating taboo words.</span></p>
