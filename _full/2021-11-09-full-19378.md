<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Makalah ini menganalisa dan membahas komponen-komponen dari frase nomina yang dapat ditemukan pada artikel yang berjudul </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“After The Bali Bombing – The Long Road To Recovery&quot;</span><span class="font1"> yang dibuat oleh </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Australian Journal of Emergency Management</span><span class="font1">. Artikel ini dipilih karena banyak terdapat frase nomina. Berdasarkan tahapan-tahapan analisis, dapat ditemukan bahwa struktur frase nomina terdiri dari 3 komponen, yaitu </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the head</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the pre-modification</span><span class="font1"> dan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the postmodification</span><span class="font1">. Untuk mengetahui sumber data, data harus dibaca, lalu digarisbawahi dan dicatat. Data dianalisa secara deskriptif kualitatif, dengan maksud data dianalisa berkaitan dengan masalah yang didasarkan pada teori.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kumci : frase nomina, modifikasi, struktur</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Phrases are divided in terms of two components, the head as the lexical category and the projection as the phrasal category. Phrasal categories are headed by the lexical categories. A phrase consists of head and one modifier or a head and more than one modifier. The head of a noun phrase is a noun, the virtue of the fact that it is headed by a noun that the phrase is a noun phrase. Phrase is said to</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">be a projection of its head. ( Quirk, et. al). In English, among the five different types of phrase. There are noun phrases, verb phrases, adjectives phrases, adverb phrases, and prepositional phrases. Noun phrases are the most common playing various syntactic functions in the sentence and clause structure, are subject, object and complement. All of them are use to things that people want to talk about people, objects, concepts, processes, and all kind of entities. (Quirk, et. al)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

There are a lot of noun phrase in this article. The data of the English noun phrase structure from data source were analyzed descriptive qualitatively.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of English Noun Phrase Structure In the Article </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">After The Bali Bombing – The Long Road To Recovery&quot;</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pre-modification Noun Phrase</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">It means that the head noun is modified by various pre-modification, adjective, participle, noun, genetive or adverb placed before the head other than determinatives.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“This co-ordinating committee”</span><span class="font1"> (in the text)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The pre-modification noun phrase “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">this co-ordinating committee</span><span class="font1">”, is slightly different. The head noun “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">committee</span><span class="font1">” is modified by determiner “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">This</span><span class="font1">” and noun “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">co-ordinating</span><span class="font1">” is included in –ing participle. The explanation of the data can be described as follows :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">This &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;co-ordinating &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;committee</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Determiner Noun (-ing participle) Head Noun</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post-modification Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">It means that the head noun is modified by many kinds of post-mofication and positioned after the head noun.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">“</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The fact that prospective clientele”</span><span class="font1"> (in the text)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The post-modification noun phrase is achieved by finite clause “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">that prospective clientele</span><span class="font1">”. The pronoun “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1">” act as the marker of the postmodification. The post-modification noun phrase completed by the use of determiner “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1">” . The explanation of the data can be described as follows :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;fact &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;that prospective clientele</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Determiner Head Noun &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finite Clause</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Multi-modification Noun Phrase</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This type includes the noun phrase which comes when the head noun of noun phrase has more than a single pre-modification or post-modification. Multimodification usually consists of the head noun, the determinative, the premodification and the post-modification.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“The tragic events of October 12, 2002”</span><span class="font1"> (in the text)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The head noun “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">events</span><span class="font1">” of the multi-modification noun phrase “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the tragic events of October 12, 2002</span><span class="font1"> “ is formed by pre-modification and post-modification noun phrase. Word “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The</span><span class="font1">” acts as determiner and adjective “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">tragic</span><span class="font1">” acts as premodification.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Then, post-modification is shown by the prepositional phrase “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">of October 12</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">2002</span><span class="font1">”. The preposition “</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">of “</span><span class="font1"> functions to give information and complete the head noun. The component of the multi-modification noun phrase can be drawn as follows :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;tragic &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;events &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;of October 12, 2002</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Determiner Adjective &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Head Noun &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prepositional Phrase</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase consists of the head, determiner, pre-modification, and postmodification. The types of complex noun phrase were categorized based on the component used to form the noun phrase. The types of noun phrase are premodification noun phrase, post-modification noun phrase, and multi-modification noun phrase.</span></p>
