<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Antioxidant is a chemical compound that could give one or more electrons to inhibit the free radical reaction. One of the plants that potentially produce antioxidants is purnajiwa (Euchresta horsfieldii (Lesch.) Benn.). The research was conducted using the fruit of purnajiwa. The purpose of this study is to know antioxidant activity from purnajiwa fruit using the DPPH method and measured by UV-Vis spectrophotometry. The extract of purnajiwa fruit gained by the maceration method using 96% ethanol solvent. The results of phytochemistry screening found out that the extract contains flavonoid, alkaloid, saponin, tannin, and terpenoid. According to flavonoid testing using quercetin, the results obtained from purnajiwa extract contained flavonoid of 1,651 mg QE/g. The activity test of antioxidants quantitatively by DPPH using UV-Vis spectrophotometry found out that purnajiwa extract has IC50 value of 57,28 ppm so that purnajiwa extract has strong antioxidant activity that could be used as natural antioxidant resources.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font4">: purnajiwa fruit, antioxidant activity, DPPH, UV-Vis spectrophotometre</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Polution, the chemical from food, alcohol, cigarettes, and UV radiation are sources of free radical that could damage the enviroment because of the characteristics were reactive and unstable. Free radical is a molecul that has one or more electron that unpairs in outer orbit so that to achieve molecular stability, free radicals will react to healthy body cells molecule by binding those molecular electrons (Sunarni </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2007). The results of free radical could be degenerative disease like cancer, tumor, stroke, and diabetes milletus. The risk of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">disease caused by free radicals could be prevented by giving an antioxidant. The antioxidant is a compund that could decrease other molecular oxidation or to neutralize the free radical (Silalahi, 2006). Enough amounts of antioxidant could help the body to increase immunity through the disease that could be happened by free radical. Purnajiwa </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">(Euchresta horsfieldii</span><span class="font4"> (Lesch.) Benn.) that one of the plants as natural antioxidant sources and known as traditional herbal that spread around countries such as India, Philipines, Thailand, and Indonesia. In</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Indonesia purnajiwa could be found in the special region in Bali famous as pranajiwa.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Morphologically, the shape of purnajiwa fruit is small, shining, oval around 1-2 cm length, the color of the raw fruit is green color and becomes purple when the fruit is ripe (Lemmens dan Bunyapraphatsara, 2003). Purnajiwa fruit has many benefits like for relief clogged up breathing, cough, asthma, and increasing the &nbsp;immunity.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Purnajiwa has biological activities as it contained secondary metabolite compounds such as flavonoid, alkaloid, tannin, saponin, steroid, and terpenoid. Flavonoid has an ability as an antioxidant. Antioxidant research that has been done by Tirta </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font4"> (2010) found out that the antioxidant capacity of pranajiwa leaves is about 126,94 ppm.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Antioxidant activity by purnajiwa fruit extract could be conducted by DPPH method. DPPH method has been used globally in antioxidant activity testing from natural compunds because it only needs a few sample, simple instrument, and fast processing. Antioxidant compunds from this sample given to the hydroxyl group through DPPH radical, so the DPPH radical that unstable before because of unpairs electron become stable (Purwaningsih, 2012). The antioxidants activity test from purnajiwa fruit extract in Indonesia is still rarely done, this is the reason why this antioxidant</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">activity testing of purnajiwa fruit extract is conducted.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Material used in this research is purnajiwa fruit species </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Euchresta horsfieldii</span><span class="font4"> that grow in Bajra Sandhi Monument, Puputan Renon, Denpasar City. The other material used as an extractor and chemical material for analysis is ethanol 96%, DPPH, methanol absolut, eluen, chloride acid, magnesium, anhydrous acetate, sulfuric acid, Meyer reactor, FeCl</span><span class="font1">3</span><span class="font4">, aquadest.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The instrument that used are blender, oven, cutting board, knife, analitical scale, rotary evaporator, jar, micropipette, filter, pipette, refrigerator, volumetric flask, funnel, stirrer stick, erlenmayer, labeling paper, filtering paper, ruler, pencil, UV-Vis spectrophotometry, digital camera, cuvette, capillary pipe, vial, aluminium foil.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Preparation sample</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Purnajiwa fruit was obtained in Bajra Sandhi Monument, Puputan Renon, Denpasar City then washed clean with flowing water, cut it to the small pieces with ± 0,5 cm. The next step, dried up samples by oven with temperature 50°C around 48 hours. Mash the dried purnajiwa fruit using blender and measure the powder is weight using analitical scale.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Extraction of purnajiwa fruit</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">About 500 g added by 96% ethanol solvent until submerged, then left it for 24 hours. After that filter it and separate the filtrate. Repeat this treatment about three times. At the last day of filtering, extract the filtrate using vacum rotary evaporator to obtained the extract.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Screening phytochemical Flavonoid</span></h1>
