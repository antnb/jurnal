<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font6">One of the pests of citrus is whitefly that causes damage directly or/and indirectly to the citrus production. To control whitefly the farmer usually use chemical insecticide, however the utilization of chemical insecticide has been reported to haves many negative effect. To minimize the utilization of chemical insecticide, the environmentally friendly method is needed. One of the method is to utilize the natural enemies. Natural enemies are including, parasitiod, predator as well as insect pathogen (entomopathogen). In 2017 entomopathogenic fungi </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Aschersonia placenta</span><span class="font6"> was found to be associated with citrus whitefly in Bali Indonesia. However the species of whitefly has not been identified. In this research the identification of whitefly, the host insect of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">A. placenta</span><span class="font6"> was conducted based on morphological and molecular identification. Morphological identification of whitefly use puparial stage, started with sample preparation by Slide Mounting Protocol. The target of mitochondrial cytochrome c oxidase subunit I (mtCOI) gen was successfully amplified (700 bp) by PCR using forward primer LCO 5'GGTCAACAAATCATAAAGATATTGG3' and reverse primer HCO 5'TAAACTTCAGGGTGACCAAAAAATCA3'. The phylogenetic analysis using software ChromasPRO, Molecular Evolutionary Genetics Analysis (MEGA 5.05), PAUP, BioEdit, and TreeGraph2 was conducted. The result shows that the mtCOI sequence of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">P. minei</span><span class="font6"> from Bali (LC491421) has the highest percentage among others with MK421974 </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">P. minei</span><span class="font6"> (score homology 96%). The morphological recognition and sequence analysis show that the species of citrus whitefly is </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Paraleyrodes minei</span><span class="font6">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font6">: whitefly, host insect, citrus, </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Aschersonia placenta, Paraleyrodes minei</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font6">Whitefly is a polyphagous insect that has a very broad host distribution, these insects are widespread in the tropics and subtropics. Whiteflies can cause damage directly or indirectly. Direct damage as a food activity, such as (1) Closure of stomata by honey dew released by nymphs and soot dew which grows on the honey dew layer; (2) Chlorotic spots on leaves resulting in</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">damage to tissue due to stylet puncture, (3) formation of anthocyanin pigments; and (4) Leaves fall so that can inhibit plant growth (De Barro 1995; Setiawati </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font6"> 2007). Indirect damage is generally whiteflies is a vector of viruses that can cause disease in plants. The abundance of whiteflies infestation has been reported in citrus plant (Syafitri </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font6"> 2017), reported causing a decrease in yield due to increased whitefly</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">infestation. The control of whiteflies is currently using insecticides that are harmful to the environment and human health. One approach that is more environmentally friendly is to use natural enemies. One of the natural enemies of the whiteflies is the entomopathogenic fungus. The fungus is </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Aschersonia placenta</span><span class="font6">, the species was found associated with whiteflies in citrus plant in Bali Indonesia (Sudiarta, </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font6">., 2017; Suputra, </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font6">., 2019). The species of whitefly as the host of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">A. placenta</span><span class="font6"> has not been identified, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;even though the</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">information is very important for basic science and applied science. The whitefly on citrus plant in Bali was predicted as </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Dialeurodes citri</span><span class="font6"> from morphological recognise from field, however the small body and the similarity morphological from one and other whitefly make difficult to accurate identification. Therefore on this study, the identification with morphological and molecular characteristic of whitefly was conducted.</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;">MATERIALS AND METHODS Morphological Identification of Whiteflies</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Samples were taken from the results of field surveys, where the symptoms of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Aschersonia</span><span class="font6"> sp. found, in various districts in Bali (Gianyar, Bangli, Tabanan and Buleleng). Sampling was conducted by</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">taking nymphs of whitefly, which is usually found under the leaf. Morphological identification of whiteflies using puparial stage, begins with sample preparation by Slide Mounting Protocol (modified from Wilkey 1962 in Gregory </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font6"> 2005). Specimens are identified using a microscope by comparing insect morphology by matching each part and compared with the literature than by photographing &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;preparations &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;using</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">microscopic cameras which are then identified by matching the literature. Morphological identification of whitefly using literature “An Identification Guide To The Whiteflies (Hemiptera: Aleyrodidae) of The Southeastern United States” by Gregory </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font6"> 2005.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>Molecular Identification of Whiteflies </span><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">DNA Extraction and amplification</span></h1>
<p><span class="font6">Protocol based on ZR Insect DNA Kit™ Catalog No. D6016 by ZYMO RESEARCH. PCR amplification using the primary pair of M-COI, LCO Primer: 5' GGTCAACAAATCATAAAGATATTGG 3' as forward primer and HCO Primer: 5' TAAACTTCAGGGTGACCAAAAAATC A 3' as reverse primer.</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Electrophoresis</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">The 1 μl PCR product (plus 2 μl of loading dye) was electrophoresed in agarose</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">gel. Electrophoresis was carried out for 30 minutes at 100 volts. The DNA that has been electrophoresed is then visualized with a translator UV.</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Sequencing</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Sequencing was held at Laboratory of Genetika Science Indonesia for sequence of nucleotides. The sequence results then were analyzed to make the alignment then was used to determine the level of homology or alignment with sequence gen mitochondrial cytochrome c oxidase subunit I (mtCOI) gen whiteflies that have been published in GenBank with the Basic Local Alignment Tool (BLAST) Program (NCBI 2014).</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Phylogenetic Analysis</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">The sequence of nucleotide data then followed by phylogenetic analysis using software ChromasPRO, Molecular Evolutionary Genetics Analysis (MEGA 5.05), PAUP, BioEdit, and TreeGraph2.</span></p>

<p><span class="font6">In addition there are two pairs of reduced pores in the anterior abdominal section and at the tip of the lingula there are two pairs of seta. Basen on the data the morphological character of citrus whitefly from Bali Indonesia is very similar with morphology of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">P. minei.</span></p>

<p><span class="font6">The citrus whitefly host insect of </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Aschersonia placenta</span><span class="font6"> from Bali Indonesia was identified as </span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">Paraleyrodes minei,</span><span class="font6"> based on the morphological and molecular characteristics</span><span class="font6" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
