<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini yang berjudul Aksen Bahasa Inggris India Dalam Sebuah Film Bollywood Berjudul Jab Tak Hai Jaan yang Disutradarai Oleh Yash Chopra menganalisis tentang karakteristik fitur-fitur segmental dan suprasegmental dari aksen Bahasa Inggris India dalam film Bollywood Jab Tak Hai Jaan. Sumber data dari penelitian ini diambil dari dialog-dialog yang ada dalam Jab Tak Hai Jaan. Metode yang digunakan untuk mengumpulkan data adalah metode dokumentasi melalui penelitian kepustakaan. Setelah data dikumpulkan, dianalisis menggunakan metode deskriptif kualitatif dengan tujuan untuk menemukan perbedaan karakteristik diantara Bahasa Inggris India dalam film dengan Cara Pengucapan Yang Diakui. Rumusan masalah pada skripsi ini dianalisis menggunakan Teori Fonologi yang dikemukakan oleh Roach (1998) dan Kelly (2000); dan Fonologi Hindi yang dikemukakan oleh Ohala (1999).</span></p>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa karakteristik pada Bahasa Inggris India terdapat dalam film, yang terbagi menjadi dua yaitu: fitur-fitur segmental dan suprasegmental. Dari fitur segmental, terdapat beberapa kejadian diantaranya: (1) pelesapan fonem Bahasa Inggris /d/; /r/; dan cara pengucapan fonem /p/; (2) penggantian fonem Bahasa Inggris /o:/; /v/; /a/; /</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Λ</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">/; /t/; /d/; /θ/; /d/; /r/; /v/; dan ^ou/; dan (3) penambahan fonem Bahasa Inggris /o/. Dari fitur-fitur suprasegmental, tekanan pada kata dan intonasid alam film berbeda dengan aturan yang terdapat pada Cara Pengucapan Yang Diakui. Sedangkan, tekanan pada kalimat dan pengucapan yang bertautan dalam film sudah sesuai dengan aturan-aturan yang terdapat pada Cara Pengucapan Yang Diakui.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Bahasa Inggris India, aksen, Cara Pengucapan Yang Diakui</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">Language used by human beings as a medium of expression of thoughts, feelings, and desires by means of a system of sounds and sound symbols of communication in the society. According to Wardhaugh (2006:1) “a language is what the member of a particular society speaks. However, speech in almost any society can take very different forms”. In fact, there can be more than one language used by people in a country. Firstly mother tongue; secondly national language; and thirdly international language. Mother tongue, which is used as first language in daily life is often</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">influenced by the people’s pronunciation when they speak another language, English, for instance. Their English will sound accented and affect the phonological system of English itself.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">English in one country is different from that in another. It is influenced by the accent which makes the pronunciation English vary or it can be said that accent gives impact on the phonological system of English in a country. Phonetics and phonology are concerned with speech – with the ways in which humans produce and hear speech. One example of English pronunciation varieties is Indian English. People in India speak English influenced by their mother tongue, generally Hindi, and sound different from Received Pronunciation (RP) because the Hindi vowel system is quite similar to that of RP, in contrast to the consonants. The use of the Indian English could be seen in India’s famous and biggest movie production called “Bollywood”, one of the most popular movies is </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Jab Tak Hai Jaan</span><span class="font5"> (in English “As long as I live”) by Yash Chopra.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font5">The data in this study were taken directly from the dialogue in </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Jab Tak Hai Jaan</span><span class="font5"> Bollywood movie directed by Yash Chopra. The collected data were analyzed and presented using the descriptive and qualitative method.</span></p>

<p><span class="font5">In this movie, the actors produced words with the English open front vowel /æ/ by pronouncing the Hindi open centre vowel /ɑ:/. They pronounced this vowel in which the back of the tongue became the highest part and the blade touched the lower teeth, as the actors in this movie did. <h2><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sentence Stress</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">“I could’ve DIED.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">In the movie, this sentence was spoken by the speaker when she complained to the hearer why he did not come early to help her when she was about to sink on the beach. There were two content words here: I and died, those were given stress and written in capitals. This sentence conveyed two ideas based on the words given stress: (1) it was her that nearly happened to die because of sinking; and (2) something wouldhappened to her (die) if she did not get help from someone early.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Intonation</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">“Stop calling me.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">This sentence belongs to imperative. The intonation must be falling (proclaiming tone) at the end of the sentence; however, it was pronounced with a fall-rise intonation (referring tone) in the movie which was usually used in a question asking for confirmation. Therefore, it was not in line with ‘imperatives rule’ of grammar and intonation in RP. A fall-rise intonation in this sentence also gave a meaning that the speaker had already told something to the hearer. The intonation of the sentence can be compared as below:</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Connected Speech</span></h2></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Assimilation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The phrase aren’</span><span class="font5" style="text-decoration:underline;">t y</span><span class="font5">ou /ɑ:ntyu:/ tended to be pronounced as /ɑ:nt</span><span class="font2">ʃ</span><span class="font5">u:/ because when phonemes /t/ and /y/ assimilates, the sound merged into ‘ch’ sound /tʃ/.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elision</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The occurrence of elision could be seen in the phrase o</span><span class="font5" style="text-decoration:underline;">n h</span><span class="font5">is body where it was supposed to be pronounced as /ɒ</span><span class="font4">nh</span><span class="font3">I</span><span class="font4">zb</span><span class="font5">ɒd</span><span class="font4">i/; however, in the movie it tended to be pronounced as </span><span class="font3" style="font-variant:small-caps;">/</span><span class="font5">o</span><span class="font3" style="font-variant:small-caps;">:</span><span class="font4">n</span><span class="font3" style="font-variant:small-caps;">i</span><span class="font4">zbo</span><span class="font3" style="font-variant:small-caps;">:</span><span class="font5">ɖ</span><span class="font4">i</span><span class="font3" style="font-variant:small-caps;">/.</span><span class="font4"> The /h/ sound was removed from the word and it can be omitted.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coarticulation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The occurrence of coarticulation in this movie is nasal aspiration. It could be seen in the phrase goo</span><span class="font5" style="text-decoration:underline;">d n</span><span class="font5">ight. The sound /d/ linked to the sound of /n/. The tongue moved into the position of the </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">/</span><span class="font5">d</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">/</span><span class="font5">, but the sound was not completed with the typical aspiration (release of air). Instead, the stopped air was released as the</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">/</span><span class="font5">n</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">/</span><span class="font5">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">(4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Intrusion /w/</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">Intrusion occurred in this movie in the phrase d</span><span class="font5" style="text-decoration:underline;">o i</span><span class="font5">t. The actors added a slight /w/ sound to link the /o/ sound and the short /i/ sound. By the occurrence of the intrusion /w/, the actors tended to pronounce /d</span><span class="font0">Ʊ</span><span class="font5">w</span><span class="font3">I</span><span class="font5">ʈ/. Therefore, this allowed both vowel sounds to be pronounced clearly.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">(5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linking between words (consonant and vowel)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">In this movie, when one word ended with a consonant and the next began with a vowel sound,the final consonant of the first word was used to link the two words. The phrase can be seen as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">“betwee</span><span class="font5" style="text-decoration:underline;">n u</span><span class="font5">s”tended to be pronounced as “betwee – nus” rather than “between – us”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">Based on the discussion, the characteristics of the Indian English accent in </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Jab Tak Hai Jaan</span><span class="font5"> Bollywood movie can be divided into segmental and suprasegmental features and it can be concluded that thesegmental features in</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">Indian English accent pronounced by the actorsoccured in the movie: (1) the deletion of the English phonemes /d/; /r/; and the manner of articulation /p/; (2) the substitution of the English phonemes /ɔ:/; /ɒ/; /æ/; /</span><span class="font3">Ʌ</span><span class="font5">/; /t/; /d/; /θ/; /ð/; /r/; /v/; and /ǝʊ/; and (3) the addition of phonemes /ǝ/. The suprasegmental features in this study are divided into four; they are: (1) word stress; (2) sentence stress; (3) intonation; and (4) connected speech. The word stress and intonation in this movie were different from the rules of RP. The sentence stress and connected speech in this movie was analyzed. They were in line with the rules of the sentence stress and connected speech in RP, therefore, it could be proved.</span></p>
