<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Penggunaan bahasa dan bentuk kesopanan dalam percakapan oleh penutur wanita umumnya berbeda dengan penutur pria. Hal ini dapat ditinjau dari acara talk show wanita, majalah wanita ataupun karya sastra dalam bentuk novel. Tulisan ini merupakan analisa terhadap novel hasil karya Charlotte Bronte berjudul Jane Eyre yang memiliki korelasi terhadap teori Lakoff yang mengungkapkan theory of women language. Berdasarkan hasil analisa tersebut, hal hal linguistic dalam bahasa wanita mencakup pertanyaan pendek pada akhir kalimat, jenis dominasi kata kata sifat untuk wanita, konsistesi terhadap kosa kata yang benar, memperhatikan intonasi suara, penggunaan tata bahasa dan bentuk kesopanan bahasa, penegasan ekspresi, intensif dalam penggunaan just and so, menghindari umpatan yang kuat serta penggunaan istilah warna yang tepat.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: novel, teori Lakoff, bahasa wanita</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Men and women grow restricted by the rules of society. Women speak by using tag question, qualifiers, and fillers in order to soften their speech, and by doing this they avoid direct, rough, and direct tone without hesitating whether to soften their speech or not. However, men speak in higher, rougher, and direct tone without hesitating whether to soften their speech or not.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The study about women language is very interesting to be carried out. The novel entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jane Eyre</span><span class="font1"> By Charlotte Bronte telling about girl’s interaction at that time with their friends, employer and also other elder persons. It is related with women and language features and about the form of politeness. It is shown clearly by the author. Charlotte Bronte was greatly admired for her use of language. This</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">novel had been chosen because it was written by a famous and a great author who used to write about women characters.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What kinds of women language features are found in the novel “Jane Eyre”?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What forms of politeness are involved in the novel “Jane Eyre”?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aim of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Related to the problems above, the aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the kinds of women language features found in the novel “Jane Eyre”</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To find out and analyze the politeness forms in the novel “Jane Eyre”</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this section, there are three points of discussions covered including data sources, method and technique of collecting data, and method and technique of analyzing data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font1">The data of this study were taken from a novel entitled”Jane Eyre” by Charlotte Bronte. It took situation In England in 1824 that fit on the problem of the study about women language that was still more polite than men.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font1">The collected data were analyzed qualitatively in a descriptive way based on the theory applied in this study. The theories that is used are proposed by Lakoff (1975).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Women Language Features</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The women language features were divided into ten as proposed by Lakoff. They are the use of tag question, the use of empty adjectives, lexical hedges or fillers, question intonation and rising intonation, intensifies (just and so) and qualifier, hypercorrect grammar, super polite form, avoidance of strong swear words, emphatic stress, precise colour terms.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Tag Question</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : ‘But Miss Temple is the best </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">– </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">isn’t she’ ?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Helen: ‘Miss Temple is very good, and very clever; she is above the rest, because she knows for more than they do’ (Bronte, 1847:53)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In this sample, tag question has functioned to state a claim. At that time she tried to give her claim, but at the same time when she used tag question in giving answer, it also reflected the lack of confidence of the speaker.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of ‘Empty’ Adjectives</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : 'I know there is, because it does not sound too </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sweet</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> It is not like such words as Liberty, Excitement, and Enjoyment: delightful sounds truly ...’ (Bronte, 1847:87)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Generally, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sweet</span><span class="font1"> shows the use of empty adjectives in admiring of something but in this case. Jane used this kind of adjective in negative form. In Jane's sentence 'It does not sound too </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sweet</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">', sweet</span><span class="font1"> is not means good but it becomes 'it dislikes me'</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Lexical Hedges or Fillers</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : 'No, thank you Bessie'</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie :'Then </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I think</span><span class="font1"> I shall go to bed, for it is past twelve o'clock; but you may call me if you want anything in the night (Bronte,1847:22)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">She used </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I think</span><span class="font1"> hedges to end the conversation politely and to preface her declaration. Bessie conveyed if she wanted to go to bed but also guarantee she will come if Jane called her.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Question and Rising Intonation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie : ‘miss eyre, are you ill?’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : ‘Let me out! Let me go into the nursery!’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">'What for? Are you hurt? What is the matter?’ </span><span class="font1">(Bronte, 1847:52)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Generally they use this feature to seek for a confirmation from their addressee. She used these form because she worry about her friend’s condition. She wanted to know what happened to Jane.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Intensifier ( Just and So )</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Mrs. Reed: 'Abbot and Bessie, I believe I gave orders that Jane Eyre should be left in the red-room till I came to her my self’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie : 'Miss Jane screamed </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> loud, ma’am’(Bronte.l847:19)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In this conversation, she said 'Miss Jane screamed </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> loud', which meant she emphasized the intensity of Jane's scream it is explained that Jane scream was very loud, as Bessie said.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Hypercorrect Grammar</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The Girl :’ </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Will you walk this way, ma’am</span><span class="font1">?’(Bronte,1847:97)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The conversation of the girl used simple future tense in the form of interrogative. It is show hypercorrect grammar used the girls in the novel at that time.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Super Polite Form</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">' </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Would you like to drink, or could you eat anything?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : 'No, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">thank you</span><span class="font1"> Bessie'(Bronte, 1847:21)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bessie used superpolite form in the form of indirect request such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">would you</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">could you</span><span class="font1"> in her sentence to offering drink also giving choice to eat something. Jane's answer also contains superpolite form in the use of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">thank you</span><span class="font1"> in her sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>5.1.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Avoidance of Strong Swear Words</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: I thought, Thornfield belonged to you'</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Mrs. Fairfax: 'To me? </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Bless you child; what an idea</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">To me? I only the housekeeper' (Bronte,1847:101)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In her surprise, she used these sentences because she never imagine will be guessed as the owner of Thornfield since she is only the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">housekeeper. She used blessing words whether than the harsh one and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">what an idea</span><span class="font1"> to ask Jane why she could think like that.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>5.1.9 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Emphatic Stress</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: 'Are there ladies at Leas?&quot;</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Mrs. Fairfax : ‘…. You should have seen the dining room that day how</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">richly</span><span class="font1"> it was decorated how </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">brilliantly</span><span class="font1"> lit up!</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(Bronte,1847:158)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">On the conversation above, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">richly</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">brilliantly</span><span class="font1"> in expressing her admiration to the party decoration that amazed her. It proved with the definition of the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">brilliant</span><span class="font1"> that means extremely clever or impressive; very successful; very intelligent or skillful; and very good or excellent.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark35"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark36"></a>5.1.10 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Use of Precise Colour Terms</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : ……The horizon bounded by a propitious sky, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">azure,</span><span class="font1"> marbled with </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pearly white</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span><span class="font1">(Bronte, 1 847:107)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Charlotte Bronte used precise colour terms to describe the colour of the sky like </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">azure</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pearly white</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Azure</span><span class="font1"> means a bright blue colour, like the sky. Azure is usually familiar with women proved by in a example: a dress of azure silk(Hornby,1995:71).</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pearly white</span><span class="font1"> phrase comes from the adjective word pearly that means of or like a pearl with the following example: pearly white teeth(Hornby, 1995:853).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark37"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark38"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Form of Politeness</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Formality</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Mrs. Fairfax : 'Oh, he set off the moment he had breakfasted! He is gone to the Leas, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Mr.</span><span class="font1"> Eshton's place, ten miles on the other side Millcote. I</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">believe there is quite a party assembled there; </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Lord</span><span class="font1"> Ingram, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Sir</span><span class="font1"> George Lynne, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Colonel</span><span class="font1"> Dent, and others.'</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Jane : 'Do you expect him back tonight?'(Bronte,1847:158)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis :</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Mrs. Fairfax used titles to mention some people in this conversation to show their high sosial status in society such as Mr, Lord, Sir and Colonel.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Camaraderie</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Jane :</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">'Is your book interesting?'</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Helen: ‘I like it&quot;</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Jane: &quot;What is it about?&quot;</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Helen: 'You may look at it'</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Jane tried to make a friend with Helen (as gestures of friendliness). It shows in her question to Helen </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">is your book interesting?</span><span class="font1"> She used camaraderie form of politeness. She showed her sympathy to Helen who read a book alone. The other reasons, Jane was like read too, like Helen was.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">After analysing, the woman language and politeness in the novel entitled Jane Eyre by Charlotte Bronte it was found that women language features involved in the are tag question, the use of empty adjectives, lexical hedges or fillers, question intonation and rising intonation, intensifies (just and so) and qualifier, hypercorrect grammar, super polite form, avoidance of strong swear words, emphatic stress, precise color terms.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Women have their own language because they want to be more polite.Women Character in this novel are using politeness to man, strangers, and the older people. In terms of politeness, it was found two forms of politeness there are formality form and camaraderie form. Formality was used as the most polite one to keep aloof between the speaker and the addressee. In the other situation, it was used to respect someone who has already met.Meanwhile, camaraderie functioned to show sympathy. The purpose of this form is to make the addressee feel that the speaker wants to be friendly with her.</span></p>
