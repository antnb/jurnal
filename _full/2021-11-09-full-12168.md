<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstract</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini berjudul “English Noun Phrase Found in the The Vampire Diaries”. Jurnal ini difokuskan pada analisis struktur dari frasa kata benda yang ditemukan pada novel The Vampire Diaries. Kemudian, analisis difokuskan pada fungsi dari frasa kata benda yang ditemukan pada novel The Vampire Diaries. Datanya adalah frasa kata benda pada nove The Vampire Diaries yang ditulis oleh L.J.Smith. Oleh sebab itu, data tersebut diharapkan menjadi representative. Data yang terkumpul kemudian dianalisis dengan menggunakan metode deskriptif kualitatif menunjuk pada teori yang digunakan pada jurnal ini. Data didapat dengan penelitian perpustakaan yang mana jurnal ini menggunakan buku – buku atau literatur – literature sebagai sumber data. Teori yang digunakan pada jurnal ini secara umum berdasarkan dari teori frasa kata benda yang dibuat oleh Leech and teori fungsi dari frasa kata benda yang dibuat oleh Rodney Huddleston(2001)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Struktur dari frasa kata benda bahasa inggris dipresentasikan berdasarkan pada komponen utama dari frasa kata benda. Frasa kata benda secara umum terdiri dari empat kompoonen dinamakan; kepala, determinatif, pra-modifikasi, pasca-modifikasi. Tapi, terdapat beberapa frasa – frasa kata benda yang tidak memiliki semua komponen tersebut. Frasa kata yang pra-modifikasi terlihat lebih sering pada novel ini. Frasa kata benda bahasa inggris pada jurnal ini memiliki beberapa fungsi diantaranya; sebagai subjek, object, predikatif pelengkap, dan pelengkap pada struktur frasa preposisi. Frasa – frasa kata benda bahasa inggris yang berfungsi sebagai sebuah objek pada sumber data terlihat lebih sering.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords: Noun phrase, Structure of noun phrase, Functions of noun phrase</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Words are classified into eight parts of speech, namely verb, noun, adjective, adverb, pronoun, preposition, conjunction, and interjection. However, phrases are also categorized based on the words that form them. One part of speeches is noun that learner must clearly know in learning language. Thus, noun phrases are also definitely important that learner must understand. After mastering the language structure itself, the learners are expected to comprehend considerably the function of noun phrases in particular. By that understanding, the learners are expected to avoid the use of ill-formed noun phrases.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the explanation above, the researcher is really interested in the study of English noun phrase found in The Vampire Diaries by using theory of noun phrase because noun is very effective in the process of learning a new language both oral and written. It is proved by its existence in every sentence and phrases. In summary, it is a must for the learners of a new language especially English to study the structure and the function of noun phrase more deeply.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Noun Phrase Found In The Vampire Diaries</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;The Structures of English Noun Phrase</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;Pre-modified Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 5 : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Each of the individuals around the table took </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">the piece of paper</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. P 38</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It shows that there is a noun phrase namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">the piece of paper</span><span class="font2"> with </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">paper </span><span class="font2">as the head of the phrase. It was categorized as a pre-modified noun phrase because it consists of pre-modification including determiner </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> and an adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">piece</span><span class="font2"> and those modifiers that occurred before the head to modify the head.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post-modified Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 3 : The door at the very top opened and Stefan’s head came out. P.40</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It shows that there is a noun phrase namely </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">The door at the very top</span><span class="font2"> with </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">door</span><span class="font2"> as the head of the phrase and with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> as the determiner of the phrase. It was categorized as a post-modified noun phrase since there are postmodifications placed after the head including prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at the very top </span><span class="font2">and the modifier occurred after the head.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1.3 . Multiple Modification Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 2 : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">It certainly felt like </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a tiny mosquito bite on the back of his neck</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. P7</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It shows that there is a noun phrase namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a tiny mosquito bite on the back of his neck</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bite</span><span class="font2"> as the head of the phrase. It was categorized as a multiple noun phrase since there are post-modification and pre-modification placed after and before the head. The pre-modification consists of determiner </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a, </span><span class="font2">adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tiny</span><span class="font2">, and noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mosquito</span><span class="font2">. The post-modification consists of prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">on the back of his neck</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function on English Noun Phrase</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function of Noun Phrase as an Object</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 16 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: He sent </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> around him. 124</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the data above, the NP functions as an Object, because it occurs in the end of the sentence, after the verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">sent</span><span class="font2">. It also can be analyzed using the formula what + S + P? The elaboration is as follows :</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: He sent </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> around him.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Formula &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: What did he sent for ?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Answer &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: one stealthy query of power.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the explanation above, it can be seen that the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> functions as an object, to be exact direct object.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In other way, the object of the sentence He sent </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> around him can be checked by changing the sentence above into passive sentence. An English noun phrase in a sentence can be said to function as an object if the object can be changed into subject in the passive.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Active Sentence &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: He sent </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> around him</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Passive Sentence &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: one stealthy query of power was sent by him.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In this case, the object </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> can be changed into subject in the passive sentence. It means that, the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">one stealthy query of power</span><span class="font2"> functioning as an object.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function of Noun Phrase as a Complement In PP Structure</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 15 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Maybe we just saw it at </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a weird angle</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. 115</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the data above, there is a noun phrase, namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a weird angle</span><span class="font2">. In the sentence, the underlined part functions as a complement, it can be seen that the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a weird angle</span><span class="font2"> functions as a complement because it is used to complete the object. Besides, there is a prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font2"> placed before the noun phrase. It can also be proved that a prepositional complement is a word, phrase, or clause that directly follows a preposition and completes the meaning of the prepositional phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function of Noun Phrase as a Predicative Complement</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 6 : He said in </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">a very low voice</span><span class="font2">. 76</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the data above, there is a noun phrase, namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a very low voice</span><span class="font2">. In the sentence, the underlined part functions as a predicative complement. It can be seen that the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">a very low voice</span><span class="font2"> functions as a complement because it</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">used to describe about what are the subject is, usually proved by an auxiliary placed after the subject. It can usually be proved that complement is a word, phrase, or clause that follows a copular, or linking, verb and describes the subject of a clause. The terms </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">predicate nominative</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">predicate noun</span><span class="font2"> are also used for noun phrases that function as predicative complements.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function of Noun Phrase as a Subject</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 22 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">The tiny old woman</span><span class="font2"> had stopped eating and was watching her</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">intently. 291</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the data above, there is a noun phrase, namely </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">The tiny old woman</span><span class="font2">. In the sentence, the underlined part functions as a subject, it can be seen that the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">The tiny old woman</span><span class="font2"> functions as a subject because it usually occur in the beginning of the sentence. Besides, it can be seen that a subject is a word, phrase, or clause that performs the action of or acts upon the verb.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">From the previous analysis in the chapter 3, it can be concluded that the noun phrase generally consist of four components, namely the head, the determinative, the pre-modification and the post-modification. But, there are several noun phrases which do not have all of the elements. The head served as the identify of noun phrase is certainly a noun. The structure of the noun phrases represented by the internal structures of the sentences. From the fifty six data analyzed randomly in the previous chapter, pre-modified noun phrase appears most frequently based on the structures of noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The noun phrase in sentences or clauses explained in this journal realized several functions, such as noun phrase as a subject, as an object, as a predicative</span></p>
