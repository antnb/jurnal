<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ANTIOXIDANT ACTIVITY TEST OF ANDROGRAPHOLIDE IN BITTER HERBS USING DPPH SCAVENGING</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4"><sup>1</sup> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Department of Pharmacy, Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences, Udayana University, Bukit Jimbaran, Badung Telp/Fax : 0361 224133, </span><a href="mailto:dekpitsusanti@unud.ac.id"><span class="font4">dekpitsusanti@unud.ac.id</span></a></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">Ni Made Pitri Susanti<sup>1</sup>, Ni Kadek Warditiani<sup>1</sup>, I Made Agus Gelgel Wirasuta<sup>1</sup></span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract </span><span class="font4">Bitter herbs (Sambiloto) have many pharmacological activities including antioxidant, antidiabetic, anticancer, antihyperlipidemic and anti-inflammation. Andrographolide is a diterpene compounds contained in bitter herbs. It is known that andrographolide compound responsible for the pharmacological activity of the bitter herbs. This study investigated DPPH free radical scavenging activity from andrographolide diterpene lactone. This study was initiated with the isolation of andrographolide compound from bitter herbs and then testing their DPPH free radical scavenging. The results suggested that andrographolide had IC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font4">value of 5.45 mg. This means andrographolide has 50% DPPH inhibition effect, i.e 5.45 mg.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords: andrographolide, antioxidant, bitter herbs, DPPH, free radical</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4"><a name="bookmark3"></a>I. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4" style="font-variant:small-caps;">Introduction</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">In our environment, there are many sources of free radicals which cannot be avoided. Among them are motor vehicle fumes, cigarette smoke, food fried in cooking oil, baked foods, certain drugs, and air pollution. Free radical is a molecule having one or more unpaired electrons. These free or unpaired electron is highly reactive to the body's cells. This suggests that the cells in our bodies are oxidized [1], [2].</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The presence of free radicals in the body that lead to oxidation reaction will cause damage to the nucleic acids, fat and DNA cell. This event will initiate the occurrence of degenerative diseases such as memory loss, cataracts, cancer and cardiovascular disorders [1], [3]. Our body has ability to protect cells against oxidation reactions that may occur in the presence of peroxidase, catalase, glutathione, histidine-peptidine. More over, the intake of foods containing antioxidants can also protect our body against free radical attack. Consumption of foods such as fruits and vegetables, contain antioxidants compounds like vitamins A (beta-carotene), C and E [4].</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Secondary metabolites contained in the plant have potential effect as free radical scavenger such as carotene, lycopene, polyphenols and flavonoids [5], [6]. The main mechanism of antioxidant compounds from secondary metabolites contained in the plant is the capability to bind, stabilize and prevent free radicals and not damage the body’s cells [5]. Dragon fruit (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Hylocereus undatus</span><span class="font4"> (Haw.) Britt. &amp;&nbsp;Rose; Cactaceae family) contains vitamin C and carotene that have potential antioxidants. Based on the research of DPPH free radical in methanol and water extracted from dragon fruit, 4.49% of water extract (equivalent to 3.08 g of fresh dragon fruit) has the ability to scavenge free radicals by</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">87.11% in the 5th minute and 90, 83% in the 30th minute; while 6.47% of methanol extract (equivalent to 4.44 g fresh dragon fruit) have the ability to scavenge free radicals by 79.18% in the 5th minute and 90.46 in the 30th minute. EC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font4">value of the water extract is 1.08%. Bitter herbs (sambiloto) play a role as an antioxidant against free radicals oxidative damage. Reference [7] suggested that the methanol extract of bitter herb (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Andrographis paniculata</span><span class="font4">) inhibit DPPH free radicals so they have an antioxidant potential. Inhibition of DPPH free radicals measurement by this bitter methanol extract is compared with gallic acid. But the study did not calculate the IC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font4">value. Thus, it is important to measure inhibition of DPPH free radicals from andrographolide compound of bitter herbs and calculate the IC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font4">value. The IC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font4">value is the concentration of a compound that shows an ability to inhibit free radicals by 50%.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4"><a name="bookmark5"></a>II.</span><span class="font4" style="font-variant:small-caps;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;methods and procedure</span></h3></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">A. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bitter Herbs Preparation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Bitter herbs obtained is dried then downsized to simplify and accelerate the extraction. Dried process is to reduce the water content in bitter herbs to prevent damage to the herbs. Damage herbs can be triggered by the growth of microbes or fungi and the enzymatic reaction due to high water content.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">B. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Extraction and Isolation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Bitter herbs powder was extracted with 96% ethanol by maceration method for 24 hours. After 2 times re-</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">maceration by using the same solvent, evaporation of the solvent in order to obtain a thick extract was done. Condensed extract of bitter herbs re-extracted with a </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font4">-hexane solvent to remove chlorophyl which were contained in the ethanol extract of bitter herbs. We separated the non soluble part in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font4">-hexane. The process was continued with extract purification process in order to obtain purified extract. Re-cristalization was done by dissolving the purified extract in hot methanol, and then cooling until it formed crystals andrographolide.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">C. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;DPPH Level Measurement</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Samples were prepared by weighing 10 mg of extract and dissolved in 1 mL of pro analysis methanol. An amount of 200 µL then was added with 1800 µL methanol. From this solution then pipette 100, 300, 500, 700 and 900 µL, respectively added by methanol to a series volume of 1 mL. Each sample was pipetted 500 µL and was added with 500 µL DPPH, incubated for 1 hour. DPPH crystals were dissolved in ethanol to get a concentration of 0.025% in methanol for immediate use and kept in light-protected low temperature.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Samples were then spotted on a TLC plate of silica gel GF 254. Plate was scanned at a wavelength of 530 nm.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">D. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;IC50 Measurement</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Inhibition of free radicals was calculated based on the result of reduction in area under the curve (AUC) forms by the sample AUC toward AUC at a wavelength of 530 nm using a calculation as refer to (1).</span></p>

Extraction using </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font4">-hexane was able to eliminate non-polar compounds compared to andrographolide chlorophyll. Andrographolide effect on inhibit DPPH was tested by adding andrographolide and reagents DPPH and performed incubation for 1 hour. Appropriate incubation time is very important note to get the value of AUC and a good spectrum at a 530 nm wavelength, maximum wavelenght for DPPH. Test for free radical scavenging begins with making spectrum in the range of 400-600 nm visible light from DPPH solution and andrographolide. This measurement is done to look at the pattern of the blank DPPH and DPPH added andrographolide spectrum.</span></p>
