<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ARTICLE</span></h1>

AND PROMOTE PLANT GROWTH OF CABBAGE</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">The high economic value of cabbage crop leads farmers to make various efforts to suppress the pathogen of club root which is detrimental to plants. Efforts that need to be put forward must be environmentally safe. One way that is environmentally friendly is to control a pathogen biologically using antagonistic microbes. Therefore, the use of microbes such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> which has been widely studied is important because it was able to suppress clubroot incidence and stimulate plant growth as well. Meanwhile, the need for plant nutrients to maximize plant growth requires an input of organic materials such as lignohumate which preserve soil nutrients, improve soil structure and increase plant resistance to biotic and abiotic stresses. The previous study on this scheme found an effective indigenous </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> to suppress clubroot, therefore it is used in the current study. The objective of this study was to find out a combination treatment of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> and lignohumate which can suppress clubroot and increase plant growth. This experiment used a Randomized Block Design with 2 factors and 3 replications. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> concentration consisted of 3 levels, namely 0 spores (control), 1x10<sup>6</sup> spores. 2x10<sup>6</sup> spores, and 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores per plant which were suspended in 150 ml of water. The lignohumate treatments were 0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5, 1.0, and 2%. The results showed that lignohumate treatment was interact with </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma </span><span class="font4">population number on disease incidence, total clubroot, root dry weight, but not to canopy dry weight. The most suitable combination of treatments was the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> population of 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores (15 g) in combination to lignohumate of 0.5%. This combination resulted in the lowest disease incidence, the lowest total clubroot, root dry weight, and the highest canopy dry weight. The higher the lignohumate concentration up to 0.5%, the higher the number of microbes (fungi and bacteria) growth, howeverit decreased above the concentration of 0.5%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font4">: cabbage, clubroot, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4">, lignohumate</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">Cabbage production in Bali is spread throughout all nine regencies in Bali except</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Jembrana, Klungkung, and Denpasar while the central production of cabbages is in Tabanan regency. Bali local cabbage</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">production from year to year since 2010 continues to decline. In 2010 cabbage production in Bali amounted to 47,077 tons, in 2011 to 42,926 tons, in 2012 amounted to 40,167 tons, and in 2013 it was only 35,781 tons (Bali Statistical Central Bureau, 2014). The decline in production is closely related to the attack of clubroot which is increasingly rampant in cabbage plants and even attacks other types. The attack of clubroot pathogens is always to be a problem to every cabbage production season and occurs in almost every cabbage &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;planting &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;area &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(personal</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">communication).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Cabbage clubroot is a</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disease"><span class="font4"> disease </span></a><span class="font4">of </span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brassicaceae"><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Brassicaceae</span><span class="font4"> </span></a><span class="font4">family caused by the soil-borne fungi </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Plasmodiophora brassicae</span><span class="font4"> Wor. The disease first appears scattered in fields, but can infect the entire field, reducing the yield significantly and sometimes resulting in no yield at all. Symptoms appear as yellowing, wilting, stunting, and</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galls"><span class="font4"> galls </span></a><span class="font4">on the roots. It is transmitted by contaminated transplants, animals, surface water runoff, contaminated equipment, and irrigation water. The pathogen can survive in a field for years as</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resting_spores"><span class="font4"> resting spores </span></a><span class="font4">without a host present and will infect the next susceptible crop.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Developing plants may not show any </span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symptoms"><span class="font4">symptoms </span></a><span class="font4">but as the plants get older they will start to show symptoms of chlorosis or yellowing, wilting during hot days, and exhibit stunted growth. Below ground, the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">roots experience</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cell_proliferation"><span class="font4"> cell proliferation </span></a><span class="font4">due to increased</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auxin"><span class="font4"> auxin </span></a><span class="font4">or growth</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hormone"><span class="font4"> hormone</span></a><span class="font4"> production from the plant as well as the pathogen. The pathogen excrete methyl transferase (PbBSMT) which reduce production of Salicylic Acid (SA), an immunity plant defense, so that the clubroot formed in root. The clubroot might be resulted by interference of root phytohormone system combined with meristematic activity of the root (Ludwig-Muller, 2015; Malinowski, 2012).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The clubroot can grow big enough to restrict the</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xylem"><span class="font4"> xylem </span></a><span class="font4">tissue inhibiting efficient water uptake by the plant. Galls appear like clubs or spindles on the roots. Eventually the roots will rot and the plant will die. Gall formation or distortion takes place on latent roots and gives the shape of a club or spindle. In the cabbage such attacks on the roots cause undeveloped heads or a failure to head at all, followed often by decline in vigor or by death. It is an important disease, affecting an estimated 10% of the total cultured area worldwide.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The high economic value of cabbage crops causes farmers to make various efforts to suppress the pathogen of clubroot. Efforts that need to be put forward must support sustainable agriculture and be environmentally safe. One way that is environmentally friendly is to control biologically using antagonistic microbes</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(Punja and Utkhede, 2003; Tian </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2007). Therefore, the use of microbes such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> which has been widely studied is important because it was able to suppress many pathogens such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">P. brassicae</span><span class="font4"> </span><a href="https://www.tandfonline.com/author/Yu,+Xue-Xin"><span class="font4">(Xue-</span></a><span class="font4"></span><a href="https://www.tandfonline.com/author/Yu,+Xue-Xin"><span class="font4">Xin Yu,</span></a><span class="font4"> 2015), </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium solani</span><span class="font4"> by 68.22% (Barari and Foroutan, 2013;), </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Rhizoctonia solani</span><span class="font4"> by 67.8-74.4% (Asad </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2014; Bastakoti </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2017). The advantage of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> application as biocontrol agent is stimulate plant growth as well (Sing </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2016; Patel </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2016). Mode of action of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> spp. is including to produce cell wall degrading enzymes as pectinase and chitinase, fast growing hypae to compete to other microbe, and able to produce antibiosis substance against pathogens (Munir </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2013; Sarma </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2014). </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma atroviride</span><span class="font4"> produce cell wall degrading enzymes such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">N</span><span class="font4">-acetyl-β-d-glucosaminidase, 1,4-β-Chitobiosidase, endochitinase, and glucose oxidase which harms to pathogens (Brunner </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2005). </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> spp. are free-living cosmopolitan species interacting with roots, soil, and foliar environments. Its mycelia is fast growing, therefore they compete strongly to other microorganisms. Furthermore, they degrade pectin and inhibit pectinases those are essential for plant-pathogenic fungi (Munir </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2013).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Meanwhile, the need to maximize plant growth requires nutrients which can be</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">derived from fertilizers that can be provided by organic materials such as humus or compost. Humic or compost affect plant physiology namely increase nutrient uptake, bind nutrients preventing leaching by run off water, increasing beneficial microbe, enhancing water preservation around root (Trevisan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2010, Canellas </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2015). Humic substances based amendments as fertilizers have been increased in recent years and proven increase plant yield. Lignohumate (LH) is one of humic sources commercially is used widely in agriculture for many crops and various conditions. The advantage of LH is preserve nutrients of leaching, improve soil structure, and increase plant resistance to biotic and abiotic stresses. The previous year's study on this scheme have been found an effective indigenous </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> to suppress clubroot and would be used in this study. The purpose of this study was to obtain the best combination treatment of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> and LH which able to suppress clubroot and also increase plant growth.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS Treatments</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">The treatments tested in this experiment was population of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma </span><span class="font4">as biocontrol agent in combination with doses of humic product Lignohumate (LH). The treatments were set according to a</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Randomized Block Design with 3 (three) replications. The indicator plant was cabbage (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Brassica oleracea</span><span class="font4"> L. var. capitata) which is susceptible to clubroot disease (field real experience).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Three days after transplanted, the lignohumete on each concentration were poured on around the root and one day after that the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> was poured for each population. The </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> applied one time, however the lignohumate was applied for three times. The first was poured on three days after transplanted and the next two were on three and six weeks after transplanted by spraying to the leaves of growing cabbage.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Treatments for LH was the variation of concentration namely: 0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5, 1.0, and 2% (w/v) in water which were poured eventually after cabbage planted. “Lignohumate AM” is a commercial humic product with microelements, produced from lignin by an industrial process, containing about 80% of humic substances, 9% K, 3% S, 0.1-1.2 g kg<sup>-1</sup> Mg, 0.1-2.0 g kg<sup>-1</sup> Fe, 0.1-1.2 g kg<sup>-1</sup> Cu and Co, 0.1-1.5 g kg<sup>-1 </sup>B, 0.05-1.15 g kg<sup>-1</sup> Mo, 0.1-1.2 g kg<sup>-1</sup> Zn (on dry matter basis).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> applied was </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">T. asperellum</span><span class="font4"> (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">identification record ITS4/ITS5 CBS 433.97 ITS region, 99%</span><span class="font4">) isolated from cabbage plantation and collected in Laboratoty of Biotechnology, Faculty of Agriculture, Udayana University. The</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">population treatment consisted of three levels, namely 0 inoculum (not spores nor mycelium, control), 1x10<sup>6</sup> spores, 2x10<sup>6 </sup>spores, and 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores per plant (equal to 0, 5, 10, and 15 g of half-cooked rice with spores) which were suspended in 150 ml of water. The treatment without inoculum (control), plants were doused with 150 ml of water per plant according to the treatment combination.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>Inoculum preparation</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">T. asperellum</span><span class="font4"> pure culture were inoculated into half-cooked rice aseptically. A half kilogram of the rice was put into plastic bag and shaking, then incubated for a week. When the blue colour appear on all around the rice, then the spores formed in it was measured by haemacytometer. In this research the amount of spores in one gram of the rice media was equals to 1 x 10<sup>6</sup> spores.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Seedling preparation</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">Cabbage seedling are prepared by means of seeds sowed on beds made of bamboo blades with a soil-humus mixture of 1:1 ratio. The beds are watered just enough everyday and after the seedling have grown and was 7 days old, the seedlings were ochered (soil fist at the root) and after 14 days were ready to be replanted to the field and arranged in a distance of 50 cm x 50 cm in a plot sized of 100 cm x 1200 cm.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Maintenance of plant</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">Plant maintenance was done to keep field capacity of humidity, removing weeds, and fertilization. All the plots (1 x 1.2 m<sup>2</sup>) were treated with basic NPK fertilization: Urea (46% N) and Ponska (15% N, 15% P</span><span class="font2">2</span><span class="font4">O5, and 15% K</span><span class="font2">2</span><span class="font4">O) with doses of Urea 2.4 g/plot + Ponska 2.4 g/plot which distributed two times. The application unorganic fertilizers were as follows: 1) one week after transplanting: &nbsp;1.2 &nbsp;g &nbsp;urea/plot &nbsp;+ &nbsp;1.2 &nbsp;g</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Ponska/plot, 2) five weeks after transplanting: &nbsp;1.2 &nbsp;g &nbsp;urea/plot &nbsp;+ &nbsp;1.2 &nbsp;g</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Phonska/plot.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Variables observed</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">During the experiment, leaf area and leaf chlorophyll content were measured at two time periods: 5 weeks and at 10 weeks after planting. After harvest the following variables were determined: disease incidence, total amount of galls on roots (clubroot), root dry weight, canopy (parts of the plant above the ground) dry weight, and microbe population including bacteria, fungi, and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> population. Chlorophyll content was measured using Chlorophyll Meter type SPAD-502 Plus (Konica Minolta) with its unit is SPAD.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>Microbe plating</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">One gram of soil sample was diluted in stages to 10<sup>-1</sup>, 10<sup>-2</sup>, 10<sup>-3</sup>, 10<sup>-4</sup>, 10<sup>-5</sup>, 10<sup>-6</sup>,</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">and 10<sup>-7</sup> in sterile water. One ml of each dilution was plated in different media on petri dish. Bacteria was plated in NA (Nutrient Agar Merck®: agar 15 g/l, beef extract 3 g/l, peptic digest of animal tissue 5 g/l, sodium chloride 8 g/l, final pH 6.0±0.2, 25<sup>o</sup>C; preparation was of 39 g powder in 1 litre of water) medium with 2% benzimidazole, while the fungus were isolated in PDA (Potato Dextrose Agar, Merck®: agar 15 g/l, dextrose 20 g/l, potato extract 4 g/l, pH 5.6±0.2, 25°C, preparation of 39 g in 1 litre of water) with 0.1% ciprofloxacyn-500). The colony appeared on medium were counted in colony counter apparatus, CFU/g soil.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Statistical analyses</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">The data were variant analysed according to the randomized complete design using the Microsoft Excel software. When the difference between the treatments was significant, this differences were compared by Duncan Multiple Range Test on level of 1 and 5%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>Disease Incidence</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">The Lignohumate (LH) and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> interacted to variable of disease incidence. Without </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4">, the exhibited of disease was decrease by the increase of LH doses while the doses of LH</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">increase, however, in the increase of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> population was not cause the decrease of disease incidence until the population of 2x10<sup>6</sup> CFU (10 g of application) (Table 1). Then, the combination of treatments that gave the best effect was the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> treatment on population of 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores (15 g). In this population the least disease incidence (7.1%) gained in LH dose of 0.5%, while the control treatment was 88.1% disease incidence. This</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">is the best combination of LH concentration (0.5%) and population of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma </span><span class="font4">(3x10<sup>6</sup> spores,15 g) which resulted to the lowest of disease incidence. When LH doses was increased from the dose of 0.5% in this the highest population, the disease incidence was increase significantly. This mean that, the doses more than 0.5% couldn’t support </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> to eliminate the pathogen infection.</span></p>

At combined application of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> with LH at rates higher then 0.02% the positive effect was much more pronounced, and in some cases the incidence of disease decreased up to 42-58%. The combination of treatments that gave the best effect was the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> treatment on population of 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores with LH-dose</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">0.5% which provided the least disease incidence (7.1%). However, at high doses of LH (1-2%) some growth of disease incidence was observed comparably to the one at lower rates (0.02-0.50%). This means that the doses more than 0.5% couldn’t support </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> to eliminate the pathogen infection.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>Root dry weight</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">High root dry weight was a result of the formation of high root cell volumes that occur due to root cell division which influenced by of the pathogen </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">P. brassicae</span><span class="font4">. When the pathogen interacts with its host, auxin will soon formed in plant cells, resulting in uncontrolled root cell division and tumor formation. High root dry weight indicates that the treatment is ineffective as in control. So the lower the dry weight of the root (to a certain extent), the more effective the treatment. Effective treatment of pressing clubroot according to low root dry weight and the most effective treatment was 0.5%</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">LH dose combination with </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma </span><span class="font4">concentration of 3x10<sup>6</sup> (15 g) which produced 3.8 g of root dry weight.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">The LH on 0.05% concentration application was able to promote the dry weight of seedling of some cabbage family plant (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lactuca sativa, Eruca sativa, Coriandrum sativum, Brassica oleracea</span><span class="font4"> var. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">capitata</span><span class="font4">) as twice of non treated plant (Suada </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2017). Lignosulfonate-humate-a and lignosulfonate-humate-b elicited hormonelike activity and leonardite displayed giberellin properties and subsequently increase the growth (Ertani </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2011).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">LH can stimulate microbial growth of up to 0.5% concentration. The higher the LH concentration, the higher the number of microbes (fungi and bacteria) population, however, after a concentration of 0.5% LH, the microbial population decreases. According to Yakov (2010) that organic matter in the soil will invite microbes to grow because microbes get food through the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">decomposition of these organic materials. In fertile soils, which contain high organic ingredients there are 100 million microbes of various types per gram of soil. In this study, increasing lignohumate will increase the number of microbes to a concentration of 0.5%, however, starting concentrations of greater than 0.5% the microbial population decreases. This contradicts to the statement that in more fertile soils more microbes inhabit the soil due to some disadvantageous happened in this condition. The LH concentration was not supported the growth of microbes.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The effect of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> was exhibited by many researcher that the fungi has some advantages through directly and undirectly in influencing pathogen and its host. Direct mechanisms is mycoparasitism, the Trichoderma’s mycelia titling other fungi mycelia and such its plasma till suffering and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">dried. In this case the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Plasmodiphora brassicae</span><span class="font4"> was not have a mycelia so this mechanism can’t be applied in this problems. Indirectly ways is competing for space and also nutrients, promoting plant defensive ability, antibiosis and moreover promoting plant growth (Benitez </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2004; Bastakoti </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2017 ). The biocontrol processes occurred may coordinately and its effectiveness is defend on the strain of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma,</span><span class="font4"> the antagonized fungus in the rhizoplane, the crop plant, and plant cirscumstance. The environmental factors which influenced to the control system is nutrient availability, iron concentration, pH, and temperature. Moreover, activity of each mechanisms will promote the production of some antibiosis compound such as plant growth factor, siderophore, antibiotic, hydrolytic enzymes, gliotixin (produced by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">T. virens</span><span class="font4">) and also carbon and nitrogen elements. The enzymes produced are able to hydrolyze chitin, protein, cellulose, and hemicellulose of cells (Canellas </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font4">, 2015).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">LH was able to increase the synthesis of chlorophyll in cabbage plants. The highest chlorophyll formation (7400.77 SPAD in Fig. 4) was achieved at 1% LH dose in this study. The increased due to LH also occurs when LH was sprayed onto leaves, it was able to increase photosynthesis rates because higher chlorophyll content achieved. The same phenomenon is reported for various</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">plants such as pepper (Karakurt </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2009) and chrysanthemum (Fan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2014). More over, according to Ertani </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">. (2011) and Poloskin </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">. (2012), LH has been shown to increase chlorophyll production. High chlorophyll has more implications for more photosynthesis which results in the formation of more carbohydrate products which means the dry weight of plants will be greater than without LH.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> species is capable of producing 100 antibiotic metabolites including polyketides, pyrones, terpenes, which are derivative metabolites from amino acids and polypeptides (Sivasithamparam and Ghisalberti,</span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2886115/%23CR144"><span class="font4"> 1998)</span></a><span class="font4">. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> as a biocontrol agent has been effective against pathogens such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Pythium aphanidermatium, Gaeumannomyces graminis var. tritici, Fusarium oxysporum, Rhizoctonia solani, Fusarium culmorum, Sclerotium rolfsii, Phytophthora cactorum, Botrytis cinerea</span><span class="font4"> and several </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Alternaria </span><span class="font4">species (Dolatabadi </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2011; Bastakoti </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2017). Role of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font4"> not only suppresses the growth of pathogens, but there are other influences that are beneficial for plants, namely increasing plant defenses, stimulating colonization by the rhizosphere microbes and stimulating plant root growth (Vinale </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2008).</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>CONCLUSIONS</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">The combination of the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma </span><span class="font4">treatment of population of 3x10<sup>6</sup> spores (15 g with the treatment of LH of 0.5% resulted in the lowest root fresh weight, the lowest of root dry weight, the highest canopy fresh weight, the highest canopy dry weight, the lowest number of clubroot, and the lowest percentage of disease attack, indicated that this combination was the most suitable treatment in suppressing clubroot attack and promote plant growth of cabbage. The higher the lignohumate concentration up to 0.5%, the higher the number of microbes growth.</span></p>
