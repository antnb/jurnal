<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstract</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini berjudul “The Constituent Structure Bearing Trivalent Verbs with Reference to “Special Delivery” by Danielle Steel. Data diambil dari novel &quot;Special Delivery&quot; karya Danieele Steel. Kegiatan ini bertujuan untuk menyelesaikan kalimat/frase ke dalam komponen-komponen dan menggambarkannya secara gramatikal. Terdapat dua teori yang digunakan di penelitian ini. Teori pertama adalah teori tentang sintaksis, yaitu tentang penelitian struktur yang konstituen yang dibuat oleh Brown &amp;&nbsp;Miller (1991) dan teori kedua adalah teori tentang valensi yang dibuat oleh Allerton (1982). Data yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini terdiri dari kalimat-kalimat berbahasa inggris yang berstruktur kata kerja bervalensi tiga, yang diambil dari novel “Special Delivery” karya Danielle Steel. Dalam pengumpulan data, metode yang digunakan adalah penelitian kepustakaan. Setelah data terkumpul, data diklasifikasikan ke masing-masing tipe, dan akhirnya analisis tentang struktur yang konstituen dilakukan. Temuan analisis menunjukkan bahwa penggunaan teori sintaksis berhasil menganalisis struktur internal dari kalimat/frase dengan menggunakan diagram pohon.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Valensi, struktur yang konstituen, diagram pohon.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on Syntax level, every sentence in every natural language has a specific syntactic (constituent) structure. It means that all phrases in a sentence, are headed by a lexical head. Every word in a sentence has their own syntactic function. However, verb is considered an important part that build up a sentence. Verb is the nucleus of the sentence of a clause, in the sense that the clause structure is determined by the nature of that verb used. To build a structure, a verb needs to be accompanied by one or more constituents. The constituents that usually follow a verb may be noun phrase, prepositional phrase, adjective phrase, and adverb phrase. Syntax also plays an important role in the stage of clause, whereas the verb binds the other elements in a sentence by forming a </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">valency </span><span class="font1">bound. In this case, a verb requires other constituents to build up the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The study of syntactic structure is mostly related to the morphological study. It means that this analysis concentrates on discussing </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">valency</span><span class="font1"> specifically from the syntactical study point of view. It now seems worthwhile to ask what a notion like </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">valency</span><span class="font1"> can contribute to our understanding of syntax. The description will cover all aspects connected to the constituent structure especially the trivalent verbs. The structural relations between the major constituents within a sentence will be represented in the form of tree diagram.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Constituent Structure Analysis of English Sentences Bearing Trivalent Verbs</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The analysis of the constituent structure of the sentences used the basis of a sentence ‘parser,’ as proposed by Keith &amp;&nbsp;Brown. Every step of sentence parsing procedure or usually known as ‘sentence parsing rule,’ will be given below:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Determining from the lexicon the form class of every word and developed partial tree for each word where the form class label dominates the word.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finding a grammar rule with X as the left-hand symbol and a category or categories as the right-hand symbol (s) (for example, X —► &nbsp;&nbsp;Y</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Z) and where the right-hand symbol(s) match some sequence of categories in the structure so far and developing a partial tree with X as the mother and the right-hand symbols as ordered daughters.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Continuing rule (2) until the tree is rooted in S and there are no unattached items.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">(Keith &amp;&nbsp;Brown, 1991: 47)</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are some conclusions that can be taken based on the analysis:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Trivalent verb is a verb that needs to be accompanied by three other constituents to build a structure in the sentence. A verb with three constituents, forms many combinations of trivalent structure. According to the combination of constituents that a trivalent verb requires, there are eight types of sentences bearing trivalent verbs found in the novel:</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + Oi + O</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Oblique O</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + Objoid i + Objoid</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Pred</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Prep O</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">6) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Prep Objoid</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">7) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Adverbial Elaborator</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">8) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sbj + V + O + Adverb Limiter</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Constituent structure of English sentences bearing trivalent verb is determined by combination of elements in the sentence, which usually known as constituents. These constituents form a specific syntactic (constituent) structure.</span></p></li></ul>
