<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kajian ini bertujuan untuk mencari tipe-tipe idiom dan menganalisis makna dari idiom pada lirik lagu di album Katy Perry yang berjudul Prism. Teori yang digunakan untuk menganalisis data adalah teori dari McCarthy dan O’dell (2010) tentang tipe-tipe idiom. Selain teori tersebut, teori dari Leech (1981) tentang tipe-tipe makna juga digunakan unutk menganalisis data. Data yang dikumpulkan dianalisa menggunakan metode deskriptif kualitatif. Ada 6 tipe idiom yang ditemukan pada lirik lagi di album Prism, yaitu simile, binomial, euphemism, cliché, fixed statement, dan idioms from other languages. Berdasarkan tipe-tipe idiom yang ditemukan pada data tersebut ditemukan dua tipe makna, yaitu connotative meaning and reflected meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Idioms, Types, Meanings, Song, Lyrics</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Idioms are words, phrases, and expressions that cannot be translated literally. In other words, they have different meanings from the basics ones in a dictionary. According to McCarthy and O’dell (2010), idioms are fixed combinations of words whose meaning is often difficult to guess from the meaning of each individual word. It means that idioms cannot be understood by defining separate words. The use of idioms in the song lyrics has become a common thing in order to express the message of the song. The idioms have its role in enriching the meaning of the language with their beautiful words that they created. In the same time, their beautiful words also often confuse people.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">When we learn about idioms, we also have to learn the meaning of idiom in order to understand it. Meaning is the thing or idea that a sound, word, sign,</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">etc. represents (Hornby, 2015). In addition, Palmer (1976:34) states that meaning is notion in semantics which studies sense relations through which assumptions can be either true or false. It is very useful to find out and understanding the meaning of idioms rather than listen without knowing or understanding their meaning in the song lyrics. Therefore, the message in the song lyrics can be delivered to the listeners well.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data in this study were taken directly from Katy Perry: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Prism </span><span class="font1">(2013) album. This album consists of sixteen songs but only ten songs that contain idioms are used as data source.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this study, the data were collected using the documentation method. Documentation method is a research conducted by documenting the data related to the taken topic. The process of collecting data was done by browsing the lyrics of songs, listening to the songs and reading the lyrics repeatedly and carefully in order to understand the meaning behind it, and taking notes of every word or phrase which is considered to be idiom then writing it on a separated paper to be analyzed further.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were analyzed using the qualitative descriptive method based on the theories. The idioms were categorized according to their types based on the theory proposed by McCarthy and O’dell (2010) and the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">meanings of the idioms were analyzed based on the theory proposed by Leech (1981).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>4.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique &nbsp;of</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>Presenting Analysis</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are two methods of presenting data according to Sudaryanto (1993) which are formal and informal. In formal method, the data are presented using symbols, tables, or graphs which are aimed to describe the data more easily. On the other hand, in informal method the analysis is descriptively presented in the forms of words and sentences. Since this study applied qualitative method, the data analysis was presented informally in descriptive sentences.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Slow cooking pancakes for my boy, still up, still </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">fresh as a Daisy </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Playing ping pong all night long, everything's all </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">neon and hazy </span><span class="font1">The simile </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">fresh as a Daisy</span><span class="font1"> and the binomial </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">neon and hazy</span><span class="font1"> are classified as having </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">reflected meaning </span><span class="font1">(Leech, 1981) as their meanings occur in cases of multiple conceptual meaning, when one sense of a word forms part of our response to another sense. The song writer chose the expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">fresh as a Daisy </span><span class="font1">rather than the expression ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">full of energy</span><span class="font1">’ or ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">really enjoying</span><span class="font1">’ and chose </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">neon and hazy</span><span class="font1"> rather than the word</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘drink’ or ‘smoking’ to give softer sense to his lyrics.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Analysis 2:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So let me get you </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in your birthday suit</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the theory of meaning proposed by Leech (1981), the idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in your birthday suit</span><span class="font1"> was classified as having </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">reflected meaning </span><span class="font1">since its meaning occurs in cases of multiple conceptual meaning, when one sense of a word forms part of our response to another sense. The songwriter chose the expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in your birthday suit </span><span class="font1">rather than the word ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">naked</span><span class="font1">’ or ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">bare</span><span class="font1">’ to give softer sense to his lyric.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark49"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark50"></a>Analysis 3:</span></h2>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I thank my sister for keeping my head above the water When </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the truth was like swallowing sand</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From Leech’s point of view (1981), the simile </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the truth was like swallowing sand</span><span class="font1"> is classified into </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">reflected meaning</span><span class="font1">, since its meaning occurs in cases of multiple conceptual meaning, when one sense of a word forms part of our response to another sense. The writer used words </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">swallowing sand</span><span class="font1"> rather than the words ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hard to accept</span><span class="font1">’ or ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hard to do</span><span class="font1">’ to give dramatic effect to his lyric.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark51"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark52"></a>Analysis 4:</span></h2>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Flying </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">high as a kite </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">on your love</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">high as a kite</span><span class="font1"> can be classified as having </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">reflected meaning</span><span class="font1">, as its meaning occurs in case of multiple conceptual meaning, when one sense of a word forms part of our response to another sense (Leech, 1981). The songwriter chose the simile </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">high as a kite</span><span class="font1"> rather than the word ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">drunk</span><span class="font1">’ to give softer sense to his lyric.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark53"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark54"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis, it can be concluded that there are six types of idioms found in the Katy Perry’s </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Prism </span><span class="font1">song lyrics; they are: simile, binomial, euphemism, cliché, fixed statement, and idiom from other language. In the song lyrics as data source, there are seven similes, two binomials, one euphemism, six clichés, two fixed statement, and one idiom from other language found. Added together, there are nineteen idioms that found.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From seven there are only two types of meaning found in the Katy Perry’s </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Prism</span><span class="font1"> song lyrics. They are, connotative meaning and reflected meaning. These two types of meaning found in the song lyrics because they use words that cannot be translated by looking up the literal meaning in the dictionary and they also use referred words to give dramatic effect or to give softer sense to the lyrics.</span></p>
