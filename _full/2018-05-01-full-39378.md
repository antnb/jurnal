<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstract</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">This study is entitled Compound Word Formation in “The Old Man and The Sea”. This study focuses on two problems; types of compound words found in the novel and the semantic transparency theypossess. The aims of this study are to find out the types of compound words and also to find and describe the semantic transparency of the compound words found in the novel.The data source used in this study is a novel written by Ernest Hemmingway entitled The Old Man and The Sea. The data which were compound words were collected by using documentation method whereas to analyze the data by using descriptive qualitative method. There were three theories used in this study. The main theories are proposed by O’Grady (1996) about the types of the compound and by Katamba (1993) about classifying the data. The theory proposed by Libben (1998) was used to describe the semantic transparency of the compound words. The results show that the types of compound words found in the novel are endocentric compound and exocentric compounds. However, the endocentric compound was more frequent than the exocentric compound. Based on the second problem, there are four types of semantic transparency ;they are (1) transparency of both members of the compound, (2) transparency of the head member – opacity of the non-head member, (3) transparency of the non-head member – opacity of the head member, (4) opacity of both members of the compound.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords: compound word, semantic transparency,word formation</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Morphology is a branch of linguistics studying forms of words. Morphology can be said as a helping branch for languages in multiplying their vocabulary. For instance, the compound word “lamplight” is the combination of the word “lamp” and “light”. Anderson, (1992: 24) stated “morphology is the branch of linguistics that studies patterns of word formation within and across languages, and attempts to formulate rules that model the knowledge of the speakers of those languages”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Since morphology is simply the study of word formation, there are many processes of word formations. O’Grady divided word formation processes into six categories; affixation, cliticization, internal change, suppletion, reduplication, and compounding. Compounding is the combination of lexical categories (nouns, adjectives, verbs, or prepositions) to create a larger word. There are two types of compound word; endocentric and exocentric. These two types of compound word would be analyzed based on their “head”, components, and meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Endocentric compounds are by far preferred to exocentric compounds in the languages of the world, because they allow much easier access to the head (Libben, 2006). On the other hand, exocentric compound is a compound which lacks a head word;that is why this kind of compound word is also known as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">headless compound</span><span class="font2">. Adams (2013) illustrates the term exocentric which describes expressions in which no part seems to be of the same kind as the whole or to be central to it.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It led to the fact that it is interesting to find out the relationship between semantic transparency and the compound word itself, since it is mentioned in Katamba’s Morphology that it is impossible to work out what exocentric compound means from the sum of the meanings of its constituents (Katamba, 1993). The novel was chosen as the data source because it is relevant since fictional literary works such as novel provides many compound words, and authors of novel are very fond of playing words and combining them into new words.</span></p>

Consequently, the compound word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">broadbill</span><span class="font2"> is an </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">exocentric compound</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compound Verb</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There was no compound verb data found in the novel, it is neither a preposition followed by a verb nor verb followed by preposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark45"></a>5.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Semantic Transparency</span></h2></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark46"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark47"></a>1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transparency of both members of the compound</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Word : Fisherman</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Meaning : person who catches fish. It refers to Santiago, an aging Cuban fisherman.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">fisherman</span><span class="font2"> is transparent both of its constituents, because the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">fisher</span><span class="font2"> means one that fishes while </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">man </span><span class="font2">means an individual human. Both of its constituents match with the meaning of the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">fisherman</span><span class="font2"> above. So, the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">fisherman</span><span class="font2"> is fully transparent</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark48"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark49"></a>2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transparency of the head member – opacity of the non-head member</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Word : Man-of-war bird</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Meaning : Frigatebirds are family of seabirds &nbsp;&nbsp;called fregatidae</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">which are found across all tropical &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;subtropical</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">oceans. This bird helps the old man locate the location of the fish.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word that determines the whole compound in this data is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bird </span><span class="font2">because the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">man-of-war bird</span><span class="font2"> refers to a certain type of bird; therefore, the head is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bird</span><span class="font2">, and it is transparent. On the other hand, the meaning of the hyphened non-head member </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">man-of-war</span><span class="font2"> is opaque, because the characteristics of the mentioned bird have no relation with the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">man-of-war</span><span class="font2">. Therefore, this word is considered as transparency of the head member – opacity of the non-head member.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark50"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark51"></a>3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transparency of the non-head member – opacity of the head member</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Word : Clear-headed</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Meaning : having or showing an ability to think clearly.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The only transparency of the nonhead member and opacity of the head</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">member that can be found in this novel is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">clear-headed</span><span class="font2">. The head </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">headed</span><span class="font2"> which determines the whole compound in this data is opaque, because the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">headed </span><span class="font2">in this context means the mind or the ability to think not the head. In contrast, the non-head member is transparent because the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">clear</span><span class="font2"> which means free from doubt and confusion matches with the meaning of the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">clear-headed </span><span class="font2">above.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark52"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark53"></a>4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Opacity of both members of the compound</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Word &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Loggerheads</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Meaning : The loggerhead sea turtle</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">carettacaretta</span><span class="font2">) is an oceanic turtle belonging to the family</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Cheloniidae. This &nbsp;turtle is</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">disliked by the old man.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">loggerheads</span><span class="font2"> isopacity of both constituents because the constituents of this compound are opaque. It can be seen from the meaning of the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">loggerheads</span><span class="font2"> above that have neither relation with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">logger</span><span class="font2">nor</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">heads</span><span class="font2">, rather than it refers to a species of an oceanic turtle.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark54"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark55"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the discussion in Chapter 3, it was found that compound words can be classified into two types which are endocentric compound and exocentric compound, and every compound has a different type of semantic transparency.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The finding of the discussion answering the first problem of this study, was to find the types of compound word found in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Old Man and The Sea </span><span class="font2">novel. The result is that there are two types of compound words according to its </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">head</span><span class="font2">, whether the compound has a head or not. The endocentric can be located from its head that mostly on the right part of the constituents, in which it determines the whole compound. On the other hand, exocentric is also known as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">headless</span><span class="font2"> compound, having no head,</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">because none of its constituents that can determine the meaning of the whole compound. As known, the endocentric compound was far more plenty in number compared to exocentric compound, and that also happened in this research that exocentric is fewer than endocentric.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The second problem was to find the types of semantic transparency of the compound words in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Old Man and The Sea</span><span class="font2"> novel. From the discussion above, it can be seen that there are four types of semantic transparency based on Libben (1998); they are; (1) transparency of both members of the compound, (2) transparency of the head member – opacity of the non-head member, (3) transparency of the non-head member – opacity of the head member, (4) opacity of both members of the compound.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Moreover, based on the discussion above that the semantic transparency is related to the type of the compound word. It can be seen that all of the endocentric &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;compounds &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;have</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">transparency of both members, while exocentric can be on other three types of semantic transparency, but most of them are opacity of both members of the compound.</span></p>
