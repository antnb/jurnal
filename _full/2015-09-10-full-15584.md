<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">THE EXORCIST</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> MOVIE</span></p><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>I Wayan Restu Indra Darmawan</span></h1>

<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Studi penerjemahan telah banyak berkembang hingga saa tini.Salah satunya adalah dengan munculnya metode penerjemahaan audiovisual. Penerjemahan audiovisual mengkhususkan pada terjemahan-terjemahan objek suara dan gambar seperti film. Salah satu teknik dalam penerjemahan audiovisual adalah ‘subtitling’. ‘Subtitling’ merupakan proses penerjemahan dari bentuk tulisan ke bentuk suara. Salah satu tantangan dalam penerjemahan dengan teknik ‘subtitling’ adalah menerjemahkan ekspresi tabu. Tabu merupakan hal-hal yang dilarang untuk diucapkan atau dilakukan. Film ‘The Exorcist’ merupakan sumber data dalam studi ini. Tujuan studi ini adalah untuk menemukan tipe ekspresi tabu dan strategi ‘subtitling’ yang digunakan dalam film ‘The Exorcist’. Hasil dari studi ini adalah terdapat enam puluh satu ekspresi tabu yang ditemukan pada film ‘The Exorcist’. Keenam puluh satu ekspresi tabu tersebut termasuk ke dalam delapan tipe ekspresi tabu diajukan oleh Jay (1992) diantaranya ‘cursing’, ‘profanity’, ‘blasphemy’, ‘obscenity’, ‘vulgarity’, ‘epithets’, ‘insult and slurs’ dan ‘scatology’. Selain itu, keenam puluh satu ekspresi tabu tersebut diterjemahkan menggunakan strategi ‘subtitling’ diajukan oleh Gottlieb (1992) diantaranya ‘paraphrase’, ‘transfer’, ‘imitation’, ‘condensation’, ‘decimation’, dan ‘deletion’.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: penerjemahan, subtitling, tabu</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In recent years, the imports of foreign movies are high. Hollywood, as one of the biggest movie industries in the world takes this chance to enlarge their business. As a global product, Hollywood’s movies used English as the main language. However, a problem comes as not all countries put English as their first or second language. Some countries such as Indonesia, Korea, Japan, etc. study English as foreign language. Therefore, it will be hard for non-native speakers to understand and enjoy the movies. The only way to solve this problem is by translation. Translation is transferring meaning from source language (SL) to the target language (TL). Many things can be the object of translation study including movies. However, a special technique is needed in order to translate a movie as it</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">is an audiovisual product. It is because the regular translation usually takes written text to be translated into written text. Therefore, in translation study there is a technique that is called as Audiovisual Translation (AVT). AVT has three modes, namely dubbing, voice-over, and subtitling. Subtitling is the most interesting one since it is not only translating one language to another language, but also from one mode to another mode, spoken to written form. One of the biggest challenges in subtitling is a cultural item. One of them is taboo. Taboo is something that is forbidden to say or to act.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the background, there are two problems of which answers need to be found in this study. The questions are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of taboo expressions are found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> movie?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What subtitling strategies are used in translating taboo expressions into Indonesian subtitles in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> movie?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to the problems, the aims of this study can be considered as follows;</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify and analyze the types of taboo expressions uttered by the characters in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> movie.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze subtitling strategies used in translating taboo expression into Indonesian subtitles in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> movie.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">As the data source of the study, a movie entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> was chosen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> is a horror movie published in 1973. The movie describes the exorcism process of a young girl. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist</span><span class="font1"> won Oscars in 1974 for Best Screenplay Best Study, Screenplay Based on Material from Another Medium and Best Sound. The movie provides many taboo expressions which are needed to this study. The data of this study were the taboo expressions uttered by the characters and also the Indonesian subtitles of the movie. In collecting the data of the movie,</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">observation and note-taking techniques were needed. Several steps were applied in order to support the observation and note-taking techniques. In order to analyze the data, qualitative descriptive method was used. This method was supported by several steps. The first step in analyzing data by categorizing the taboo expressions based on their types proposed by Jay (1992: 2-9). The next step was comparing the taboo expressions and their translation in the Indonesian subtitle in the form of a table. In addition, the time (duration) when the taboo expressions were being uttered by the characters in the movie were also inserted in the table. Finally, analyzing the subtitling strategies proposed by Gottlieb (1992).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Results And Discussions</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Taboo Expressions</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In order to analyze the types of taboo expressions found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Exorcist </span><span class="font1">movie, theory of types of taboo expressions proposed by Jay (1992) was used. After analyzing the data, there are 61 taboo expressions found in the movie. They are included in the eight (8) types of taboo expressions proposed by Jay (1992) such as cursing, profanity, blasphemy, obscenity, vulgarity, epithets, insult and slur, and scatology.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cursing</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The core of cursing is to invoke harm on another person. Those words are related to religious or social demarcation which has power (Jay, 1992). There are four (4) expressions that are considered cursing such as one </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">damn</span><span class="font1"> (1) and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">goddamn</span><span class="font1">(3).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Profanity</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Profanity is based on a religious distinction. It deals with to be secular or behaving out (Jay, 1992). There are eighteen (18) expressions that are considered profanity such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Oh my God!</span><span class="font1">(6)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, Jesus Christ!</span><span class="font1">(4)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, Christ’s sake</span><span class="font1">(3), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">God’s sake</span><span class="font1">(2), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Oh God!</span><span class="font1">(2)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Oh Christ!</span><span class="font1">(1)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blasphemy</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Blasphemy usually deals with the church. The attack on religion or religious doctrine can be called blasphemy. There are two (2) expressions that are considered blasphemy such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">priest</span><span class="font1"> (2)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Obscenity</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Obscenity usually deals with sexual terms in American English (Jay, 1992). There are nine (9) expressions that are categorized as obscenity such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Fuck</span><span class="font1"> (3), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Ass</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Fucking</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lick</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Cunting</span><span class="font1">(1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Cocksucker</span><span class="font1"> (1), and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sucks cock</span><span class="font1"> (1).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vulgarity</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Vulgarity means the language of the common person, particularly person who spend most of their time in the street, or the unsophisticated, un-socialized, under educated (Jay, 1992). There is one expression that is categorized as vulgarity such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Bloody.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Epithets</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Epithets are short and brief word or phrases, but it is forceful burst of emotional language (Jay, 1992). There are fourteen (14) expressions that are considered as epithets such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Shut up!</span><span class="font1"> (3), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Fuck</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Christ’s sake</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">My ass</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Goddamn</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Oh Christ</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Shit</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hell</span><span class="font1"> (1), Bullshit (1), Fucking (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jesus Christ</span><span class="font1"> (1), and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Son of a bitch</span><span class="font1"> (1).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Insult and slurs</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Slur deals with racial, ethnic or social which denote the physical mental or psychological qualities of the target while children’s insults comes from abnormal physical, psychological or social characteristics (Jay, 1992). There are twelve (12) expressions that are considered as insult and slurs such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Bastard</span><span class="font1"> (6), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pig</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Cunting Hun</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sow</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Crazy</span><span class="font1"> (1), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Bitch</span><span class="font1"> (1), and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Faggot</span><span class="font1"> (1).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scatology</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Scatological terms refer to human waste products or processes (Jay, 1992). There is one expression that is considered scatology such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Shit.</span></p>
