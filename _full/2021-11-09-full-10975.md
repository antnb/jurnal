<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">LANGUAGE FUNCTION OF PIDGINIZED ENGLISH USED BY THE</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">TRADERS IN TANAH LOT</span></p><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>I Gusti Agung Istri Satyawati</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">English Department, Faculty of Letters &amp;&nbsp;Culture, Udayana University</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Judul artikel ini adalah fungsi bahasa dari Pidginized Inggris yang digunakan oleh para pedagang di Tanah Lot.. Artikel ini menganalisa dan membahas tentang fungsi bahasa dan jenis kesalahan yang dibuat oleh para pedagang di Tanah Lot dalam lingkup Pidginized inggris. Data diambil dari bahasa lisan dan tulis dari pedagang di Tanah Lot. Data diklasifikasikan dan dianalisa dengan menggunakan teori dari Mulhausler (1986), Hudson (1980), Holmes (1992) dan Haliday (1973). Berdasarkan analisisa, ada 8 jenis fungsi bahasa yang ditemukan dalam percakapan antara pedagang dan pelanggan asing di Tanah Lot, ada fungsi sapaan, mengundang, menawarkan sesuatu, menjelaskan sesuatu, permohonan, pemintaan maaf, terimakasih dan mengatakan selamat tinggal. Dan ada juga beberapa penyederhanaan dari tata bahasa yang ditemukan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Fungsi bahasa kesalahan, Pidginized</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this field study, the speaker in this case traders use the language in order to attract the customer. Although the language used is sometimes impolite and rude, but as long as both of the customers and the speakers (traders) understand each other, the conversation is going on. Based on this condition, the language functions are generally used by traders to interact with the foreign customers. Their use of English is much influenced by their own dialects and combination with their local term, words, and expression. Thus their pronunciations is rather strange, funny, and different from Standard English. The combination of two or more languages may result is some language phenomena. One of those phenomena is Pidgin language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of language function and forms of Pidginized English were used by the traders in Tanah Lot?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What kind of errors were made by the traders in Tanah Lot in scope of Pidginized English?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify and describe the types of language functions and the form of the Pidginized English used by the traders in Tanah Lot.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze kind of errors made by the traders in Tanah Lot in scope of Pidginized English.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Research method is a scientific method (the steps used in doing scientific research) as a procedure of taking, tabulating, and analyzing the data. The method used in this study can be described as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data for this research were taken from the written language of the traders found in Tanah Lot. The written data were in the form of questionnaire, which were distributed to the traders.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The Data were collected through </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">field research.</span><span class="font1"> In this case, the number of the respondents was limited up to 10, taken randomly. And The techniques used for collecting the data were observation and interview.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The method used in analyzing data is qualitative method, which means the data were analyzed based on the theory applied as theoretical basis. And the techniques used for analyzing the data were collected through transcribing and categorization.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The classified data were analyzed using the theory proposed by Mulhausler (1986) as a main theory. Beside the main theory proposed by Mulhausler, the current study was also supported by other supporting theories which are from Hudson (1980), Holmes (1992) and Haliday (1973).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Language Function of Pidginized English Used by The Traders in Tanah Lot</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The form of Pidginized English and the types of language function which are used by The traders in Tanah Lot.</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This part describes the analysis of the types of language function and form of pidginized English used by the traders when they talked to the foreign customers in Tanah Lot.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Greeting</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Greeting</span><span class="font1"> is used when we give a sign or word of welcome or pleasure when we are meeting somebody. In this case traders gave greeting to visitors as one way to attract attention and as well as courtesy. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Hello sir, how you doing?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Inviting</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this occasion, the function of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">inviting</span><span class="font1"> used by the traders to invite the customer, in order to glance at them and at the same time they would see their product. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Hello, come to my shop, free for looking looking</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Offering Something</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This Function of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">offering something</span><span class="font1"> is used when the traders put forward something to be considered, so it can then be either accepted or refused. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">You want Sir? You have cigarette?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Describing Thing</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Describing thing</span><span class="font1"> is the language function was used to describe an object, or the product. The traders may need to do this because their customer is unfamiliar with the object. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">This made in Bali</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Requesting</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The Function of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">requesting</span><span class="font1"> is used to ask politely for something. This Function was used by traders with a hope they influence the customer to do something. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Please buy one Sir, my best one</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>5.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Apologizing</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The function of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">apologizing</span><span class="font1"> one is making apology, especially for having done, something wrong or for disappointing somebody. The traders should know what the customer expected, in order to make the customers satisfied. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I am sorry, I will give you the other one</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>5.1.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thanking</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Thanking</span><span class="font1"> is used to express gratitude to someone else, who help us or because we want to respect them. The traders said thanks to show their respect for buying their product. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Thank you sir, next time come back again ya!</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>5.1.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Saying Good bye (Leave-taking)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Saying good bye</span><span class="font1"> is used by traders to say goodbye to their customer at the end of conversation. It is a sign, when you want to leave someone you usually wave your hand. It was used at the end of the conversation. It was used in afriendly way and keeps smiling. Example : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Good bye, see you next time</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kinds of Errors made by the traders in Tanah Lot in scope of Pidginized English compared to the Standard English.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Absence of Subject Pronoun</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Cheap price. Nice style</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It is cheap and the style is nice</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the examples above, there are no subject pronouns in the beginning of the sentence. Most of the sentences above belong to declarative sentence. Therefore, the word order should be: S + V/to be O.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example the trader simplified omitted the subject pronoun ‘it’, which referred to the ‘statue’. Besides, this sentences also lack of to be “’is” after the subject.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>5.2.2 . The Absence of “to be” after Subject</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark38"></a>This the coca cola, five thousand for the small</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">This is a coca-cola Sir, and the price is five thousand rupiahs for small one</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Besides the absence of subject pronoun, the other evidence of simplifications in syntactical form was the absence of “to be”. As in Example the trader seemed to simplify the sentence. This sentence was lack of to be “is” after the subject “this”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark39"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark40"></a>5.2.3 a. The Absence of Auxiliary in the Interrogative Forms</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>You want Sir? You have cigarette?</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Do you want to have this cigarette box?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the examples above, there were no auxiliary, modal or verb before the subject in the interrogative forms. The trader used the simple expression in offering something or communicating to the tourist, supported by gesture, and by rising their intonations.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in the example the trader simplified the auxiliary ‘do’ in interrogative form, before the subject ‘you’ .This sentence should use auxiliary ‘do’ because there were verb ‘want’. The traders also omitted auxiliary “do” as the operator in the beginning of the interrogative sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>5.2.3 b. The omission of auxiliary verb “do” and “will” in Negative Clauses Hello Mom, Just look not buy never mind</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : Hello Mom, You can have a look. It does not matter if you do not buy.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the examples above, the traders tended to use ‘not’ as the negator. They tended to omit auxiliary verb ‘do’ in negative clauses as in example.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark45"></a>5.2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Selection of The “me” rather than “I” as the Pronoun</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark46"></a>Sorry, but me not lie to you</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sorry, but I am not lying</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the sentence, the traders used “me” as the pronoun. In Standard English, pronoun “me” usually has the function as object in the sentence. However, in this sentence, the traders used “me” as the subject of the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark47"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark48"></a>5.2.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deletion</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark49"></a>Statue? About like this, pardon?</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pardon me sir, Do you mean a statue like this?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example, the trader deleted some elements of the sentence. As the interrogative sentence, this sentence was lack of auxiliary “do” as the question marked. There was also the simplification of subject “you”, verb “mean” and object “me”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark50"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark51"></a>5.2.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Permutation of Word Order</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark52"></a>Just look not buy never mind, no problem</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It is no problem if you only are having a look and you do not have to take it.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example, there was the permutation of word order “It is no problem if you are only having a look and do not take it” into “just look not buy never mind, no problem”. It was caused by the interference of Bahasa Indonesia. The traders tended to translate the words from Bahasa Indonesia into English without change into-ing form and the word order.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark53"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark54"></a>5.2.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Absence of Definite Article</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark55"></a>Cheap price. Nice style</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : The </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">price is cheap and the style is nice</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example, the trader simplified the article “the” before the noun “price”. In this sentence, definite article had shown the usage of “the” before noun, which could represent the particular thing “price”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark56"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark57"></a>5.2.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Absence of “to be” in Interrogative Forms</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark58"></a>Hello Sir, First time in Bali?</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Sir, is this your first time in Bali</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example, there was the simplification of to be “is” in interrogative form, before the subject “this” and the object “your first time” which was also omitted by the trader.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark59"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark60"></a>5.2.9 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Absence of Suffix “-s” as Plural Marker</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark61"></a>What sandals you want Madam? Many style and color here.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">What kind of sandals are you looking for, Madam?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">There are many style dan colors available</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As in example, there was the simplification of suffix “-s” as the plural markers of countable noun “style and color” and preceded by determiner “many”, therefore it should be pluralized by adding suffix “-s”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark62"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark63"></a>5.2.10 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Repetition</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark64"></a>Good bye sir, tomorrow tomorrow come back ya</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Please come again next time</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In example, the trader used the repeated word “tomorrow”. In this case, the trader wanted to make the meaning of the sentence stronger by repeating the same word. This was the interference of Bahasa Indonesia or Balinese language, because in both languages there was the form of repetition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark65"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark66"></a>5.2.11 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Addition of Unnecessary Suffix “-s” in the Noun</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark67"></a>Wanna looking this traditional sandals?</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Would you have a look of this traditional sandal?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In example, the use of suffix “-s” was unnecessary because it was preceded by the subject “this” that means singular.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark68"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark69"></a>5.2.12 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Incorrect Usage of Personal Pronoun</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark70"></a>Common sir, it nice to you daughter</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The standard form : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It is nice for your daughter</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the example above, the trader used the pronoun “you” followed by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">to be </span><span class="font1">“are” to show the possessive pronoun. If compared to the Standard English, the pronoun “you” followed by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">to be</span><span class="font1"> “are” in that sentence should be changed into “your”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark71"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark72"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis of language function of pidginized English used by the trader in Tanah Lot , there are some point to be presented as the conclusion in this study. There are 8 types of language function found in conversation between traders and foreigner customers in Tanah Lot, there are The Function of Greeting, Inviting, Offering Something, Describing Thing, Requesting, Apologizing, Thanking, and Saying Good Bye (Leave-Taking)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Furthermore, there are some simplification of grammar found, they are the absence of subject pronoun, the absence of “to be” after subject, the absence of auxiliary in the interrogatives forms, the absence of “to be” in interrogative forms, the selection of the “me” rather than “I” as the pronoun, deletion, the permutation of word order, the omission of verbal auxiliary “do” and “will”’ in negative clauses, and the absence of definite article, the absence of suffix “-s” as plural marker, the repetition, the addition of unnecessary suffix “-s” in the noun and the incorrect usage of personal pronoun.</span></p>
