<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Studi yang berjudul “English Borrowing Words in Indonesian Novel entitled Manusia Setengah Salmon” ini ditulis untuk menganalisis jenis kata-kata serapan dari bahasa Inggris yang diterapkan di dalam novel berbahasa Indonesia dan proses penyesuaiannya berdasarkan referensi dari Ejaan Yang Disempurnakan (EYD).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sumber data diambil dari novel berjudul Manusia Setengah Salmon yang ditulis oleh Raditya Dika (2011). Studi ini menggunakan teori Haugen yang mengelompokkan jenis-jenis kata serapan dan juga referensi dari Pedoman Umum Ejaan Bahasa Indonesia Yang Disempurnakan yang menjabarkan proses penyesuaian kata-kata serapan dari bahasa Inggris ke dalam bahasa Indonesia.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil studi ini menunjukkan bahwa terdapat 84 tipe “loanword” dan 2 tipe “loanshifts” yang ditemukan. Secara umum, kata-kata serapan dari bahasa Inggris yang diterapkan di dalam novel berbahasa Indonesia telah sesuai dengan Ejaan Yang Disempurnakan (EYD).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Kata-kata kunci: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kata-kata serapan</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">loanword</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">loanshift</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">To be able to get along with the development of technology, information, science, and the rapid communication in the globalization era, Indonesian language absorbs and borrows the aspect of forms and vocabulary from any foreign languages. Borrowing is the usual term for the process by which a language takes new linguistic material from another language. According to Haugen (1950: 212), borrowing is the process of importing linguistic items from one linguistic system into another that occurs any time when two cultures are in contact over a period of time.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The borrowing vocabulary from foreign language in Indonesian language is dominantly from English which has a high influence on other languages around</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">the world. The more intensive the relationship between science and technology in Indonesian people with English user society can also be the reason why it occurs in Indonesian. The use of English borrowing words can be shown by the words or phrases which morphologically and phonologically, therefore, they can enter the system of Indonesian language. Let it be illustrated by some examples. The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“komik”</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian language comes from the word “comic” in English. The word “comic” has been morphologically and phonologically adapted into Indonesian language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The use of English borrowing words has spread in almost all aspects of social life in Indonesia, especially in literature. One of the favourite literary works is novel, which is one of the most influential writings in the usage of English borrowing words in Indonesian language. The novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manusia Setengah Salmon</span><span class="font2"> which is written by an Indonesian writer; Raditya Dika (2011) is the novel which is most widely read these days. In that novel, there were found many foreign terms which do not originally come from Indonesian language and they belong to English borrowing words.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>4.2. The Adaptation of English borrowing words based on the Standard</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">EYD</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">English borrowing words have to follow the standard Indonesian spelling system called </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2"> (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Ejaan yang Disempurnakan</span><span class="font2">). The adaptations of English borrowing words will be discussed in the 4 following examples.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The spelling of borrowing word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“kontraktor”</span><span class="font2"> is in accordance with the Standard Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2">. It is adapted from the English word “contractor”. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">k</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ontra</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">k</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">t</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">or</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> ÷ </span><span class="font2">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">c</span><span class="font2">ontra</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">c</span><span class="font2">t</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">or</span><span class="font2">”, where:</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font2">(c), before vowels (a) or (o) or (u) or the consonant, is changed into (k) </span><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font2">suffix (-or) does not change</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“kriminalitas”</span><span class="font2"> is an English loanword in Indonesian language is borrowed from the word “criminality”. The spelling of the word “criminality” has changed and followed the Standard Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">k</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">riminal</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">itas </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> ÷ </span><span class="font2">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">c</span><span class="font2">riminal</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">ity</span><span class="font2">”, where:</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font2">(c), before vowels (a) or (o) or (u) or the consonant, is changed into (k) </span><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font2">suffix (-ity) is changed into (-itas)</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The word “frictional” has been adapted into Indonesian language and become </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“friksional”</span><span class="font2">. The change is followed the Indonesian rules both in phonology and morphology. Morphologically, spelling of the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“friksional”</span><span class="font2"> has been appropriate with the Standard Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">f</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ri</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ks</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ion</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">al</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> ÷ </span><span class="font2">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">f</span><span class="font2">ri</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">ct</span><span class="font2">ion</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">al</span><span class="font2">”, where:</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(f) does not change</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c), before vowels (a) or (o) or (u) or the consonant, is changed into (k)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(t), when it exists before vowels (i) and its pronunciation is /</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">s</span><span class="font2">/, is changed into (s)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;suffix (-al) does not change</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The spelling of borrowing word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“identikal”</span><span class="font2"> is in accordance with the Standard Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2">. It is adapted from the English word “identical”. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">i</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">denti</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kal</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> ÷ </span><span class="font2">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">i</span><span class="font2">denti</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">cal</span><span class="font2">”, where:</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i), when it exists at the beginning of a word before a vowel, it does not change</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c), before vowels (a) or (o) or (u) or the consonant is changed into (k) </span><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font2">suffix (-al) does not change</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the analysis in the previous section, there were 84 loanwords found where 84 of them belong to the pure loanword only and none belong to the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">loanblend. The other type of borrowing word, loanshift, was also found in the novel. There were 2 loanshifts found 2 of which belong to the extension only and none belongs to the creation. It was also found that generally those English borrowing words are adapted into Indonesian language according the Standard Indonesian Spelling System or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">EYD</span><span class="font2">. They are changed according the Indonesian rules that exist in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Pedoman Umum Ejaan Bahasa Indonesia yang Disempurnakan</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
