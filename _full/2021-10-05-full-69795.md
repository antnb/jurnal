ABSTRACT The purpose of this study is to examine the legal arrangements related to the production of processed food carried out by MSMEs based on the provisions of the regulations. This study also aims to provide an understanding of the legal protection provided to consumers who experience losses due to processed food whose packaging is repackaged without being re-registered by MSMEs to ensure its safety. The type of research in this article is normative legal research which examines the problem of norms in the form of vague norms and is based on a statutory regulatory approach. The research results from this study explain that every processed food production carried out by MSMEs must obtain a distribution permit with due regard to food safety and registration provisions in accordance with the provisions of PP No. 86 of 2019 and PBPOM No. 27 of 2017.
