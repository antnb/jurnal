<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal iniberjudul “Derivational Suffixes found in Gilbert’s Eat Pray Love”.Tujuan penelitian ini bertujuan untuk menganalisa jenis-jenis proses morfologi yang terjadi pada kelas-kelas kata yang dapat berubah menjadi kelas kata lain yang ditemukan dalam novel karya Elizabeth Gilbert yang berjudul Eat Pray Love. Teori yang digunakan dalam jurnal ini adalah teori morfologi yang dikemukakan oleh Bauer (1984) dan Katamba (1993).Teori pendukung lainnya juga diambil dari buku karya Frank(1072) dan Quirk(1973). Dalam menganalisis jurnal, metode yang digunakan adalah metode kualitatif. Pembahasan data di awali dengan membaca, memberi tanda dan mencatat kata benda,kata sifat,kata kerja dan kata keterangan yang ditemukan.Hasil dari pembahasan menunjukan bahwa ada dua jenis proses morfologi yaitu proses derivasi dan infleksi. Kelas-kelas kata yang dapat di modifikasi oleh kedua jenis proses morfologi tersebut adalah kata kerja, sifat, benda dan kata keterangan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kunci: morfologi, derivasi, infleksi.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Words as the main elements of a sentence can be divided into some classes such as : noun, verb, adverb, adjective, pronoun, preposition, conjunction and interjection. In some cases, a word in English may change its form, for example; the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">drive</span><span class="font2"> (verb) when it is attached by suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2">, it becomes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">driver</span><span class="font2"> (noun). But when the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">boy</span><span class="font2"> (noun) is attached by suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–hood</span><span class="font2">, it becomes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">boyhood</span><span class="font2"> (noun). In this case, suffixes can change the class of words and they maintain the class of words after they are attached into a word. The aims of this writing are identifying the forms of derivational suffixes in English used in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Eat Pray Love</span><span class="font2"> and analyzing the functions of derivational suffixes in English used in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Eat Pray Love.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

In conjunctions with the theory which is applied in this thesis, these four types of suffixes are the main topic of this section.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are some points that can be taken as conclusion from the analysis above. Suffixes that are used in the data source are mainly the suffixes that belong to class changing suffixes. There are few examples of suffixes that belong to class maintaining suffixes used in the data source.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffixes that belong to class changing suffixes can be categorized into four types: suffixes forming noun (-</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ation, -al, -er/-or, -men, -cy, -ness, -th, -ity, -ee,</span><span class="font1"> -</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ion, -y, -age,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ance/-ence</span><span class="font1">). Suffixes forming verb ( -</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ify, -ize</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-fy</span><span class="font1"> ), suffixes forming adjectives (-</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">al, -able, -less, -ful, -ous, -y</span><span class="font1">) , suffixes forming adverbs (</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ily, -ly, -y</span><span class="font1">). The base form to which these suffixes are attached are verb, nouns, and adjective.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Looking at the frequency of use, these suffixes can be put into the following order.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffix forming nouns ( </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ation, -ness, -er/-or, -ity, -ment, -ion, -ant, -cy, -ance/-ence, -ary, -y, -ure, -al, -dom, -ee,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-age.</span><span class="font1">)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffix forming adjectives ( </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ful, -less, -al, -able, -ous, -y, -ic, -ial</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ish</span><span class="font1"> )</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffix forming verbs ( </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ize, -ify</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-fy</span><span class="font1"> )</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffix forming adverbs ( </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ly, -ily,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-y</span><span class="font1"> )</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Suffixes that belong to class maintaining suffixes can be categorized into two types : suffixes forming nouns from nouns ( </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-hood, -ship, -ese, -er, -ist, -ism, -an, -y,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-cy</span><span class="font1"> ) and suffixes forming adjective from adjective ( -</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">al</span><span class="font1">).</span></p>
