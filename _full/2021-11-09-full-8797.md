<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Judul artikel ini adalah “Derivational Affixes With Special Reference to Classic Bike Magazine and Street Machine Magazine”. Materi yang dipaparkan dalam jurnal ini mengenai derivational affixes yang ditemukan dalam dua majalah automotif yaitu Classic Bike dan Street Machine. Untuk mendukung penyusunan jurnal ini, data yang diperoleh dari artikel-artikel dalam kedua majalah tersebut dianalisa sesuai dengan bentuk-bentuk derivational affixes.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Teori yang diterapkan dalam penyusunan jurnal ini terdiri atas teori Francis Katamba dengan bukunya berjudul ‘Morphology’ tahun 1993dan teori kedua berasal dari Laurie Bauer dengan bukunya berjudul ‘English Word-Formation’ yang diterbitkan tahun 1983.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Mengacu kepada data-data yang terkumpul dari artikel-artikel tersebut, banyak ditemukannya derivational suffixes kemudian diikuti derivational prefixes. Derivational prefixes yang ditemukan lebih didominasi oleh prefix ‘un-’, prefix ‘pre-’. Dan derivational suffixes yang mendominasi adalah suffix ‘-ful’, suffix ‘-ment’, dan suffix ‘-ence or –ance’.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Artikel majalah, Awalan derivative, Akhiran derivative.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Morphology as a branch of linguistics is concentrated on word formation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Word formation is affected by processing of derivational affixes. Derivational affixes are divided into two categories based on the process of forming lexeme, they are class maintaining affixes and class changing affixes. Both of them</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">perform different functions; the class changing is used to change the word class of the base lexeme, and the class maintaining is used to preserve the word class of based lexeme.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The problems discussed in the present study are::</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of derivational affixes found in the </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Classic Bike Magazine</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Street Machine magazine</span><span class="font0">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How is the process of forming new words formed by derivational affixes found in the </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Classic Bike magazine</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Street Machine magazine?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">In relation to the problems, there are two aims of this writing.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Identifying the meanings of derivational affixes found in the </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Classic Bike Magazine</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Street Machine Magazine.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Describing the process of forming new words formed by derivational affixes found in the </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Classic Bike Magazine</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Street Machine Magazine.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The research method consists of data source which supports the research, the process of collecting and analysing data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The data source was chosen from two automotive magazines. The first magazine was </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Classic Bike Magazine</span><span class="font0"> by Media House which was printed in Peterborough and published in April 2012. The second magazine was </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Street</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Machine magazine</span><span class="font0"> by Bauer Media which was printed in Sydney, Australia and it was published n June 2013.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The data were collected through the documentation. The documentation was done by reading the articles and taking notes.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analysing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The data of this paper were descriptively presented. The identification data was based on the prefixes and suffixes. There were two theories used as a base to analyse the data coming from Francis Katamba (1993) in his book entitled ‘</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Morphology’</span><span class="font0"> and Laurie Bauer (1983) in his book entitled ‘</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">English WordFormation’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Derivational Affixes</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The analysis of derivational affixes is classified into derivational prefixes and derivational suffixes.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Derivational Prefixes</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Derivational prefixes are dominated by prefixes </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">‘un-’</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">‘pre-’.</span><span class="font0"> Most of the articles consist of these two prefixes.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

What he saw on his travels back in1952 inspired Guevara to become involved in the Cuban revolution and he became a 20<sup>th</sup> century icon.</span><span class="font0"> (Classic Bike; Chasing Che, 2012:59) The lexeme </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">‘pre-war’</span><span class="font0"> derived from the word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">‘war’</span><span class="font0">.
