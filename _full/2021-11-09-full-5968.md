<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>THE GRAMMATICAL COHESION IN THE TEXTS OF </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">THE EMPEROR AND THE NIGHTINGALE</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> AND </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ALADDIN</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">I Gusti Ayu Sri Krisnawati</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Extension Program Faculty of Letter Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini difokuskan pada analisis jenis kohesi gramatikal yang ditemukan dalam cerita anak. Data dalam penelitian ini diambil dari dua cerita anak-anak Inggris berjudul ”Kaisar dan Nightingale” dan ”Aladdin”. Data diperoleh melalui metode dokumentasi. Kemudian dianalis secara kualitatif dengan penyajian data dalam bentuk contoh dari setiap jenis kohesi gramatikal dalam teks dengan pembahasan masing-masing sampel. Teori yang digunakan adalah eori kohesi dalam buku ”Cohesion in English” karangan Halliday dan Hasan (1976), dan didukung dengan menggunakan teori yang diusulkan oleh Nunan (1993) dalam bukunya yang berjudul ”Introducing Discourse Analysis”. Analisis ini membahas beberapa jenis kohesi gramatikal yang ditemukan dalam cerita. Jenis pertama kohesi gramatikal yang dibahas dalam cerita adalah referensi yang dibagi menjadi referensi pribadi, referensi demonstratif, dan referensi komparatif. Tipe kedua kohesi gramatikal yang digunakan dalam cerita adalah substitusi yaitu substitusi nominal dan substitusi verbal. Jenis kohesi gramatikal berikutnya yang ditemukan dalam cerita adalah ellipsis yang termasuk elipsis nominal. Jenis kohesi gramatikal terakhir adalah konjungsi; yang ditemukan dalam cerita adalah konjungsi aditif, konjungsi adversatif, konjungsi kausal dan konjungsi temporal.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: teks, kohesi gramatikal, dan cerita anak</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">People need language to make a communication to each other. They can use written language or spoken language to communicate, and to make other people understand what they want to say. In linguistics, the word text is used to refer to any passage. A text can be spoken or written. It can be in the form of speech or writing to express what we think of. According to Halliday (1985: 10), text is any instance of living language that is playing some part in a context of situation. It may be spoken or</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">written, or indeed in any other medium of expression that we like to think of. As Halliday mentioned, there are three types of written text based on their functions, they are for action, information and entertainment. One of the examples of entertainment is in the form of story such as a children story.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Children stories are interesting to read. It is also interesting enough to analyze especially the grammatical cohesion. It is useful to indicate the structure and how the content of the story impacts the reader’s understanding and interpretation through the texts that is used. Therefore, the writer was interested to propose this thesis through the text of children story. In this opportunity, the texts that are analyzed were taken from two English children story books entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The problem of this study is::</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">What kinds of grammatical cohesion are found in the stories </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin</span><span class="font1"> ?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aim of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The aim of this study is:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">To find out kinds of grammatical cohesion used in the stories </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin</span><span class="font1"> .</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data in this study were taken from two English children stories entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin.</span><span class="font1"> They were collected through documentation method, in which data were found by reading the books and classifying the grammatical</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">cohesion in those two stories. The data were analyzed qualitatively by presenting the data in the form of examples of each type of grammatical cohesion in texts with discussion of each sample. Then the collected data were analyzed based on the theory of cohesion in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Cohesion in English</span><span class="font1"> proposed by Halliday and Hasan (1976), and supported by using the theory proposed by Nunan (1993) in his book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Introducing Discourse Analysis</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are four types of grammatical cohesion found in the stories </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin”</span><span class="font1">; those are reference, substitution, ellipsis and conjunction. Reference can be defined as a specific nature of information that is signaled for retrieval. It can be divided into personal reference, and demonstrative reference, comparative reference (Halliday and Hasan, 1976:6). Personal reference can be defined as reference by means of function in the speech situation, through the category of person. Some personal reference found in these stories are : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I, you, we, they, he, she, it.</span><span class="font1"> This is one example of personal reference </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font1"> functioning as subject found in the stories: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">”The nightingale sings for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">me</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> every evening,” the little kitchen maid said </span><span class="font1">Demonstrative reference is a reference that is identified by locating it on scale of proximity and expressed through adverbs and determiners. Demonstrative reference, in these stories are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">this, that, the, then and now</span><span class="font1">. This is one example of demonstrative reference </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1"> functioning as modifier: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">She led the courtiers into the forest and pointed to any tiny grey bird. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">The</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> bird was not pretty , but her singing was the most beautiful they had ever heard.”</span><span class="font1"> And the last kind of reference is comparative reference, namely: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">same, different, more, prettier</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">stronger.</span><span class="font1"> A comparative reference is a reference which expresses identity and similarity. This is the example of comparative reference expressing </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">quantity: ”Aladdin told Jafar that a genie was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">stronger</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> than a sultan.</span><span class="font1">”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The second type of grammatical cohesion used in the two stories are: nominal substitution and verbal substitution. According to Halliday and Hasan (1976:89) nominal substitution is showed by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ones</span><span class="font1"> that function as Head of a nominal</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">group, and can substitute only within itself. The nominal substitution found in these stories is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1">. The example of nominal substitution is: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">”The real nightingale sang as the mood took her, while the mechanical </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">one</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> could only sing the notes it had been made to sing</span><span class="font1"> .”The substitution form in verbal substitution is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font1">, with the usual morphological scatter </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do, does, did, doing, done.</span><span class="font1"> The function of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font1"> as head in a verbal group and it is usually occupied by the lexical verb. There are some verbal substitutions found in these stories, they are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">did</span><span class="font1">. One of the examples is: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">If I </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">do</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> marry, I want it to be for love”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Ellipsis is the form of substitution in which the item is replaced by nothing. Halliday and Hasan (1976:142) states the term as an omission of an item of frequently said substitution by zero The next type of grammatical cohesion found in the stories is nominal ellipsis which means ellipsis within nominal group. The nominal ellipsis found in these stories is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the strongest</span><span class="font1">. From the analysis of the two stories, there is only one nominal ellipsis found in these stories and function as epithet: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jafar wanted to be </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">the strongest</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">!”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">According to Halliday and Hasan (1976:226), there are four types of conjunction, they are: additive conjunction, adversative conjunction, causal conjunction and temporal conjunction. Addititve conjunction occurs when two sentences structurally grouped together . The only one additive conjunction found in these stories is: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1">. The example is: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin often looked at the palace </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">and</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> dreamed of a different life”. </span><span class="font1">Aversative conjunction is something contrary to expectation, the kind of additive conjunction found in these stories is: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">but</span><span class="font1">. The example of adversative conjunction is: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The bird was not pretty , </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">but</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> her singing was the most beautiful they had ever heard</span><span class="font1"> .” Causal conjunction is conjunction that tends to be specific such as result, reason and purpose. There are some kinds of causal conjunction found in these stories, they are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">because</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1">. One example of causal conjunction found in these stories is: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jafar was mad </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">because</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> Aladdin had the magic lamp.”</span><span class="font1"> And the last kind of conjunction is temporal conjunction which is conjunctive form that presents an additional component in the meaning as well as succession in time of. The temporal conjunction found in</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">these stories are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">finally</span><span class="font1">. The example of temporal conjunction found in these stories is: ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">They ran down the street</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">Finally</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, Aladdin and Jasmine were safe.”</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark13"></a>6.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis of grammatical cohesion in the stories </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“The Emperor and the Nightingale</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aladdin”</span><span class="font1">, four types of grammatical cohesion were found; those are reference, substitution, ellipsis and conjunction.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Grammatical cohesion which is found in the stories are reference, substitution, ellipsis and conjunction. Reference can be divided into personal reference, demonstrative reference and comparative reference. Personal references which are listed are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I, You, We, They, He, She, It, Me, Him, Them, Your, Our, His, Her,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Its. </span><span class="font1">Demonstrative references are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">this, that</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">now</span><span class="font1">. And comparative references which are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">same</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">different</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">more</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">prettier</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">stronger</span><span class="font1">. The second group is substitution consist of: nominal and verbal substitution. The nominal substitution found is: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1">. The verbal substitutions which are listed are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">did</span><span class="font1">. The ellipsis is presented by nominal ellipsis which is demonstrated by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the strongest</span><span class="font1">. The conjunctions which are found in the stories are: additive, adversative, causal and temporal conjunction. The additive conjunction found is: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1">. The next kind of conjunction is adversative conjunction namely: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">but</span><span class="font1">. The other kind of conjunction is causal conjunction, they are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">because</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1">. The last kind of conjunction is temporal conjunction namely: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">finally.</span></p>
