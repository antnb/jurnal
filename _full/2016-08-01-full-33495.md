<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini berjudul“The Derivational Adjectives in the “New York Times”. Judul penelitian ini dipilih karena beberapa kata diproduksi dari proses morfologi yaitu memberikan akhiran pada dasar kata. Menguasai aturan ini dapat memberikan pemahaman bagaimana memproduksi kata baru dan membedakan kelas kata. Skripsi ini membahas tipe tipe dan posisi dari kata sifat derivatif yang ditemukan di artikel. Data diambil dari beberapa artikel tahun 2015 di koran online New York Times. Metode pengumpulan data adalah metode dokumentasi yaitu mengumpulkan kata-kata sifat derivatif dan menabelkan berdasarkan akhiran pembentuk kata sifat. Sedangkan metode yang digunakan dalam menganalisa data adalah metode deskripsi kualitatif. Teori utama yang digunakan adalah teori dari Quirk, et al (1985) pada bukunya berjudul “A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language”. Hasil dari analysis menunjukkan bahwa akhiran –al,-able, -ous, -ive, -ent, -ous, -ly, -y, -less, -ful, -ic yang dilampirkan pada akhiran dasar kata dapat mengubah kelas kata dari kata benda dan kata kerja menjadi kata sifat. Analisa sintaksis menemukan bahwa kata sifat derivatif mempunyai posisi sebagai predikatif yang memodifikasi subyek dan posisi sebagai atributif.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Akhiran, Derivasi, Kata Sifat</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">A word may be formed by one or more morphemes. Morpheme is the smallest unit of meaning that cannot be divided into smaller unit. Morphology is the study of word structure (Katamba, 1993: 19). Derivation is one of the word formation processes which can change the word class, meanwhile inflectional morpheme cannot change it. Derivational process means the formation of word of certain word classes into another word class by adding suffixes. This thesis is concerned with the analysis of the adjectives derived from other word classes and their syntactical functions. This study used the theory proposed by Quirk, et al (1985) to analyze the derivational process of adjectives in English words and the syntactical functions of adjectives. Quirk, et al (1985:418) stated that the adjective has three positions; they are predicative, attributive and post positive.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Furthermore, many people are still confused when differentiating word class. This study is interesting and may benefit the learners, because it can enrich and widen the vocabulary of the learners by deriving words and also the learners may have good knowledge as to how differentiate the word class. Thus this study is concerned with the analysis of the derivational adjectives in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">New York Times</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The main data were taken from e-newspaper in </span><a href="http://www.nytimes.com"><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">www.nytimes.com</span></a><span class="font2">. There are 1 article taken on Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015, 9 articles taken on Oct 5<sup>th</sup> 2015, 4 articles on Oct 6<sup>th</sup> 2015, and 1 article taken on Oct 9<sup>th</sup> 2015. The articles were selected randomly. Documentation method and note taking technique were the methods which were applied to collect the data by downloading 15 articles first, then collecting derivational adjectives and the last was grouping and tabling the adjectives based on suffixes which form the adjectives. The method applied to analyze the data was the descriptive qualitative method. First, the data were classified based on the suffixes forming them and then, analyzing the process of suffixes forming the adjectives. Next, the clauses or phrases which contain derivational adjectives were found. The last step was analyzing the syntactical positions of Adjectives.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1. Derivational Process</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix –able</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Valuable</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">(Reif Larsen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sarajevo’s Enduring Optimism</span><span class="font1">. Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015: Line 133)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “valuable” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">value</span><span class="font2"> (noun). The resulting word is derived from the noun to which the suffix –able is attached which makes the class word change into adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – ive</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Administrative</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">(</span><span class="font1">Reif Larsen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sarajevo’s Enduring Optimism</span><span class="font1">. Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015: Line 171)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word “administrative” is derived from the verb ‘administrate’. The suffix –ive is attached a</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dministrate</span><span class="font2">, causing the word to change into the adjective “administrative”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – ent</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Persistent</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">(Gretchen, R. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Ask Well: When Sitting can be Good for You</span><span class="font1">. Oct 5<sup>th</sup> 2015: Line 1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “persistent” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">persist</span><span class="font2"> and the class word is verb. Persistent means being determined to do something despite difficulties (A.S.Hornby, 2010: 1092).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – al</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- National</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">(Hiroko Tabuchi, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">American Apparel Files For Bankruptcy</span><span class="font1">, Oct 5<sup>th</sup> 2015: Line 22)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “national” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">nation</span><span class="font2"> which has the word class noun. After the suffix –al is attaced to the base, then the resulting word class becomes an adjective, “national”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – ous</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Infectious</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">(Jane Perlez. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Answering an Appeal by Mad Led Tu Youyou, a Chinese Scientist, to a Nobel Prize</span><span class="font1">. Oct 6<sup>th</sup> 2015: Line 23)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “infectious” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">infect</span><span class="font2"> and the word class is verb. The suffix – ous attached to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">infect</span><span class="font2"> makes the word class change into an adjective, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">infectious</span><span class="font2">. Infect means to make a disease or an illness spread to a person, an animal or a plant (A.S.Hornby, 2010: 768).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>f. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – less</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Worthless</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(Oregon Killer’s Mother Wrote of Troubled Son and Gun Rights</span><span class="font1">. Oct 5<sup>th</sup> 2015: Line 58)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “worthless” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">worth</span><span class="font2"> (noun). The derivational adjective derived from a noun to which the suffix –less is attached. The word class changes into an adjective called denominal adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>g. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – y</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Lengthy</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">(</span><span class="font1">Reif Larsen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sarajevo’s Enduring Optimism</span><span class="font1">. Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015: Line 109)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">As an adjective, the base of the word “lengthy” is the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">length</span><span class="font2">. After the suffix – y is attached to the base “length”, the word class changes into an adjective and the process is called denominal adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>h. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – ful</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Graceful</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">(</span><span class="font1">Reif Larsen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sarajevo’s Enduring Optimism</span><span class="font1">. Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015: Line 186)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “graceful” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grace</span><span class="font2"> which belongs to the word class noun. The suffix –ful attached to the base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grace</span><span class="font2"> changes the grammatical category of the resulting word into an adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>i. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix – ic</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Horrific</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">(</span><span class="font1">Reif Larsen. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sarajevo’s Enduring Optimism</span><span class="font1">. Oct 2<sup>nd</sup> 2015: Line 33)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “horrific” is the verb, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">horrify</span><span class="font2">. The change of the word class from verb into adjective is caused by the attachment of suffix –ic to the base “horrify”. The word class changes from a verb into an adjective called deverbal adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">- Jewish</span></p></li></ul>

<p><span class="font2">The base of the word “Jewish” is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Jew</span><span class="font2">, a noun. The adjective is formed by attaching the suffix –ish to the base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Jew</span><span class="font2"> which makes the word class change from a noun into an adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The analysis showed that the derivational suffixes include suffix –al,-able, -ous, -ive, -ent, -ous, -ly, -y, -less, -ful, -ic which are attached to the bases, making the word class change from noun and verb into adjective. The change of the word class from a noun into an adjective is called denominal adjective; meanwhile, the change from a verb into an adjective is called deverbal adjective. The discussion found that derivational adjectives have the position as predicative that modifies the subject and can take the position as attributive or noun modifier. Predicative adjective is the function as a subject complement which describes the subject. Meanwhile, attributive functions to modify the head of the noun phrase.</span></p>
