<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah mengenali jenis-jenis kata preposisi sederhana, menggambarkan fungsi dan menganalisis makna preposisi pada ungkapan preposisi dalam kalimat yang ditemukan di dalam artikel bisnis dari koran Jakarta Post edisi November 2014, Juni 2015, dan November 2015. Dalam penelitian ini ada teori yang digunakan untuk menjawab 3 pokok permasalahan. Adapun teori yang dimaksud, teori tentang ungkapan preposisi oleh Quirk dan kawan-kawan (1985). Penelitian ini menemukan 20 preposisi yang terbagi menjadi 2 katagori sesuai jenis-jenis preposisi sederhana dan 76 ungkapan preposisi yang terbagi menjadi 8 katagori, 3 fungsi ungkapan preposisi dan 5 makna preposisi yang ada di ungkapan preposisi dalam sebuah kalimat. Di dalam penelitian ini terbukti bahwa preposisi memiliki peran penting dalam kalimat untuk menghubungkan dengan kata-kata yang lain dalam kalimat. Preposisi tidak bisa berdiri sendiri, preposisi harus diikuti dengan pelengkap preposisi, bisa berupa nama tempat, waktu, kata ganti, atau kata benda.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: ungkapan preposisi, preposisi, fungsi, dan makna.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Prepositional phrase is one of the sentence elements, which functions to indicate certain relation between other words in one sentence. It used to make the sentence complete and understandable (by Quirk, et.al). The prepositional phrase consists of preposition followed by a prepositional complement. There are some theories used in this study related to prepositional phrases. They are theory of Quirk, et.al as main theory and the last Yule’s theory as support theory.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">According to Quirk, et.al (1985:673) a preposition emphasizes the relation between two entities, that one being represented by the prepositional complement. In this case time</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">and place are easier to be identified, however in term of labels; preposition in systematic meaning is difficult to be described. Besides theory of Quirk, et.al, Yule’s theory is also used to support in analyzing of this study.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Yule (1998:156) states that preposition can be followed by a noun phrase from prepositional phrase. So following noun phrase, verb, or adjective can become a mark of the relationship in terms of what can be a mark of preposition in English.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Quirk, et.al (1985:659) also state that there are several points of similarity between preposition and other word classes and constructions in English grammar, particularly conjunction and adverb, as well as participle and adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What syntactic functions of simple prepositional phrases are found in business articles of the Jakarta Post?</span></p></li>
<li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2.</span><span class="font3"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe the syntactic functions of the simple prepositional phrases found in business articles of the Jakarta Post.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">3.</span><span class="font3"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze about the meanings of simple prepositional phrases found in business articles of the Jakarta Post.</span></p></li></ul>

<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Methods</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are some steps needed to be followed when we do a research. There are data source where all the data used to sport all the analysis taken from, method and technique of collecting data and analyzing data are important in helping to solve difficulty in collecting and analyzing process. So, the research resulted becomes quicker, better and clearer.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data were taken from the articles in business topic of Jakarta Post newspaper. The Jakarta Post was chosen as the data source because it is an international newspaper in Indonesia. Besides, in Jakarta Post especially in business articles there are many simple prepositions.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Library research method was applied in collecting the data relevant to the topic. The data were taken from Business articles in The Jakarta Post. The data were collected through intents and comprehensive reading and then marking the occurrences of simple prepositions, and the last input into Microsoft word of computer program.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The collected data were descriptively and qualitatively analyzed based on the main theory of Quirk, et.al (1985) about prepositional phrase in their book entitled A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language. Based on that theory, the data were classified based on types of simple prepositions. After that the syntactic functions and meanings of simple prepositional phrases were analyzed qualitatively.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There were two types of simple prepositions, three types of syntactic functions and five types of simple prepositional meanings found in business articles of the Jakarta Post. </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Simple Prepositions</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">There are some types simple prepositions found in the business articles. They can be identified as follows:</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>Monosyllabic Prepositions</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">for &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;to &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;of &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;from &nbsp;on &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;as &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;in</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">at &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;since &nbsp;with &nbsp;but &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;by &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;or &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;up</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Polysyllabic Prepositions</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">after &nbsp;into &nbsp;&nbsp;under among</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">between &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;through</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Syntactic Functions of Simple Prepositional Phrases</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>Post modifier in a Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…,Teten Masduki, a member </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">of the President’s communications team</span><span class="font3">, said in a</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">recent statement. (A.2; p.13; line 11)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">of the President’s communications team</span><span class="font3">. It gives specific information and modifies to the preceding noun </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">member. </span><span class="font3">It tells that Teten Masduki is a President’s communications member</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">. </span><span class="font3">Besides, it is in place after noun </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">member</span><span class="font3">, so the simple prepositional phrase functions as post modifier in a noun phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">a member of the President’s communications team.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>Adverbial</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">In the future</span><span class="font3">, the government aims to further bring down the micro lending rate to</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">9 percent, ... (A.1; p.13; line 84)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in the future</span><span class="font3">. It functions as adverbial, especially adjunct, which expresses time. The adjunct is relatively integrated within the structure of the sentence. The meaning of the sentence is still remaining if the position of the prepositional phrase is changed. It can be placed at the middle after the subject or the end of the sentence, without changing the meaning of the sentence, as the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The government, </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in the future</span><span class="font3">, aims to further bring down the micro lending rate to 9 percent,…</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The government aims to further bring down the micro lending rate to 9 percent </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in the future</span><span class="font3">,..</span></p></li></ul>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>Complementation</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The government is close </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">to finalizing a regulation</span><span class="font3">. (A.2; p.13; line 1)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">to finalizing a regulation. </span><span class="font3">It functions as complementation of an adjective </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">close</span><span class="font3">. The simple prepositional phrase complements the preceding adjective and completes its specification of meaning relationship.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simple Prepositional Meanings of Simple Prepositional Phrases</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>Simple Prepositions Denoting Spatial Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Indonesian currency has dropped 10 percent this year in the second-worst</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">performance </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in Asia</span><span class="font3">. (A.7; p.13; line 57)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The simple prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in Asia</span><span class="font3">. It means Asia is the place where the Indonesian currency has dropped. The preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">in </span><span class="font3">belongs to dimension type 2 denoting area and including positive position.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Simple Prepositions Denoting Time</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By keeping all rates on hold, BI has maintained them at the same level </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">since</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">February</span><span class="font3">. (A.3; p.13; line 39)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The simple prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">since February. </span><span class="font3">It expresses the stretch time when BI maintained rates. Preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">since </span><span class="font3">is a prepositions denotes time.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>Denoting Cause or Purpose Spectrum</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1 .“The new proposal will be good </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">for investment </span><span class="font3">and capital flow into Indonesia, as well as our tourism industry,” he said. (A.2; p.13; line 37)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The simple prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">for investment</span><span class="font3">. It means Indonesia is expected become a place of investment. The preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">for </span><span class="font3">denotes purpose.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>Denoting Means or Agentive Spectrum</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Air law Community chairman Andre Rahadian said in a discussion on Thursday</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">that several regulations imposed </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">by the government </span><span class="font3">aimed to improve flight safety and security had burdened. (A.13; p.14; line 8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">by the government</span><span class="font3">. It means the government imposes several regulations to improve flight safety. The preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">by </span><span class="font3">denotes agentive. The sentence is passive voice. So, the agentive is expressed </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">by-phrase: by someone.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>Other Simple Prepositional Meanings</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When contacted, Hariyadi B. Sukamdani, chairman </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">of the Indonesian Employers</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Association </span><span class="font3">(Apindo)…. (A.3; p.13; line 84)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The prepositional phrase is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">of the Indonesian Employers Association. </span><span class="font3">It means Hariyadi B. Sukamdani leads Indonesian Employers Association (Apindo) and he is part of Indonesian Employers Association. Preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">of </span><span class="font3">denotes partition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are many simple prepositional phrases found in business articles of The</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Jakarta Post. These give a fact that preposition has an important role and function in</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">English especially written English Language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The simple prepositions can be divided into two major parts based on the type. They are monosyllabic and polysyllabic prepositions. Monosyllabic prepositions are found mostly than polysyllabic prepositions.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the syntactic functions, simple prepositional phrases functioning as adverbial are commonly occurred, especially adjunct.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In addition, the simple prepositional meanings can be divided into five. They are prepositions denoting spatial relation, denoting time, denoting the cause or purpose spectrum, the means or agentive spectrum and other prepositional meanings. In this case,</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">preposition denoting time is most commonly occurred in simple prepositional phrases in sentences.</span></p>
