<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Penelitian ini membahas tentang penerjemahan istilah-istilah agama budaya dalam bahasa Sanskerta (bahasa sumber) kedalam bahasa Inggris (bahasa sasaran 1) dan Bahasa Indonesia (bahasa sasaran 2). Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk menganalisis bagaimana istilah agama budaya dalam Bhagavad Gita tersebut diterjemahkan ke dalam bahasa Inggris dan bahasa Indonesia.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Penelitian ini menggunakan pendekatan kualitatif. Sumber data dalam penelitian ini adalah </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> dalam bahasa Sanskerta dan terjemahannya ke dalam bahasa Inggris “Bhagavad Gita As It Is” dan dalam bahasa Indonesia “Bhagavad Gita Menurut Aslinya”. Teori yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini adalah teori dari Newmark (1988) tentang kategori istilah budaya dan prosedur penerjemahan, teori dari Bell (1991) tentang pendekatan arti kata serta teori dari Nida (di Venuti, 2000) tentang kesepadanan dalam penerjemahan. Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa istilah-istilah agama budaya dalam </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> bisa diklasifikasikan berdasarkan kategori budaya dari Newmark yaitu istilah agama budaya yang berkaitan dengan ekologi, budaya material,sosial budaya, organisasi, tingtkah laku dan kebiasaan. Prosedur penerjemahan yang diterapkan oleh para penerjemahan dalam menerjemahkan istilah-istilah agama budaya dalam </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> adalah: penerjemahan secara lansung (literal), prosedur pemindahan ( transferrence procedure), kesepadanan budaya, naturalisasi and kesepadanan deskriptif. Hasil penelitian yang terakhir adalah bahwa ada informasi yang hilang dalam penerjemahan istilah-istilah agama budaya dalam Bhagavad Gita ke dalam Bahasa Inggris dan Bahasa Indonesia. Hilangnya informasi ini dikarenakan oleh prosedur penerjemahan dan orientasi penerjemah yang dipilih oleh penerjemah disamping adanya perbedaan latar-belakang budaya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">istilah agama budaya, prosedur penerjemahan</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">This research concerns with the translation of religious-cultural terms found in the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> in Sanskrit (SLT) into English (TLT1) and Indonesian (TLT2). The aims of this study is to investigates how religious-cultural terms in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> are translated into English and Indonesian.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This study applies qualitative research approach. The data source were taken from </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> in Sanskrit and the translation in English: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita As It Is”</span><span class="font4"> and in Indonesia: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita Menurut Aslinya</span><span class="font4">”. The theories used in this study are from Newmark (1998) about cultural word categories and translation procedures, theory from Bell (1991) about word meaning approach and from Nida (in Venuti, 2000) about equivalence in translation. The result of the analysis shows that the types of religious-cultural terms found in Bhagavad Gita can be catagorized based on Newmark cultural word categories, namely religious cultural terms related to ecology, material culture, social culture, organizations, gestures and habits. The translation procedures applied by the translators in dealing with the translation of religious-cultural terms from Sanskrit into English (TLT 1) and Indonesian (TLT 2) are literal translation, transferrence procedure, cultural equivalent, naturalization and descriptive equivalent, and lastly there are some losses of information occured in the translation of the religious cultural terms from Sanskrit (SLT) into English (TLT1) and Indonesian (TLT2). These losses of information are due to the procedures applied and the orientation chosen by the translators besides the cultural differences among Indian culture (SLT), English culture (TLT1) and Indonesian culture (TLT2).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Keywords: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">religious-cultural terms, translation procedures.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Translating religious cultural terms are interesting for number of reasons. Deep understanding concerning the meaning of religious-cultural terms in the source language text is greatly required in order to accurately translate those terms into the target language text. Newmark (1988:95) categorizes cultural terms into five categories: (1) ecology; (2) material culture (artifacts); (3) social culture; (4) organizations, customs, activities, procedures, concepts and (5) gesture and habits. According to his classification, religious cultural term belongs to the fourth</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">category namely organization, customs, activities, procedure and concept. As part of the culture, the religious aspect has important influences on human behavior. A religion functions as a moral and spiritual resource. Most religions have a holy book or scripture that is used as a guidance by their adherents.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Apart from the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Vedas</span><span class="font4">, the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> is also considered as one of the important holy books in Hindu religion. It is a sacred text which is believed to be a holy scripture for Hindu People that is revealed from </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lord Krishna</span><span class="font4"> to his devotees named </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Arjuna</span><span class="font4"> as guidance for religious and social life. The </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> contains many religious-cultural terms therefore it is used as the data source in this study.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This study aims at identifying the types of religious-cultural terms found in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> and investigating the translation of the religious-cultural terms into English and Indonesian, identifying the translation procedures applied by the translators in translating the religious-cultural terms found in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> into English and Indonesian and analyzing the effect of loss or addition of information to the meaning in the target language texts.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>RESEARCH METHODS</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">This study belongs to qualitative research approach in which the data were analyzed descriptively. This present study was conducted by observing the translation of religious-cultural terms in the holy book of “Bhagavad Gita” in Sanskrit into English and Indonesian as the data source.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data source of this study were the forms of religious cultural terms taken from </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> in Sanskrit and the translation in English: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita As It Is</span><span class="font4">” and in Indonesian: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita Menurut Aslinya</span><span class="font4">”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The method and technique of collecting data in this study is documentation technique as part of the qualitative method. This method was conducted by reading attentively the data source with the focus on the religious-cultural terms and their translation in TLT texts.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The method of analyzing data was qualitative-descriptive method that aimed to describe systematically, factually, and accurately the characteristics and correlations that occur among the phenomena (Djajasudarma, 1993:10).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The result of analysis in this study is presented formally and informally as proposed by Sudaryanto (1993:145). The analyses of the data or the explanation were presented in descriptive sentences.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>DISCUSSION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">The analysis of religious cultural terms are limited to the level of words and phrases found in verses of Bhagavad Gita. All the religious-cultural words found are classified by using Newmark’s cultural words category, namely, ecology, material culture, social culture (work and leisure), concepts, as well as gestures and habit.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Religious Cultural Terms Related to Ecology</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Agvatthau</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"> ( </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">AiTQa&quot;</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">) </span><span class="font4">is one of the religious cultural terms related to ecology. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Asvatthah</span><span class="font4"> can be categorized as religious cultural terms since this plant</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">mostly grow in India and reflect the Indian culture. The verse in Sanskrit</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">containing the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Açvatthaù </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font4"> its translation into English and Indonesian are presented below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SLT : </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">sarva-vrksanam devarsinam ca naradah, gandharvanam citrarathah siddhanam kapilo munih </span><span class="font4">(BG,10:26)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TLT1 : Of all trees I am </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">the banyan tree</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">,</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font4">and of the sages among the demigods I am Narada. Of the Gandharvas I am Citraratha, and among perfected beings I am the sage Kapila</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TLT2 : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Di antara semua pohon, Aku adalah </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">. Di antara resi-resi di kalangan para dewa Aku adalah Narada. Di antara para Gandharva Aku adalah Citraratha, dan di antara makhluh-makhluk yang sempurna Aku adalah resi Kapila</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> tree is a huge tree with very extensive branches and it is a native tree of India. According to Sankrit English Dictionary </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> is the holy fig tree, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Ficus Religiosa</span><span class="font4"> (William,2004:115). According to Subhas (2012), this tree is representing the growth stability, continuity and knowledge of Eastern Indian old tradition. This tree is also very important to other religions in India such as Buddhism and Jainism since this is as a symbol of continuous flow of the emanating knowledge.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The term “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> “ was translated directly into “banyan tree” in TLT1 and “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4">” in TLT2. The translators in translating the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> (SLT) into “banyan tree” in TLT1 and “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4">” in TLT2 both applied the literal translation procedure since the term above is literary translated into TLT1 and TLT2. The translation in TLT1 and TLT 2 both reflect formal equivalence in translation since it is a word by word translation. By applying the formal equivalent orientation, the translators may believe that the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">result of translation can reveal as much as possible of the form and content of the original message to the target reader.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Based on the semantic features above we can see that both </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù </span><span class="font4">(SLT), and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4"> (TLT2) share all the features. In Bali which mostly inhabited by Hindu followers, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4"> is respected by the people due to its sacred and ritual value, namely the value related to the supernatural power called </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tenget</span><span class="font4">. The Balinese people believe there is a supernatural power inhibits </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4">. In this belief, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4"> functions as one of the attributes used by the Hindu followers in Bali in several ceremonies. In addition, many Indonesian people consider </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4"> as special tree different from common trees. Even, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4"> is used as the symbol of unity in the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Pancasila</span><span class="font4"> (state ideology of Indonesia). Since there is no loss of information found in the translation of the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> into Indonesian, the translation is considered equivalent to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pohon beringin</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">On the other hand, there is one semantic feature lost in the translation of</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">asvattha</span><span class="font4"> (in SLT) into “banyan tree” (in TLT1). In SLT, </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> is considered as a sacred tree but in TLT1, banyan tree is considered as an ordinary tree. There is no specific belief whether this tree is a sacred tree in western culture and in other cultures that are not related to Hinduism.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Loss of information especially an implicit loss occured in the translation of the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> into”banyan tree” in TLT1 since the sacred value of </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">açvatthaù</span><span class="font4"> is not transferred into TLT1. This loss is probably due to the procedure applied by the translator and the orientation of the translator in translating the term.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Therefore, to retain all the meaning components in TLT, the following suggested translation by applying a transferrence procedure with an addition of information in the form of a note added next to the term borrowed from the SLT is worth considering so the target readers may notice that banyan tree is not an ordinary tree but it is a sacred tree in Hinduism.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>Religious Cultural Terms Related to Social Culture(work and leisure)</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">One of the religious cultural terms related to social culture (work and leisure) found in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> (</span><span class="font3">YaaeGa</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">). </span><span class="font4">The verse containing the term </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> and its translation into TLT1 and TLT2 are presented below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SLT : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Yoga-sthah kuru karmani sangam tyaktva dhananjaya</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Siddhy-asiddhyoh samo bhutva Samatvam </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">yoga</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> ucyate (BG,2:48)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TLT1: Perform your duty equiposed, O Arjuna, abandoning all attachment to success or failure. Such equanimity is called </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">yoga</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TLT2: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Wahai Arjuna, lakukanlah kewajibanmu dengan sikap seimbang, lepaskanlah segala ikatan terhadap sukses maupun kegagalan. Sikap seimbang seperti itu disebut </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">yoga</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The term </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> refers to application or concentration of the thoughts, abstract contemplation, meditation,self-concentration, abstract meditation and mental abstraction practised as a system as taught by Patanjali and called the Yoga philosophy (William, 2004: 856).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In translating the term </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> into English (TLT1) and Indonesian (TLT2) the procedure used by the translator is transference procedure since lexical item in SLT is used both by TLT1 and TLT2. One of the initial reasons for using this procedure is because among those three languages (Sanskrit,English and Indonesia) there are semantic gap in its lexicon, meaning that there is no existing word in the TLT 1 and TLT2 which has the same meaning with word in SLT. This procedure also used to maintains the cultural effect of the SLT. The translators chose not to paraphrase or provide extra information to the target readers because of the familiarity reason. The translators of TLT1 and TLT2 assume that the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> has been known by target reader in TLT1 and TLT2 since </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> has been practiced by people all over the world. The use of transference procedure to translate the term </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> into TLT1 and TLT2 show the formal equivalence orientation because it is basically source oriented, it is designed to reveal as much as possible of the form and content of the original</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">message. There is no loss or addition of information found in the translation of the term </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yoga</span><span class="font4"> into TLT1 and TLT2.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>CONCLUSION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Based on the data analysis and discussion, the conclusion was drawn out as follows . First, the types of religious-cultural terms found in the translation of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bhagavad Gita</span><span class="font4"> into English (TLT1) and Indonesian (TLT2) are religious cultural terms related to ecology, material culture, social culture (work and leisure), concepts, gestures and habit. Second, The translation procedures applied by the translators in dealing with the translation of religious-cultural terms from Sanskrit into English (TLT 1) and Indonesian (TLT 2) are literal translation, transferrence procedure, cultural equivalent and functional equivalent. Third, There are some losses of information occured in the translation of the religious cultural terms from Sanskrit (SLT) into English (TLT1) and Indonesian (TLT2). These losses of information are due to the procedures applied and the orientation chosen by the translators. In addition, the cultural differences among Indian culture (SLT), English culture and Indonesian culture are considered as other factors causing loss of information in the translation. The effects of these losses of information are the loss of sacred value in the translation of the religious cultural terms in TLT and different interpretation regarding the form and function of a certain term in TLT.</span></p>
