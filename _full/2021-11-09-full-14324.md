<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Bahasa Inggris sangatlah penting dalam era globalisasi. Bahasa Inggris harus diaplikasikan dengan benar sesuai dengan aturannya. Kalimat yang benar dalam Bahasa Inggris meliputi banyak aspek. Salah satu diantaranya adalah jenis kata. Sesuai dengan tulisan ini yang berjudul The Analysis of Prepositions in, at, &amp;&nbsp;on in The Short Story “Rip Van Winkle” by Washington Irving, preposisi sangatlah penting untuk membuat kalimat yang baik. Itu mengapa penulis ingin meningkatkan pemahaman dan kemampuan dalam menggunakan preposisi khususnya in, at, &amp;&nbsp;on.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Sumber data dari tulisan ini adalah cerita pendek yang berjudul Rip Van Winkle yang ditulis oleh Washington Irving. Metode pengumpulan data dilakukan dengan menyimak bacaan dan mencatat data yang dibutuhkan dari cerita pendek tersebut. Dalam menganalisa data, tulisan ini menggunakan metode deskriptif kualitatif. Metode ini digunakan untuk menjelaskan karakter dari data sesuai dengan teori yang digunakan dalam tulisan ini.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Dalam tulisan ini preposisi in, at, &amp;&nbsp;on dianalisa sesuai dengan fungsi dan maknanya, menurut teori Quirk, et. Al. Sesuai dengan analisa preposisi tersebut dibagi menjadi dua fungsi yaitu adverb dan complementation. Dari banyak kategori makna, ditemukan preposition dalam kategori positive position, space, time position, dan goal/target.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Dari tulisan ini ditemukan bahwa fungsi adverbial akan ditemukan jika preposisi in, at, &amp;&nbsp;on terdapat pada kalimat lengkap yang terdiri dari subjek, predikat, dan objek. Di sisi lain, fungsi complementation akan ditemukan pada kalimat yang tidak lengkap. Kategori makna preposisi </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">terdiri dari positive position, space, dan time position, preposisi </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">at </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">terdiri dari positive position, time position, dan goal/target, sedangkan preposisi </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">on </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">terdiri dari positive position dan time position.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Preposisi, fungsi, makna</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1.</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Language is one of essential part of human culture which has a very important role in the human daily life. As a means of communication, language has a role when we express ideas, thought and feeling, to convey information, to transfer knowledge, science, and technology, either in spoken or written form.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Many aspect can be found in the language whether internal or external ones. From the internal point of view, there are morphology, phonology, syntax, semantics, and grammar. They are branches of linguistics. These aspects are often called micro linguistic elements.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Focusing on the research, the topic of discussion is taken from a micro linguistic study (grammar) especially preposition which is one of the important elements of the English sentences. In English sentences we can find five elements that must be combined correctly, such as subject, verb, complement, object, and adverbial. As we know there are simple prepositions and complex prepositions. A simple preposition consists of a word or one word, for examples </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">of</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">with</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font0">, some of them also adverbs. Complex prepositions consist of combinations of two or three words that function in the same way, for examples, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">according to</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">regardless of</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in addition to</span><span class="font0">. Some of the relationships which can be expressed by prepositions are </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">place</span><span class="font0"> or </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">position</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">time</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">manner</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">agent</span><span class="font0">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Michael Swan in “Practical English Usage” said that it is difficult to learn how to use prepositions correctly in a foreign language. Most English prepositions have several different functions (for instance, one well known dictionary lists eighteen main uses of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">), and these may correspond to several different prepositions in another language. (Swan, 1995: 444)</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">According to the statement above, that is why it is very interesting to learn prepositions, especially prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in, at</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> in detail. These prepositions have some types and various meanings in sentences. They are also found in various word classes in sentences. In addition, by means of this research we can increase the understanding of these prepositions, in order to use those in a sentence correctly.</span></p>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Positive Position and Destination</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He used to tell story to every stranger that arrived </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">at </span><span class="font0">Mr. Dollitle’s hotel.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.., but I fell asleep </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">on </span><span class="font0">the mountain, ...</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.., yet it was the worst conditioned farm </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">in </span><span class="font0">the neighborhood.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Prepositional phrases </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Mr. Dolleitle’s hotel</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1), </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on the mountain</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in the neighborhood</span><span class="font0"> above indicate location.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The function of prepositional phrases </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Mr. Dollitle’s hotel</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) is complementation of verb because the preceding word of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> is a verb ‘arrived’ and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on the mountain</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) is complementation of adjective because the preceding word of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> is an adjective ‘asleep’. If we omit the prepositional phrases they will be incomplete. So, they are required to complete the sentence. However, the function of prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in the neighborhood</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (3) is as adverbial. It gives more information about the noun ‘farm’. It can be omitted without destroying the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Space</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He caught his daughter and her child </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">in </span><span class="font0">her arms.</span></p></li>
<li>

The function of prepositional phrases in both sentence are adverbial. Preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) and sentence (2) gives more information about the noun ‘child’ and ‘tongues’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Time Position</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.., took his part in all family squabbles and never failed, whenever they</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">talked those matters over </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">in </span><span class="font0">their evening gossiping, …</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.., as fine lady does her train </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">in </span><span class="font0">bad weather.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;.., they are clothed in blue and purple, and print their bold outlines </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">on </span><span class="font0">clear evening sky, …</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The prepositional phrases </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in their evening gossiping</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) explain that the period of their evening gossiping is shorter than a day, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in bad weather</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) explains the period of bad weather is shorter than a day, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on clear evening sky</span><span class="font0"> explains the period of the clear evening sky.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The function of prepositional phrases in </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">their evening gossiping</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) is complementation of adjective because the preceding word of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0"> is an adjective ‘over’, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in bad weather</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) is as adverbial because it gives more information about the noun ‘train’, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on clear evening sky</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (3) is as adverbial because it gives more information about the noun ‘outlines’. The prepositional phrases in sentence (2) and (3) can be omitted without destroying grammaticality of the sentence.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>5.4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Goal/Target</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…; and not a dog would bark </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">at </span><span class="font0">him throughout the neighborhood.</span></p></li>

<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…, he sneaked about with a gallows air, casting many a sidelong glance </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">at </span><span class="font0">Dame Van Winkle, …</span></p></li>

<p><span class="font0">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A troop of strange children ran </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">at </span><span class="font0">his heels, …</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at him throughout the neighborhood</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) explains that ‘him’ as the intended target, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Dame Van Winkle</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) explains that ‘Dame Van Winkle’ is the intended target, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at his heels</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (3) explain that ‘his heels’ is the intended goal. The words or a phrase which are followed by preposition at in every sentence has a role as a noun or noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The function of the prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at him throughout the neighborhood</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (1) is as complementation of verb, because the preceding word of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> is a verb ‘bark’, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Dame Van Winkle</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (2) is as adverbial, because it gives more information about the noun phrase ‘a sidelong</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">glance’, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at his heels</span><span class="font0"> is as complementation of verb, because the preceding word of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> is the verb ‘ran’.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Based on the result of the data analysis which is presented above, it can be drawn by some conclusions. The conclusions of the research of the prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> are found from the data source that is the short story “Rip Van Winkle” by Washington Irving. They were analyzed based on the theory of Quirk, ET. Al. Some conclusions which are drawn can be formulated as follows.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> which were analyzed in this writing describe that they have syntactic function. The function of prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> is divided into two points, such as postmodyfier in noun phrase, adverbial and complementation. From the data source it could be found only two functions that are adverbial and complementation, to develop a well structure. The application of the functions above makes the concern of the research to that’s two functions. From this writing can be found that the function of </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">adverbial</span><span class="font0"> will occur if prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> are presented in the complete sentence which consist of; subject, predicate and object.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">On the other side, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">complementation</span><span class="font0"> will occur if the sentence is not complete as well.
