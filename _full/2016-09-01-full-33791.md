<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Beberapa kesalahan dihasilkan oleh murid ketika mereka berada dalam proses pembelajaran atau dalam berlatih bahasa Inggris mereka. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengidentifikasi jenis kesalahan bentuk kata kerja yang dilakukan oleh murid XII IPA 1 Sman 1 Kuta Selatan dalam bahasa tulis dan bahasa lisan dan menjelaskan faktor penyebab terjadinya kesalahan tersebut. Jenis kesalahan dalam penggunaan kata kerja oleh para murid diklasifikasikan ke dalam empat kategori menurut teori yang dikemukakan oleh Dulay (1982) dalam bukunya berjudul “Language Two” dan faktor penyebab terjadinya kesalahan menurut teori yang dicetuskan oleh Richards (1974) dalam bukunya yang berjudul “Error Analysis: Perspective on Second Language Acquisition”. Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa analisis penggunaan kata kerja dengan topik hobby, holiday, family, future goal, close friends, dan memorable experience “omission” ialah jenis kesalahan yang paling banyak dilakukan oleh para respondents dalam bahasa tulis dan lisan, dan “incomplete application of rule” ialah faktor yang terbanyak yang menyebabkan para responden melakukan kesalahan penggunaan kata kerja dalam hal tulisan maupun lisan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Kata kunci: Analisis Kesalahan, Bentuk Kata Kerja, Sman 1 Kuta Selatan</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">In daily communication practice, error cannot be avoided. It sometimes deviates from a rule existing in a language, there are some errors in speaking. They happen without being known by the speaker due to many factors. In learning vocabularies, learners might have able to master a number of words and also put them in certain categories, such as: noun, verb, adjective, or adverb. In this study, one of those categorical classifications, namely, a verb was analyzed. This study is concerned with the errors in verb forms made by the students of SMAN 1 Kuta Selatan. Sometimes, some errors are produced by the students when they are in learning process of or in practicing their English</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The problems discussed in this study are formulated as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of verb form errors are made by the students in written language and spoken language?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What factors are contributing to such errors?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify types of verb form errors made by the students in written language and spoken language.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;T o explain the factors contributing to such errors.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data source of this study were taken from the result of written language, and spoken language test done by 20 XII IPA 1 students of SMAN 1 Kuta Selatan. The samples for this study were essays in which the paragraph writing given to the students. The spoken language test was conducted by interviewing directly to the students. The topics of the written language and spoken language tests were about hobby, holiday, family, future goal, close friends, and the memorable experience</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The nature of field research was the method in collecting data. The students were required to write at least 2 paragraphs for the test and must be submitted within 60 minutes in written language test. Meanwhile, the spoken language test was collected by recording interview to the students in which the students were the interviewees and they were asked to speak at least 2 minutes.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Qualitative method was used in analyzing the data. There were some steps in analyzing the data. First, the types of verb form errors were identified according to Dulay’s surface strategy taxonomy (1982) therefore, classified into some categories. Second, the factors contributing the errors were explained by applying the theory of Richards (1974).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The result of the analysis was divided into 4: Omission Error, Addition Error, Misformation Error, and Misordering Error.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Omission error</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">a)</span><span class="font3"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written language (hobby) </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">:</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My father </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">give</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> support for me and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">teach</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me how to be a good player.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My father </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">give</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">s support for me and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">teaches</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me how to be a good player.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The error made by the student is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the omission of s/es marker</span><span class="font3"> in simple present tense, the grammatical sentence is should be </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My father </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">give</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">s support for me and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">teaches </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">me how to be a good player,</span><span class="font3"> In this case, the student omitted s/es marker in the verb for the third person singular, which functions to show the present tense</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The Interlingual factor</span><span class="font3"> as the factor contributed the error because the third person singular marker is omitted as the students transfer their native language, which automatically interferes with the learning of their second language. In Indonesian there is no agreement that third person singular must be followed by –e/es marker on the verb. The students use their native language structure and disobey the agreement in the target language</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written Language (family) :</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My father is well </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">know</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> in my hometown in Gianyar</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My father is well </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">known</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> in my hometown in Gianyar</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The sentence is passive sentence which is formed by putting </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to be</span><span class="font3"> and adding the past participle of the irregular verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">known</span><span class="font3">. It is kind of the omission of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">past participle in irregular verbs,</span><span class="font3"> the student omitted the use of past participle in irregular verb know in the sentence. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The incomplete application of rule</span><span class="font3"> as the factor contributed the error because this error results from the student using incomplete rule of passive sentence. The students applied to be </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">is</span><span class="font3"> correctly but failed to invert the past participle of irregular verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">know</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written Language (future goal) : </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I was </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">design</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> T-shirt, jacket, and short pants for opening my friend’s store.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I was </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">designing</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> T-shirt, jacket, and short pants for opening my friend’s store.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This sentence is past progressive because it expresses an action which began before and maybe continued after it. In this case the student omitted the use of progressive –ing in the verb design. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The incomplete application of rule</span><span class="font3"> as the factor is contributed the error because the student fail to form the past progressive well, he failed to invert the past progressive –ing , therefore the sentence is ungrammatical..</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">d) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written language (close friends) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">One day I </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">invited </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">by william’s family to</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">holiday.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">One day I </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">was invited </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">by william’s family to holiday.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The student tried to form the passive sentence even though the sentence is ungrammatical. In this case, the error made by the student is the omission of auxiliary. The student applied the use of the past participle of the verb correctly; however, He omitted </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to be</span><span class="font3"> that should be followed by the past participle. The incomplete application of rule as the factor influenced the error because the student failed to form the complex structure of passive sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Addition Error</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font3">Double marking error</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written Language (hobby) :</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My coach will always </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">helps</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> us to be the winner in the tournament.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">My coach will always </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">helps</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> us to be the winner in the tournament.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This sentence use modal verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">will</span><span class="font3">. All of modal verbs should be followed by base form of verb. In this case two items, they are modal verb and verb are marked for</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">the same features to form the present tense. In this case the student failed to delete the – s marker in the verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">helps</span><span class="font3">, which is not required in the sentence because there is a modal verb that should be followed by the base verb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written language (memorable experience) : </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">They still </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">didn’t allowed</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me to sit down.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">They still </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">didn’t allow</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me to sit down.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">From the sentence above the auxiliary is not the main verb, it takes the tense. This sentence is classified as double marking error because there are two items marked for the same features (auxiliary and verb) to form the negation in the simple past. Therefore, to make the sentences are grammatical, the verbs should be in the base form for the negation </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">allow.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The over-generalization</span><span class="font3"> as the factor contributed for those two sentences because in the sentence in written language (hobby), the studnets interpreted that in order to perform the simple present the third person singular (my coach) should be added the –s marker to the verb, and he didn’t realize that there is a modal verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">will</span><span class="font3"> in the sentence. And for the second sentence in written language on topic memorable experience, the student interpreted that in order to perform the negative sentence in simple past, the verb is in past verb too. The students ignored the rule of simple past tense in forming the negative.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font3">Regularization</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written Language (holiday) : </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">weared</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> a unique costume.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">wore</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> a unique costume.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The sentences is ungrammatical because the students applied the incorrect irregular verb in past tense that should be </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">wore</span><span class="font3">. The use of –ed marker in forming the simple past tense will be correct if the sentence is in affirmative form and the verb is a regular one. In this case, the verb in the sentence is irregular so the past verb is formed not by adding –ed marker. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The false concept hypothesized</span><span class="font3"> is a factor contributing the error because the students hypothesized that in order to perform the simple past, all of</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">the verbs should be added the-ed marker, however he didn’t realized that not all the verbs are regular. The irregular verbs have their own form.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">- </span><span class="font3">Simple Addition Error</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">d) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spoken Language (family) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">He </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">was told</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me that position can earn more</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">money by tips</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">He </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">told</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> me that position can earn more money by tips.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In order to perform the affirmative form of the simple past tense, the formula should be:</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">subject + the past verb.</span><span class="font3"> However, the student added unnecessary auxiliaries </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">was</span><span class="font3">, making the sentences ungrammatical. From the sentence above, the auxiliary should be omitted to make grammatical structured of simple past tense.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">e) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Written language (close friends) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">We </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">are come</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> from a different family</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">background</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">We </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">come</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> from a different family background</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The student created unnecessary </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">auxiliary are</span><span class="font3"> that made the sentence is ungrammatical. The application of auxiliary is correct if the sentence is followed by adjective, noun, adverb, and the verb which states that the subject is doing something. In this sentence </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">come</span><span class="font3"> is a verb, therefore the auxiliary should be omitted. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The false concept hypothesized</span><span class="font3"> is a factor contributed to those errors because in the sentence in spoken language (family), the auxiliary </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">was</span><span class="font3"> may be interpreted as the markers of the past tense, so the student hypothesized that the auxiliaries should be added to form the simple past tense. In the sentence in written language (close friends), the auxiliaries </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">are</span><span class="font3"> may be interpreted as the markers of simple present tense, so the students hypothesized that the auxiliary should be added before the verb to form the simple present tense</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Misformation error</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) Written language (close friends) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">He </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">not</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> become arrogant</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">He </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">doesn’t</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> become arrogant</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In this case the student supplied </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the misformation of negative form.</span><span class="font3"> L1 learners have been observed to use only </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">no</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">not</span><span class="font3"> to express negation, while L2 learners use </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">don’t</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">doesn’t</span><span class="font3"> as well as no and not to produce the negation (Dulay et al 1982:162). The interference of learning a second language is influenced by native language. The student transferred their native language in learning a second language, this kind of error is influenced by the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">interlingual factor</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Misordering error</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) Spoken Language (close friends) : </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">We two times a week </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">play</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;"> futsal</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">We play futsal two times a week</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In the sentence above, the incorrect placement of morphemes in an utterance occur systematically both L1 and L2 learners in construction, in this case the student made misordering error that is word-for-word translation of native language structure. The correct sentence should be </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">We play futsal two times a week. The interlingual factor </span><span class="font3">is contributed to the error because the students transfer their native language structure in learning a second language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion and Suggestion</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>6.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The conclusion for this study showed that in written and spoken language tests, the analysis of verbs on the topics </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">hobby, holiday, close friends</span><span class="font3">, and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">memorable experience</span><span class="font3">, the dominant error found was the omission error. Meanwhile, on the topics </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">family</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">future goal,</span><span class="font3"> the dominant error found in written language test was the omission error and in spoken language test was the addition error. The misformation and misordering error are also found in written and spoken language tests on the topics </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">hobby, family, future goal</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">close friends,</span><span class="font3"> however the number of them are not many. The incomplete application of rule is dominant factor found in written and spoken language test.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>6.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suggestion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The students should encourage themselves by practicing their English in their daily activities with their friends or their English teacher in order to make the learning process runs effectively. The teacher should active in learning process by giving an</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">extra attention to the students in order to improve their vocabulary like more exercises in speaking or writing test</span></p>
