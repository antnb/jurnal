<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Studi ini membahas mengenai kata ganti yang ditemukan pada cerita pendek yang berjudul “The Horse Dealer’s Daughter”. Kata ganti dapat dibedakan menjadi tiga jenis menurut teori dari Halliday dan Hasan (1976). Ketiga jenis kata ganti tersebut ditemukan dalam cerita pendek “The Horse Dealer’s Daughter” yakni kata ganti personal, kata ganti demonstratif dan kata ganti komparatif. Sementara kata kanti yang paling banyak digunakan dalam cerita pendek “The Horse Dealer’s Daughter”adalah kata ganti personal. Semua jenis kata ganti yang ditemukan dalam cerita pendek “The Horse Dealer’s Daughter” direpresentasikan secara anaporik dan kataporik sesuai dengan teori yang digunakan untuk menganalisis kata ganti tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kata ganti, anaphorik, dan kataphorik</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language plays important role in human’s life especially for communication. It means that language is the principle modality of human communication. In terms of the way language is used, it is very important to understand what the speaker means despite what they say through the language, and take part in the activity called conversation. Brown and Yule (1984:6) state,”we shall use text as technical term to refer to the verbal record of communication act. It can be written and spoken text. Written and spoken text has to be cohesive and coherent. The term cohesion has some devices to make a tight relation in a text namely cohesive devices including reference.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Reference is the specific nature of information that is signalled for retrieval. As part of cohesive devices reference has to be applied in the text since if the people read or hear passage which is more than one sentence in length, sometimes they are difficult to understand the information that refered by the text. Reference can helps</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">the reader understand the information along the text easily. Thus, it would be interesting to analyze the use of reference in the short story.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data source were taken from the short story entitled The Horse Dealer’s Daughter by D.H Lawrence. Some sentences contain reference items were randomly chosen and presented as the data to be analyzed. Then, they were classified in terms of the types of referential cohesion as where it belongs to personal, demonstrative and comparative reference. The data would be analyzed using the theory of cohesion proposed by Halliday and Hassan (1976). Then, the short story was read intensively and continued by counting the reference item that lies in the short story. Then, identify the reference item that dominantly used in the short story. After that, it was continued by analyzing the information that refered by the reference item by using the theory of cohesion proposed by Halliday and Hasan (1976)</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>3.2 The Reference Items Presented in The Short Story.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In terms of reference items presented in the short story, it can be divided into two: exophora and endophora. Endophora can function either cataphoric or anaphoric. Anaphoric is reference which refers to the preceding text. Meanwhile cataphoric is reference which refers to the following text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.1 Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘I</span><span class="font2"> don’t see what else you can do</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">,’</span><span class="font2"> persisted</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Fred Henry</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. (49)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Example (a) above, personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font2"> cataphorically refers to Fred Henry. It belongs to cataphoric reference because reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font2"> refers to the word that stands on the following text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘</span><span class="font2">What are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2"> going to do, then, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Miss Pervin</span><span class="font2">?’ asked Fergusson</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(137)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Example (e) above personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2"> is classified as cataphoric reference because personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2"> is said by Fergusson and it refers to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Miss Pervin</span><span class="font2"> which is located in the following clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">we</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘</span><span class="font2">What’s doing?’ asked the newcomer, evidently addressing Fred Henry.(96)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">‘Same</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. We’</span><span class="font2">ve got to be out by Wednesday</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. (97)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">we</span><span class="font2"> here is identified as anaphoric reference since it refers to the information in the previous text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">they</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘</span><span class="font2">Let </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Lizzie </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">May </span><span class="font2">miss </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">their</span><span class="font2"> night for once, eh?</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">’(160)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">their</span><span class="font2"> refers anaphorically to the information which occurs in the previous clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">he</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">The doctor </span><span class="font2">smiled faintly.(150) ‘What’s she going to do, then?’</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">he</span><span class="font2"> asked</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(151) </span><span class="font2">Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">he</span><span class="font2"> refers anaphorically to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the doctor</span><span class="font2"> which is located in the preceding sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">For months, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Mabel </span><span class="font2">had been servantless in the big house, keeping the home together in penury for her ineffectual brothers</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(171) She</span><span class="font2"> had kept house for ten years</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(172)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span><span class="font2"> is identified as anaphoric reference because reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span><span class="font2"> refers to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mabel</span><span class="font2"> which is located in the preceding sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">The house </span><span class="font2">was large, but </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font2"> was servantless now, and desolate</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(163)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It belongs to anaphoric reference because it refers to the information which is located in the preceding text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Demonstrative reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">this</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">This</span><span class="font2"> was</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">the last time</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(16)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Demonstrative reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">this</span><span class="font2"> above is identified as cataphoric reference since it refers to the information which is located in the following clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.9 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Demonstrative reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘That’s</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> the orders</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">,’</span><span class="font2"> replied the other</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.(124)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Demonstrative reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font2"> is identified as cataphoric reference since it refers to the information in the following clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2.10 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Comparative reference</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">He seemed to see her in the midst of such obscurity, that he was </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">like</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> a clairvoyant</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font2"> seeing rather with the mind’s eye than with ordinary sight.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">(255)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example (e) above comparative reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">like</span><span class="font2"> is identified as cataphoric reference since it refers to the information in the following clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Three types of reference that occur in the short story are personal reference, demonstrative reference and comparative reference. The first type is personal reference including </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I, you, we, she, he, they</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it.</span><span class="font2"> The second type is demonstrative reference including </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">this, that</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the.</span><span class="font2"> The third types is comparative reference including </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">another, like,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2">. Besides, personal reference </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">he</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span><span class="font2"> are most frequently used in the short story. The reference items are presented in both ways, exophorically and endophorically. The reference items which is used in this short story are presented as anaphoric reference and the others are presented as cataphoric reference.</span></p>
