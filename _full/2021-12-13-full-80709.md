<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Contribution of Transposition Procedure to the Structural Adjustments in the Translation of English Memorandum of Understanding into Indonesian</span></h2>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract*</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">This current study, in which the descriptive qualitative approach is applied, is intended to reveal the extent to which the transposition procedure contributes to the structural adjustments in the translation of an English Memorandum of Understanding into Indonesian language. The data were collected through observation using close reading and note-taking techniques and qualitatively analyzed. The structural adjustments are adopted from the theories proposed by Viney and Darbelnet (in AUSSI, 1987: 143-147) and Catford (in AUSSI, 1987:145). The result of analysis shows that the Transposition Procedure highly contributes to the structural adjustments in the translation of the Memorandum of Understanding. They allow the translator to obtain dynamic equivalence and support the readability, acceptability and naturalness of the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Structural adjustments cannot be separated from the syntactic and structural aspects. In this stage the raw results of analysis of the SL (source language) are transformed in such a way into the structural and stylistic forms of the TL (target language) that the translation product becomes readable, acceptable and natural to the target reader. As well, in this stage what is understood and interpreted from the SL is encoded again using the structures of the TL. What is important is that the translation product should be functionally equivalent to the SL. It is here that the TPs (Translation Procedures) are needed as a consequence of the contact between two different cultural and linguistic systems. Apart from that, English language as the SL and Indonesian language as the TL come from two different languages which are not genetically related. As a result, in the process of translating the English memorandum into Indonesian structural adjustments are positive in nature and cannot be avoided.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">According to AISSI (1987:134), there are two factors determining what TPs can be applied by the translator to translating the SL into the TL. They are (1) the nature of the textual material of the SL as the TPs needed to translate scientific texts are certainly different from those applied to translate the other texts (for example, poetry) and (2) the extent to which the two languages are different from and similar to one another. Vinay and Darbelnet (in AUSSI, 1987:134) classify the TPs into two. They are (1) the direct TPs which are oriented towards the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL which include borrowing, calque and literal translation and (2)and indirect TPs which are oriented towards the TL which include transposition, modulation, equivalence and adaptation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This current study focuses on the contribution of the Transposition Procedure to the structural adjustments in the translation of an English Memorandum of Understanding into Indonesian.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The descriptive qualitative approach was applied in the current study for two reasons. The first reason is that the study is more oriented towards the descriptions of meaning revealed through the process of interpreting data in the forms of translation units such as words, phrases and sentences. The second reason is that the data used in the study were searched out, collected, analyzed and generalized based on the observed phenomena (Sutopo, 2002 in Handayani, 2009, 53). The data source is a Memorandum of Understanding which is in the form of translation corpora (Johansson, in Olohan, 2004:24-25) as it is unidirectional in nature and consists of one text in two language versions, namely in English language and Indonesian language. The English version is the SL and the Indonesian version is the TL. The data were collected through observation using close reading and note-taking techniques. The SL was juxtaposed with the TL in such a way that different translation problems and incongruities could be identified. The problems and incongruities, which were grouped into translation units, causing the translator to make structural adjustments, were underlined before they were transferred to the data book.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discussion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">In general, transposition is one of the translation procedures in which one grammatical unit in the TL substitutes for another different grammatical unit in the SL. According to Viney and Darbelnet (in AUSSI, 1987: 143-147), transposition refers to the restructuration in which one word in the TL substitutes for another word of different word class in the SL. The syntactic and structural differences between the SL and TL cause the linguistic level to become modified and changed. Therefore, this translation procedure is highly frequently used in the translation process. Furthermore, Viney and Darbelnet classify transposition into two; they are obligatory transposition and optional transposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Obligatory Transposition</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Obligatory transposition means that there is only one procedure which can be used to translate one translation unit from the SL into the TL and to maintain the meaning of the SL in the TL, as what is done by the translator when he/she translates an English noun phrase with the rule Adjective + Noun into Indonesian language with the rule Noun + Adjective. It is the different rules that require the translator to apply the obligatory transposition translation procedure. In the text of the Memorandum of Understanding used as the data source in the current study, it highly contributes to the structural adjustments in its translation as shown by the following data:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 1</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Mr. Nigel Stead in his </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">individual capacity </span><span class="font4">on behalf of the investor</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">group (or such corporate entity subsequently set up by the investor group) with his office at 38B Circular Road, (S) 049394 hereinafter referred to as “NS” and; (Stead dkk, 2006:1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Bapak Thomas Graeff dalam </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">kapasitas individu</span><span class="font4">nya, (atau kesatuan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">bersama semacam itu yang kemudian dibentuk oleh kelompok yang kemudian dibentuk oleh Thomas Graeff) yang berkantor di Jalan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Circular 38B, (S) 049394 yang selanjutnya disebut “TG” dan; (Kurnia, 2017:1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 2</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: WHEREAS MC2, TG, and NS are desirous of joining together to form a</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">special purporse </span><span class="font4">joint venture development company to develop the Project and will work together to raise the balance of capital required. (Stead dkk., 2006: 1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Bahwa MC2, TG, dan NS berkeinginan untuk bergabung mendirikan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">perusahaan pengembang usaha bersama dengan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">tujuan khusus </span><span class="font4">untuk mengembangkan Proyek tersebut dan akan bekerjasama mengumpulkan kekurangan modal yang diperlukan. (Kurnia, 2017:2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 3</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: If </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">additional capital </span><span class="font4">is required in excess of USD950,000 for the initial</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">land closing and/or after the land closing but before additional investor capital is secured, ... (Stead dkk., 2006:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Jika </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">modal tambahan </span><span class="font4">diperlukan melebihi USD950.000 untuk menutup</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">lahan awal dan/atau setelah menutup lahan tersebut namun sebelum tambahan dari Penanam Modal diperoleh, ... (Kurnia, 2017:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In data 1 the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">individual capacity</span><span class="font4"> with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">adjective + noun</span><span class="font4"> is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">kapasitas individu </span><span class="font4">with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">noun + adjective</span><span class="font4">; the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">special purporse</span><span class="font4"> with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">adjective + noun</span><span class="font4"> is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">tujuan khusus </span><span class="font4">with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">noun + adjective</span><span class="font4">, and the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">additional capital</span><span class="font4"> in data 3 with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">adjective + noun</span><span class="font4"> is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">modal tambahan</span><span class="font4"> with the structure </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">noun + adjective</span><span class="font4">. In this case, the SL’s structure cannot be forced in the TL, unlike the Structural Calque Translation Procedure which allows the SL’s structure to be used in the SL as illustrated by the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Post</span><span class="font4">, which is translated into Bali Pos.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Optional Transposition</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">When translating one word from the SL into the TL, the translator is frequently faced with more than one word choice. Which one will be decided to be used as the translation equivalent of the word is determined by the stylistic consideration without having to change the meaning conveyed by the word (Vinay and Darbelnet, in Venuti, 2000:88) (AUSSI, 1987:144-145), as illustrated by data 4 &amp;&nbsp;5 as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 4</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: There will be a </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">monthly </span><span class="font4">Board meeting to review the Project progress, and to</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">make all required decisions, which will then be executed by the Project Manager. (Stead dkk, 2006:4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Dewan akan mengadakan pertemuan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">setiap bulan </span><span class="font4">untuk meninjau</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">perkembangan Proyek dan untuk membuat segala keputusan yang diperlukan, yang kemudian akan dilaksanakan oleh Manajer Proyek. (Kurnia, 2017: 5)</span></p>
<div>
<p><span class="font4">Data 5 SL</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<p><span class="font4">: In accordance with the Master Plan and the </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">monthly </span><span class="font4">Board meetings</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">, </span><span class="font4">funds will be advanced from the master account to the working account. (Stead dkk., 2006: 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Sesuai dengan Rencana Induk dan hasil pertemuan Dewan yang diadakan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">setiap bulan</span><span class="font4">, dana akan diajukan dari akun induk ke akun kerja. (Kurnia, 2017: 5).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">monthly</span><span class="font4"> in data 4 and 5 above is an adjective in the SL; however, it is not translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bulanan</span><span class="font4">, which is an adjective as well in the TL. It is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">setiap bulan</span><span class="font4">, which is an adverb in the TL without having to change the meaning. If it were translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bulanan</span><span class="font4">, which is an adjective in the TL, the translation of the phrase ‘monthly board meeting’ could have been </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">rapat bulanan Dewan</span><span class="font4"> as another choice.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 6</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ..., NS and/or PDL retains the right to remove MC2 as Project Manager</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">without </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">payment</span><span class="font4"> of any further compensation or profit share other than that already paid up till the date of removal and MC2 will forfeit all shareholding interests in PDL. (Stead dkk., 2017: 5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ..., NS dan/atau PDL mempertahankan haknya untuk mengganti MC2 sebagai</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Manajer Proyek tanpa </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">membayar </span><span class="font4">kompensasi atau pembagian keuntungan lebih lanjut selain yang telah dibayarkan sampai tanggal pemberhentian MC2 sebagai manajer Proyek dan MC2 akan kehilangan semua bunga yang didapat dari sahamnya di PDL (Kurnia, 2017: 5-6)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Similarly, data 6 also shows the application of the optional transposition procedure in which the noun </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">payment </span><span class="font4">in the SL is not translated into the noun </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pembayaran</span><span class="font4"> in the TL; in fact, it is translated into the verb </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">membayar</span><span class="font4"> as in this active clause the agent of the activity mentioned in the predicate is NS and/or PDL (Kridalaksana, 1993: 10). If it were translated into the noun </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pembayaran</span><span class="font4"> the translation of the phrase ‘without payment of any further compensation</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">..</span><span class="font4">’ could have been </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tanpa pembayaran kompensasi yang lebih jauh</span><span class="font4"> … as another choice, which is also grammatically correct and understandable in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>3.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Translation Shift</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">In translating an SL into a TL, the translator frequently has to start from the formal equivalence before acquiring the textual equivalence, leading to what is referred to as translation shift, which, according to Carford (in Munday, 2008:60), can be classified into (1) level shift and (2) category shift. He further classifies the category shift into (a) structural shift, (b) class shift, (c) unit shift and intra-system shift.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>3.3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Level Shift</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This refers to the shift in which one grammatical item in the SL is translated into lexical item in the TL, as illustrated in data 7 and 8 as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 7</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: WHEREAS NS and/or its appointed nominee </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has </span><span class="font4">signed an Akta Jual</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Beli, Purchase Agreement, and various Access Road purchase/right of use Agreements which govern the overall land purchase transaction ... (Stead dkk., 2006:1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Bahwa NS dan/atau wakil yang ditunjuknya </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">telah </span><span class="font4">menandatangani Akta</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Jual Beli dan Perjanjian pembelian/hak pakai berbagai Akses Jalan yang mengatur keseluruhan transaksi pembelian tanah ... (Kurnia, 2017:1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 8</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: All parties </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">shall </span><span class="font4">resolve any dispute arising out of or in relation to this</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Agreement amicably. (Stead dkk., 2006: 6)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: &nbsp;&nbsp;Semua pihak </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">harus </span><span class="font4">menyelesaikan perselisihan yang timbul atau</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">berhubungan dengan perjanjian ini secara damai. (Kurnia, 2017: 6)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has </span><span class="font4">in data 7 in the SL is used to express the aspect of the grammatical category used to show that the activity of signing an </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Akta Jual Beli</span><span class="font4"> is already done; however, in the TL it is translated into the lexical item </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">telah</span><span class="font4">, as the grammatical category of this type is not available in the TL.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">shall </span><span class="font4">in data 8 which is commonly used to express a future activity is not translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">akan</span><span class="font4"> in the TL. It is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">harus</span><span class="font4">. The reason is that this phrase is used to show the promise made by the parties involved in the agreement to do what they are supposed to do. Apart from that, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">harus</span><span class="font4">, as its translation equivalent in the TL, is also used to show that the agreement is a legal document.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>3.3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Category Shift</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Recasting or Restructuring</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This translation procedure is applied if the SL’s structure does not exactly correspond to the TL’s. In other words, the SL’s sentence structure should be modified in such a way that it suits the use and convention of the TL. Therefore, this transposition is identical with the obligatory transposition (see 3.1).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">i) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the phrase level</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Adjetive + Noun </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Noun + Adjective</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 9</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: GM, RM and mc2 hereby fully admit, acknowledge and confess that they</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">have conducted the main land transaction without securing the access road which is in contradiction with the </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">initial understanding </span><span class="font4">between GM, RM, mc2 and the Investors.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: GM, RM dan mc2 dengan ini sepenuhnya mengakui bahwa mereka menutup</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">transaksi tanah utama tanpa mengikutisertakan tanah untuk akses jalan, yang mana hal ini bertentangan dengan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">kesepakatan awal </span><span class="font4">antara GM, RM, mc2 dan para Penanam Modal. (Kurnia, 2017: 14)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The structural shift in the phrase level in data 9 results from the fact that the rule of the noun phrase in the TL is different from that in the SL. The rule in the SL is Modifier + Noun but in the SL it is Noun + Modifier.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>ii) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the clause or sentence level</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The sentence structure in the SL sometimes needs to be changed in such a way that it suits the use and characteristics of the TL, as exemplified in data 10 as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 10</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: A minimum of two Director signatures will be required </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">for all transfers,</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">checks or disbursements of any thing</span><span class="font4">. (Stead dkk., 2006: 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Untuk semua jenis transfer, check atau pembayaran </span><span class="font4">diperlukan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">minimal dua tandatangan Direktur. (Kurnia, 2017: 5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 10 above shows an extended simple sentence (Muslich, 2010: 148) in which apart from the nuclear components (subject, predicate, object and complement) there is also one non-nuclear component used to complete the meaning of the sentence, namely an adverb used to express ‘purpose’. In short, the structural change is as follows Subject + Predicate + Adverb of Purpose (SL) </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Adverb of Purporse + Prediate + Subject (TL).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>3.3.3 Class Shift</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Class shift will take place if the translation equivalent of one lexical item in the SL is translated into the lexical item of another word class in the TL (Catford, 1965 in AUSSI, 1987:145). In general, class shift involves four classes of content words, namely noun, verb, adjective and adverb. However, the current study only discusses 3 (three) class shifts; they are a) noun </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">verb; b) adjective </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Noun and c) adverb </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">adjective a) Noun </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Verb</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 11</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: The profit share formula is defined as; Profit equals Net Profit after</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">deduction </span><span class="font4">of Interest but after adding back Project Management Fees and Project Overheads. (Stead dkk., 2006: 3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Rumusan pembagian keuntungan adalah sebagai berikut; Keuntungan sama</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">dengan Laba Bersih setelah </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">dikurangi </span><span class="font4">bunga dan Biaya Pengelolaan Proyek, dan Biaya Proyek ditambahkan kembali. (Kurnia, 2017: 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 11 shows that the word ‘deduction’ is a derived noun translated into the verb </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dikurangi</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjective </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Noun</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 12</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Specifically, this relates to any four to five star </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">residential </span><span class="font4">oriented</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">developments on the Bukit with a listed sales price between USD225, 000 and USD500, 000.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Secara khusus, ini berkaitan dengan pembangunan-pembangunan yang</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">berorientasi pada </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">hunian </span><span class="font4">bintang empat atau lima di Bukit dengan harga yang sudah ditentukan antara USD225, 000 dan USD500, 000.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 12 shows that the adjective ‘residential’ in the TL is translated into the noun </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">hunian</span><span class="font4"> in the TL</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">Adjective</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 13</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ... and any other parties to whom GM, RM, DF and/or mc2 have </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">wrongly</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">done shall be entitled and be fully empowered to report immediately such wrongdoings and the criminal charges thereon to the Police of the Republic of Indonesia ... (Stead dkk., 2006:17)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ... dan pria pihak lainnya yang terkena dampak perbuatan-perbuatan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">salah</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">tersebut berhak dan diperdayakan secara penuh untuk segera melaporkan perbuatan-perbuatan tersebut dan untuk mengajukan tuntutan-tuntutan pidana kepada Kepolisian Republik Indonesia ... (Kurnia, 2017:19)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 13 shows that the translation equivalent of the word ‘wrongly’, which is an adverb in the SL, is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">salah</span><span class="font4">, which is an adjective in the TL.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">What can be concluded from data 11, 12 and 13 is that in search for the dynamic equivalent the meaning should be maintained (Larson, 1988) and the form should be adjusted to that which can be used to express the more or less exact meaning in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>3.3.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unit Shift</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Unit shift takes place if, for example, one word in the SL is translated into a phrase in the TL and vice versa. According to AUSSI (1987:147-148), if the translation equivalent of any lexical unit in the SL is in the lower level in the TL, contraction will take place, and if the translation equivalent of any lexical unit is in the higher level in the TL, expansion will take place.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Contraction</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A group of words </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">word</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 14</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">In addition to </span><span class="font4">the specific purposes set forth above, the purpose of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">joint venture is also to conduct any business, ... (Stead dkk., 2006:2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Selain </span><span class="font4">tujuah khusus yang ditetapkan di atas, tujuan dari usaha patungan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">ini juga untuk menjalankan bisnis yang legal, ... (Kurnia, 2017:2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 14 shows that the expression ‘in addition to’ in the SL, which is a group of words, is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">selain</span><span class="font4">, which is a word in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">word</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 15</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Should Mr. Ali Khalpey agree to purchase the Grand Residence and his</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">associates agree to buy </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">both of </span><span class="font4">the Ocean Residences on the accelerated purchase schedule presented to him, it is agreed that ... (Stead dkk., 2006: 2-3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Jika Bapak Ali Khalpey sepakat untuk membeli the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Grand Residence</span><span class="font4"> dan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">rekan-rekannya juga sepakat untuk membeli </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">kedua </span><span class="font4">the Ocean Residences pada skema pembelian yang dipercepat yang ditunjukan kepadanya, disepakati bahwa ... (Kurnia, 2017:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">It can be seen that in data 15 the translation equivalent of ‘both of’, which is a phrase in the SL, is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kedua</span><span class="font4">, which is a word in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Expansion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Unlike contraction when a lexical unit of higher level in the SL is translated into a lexical unit of lower level in the TL, expansion is a shift in which a lexical of lower unit in the SL is translated into a lexical unit of higher level in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kata </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">frase</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 16</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Specifically</span><span class="font4">, this relates to any four to five star residential oriented</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">developments on the Bukit with a listed sales price between USD225,000 and USD500,000. (Stead dkk., 2006:7)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Pada khususnya</span><span class="font4">, ini berkaitan dengan pembangunan-pembangunan yang</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">berorientasi pada hunian bintang empat atau lima di Bukit dengan harga jual yang sudah ditentukan antara USD225.000 dan USD500.000. (Kurnia, 2017:8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 16 shows that the word ‘specifically’ in the SL is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pada khususnya</span><span class="font4">, which is a phrase in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Clause</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 17</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL ` &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ... and </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">in the event of any such delay </span><span class="font4">the time for any Party’s performance shall be</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">extended for a period equal to the time lost by reason of the delay PROVIDED THAT ... (Stead, 2006: 8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: ... dan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">jika penundaan semacam itu terjadi</span><span class="font4">, yang bersangkutan akan diberikan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">waktu perpanjangan yang sama dengan waktu yang hilang sebagai akibat dari penundaan tersebut, ASALKAN ...</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The phrase ‘in the event of any such delay’ in data 17 is a phrase in the SL; however, its translation equivalent in the TL is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">jika penundaan semacam itu terjadi</span><span class="font4">, which is a clause, meaning that there is a shift from phrase to clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>3.3.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Internal Shift</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">In translation the internal shift includes the shift within one system, for example, active construction </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">passive construction and vice versa, plural form </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">singular form and vice versa, transitive form </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">intransitive form and vice versa, and present </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">past and vice versa. However, in this study, as the data available are limited, the transitive form </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">the intransitive form and vice versa and present </span><span class="font0">÷ </span><span class="font4">past and vice versa are not discussed.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Active </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Passive</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 18</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: MC2 is assigning all rights to purchase the land to NS (Stead dkk.,</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">2006:2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Wewenang untuk membeli tanah tersebut diberikan kepada NS oleh MC2</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(Kurnia, 2017:2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 18 shows the shift from active construction to passive construction in which the syntactic functions of several elements change. In the SL, as an active construction, ‘all rights to purchase the land’ functions as the object of the sentence; however, in the TL, as a passive construction, its translation equivalent </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">wewenang untuk membeli tanah tersebut </span><span class="font4">functions as the subject of the sentence, meaning that the change in view point referred to as modulation takes place.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Passive </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Active</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 19</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Among other opportunities, </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">the right to purchase land directly or</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">indirectly adjacent to the Project land is included</span><span class="font4">. (Stead dkk., 2006:8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Peluang-peluang tersebut meliputi, </span><span class="font4">di antaranya, </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">hak untuk membeli</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">tanah yang berdekatan dengan tanah Proyek secara langsung atau tidak langsung. </span><span class="font4">(Kurnia, 2017: 9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 18 and 29 show that, essentially, both active construction and passive construction have the same meaning; however, the interrelatedness among their elements are different, depending on which element is used as the starting point (Eagleson et al., 1983: 13-14) and what information in the Agreement is already known by the Parties involved in it. The passive construction in the SL allows the translator to give emphasis on the final position occupied by MC2. On the other hand, if the translator considers that the agent is not important it will not be made to appear and the focus of attention is only to the essence of the massage, namely ‘the right to purchase land directly or indirectly adjacent to the Project land’. Data 19 shows that the focus of attention is ‘the other opportunities’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plural </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Singular</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 20</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: The initial </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">shareholdings </span><span class="font4">and subsequent profits shall be divided in the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">following manner between the parties (after all </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">investments</span><span class="font4">/</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">loans </span><span class="font4">from NS including the agreed interest and currency adjustment have first been repaid by the Joint Venture to NS). (Stead dkk., 2006:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Kepemilikan saham </span><span class="font4">awal dan keuntungan-keuntungan selanjutnya akan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">dibagi di antara pihak dengan cara sebagai berikut (setelah semua </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">investasi/pinjaman </span><span class="font4">dari NS termasuk penyesuaian bunga dan mata uang yang disepakati pertama kali dilunasi oleh Perusahaan Patungan ke NS). (Kurnia, 2017: 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word ‘shareholdings’ in data 20, if viewed from its form, is in plural form in the SL; however, it is translated into singular form in the TL as it is acceptable in the TL. But the word ‘profits’ in the SL is in plural form and has to be translated into plural form as well to avoid ambiguity and is expressed using a reduplication as the benefits that will be enjoyed</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">by the investors vary. The word ‘investments/loans’ in the SL is treated the same as the word ‘shareholdings’; it is translated into singular form in the TL as it is already preceded by the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">semua</span><span class="font4"> (all) as the plural marker.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>d) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Singular </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Plural</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 21</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: In order to protect the </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">interest </span><span class="font4">of NS shareholders, it is agreed that all</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">money received from the sale of land and villas, all assets acquired by the Joint Venture including the building of any villas will be held to the benefit of NS shareholders until such time as they receive a return of their capital ... (Stead dkk., 2006: 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Untuk melindungi </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">kepentingan-kepentingan </span><span class="font4">para pemegang saham NS,</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">disepakati bahwa semua uang yang diterima dari penjualan lahan dan villa, semua aset yang diakuisisi oleh Perusahaan termasuk bangunan villa apapun akan disimpan untuk kepentingan para pemegang saham NS sampai mereka mendapatkan pengembalian modal ... (Kurnia, 2017:5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 21 shows that the word ‘interest’ in the SL is in singular form; however, its translation equivalent in the TL is in plural form. Based on the macro context of the text, this is intended to clarify that obtaining benefit is not the only thing expected by the shareholders. There are still several other interests as indicated in point 15 of this Agreement, two of them are free stay for 10 (ten) days at the resort per unit of shareholders and the right to developing the surrounding area.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>e) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One sentence </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Three sentences</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 22</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">[</span><span class="font4">None of the Parties shall be considered to be in default of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">performance of its obligations under this Agreement if the performance is directly prevented or delayed by any acts of God, fires, strikes, lockouts or labor disputes, wars, riots, acts of terrorism, earthquakes, storms, typhoons or floods and in the event of any such delay the time for any Party’s performance shall be extended for a period equal to the time lost by reason of the delay PROVIDED THAT the Party invoking such event or events shall give notice of any event of force majeure within seven (7) calender days of the happening of the event and any abatement thereof to the other Parties and shall take all action necessary and reasonable under the circumstances to mitigate such prevention or delay</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">] </span><span class="font4">(Stead dkk., 2006:8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">[</span><span class="font4">Apabila ada pihak yang terhalang atau tertunda melakukan kewajiban-</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">kewajiban yang ditentukan dalam Perjanjian ini karena bencana alam, kebakaran, pemogokan, penutupan atau perselisihan perburuhan, kekacauan, tindak terorisme, gempabumi, topan, badai atau banjir, yang</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">bersangkutan tidak digolongkan sebagai pihak yang gagal dalam melaksanakan kewajiban-kewajiban tersebut</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">]</span><span class="font4">. </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">[</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Jika</span><span class="font4"> terjadi penundaan seperti itu, yang bersangkutan akan diberikan waktu perpanjangan yang sama dengan waktu yang hilang sebagai akibat dari penundaan tersebut, ASALKAN yang bersangkutan menginformasikan peristiwa yang dialami tersebut di atas dalam waktu 7 (tujuh) hari kalender semenjak kejadian tersebut kepada pihak-pihak lain</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">]</span><span class="font4">. &nbsp;</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">[</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Selain itu</span><span class="font4">, yang</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">bersangkutan harus mengambil segala tindakan yang dianggap perlu dan wajar dalam suasana seperti itu untuk meredakan hal-hal yang menghalangi atau menunda tersebut</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">]</span><span class="font4">. (Kurnia, 2017:9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 22 shows that the SL is an extremely long and complex sentence, as one of the characteristics of the legal English; however, it is translated into three much shorter and simpler sentences without changing the meaning to support the degree of its readability for the target reader, especially the related local parties involved in the Agreement. This is in accordance with what is stated by Gunning (1968, in Kolahi, 2012:358) that the text should be written using simple and short sentences without any useless words to make it easily read. The second sentence starts with the conditional subordinate conjunction </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">jika </span><span class="font4">(if) followed by an embedded clause indicating the condition required to enable what is stated in the main sentence to be able to be carried out. The third sentence starts with the conjunct </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">selain itu</span><span class="font4"> (apart from that) used to mention what is not mentioned in the previous sentence (Muslich, 2010: 116), namely </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mengambil segala tindakan yang dianggap perlu dan wajar untuk meredakan hal-hal yang menghalangi atau menunda tersebut</span><span class="font4"> (take all action necessary and reasonable under the circumstances to mitigate such prevention or delay). The attempt to simplify the language used in the translation is made by dividing the long and complex sentence of the SL into several shorter sentences in the TL. (Bield, 2009:1)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">f) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two Sentences </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">÷ </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">One Sentence</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Data 23</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: [If at any time MC2 ceases to be adequately capitalized to perform its</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">functions and/obligations under this agreement, it shall immediately notify all parties and MC2 agrees to provide said notification in a timely manner in order to protect the interests of NS, TG and PDL]. [NS, TG and PDL retain all rights to alter and/or amend the terms of appoinment of MC2 including PM fees and shareholdings and profit share should MC2 fail to be adequately capitalized and or fail to perform its duties and/or obligations hereinunder]. (Stead dkk., 2006:7)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: Jika sewaktu-waktu MC2 tidak punya modal yang cukup lagi untuk</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">menjalankan fungsi-fungsi dan/atau kewajiban-kewajiban mereka berdasarkan pernjanjian ini, mereka harus segera memberitahukan kepada semua pihak dan mereka sepakat untuk menginformasikan hal tersebut kepada NS, TG dan PDL, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yang</span><span class="font4"> tetap memiliki semua hak untuk mengubah dan/mengubah persyaratan-persyaratan yang diperlukan untuk menunjuk MC2 termasuk imbalan jasa untuk PM dan kepemilikan saham dan bagi hasil jika MC2 gagal mendapatkan modal yang cukup dan atau gagal melakukan tugas-tugas dan/atau kewajiban-kewajibannya di bawah ini. (Kurnia, 2017:8).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In the legal language there are several steps which can be taken to create precise communication, one of which is avoiding the use of pronoun (Tiersma, 1999:6). Data 23 shows in the SL that the pronoun ‘they’ is not used to substitute for NS, TG and PDL in the SL when they are mentioned for the second time and so forth. This is also indicated by the use of the expression ‘the parties’ in the SL and the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">para</span><span class="font4"> (collective plural maker referring to people who comprise a group (Echols &amp;&nbsp;Shadily, 2008: 410) in the TL. However, in the TL when the three words are mentioned for the second time, the relative pronoun </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yang</span><span class="font4"> (who/that) is used to replace the antecedent NS, TG and PDL, allowing the two sentences in the SL to be translated into one sentence in the TL.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark45"></a>4. Conclusion</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">From what was discussed above, it can be concluded that the TP (Transposition Procedure) highly contributes to the structural adjustments in the translation of the English Memorandum of Understanding into Indonesian. It leads the translator to the dynamic equivalence, causing the translation product to be natural, acceptable and readable. The structural adjustments in the SL also show that the translator does her best to carry the drafter of the Memorandum of Understanding to the target reader. She attempts to adjust the SL to the context already familiar to the target reader and to help the target reader to understand what the SL contains. The target reader does not need to process the translation product in its original context.</span></p>
