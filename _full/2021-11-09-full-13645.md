<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">LADY GAGA’S ALBUM</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">“</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">THE FAME</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">”</span></p>

<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Makalah ini berjudul “Analysis of Figurative Language Used In The Song Lyrics of Lady Gaga’s Album “The Fame”. Ada dua tujuan dari penulisan yaitu, pertama adalah untuk mengidentifikasi jenis-jenis bahasa figurative yang terdapat dalam lirik lagu. Yang kedua adalah bertujuan untuk menganalisis makna dari bahasa figurative yang disampaikan dalam lirik lagu Lady Gaga. Data diambil dari album lagu Lady Gaga yang berjudul “The Fame”. Album ini dirilis pada tahun 2008. Teori-teori yang digunakan dalam menganalisis tulisan ini yaitu, pertama adalah teori yang diusulkan oleh K.L Knickerbocker &amp;&nbsp;H.Williard Reninger (1963) yang mengusulkan jenis-jenis bahasa figuratif seperti simile, metaphor, personification, synecdoche, metonymy, hyperbole, irony, paradox, dead metaphor, dan allusion. Teori kedua diusulkan oleh Leech (1974) yang mengusulkan makna-makna bahasa figurative yaitu stylistic meaning, affective meaning, reflective meaning, locative meaning, dan thematic meaning. Hasil penulisan ini menunjukkan bahwa jenis bahasa figuratif yang diusulkan oleh K.L Knickerbocker &amp;&nbsp;H.Williard Reninger (1963) yang digunakan pada lirik-lirik lagu dalam album ini adalah hyperbole dan metaphor. Selain itu hasil juga menunjukkan makna-makna bahasa figuratif yang diusulkan oleh Leech (1974) yang terdapat pada album lagu ini adalah connotative meaning. Mereka adalah jenis-jenis dan makna bahasa figuratif yang ditemukan dalam beberapa lirik lagu pada album ini.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Bahasa Figuratif, Hiperbola,Makna Konotasi</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">A song is a composition of voice or voices, performed by a singer or by musical instruments. The song writer in the fame album is Lady Gaga itself. There are many messages delivered in her songs and the language that is used to convey the messages in the song is figurative language, so the listener will be interested in listening to the song to obtain the purpose and the messages delivered in the song. Most of the song lyrics in her song use figurative language to convey the ideas as well as to make the effect of beauty so that the language or the words in the song are more interesting. Knickerbocker and Reninger (1963:366) stated that figurative languages are images used in particular way to explore the less known through the known. It can be a special repetition, arrangement or omission of words with literal meaning or a phrase with a specialized meaning not based on the literal meaning of the words in it. Figurative language will give more meaning to the words, beautify them, and emphasize their meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Due to the interest in the study of figurative language, this study focuses on figurative languages which are used mainly in the song lyrics of Lady Gaga. Those beautiful lyrics or those figurative languages would be useless if the listeners could not fully understand what the true meaning behind those song lyrics. In order to understand the meaning, it is important to learn how to interpret figurative language. Therefore, it is very interesting to analyze the figurative language that is found within the song lyrics more deeply.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Figurative Language</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Song lyric</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: Love Game intuition (4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Play the cards with Spades to start (5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Analysis</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: The sentence in line 5 contains dead metaphor. Dead metaphor is a metaphor which has lost its figurative meaning through endless use. (Knickerbocker and Reninger; 1963 : 368). The sentence is called dead metaphor because it give more stress to the thing which is talked about. The sentence “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Play the cards with Spades to start”</span><span class="font1"> shows the specific part of the card, which becomes the central part of the topic. The author seems to be playing on the original meaning of a spade, a shovel with which she could dig into susceptible man’s heart. Thus, she says </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“love game intuition”</span><span class="font1"> she wants to get inside his heart so she could get with him.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Song lyric</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: I'll play the one that's on his heart (7)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Analysis</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: The sentence in line 7 contains hyperbole expression. Hyperbole is a figure of an exaggeration used for special effect. (Knickerbocker and Reninger; 1963 : 367) This is showed by “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">play the one on his heart”</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Imagine there are no people in the world who can play spades card in someone’s heart in their real world. The word “play” is usually used for daily activities or for joking. Like playing football, playing video game, playing card, and etc. the author uses the word “play” to strengthen the lyric. On other hand, the sentence “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">play the one that’s on his heart</span><span class="font1">” could mean make someone fall in love.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The hyperbolic expression of this lyric contained of connotative meaning. As stated by Leech (1974: 14), connotative meaning cannot be translated literally, everyone will translate it differently depends on their culture, background, education, etc. we can see that no human in real world can play the cards in someone’s heart. It is impossible to do, and the meaning was also seems hard to described logically.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Song lyric</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: He can't read my poker face (11)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Analysis</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: This sentence contains dead metaphor because it gives more stress to the thing which is talked about (Knickerbocker and Reninger, 1963; 368). In the sentence “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">He can't read my poker face</span><span class="font1">” shows the specific part of the poker face, which becomes the central part of the topic. Poker face may mean that a face without expression, so how we can read the face without expression? It is unacceptable in our mind. The author here wants to show the expression of this sentence which may mean that we cannot understand the purpose that they have in their empty mind.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">It seems that the sentence contains beautiful words which can be seen from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">He can't read my poker face</span><span class="font1">, in the first paragraph, we can conclude that clearly it is a connotative meaning because it is unacceptable in our mind or simply illogical.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Song Lyric</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: I'm not lying; I'm just stunnin' with my love-glue-gunning (20)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Analysis</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">: In line 20, hyperbole is used in this sentence. Hyperbole is a figure of</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">an exaggeration used for special effect. (Knickerbocker and Reninger; 1963 : 367). The sentence that shows hyperbole is “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with my love-gluegunning”,</span><span class="font1"> as we know that love does not have a glue, but it has the characteristic as glue. The sentence in the song is greatly exaggerated. The author here wants to show the expression of this sentence which can mean that she does not lie with her feelings; she is amazing with its love. She wants to stick with her love</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">This hyperbolic expression contains connotative meaning. The hyperbole can be translated into many forms, as stated by Leech that connotative meaning is indeterminate and open in the same way as our knowledge and belief about the universe are open ended.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the types of figurative language found in the lyric of the Fame Album, we can conclude that there are ten types found in Lady Gaga songs. Hyperbole and metaphor are the most frequent figurative language which appear and are used in that album.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Most of the meanings of the figurative languages found in the songs are classified into connotative meaning because they cannot be translated literally and everyone will often interpret them differently depending on many factors. Only allusion which is stated to have stylistic meaning since this type can be translated literally but not everyone understands the meaning.</span></p>
