<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Detection of </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> and </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> at Bali Cattle Slaughterhouse by culture analysis and PCR</span></h1>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Deteksi Mycobacterium bovis dan Klebsiella pnewmoniae pada Rumah Potong Sapi Bali melalui Analisis kultur dan PCR</span></h2>
<h3><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>I Gede Oka Darsana<sup>1</sup>, I Nyoman Dibia<sup>2</sup>, Hapsari Mahatmi<sup>3*</sup></span></h3>
<p><span class="font3"><sup>1</sup>Magisterial Program of Veterinary Medicine, Udayana University, Denpasar, Bali <sup>2</sup>Animal Disease Investigation Centre, Denpasar, Bali</span></p>
<p><span class="font3"><sup>3</sup>Laboratory of Microbiology, Faculty of Veterinary Medicine, Denpasar, Bali </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">*</span><span class="font4">Corresponding author: </span><a href="mailto:hmahatmi@yahoo.co.id"><span class="font4">hmahatmi@yahoo.co.id</span></a></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Penelitian mengenai prevalensi bovine tuberculosis dan Klebsiella pneumoniae pada rumah potong sapi di Bali dilakukan di Rumah Poyong Pesanggrahan dan Mambal dari Bulan Januari sampai dengan Maret 2015. Pengamatan terhadap lesi dilakukan pada organ paruparu dan limfenodus yang selanjutnya diperiksa melalui analisis DNA untuk Mycobacterium bovis dan kultur untuk isolasi Klebsiella pneumoniae. Dari seluruh sampel, tidak ada sampel yang ditemukan lesi yang mengidikasikan menderita tuberculosis di kedua rumah potong hewan. Namun, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2"> teramati pada 1/4513 sampel (0,02%) hanya di Rumah Potong Sap Mambal. Penelitian ini merupakan laporan pertama adanya </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2"> di Bali. Karenanya, dibutuhkan kehati-hatian terhadap Klebsiellosis yang besifat zoonotik ini dan formulasi management kontrolnya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Kata kunci: sapi Bali, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2">, prevalensi</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRACT</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A study to determine the presence and prevalence of bovine tuberculosis and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2"> at cattle slaughterhouse in Bali was carried out in Pesanggaran and Mambal abattoirs from January to March 2015.The Lungs and lymph nodes were inspected for lesions and then examined through DNA analysis for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font2"> and culture for isolation of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2">. Of the samples examined, no one had lesions suggestive of tuberculosis in two abattoirs.However, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2"> was observed 1/4513 sample (0.02%) only in Mambal abattoir. This study is the first report of the presence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2"> in Bali. Therefore, a aware of this zoonotic Klebsiellosis and its control management formulation are need.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Keywords : Bali Cattle, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font2">, prevalence</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">Bali cattle plays an important role in supplying beef in Indonesia because it has good quality, high fertilization and low fat percentage (Merliana </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2014). The Bali cattle is considered to be well adapted to the country’s harsh environmental tropical conditions. Despite it is superior qualities this breed has weakness. The Bali cattle has a susceptible to Jembrana diseases and Malignant Catarrhal Fever. Another disease, such as Bovine tubercolosis (BTB) and Klebsiellosis until recent years there was a</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">little concern about these infection in bali cattle.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Bovine tuberculosis is caused by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font3"> (bovine tubercle bacillus) which is a member of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium tuberculosis</span><span class="font3"> complex. This disease is a chronic infectious disease and contagious zoonotic disease of domestic animals, wild animals, and humans. Sometimes this disease can be acute and progressive, specially on calves (Poeloengan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2014). And, Klebsiellosis is a disease that caused by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> bacteria (Rahmawati, 2009). </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3">is</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">an opportunistic pathogen on human and also animal (Younan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2013).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In Bali, there have been limited studies to determine the prevalence of Bovine tuberculosis and Klebsiellosis. According to Putra </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">. (2013), seroprevalence of bovine tuberculosis in bali cattle from Bangli region was 2.22%. Moreover, former report stated that Klebsiellosis event was found on the cattle lung samples that show the sign of pneumonia from cattle slaughterhouse in Gorontalo (Retnowati &amp;&nbsp;Nugroho, 2015).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This study was aimed to determine the prevalence of BTB at cattle slaughterhouse based on polymerase chain reaction (PCR) techniques and Klebsiellosis based on cell culture methods.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">MATERIALS AND METHODS</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>Sample</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">Samples were collected from Pesanggaran Abattoir, Denpasar and Mambal Abattoir, Badung. The selection of the cattle sampled at each abattoirs was strictly based on the clinical signs, following postmortem observation of typical granulomatous lesions of Bovine tuberculosis in the lungs or the lymph nodes.</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Bacterial Culture and Identification</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">Culture was conducted in category III containment laboratories. Samples were transported in refrigerated condition, and processed within 24h of collection. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> bacteria were tested by culturing on MacConkey media. Confirmatory testing of presumptive positives was by Gram colouring test, biochemical and sugar test.</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) Method</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">The tissue samples showing clinical signs of Bovine tuberculosis were collected and transported on ice pack to laboratory. Genomic DNA were extraction using </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">genomic DNA mini kit. DNA were amplified</span><span class="font3"> under standard conditions as described Geneaid Product Information. The primers were 5’-CAGGGATCCACCATGTTCTTAGCGGGT TG-3’ &nbsp;(forward) and 5’-TGGCGAATTT</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">CTTACTGTGCCGGGGG-3’ (reverse) (Nahar </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2011). The reaction was performed in a final volume of 25 μL. After an initial denaturation step (at 94°C, for 3 min), 40 amplification cycles were performed as follows: denaturation at 95°C for 15 second, annealing at 63°C for 15 sec, and extension at 72°C for 30 sec. A final extension was performed at 72°C for 15 min. The amplified PCR products were electrophoresed in 1.5% a g ar o s e g e l .</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Data Analysis</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">The obtained data were analyzed descriptive statistically using SPSS Program for Windows.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></p>

<h3><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Discussion</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">The result of PCR technique in this study confirmed negative for </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font3">. Refers to the epidemiology concept that a disease is due to the interaction of agent, host, and environment factors (Martin </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font3">, 1987). The negative result of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis </span><span class="font3">as the cause of BTB in this study can be overviewed from those 3 factors. Poeloengan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font3"> (2014) stated that the susceptible hosts of BTB infection is so wide in range, such as guinea pig, rabbit, mencit, hamster, monkey, horse, dog, cow, pig, cockatoo and fowl. Wild animal that is a permanent reservoir from the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font3"> infection is fox (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Meles-meles</span><span class="font3">) in Britain, possum (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Trichosurus Vulpecula</span><span class="font3">) in New Zealand and monkey in</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Indonesia (Poeloengan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2014). BTB agent have the ability to live a couple of days outside of its source (Tarmudji and Supar, 2008), this indicates that in the amount of time the BTB agent still have the ability to infected the host. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis </span><span class="font3">infection can spread rapidly towards the cattle especially through the aerosol inhale, from the cough or sneezing of the animal that have tuberkulosis or from the dust particle which contain the agent. The disease also spread rapidly in a very dense location of cattle (Cousins, 2001), or when a wild animal and cattle herd in the same field (Cosivi </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al., </span><span class="font3">1998). While the infection in human, it is usually through the drink of fresh milk and consuming the raw animal product. Besides, the farm worker that keep the animal inside the cage can increase the risk of the infection in aerosol way, from human to animal, or the opposite (Cosivi </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 1998).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The major finding in the present study is the presence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae in cattle. T</span><span class="font3">he animal that susceptible to Klebsiellosis infection caused by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> bacteria are buffalo and cow (Sayed dan Zaitoun, 2009), dog, monkey, guinea pigs, muskrats (Brisse </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2006). Horse and camel (Younan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2013). While the red swallow (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Milvus milvus</span><span class="font3">), Egypt vulture, Antarctic skua (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Catharacta spp</span><span class="font3">.), Red-billed chough (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Pyrrhocorax pyrrhocorax</span><span class="font3">), wild turkeys (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Cathartes aura</span><span class="font3">), peregrinefalcon (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Falco peregrinus</span><span class="font3">) were suspected as the carrier agent of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> (Sharma </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.,</span><span class="font3"> 2014).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Factors that may influence the presence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> in cattle in Bali are due to environmental factor. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3"> can grow under the aerob condition at a temperature of 12-43ºC with the optimal growth at a temperature of 35-37ºC (Rahmawati, 2009). Besides, the environmental factor that causing the Klebsiellosis such as the stress of carriage, the polluted water and environment from the waste of the paper factory and wood finishing textile, waste of the plants product and sugar cane (Brisse </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2006). Although the prevalence of Klebsiellosis is small, a proper</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">postmortem inspection should be practiced effectively at the abattoir, before taking out beef to the public. Besides, the zoonotic agent, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae</span><span class="font3">, can also cause an economical loss on Bali cattle.</span></p>
<h3><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>CONCLUSION</span></h3>
<p><span class="font3">The prevalence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Klebsiella pneumoniae </span><span class="font3">in cattle was very low (0.02%). </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mycobacterium bovis</span><span class="font3"> was not found in cattle slaughterhoused based on PCR technique.</span></p>
