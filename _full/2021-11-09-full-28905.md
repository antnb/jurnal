<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan penelitian ini adalah untuk mendeskripsikan tipe-tipe pergeseran dalam penerjemahan. Teori “transposisi dan modulasi” oleh Blum-Kulka (Venuty 2004:141) digunakan untuk mengidentifikasi dua prinsip padanan dalam penerjemahan. Fokus penelitian ini adalah unit terjemahan berupa kata, frasa atau klausa. Unsur leksikal dari makna kiasan dikutip dari sebuah novel berbahasa Inggris ʻLone Eagleʼ yang diterjemahkan ke Bahasa Indonesia ʻCinta Pertamaʼ. Metode deskriptif kualitatif digunakan untuk menganalisa data. Hasil penelitian ini adalah perbedaan dua sistem linguistik dan budaya yang menyebabkan pergeseran dalam penerjemahan tidak dapat dihindari. Ada dua tipe pergeseran dalam penerjemahan, yaitu: transposisi yang identik dengan pergeseran kategori gramatikal dan modulasi yang identik dengan pergeseran sudut pandang budaya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kunci: pergeseran, transposisi, modulasi.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Translation always involves two languages in two cultures. It is more difficult when the two languages are not in the same language families. There will be uniqueness of a certain culture in a source language which is not always found in the target language vocabulary. The cultural specific terms might result in different vocabularies and ways of saying something. However, the different expressions of the two languages in the translated texts can always be searched either cohesively through the linguistic forms or coherently through the ways people in a culture express the concept of meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It is known that an ideal translation should be accurate and at the same time intelligible. However, a translator is always facing a dilemma. In one hand as a transposition shift, he has to keep or make a necessary adjustment of forms for the accuracy of the meaning. In the other hand, as a modulation shift, he has to change the point of view for the reader.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A simile </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">as white as snow</span><span class="font2"> can be translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">seputih salju</span><span class="font2"> for the sake of accuracy of form although some of the TL readers might not familiar with the characteristics of snow. The other alternative is that it can be translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">seputih kapas</span><span class="font2"> by neglecting the form of snow to make the text comprehensible by the reader. A matter of choice of word in translation is much influenced by the two principles orientations in translation either target or source language orientations.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Objectives</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The objectives of the study were:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe the types of transposition shift of lexical items of figurative sense in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Lone Eagle</span><span class="font2"> and its translation in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cinta Pertama.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the types of modulation shift of lexical items of figurative sense in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Lone Eagle</span><span class="font2"> and its translation in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cinta Pertama.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Methods</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data was taken from an English best seller novel, as the source language (SL), entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Lone Eagle</span><span class="font2"> written by Danielle Steel, 2001 published by Bantam Press, Germany. The Indonesian novel, as the target language (TL), entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cinta Pertama </span><span class="font2">translated by Gita Yuliani K, 2002 published by PT. Gramedia Pustaka Utama, Jakarta.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There were two reasons why the bilingual novels were chosen. First, as a narrative text the current events in the novel were told orderly so it would be easy to understand. Second, as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Lone eagle</span><span class="font2"> was a love story novel, it talked a lot about human feelings and emotions between two lovers. The ways the author presenting the dialogue was rich with kinds of figures of speech.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methods and Techniques of Collecting Data</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The method used was library research method. Meanwhile, the techniques were done in two steps. First, while reading comprehensively the source language text, the researcher observes the lexical items containing the forms of figurative sense. At the same time the translated text was compared to assure that they were equivalent. Second, the figurative senses which had been observed and noted down, then classified.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Techniques of Analyzing Data</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">A descriptive qualitative method was used to analyze the translated shifts of figurative senses. First, the collected data were selected to a certain number needed for the type of data presentation. Second, the selected data were described deeply for their equivalence from the different linguistics systems and different point of view of two languages and cultures.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">As a transposition shift in translation, there was a TL parallel grammatical category change. However, as a modulation shift there was parallel concepts change in point of view.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Transposition Shifts of the Figurative Senses Applied in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Lone Eagle </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">and its Translation in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Cinta Pertama</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h2><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Structural Shifts</span></h2></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">M &nbsp;&nbsp;H</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: giant bird</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">H &nbsp;&nbsp;M</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: burung raksasa</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The SL </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">giant bird</span><span class="font2"> was translated into TL became </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">burung raksaksa.</span><span class="font2"> A phrase combination of two words: </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">giant</span><span class="font2"> modifier (M) and </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bird</span><span class="font2"> head (H) in the SL phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">giant bird</span><span class="font2"> each had a function. The first element </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">giant</span><span class="font2"> modified the second element </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bird</span><span class="font2">. Such structural construction was called modifier-head phrase (M-H). However, the structural shift had to be made to follow the TL linguistic system, namely, head-modifier phrase</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(H-M) as in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">burung raksasa.</span><span class="font2"> From the reversed of the SL construction M-H became H-</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">M in TL, it was indicated as structure shift.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unit Shifts</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The ways the bundle of the meaning unpacked differently, had made changes the unit shift from: (1) a low to a higher unit, or (2) a high to a lower unit.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.1.2.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From a Low to Higher Unit</span></h2>

<p><span class="font2">The base of </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">electricity</span><span class="font2"> is </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">electric</span><span class="font2">. Literal or word for word translation of </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">electric</span><span class="font2"> is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">listrik</span><span class="font2"> in the TL. Morover, it was given suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ity</span><span class="font2"> became </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">electricity</span><span class="font2">. From the addition of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">aliran</span><span class="font2"> in TL, it could be classified as unit shift from a low to higher unit.</span></p>

Therefore, it was indicated as unit shift from a high to lower unit.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.1.3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Class Shifts</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">SL: </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">a promise</span><span class="font2"> (NOUN)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">berjanji</span><span class="font2"> (VERB)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">A promise</span><span class="font2"> in SL was shifted into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">berjanji</span><span class="font2"> in TL</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2">The word class </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">promise</span><span class="font2"> as a noun in the SL had changed into word class </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">berjanji</span><span class="font2"> as a verb in the TL. The change of SL noun into TL verb is classified as class shift.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.2 . Types of Modulation Shifts of the Figurative Senses Applied in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Lone Eagle</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> and its Translation in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Cinta Pertama</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the examples below, the difference of two cultures had changed the focus of the cognitive point of view.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lexical Modulation</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The cognitive point of view of vocabulary of the SL culture might different from the TL as could be seen in the following new mode of expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: and he was already </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">hungry</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tetapi ia sudah </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">haus</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The SL </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">and he was already hungry</span><span class="font2"> was translatd into new expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tetapi ia sudah haus</span><span class="font2"> in the TL. A hyperbole of the SL lexical item </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">hungry</span><span class="font2"> which means need, desire to eat was translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">haus</span><span class="font2"> which means need, desire to drink. Eventhough </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">hungry</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">haus</span><span class="font2"> literally are not the same; however, in this context it indicated the same meaning that is a desire to be with someone. The TL different cognitive point of view was exaggerated for more said than the fact.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>5.2.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Structural Modulation</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It was common that the construction in the SL needs to be translated with a TL new construction natural form.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: bottom</span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">less</span><span class="font2"> pool of grief</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kolam kesedihan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">yang sangat dalam</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The suffix – </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">less</span><span class="font2"> in the SL </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bottomless</span><span class="font2"> literally means </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">without bottom</span><span class="font2"> or </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">very deep </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tanpa dasar</span><span class="font2"> or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">sangat dalam</span><span class="font2">. Structurally, in the SL the suffix – </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">less</span><span class="font2"> in </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bottomless </span><span class="font2">preceding a noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">pool of grief</span><span class="font2">, while in TL it was changed into a phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">sangat dalam</span><span class="font2"> following a noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">pool of grief</span><span class="font2">. That is to say that the </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bottomless pool of grief</span><span class="font2"> can be expressed descriptively in another way into the TL </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kolam kesedihan yang sangat dalam</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Message Modulation</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The different cultural cognitive point of view of the TL changes the SL message form into a natural TL message form of expression.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">Shame on you</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">Keterlaluan sekali</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The SL </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">shame on you</span><span class="font2"> means you should be ashamed of yourself (Horby, 1980: 784), or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tak tahu malu</span><span class="font2"> (Echol, MJ and Shadily, H.: 1979: 518). Meanwhile the TL </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">keterlaluan</span><span class="font2"> might mean </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">excessive</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">far too much</span><span class="font2"> (Echol, MJ and Shadily, H.: 2001: 325). Neither lexically nor structurally linked the two linguistic forms. Apparently, the back translation from TL to SL does not match due to the covert relationship. However, the reader’s shared knowledge might bring him to mean the same nuance of meaning that someone should not do something that disgraced him.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark35"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark36"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It was found in the study that the different linguistics systems had affected translation. It was done by changing the target language surface structure grammatical category which was so called transposition. It could be subdivided into structural shift, unit shift, and class shift. Meanwhile, the different cultures also had affected translation. It was done by changing in point of view which was so called modulation. It could be subdivided into lexical modulation, structural modulation, and message modulation. Both were applied in the translation for the closest natural equivalent.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">To preserve the meaning of the figurative senses of the two languages which were not in the same families had made shifts in translation were unavoidable. It was due to the two different linguistics system and cultures.</span></p>
