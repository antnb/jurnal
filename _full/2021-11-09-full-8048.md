<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini berjudul &quot;&nbsp;The Grammatical Cohesion and Context of Situation in the Articles of Hot English Magazine and Hello Bali Magazine&quot;. Tujuan penelitian dalam jurnal ini adalah untuk menganalisis tipe kohesi gramatikal dan konteks situasi yang di temukan dalam majalah yang berjudul Hot English dan Hello Bali. Teori yang digunakan dalam jurnal ini adalah teori konteks dan kohesi yang dikemukakan oleh Halliday and Hasan (1985 dan 1976) dan juga teori dari Nunan (1993). Dalam menganalisis jurnal metode yang digunakan adalah metode kualitatif. Pembahasan data diawali dengan membaca, memahami, memberi tanda dan mencatat data yang berhubungan dengan topik. Hasil dari pembahasan menunjukkan bahwa di artikel-artikel majalah yang digunakan memiliki tiga fitur konteks situasi yaitu field, tenor dan modus. Dan menemukan tipe dari kohesi gramatikal yaitu referensi, substitusi dan konjungsi.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kunci : kohesi gramatikal dan konteks situasi</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Language becomes functional if it is able to fulfill people's need in society effectively. The term functional means language that is doing some jobs in some context. It means that every language that is playing some parts in a context of situation is called text.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Grammatical cohesion is a kind of cohesion that is the meaning relation, which is performed through grammar.This study is focused on the analysis of</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">types of grammatical cohesion including reference, substitusion, ellipsis, and conjunction, and the context of situation including field, tenor, mode. This study includes the comparison of the use of grammatical cohesion and context of situation between those two articles with different magazines.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the background above, the problems of the study are :</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What was the context of situation in the articles of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine like?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of grammatical cohesion were used in the articles of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English </span><span class="font1">magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Related to the problems of the study, the general aims of the study is to analyze the language used in the articles of magazines according to the context of situation, and to find out the three components of the context of situation namely field, tenor and mode and types of Grammatical Cohesion in the articles of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font1">The method that is used to conduct a research project, has a very important role in a scientific writing, because it is influental to the validity of the result of the research itself. It contains data source, method and technique of collecting data, and method and technique of analyzing data.The research method used in this study is qualitative research.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data source were taken from two different magazines they are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine no.123 edition June 2012, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine, vol. 17, no. 10 edition October 2012. The reason in choosing these articles as the data source, is that the writer wants to find grammatical cohesion and context of situation in</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine. They are tourist magazines functionery to promote Balinese culture.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font1">The technique of analyzing data applied in this study was qualitative method. First, every data was presented in order to find out the detail of the message in every paragraph. Second, the data were selected to find appropriate data. Then the data were analyzed of using the theory of cohesion according to Halliday (1976), to know the cohesiveness of the text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Grammatical Cohesion and Context of Situation in the Articles of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Magazine.</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Context of Situation</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The context of situation consists of three dimensions, they are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">field</span><span class="font1"> (what the text is all about), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tenor</span><span class="font1"> (the role of the participants), and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mode</span><span class="font1"> (the medium or the channel of the text).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark23"></a>a.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Field</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The articles in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine have three features of context of situation namely </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">field</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tenor</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mode</span><span class="font1">. The </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">field</span><span class="font1"> of the first text is about the Balinese myths and legends. It describes the story of Kbo Iwa and the story of Ramayana, and the second text is about tourist objects which refers to Nusa Penida island.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tenor</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Tenor in the first text were involved some participants who are in charge namely journalist, editor, publisher, and readers. The journalist officially (The team of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine). The participants involved in the second text are the journalist, writer (Katy robert) and the team of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine, and also the readers.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mode</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the first text, The text consists of long sentences in paragraph. The text consists of forty-four and seven paragraphs. The second text consists of ten paragraphs and fifty one sentences, which have long sentences.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Grammatical Cohesion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Grammatical cohesion is a kind of cohesion that is performed through relation in grammar. According to Halliday and Hasan (1976) , the types of grammatical cohesion are divided into four items; they are reference, substitution,ellipsis,and conjuction.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>a. Reference</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Personal Reference</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font1"> would help the balinese people build temples and villages, digditches and cut terraces out of the hillsides for rice paddies&quot; (S.4)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font1"> refers anaphorically to Kbo Iwa. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font1"> is a personal reference, and belongs to personal pronouns.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">II. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;While renowned for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">its</span><span class="font1"> magnificent diving trenches, the sightings of Mola</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Mola, ......&quot;(S.5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Its</span><span class="font1"> refers anaphorically to Nusa Penida. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Its</span><span class="font1"> is a personal reference, and is a possessive pronoun.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Demonstrative Reference</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;One year </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there</span><span class="font1"> was a very poor harvest of food and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1"> Balinese people could barely feed themselves, much less a giant.&quot;(S.7)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">There</span><span class="font1"> refers cataphorically to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there was a very poor harvest of food. There</span><span class="font1"> is demonstrative reference, and it is a circumstantial demonstrative. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The</span><span class="font1"> refers cataphorically to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the Balinese people could barely feed themselves, much less a giant. The</span><span class="font1"> is demonstrative reference and it is classified as neutral.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Comparative Reference</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;It is believed that there was once a giant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">as</span><span class="font1"> tall </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">as</span><span class="font1"> a mountain who roamed the island,....&quot; (S.3)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">&quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">As</span><span class="font1"> is comperative reference that expresses the particular comperative.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;” </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So</span><span class="font1"> while bargaining, do not drive too hard.&quot; (S.26)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So</span><span class="font1"> is comparative reference that expresses a particular comparative.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Substitution</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;During </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1"> battle, Rama sped towards Rawana shooting arrows as he ran.” (S.37)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">One</span><span class="font1"> in the three sentences above can be classified as substitution. It can be noted that not all occurrences of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1"> are instances of substitution.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;..........., and many international companies operate in associatiation with</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">reputable local </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ones</span><span class="font1">.” (S.35)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Ones</span><span class="font1"> refers to nominal substitution, it is also related to category of general nouns.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conjunction</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Additive Conjunction</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;The lime set around the giant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1"> when he woke up he was unable to escape.&quot;(S.1)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This will take you to some stunning locations </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1"> back on a boat before sundown.” (S.16)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">And</span><span class="font1"> in the two sentences above belongs to additive conjunction. Its functioned is to add new information to what has been mentioned before.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Adversative conjunction</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;..........., his appetite was enormous, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font1">, and there was never</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">enough food to satisfy him.” (S.5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">However</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence above is adversative cunjunction. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">However</span><span class="font1"> stands for relation between those two sentences, which are contrasted in meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">However</span><span class="font1">, they concluded that because it had no graveyard, it may have had purposes beyond that of a pura dalem,....&quot;(S.40)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">However</span><span class="font1"> can be grouped into adversative conjunction.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Causal conjunction</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;This angered Kbo Iwo </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> much that he rampaged across the island,.....&quot; (S.8)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence above belongs to conjunction with causal relationship. It indicates that there is specific information in this sentence as a result of the previous sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;Not </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> long ago Penida was a penal colony, and you didn't have to do much to end up there.&quot;(S.8)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence above belongs to conjunction with causal relationship. It indicates that there is specific information in this sentence as a result of the previous sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">&gt; </span><span class="font1">Temporal conjunction</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">I.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;The Balinese people </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font1"> asked him to build a well, which he started digging.&quot; (S.12)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Then</span><span class="font1"> is a temporal conjunction which occurs to express the presence of the following event after the previous one.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">II.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;&nbsp;Even two years ago if you wanted a could beer you had to buy it from the markets or a shop, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font1"> find a place with ice and store your bottle there until you could take it to the only cafe on the sea front to drink it.&quot; (S.7)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Then</span><span class="font1"> is a temporal conjunction which occurs to express the presence of the following event after the previous one.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis in previous chapter about grammatical cohesion and context of situation, the conclusion can be drawn as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The articles of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hot English</span><span class="font1"> magazine and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hello Bali</span><span class="font1"> magazine have three features of context of situation namely </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">field</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tenor</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mode</span><span class="font1">. Every text also has grammatical cohesion. The types of grammatical cohesion in the articles include reference, substitution, and conjunction. There is no ellipsis found in both texts. The appearance of the reference in both texts is marked by </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">personal ,demonstrative and comparative reference</span><span class="font1">. The </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Personal reference</span><span class="font1"> involves the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">personal pronouns </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">it ,they, he, him, you,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">possessive determiners</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, their, his, </span><span class="font1">and also </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">possessive pronouns </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">its.</span><span class="font1"> The </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Demonstrative reference</span><span class="font1"> in both texts is performed by the definite articles </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the, that and there</span><span class="font1">, applied in the texts as anaphoric and cataphoric. The </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Comparative reference </span><span class="font1">in both texts is performed by the definite articles </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">as.</span><span class="font1"> The only </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">substitutions </span><span class="font1">found are </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">nominal substitution </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">verbal substitution </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font1">. And the last, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">conjunctions</span><span class="font1"> found were: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">additive</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">adversative, causal</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">temporal</span><span class="font1">. The </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Additive conjunction</span><span class="font1"> is marked by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1"> in both texts.
