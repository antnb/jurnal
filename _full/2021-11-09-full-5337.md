<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">THE USE OF DEIXIS IN THE TRANSCRIPTION OF </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">THE INTERVIEW</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">BETWEEN OPRAH WINFREY, PRESIDENT BARACK OBAMA AND FIRST LADY MICHELLE OBAMA IN CHICAGO, MAY 2<sup>ND</sup>, 2011</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">By:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Sherly Marliana</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">English Department</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Faculty of Letters, Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Deixis adalah bagian dari cabang ilmu pragmatik yang membahas hubungan antara bahasa dan konteks yang tercermin dalam struktur bahasa itu sendiri. Deixis mengacu pada penanda bahasa yang kontekstual serta memiliki fitur grammatikal dalam sebuah konteks ujaran dalam percakapan dan interpretasi dari percakapan tersebut serta bergantung pada analisa konteks ujaran. Dalam sebuah diskusi tanya-jawab seperti wawancara dalam sebuah acara pertunjukan di televisi, terdapat deiksis yang menggunakan teori Levinson (1983). Adapun tujuan dalam penelitian ini yaitu untuk mengidentifikasi dan mengkategorikan jenis-jenis deiksis yang terdapat dalam transkripsi wawancara antara Oprah Winfrey, Presiden Barack Obama dan Ibu Negara Michelle Obama di Chicago, 02 Mei 2011, sehingga dapat menyajikan presentase dari tanda-tanda deiksis yang ditemukan dalam transkipsi wawancara tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: deiksis, deiktik, wawancara, ujaran</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The best way to understand the relationship between language and context is reflected in the structures of language themselves, through the phenomenon of deixis. Basically, deixis is concerned with the way in which languages encode or grammaticalize features of the context of utterance or speech event, and thus is also concerned with the interpretation of utterances which depends on the analysis of that context of utterance (Levinson, 1983).</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the background above, I chose the transcription of the interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, May 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2011. The interview was so interesting to analyze because the content of the interview is useful to us as social beings to keep the world peaceful and love in humanity life and in this interview there are a lot of Deixis. However, the results of the study will have important value as they can be used in the analysis of narrative text using Deixis theory.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">To what extent do the Deixis were used in the transcription of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, May 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2011</span><span class="font1">?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">To describe to what extent Deixis were used in the transcription of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, May 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2011.</span><span class="font1"> In addition, to find out what types of deixis were more dominated in the transcription.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this study, the data source is the transcription of the interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama because Oprah Winfrey Talk Show is a popular talk show produced by Harpo, Inc and broadcast by CBS International Broadcaster, Chicago. The show is popular because it always raises interesting (but still ‘real’) topic to talk about. Oprah Winfrey, known as The Queen of Talk Show, hosts it. She is able to conduct interactive and ‘natural’ interviews, with regular people or celebrities, politicians, and even the President.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The method applied in this study was documentation method. This was done by downloading the interview video of Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, 2<sup>nd</sup> May, 2011 from the internet.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">a. Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In analyzing the data, the methods used were qualitative-descriptive-method and quantitative method. The analysis was only focused on some sentences which were categorized based on the proposed deixis types and the use of the deictic markers. Next, it was continued by classifying those expressions based on the types of deixis. After the deictic markers were found, it was found related discourse in previous or following text. Each deictic reference had a function as the indicator of related referents. Those were all classified by Levinson, in terms of the types of deixis they belonged to and were also analyzed based on the anaphoric and cataphoric types. The quantitative method was used to analyze the frequencies of the data especially the frequencies of the deictic markers occurring in the interview. The percentages of deixis frequency as the final result were counted by dividing the number of specific types of deixis and total of deixis times 100%.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Deixis in The Transcription of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, May 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2011</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Person Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In person deixis is concerned the encoding roles of participants in the speech event in which the utterance is delivered. It is generally known as pronouns.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Person Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">WINFREY: I </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">am so honored that in these remaining days of the &quot;Oprah&quot; show they chose to be here.</span><span class="font1"> (p.1, no.1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">At first, it may seem that </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font1"> refers to someone and speaker.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">According to Levinson, this reference was categorized as singular</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">pronoun of the first person. This is the deictic element, in which the information signals who the speaker is.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font1"> refers to the role of the first person, in which the utterance is narrated by one person. Therefore, it is tied to the speaker of this transcription named Oprah Winfrey, as a host of this show.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Second Person Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">WINFREY</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">: And now the both of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">you </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sit here….</span><span class="font1"> (p.11, no.170)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Here </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font1"> is the reference to person identified as addressee, which is observable in the conversation between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and Michelle Obama. Oprah encoded the presence of the audience or the viewers using deictic reference. Oprah was delighted that the guests were coming and could have conversation in the studio.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Third Person Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">MICHELLE: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">he </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">can't just go in and say, health care shall be.</span><span class="font1"> (p.7, no.106)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The personal pronoun </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">he</span><span class="font1"> above is categorized as the third person deixis, which is used by considering the gender of the addressees, especially for male. Here </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">he</span><span class="font1"> refers to President Barack Obama.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">“</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">MICHELLE: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It can be painful to watch. But, you know, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">Barack was in the Senate for many years</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, and I think he had a taste of how challenging it is when you work in a democracy, and that's one of the beauties of our country. We have a democracy, which means -- I always tease people, Barack is not a dictator. And that's a good thing.”</span><span class="font1"> (p.7, no.106)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">According to the quoted text above, this deictic phenomenon can be categorized as an anaphoric deictic, in which all the referent were described in the previous discourse.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Time Deixis</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Time deixis is reference to time relative to a temporal reference point. This point is the moment of speech event.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">WALTER</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">: I was on the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">&quot;Today&quot; </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">show then. And she sat the way I sat and she looked down at notes and so forth.</span><span class="font1"> (p.22, no.319)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Here </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“Today”</span><span class="font1"> is used by the speaker (</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Walter</span><span class="font1">) to show the situation, which refers to that time, when Oprah introduced herself in the video.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Place Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Place deixis is concerned the encoding of spatial locations relative to the location of the participants in the speech event. It refers to the words indicating locations for example </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there, yonder, etc.</span><span class="font1"> which are reflected in demonstratives like </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">this</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1">, or adverbs like </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there</span><span class="font1">, where spatial locations are indicated by the place of speaking.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">WINFREY: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">And I am so honored that in these remaining days of the &quot;Oprah&quot; show they chose to be </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> (p.1, no.1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Here</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence above contains an adverb of place, which is used for demonstrating such place that is relatively close to the speaker and also it is used to demonstrate the place where the show held.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discourse Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Discourse Deixis is concerned with the encoding reference to portions of the unfolding discourse in which the utterance (which include the text referring expression) is located. The analysis to the transcript can be presented as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">WINFREY</span><span class="font1">: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">So let’s all just take </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">this </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">in for a moment. In 25 years, we have never had the leader of the free world, a sitting president, and First Lady together on our stage</span><span class="font1">. (p.1, no. 1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the sentence above it can be seen that the reference </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">this</span><span class="font1"> is related to some facts. It shows that </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">this</span><span class="font1"> refers to the same referent as some prior terms.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">“</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">WINFREY: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The presidential motorcade just pulled into our studios on Carpenter Street. Everybody in the building has been screened and cleared for what is an historic moment for our show.</span><span class="font1">” (p.1, no.1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the previous text, it can be seen that the reference used as reference of the situations when President Barack Obama and First Lady came to the show, and many people crowded around the studios. Therefore, it is categorized as discourse deictic which is used to refer the prior statement made by the sentence, not to the sentence, in this case, the situation when President Barack Obama was coming.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Social Deixis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Social Deixis is concerned the encoding of social distinctions that are relative to participant-roles, particularly aspects of the social relationship holding between the speaker and the addressee(s) or speaker and some referent. The aspect of language structure that encodes the social identities of participants or the social relationship between them or between one of them and persons and entities referred to.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mr. President</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">WINFREY</span><span class="font1">: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Thank you, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Mr. President.</span><span class="font1"> (p.23, no.325)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on Oxford Dictionary, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">President</span><span class="font1"> is the leader of a Republic, especially the United States. There are two types of social deixis relational and absolute. Relational social deixis is where the form of word used indicates the relative social status of the addressee. By contrast, absolute deixis are forms uniformly attached to a social role. By the sentence above, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Mr. President</span><span class="font1"> can be categorized as the absolute deixis.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First Lady</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">WINFREY</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">: Please welcome home President Barack Obama and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">First Lady </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Michelle Obama. (APPLAUSE)</span><span class="font1"> (p.1, no.1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the Oxford Dictionary, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Lady</span><span class="font1"> is a word used to mean “woman” that some people, especially older people, consider more polite. </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“First Lady”</span><span class="font1"> means the wife of the leader (President in USA). Therefore, it can be said </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“First Lady”</span><span class="font1"> can be categorized as the absolute deixis.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.2 Analysis of Deictic Markers Distributions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In analyzing deictic markers distribution in this transcription, the analysis was focused only on the person deixis, time deixis, place deixis and discourse deixis. The analysis was focused on those types of deixis whereas the elements have their own specific markers that have been grammaticalized and lexicalized. Meanwhile, the social deixis have no markers of their own, the elements were identified only by looking at social interaction between speaker, addressee and by stander.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>Percentages of Types Deixis</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">The highest frequencies of deixis are : person deixis </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">68, 07 % </span><span class="font1">and then followed by discourse deixis </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">28, 48 %</span><span class="font1">, time deixis </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">2, 8 %</span><span class="font1">, place deixis </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">0, 43 % </span><span class="font1">and the last social deixis </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">0, 22 %</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to the analysis in the transcription of the interview between Oprah Winfrey, President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama in Chicago, May 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2011 could be found the Deixis elements, which were divided into several types of Deixis marked by deictic markers. The types of deixis in the transcription that can be found; they are person deixis, time deixis, place deixis, discourse deixis and social deixis. Person deixis is concerned with the encoding role of the participants which is generally indicated by pronouns establishing anaphoric or cataphoric relations such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I, He, She, We, They,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">You</span><span class="font1">. For the time deixis, the transcription used reference encoding time such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">two and a half years ago, today and now.</span><span class="font1"> For the place deixis, this transcription mostly used locative adverbs of place such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there</span><span class="font1">. This</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">transcription also has discourse deixis elements such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It, this/these, that/those</span><span class="font1">. For the social deixis, this novel used some terms for encoding the social relationship status, such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Mr. President and First Lady.</span></p>
