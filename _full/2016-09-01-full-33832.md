<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Mengetahui maksud atau tujuan dari sebuah ujaran dalam percakapan merupakan aspek yang penting dalam berkomunikasi .Dalam menganalisis makna atau maksud dari suatu ujaran, terutama dalam bahasa inggris, sangat penting untuk mengetahui fungsi dan konteks dari percakapan tersebut. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui tipe tindak ilokusi yang ditemukan di film “Twelve Years a Slave”. Maksud atau tujuan dari ujaran yang ditemukan diinterpretasikan menurut teori tindak tutur yang dikemukakan oleh Searle (1979) dan teori konteks situasi yang dikemukakan oleh Halliday (1989). Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukan bahwa ada lima tipe tindak ilokusi, yaitu “representatif”, “direktif”, “ekspresif”, “komisif” dan “deklarasi”. Maksud atau tujuan dari tipe tindak ilokusi tersebut adalah “mendesak, meyakinkan dan menuntut”, “memerintah dan meminta”, “mengancam dan menjanjikan”, “memberi selamat dan menyatakan ketidaksukaan”, serta “mengucilkan dan menghukum&quot;.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Kata Kunci: makna, ilokusi, konteks.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Identify the intention of an utterance in communication is important in order to achieve a communicative communication. In doing a conversation, the speaker will always try to convey information, ideas or thoughts to the hearer. Hence, it is important to study about the intention of an utterance by analyzing the function and the situational context of communication.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The problems formulated in this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of illocutionary act are found in “Twelve Years a Slave” movie?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is the intended meaning of the illocutionary act in the movie “Twelve Years a Slave”?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The aims of this study are as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To find out types of illocutionary act in “Twelve Years a Slave” movie</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To find out types of illocutionary act in “Twelve Years a Slave” movie</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The data used in this study was taken from the movie Twelve Years a Slave. The analysis was focus on the utterances of the characters and the context in which the conversation is happened.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In collecting the data, the study used documentation method. The techniques were done by watching and reading the movie scripts. Then, the utterances were classified based on the situational context and illocutionary act.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In analyzing the data, qualitative method was used in this study, with some steps of analyzing the data. First, the illocutionary acts were analyze by using the Speech Acts theory, specifically Illocutionary act proposed by Searle (1979). This analysis was supported by the analysis of situational context proposed by Halliday (1989). Then, the intention of utterances were interpreted based on the analysis of situational context on lllocutionary Acts.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">To analyze the intended meaning found in “Twelve Years a Slave” movie, it is important to find out the function of its utterance by using Speech Act theory proposed by Searle (1979) and Situational context theory by Halliday (1989).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Representatives Illocutionary Acts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Representatives illocutionary acts are types of act to state what the speaker believes to be the case or not. It is an act that represents what the speaker believes. The examples of representatives illocutionary acts are;</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark15"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Solomon : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Master Tibeats wants to whip me for using the nails you</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>give me</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of insisting</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark18"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Solomon : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">And I promise you - I promise upon my liberation I</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>will have satisfaction for this wrong.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of claiming</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Radburn : Rags and Tetters</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of asserting</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Directive Illocutionary Acts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Directive illocutionary acts are types of act to ask the addressee to do something. It is an act that used by the speaker to requests the hearer to carry out some actions or to bring out some stated of affair.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark21"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Solomon : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Master Ford, you must know; I am not a slave.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of ordering</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark23"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Parker &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">He is Solomon Northup.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of commanding</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark25"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Tibeats &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">It's a scheme. Plenty of engineers have schemed</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>similarly. The passes are too tight</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of ordering</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Commisives Illocutionary Acts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Commisive illocutionary acts are types of act to commit the speaker to future action. It had the purpose to commit the speaker to bring about some states of affairs</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Tibeats : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">My name is John Tibeats, William Ford’s chief</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">carpenter. You will refer to me as master</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of threatening</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark28"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Brown &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Forty-three dollars. All to you.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of promising</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Tibeats &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Make them boards flush</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of threatening</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Expressives Illocutionary Acts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Expressive illocutionary acts are types of act used to express the psychological states which can be statements of pleasure, pain, likes, dislike, joy or sorrow.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark30"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Ford &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Platt, you are a marvel</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of congratulating</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark32"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Solomon &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Eliza, Eliza, Stop! Stop it!</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of expressing dislike</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark34"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Patsey &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Do it Platt. Don’t stop until I’m dead</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of expressing dislike</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Declarations Illocutionary Acts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Declarations illocutionary acts are types of act used to changes the world via the speaker utterances.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark35"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark36"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Mrs. Epps : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">There’ll be none for you, Patsey</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of excommunicating</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark37"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark38"></a>• &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font4">Mr. Epps : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">I will rid myself of yah well before I do away with her!</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The utterance above has the intended meaning of sentencing</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark39"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark40"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">It can be concluded that all types of Illocutionary Acts are found in this study, such as representative, with the intended meaning of insisting, asserting and claiming. The next is directive, with the intended meaning of commanding and ordering. Besides, commisive is also found in this study with the intended meaning of threatening and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">promising. Then, there is expressive, with the intended meaning of congratulating and expressing of attitude. The last is declaration, with the intended meaning of excommunicating and sentencing.</span></p>
