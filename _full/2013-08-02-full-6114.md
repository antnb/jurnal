<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini membahas tentang bentuk, fungsi dan posisi dari adverbia tingkatan yang ada pada media cetak berbahasa Inggris. Sumber data yang digunakan dalam skripsi ini adalah artikel yang berjudul kehidupan nyata di majalah Cosmopolitan. Data ini dikumpulkan dengan beberapa tahap dan dianalisa secara rinci menggunakan kajian teori dari Huddleston (1984) dalam buku yang berjudul Introduction to the Grammar of English, Quirk et al (1973) dalam buku yang berjudul A University Grammar of English dan Alter (1993) dalam buku yang berjudul Essential English Usage and Grammar 5.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Analisa dalam skripsi ini ditemukan bahwa adverbia tingkatan memiliki beberapa perbedaan dalam hal bentuk, fungsi, dan posisi. Mengenai bentuk, ada kata adverbia tingkatan memiliki bentuk murni seperti </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">very, too, only, quite, almost, enough, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dll. Sebagian besar adverbia tingkatan dibentuk dengan menambahkan akhiran -ly dari kata sifat melalui proses sufiksasi. Contohnya: kata sifat </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">complete </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">+ akhiran (-ly) menjadi </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">completely</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, kata sifat </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">extreme </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">+ akhiran (-ly) menjadi </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">extremely</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, kata sifat </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">near </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">+ akhiran (-ly) menjadi </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">nearly</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, dll. Mengenai fungsi, adverbia tingkatan memiliki fungsi sebagai pengubah kata sifat, pengubah kata keterangan, pengubah frase preposisi, pengubah kata penentu, angka kardinal dan frase nomina. Mengenai posisi, adverbia tingkatan dapat terjadi pada semua posisi dalam kalimat, yaitu: kalimat awal, kalimat tengah, dan kalimat akhir.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: adverbia tingkatan, fungsi, majalah</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">English language is an international language used all over the world as a means of communication. It plays an important role for the people all over the world for political and commercial purposes and event tourism matters. And the important thing of which learning English is Grammar. Grammar is the study of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">words and their functions; one of its aspects in learning grammar is adverbs. Adverb is one of the major classes of words in English that influences the expression of English grammatically. It is commonly used by the speaker in communication.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study focuses on adverb of degree. Adverbs of Degree vary in terms of their morphology, functions and positions. The functions of adverbs of degree in sentences are also important to be described and observed in which their functions are as adjective modifier, as adverb modifier, as prepositional phrase modifier, as determiner modifier and as noun phrase modifier. Besides, the positions of adverbs of degree still affect their functions as well; different position may cause different function. Therefore, it is important to know and observe the forms, functions, and positions of adverbs of degree in order to be able to use them correctly in communication. In addition, through this study the readers will enrich vocabulary and also gives better understanding to them about English adverbs especially adverbs of degree.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Adverb of Degree Used in “Real Life Reads” pages of Cosmopolitan Magazine</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Forms of Adverbs of Degree</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Adverb of degree can be formed by two ways. They are derivational form (adverbs which are formed by suffixation process) and non-derivational form (adverbs which are in the form of single word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>2.3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Derivational forms</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1 . Ken isn’t </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">completely</span><span class="font2"> off my mind.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2....that makes prosecution </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">nearly</span><span class="font2"> impossible.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This kind of adverb (1) and (2) is derivational form or derived from adjective. In this case, there is suffixation process from adjective into adverb. In suffixation process, adverb is formed by: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">complete</span><span class="font2"> + (-ly) suffix becoming </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">completely</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">near</span><span class="font2"> + (ly) suffix becomes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">nearly</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>2.3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Non-derivational forms</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I felt </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> blissful with him.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I was </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pretty</span><span class="font2"> sure.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentences (1) and (2) consist of one kind of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pretty</span><span class="font2">. This adverb belongs to pure form class or non-derivational form because its adverb cannot be formed again.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Functions of Adverbs of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The function of adverb of degree is as a modifier, that is, a sentence element used to modify the adjective, adverb, prepositional phrase, determiner, and noun phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree as Adjective Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sure enough</span><span class="font2">, he remembered her and greeted her with a double Scotch on the rocks and introduced her to some of the regulars.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He seemed excited when he arrived at my apartment, which made </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">really happy</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The function of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">enough</span><span class="font2"> in sentence (1) as post-modifier of the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">sure</span><span class="font2">. And </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">really</span><span class="font2"> in sentence (2) as pre-modifier of adjective </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">happy</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree as Adverb Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1 . And had </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just recently</span><span class="font2"> moved to a small city in Washington state,...</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2....that alcohol abuse can </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so easily</span><span class="font2"> expose woman...</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The function of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> in sentences (1) and (2) as premodifier of the other adverb and as intensifier.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree as Prepositional Phrase Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She did not hit him again until </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just before</span><span class="font2"> sunset.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He came </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">right in</span><span class="font2"> the months of sudden bad weather.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">right</span><span class="font2"> in sentences (1) and (2) have a function to pre modify the prepositional phrases </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">before sunset</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">in the months of sudden bad weather.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree as Determiner Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I’m </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">almost 16</span><span class="font2"> years old, and I still play with dolls!</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hardly any</span><span class="font2"> water in some parts of it.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the sentence above, the adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">almost</span><span class="font2"> has a function to premodify cardinal numeral </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">16</span><span class="font2">. And the adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hardly</span><span class="font2"> ha s a function to premodify determiner </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">any</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree as Noun Phrase Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He had plowed up </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">quite a piece of ground</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She had </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">quite a party.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The function of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">quite</span><span class="font2"> in sentences (1) and (2) as premodifier of noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">a piece of ground</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">a party</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Positions of Adverb of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Adverb of degree can occur in different positions in a sentence. There are three positions for them: Initial or Front position, Medial or Middle position, and Final or End position.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>5.3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Initial or Front Position of Adverb of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pretty</span><span class="font2"> quickl</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">y</span><span class="font2">, Nancy passed out in the back of the taxi and remembered nothing about the ride...</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Too tired</span><span class="font2"> to walk any longer,...</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adverb of degree in examples (1) and (2) occur in initial or front position. The adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pretty</span><span class="font2"> in this sentence appears before the subject of the sentence and the adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">too</span><span class="font2"> in this sentence initiates the presence of the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>5.3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Medial or Middle Position of Adverb of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She was </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">obviously</span><span class="font2"> drunk,...</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just</span><span class="font2"> a temporary.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The position of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">obviously</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">just</span><span class="font2"> in examples (1) and (2) is in the middle of sentence. This adverb occurs after the finite be (was) and it belongs to post-finite position.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>5.3.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Final or End Position of Adverb of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I don’t think </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I couldn’t get </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">enough</span><span class="font2"> air into my lungs.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In sentences (1) and (2), the adverbs of degree </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">enough</span><span class="font2"> occur in the end position, in which they are placed following the verbs </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">think</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">get</span><span class="font2"> at the end of sentence.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">After discussing and analyzing the data from the data source in the previous chapter, there are some significant points that can be concluded from the analysis on the adverbs of degree in “Real Life Read” pages of Cosmopolitan Magazine.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Most of adverbs of degree are formed by adding</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ly</span><span class="font2"> suffix from adjectives or they have the suffixation process. In grammar adverbs of degree have two forms, they are: derivational form and non-derivational form or pure form. Based on their function, adverbs of degree in the sentences are used to modify Adjective, Adverb, Prepositional Phrase, Noun phrase, Determiner and cardinal number. As far as the position is concerned, adverbs of degree can appear in all positions in the sentence: sentence initial, sentence medial, and sentence final.</span></p>
