<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Dalam proses penerjemahan, makna dalam sebuah bahasa baik bahasa terucap atau bahasa tertulis terkadang diungkapkan secara implisit. Pengimplisitan makna dilakukan karena beberapa faktor seperti penghematan dalam penggunaan kata, menghindari pengulangan kata, atau bahkan untuk membuat sebuah karya sastra lebih menarik (apabila makna tersebut diimplisitkan dalam karya sastra). Novel yang merupakan salah satu karya sastra terkadang mengandung beberapa makna implisit di dalam kalimat – kalimat atau dialog – dialognya. Peneitian ini dilakukan dengan tujuan mengetahui jenis makna implisit apa saja yang terkandung dalam novel dan bagaimana penerjemah menerjemahkan makna implisit tersebut ke dalam bahasa sasaran. Jenis – jenis makna implisit diklasifikasikan dengan teori dari Larson (1998) dan Halliday dan Hasan (1976) sedangkan prosedur penerjemahan diklasifikasikan dengan teori dari Vinay dan Darbelnet (in Venuti, 2000) dan Catford (1965). Hasil penelitian menunjukkan bahwa jenis makna implisit yang terdapat dalam novel adalah makna implisit referensial, organisasional, dan situasional, sedangkan prosedur penerjemahan yang digunakan oleh penerjemah untuk menerjemahkan makna implisit tersebut adalah penerjemahan parsial, literal dan adaptasi.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Kata Kunci: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">penerjemahan, makna implisit, prosedur penerjemahan</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In English, the meaning of the language whether it is spoken or written can be stated implicitly. In spoken English, the speakers sometimes leave the meaning implicit rather than explicit to hide the real meaning and want their interlocutors to find out what they mean. On the other hand, in written language, the writers or the authors of literary work like novel or poetry leave the meaning implicit to save</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">words and prevent the waste of words. They also want the reader to find out the meaning in order to make their work more interesting and arouse the reader’s curiosity.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English novels, as literary works, are often translated into other languages nowadays. In the process of making the novel, the author sometimes leave some meanings implicit rather than explicit in order to make their work more interesting. The translators who translate the novel need to find out the real meaning in order to be able to deliver the messages appropriately in the target language. These implicit meanings need to be solved in order to avoid ambiguity in the future; besides, the readers also need to know the real meaning in order to know the whole story and the messages which the author intends to deliver.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of implicit meaning are found in novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson and The Olympians: The Lightning Thief</span><span class="font2"> by Rick Riordan and its Indonesian translation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson dan Dewa - Dewi Olympia: Pencuri Petir</span><span class="font2"> by Femmy Syahrani?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What procedures of translation are used in the process of translating the implicit meaning in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson and The Olympians: The Lightning Thief </span><span class="font2">by Rick Riordan and its Indonesian translation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson dan Dewa – Dewi Olympia: Pencuri Petir</span><span class="font2"> by Femmy Syahrani?</span></p></li></ul>

<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data were directly collected from an English novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson and The Olympians: The Lightning Thief</span><span class="font2"> (2013) written by Rick Riordan (7<sup>th</sup> edition) as the source language and its Indonesian translation entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson dan Dewa - Dewi Olympia: Pencuri Petir</span><span class="font2"> (2015) translated by Femmy Syahrani (14<sup>th</sup> edition). These novels were chosen since there were a lot of sentences and dialogues stated implicitly by the author. Moreover, the translation process of the novel also needs attention to know the procedures of translation used by the author in the process of translating the implicit meanings in the novel.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Library research method and note taking techniques were used in the process of collecting the data. There were some steps in the process, started by reading both the English version of the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson and The Olympians: The Lightning Thief</span><span class="font2"> (2013) written by Rick Riordan as the source language and its Indonesian translation entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson dan Dewa - Dewi Olympia: Pencuri Petir</span><span class="font2"> (2015) by Femmy Syahrani as the target language. Next, the sentences and dialogues that contain implicit meaning were identified, and last the data were grouped into two types; the English version and Indonesian version in order to make them easily analyzed.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The qualitative method through descriptive explanation technique was used in this study. There were some steps in the process of analyzing the data started by categorizing the types of implicit meanings found in the English version of the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson and The Olympians: The Lightning Thief</span><span class="font2"> (2013) written by Rick Riordan as the source language and its Indonesian translation entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Percy Jackson dan Dewa - Dewi Olympia: Pencuri Petir</span><span class="font2"> (2015) by Femmy Syahrani as the target language. Next, the data were classified into the types of implicit meanings and then they were divided in tables to make it systematic. Then, the data were analyzed using the theory of implicit meaning by Larson (1998), and supported by the theory proposed by Halliday and Hasan (1976) about types of references and substitution. The theory from Vinay and Darbelnet (in Venuti, 2000) about procedures of translation and a theory proposed</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">by Catford (1965) about the extent translation were also used in this study. The data were presented descriptively in form of table and text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">According to Larson (1998: 44-47), there are three types of implicit meanings, they are referential meaning, organizational meaning and situational meaning. Halliday and Hasan (1976: 37-91) also proposed some types of referential (personal, demonstrative and comparative reference) and substitution (nominal, verbal, and clausal). Vinay and Darbelnet (in Venuti, 2000: 84-93) defined seven procedures of translation, they are borrowing, calque, literal translation, transposition, modulation, equivalence, and adaptation. Meanwhile Catford (1965: 21) proposed the theory about extent translation (full vs. partial) which is known as types of translation. Here are the examples of implicit meanings based on their types and procedures of translation used to translate the meanings.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font2">In table above, the data show a personal reference. The pronoun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font2"> refers to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">water (noun)</span><span class="font2"> in the previous statement and the implied information is about an </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">event </span><span class="font2">that happened to Nancy. In the target language the pronoun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font2"> is left </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">implicit</span><span class="font2"> because the word “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">airnya</span><span class="font2">” has been mentioned in previous sentence. In this data, partial </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">translation </span><span class="font2">was used to translate the implicit meaning. The pronoun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font2"> which refers </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">water (air)</span><span class="font2"> is omitted in the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“seolah-olah menyambar Nancy”</span><span class="font2"> to prevent ineffectiveness in</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">using words. The pronoun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font2"> is left </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">untranslated</span><span class="font2"> in order to keep the information implicit and avoid the ineffectiveness in the use of words.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">In the table above the organizational meaning is indicated by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">substitution form</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">So</span><span class="font2"> changes whole clause in the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">It's got a good ring to it</span><span class="font2">. The lexical item </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> indicates the implied agreement between Percy and the bed seller in the use of name </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Crusty</span><span class="font2"> instead of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Procrustes</span><span class="font2">. The author left the information implicit in order to avoid repetition and ineffectiveness in the use of words. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Literal translation</span><span class="font2"> procedure was used in this data. The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">maka; begitu</span><span class="font2"> in the target language. The translator decided to leave it implicit in the source language since if the information was stated explicitly as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Menurutmu kedengaran lebih enak dengan nama Crusty?</span><span class="font2"> the sentence would be too long and not effective anymore.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">In the table above, the types of implicit meaning is the situational meaning which is caused by the cultural background between the two languages. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Summer home </span><span class="font2">is the implied information about </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Camp Half Blood</span><span class="font2"> which is known as the main setting in the story. The procedure of translation used in the process of translating the implicit</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">meaning is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">adaptation</span><span class="font2">. The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">summer</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">musim panas</span><span class="font2"> while </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">home</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">rumah</span><span class="font2"> in the target language. The translator translated it into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">alamat liburan</span><span class="font2"> which is adapted from the target language instead of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">rumah musim panas</span><span class="font2">, since </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">rumah musim panas</span><span class="font2"> is unknown in the target language’s culture; therefore, the translator used another term in order to make the information acceptable in the target language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded that the types of implicit meaning found in the novel are referential meaning, organizational meaning, and situational meaning. The procedures of translation used to translate implicit meanings are partial translation, literal translation, and adaptation. Moreover, the role played by the translators in the process of translating the implicit meaning is also really important, since they have to find the real meaning first and then deliver the meaning into the target language appropriately.</span></p>
