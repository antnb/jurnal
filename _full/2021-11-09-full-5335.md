<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini membahas tentang kategori dan fungsi dari klausa nominalyang ada pada media cetak berbahasa inggris. Sumber data yang digunakan dalam skripsi ini adalah artikel – artikel di majalah Red Bulletin. Data ini dikumpulkan dengan beberapa tahap dan dianalisa secara rinci menggunakan kajian dari Quirk (1985) dalam bukunya yang berjudul A Comprehensive Grammar Of English Language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Analisa dalam skripsi ini mengungkapkan bahwa enis – jenis klausa nominal yang ditemukan dalam sumber data analisa adalah: klausa dengan kata penghubung “that”, klausa pertanyaan dengan kata Tanya diawali “wh-”, klausa to infinitive, kalusa pertanyaan alternative “yes- no”, klausa seruan dan klausa nominal relative. Fungsi dari klausa yang ditemukan pada kategori klausa dengan kata penghubung“that” adalah objek langsung dan pelengkap subjek. Fungsi dari infinitive “to” adalah sebagai objek langsung. Dan Fungsi dari klausa pertanyaan dengan kata tanya yang diawali “wh” adalah sebagai subyek, objek langsung, pelengkap subjek, dana positif, pelengkap kata sifat dan pelengkap kata depan. Selain “yes- no” dan klausa pertanyaan alternative berfungsi sebagai subjek dan objek tak langsung.Sebagai tambahan fungsi klausa nominal yang paling banyak ditemukan dalam artikel majalah The Red Bulletin adalah sebagai objek langsung dalam kalimat.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: klausa nominal, fungsi dan kategori (jenis).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are many media which help us get new information in our daily life.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The information can be accessed through magazines, newspapers, books, and also from the internet. Magazine is a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations, typically covering a particular subject. In the study of syntax there are many discussion that can be found which deal with how words are combined into units. It is the study combination of words, phrases, and clauses forming a sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study was focused on nominal clauses. Nominal clauses are subordinate clauses which function as nouns. This study is chosen because there are nominal clauses used in the English printed mass media such as in literature and in the printed mass media. Nominal clauses are also commonly used in the spoken language of daily communication or oral usage. Besides, many people are still confused to understand the use of nominal clauses in learning English correctly. As we know that nominal clauses is grammatical point in learning English fluently. So this study tried to analyze the categories and the functions of nominal clauses in sentences used in English articles based on Quirk’s theory (.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language </span><span class="font2">and the book entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A University Grammar of English</span><span class="font2">).</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Research method is system or procedure is used in order to examine certain object. It includes data source, method and technique of collecting data and method and technique of analyzing data.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data in this study were taken from the articles of “The Red Bulletin Magazine” published on January 2011. The topics in this article were about sport, and life style.There were some articles in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Red Bulletin Magazine</span><span class="font2"> which contained nominal clauses, the title of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">those articles are</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">: A STELLAR LINE- UP, More Body &amp;&nbsp;Mind, Superfly Guy</span><span class="font2">. Totaled the articles in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Red Bulletin Magazine </span><span class="font2">contain nominal clauses 18 articles.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The method of used to collect the data was the documentation method. The data were collected through steps. Firstly, the article was read to find out the nominal clauses and the sentences that contained nominal clauses were noted down followed by classifying the data according to their types(</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that-</span><span class="font2">clauses, or subordinate declarative clauses, subordinate interrogative clauses, subordinate explanative clauses, nominal relative clauses, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2">-infinitive clauses, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ing</span><span class="font2"> clauses, bare infinitive clauses and verbless clauses) and their functions (subject, direct object, subject complement, object complement, adverbial, adjectival complementation, and prepositional complement). </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">4.3 Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The data were analyzed using qualitative method. The technique of analyzing data is done according the scope of discussionbased on Quirk’s theory (.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language </span><span class="font2">and the book entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A University Grammar of English</span><span class="font2">).</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Nominal Clauses Used In The Article Of The Red Bulletin Magazine.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5.1</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">-clauses</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The nominal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font2">-clauses usually introduced by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font2"> may function as a subject, a direct object, a subject complement, appositive, and an adjectival complement. There were 11 </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that-clauses</span><span class="font2"> found in the data source, those are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.1</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That-</span><span class="font2">clauses as direct object</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1.</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The World Health Organization said Tuesday </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that antiviral drugs should be given to only patients most at risk.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence (1) is formed by subject, verb, adverbial and object. The subject is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The World Health Organization, said</span><span class="font2"> as the verb, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tuesday</span><span class="font2"> as the adverbial and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that antiviral drugs should be given to only patients most at risk.</span><span class="font2"> In this sentence is as direct object.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.2</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wh</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">-interrogative clauses</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The nominal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">-interrogative clause may functions as subject, direct object, subject complement, appositive, adjectival complementation, and prepositional complement. The analyzed data below are the example of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh-</span><span class="font2">interrogative clauses found in the data source:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.2.1</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wh</span><span class="font2">-interrogative clauses as subject</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What scholars show</span><span class="font2"> is that liberal democracies almost never go to war with each other.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence (1) is formed by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">what scholars show</span><span class="font2"> as the subject, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">is</span><span class="font2"> as the verb, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that liberal democracies almost never go to war with each other</span><span class="font2"> is the object. The nominal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">-interrogative clauses </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">what scholar show</span><span class="font2"> which is occurs in the beginning functions as the subject of sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.3</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes-no</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> Alternative Interrogative Clauses</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are two types of alternative questions. The first resembles a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yes-no</span><span class="font2"> question, and the second a wh-question. The subordinate </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yes-no </span><span class="font2">interrogative are introduced by the subordinators </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whether</span><span class="font2"> or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">if.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.3.1</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes-no</span><span class="font2"> and alternative interrogative clause as subject</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;If the process is considered slow,</span><span class="font2"> then please provide us with insight.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentences above are identified by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yes-no</span><span class="font2"> and alternative interrogative clauses as the subject. In sentence (1), </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">if the process is considered slow </span><span class="font2">is the alternative interrogative clause as subject. The subject of this sentence is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">if the process is considered slow, then please</span><span class="font2"> as the adverbial, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">provide</span><span class="font2"> as the verb, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">us</span><span class="font2"> as the object and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">with insight</span><span class="font2"> as the complement.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.4</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To</span><span class="font2">-infinitive nominal clauses</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The presence of a subject in a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to-</span><span class="font2"> infinitive clause normally requires the presence of a proceeding </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for.</span><span class="font2"> When clause is a direct object (as object and apparently more directly involved in being acted on in the situation or sentence); However,</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2">is generally absent before the subject.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.4.1</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To-infinitive nominal clause as direct object</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The US military continues </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to dispute the toll and a military spokesman said some of the names could be fake.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence (1), it begins with subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The US military,</span><span class="font2"> then the verb is marked by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">continues,</span><span class="font2"> the object is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to dispute the toll and a military spokesman said some of the names could be fake.</span><span class="font2"> The nominal to-infinitive clause has functioned as the direct object of sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.5</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exclamative Clauses</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Subordinate exclamative clauses generally have the same form as subordinate interrogative clauses introduced by what or how. These clauses generally have function as extraposed subject, direct object, or prepotional complement.The analysis data below are the examples of some statements above:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.5.1</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exclamative Clause as direct object</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A woman in Yamaguchi Prefecture wrote, “I was astonished by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">how small a 996 kilo calorie meal looked</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">All the sentences above are identified by subordinate interrogative clause </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">what</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">how</span><span class="font2">. There are main clause and subordinate clause in sentence (1). The main clause is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a woman in Yamaguchi Prefecture wrote</span><span class="font2"> which has subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a woman</span><span class="font2">, adverbial </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in Yamaguchi Prefecture</span><span class="font2"> and verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wrote</span><span class="font2">. While the subordinate clause </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I was astonish by how small a 996 kilo calorie meal looked </span><span class="font2">has subject I, verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">was</span><span class="font2">, adjectival </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">astonished by</span><span class="font2">, and the object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">how small a 996-kilo calorie meal looked</span><span class="font2"> as the exclamative clause.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.6</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nominal Relative Clause</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Nominal relative clauses are introduced by a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">-element. They may function as subject, direct object, indirect object, subject complement, object complement, appositive, and prepositional complement. These clauses are more like noun phrases and refer to objects (including person) and have some properties of a noun phrase consisting of head and post modifying relative clause to form a single </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">-interrogative clause. There is an example of some the statement above found in the data source:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.6.1</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nominal Relative Clause as Subject</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whoever wins the election and becomes the island’s next governor </span><span class="font2">is likely to face dozens of lawsuits and in the end will barely be able to run the administration well,” he said.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The sentence above is introduced by subordinate clause </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whoever</span><span class="font2">. Sentence (1) consists of main clause and subordinate clause. The subordinate clause consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">whoever wins the electionand becomes the island’s next governor</span><span class="font2"> as the subject, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">is</span><span class="font2"> as the verb, and likely to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">face dozens of lawsuits and in the end will barely be able to run the administration well</span><span class="font2"> as the complement.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>

Some of six nominal clause categories and the functions were found in The Red of Bulletin Magazine. Those categories were </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that –</span><span class="font2">clause, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">- interrogative clause, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yes- no</span><span class="font2"> and alternative interrogative clause, nominal clause and exclamative clause. The functions at nominal clauses which were found in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tha</span><span class="font2">t -clause category are as subject, direct object, subject complement: the function of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wh</span><span class="font2">- interrogative clause were as subject, direct object, subject complement, appositive, adjectival complement, prepositional complement.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Beside, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yes- no</span><span class="font2"> and alternative interrogative clause functions were as the subject and direct object. The function of to infinitive were as a direct object. The function of nominal clause was as subject; and the function of exclamative clause as direct object. The most function of nominal clause was as direct object.</span></p>
