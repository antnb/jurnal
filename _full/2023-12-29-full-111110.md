<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Background: </span><span class="font4">Sunscreen cream is used to protect the skin from the bad effects of sunlight. Making a good sunscreen cream requires paying attention to the composition of the constituent compounds to obtain a standardized cream form quality. Formula optimization is carried out in each cream formulation, including to produce of Gumitir flower extract sunscreen cream</span><span class="font3">. </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Objective: </span><span class="font4">The aim of this research is to produce an optimum formula for Gumitir flower extract sunscreen cream. </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Methods: </span><span class="font4">The method used in this research is the Simplex Lattice Design optimization method to find the optimum base between stearate acid cream base and triethanolamine. </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Results: </span><span class="font4">Optimization results show that variations in stearic acid and triethanolamine cream bases influence the physical properties of viscosity and adhesiveness of sunscreen cream preparations, with stearic acid as the dominant factor influencing viscosity. </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Conclusion: </span><span class="font4">The conclusion obtained from this research is that the optimum formula for Gumitir flower extract sunscreen cream was obtained with a base composition of 13.5% stearic acid and 2.5% triethanolamine.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font4">Optimization; Gumitir; Extract; Sunscreen</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">Indonesia is known as an archipelagic country located on the equator so it gets sunlight all year round<sup>[1]</sup>. Sunlight can have both beneficial and detrimental effects. The beneficial effects of sunlight such as photosynthesis and light sources<sup>[2]</sup>. However, excessive sun exposure can be detrimental to unprotected skin such as wrinkles, sunburn, and long-term effects can cause skin cancer<sup>[3]</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Sunscreen sources can come from natural or synthetic sources. Nowadays the &quot;back to nature&quot; trend has developed rapidly, the use of plants and other natural ingredients can be used as alternative medicines and cosmetics. Natural ingredients that have the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">potential to act as sunscreens in plant compounds are preferred due to concerns about the side effects of synthetic chemicals used in sunscreen formulations<sup>[4]</sup>. One of the side effects that can be caused by synthetic chemical sunscreen active ingredients is mutagenic activity when using dioxybenzone at doses of 4, 6, 8 and 10 μL<sup>[5]</sup>. Natural ingredients have potential as sunscreen because they contain flavonoid compounds which have chromophore groups with their ability to strongly absorb sunlight at wavelengths of 290 to 320 nm<sup>[6]</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">One of the plants that contains flavonoids is the gumitir flower with a total flavonoid of 97.00 mg RE/g in ethyl alcohol: </span><span class="font3">93</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">water (7:3) solvent<sup>[7]</sup>. Research on the sunscreen activity of gumitir flowers was carried out with a concentration of 1% gumitir flower extract in petroleum ether solvent giving an SPF value of 1.08 ± 0.02 which is included in the minimum category<sup>[8]</sup>. Other research conducted by Bambal<sup>[9]</sup>, stated that a concentration of 1% ethanol extract from gumitir flowers in sunscreen cream obtained an SPF value of 8.67 ± 1.3 which was included in the extra category. Based on this data, gumitir flower extract has sunscreen activity.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The form of sunscreen dosage form that is commonly used in society is cream. Creams are preferred because they are easy to clean<sup>[10]</sup>, easy to spread, practical, and moisturize the skin<sup>[11]</sup>. In sunscreen cream formulations, the additional ingredients stearic acid and triethanolamine are often found in the cream base. According to the Indonesian National Standards, there are several parameters that must be met by sunscreen cream preparations so that they can be said to be of quality, namely appearance, pH, viscosity, and so on<sup>[12]</sup>. Research conducted by Widianto<sup>[13]</sup>, stated that stearic acid and triethanolamine had an effect on reducing viscosity and adhesive power, while the spreadability value increased in the ethanol extract of rosella flower petals with a cream preparation as sunscreen.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In making cream preparations, for every addition of ingredients formulated with certain active compounds, optimization needs to be carried out in order to obtain the best composition from the formula mixture so as to create good quality cream preparations. The aim of this research is to carry out optimization to produce the optimum formula for the compounds that make up the cream, namely stearic acid and triethanolamine with the active substance gumitir flower extract.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Each formula is made into 100 grams of sunscreen cream. Making gumitir</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">flower extract sunscreen cream begins with weighing the ingredients needed in the formulation according to predetermined levels. Next, the stearic acid, cetyl alcohol, nipagin, and nipasol were evaporated in a water bath at a temperature of 950C until they melted. Then mix the melted oil phase, grind it quickly with triethanolamine until a white cream base appears, then add glycerin and distilled water, and stir until homogeneous using a mixer. Next, add the gumitir flower extract, stir until homogeneous. Then the homogenized cream formula is stored in an appropriate container<sup>[15]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Viscosity Test</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The cream viscosity was used using a Brookfield LV viscometer and replicated three times. The viscosity results are recorded after showing a number that does not change<sup>[16]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spreadability Test</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Gumitir flower extract cream was placed in 1 gram on a glass scale, then covered and given a weight of 50 g within 1 minute, then the diameter of the spread was measured with a ruler. Then the weight of the load was added to 100 g and 150 g within 1 minute and the diameter of the spread was measured with the help of a ruler. Replication was carried out 3 times<sup>[16]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adhesion Test</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Gumitir flower extract cream is placed 1 gram between two glass objects that have been given a weight of 1 kg for 5 minutes. Then the weight is lifted, the object glass containing the cream is placed on the test equipment. A load of 80 g was attached to the test equipment and the time required for the cream to be released from the object glass was recorded.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Replication was carried out three times<sup>[16]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h2><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>DISCUSSION</span></h2>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Viscosity</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Cream viscosity testing aims to determine the viscosity of the cream preparation. The viscosity of a preparation should not be too high or too low, if the cream formed is too viscous it will be difficult to remove from the packaging, whereas if the preparation is too runny then the preparation will not stay on the skin for long when used.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">To determine the effect that stearic acid and triethanolamine can have on viscosity, researchers used quadratic</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">polynomial analysis and obtained the following equation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Y = 3627.5 (A) + 1617.5 (B) – 728 (A)(B)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Based on the equation above, it shows that there is an interaction between stearic acid and triethanolamine which has a negative influence on the viscosity of the gumitir flower extract cream preparation with a coefficient value of -728. Judging from the coefficient value, stearic acid has a dominant influence on viscosity properties. The increase in cream viscosity is influenced by the presence of fatty acids contained in the cream, namely stearic acid. The more fatty acids used, the thicker the cream produced. The use of stearic acid as an emulsifier in topical preparations will form a thick base and the level of viscosity is determined by the amount of triethanolamine used<sup>[17]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Spreadability</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This spreadability test assessment is to describe the ease of the cream when applied to the skin. The easier it is to spread on the skin means it will expand the area of skin in contact with the cream. Cream preparations can be said to fulfill optimal mechanical properties, if the preparation is easy to remove from the container and has good spreading power on the skin when the preparation is applied so that it can provide comfort for use by consumers. Specifications for the diameter of spreading power are 5–7 cm. To determine the effect that stearic acid and triethanolamine can have on spreadability, researchers used quadratic polynomial analysis and obtained the following equation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Y = 5.76 (A) + 6.78 (B) + 0.85 (A)(B)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Based on the equation above, it shows that there is an interaction between stearic acid and triethanolamine which has a positive influence on the spreadability of the gumitir flower extract cream preparation with a coefficient value of +0.85. Judging from the coefficient value, triethanolamine has a dominant influence on the spreadability properties. The lower the concentration of triethanolamine, the viscosity of the cream will decrease so that the spreadability becomes smaller<sup>[17]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adhesion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Cream adhesion testing is carried out to determine the adhesion power of the cream to the skin by measuring the length of time the cream adheres to the adhesion test tool. This will be related to the length of time the cream is in contact with the skin. To determine the effect that stearic acid and triethanolamine can have on adhesive strength, researchers used quadratic polynomial analysis and obtained the following equation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Y = 1.48 (A) + 0.77 (B) - 0.01 (A)(B)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Based on the equation above, it shows that there is an interaction between stearic acid and triethanolamine which has a negative influence on the adhesive power of the gumitir flower extract cream preparation with a coefficient value of -0.01. Judging from the coefficient value, stearic acid has a dominant influence on the adhesive properties. The higher the concentration of stearic acid, the viscosity of the cream will increase so that the adhesion becomes higher<sup>[17]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Optimum Formula</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">From the results of the verification and t test, the significance value for each viscosity, spreadability and adhesiveness response was obtained above p value &gt;0.05. This means that there is no significant difference between the response from the prediction formula and the response from the formula created, which means that the formula model is valid for optimizing the preparation of gumitir flower cream<sup>[17]</sup>.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>CONCLUSION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">Variations in the concentration of stearic acid and triethanolamine affect the physical properties of the gumitir flower extract cream preparation. Stearic acid is a constituent component as the dominant factor that influences the viscosity and stickiness of cream. Meanwhile, triethanolamine is the dominant factor that influences the spreadability response of cream. The optimum formula for sunscreen cream with gumitir flower extract is 13.5% stearic acid and 2.5% triethanolamine.</span></p>
