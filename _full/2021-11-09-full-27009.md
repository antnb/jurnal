<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui distribusi penggunaan kata sifat </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">admirable </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan sinonimnya, yaitu </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">laudable</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">; menjelaskan struktur kolokasi kata tersebut dan menganalisis maknanya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Data yang digunakan bersumber dari Corpus of Contemporary American English (COCA) yang mencakup lima ranah, yaitu ranah bahasa lisan, fiksi, majalah, surat kabar, dan akademik. Data dikumpulkan melalui metode korpus dengan pendekatan kuantitatif dan kualitatif. Data yang ditemukan dianalisis dengan teori kolokasi yang diusulkan oleh Benson, dkk didukung dengan Quirk, dkk, dan teori makna oleh Leech.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil analisis menunjukkan bahwa admirable merupakan kata sifat dengan jumlah frekuensi penggunaan tertinggi,yaitu 500 penggunaan dan yang terendah yaitu estimable dengan 177 penggunaan. Dalam hal kolokasi leksikal jenis pertama, yakni kata sifat + kata benda, frekuensi penggunaan tertinggi yaitu admirable + kata benda yang memiliki 457 penggunaan. Sementara itu, yang terendah yakni estimable + kata benda memiliki 96 penggunaan. Selanjutnya, jenis kolokasi leksikal yang lain yaitu kata keterangan + kata sifat. Pada jenis ini, penggunaan tertinggi yakni kata sifat + admirable yang memiliki 351 penggunaan. Namun, yang terendah yaitu kata keterangan + estimable yang memiliki 26 penggunaan. Dilihat dari fungsinya, fungsi atributif muncul pada kolokasi leksikal jenis pertama. Sementara itu, fungsi atributif dan predikatif muncul pada kolokasi leksikal jenis kedua. Makna yang ditemukan yakni makna kolokasi dan konseptual.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: kata sifat, sinonim,corpus</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language is a tool that can be used to communicate and express meaning. According to Hurford (1983:1), the study of meaning in language is called Semantics. English is an International language. Many people around the world use English as their language, although they come from different countries and</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">cultures. It means that they are able to understand each other. According to Crystal (2007), English has a larger number of words.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In all languages, antonym and synonym have important role. It means that by using antonym and synonym, people can improve their vocabularies, especially English. According to Hornby (2010:54), antonym is a word that means opposite of another word, for example </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">happy</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">sad</span><span class="font2">. Meanwhile, synonym is a word or expression that has the same or nearly the same meaning as another in the same language (Hornby, 2010:1515). </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Admirable</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2"> .</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Synonym is a very interesting topic to be discussed. English learners need many various words in order to improve their vocabularies. It can be used in written forms such as an essay. This example of written forms, essay, will be more beautiful with various words which have similar meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The four synonymous adjectives </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable, commendable, laudable,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> are used in this study in order to see the frequency of use, to identify their collocational structure that consists of words they collocate with and functions. According to Quirk, et al (1985) adjectives have their own syntactic functions, namely predicative and attributive. Furthermore, the four synonymous adjectives are used to explore their meanings found in all genres (spoken, fiction, magazine, newspaper, and academic) in COCA.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Well, Margaret, I think that's an </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">admirable idea</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 1 shows that </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2"> in the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable idea</span><span class="font2"> functions as attributive. It is because this adjective refers to the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">idea</span><span class="font2">. It means that there is an admirable idea from Margaret that can inspire others. This adjective appears between article </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an</span><span class="font2"> and noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">idea.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was truly a </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">commendable concept</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2"> in data 2 appears after the article </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a</span><span class="font2"> and before the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">concept</span><span class="font2">. This adjective, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2">, modifies the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">concept</span><span class="font2">. Furthermore, it can be said that this adjective a modifier. Therefore, it is attributive in function.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You may have had less than </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">laudable motives</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">to start out, but you've got good business sense about acquisitions.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 3 shows that </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">laudable</span><span class="font2"> appears before noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">motives</span><span class="font2">. It can be said that this adjective is as the modifier of noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">motives.</span><span class="font2"> Thus, the function of adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">laudable</span><span class="font2"> is attributive.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That was an </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">estimable achievement</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">for a film, but a limited one.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> appears after the article </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an</span><span class="font2"> and before the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">achievement</span><span class="font2">. This adjective refers to the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">achievement</span><span class="font2"> that there is an excellent achievement. Furthermore, it can be said that this adjective modifies the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">achievement</span><span class="font2">. Therefore, the function of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> in data 4 is attributive.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THEROUX: She's a </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">very admirable</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">woman, Irish woman.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 5 shows that the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2"> appears between adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font2"> and noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">woman.</span><span class="font2"> This adjective modifies the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">woman</span><span class="font2">. Furthermore, it explains that the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span><span class="font2"> as Irish woman is very admirable woman. Therefore, the function of this adjective is attributive.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I read your first book. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Very commendable</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adjective phrase in data 6 consists of adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font2"> followed by adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2">. From its position, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2"> is predicative in function because this adjective refers to previous noun phrase, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">your first book</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His intentions were </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">perfectly laudable.</span></p></li></ul>

From this position, it can be considered that this adjective belongs to predicative in function. The reason is that this adjective refers to subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">his intentions</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">8. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;One hundred kinds of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">most estimable</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">apples cultivated in our country.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 8 shows that the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> appears after the adverb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">most</span><span class="font2"> and before the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">apples</span><span class="font2">. From this position, the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> is attributive in function. The reason is that this adjective modifies noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">apples</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Meaning of Synonymous Adjectives </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Admirable, Commendable, Laudable,</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Estimable</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> Found in COCA</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The meaning of synonymous adjectives is analyzed using the theory proposed by Leech (1974).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">9. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&quot;You are an </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">admirable</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">astronomer, but you make a less than admirable theologian. I have spoken of this before. &quot;</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Admirable</span><span class="font2"> astronomer means that astronomer who is admired by people. It is because the astronomer can inspire others. Therefore, the meaning in data 9 is collocative meaning. The reason is that the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2"> collocates with noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">astronomer.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">10. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This department shows a very </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">commendable</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font2">profit on its operations.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Commendable</span><span class="font2"> in data 10 means awesome. This adjective collocates with the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">profit</span><span class="font2">. Therefore, the meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">commendable</span><span class="font2"> above is collocative meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">11. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The results were </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">laudable</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Laudable</span><span class="font2"> based on data 11 means to be praised. Therefore, the meaning of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">laudable</span><span class="font2"> is conceptual meaning because it is based on the dictionary.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">12. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;People called her looks </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">estimable</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Data 12 shows that </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> is conceptual in meaning. It is because this adjective means to be admired. Furthermore, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> refers to noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">her looks</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the foregoing analysis and discussion, the following points can be drawn as conclusion: the four synonymous adjectives have different frequency of use in each genre. The highest frequency of use among those synonymous adjectives is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2"> which has 500 frequency of use. Meanwhile, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> is the lowest which has 177 usages from all genres.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the first type of the lexical collocation, the highest frequency of use is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2"> + noun where it has 457 usages while the lowest is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> + noun which has 96 usages. Furthermore, the attributive function appears in this first type. In the second type, adverb + adjective, the highest frequency of use is adverb + </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">admirable</span><span class="font2">. It has 351 usages from all genres. Meanwhile, the lowest is adverb + </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> which has 26 usages. Furthermore, the lowest is adverb + </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">estimable</span><span class="font2"> which has 26 usages. Based on the functions of adjective, both attributive and predicative functions appear in the second type of the lexical collocation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The meanings found in the four synonymous adjectives are conceptual and collocative meaning. The reason is that this study focused on the meaning of the four synonymous adjectives in the phrase and the data to be discussed are limited.</span></p>
