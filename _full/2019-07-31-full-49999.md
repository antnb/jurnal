<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Linguistic Landscapes In Desa Kuta</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7"><sup>1</sup>I Wayan Mulyawan, Udayana University <sup>2</sup>Ni Ketut Ratna Erawati, Udayana University</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><sup><a name="bookmark3"></a>1</sup>Corresponding Author :</span><a href="mailto:moelya01@gmail.com"><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">moelya01@gmail.com</span></a><br><br><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Received Date: 10-01-2019 Accepted Date: 28-02-2019 Published Date: 31.-07-2019</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstract</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> -- </span><span class="font7">This research aimed to identify the presence of Linguistic Landscapes (LL) in </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">. LL is sociolinguistic study of language used in outdoor signage that firstly introduce by Landry and Bourhis (1997). </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> as one of tourist destination in Bali experiences a great development due to globalization effect. This great development can be seen clearly through the presence of outdoor signs along the main roads of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">. The research was conducted from February to March 2019. The finding showed there are 2.549 signs along the main roads of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">. The language used mostly in English with 1.332 signs (52%). As for the types, there are 1.603 signs of commercial signs (65%). In term of the function, 2.320 signs (93%) are informative.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">: </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">linguistic landscapes, language, function</span><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font7">Language is the key interaction of human as a social being. Formerly, language is use as a means of communication between people either spoken or written. Nowdays, in this globalization era, there is one other form of communication between people and their environment. This communication is known as mass communication in form of outdoor signs. These outdoor signs may be in form of traffic signs, billboards, place names, road names, etc. This mass communications of outdoors signs is known as Linguistics Landscapes (LL). This study was firstly introduced by Landry and Bourhis (1997) as the study ole language in public space. Mulyawan (2016) stated that LL is a multilingual study of outdoor signs especially as the language of commercial signs.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">The presence of outdoor signs of every place, region or territory are highly depends on the status or development of the place. So that, one place may full of outdoor signs where as other has only few out door signs. Besides, the presence of outdoor signs also indicates the characteristic of the place.</span></p>

<p><span class="font7">These transformation is the continues effect of globalization which not only influence the</span></p>

<p><span class="font7">In order to preserve the local, especially the Balinese language against effect of globalization, there for Balinese government encourages the use of Balinese language in public sphere. Majidi (2013: 37) said that the survival of local language can be seen from the use of the language as an active communication in public.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Furthermore, Mulyawan (2017b: 82) stated that the proses of emerging local culture (i.e. language) to be part of international communication in a development area known as glocalization process. One way to glocalize Balinese language is to use it as a mass media communication in public space, so that Balinese language will be read and recognized by the foreigners in Bali especially in </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">The fight of Balinese language and culture in public space against globalization effect, made </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> as an ideal place to conduct LL study.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font7">Based on its data and analysis, this research is a qualitative research, because its aims to identify the phenomena of outdoor signs in </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> as a way to preserve the local from negative effect of globalization. The data source of this research is primary data source in form of outdoor signs in surrounding </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">, such as traffic signs, billboards, place names, street names, buildings names, etc. Based on method and technic of collecting Data proposed by Kothari (2004: 7), the method used in this research is field research with participant observation, by which the process of collecting data is direct observation to </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> by the researcher himself. The technic of collecting data was photo graphic techniques by which all data were collected through documenting the outdoor signs using a digital camera. The method of analysing data was qualitative method which divided into two technic: quantification of qualitative data and subjective interpretation upon the data.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Theoretical Basis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font7">Linguistic Landscapes (LL) is new trend of analysis which focuses on the use of language in public space. This study was initially proposed by Landry and Bourhis (1997: 23) “</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Linguistic landscape refers to the visibility and salience of languages on public and commercial signs in a given territory or region</span><span class="font7">”. Furthermore they limit the form of LL into “... </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">language of public road signs, advertising billboards, street names, places names, commercial shop signs, and public sign on government buildings...”</span><span class="font7">(1997: 25).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Further study showed that LL is not only about the language use in public sphere but it is also involving the reflection and language status of the given territory as well as the social and cultural condition of the society (Gorter, 2006: 2; Cenoz and Gorter, 2006: 67-68; Ben-Rafael E. et al., 2006: 7; Moriarty, 2014). This approach indicates that LL is the corpus of data and the analysis will be vary depend on the interest of the researcher, in short it can be said that LL is a multidisciplinary approach.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">346</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/49999-8.png" alt="" style="width:68pt;height:37pt;">
<p><span class="font7">Mulyawan (2017a) focused his study on the use of language in commercial out door signs in </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7">. His study showed that the presence of LL in </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> is the reflection of the need of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Desa Kuta</span><span class="font7"> as a tourist destination.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result And Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
