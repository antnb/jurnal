<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini berjudul “Types of Word Formations on Instagram Hashtags”. Tujuan penelitian ini adalah untuk menklasifikasikan dan mendeskripsikan tipe-tipe pembentukan kata yang membentuk tanda tagar di Instagram serta menentukan makna yang terkandung dalam istilah tersebut. Data-data yang dikumpulkan untuk penelitian ini diambil dari jejaring sosial Instagram. Metode yang digunakan untuk mengumpulkan data di penelitian ini adalah metode dokumentasi; dan dengan teknik mencatat. Metode kualitatif deskriptif digunakan untuk menganalisa tipe pembentukan kata serta makna. Metode kuantitatif berhubungan dengan persentase dari tipe-tipe pembentukan kata yang membentuk tanda tagar tersebut. Teori yang digunakan dalam peneltian ini adalah teori pembentukan kata oleh Lieber (2009) dan teori tentang makna oleh Chaer (2007). Hasil penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa ada 8 (delapan) tipe pembentukan kata yang membentuk tanda tagar di Instagram yakni: (1) affixation; (2) compounding; (3) coinage; (4) blending; (5) acronym; (6) initialism; (7) clipping; and (8) multiple processes. Dari segi semantis terdapat makna gramatikal, dan makna leksikal, serta makna kontekstual yang berkaitan dengan istilah-istilah dalam Instagram.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata Kunci: pembentukan kata, makna, Instagram</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language plays an important role in our daily communication. As a tool of communication, it is constantly in the process of change. That may occur because of developing technologies, particularly the internet. By the invention of internet, social media appear as media of communication. Thus, people start to use and create new, simple, short, and meaningful language. One of the new social media which attract people’s attention is Instagram which becomes media of photo and video sharing. This social media contributes in making some ‘new words’ which are related to word formation types. Word formation types can be found on Instagram’s caption on photo or video which is using ‘hashtag’. Thus, hashtag often made of ‘new words’ which is becoming a trend and used in daily life nowadays.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of word formation are found on Instagram hashtags?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What meanings are contained in hashtags found on Instagram?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To classify and describe the types of word formation on Instagram hashtags.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To determine the meanings contained on Instagram hashtags.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Methods</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data source of this study was taken from social media, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Instagram</span><span class="font2">. It contains photos shared by its users from around the world. Photo captions which using hashtags and related to word formation types were used in this research.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The data were collected using documentation method. First step was determining Instagram hashtags which commonly used and listing the hashtags. Then, searching photos using hashtags which related to word formation types. Furthermore, several photos which contained hashtags will be captured as screenshots. The screenshots of Instagram photos will be printed. The photo caption was read and hashtags which used word formation types were identified. The technique of collecting the data was taking note in order to sort and classify them into the types of word formation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Descriptive qualitative method was used to classify and analyse the types of word formation and the meaning found in word formation. Descriptive meant that the data were analysed by explaining or describing it in words or sentences. The collected data were classified into eight types using word formation theory proposed by Lieber (2009) and the meaning that constructed hashtags which using word formation were described using the theory proposed by Chaer (2007). The quantitative method is related to the percentage of the categories of the word formation types that construct hashtags.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study used descriptive method in presenting the analysis. First, in order to answer the first research question, the data were classified into the types of word formation, by showing the data in the form of table. The discussion of each hashtag was presented descriptively based on the word formation types. Second, the analysis of those data based on meaning was presented descriptively. The last, the percentage of word formation types that construct hashtags was presented using a pie diagram.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Results and Discussions</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Affixation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">New lexemes that are formed with prefixes and suffixes on a base are often referred to as derived words, and the process by which they are formed as affixation. Based on data above, prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">re-</span><span class="font2"> and suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ie</span><span class="font2"> were used to create hashtags on Instagram.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-1) </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#regram</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻregramʼ</span><span class="font2"> is formed by adding prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">re-</span><span class="font2"> in the beginning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻgramʼ</span><span class="font2"> which is coined from word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻInstagramʼ</span><span class="font2">. Prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">re-</span><span class="font2"> does not change the class of the word, but it changes the meaning. Hashtag </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻregramʼ</span><span class="font2"> belongs to quantitative affixes because the prefix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">re-</span><span class="font2"> that means ‘repeated’ action. From its contextual meaning, it can be defined as action of Instagram user when they like a certain post and re-upload it using their own Instagram account. Sometimes this hashtags is also used to promote events or products.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-2) </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#selfie</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This hashtag is formed by adding suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ie</span><span class="font2"> in the end of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻselfʼ</span><span class="font2">. Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ie</span><span class="font2"> does not change the class category of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">self</span><span class="font2"> (a noun) into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">selfie</span><span class="font2"> (a noun), but it changes the meaning. Semantically, word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻselfʼ</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“a personʼs personality or character that makes them different from other people”</span><span class="font2">. It is different from the base of this word, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻselfieʼ</span><span class="font2"> has lexical meaning as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“a photo of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yourself that you take, typically with a smartphone or a webcam, and usually put on a social networking site”.</span><span class="font2"> From its contextual meaning, this hashtag refers to a self-portrait photograph typically taken with a cell phone or digital camera. It has become a trending topic on Instagram becoming the “word of the year” as announced by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Oxford English Dictionary</span><span class="font2"> in November 2013. This hashtag has no relation to common affixes categories.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compounding</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Compounds are words that are composed of two (or more) bases, roots, or stems.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-3) </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#outfit</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Morphologically, this word is formed by grouping two words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutʼ</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻfitʼ</span><span class="font2">. Word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻfitʼ</span><span class="font2"> becomes the head of this compound word. This word belongs to a right-headed compound because head of compound word is in the right side and determine the word class of compound word itself. Word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutʼ</span><span class="font2"> belongs to a preposition and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻfitʼ</span><span class="font2"> belongs to a noun. Result of grouping these words is a new word and belongs to word class noun with following pattern Prep+N=N.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Semantically, this word has different lexical meaning, word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutʼ</span><span class="font2"> has lexical meaning </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“away from the inside of a place or thing”</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻfitʼ</span><span class="font2"> has lexical meaning </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“the way that something, especially a piece of clothing”.</span><span class="font2"> Thus, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutfitʼ </span><span class="font2">has different lexical meaning as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“a set of clothes that you wear together, especially for a particular occasion or purpose”.</span><span class="font2"> From its contextual meaning, this hashtag refers to Instgrammers who pay a lot of attention about fashion. From their feeds, the posts were about showing off what clothes he/she wears again at that time.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on its form, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutfitʼ</span><span class="font2"> can be categorized as closed compound word because simply because those two words are melded together, forming a single word.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coinage</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">A possibility to make up entirely new words from whole cloth is called coinage.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-4) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#foodporn</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The hashtag that exists above is formed by coinage process. The term appeared to have been coined by the feminist critic Rosalind Coward in her 1984 book </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Female Desire.</span><span class="font2"> This word has a lexical meaning, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“a glamourized spectacular visual presentation of cooking or eating in advertisements, infomercials, blogs cooking shows or other visual media, foods boasting a high fat and calorie content, exotic dishes that arouse a desire to eat or the glorification of food as a substitute for sex.”</span><span class="font2"> From its contextual meaning, food porn often takes the form of food photography and styling that presents food beautifully. It is important to realize that the term food porn does not strictly deal with the connection, often established throughout history, between food items and sexual contents.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Blending</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Blending is a process of word formation in which parts of lexemes that are not themselves morphemes are combined to form a new lexeme.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-5) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#instagood</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻinstagoodʼ</span><span class="font2"> is made up from the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻinstaʼ</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻgoodʼ</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻInstaʼ</span><span class="font2"> is derived from ‘Instagram’. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻinstaʼ</span><span class="font2"> belongs to a noun and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻgoodʼ</span><span class="font2"> belongs to an adjective. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Goodʼ</span><span class="font2"> has lexical meaning as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“high quality or an acceptable standard”. ʻinstagoodʼ</span><span class="font2"> has no dictionary meaning. From its contextual meaning, this hashtag refers to something that is very artsy on Instagram.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Acronyms</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Acronym is a process when the first letters of words that make up a name or a phrase are used to create a new word and the results are pronounced as a word.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-6) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#YOLO</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻYOLOʼ</span><span class="font2"> is an abbreviation for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻYou Only Live Onceʼ.</span><span class="font2"> From its contextual meaning, it can be interpreted in a number of ways, but most often indicates an activity that shows someone is living on the edge, either literally or sarcastically. This hashtag generally used by teenagers who doing an extreme activity and then capture the moment before posts it on their Instagram account.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Initialisms</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Initialism is a process when the first letters of words that make up a name or a phrase are used to create a new word and the results are pronounced as a series of letters.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-7) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#OOTD</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This is formed by series of letters from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻOutfit of the Dayʼ.</span><span class="font2"> Based on its contextual meaning, this hashtag refers to Instagrammers who have interests to fashion and to show what they have worn. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻ#OOTDʼ</span><span class="font2"> is an extremely popular hashtag used on Instagtam. Users use this hashtag when they upload photos of themselves in an outfit. Users often upload </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Outfit of the Day</span><span class="font2"> photos when they get a new outfit or feel like their outfit looks nice.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clipping</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">When the formation process is shortening or reducing language word in order to create a new word, it is called clipping.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-8) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#pict</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻPictʼ</span><span class="font2"> stands for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻpictureʼ</span><span class="font2"> (a noun). The changing of this word belongs to back clipped word. Back clipped word is removing the end of a word. Semantically, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻpicʼ</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻa pictureʼ.</span><span class="font2"> From the whole word, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻpictureʼ,</span><span class="font2"> it has lexical meaning as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“a painting or drawing, etc. that shows a scene, a person or thing; a photograph”.</span><span class="font2"> This hashtag often use for various photos about animal, people, nature, food, and so on.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Multiple Process</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This process is combination of more than one type of word formations.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-9) &nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">#IGer</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">IGer</span><span class="font2"> is formed by multiple processes, they are initialism and affixation. Initialisms are resulted when the first letters of words that make up a name or a phrase are used to create a new word, they are pronounced as a series of letters.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The hashtag that exists above is formed by initialism. As mentioned above, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻIGʼ</span><span class="font2"> is an abbreviation of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻInstagramʼ.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">IGer</span><span class="font2"> means Instagrammer, people who use Instagram. This hashtags also formed by affixation process. It is resulted when new lexemes are formed with prefixes and suffixes on a base. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻInstagrammersʼ</span><span class="font2"> is made up of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻInstagramʼ</span><span class="font2"> and added suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> in the end of the word. This word belongs to personal affixes because it creates </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻpeople nounʼ</span><span class="font2">. Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> forms agent nouns (the ‘doer’ of the action). ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">IGer</span><span class="font2">’ is one of the most popular hashtags on</span><a href="http://instagram.com/"><span class="font2"> Instagram,</span></a><span class="font2"> with around 100 million tags. It is used on all kinds of photos. Users began using the tag in an attempt to gain followers or likes.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded that there are eight types of word formations found on Instagram hashtags. The first type is initialism which exists in eight hashtags (25%), the multiple processes types are six hashtags (18%), the affixation, compounding, blending and clipping types are four hashtags (12%) per each, the acronym types are two hashtags (6%), and the coinage type is one hashtag (3%).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Moreover, from 33 hashtags found, not all hashtags have those three types of meaning. There are only five hashtags which have grammatical meaning</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> In addition, twenty hashtags do not have their lexical meaning (dictionary meaning) while the rests of them acquire their lexical meaning. Overall, all of the hashtags found have their contextual meaning which is related to Instagram term.</span></p>
