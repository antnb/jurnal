<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Imbuhan merupakan salah satu komponen yang dapat digunakan untuk membentuk kata baru dalam Bahasa inggris. Imbuhan yang digunakan dalam hal ini adalah imbuhan derivatif. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui bentuk, fungsi dan makna dari imbuhan derivatif yang ditemukan di “Corpus of Contemporary American English (COCA)” khususnya dalam kategori wacana akademis. Bentuk, fungsi dan makna dari imbuhan derivatif yang ditemukan diklasifikasin berdasarkan teori yang dikemukakan oleh Plag (2002) dalam bukunya yang berjudul “Word-Formation in English.” Hasil dari penilitian ini menunjukan bahwa ada empat bentuk imbuhan derivatif, yaitu “nominal suffix,” “verbal suffix,” “adjectival suffix” dan “adverbial suffix.” Fungsi dari imbuhan tersebut adalah “class-changing” atau “classmaintaining.” “Connected with,” “area,” “status” dan “quality” adalah beberapa makna dari imbuhan derivatif tersebut.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Considering that there is no limit to the number of word that can be produced in a language, it is essential to study about derivational suffix in English. When speakers or writers do not know words that can convey what they mean, they can easily coin new words by using morphological process, especially by adding derivational suffixes into the base word. Hence, it is important to study about the forms, functions and meanings of the derivational suffixes in English.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The problems that the writer would like to discuss in this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What forms of derivational suffixes are used in academic discourse on COCA?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What functions and meanings of derivational suffixes are used in academic discourse on COCA?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the forms of derivational suffixes used in academic discourse on COCA.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the functions and meanings of derivational suffixes used in academic discourse on COCA.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data source of this study were taken from Corpus of Contemporary American English (COCA). Words in academic discourses that is mostly used were the concentration of this study. One thousand mostly used words in academic discourses were the data of this study.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The study used documentation method in collecting the data. The method was done by accessing the website of COCA. Then, words were filtered based on mostly used words in academic discourses. Finally, founded words with derivational suffix were noted.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Qualitative method was used in analyzing the data in this study. Some steps were done in analyzing the data. First, the derivational suffixes were analyzed to identify their form by applying the theory of Morphology proposed by Plag (2002). Second, examples of words with derivational suffix were put into context taken from its usage in academic discourse on Corpus of Contemporary American English. Third, functions and meanings of the derivational suffixes were analyzed by using Morphology theory proposed by Plag (2002). Finally, the analysis of this study was described descriptively.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font3">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forms of Derivational Suffix</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">To analyze forms of derivational suffix found in academic discourse on Corpus of Contemporary American English, theory proposed by Plag (2002) was used. Plag states that there are four forms of derivational suffix, namely nominal suffix, verbal suffix, adjectival suffix and adverbial suffix (Plag, 2002: 109).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nominal Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Nominal suffix is suffix that changes part of speech of the base into noun or maintains the part of speech of the base which is already noun. According to Plag (2002), there are twenty two forms of nominal suffix, those are </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-age, -al</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ance</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ant</span><span class="font3">,-</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">cy</span><span class="font3">, -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">dom</span><span class="font3">, -ee, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-eer</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-er</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-(e)ry</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ess</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ful</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-hood</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-an</span><span class="font3">, -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ing</span><span class="font3">, -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ion</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ism</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ist</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ity</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ment</span><span class="font3">, -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ness</span><span class="font3">, and -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ship</span><span class="font3">. However, not all forms of derivational suffixes were found in the data source. There were six nominal suffixes that were not found in the data source, namely suffix </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-dom</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-eer</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-(e)ry, -ess, -ful</span><span class="font3">, and </span><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ist</span><span class="font3">. Examples of words with nominal suffix attached to it found in the data source were: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Marriage, Approval, Performance, Participant, Efficiency, Committee, Driver, Childhood, American, Teaching, Instruction, Terrorism, Activity, Development, Awareness</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Citizenship</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Verbal Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Verbal suffix is suffix that changes word class of the base into verb. There are four verbal suffixes according to Plag (2002), those are –ate, –en, –ify and –ize. All those verbal suffixes were found in the data source. Examples of words with verbal suffix attached to it found in the data source were: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Formulate, Activate, Strengthen, Weaken, Classify, Clarify, Realize</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Criticize</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjectival Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Adjectival suffix is suffix that changes part of speech of the base into adjective or maintains the part of speech of the base which is already adjective. Based on Plag (2002), there are thirteen forms of adjectival suffix, those are </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-able, -al, -ary, -ed, -esque, -ful, -ic, -ing, -ish, -less, -ive, -ly, -ous</span><span class="font3">. Nevertheless, not all forms of this derivational suffixes were found in the data source. There were two adjectival suffixes that were not found in the data source, namely suffix -</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">eque</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font0">-</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ish</span><span class="font3">. Examples of words</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">with adjectival suffix attached to it found in the data source were: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Acceptable, Biological, Disciplinary, Skilled, Beautiful, Strategic, Supporting, Attractive, Homeless, Friendly</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Dangerous</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverbial Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Adverbial suffix is suffix that changes word class of the base into adverb. There are two adverbial suffixes, based on Plag (2002), namely –ly and –wise. Both these adverbial suffixes were found in the data source. Examples of words with adverbial suffix attached to it found in the data source were: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Effectively, Publicly, Pairwise</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Likewise</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Functions and Meanings of Derivational Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Plag (2002:22) states that derivational suffix often changes part of speech of the base. However, it may also do not change word class of the base. Every suffixes have their own meaning/meanings. The meaning of the suffix is often extended so that it may have more than one meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Class-Maintaining Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Noun from Noun</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are some suffixes that form noun from noun, namely /-age/, /-cy/, /-dom/, /-ee/, /-eer/, /-er/, /-(e)ry/, /-ess/, /-hood/, /-ship/, /-ful/, /-an/, /-ing/, /-ist/, and /-ism/. Five noun class maintaining suffixes were found in the data source, those were /-er/,/-hood/, /-ship/, /-an/, and /-ism/.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">(5-1) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">He would require a mastery of more American history as one qualification for </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">citizenship</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">citizenship</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">citizen</span><span class="font3"> which is a noun plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">an</span><span class="font3">. This suffix does not change the word class of the base. The meaning of this suffix in word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">citizenship</span><span class="font3"> is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">status</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjective from Adjective</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There is only one suffix that forms adjective from adjective. That suffix is /-ish/ which has meaning ‘somewhat’. In mostly used word on academic discourse, there is no any single word listed in the data source that contains this suffix.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Class-Changing Suffix</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Noun from Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are ten suffixes that can be used to form noun from verb. Those suffixes are /-age/, /-al/, /-ance/, /-ant/, /-ee/, /-er/, /-(e)ry/, /-ing/, /-ment/ and /-ion/. Only word with suffix /-(e)ry/ that was not found in the data source.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">(5-2) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Using action research, the </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">teacher </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">should be able to clearly answer these questions.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">teacher</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">teach</span><span class="font3"> which is a verb plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">er</span><span class="font3">. This suffix does change the word class of the base into noun. The meaning of this suffix in word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">teacher</span><span class="font3"> is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">a person that X</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Noun from Adjective</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">/-ness/ and /-ity/ are suffixes used to form noun from adjective. These suffixes have two meanings, those are ‘state’ and ‘quality’. Both of these suffixes were found in the data source.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">(5-3) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">For youth with significant disabilities, it may take more time and </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">creativity </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to gather preferences and ideas.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">creativity</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">creative</span><span class="font3"> which is an adjective plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ity</span><span class="font3">. This suffix does change the word class of the base into noun. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">quality</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Verb from Other Word Class</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Four suffixes that can be used to form verb from other word class are </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ate, -en, -ify</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-</span><span class="font3">ize. The base word of these suffixes are noun and adjective All words containing these suffixes were found in the data source.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">(5-4) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">These tests </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">activate </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">several cognitive processes simultaneously and do not make inferences about the specific processes involved.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">activate</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">active</span><span class="font3"> which is an adjective plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ate</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into verb. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">make X</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">(5-5) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">These principles </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">summarize </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">large volumes of research and can be used by practitioners in concert with the other.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">summarize</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">summary</span><span class="font3"> which is a noun plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ize</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into verb. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">make into X</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">d) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjective from Noun</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are some suffixes used to change noun into adjective. Those suffixes are </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-able, -al, -ary, -ed, -esque, -ful, -ic, -less, -ly, -ish,</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ous</span><span class="font3">. These suffixes were found attached to some noun-base word in the data source. There were only suffix </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">–able</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">-ish</span><span class="font3"> which were not found in the data source.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(5-6) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Climate change poses risks to food security via reductions in </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">agricultural </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and fishery yields.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">agricultural</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">agriculture</span><span class="font3"> which is a noun plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">al</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into adjective. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">connected with X</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">e) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjective from Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are three suffixes used to change verb into adjective. –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ing</span><span class="font3">, –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ive</span><span class="font3"> and –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">able </span><span class="font3">are those suffixes. These suffixes were found attached to some verb-base words in the data source. All these suffixes were found in the data source.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(5-7) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">This study is </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">notable </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in that it was the first systematic attempt to examine the effectiveness of communication strategies.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">notable</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">note</span><span class="font3"> which is a noun plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">able</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into adjective. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">worthy to be X-ed</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">f) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb from Other Word Class</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are two suffixes used to make adverb from other word class. Those suffixes are –ly and –wise. These suffixes were attached to noun or adjective to form adverb. Both these suffixes were found in the data source.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(5-8) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">It is essential that patients are </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">clearly </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">informed of the health benefits of insulin and the health costs of delay.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">clearly</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">clear</span><span class="font3"> which is an adjective plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ly</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into adverb. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in a/an X manner</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(5-9) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Missing values were excluded </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pairwise</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">pairwise</span><span class="font3"> is formed from word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">pair</span><span class="font3"> which is a noun plus suffix –</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">wise</span><span class="font3">. This suffix changes the word class of the base into adverb. The meaning of this suffix is </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in the manner of X</span><span class="font3"> (with X as the base word).</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">It can be concluded that there are four forms of suffixes found in the data source, namely </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">nominal suffix</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">verbal suffix</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adjectival suffix</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adverbial suffix</span><span class="font3">. Functions of the suffixes found in the data source are either class-maintaining or class-changing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Class-maintaining suffixes can be divided into two: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">noun from noun</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adjective from adjective</span><span class="font3">. While, class-changing suffixes can be divided into: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">noun from adjective</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">noun from verb</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">verb from other word class</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adjective from noun</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adjective from verb</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">adverb from other word class</span><span class="font3">. Derivational suffixes found in the data source have various meanings, such as: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">quality</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">connected with X</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in a/an X manner</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">make X</span><span class="font3">, etc.</span></p>
