<p><span class="font2">6</span></p>
<p><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font5">The Potency of Endofit Fungi in Cocoa as Biological Agent to Control Cocoa Pod Disease Caused by </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthota Palmivora</span><span class="font5"> (Butler) Butler</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">I Made Sudarma*, Ni Made Puspawati, and I Ketut Suada</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Study program of Agroecotechnology, Faculty of Agriculture, Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Jl. PB. Sudirman, Denpasar, Bali</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">*Corresponding author:</span><a href="mailto:sudarma_made@ymail.com"><span class="font2"> </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">sudarma_made@ymail.com</span></a></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract. </span><span class="font2">Cocoa pod disease caused by the fungus </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora palmivora</span><span class="font2"> has resulted in loss of cacao in Indonesia, especially in Bali. So far the disease control strategy is not fully understood. So there is a need to find an alternative by using endophytic fungi associated with cocoa plant. Endophytic fungi are needed to be explored in all parts of the cocoa plant such as stems, leaves and husks. The prevalence of fungal endophyte was determined by the size of its domination on the surface tissue for protection against pathogen. The exploration of the endophytic fungi benefits is aimed at finding biological agents that could control of pathogenic </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2">. The results showed that 15 types of endophyte fungi have been found in the healthy leaves, stem and pod husks, with the prevalence of fungal endophyte originated from healthy leaf </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mecelia sterilia </span><span class="font2">(hyphae sterile) around 30%, the endophyte fungi originated from the healthy cocoa stem are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mycelia sterilia</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora </span><span class="font2">spp and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp around 25%. While the endophytic fungi originated from healthy skin fruit is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. around 35%. The in vitro test results of endophytic fungi antagonistic against </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> indicated that the endophyte fungi originated from the leaf namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus</span><span class="font2"> spp was obtained at 80 ± 2%, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. niger</span><span class="font2"> 90 ± 2%, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. flavus</span><span class="font2"> 100%, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. 90 ± 1.5%, the endophytic fungus originated from rods namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp. was 95 ± 2%, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. was 90 ± 2%. While the endophytic originated from rind namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp . was 95 ± 1.5 % and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. was 80 ± 2%. The results of in vivo test of antagonistic endophytic fungi against </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> showed that all of endophytic fungi (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. niger, A. flavus, Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> sp., and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> sp.) have a significant effect in suppressing the growth of mycelium </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">: Endophytic Fungus, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Phytophthora Palmivora</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">, Inhibition, Prevalence, Biological Agents</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">I </span><span class="font2" style="font-variant:small-caps;">I</span><span class="font1" style="font-variant:small-caps;">ntroduction</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Pod rot is important disease in cocoa cultivation in Indonesia recently, and also in some cocoa producer countries [1]. This disease has average about 20-30% per year on damaging cocoa plant all over the world. In some cases as happen in Samoa America, the cocoa did not planted commercially because of this disease [2]. Cocoa was planted around 532.000 ha in Indonesia on the year of 1999. More than 70% cocoa farmers are the farmer in Indonesia. Indonesia is the top third cocoa exporting countries in the world, produce around 335.000 ton per year, with value 294 million US dollars [3].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Indonesia is the biggest archipelago in the world with has 17.000 islands (6000 has inhabitant). Indonesia is tropical country with various climate and humidity from highland to the lowland. Most of the areas are lowland, while the biggest island has a mountain. Tropical climate with rainy season and high humidity in many areas, some </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora</span><span class="font2"> diseases caused a significant damage and uncontrollable. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora</span><span class="font2"> spp. causes disease on the agricultural plants, horticultural and industrial in Indonesia. At least 11 species </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora</span><span class="font2"> has been reported as they resulted in economically yield loss in Indonesia. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora palmivora</span><span class="font2"> has been identified to</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">cause an important disease from economical point of view compared to other </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora</span><span class="font2"> species in Indonesia. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phytophthora palmivora</span><span class="font2"> infected more than 138 plants species, with caused average loss around 2550% on cocoa plant, while </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. capsici</span><span class="font2"> caused 52% of decreasing yield on pepper plant. This disease infected many plants but the demage still not be able to be counted, direct demage through fruit infection is showed by the black rotten. Recently, a set of fruits from young fruit to mature one is very sensitive to the infection. [3].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Endophyitic fungi which have been isolated from the tissue of healthy give an new hope for biocontrol of cocoa pod disease. The study from Mejia </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font2"> (2008) showed that 40% (21/52) of isolated endophytic fungi were able to control </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> [4]</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> One of the isolated fungi which has antagonist characteristic through a simple competition mechanism is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> sp.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">II </span><span class="font1" style="font-variant:small-caps;">research method</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Endophyte Isolation</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Endophytic fungi used in this study were collected from cocoa planted in Tabanan. The survey of cocoa leaf and fruit was done in four different locations at cocoa plantation centre in Tabanan regency. The obtained leaf and fruit were washed by flowing water with following</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">methods: 32 peaces of leaf with size 4 mm<sup>2</sup>, the shoot and the flower were cut from the middle of each, the surface was sterilized in 0,525% sodium hypochlorite for 3 minute, and 70% ethanol for 2 minute; and washed by sterile water for 1 minute; and then put in the PDA media (containing antibiotic livoploxacyne 0,1% (w/v). The</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">grown fungi from the piece of leaf were transferred to the tube containing PDA for storage and classified through morphospecies. In order to isolate the endophyte from rod and fruit, those parts were washed by flowing water and then devided into 8 pieces. Fruit were cut into 16 cubical form (8 mm<sup>3</sup>), 8 from exocarp and 8 from mesocarp. The surfaces were sterilized and storage with similar procedure as isolation from the leaf.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Identification of Endophytic Fungi</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The storage endophytic fungi were grown on petri disc containing PDA and repeated 5 times. The cultures were incubated at dark room and temperature ±27<sup>o</sup>C. The isolate was identified macroscopically after 3 days of culture periode by observe of the colony colour, growth rate, and microscopically identification was done to investigate septa on the hyphae, the spora form/conidia and the sporangiophore. Fungi identification was done by using some reference books of Samson </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font2">., 1981; Pitt and Hocking, 1997; Barnett and Hunter, 1998; Indrawati </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font2">., 1999 [5][6][7][8].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Prevalence of Endophytic Fungi</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Determination of endopyhtic fungi prevalence were done based on frequency of endophytic fungi isolate were found (eight pieces from leaf, rod, flower and fruit) per petri disc, divided by all founded isolates timed by 100%. The number of isolate prevalence will be determining the dominancy of the endophytic fungi on the healthy cocoa plant.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Inhibition Assay of Endophytic Fungi to the Pathogen</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The ability of each endophytic fungi to inhibit the growth of pathogenic </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> was tested by dual culture technique (one pathogen colony in the middle and two endophytic fungi next to the pathogen, side by side). Their inhibition can be calculated as follow [9][10]:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A – B</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Inhibitory (%) = &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;x 100</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A = colony diameter </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> in the single culture (mm)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">B = colony diameter </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2"> in the dual culture (mm).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">In Vivo Antagonistic Assay</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In vivo antagonistic assay of endophytic fungi was done by impaling the fresh fruit by small spleden needle for 20 times, and then covered by the spore of antagonist fungi (spore from one petri dish was dissolved in 250 ml sterile aquadest), then dyed into the spore of pathogenic fungi (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2">). The assays were setting as follows: A = &nbsp;control (without covering by antagonist)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">B = Antagonist treatment 1 (spore suspension 5x10<sup>7</sup>)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">C = Antagonist treatment 2 (spore suspension 5x10<sup>7</sup>)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">D = Antagonist treatment 3 (spore suspension 5x10<sup>7</sup>)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">E = Antagonist treatment 4 (spore suspension 5x10<sup>7</sup>) F = Antagonist treatment 5 (spore suspension 5x10<sup>7</sup>)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">All of the treatment was repeated 5 times. Experiment was designed by random group design, and after variance analysis by ANOVA following by signi-ficance test at level of 5%. The infection parameters were measured by counting the number of infected impale divide by all impale (20 times) timed by 100%.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">III</span><span class="font1" style="font-variant:small-caps;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;results and analysis</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Endophytic Fungi</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The results showed that endophytic fungi obtained from cocoa plant namely the endophyte from leaf were 7 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Micelia sterilia</span><span class="font2"> with, 2 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus </span><span class="font2">spp., 3 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus niger</span><span class="font2">, 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus flavus</span><span class="font2">, 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font2"> sp., 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mucor</span><span class="font2"> sp., 5 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. and 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Verticillium</span><span class="font2"> sp. Endophyte from the rod were 5 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Micelia sterilia</span><span class="font2">, 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Botryoderma</span><span class="font2"> sp. 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Dactylium</span><span class="font2"> sp. 2 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font2"> sp., 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Oidium</span><span class="font2"> sp. 5 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp. and 5 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. The endophyte obtained from the skin fruit were 3 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Micelia sterilia</span><span class="font2">, 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cylindrocarpon</span><span class="font2"> sp., 2 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font2"> sp. 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mortierella</span><span class="font2"> sp. 5 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neuro-spora</span><span class="font2"> spp., 1 isolate of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Septocylindrium</span><span class="font2"> sp. and 7 isolates of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. as showed in Table 1 and Fig. 1.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Endophytic fungi normally exist without any symptoms (asymptomatically) in the tissue of the host plant and they have strong attachment to their host. There are two main reasons of thus attachment i.e. first, they are growing indicated that endophyte is able to found in all kind of plant with high abundance and vary. Most of this endophyte are found in internal infection site at leaf, root, rod and skin then transmitted horizontally through the spore. Secondly, the endophyte can produce mycotoxin and convert the physiology and morphology of the host plant. Mycotoxin from endophyte has an advantage to the host plant as acquired plant defenses to face herbivore insect and grass host [11].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Endophyte from leaf and rod of Endofit yang berasal dari daun dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hevea brasiliensis</span><span class="font2"> which frequent to found are the genus of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penicillium, Pestalotiopsis</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> [12]. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font2"> produced bioactive compound from the host which contain insecticide activity, cytotoxic and anticancer [13]. The study from Amin </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font2">. (2014) have found 6 genus of endophytic fungi in VSD M.05 resistant cocoa plant, namely </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Curvularia</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Geotrichum</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Gliocladium</span><span class="font2"> sp., and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Colletotrichum</span><span class="font2"> sp., and another four were not be able to identified [14].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Prevalence of Endophytic Fungi</span></p>

While in the endophyte from the fruit skin is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. 35%</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(Table 1). </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp., are the dominant genus in south part of China (4 isolates, 23,5%). It has been reported that endophytic fungi can produce antitumor or antifungi activity. The fungi which has been isolated from</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">the skin fruit of medicinal plant including </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Pasecilomyces </span><span class="font2">sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cephalosporium</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mortierella</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mucor</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> sp., and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cladosporium</span><span class="font2"> sp. <p><span class="font2">IV</span><span class="font1" style="font-variant:small-caps;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;conclusion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Endophytic fungi which have been found on the leaf, rod and fruit skin of healthy cocoa are 15 species, with prevalence of endophytic fungi from healthy leaf are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Mecelia sterilia</span><span class="font2"> (sterile hifa) around 30%, on the endophyte from healthy rod it’s found mycelia sterilia, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp. and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. around 25% of each. While at the endophyte originated from fruit skin, it’s found </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. around 35%. The </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in vitro </span><span class="font2">antagonistic assay results of endophytic fungi to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">palmivora</span><span class="font2"> showed that at the endophyte from leaf its obtained </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergilus</span><span class="font2"> spp. around 80±2%, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. niger</span><span class="font2"> 90±2%, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. flavus</span><span class="font2"> 100%, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. around 90±1,5%. On the endophyte from rod, it’s obtained 95±2% of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp. and 90±2% of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. While endophyte from the fruit skin, its obtained 95±1,5% of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> spp. and 80±2% of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> spp. The result of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in vivo</span><span class="font2"> antagonistic assay showed that all of endophytic fungi (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aspergillus</span><span class="font2"> sp., </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A. niger, A. flavus</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Neurospora</span><span class="font2"> sp., dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Trichoderma</span><span class="font2"> sp.) have a significant effect on inhibition the mycelium growth of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">P. palmivora</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
