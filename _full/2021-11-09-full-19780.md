<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstract</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Penerjemahan mengacu kepada menerjemahkan makna dari suatu unit bahasa secara keseluruhan atau sebagian, dari suatu bahasa ke bahasa yang lain. Dalam menerjemahkan suatu bahasa ke bahasa yang lain, penerjemah menggunakan beberapa tipe prosedur yang berguna dalam penerjemahan. Prosedur penerjemahan adalah sebuah langkah untuk menyelesaikan suatu terjemahan. Prosedur hanya diterapkan untuk kalimat atau bagian terkecil dari bahasa, seperti klausa, frasa, kata, dan lain sebagainya. Prosedur penerjemahan yang diterapkan dalam sebuah penerjemahan memberikan pengaruh bagi Bahasa Sasaran; oleh karena itu, tujuan dari pembelajaran ini adalah untuk mengetahui tipe prosedur penerjemahan yang diterapkan dalam sumber data dan pengaruhnya terhadap Bahasa Sasaran. Data dari pembelajaran ini diambil dari novel berjudul The Hunger Games:Mockingjay karya dari Suzanne Collins (2010) dan terjemahannya dalam bahasa Indonesia oleh Hesti Rusli dengan judul yang sama The Hunger Games: Mockingjay. Data dianalisis berdasarkan prosedur-prosedur translation oleh Peter Newmark dalam bukunya yang berjudul A Textbook of Translation (1988). Data dikumpulkan dengan menggunakan metode dokumentasi serta teknik membaca dan mencatat. Data yang telah dikumpulkan dianalisis secara deskriptif and kualitatif. Hasil dari data yang dianalisis menunjukkan bahwa beberapa tipe prosedur diterapkan dari Bahasa Sumber ke Bahasa Sasaran. 10 dari 20 prosedur yang ditemukan telah dianalisis. Pengaruh yang diberikan oleh prosedur penerjemahan berguna untuk menyesuaikan budaya Bahasa Sumber ke dalam budaya Bahasa Sasaran yang juga berguna bagi pembaca karena cerita menjadi lebih dimengerti dan lebih menarik.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords: Penerjemahan, Prosedur Penerjemahan, Pengaruh</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Translation is the process of turning an original or &quot;source&quot; text into a text in another language. Translation has an important role in communication and information. Without translation, the exchange between cultures could never happen. Translation is useful to adjusting SL culture to TL culture without changing the meaning or message of SL. In translation, there are some procedures that are used. Procedure means an established or official way of doing something. Meanwhile, translation procedure is a method applied by translators when they formulate equivalence for the purpose of transferring elements of meaning from the SL the TL (Delisle, 1999:139). Translation procedures are used for sentences and the smaller units of language (Newmark, 1988:81). There are some different translation procedures that are commonly used, they are: Transference, Naturalization, Cultural Equivalent, Functional Equivalent, Descriptive Equivalent, Synonymy, Through Translation, Shifts or Transpositions, Modulation, Recognized Translation, Translation Label, Compensation, Componential Analysis, Reduction and Expansion, Paraphrase, Couplets, Other Procedures (Equivalence, Adaptation), and Notes, Addition, Glosses. Based on the title of this study, English is used as source language (SL) and Bahasa as target language (TL). The topic was relevant to be discussed because the novel contains some different procedures. The topic was interesting to be discussed because the influences of different procedures from SL to TL are occurred in some translations.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe the influences that are given by the procedures used in the translation of English novel </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay</span><span class="font1"> into Indonesian.</span></p></li></ul>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were taken from a novel entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay </span><span class="font1">written by Suzanne Collins (2010) which is translated into Indonesian by Hetih Rusli (2014) with the same title </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There were some techniques in collecting the data. First, reading the novel in both versions, then identifying and classifying the procedures used from source language to target language. Next, making a note based on the collected data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were analyzed by using descriptive and qualitative method. The data were analyzed based on translation procedures proposed by Newmark (1988). The techniques of analyzing the data are; first, identifying the procedures used to translate the source language to target language. Next, classifying the types of procedures, then sorting the data, and the last analyzing the chosen data based on translation procedures.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Procedures in The Translation of English Novel </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Hunger Games: Mockingjay</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> into Indonesian</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transference</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Transference or loan word is transferal of a word from the SL directly into the TL without translating it at all. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : The odd </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">reporter</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Reporter </span><span class="font1">yang aneh itu.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The influence of Transference is transferring SL words which is commonly used in TL. The SL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">reporter</span><span class="font1"> is translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">reporter</span><span class="font1"> in TL. The word</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">from SL into TL is not translated at all because it is considered common word which is often used in TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naturalization</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Naturalization is a procedure where translation is done by transferring and adapting the words in the SL into the normal pronunciation in TL. For example: SL : In my head I hear </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">President </span><span class="font1">Snow’s words.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Kata-kata </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Presiden </span><span class="font1">Snow berdentam dalam kepalaku.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the example above, SL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">president</span><span class="font1"> is translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">presiden</span><span class="font1"> in TL, where the letter ‘t’ is deleted. The pronunciation of SL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">presidents</span><span class="font1"> with TL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">presiden</span><span class="font1"> is not completely different, so the naturalness of the translation could be seen.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cultural Equivalent</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Cultural Equivalents is used in which the cultural word in SL is translated based on TL culture word. For example:</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark24"></a>SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Homeless</span><span class="font1">.</span><br><br><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark25"></a>TL &nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Gelandangan.</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">In the example above, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">homeless</span><span class="font1"> means ‘a person without home or living on the streets’, in TL, there’s a cultural term </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">gelandangan</span><span class="font1"> which has the same meaning as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">homeless.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Descriptive Equivalent</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Descriptive equivalent is translating a SL text or word using a description in the TL. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Natural thing</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Makhluk hidup yang bukan hasil rekayasa.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through Translation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Through Translation is literal translation of collocations and combinations, where the most obvious example is the name of organizations. For example: SL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Justice Building</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark29"></a>TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Gedung Pengadilan</span><span class="font1">.</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">The translation by using Through Translation doesn’t give many changes because the translator covers the exact information from SL into TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Componential Analysis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Componential analysis means a basic process to compare a SL word with TL word, by demonstrating their common and then their differing sense components. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Something I have withheld from them: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">credit.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Apa yang selama ini kutahan: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">penghargaaan.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1">In example above, the words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">credit</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">penghargaan</span><span class="font1"> are considered an object and a praise or approval for something good given to someone who get an achievement. <ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reduction and Expansion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Reduction and expansion mean adding or removing elements in translation. For example</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reduction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Struggling to keep up with her on his </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">chubby toddler legs</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Berjalan tertatih-tatih dengan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">kaki balita</span><span class="font1">nya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In example above, if </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">chubby toddler legs</span><span class="font1"> is translated literally, it means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kaki balita gemuk.</span><span class="font1"> There is a deletion of the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">gemuk</span><span class="font1"> in TL, so that </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">chubby toddler legs</span><span class="font1"> is changed into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kaki balita.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Expansion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : The children are being </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">educated</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark39"></a>TL : Anak-anak </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">dididik di sekolah</span><span class="font1">.</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">In example above, the SL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">educated</span><span class="font1"> literally only means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dididik.</span><span class="font1"> There is an additional information ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">di sekolah’</span><span class="font1"> which is placed after </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dididik.</span><span class="font1"> In TL, it could give new information that </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dididik</span><span class="font1"> is implemented at school</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>8. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Synonymy</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Synonymy is translating a SL text or word or expression with TL expression that is nearly equivalent. Here are synonymies found in the novel:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : She </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">purses </span><span class="font1">her lips.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL &nbsp;&nbsp;: Prajurit York hanya </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">memonyongkan </span><span class="font1">bibirnya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In example above, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">purses</span><span class="font1"> is translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">memonyongkan. </span><span class="font1">Literally, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">purses</span><span class="font1"> means ‘mengerucutkan’ in TL. ‘mengerucutkan’ has one equivalent word in TL as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">memonyongkan</span><span class="font1"> where both words are often used to show ‘the condition of lips or the shape of lips’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>9. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paraphrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Paraphrase is a translation procedure whereby the translator replaces a word in the source text by a group of words or an expression in the target text. For example:</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark45"></a>SL &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">The brains.</span><br><br><span class="font1"><a name="bookmark46"></a>TL &nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Kelompok-kelompok cerdas</span><span class="font1">.</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">In example above, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the brains</span><span class="font1"> literally means ‘otak’ in TL, but based on the context of the plot, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the brains</span><span class="font1"> refers to ‘a group of smart people’, and ‘otak’ does not refer to it, therefore ‘otak’ needs to be rephrased into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kelompok-kelompok cerdas.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark47"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark48"></a>10. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Couplet</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Couplet is procedure where translation is done by using more than one procedure. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark49"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark50"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Synonymy – Paraphrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Gale and Beetee’s </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">brainchild</span><span class="font1">. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Collins, 2010:360)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Itu </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">hasil kecerdasan </span><span class="font1">Gale dan Beetee. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(Rusli, 2014:389)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Two procedures, Synonymy and Paraphrase are applied from SL word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">brainchild</span><span class="font1"> into TL phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hasil kecerdasan.</span><span class="font1"> Literally, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">brainchild</span><span class="font1"> means ‘gagasan’ or ‘ide’ in TL. ‘gagasan’ or ‘ide’ has an equivalent word as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hasil kecerdasan</span><span class="font1"> where these words usually refer to ‘the result of thinking’. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brainchild</span><span class="font1"> means ‘gagasan’ literally, but the literal meaning has to be rephrased into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hasil kecerdasan.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark51"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark52"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis of the previous chapter, it can be concluded that some procedures are applied in English-Indonesian translation of novel </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Hunger Games:Mockingjay.</span><span class="font1"> There are 18 procedures based on Peter Newmark’s book entitled A Textbook of Translation (1988) and 10 of them are found as procedures applied in the novel, they are; Transference, Naturalization, Cultural Equivalent, Descriptive Equivalent, Through Translation, Componential Analysis, Reduction and Expansion, Synonymy, Paraphrase, and Couplet. The procedures are mostly found in the translation of words or phrases from source language into target language. Translation procedures found based on the analysis cause some source language words or phrases to be clearer, understandable, and adjusted towards the </span><span class="font0">62</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">culture of target language. There is no change of the message or the meaning which given by the text, although some procedures are applied or used from source language into target language.</span></p>
