<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>The Use Of Preposition “With” With Reference To The Corpus Of Contemporary American English</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I Wayan Budi Juni Ardana<sup>1*</sup>, I Gede Budiasa<sup>2</sup>, I Nengah Sudipa<sup>3 </sup></span><span class="font1"><sup>123</sup>English Department Faculty Of Arts, Udayana University <sup>1</sup>[</span><a href="mailto:budi.juni@yahoo.co.id"><span class="font1">budi.juni@yahoo.co.id</span></a><span class="font1">] <sup>2</sup>[</span><a href="mailto:budiasa.igede@yahoo.com"><span class="font1">budiasa.igede@yahoo.com</span></a><span class="font1">] <sup>3</sup>[</span><a href="mailto:nengahsudipa@yahoo.co.id"><span class="font1">nengahsudipa@yahoo.co.id</span></a><span class="font1">]</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Corresponding Author</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengidentifikasi bentuk frasa, menganalisa hubungan makna dan menjelaskan fungsi dan makna dari preposisi “with”. Data yang dikumpulkan dari bank kata (corpus) yang bisa diakses melalui internet. Data dikategorikan berdasarkan teori yang dikemukakan oleh Quirk (1985), dan dianalisis secara deskriptif dengan metode kuantitatif. Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukan bahwa bentuk suatu frasa bisa diawali oleh “with” dan diikuti oleh objek preposisi dan kata keterangan. Hubungan makna antara “with” dengan elemen lainnya menunjukkan kelakuan, sarana dan prasarana. Fungsi yang ditunjukan hanyalah fungsi “adjunct”, “disjunct” dan “postmodifier”. Sementara makna yang ditunjukan hanyalah makna dari “accompaniment”, “pervasive”, “manner”, “means and instrument”, “support and opposition” dan “having”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: preposisi, bentuk, semantic, fungsi, makna</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Prepositions introduce prepositional phrases, and express the relation of position, place, time, possession, manner, etc. Many prepositional phrases seem adverbial in meaning. However, the same prepositional phrases may be used as adjectives, adverbs, or nouns. Furthermore, the preposition is the part of grammar in English. Mostly the sentences use the preposition inside to make overt the covert meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The problems discussed in this present study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What forms of prepositional phrase with preposition “with” are used in the</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Corpus of Contemporary American English</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How the semantic relations occur between the preposition “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with</span><span class="font1">” and other elements found in the Corpus of Contemporary American English</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are functions and meanings of the preposition “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with</span><span class="font1">” used in the Corpus of Contemporary American English</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe the forms of prepositional phrase with preposition “with” that are used with reference to the Corpus of Contemporary American English.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the semantic relations between the preposition “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with</span><span class="font1">” and other elements found in the Corpus of Contemporary American English.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the function and meanings of English prepositions “with” that is found in the Corpus of Contemporary American English.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data was taken from Corpus of Contemporary American English. The Corpus of Contemporary American English is chosen as the data source because it contains the data related to this study and the content was taken from many literary works in 1990-2012. Furthermore, at least 1000 examples were taken from the data source in order to strengthen the result.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">This study used documentation method, including downloading, and note-taking technique in order to collect the data. Furthermore, the quantitative method was applied to analyze the collected data. The technique was furthermore analyzed descriptively and quantitatively, because of the nature of the research question. The data was analyzed based on the theory proposed by Quirk (1985) and the result was presented descriptively.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Form of Preposition &quot;with&quot;</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The form of prepositional phrases containing the preposition “with” has some characteristics. According to the data source, the prepositional phrase will begin with the preposition “with” and is followed by modifier or no modifier and a noun phrase or clause as the object of the preposition.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(5-1) She was on her feet, working next to people </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with bad feet</span><span class="font1">, … (5-2) Individuals </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with higher sexual compulsivity scores</span><span class="font1"> reported …</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Semantic Relations between Preposition &quot;with&quot; and the Other Element</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The meaning of preposition “with” is influenced by other elements in the sentence, such as the verb and the object of preposition. As one of the prepositions, “with” has no meaning without other elements. According to Quirk (1985), the preposition “with” only indicates the semantic role of process showing the semantic relation of process between the preposition and other elements, including manner, means and instrument.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-3) ... a lot of run on morphine,&quot; Madonna says </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with a laugh</span><span class="font1">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-4) …; (b) circumferential; (c) circumferential </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with crosshatching</span><span class="font1">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-5) Teachers were provided </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with a wireless microphone</span><span class="font1"> to enable the researchers…</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Function of Preposition &quot;with&quot;</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The function of preposition is divided into five categories. They are postmodifier in nounphrase, adjunct, disjunct, subjunct and conjunct. The preposition “with” indicates the function of postmodifier in the nounphrase, adjunct and disjunct in the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(5-6) A vast pond </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with ducks and geese</span><span class="font1"> is great to circumnavigate … (5-7) …education of young children </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with severe physical impairment … </span><span class="font1">(5-8) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">With his head bowed</span><span class="font1">, he took the path past the store …</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Meaning of Preposition &quot;with&quot;</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to Quirk (1985; 673), mostly the preposition “with” indicate the meaning of means spectrum. The means spectrum can be divided into six categories. They are manner, means and instrument, agentive, support and opposition, stimulus, and accompaniment. However, some examples indicate another meaning, including the meaning of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">having</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Pervasive</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to Hornby (2005: 1094), pervasive has the meaning of something spreading widely throughout an area. Based on data source, the preposition “with” is rarely denoting the pervasive meaning. There are two examples or 0,2 % of 1000 examples are taken from the Corpus of Contemporary American English. Therefore, the meaning of the pervasive meaning is rarely conveyed by the preposition “with” in the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(5-9) Staff cell phones buzz all day </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with questions sent by text</span><span class="font1"> …</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Manner</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the data source, the example of preposition “with” denoting the manner is sixty examples. Around 1000 examples are taken from the corpus and 6 % of all the examples are denoting the meaning of manner. Therefore, the meaning of the manner is sometimes conveyed by the preposition “with” in the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(5-10) ... a lot of run on morphine,&quot; Madonna says </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with a laugh</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Means and instrument</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The preposition &quot;with&quot; followed by instrument will indicate the meaning of instrument. The existence of prepositional phrase denoting the instrument meaning can be omitted according to the context. Around 1000 examples are taken from the corpus and 15, 2 % of all the examples are denoting the meaning of means and instrument. (5-11) Teachers were provided </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with a wireless microphone</span><span class="font1"> to … (5-12) …; (b) circumferential; (c) circumferential </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with crosshatching</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.4.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Accompaniment</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The preposition &quot;with&quot; denoting the meaning of accompaniment helps the readers to expand the focus or describes other objects related to the sentence. Around 1000 examples are taken from the corpus and 74, 5 % of all the examples are denoting the accompaniment.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(5-13) On television, he worked </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with Art Carney and Red Buttons</span><span class="font1"> ...</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.4.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Support and Opposition</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Quirk (1985: 702) explained that the preposition &quot;with&quot; denoting support indicated the solidarity or movement of sympathy. The preposition &quot;with&quot; denoting opposition conveys the idea of opposition between people. The keyword of opposition is 'fight with', 'quarrel with', and 'argue with'. Around 1000 examples are taken from the corpus and 1, 8 % of all the examples are denoting the meaning of support and opposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-14) Let's let people who </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">disagree with</span><span class="font1"> it come and testify…</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-15) Mr. BASHEER: I </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">agree with</span><span class="font1"> Dr. Abu Jaber, that every time…</span></p></li>
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.4.6 Preposition &quot;with&quot; Denoting Support and Opposition</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to Quirk (1985: 704), the notion of 'having' is more generally expressed by preposition &quot;with&quot;, especially with concrete attributes. Around 1000 examples are taken from the corpus and 1, 4 % of all the examples are denoting the meaning of having.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(5-16) About 80 percent of people </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">with asthma</span><span class="font1"> find that exercise can cause...</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The form of prepositional phrases containing the preposition “with” has some characteristics. According to the data source, the prepositional phrase will begin with the preposition “with” and is followed by modifier or no modifier and a noun phrase or clause as the object of the preposition. The preposition “with” only indicates the semantic role of process showing the semantic relation of process between the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">preposition and other elements, including manner, means and instrument. The preposition “with” indicates the function of postmodifier in noun phrase, adjunct and disjunct in the sentence. According to the examples in the data source, the preposition “with” indicates the meaning of agentive spectrum, including pervasive, accompaniment, manner, means and instrument, support and opposition, and having.</span></p>
