<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Dengue hemorrhagic fever (DHF) is a disease caused by dengue virus infection where the vector is the female </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font4"> mosquito. DHF causes high mortality and morbidity because a vaccine for DHF has not yet been found. DHF therapy is still supportive and symptomatic, causing this disease to remain a health problem for the community. The purpose of this study was to determine the effectiveness of kecombrang flower (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font4">) as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font4"> instar III larvicide. This research was conducted at the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Institute of Tropical Disease</span><span class="font4"> (ITD) Universitas Airlangga in July 2023. The research design used was true experimental design with </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">post-test only group control design</span><span class="font4">. Divided into 7 groups, namely negative control, 0.1%, 0.6%, 1.1%, 1.6%, 2.1% and 2.6%. The number of samples used in the study was 560 larvae. Each group contained 20 larvae with 50ml of solution containing kecombrang flower extract. Repetition was done 4 times at each concentration. The test used was </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Kruskal-Wallis</span><span class="font4"> (p&lt;0.05), </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Post-Hoc Mann Whitney U test</span><span class="font4"> (p&lt;0.05). The results showed the highest average larval mortality at concentrations of 2.1% and 2.6% at 100% and the lowest average at a concentration of 0.1% at 28.75%. In the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Kruskal-Wallis test</span><span class="font4">, each treatment group showed significant differences (p&lt;0.05). The </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Mann Whitney U post hoc</span><span class="font4"> test showed significant differences between the two treatment groups.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font4">Aedes aegypti, Kecombrang flower (Etlingera elatior), Larvacide</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4">Demam berdarah dengue (DBD) merupakan penyakit yang disebabkan oleh infeksi virus dengue dimana vektornya adalah nyamuk </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font4"> betina. DBD menyebabkan angka kematian dan kesakitan yang tinggi karena vaksin DBD belum ditemukan. Terapi DBD yang masih bersifat suportif dan simtomatik menyebabkan penyakit ini masih menjadi masalah kesehatan masyarakat. Tujuan penelitian ini adalah untuk mengetahui efektivitas bunga kecombrang (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font4">) sebagai larvasida nyamuk </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font4"> instar III. Penelitian ini dilakukan di </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Institute of Tropical Disease</span><span class="font4"> (ITD) Universitas Airlangga pada bulan Juli 2023. Desain penelitian yang digunakan adalah true eksperimen design dengan post-test only group control design. Dibagi menjadi 7 kelompok yaitu kontrol negatif, 0,1%, 0,6%, 1,1%, 1,6%, 2,1% dan 2,6%. Jumlah sampel yang digunakan dalam penelitian adalah 560 larva. Setiap kelompok berisi 20 larva dengan 50 ml larutan yang mengandung ekstrak bunga kecombrang. Pengulangan dilakukan sebanyak 4 kali pada masing-masing konsentrasi. Uji yang digunakan adalah uji </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Kruskal-Wallis</span><span class="font4"> (p&lt;0,05), uji </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Post-Hoc Mann Whitney U</span><span class="font4"> (p&lt;0,05). Hasil penelitian menunjukkan rata-rata kematian larva tertinggi pada konsentrasi 2,1% dan 2,6% sebesar 100% dan rata-rata terendah pada konsentrasi 0,1% sebesar 28,75%. Pada uji </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Kruskal-Wallis</span><span class="font4"> setiap kelompok perlakuan menunjukkan perbedaan yang signifikan (p&lt;0,05). Uji </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">post hoc Mann Whitney</span><span class="font4"> U menunjukkan perbedaan yang signifikan antara kedua kelompok perlakuan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font4">, bunga kecombrang, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font4">, larvasida</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">EFFECTIVENESS OF KECOMBRANG FLOWER (ETLINGERA ELATIOR) AS A LARVACIDE… Ni Putu Ita Destriani1*, Risma2, Irma A. Pasaribu3, Etik A. Rohmah4,5</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">INTRODUCTIONS</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) cauases high mortality and morbidity due to the absence of a DHF vaccine and the primarily supportive and symptomatic nature of its treatment. This disease is transmitted by the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Dengue</span><span class="font3"> virus through the bites of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes albopictus </span><span class="font3">mosquitoes <sup>1,2</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In 2019, Southeast Asia recorded 658,301 cases of DHF, with Indonesia being one of the five countries in the region with the highest DHF cases<sup>3</sup>. In January to September 2022, there were 87,501 DHF cases and 816 deaths in Indonesia<sup>4</sup>. East Java reported 8,894 cases and 110 deaths <sup>5</sup>, with Surabaya ranking third, following West Java and Bali, in the number of DHF cases in 2018 form January to October<sup>6</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Mosquito population control is crucial to combat DHF cases. Temephos 1% is a commonly used chemical larvicide. However, its long-term use can lead to mosquito resistance, and it can also contaminate dringking water<sup>7</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The use of natural substances as an alternative is being considered due to their environmentally friendly nature. Local plants, such as Etlingera elatior, also known as “Kecombrang flowers,” cam serve as larvacides. Kecombrang flowers contain useful larvicidal components, such as alkaloids, flavonoids, tannins and saponins<sup>8</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Alkaloid in kecombrang flowers are toxic to insect nerves, while flavonoid inhibit the insect’s respiratory system. Tannin disrupt the metabolism of the insect’s digestive system by inhibiting protease enzymes. Saponins lead to an inflammatory process in the insect’s gastrointestinal mucosa, causing fluid extravasation and significant loss of body fluid<sup>8,9</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Based on above explanation, the researchers are interested in studying the effectiveness of kecombrang flowers (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font3">) as a larvicide for </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> instar III. The concentrations used in this study are 0.1%, 1.1%, 1.6%, 2.1% and 2.6%. The extraction method used in this study is maceration woth 96% ethanol as the solvent.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The goal of this research is to determine the effectiveness of kecombrang flowers (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font3">) extracts as a larvicide for </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> instar III. This study aims to measure the effectiveness of kecombrang leaves on the mortality of third instar </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">MATERIAL AND METHOD</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Materials</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The equipment used in this research includes a micropipette, blender, rotatory evaporator, cloth, vacuum, spatula rod, digital scale, sieve, Erlenmeyer flask, measuringglas and reaction tube.The materials used consist of kecombrnag flowers &nbsp;(</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera &nbsp;elatior</span><span class="font3">), &nbsp;distilled water</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(aquadest), tween 20, 96% ethanol and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> instar III. </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Methods</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This study is a true-experimental research with a post-test only group control design. The research was conducted at the Entomology Laboratory of the Institute of Tropical Disease (ITD), Universitas Airlangga, Surabaya. The study consist of seven groups, including one negative control (aquadest 50ml) and six treatment groups with concetration 0.1%, 0.6%, 1.1%, 1.6%, 2.1% and 2.6%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Larva samples were obtained from rearing at Entomology Labpratory ITD, Universitas Airlangga. The larvicide test used third instar Aedes aegypti larvae that were not fed for 24 hours. The samples used were </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> larvae that had reached instar III. Each treatment group had 20 larvae with four repetitions as calculated usingthe Freeder formula.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Data collection was carried out after 24 hours of larval exposure to the extract. The extract is considered effective if the statistical test result yield p&lt;0,05. Data analysis was conducted in staged, starting with a normality test using the Saphiro-Wilk test to determine wheter the data was normally distributed. Subsewuently, a homogeinity test was performed using the Lavene test to asses data homogeinity. If the result of both normality and homogeneity test had p&gt;0,05, a One-Way ANOVA test would be conducted. However, in this study, the data did not follow a normal distribution and were nit homogeneous, so a non-parametric Kruskal-Wallis test was used. If the Kruskal-Wallis test yield p&lt;0,05, a post hoc Mann Whitney-U test would be conducted to identify groups with significant differences.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Preparations of Kecombrang Flower (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior) </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Extract</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This study used 5.5 kg of kecombrang flowers were obtained from Bangli, Bali. After that, the kecombrang flower will be checked and adjusted to the morphological characteristics of the kecombrang flower. The flowers were washed with running water until clean and the drained. the flower petals were cut into smaller pieces and dried in a shaded area. Once dried, the flowers were ground into a fine powder using a blender and sieved to obtain kecombrang simplisia powder.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The extraction process involve macerating the simplisia powder with 96% ethanol solvent for 24 hours to ensure complete immersion, with stirring every 8 hours. The macerate was poured and flitered, and the residue was macerated again with the solvent for a total of three repetitions to extract all secondary metabolites. All the solvent macerates obtained were the evaporated using a rotatory evaporator, resulting. in a concentrated extract 41.5g as semi-solid.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Preparation of Concentration Series</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The kecombrang flower extract was made into a 2.6% stock solution, which was the diluted to create required concentration series. The stock solution was prepared using the formula % = (g/ml) x 100%. To dissolve the extract, 16,9g was mixed with 60ml of aquadest. The concentration series of 0.1%, 0.6%, 1.1%, 1.6%, 2.1% and 2.6% were created using the dilution formula V1 x M1 = V2 x M2. In the larvicidal activity test, there were 7 treatment groups with diluted kecombrang flower extract.</span></p>

<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">DISCUSSION</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The reaseacrh result indicate that larval mortality is directly proportional to the concentration of the given extract. this is because a greater number of secondary metabolites contained within the extract, such as flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, ans saponin enter the larvae’s bodies. The varying result can be attributed to the mosquito’s threshold for detoxifying substance that enter its body<sup>10</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Flavonoid compounds can cause paralysis in the insect’s respiratory muscle system. Alkaloids work by inhibiting acethycholinesterase enzymes, which can result in nerve damage and the degradation of insect cell walls. Tannins inhibit protease enzymes, and larval digestive activity decreases, causing iesect to obtain less food. Saponins trigger an inflammatory process in the insect’s gastrointestinal mucosa, leading to decreased appetite. Furthermore, saponons cause fluid extravasation in the larva’s body, resulting in significant </span><span class="font1">11,12 </span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The result of this research show better effectiveness compared to the study conducted by Palgunadi<sup>11</sup>. In Palginadi’s study, a mortality rate of 99,2% was obtained at a concentrations of 15%. This difference can be attributed to various factors such as extraction method, solvent use, and the geographical location of sample collection. The maceration method was chosen due to its simplicity and the use of basic equipment, preventing damage to thermolabile substances like flavonoids, alkaloids, and tannins. The use of 96% ethanols as the solvent was based on its high polarity, ensuring optimal compound extraction. This is because the active compounds in plant have nearly the same polarity as 96% ethanol<sup>13</sup>. Geographical location affects air humidity, soil quality, rainfall, and plant age, all of which influence the secondary metabolites contained in kecombrang flowers<sup>14</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">CONCLUSIONS</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The concentrations of kecombrang flower (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Etlingera elatior</span><span class="font3">) extract at 0.1%, 0.6%, 1.1%, 1.6%, 2.1%, and 2.6% are</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">effective for use as larvacides for </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Aedes aegypti</span><span class="font3"> instar III mosquitoes.</span></p>
