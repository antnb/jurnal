<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstract</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">This study entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Morphological Processes of Noun Forming Derivational Affixes in Dickens’ A Christmas Carol </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">aims to analyze the word class of base forms and derivational affixes forming nouns used in a fiction novel. The data were taken from the novel entitled A Christmas Carol written by Charles Dickens in 1843 by applying documentation method and note-taking techniques. The technique of analyzing data was done by classifying the derivational affixes forming nouns according to type of affixes and reducing the words containing derivational affixes that were similar. The data were analyzed by applying the theory of how new words are formed as derivations in morphology by Katamba (1993) as the main theory and the theory by Bauer (1983) as the supporting one. The findings showed that there were three classes of the base form used to produce new noun-based lexeme in the data, namely verb, adjective and noun. Types of affixes that were used were only suffixes, namely suffix –al, -er, -ar, -or, -ing, -y, -ion, -ation, -ment, -ance, and –age which are attached to verb base, suffix –ity, -th, -ness, and –ence which are attached to adjective base and suffix –y which are attached to noun base.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">: noun, morphological process, affixation, derivational affixes</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk menemukan kelas kata dari kata dasar dan imbuhan kata derivasi yang membentuk kata benda pada sebuah novel fiksi. Data diambil dari novel berjudul A Christmas Carol karya Charles Dickens pada tahun 1843 dengan mengaplikasikan metode dokumentasi dan teknik mencatat. Teknik menganalisis data dilakukan dalam dua langkah yaitu mengklasifikasi imbuhan kata derivasi yang membentuk kata benda sesuai dengan tipe-tipe imbuhan dan menghilangkan kata yang memiliki imbuhan yang sama. Data dianalisis dengan menggunakan teori tentang bagaimana kata baru terbentuk sebagai derivasi di morfologi yang dikemukakan oleh Katamba (1993) dan Bauer (1983). Hasil analisis menunjukkan bahwa ada tiga kelas kata yang digunakan untuk membentuk kata benda baru, yaitu kata kerja, kata sifat dan kata benda itu sendiri. Jenis imbuhan yang digunakan hanya berupa akhiran, yaitu akhiran –al, -er, -ar, -or, -ing, -y, -ion, -ation, -ment, -ance, dan –age ditambahkan pada kelas kata kerja, akhiran –ity, -th, -ness, dan –ence yang ditambahkan pada kelas kata sifat dan akhiran –y yang ditambahkan pada kelas kata benda.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">: kata benda, proses morfologi, afiksasi, imbuhan derivasi</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Morphology as a major level of linguistic analysis plays a considerable role in producing and building thousands of English words (Mhamad, 2013). English derives a huge number of words on a daily basis from all the languages of the world and morphology gives an idea about the source of the words with rules and regulations on how to form new words.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word-formation processes in morphology can be done by affixation, conversion and compounding. There are two kinds of word-building in affixation namely inflectional process and derivational process. This study is concerned on the derivational process. Specifically, this study is focused on the derivational affixes which form nouns in the novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Christmas Carol </span><span class="font2">written by Charles Dickens in 1843.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In analyzing noun forming derivational affixes, theories about how new words are formed as derivations in morphology proposed by Katamba (1993) and Bauer (1983) were applied. Futhermore, the meaning of new noun lexemes were based on the meaning of words in English Dictionary.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study of defining the word formation may benefit people since it can enrich and widen the vocabulary. From a word class, people can make many new words in other word class by deriving the words. By attaching affixes to the base words, new words can be formed. That is why derivational process is important and interesting to be analyzed.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>5.2.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">–er</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> is commonly attached to a verb in order to form a noun. This suffix means a person or thing that does something or the act of something. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The register of his burial was signed by the clergyman, the clerk, the undertaker, and the chief </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">mourner</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. </span><span class="font2">(1834:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The underlined word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mourner,</span><span class="font2"> is the example of word that gets the attachment of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2">. According to its sentence structure, it is categorized as noun because it acts as the head of a noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The new lexeme </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mourner</span><span class="font2"> is a noun which is derived from the verb with the word base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mourn</span><span class="font2">. The addition of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> changes the word-class that the base belong to, from a verb into a noun. The derivational process which occurred is class-changing derivation which produces the new lexeme and belongs to different form class from its base.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> means a person or thing. Thus, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mourner</span><span class="font2"> means a person or thing that mourns. Related to the novel, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">mourner</span><span class="font2"> means the person who is sad for someone’s death.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ity</span><span class="font2"> is commonly attached to an adjective to form a noun. This suffix means the state or condition of something. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Scrooge followed to the window: desperate in his </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">curiosity</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> (1834:32)</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark41"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark42"></a>5.2.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-th</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–th</span><span class="font2"> is commonly attached to an adjective in order to form a noun. This suffix means the condition of something. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">…and means of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">warmth</span><span class="font2">. (1834:13)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The underlined word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> is an example of the word that gets the attachment of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–th</span><span class="font2">. The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> is a noun because it acts as the head of a noun phrase according to its phrase structure.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The new lexeme </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> is a noun derived from an adjective with the word base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warm</span><span class="font2">. The addition of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–th </span><span class="font2">changes the word-class that the base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warm</span><span class="font2"> belongs to, from an adjective to a noun. The derivational process occurring is class-changing derivation which produces the lexeme </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> and belongs to different form class from its base.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–th</span><span class="font2"> means condition.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Thus, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> means the condition of being warm. Related to the novel, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warmth</span><span class="font2"> means kindness or affection.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark43"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark44"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Word Class Maintaining Affixes</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The class-maintaining derivational process involved affixes that are attached to noun. There is one kind of affixes in the data that can be attached to base form with noun as the word class and to produce noun, namely suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y</span><span class="font2"> is sometimes attached to a noun in order to form a new lexeme with the same word-class. This suffix means a</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">person or thing concerned with or characterized by being something. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">…to regard a coffin-nail as the deadest piece of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">ironmongery</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;"> in the trade</span><span class="font2">. (1834:3)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The underlined word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ironmongery</span><span class="font2"> is a word that gets the attachment of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y</span><span class="font2">. According to the sentence structure, the underlined word is categorized as noun because it acts as the head of the noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The new lexeme </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ironmongery</span><span class="font2"> is a noun derived from noun with the word base </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ironmonger.</span><span class="font2"> The addition of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">– y</span><span class="font2"> in this case does not change the wordclass that the base belongs to. Thus, the word-class is still the same, from noun to noun. The derivational process occurring is class-maintaining derivation which produces the new lexeme that belongs to the same form classes as its base. The suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y</span><span class="font2"> means concerned with. Thus, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ironmongery</span><span class="font2"> means something concerned with an ironmonger. Related to the novel, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ironmongery</span><span class="font2"> means the merchandise that is sold in an ironmonger’s shop, like ironware.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark45"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark46"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are some points which can be drawn as the conclusions of the study based on data analyzed about the noun forming derivational affixes found in the novel A Christmas Carol.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The classes of the base form that were used to produce new noun lexemes in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Christmas Carol</span><span class="font2"> novel were verb, adjective and noun with verb as the most used class of the base form. However, there was no adverb found in this process. The derivational processes found were class-changing derivations which produced the new lexemes from verbs and adjectives and class-maintaining derivation which produced the new lexeme from noun.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Types of affixes that were used to produce new noun lexeme in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Christmas Carol</span><span class="font2"> novel were only suffixes, namely suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–al</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-er, -ar, -or, -ing, -y, -ion, -ation, -ment, -ance,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">– age</span><span class="font2"> which were attached to verb base, suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ity, -th, -ness,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ence</span><span class="font2"> which attached to adjective base and suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y </span><span class="font2">which attached to noun base. However, there was no prefix found in this process. The addition of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–al</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-er, -ar, -or, -ing, -y, -ion, -ation, -ment, -ance, –age, – ity, -th, -ness,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ence</span><span class="font2"> changed the word-class that the base belongs to, from a verb and adjective into a noun. Meanwhile, the addition of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–y </span><span class="font2">which was attached to noun did not change the word-class that the base belongs to. Thus, the word-class was still the same, from noun to noun. The suffixes that were attached to verb base had the meanings of action, agent, result, process, and condition. For the ones that were attached to adjective base had the meanings of state or condition. Meanwhile, the suffix attached to noun base had the meaning of state or condition of something. Thus, the derivational processes found in the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Christmas Carol</span><span class="font2"> are only suffixations.</span></p>
