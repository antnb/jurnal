<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal berjudul The Analysis of Conjunction in Political and Business Articles of International Bali Post bertujuan untuk menganalisa jenis dan penggunaan konjungsi yang terdapat pada artikel politik dan bisnis dalam koran International Bali Post. Data yang digunakan diambil dari enam artikel dalam koran International Bali Post yang terbit pada tanggal 24 Mei 2015. Analisa menggunakan metode kualitatif dan teori Kohesi (Language, Context and Text; Aspect of Language in A Social-Semiotic Perspective, di dalam buku Halliday M.A.K and Hasan, 1976). Hasil dari studi ini menunjukan ada beberapa jenis konjungsi dan penggunaan kunjungsi dalam artikel.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Konjungsi, Aditif, Adversatif, Kausal, Temporal</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Newspaper is one of the mass-media, which it is published daily. It contains news, features, advertisements, and correspondence. In the newspaper there are a lot of subjects such as medical, political, economic, business, sports and many others. The analysis of this study is needed in order to know more about the types of conjunction contained in the text in the newspaper, the meaning of conjunction and how it is used in the text. This study focuses on the types and the use of conjunctions found in political and business articles of International Bali Post, which were published on 25 May 2015. There are four types of conjunction: additive, adversative, causal, and temporal. Based on the theory proposed by Halliday and Hasan (1976:320) a conjunction is somewhat different from other cohesive relations. It is based on the assumption that there are in the linguistic form systematic relationships between sentences.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the background, there were two problems focused in this study as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of conjunction were used in the political and business articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">International Bali Post</span><span class="font3">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How were the conjunction used in political and business articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">International Bali Post</span><span class="font3">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The aims of the study are classified into two parts:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the types of conjunction used in the Political and Business articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">International Bali Post</span><span class="font3">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the use of conjunction in the Political and Business articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">International Bali Post</span><span class="font3">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Research method consists of data source, method and technique of collecting data and technique of analyzing data. The data were taken from six articles of political and business of International Bali Post which were published from May 25, 2016. The data were collected by close reading and note taking and were analyzed qualitatively by applying theory of Cohesion proposed by Halliday M.A.K and Hasan in 1976.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis Of Conjunction In Political And Business Articles Of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">International Bali Post</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The analysis began to find out the types of conjunction including additive, adversative, causal and temporal. The other one was to find the function of conjunction. Furthermore, the data that had been collected in this study were shown by the analysis of every sentence in an orderly way.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">According to the type of conjunction, this analysis is divided into four (Additive, Adversative, Causal, and Temporal)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Additive</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. Example of Simple Additive Relation:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">……..these entrepreneurs will be able to run their business well, expand </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and </span><span class="font3">provide job opportunities ……..</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">……..these entrepreneurs will also be given support </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> capital if need before further business development.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">……..novice entrepreneurs will </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3"> be provided so that they can take their business to the next level.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">……..these entrepreneurs will </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3"> be given support and capital if need……..</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Simple additive relation expressed either the external or the internal type of conjunctive relation. In the example above, it can be seen that the use of simple additive relation is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3">. In the first and second examples, the relationship between “support and capital” can be seen, which has the internal sense “there is something more to be said”. In the third and fourth examples, in fact, there is no very clear difference between the previous two. It is expressed by the example above, “entrepreneurs will </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3"> be provided” and “entrepreneurs will </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3"> be given”. There is a relationship between previous information, which was delivered before. Based on the analysis in the first and second examples in the use of the conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3">, the use of conjunction was found in the first example to add further information from the previous information which makes the sentence cohesive. It can be seen from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“support </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">capital”.</span><span class="font3"> In this situation, the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> is also used in the internal sense </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“there is something more to be said”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The use word </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">also</span><span class="font3"> in the example above has similar function to the conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3">, that is, to add further information from the previous information. It can be seen from “will also be provided, where it has internal sense, which is related to the previous information.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adversative</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. Example of Adversative Relation ‘proper’</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">…….. protection system on food and beverage abroad. “</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">However</span><span class="font3">, if they are in the form of therapeutic products……..</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The example of the adversative relation above is showed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3">, which has the expression like </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">as a result, in consequence</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">because of that.</span><span class="font3"> All this is regularly combined with the initial </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3">. It can be seen from “</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3">, if they are…”, which it expresses the result </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“protection system on food and beverage abroad”.</span><span class="font3"> The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3"> is applied to the example above. It does not indicate the strong sense of contrast as </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">against</span><span class="font3"> but shows other alternative. This example shows external sense because it is used to link two different facts of what was being said. It can be seen from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“protection system on food and beverage abroad”</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Causal</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. Example of Reserved Causal Relation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">A ministerial meeting originally scheduled </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> this week has been delayed after other members……..</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">……..and widening their options </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> financing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In the example above, the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> , as reserved causal relation, expresses </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“for what to do something”</span><span class="font3">, which talks about time. It can be seen from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“this week”</span><span class="font3"> in the first example. In example two, it is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“for explain something”.</span><span class="font3"> The example above shows the use of the conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> in the sentence. In the first example, the use of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> in the sentence is to express </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">“for what to do something”</span><span class="font3"> which talks about time. It has internal sense, because it has relationship to the previous clauses. It can be seen from “originally scheduled </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> this week” that there is relationship between the use of the conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3">, it is to explain for something. In the example, it can be seen from “….and widening their options </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> financing.” The use of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3"> as a conjunctive which has the internal sense “this is the reason for what was just said”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Temporal</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. Example of Simple Temporal Relation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">A ministerial meeting originally scheduled for this week has been delayed </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">after </span><span class="font3">other members preferred to wait……..</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In the example above, the conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">after</span><span class="font3"> expresses simple temporal relation which indicates that sentences are concerned with time. The use of conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">after</span><span class="font3"> in the example above shows the sequential time. It refers to the time and it is used to join one clause to another. The conjunctive </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">after</span><span class="font3"> in the example above is in the middle of the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the analysis in the previous chapter, there are some points that can be concluded as the answers to the problems of this writing. There are four types of conjunction occurring in 6 articles International Bali Post that were taken as the data source of this writing. They are Additive, Adversative, Causal and Temporal.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">There are two types of additive relation found in six articles occurring in the text, they are simple additive relation and complex additive relation. Simple additive relation is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and, also</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">or</span><span class="font3">. Besides, complex additive relative is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in addition</span><span class="font3">. The conjunctions </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and, also</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">or</span><span class="font3"> in the text are used to add the information of what has been said before. Besides, the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in addition</span><span class="font3"> in the complex additive relation is used to add another point of information which has the link with the previous one.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In the adversative relation, there are two types of conjunctions occurs in the text, there are adversative relation proper and contrastive relation. The adversative relation proper are expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">but, though</span><span class="font3">, and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3">. The conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">but</span><span class="font3"> contains the element ‘and’ as one of its meaning component. On the other hand, the word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3"> in contrastive relation is used to introduce a statement that contrasts with, or seems to contradict with something that has been said previously. The word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">however</span><span class="font3"> indicates meaning “contrary expectation”. In relation to causal, there are three types occurring in the text, there are causal relation in general, reserved causal relation and conditional relation. Causal relation in general is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">therefore</span><span class="font3">. The use of</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">conjunctions </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">therefore</span><span class="font3"> shows the relationship between </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">cause</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">consequence</span><span class="font3">. Reserved causal relation is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font3">. It shows the relation between </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">cause</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">consequence</span><span class="font3"> because the following sentence is the effect of the previous sentences. Conditional relation in the text is presented by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">then</span><span class="font3">. The use of the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">then </span><span class="font3">expresses “under the circumstances”. In the temporal relation, there is one type of conjunction found in the text, that is, simple temporal relation which is expressed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">before</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">after</span><span class="font3">. The use of the conjunctions </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">before</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">after</span><span class="font3"> in simple temporal relation refers to the sequential time.</span></p>
