<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini berjudul Ambiguitas Leksikal dalam Slogan Iklan Berbahasa Inggris dari Produk-Produk Unilever. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengidentifikasi makna yang paling sesuai dari ungkapan ambigu dalam slogan iklan berbahasa inggris dari produk-produk Unilever dan menemukan faktor leksikal yang dapat menyebabkan ambiguitas leksikal.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Sumber data penelitian ini diambil dari situs terkenal Unilever yang dimuat dalam situs </span><a href="https://www.unilever.com/brands"><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">https://www.unilever.com/brands</span></a><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">. Penelitian ini merupakan penelitian pustaka dan data penelitian ini dianalisis menggunakan metode kualitatif. Penelitian ini menerapkan Teori Makna oleh Geoffrey Leech (1974: 10-22) untuk menemukan makna yang paling sesuai untuk kata-kata ambigu dan juga didukung dengan Kamus Oxford Advanced Learner untuk menentukan makna leksikal dari kata ambigu. Teori lain yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini adalah teori Ambiguitas leksikal oleh Ullmann (1977: 158) untuk menganalisis faktor-faktor leksikal yang dapat menyebabkan ambiguitas leksikal.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Temuan menunjukkan bahwa pada bagian pertama diantara 13 merek global dari Unilever yang dianalisis, 10 slogan berisi kata-kata ambigu yang mana dari katakata ambigu tersebut dikategorikan sebagai makna Konseptual dan makna dari katakata ambigu pada 3 slogan lainnya dikategorikan sebagai makna Konotatif. Sedangkan, pada bagian kedua ditemukan bahwa faktor leksikal yang menyebabkan ambiguitas leksikal pada 7 slogan merek adalah Polysemi dan faktor leksikal yang menyebabkan ambiguitas leksikal pada 6 slogan merek lainnya adalahHomonimi. Hal ini berarti bahwa ambiguitas leksikal bisa terjadi dalam slogan produk-produk merek terkenal Unilever. Tidak dapat dipungkiri bahwa ambiguitas semakin membuat produk-produk Unilever menjadi terkenal dan siapapun yang melihat iklan-iklan tersebut akan tertarik khususnya pada produk-produk tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: ambiguitas leksikal, makna leksikal, iklan, slogan</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Advertisement can be defined as a notice or announcement to promote products or services. In advertisement there is usually a Slogan as a part of language to make the advertisement more interesting. Sometimes, some slogans in advertisements contain ambiguities. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Ambiguity</span><span class="font4"> occurs when both words and sentences have more than one meaning. If it occurs in the level of word it is called </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">lexical ambiguity</span><span class="font4">. In analyzing the lexical ambiguity, it is important to know the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">lexical meaning</span><span class="font4"> (meaning of the word based on the dictionary) of the ambiguous words and the lexical factors that may lead to the lexical ambiguity such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polysemy</span><span class="font4"> (when a single word has two or more different but related meaning) and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">homonymy</span><span class="font4"> (when words having the same spelling and pronunciation but completely different meaning). The reason why the analysis of lexical ambiguity in English advertisement slogan has become the topic because the ambiguous expression appears in slogan advertisement campaign of Unilever products and rich in lexical ambiguity. To find the most suitable meaning of the ambiguous expression in the slogans can be analyzed using the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Theory of Meaning</span><span class="font4"> proposed by Leech. The extended analysis about the lexical factors that may lead to Lexical ambiguity can be analyzed using the theory of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lexical Ambiguity</span><span class="font4"> by Ullmann.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Dove helps women to improve the condition of skin and hair through its products because Dove products offer softness and smoothness to the skin and hair of the user. Based on the Oxford Advanced Learner’s Dictionary, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Dove</span><span class="font4"> in the slogan “All you need is Dove” is Lexical ambiguity because </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dove</span><span class="font4"> can mean “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bird</span><span class="font4">” and “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">A</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">person who advocates peace</span><span class="font4">”. Dove as a </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bird</span><span class="font4"> (n) represents femininity, peace, tenderness that symbolizes the beauty of women and became the basis of Dove products campaign implied through its slogan campaign “Campaign for Real Beauty” (2004). Meanwhile, Dove as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">a person who advocates peace</span><span class="font4"> (n) refers to the women themselves encouraged by Dove through its products to make peace with their own beauty. From both meanings, the most suitable meaning for the ambiguous word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dove</span><span class="font4"> is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bird</span><span class="font4"> because as a bird, dove has all the positive symbolism attached to them as the reflection of Dove products. Dove as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bird</span><span class="font4"> also contains the values of certain emotions such as softness, tenderness, and joy in which related to the end results after using the products. Thus the meaning indicates </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Connotative meaning</span><span class="font4">. Both meanings are different but related meanings and come from the same word class that is Noun. Thus, lexical factor that makes the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Dove</span><span class="font4"> Lexical ambiguity is </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Polysemy</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Rexona as the world’s no. 1 and the world’s bestselling deodorant formulated with fresh light fragrance and 48-hours wetness protection will keep the user’ body dry, fresh, and confident throughout the day. Based on the Oxford Advanced Learner’s Dictionary,</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Down as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">in a lower or less in some value</span><span class="font4"> (adverb) reflects how confidence is affected by the use of this product. <h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data 8</span></h2>

<p><span class="font4">Zwitsal is widely recognized for delicate, high-quality baby skincare products. Zwitsal prides themselves on being tested hypo-allergenic (it is safe for babies with sensitive skin), pH balanced and an extra soft formulation which is gentle and</span></p>

For example, Hypo-allergenic protection is reflected in this product obviously has a positive impact, smooth and healthy on the baby's skin. So, people who see will also feel pleased. The meaning contains the values of certain emotions, such as beautiful. This is related to the end results after using the products thus, the meaning indicates </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Connotative meaning</span><span class="font4">. Meanwhile, beautiful as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">very good</span><span class="font4"> this can be associated with hypo-allergenic test and the use of perfect formula by Zwitsal in its products make the product suitable and good for all babies’ skin types. From both meanings, the most suitable meaning of the ambiguous beautiful is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pleasing the sight or mind</span><span class="font4"> since it is related to the product that has aesthetic sense and positive impact for the baby’s skin after the use of product. Both meanings are different but related. They come from the same word class that is Adjective thus, the lexical factor that makes </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">beautiful </span><span class="font4">lexically ambiguous is </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Polysemy</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Hellmann’s mayonnaise is America's no. 1 mayonnaise brand. Its great taste is suitable for fish, chicken, grains, vegetables and salads. Based on the Oxford Advanced Learner’s Dictionary, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the best</span><span class="font4"> in slogan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">“Meet our family. Bring out the best at your table” is Lexical ambiguity because </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the best</span><span class="font4"> can mean “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">The most excellent quality</span><span class="font4">” and “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">The highest degree; beyond all others</span><span class="font4">”. The best as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the most excellent quality</span><span class="font4"> (adjective) in this case, the most excellent quality is a quality owned by Hellmann's Mayonnaise products. Considering that the product is suitable for various foods. Meanwhile, the best as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the highest degree; beyond all others</span><span class="font4"> (adverb) show that this product is superior to other types of mayonnaise, such as Kewpie or Duke's Mayo. It was proved by Hellmann's mayonnaise which becomes no. 1 mayonnaise brand in America. This meaning refers to the actual meaning thus, indicates </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Conceptual meaning</span><span class="font4">. From both meanings, the most suitable meaning of ambiguous </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the best</span><span class="font4"> is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the highest degree; beyond all others</span><span class="font4"> because indirectly describe the quality of this product, including taste and ingredients, which make it a famous mayonnaise brand especially in America. Both meanings are different and come from different word classes too. However, both are spelt and pronounced alike i.e. /bɛst/ thus, the lexical factor that make </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">the best</span><span class="font4"> lexical ambiguity is </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Homonymy</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>6. Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">After analyzing 13 Unilever global brands, it was found that 10 slogans contain ambiguous words of which the meanings of ambiguous words are categorized as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Conceptual meaning</span><span class="font4"> (because the meanings of the ambiguous words refer to the actual meaning) and the meanings of ambiguous words on 3 other brands categorized as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Connotative meaning</span><span class="font4"> (because the meaning of the ambiguous words contain value of certain emotion). Meanwhile, the lexical factor that caused the lexical ambiguity on 7 brands slogans is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Polysemy</span><span class="font4"> and lexical factor that caused lexical ambiguity in 6 other brands slogans is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Homonymy</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
