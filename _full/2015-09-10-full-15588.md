<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">JOHN GRISHAM’S NOVEL “THEODORE BOONE”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">BY</span></p>

<p><span class="font1">English Departement, Faculty of Letters and Culture, Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Judul dari studi ini adalah Derived Nouns with Reference to John Grisham’s Novel “Theodore Boone”. Studi ini difokuskan dalam menganalisis afiksasi kata benda. Adapun tujuan-tujuan dari studi ini adalah untuk mengidentifikasi bentuk-bentuk dasar dari kata benda perolehan, untuk menjelaskan arti dari afiksasi kata benda dan juga untuk menganalisis struktur kata dari kata benda perolehan. Teori pokok dari studi ini diambil dari Bauer (1983) mengenai proses morfologi. Untuk tambahan terhadap teori ini, studi ini juga menggunakan teori terkait yang dikemukakan oleh Quirk (1973), teori yang dikemukakan oleh Frank (1972) dan teori yang dikemukakan oleh Spencer (1991). Data dari studi ini diambil dari sebuah novel yang ditulis oleh John Grisham, yang berjudul Theodore boone, yang telah dipublikasikan di Britania Agung oleh Hodder dan Stoughton pada tahun 2010. Data-data pada studi ini dikumpulkan dengan membaca novel tersebut kemudian data yang terkumpul dianalisis secara deskriptif dan secara kualitatif.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil analisis menunjukkan bahwa ada satu kelas awalan tetap yang ditemukan dalam novel, yaitu awalan step-. Selanjutnya, ada empat kelas akhiran tetap yang ditemukan dalam novel ini seperti: -ship, -er, -ist dan –hood. Sedangkan tidak ada satupun kelas awalan ganti yang ditemukan dalam novel. Akan tetapi, ada empat belas kelas akhiran ganti yang ditemukan, yaitu: -ation, -al, -er/-or, -ment, -ure, -ant, -ion, -age, -ence/-ance, -ness, -cy, -ity, -dom, and –th.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci : kata benda perolehan, struktur kata, afiksasi kata benda</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Word is the basic unit of a language. The development of a language is influenced by how the words are developed. Languages organize their parts of speech into classes according to their functions. English words are alive. They can grow, change and move from one class into another. The study about word</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">formations is called morphology. Many people are unconscious that a lot of words are comprised of morphemes and cause the classes of the words changed. This study concerns with the analysis of derivational affixation particularly, in the derived nouns to find out which word classes can be changed into noun. From those categories of words, a noun is chosen since it has three hundred thousand members, the largest numbers in English word classes. The data is obtained from a novel, entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1"> written by John Grisham. The reason for choosing the topic is the unconsciousness to the alteration in English words that change the word class of the word itself since the additional process of morpheme.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In order to understand the derivational affixation, mainly in derived nouns formation, the problems that appear in this study as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the base forms of the derived nouns found in the novel entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1"> by John Grisham?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of the nominalizing affixes of nouns found in the novel written by John Grisham entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How are the word structures of the derived nouns found in novel written by John Grisham entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1"> can be represented in terms of treediagrams analysis?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In order to answer the previous problems, the aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the base forms of the derived nouns in the novel entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1"> by John Grisham.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the meanings of the nominalizing affixes of nouns found in a novel written by John Grisham entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the word structures of the derived nouns found in a novel written by John Grisham entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone</span><span class="font1"> using tree-diagrams analysis.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were taken from a novel written by John Grisham, entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Theodore Boone,</span><span class="font1"> which consists of 22 chapters which were collected by using library research with some supporting techniques. Those data were analyzed by using qualitative-descriptive method by applying the theory proposed by Bauer (1983) as the main theory. In its application is supported by other theories, they are: theory proposed by Quirk (1973), theory proposed by Frank (1972) and theory proposed by Spencer (1991).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In order to analyze derivational morpheme of the collected data, there are two classes of derivational morpheme used in this study. They are class changing derivational morpheme and class maintaining derivational morpheme. In the class changing derivational morpheme, it is only class changing suffixes used here, which consists of de-verbal and de-adjectival noun. Meanwhile, the class maintaining derivational morpheme consists of class maintaining prefixes and class maintaining suffixes in which both of them form derived nouns from noun bases. In addition, those derivational processes are also represented by using treediagram analysis as below.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the previous analysis, it can be concluded that there are three categories of nouns that are found from the novel. The first category is de-verbal noun, it is noun derived from verb bases. Second category is de-adjectival noun, it is noun that derived from adjective bases and third category is class maintaining with a noun base. Here, the attachment of affixes does not influence the grammatical class of the words. Each of the derivational affixes has their own meaning; suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–er (-or)</span><span class="font1"> means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">agentive and instrumental,</span><span class="font1"> suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ness</span><span class="font1"> means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">state, quality or measure of the idea</span><span class="font1"> and suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ist</span><span class="font1"> means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">member of party or occupation.</span><span class="font1"> Moreover, the form of derived noun in a structure is also needed. This way is used in order to clarify what components that forms the words which categorized as derived noun. This structure is shown by tree diagrams as in the previous analysis.</span></p>
