<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Studi ini berjudul “Types and Meaning of Idiom in the Song Albums of John Legend and Coldplay”. Studi ini fokus membahas jenis idiom dan arti dari idiom yang ada dalam lirik lagu tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Data yang digunakan dalam studi ini berupa lagu yang diambil dari album John Legend yang berjudul “Love in the Future” yang dirilis pada than 2011 dan album dari Coldplay yang berjudul “Parachutes, Head Full of Dreams dan X&amp;Y”. Data dalam studi ini dikumpulkan dengan menggunakan metode kualitatif dengan teknik pencatatan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Studi ini menggunakan Teori Palmer untuk menganalisa jenis idiom yang terdapat di setiap lirik lagu. Berdasarkan teori Palmer, ada tiga jenis idiom yang terdapat dalam lirik lirik lagu dalam album ini. Dan untuk menganalisa arti yang terkandung dalam idiom tersebut, studi ini menggunakan teori Leech; teori ini menjelaskan bahwa terdapat tujuh jenis meaning. Arti dari idiom itu sendiri memiliki hubungan yang sangat erat dengan pesan yang terkandung.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Berdasarkan atas hasil analisis terdapat tiga jenis idiom yang terkandung dalam lirik lagu.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: idiom, jenis idiom, arti dari idiom</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The English language is an important language since it becomes an international language. There are many ways to express feeling through language such as writing a novel or poetry, or people can write a song. As one of the media to express feelings, song seems interesting to be analyzed. The language of the song is built in a specific way; therefore, it becomes an ordinary language. It is interesting and usually written in beautiful and nice words to attract us and hold our attention.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As one of the media to express feelings, song seems interesting to be analyzed. An idiom is a number of words which are joined together, which means something different from the meaning of separate words that make them up. What makes idiom interesting is that the meaning of idiom cannot be predicted from the meaning of word by word. Sometimes idiom takes many different structures. They can be very short, or rather long. That is why people find it hard to understand its meaning.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This part deals with the result and the discussion of the data. The data analysis is divided into two parts; types of idiom and analysis of meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Idiom</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phrasal verb is a very common type of idiom in English which includes</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">the meaning of adverb/particle. What is usually called a phrasal verb is the combination of a verb and adverb (Palmer, 1976).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wanna run to the light</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">run to</span><span class="font1"> can be considered a phrasal verb. It consists of two words formed by the verb ‘run’ and the adverb ‘to’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light it up, tell me more, explore</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">light it up</span><span class="font1"> is considered a phrasal verb where the verb ‘light’ and the adverb ‘up’ are separated by the direct object ‘it’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prepositional Verb. According to Palmer (1976: 99), a prepositional verb is the sequence of verb and preposition.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stop standing in line, cause you’re all mine</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">standing in</span><span class="font1"> is a prepositional verb where the verb ‘stand’ is followed by the preposition ‘in’ and the adverb ‘line’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So, we can set the mood right off</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">right off</span><span class="font1"> is a prepositional verb in which the verb ‘right’ is followed by the preposition ‘off’.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Partial idiom is if one of the words has its usual meaning and the other has meaning that is peculiar to a particular sequence (Palmer, 1976:99).</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cards on the table, we’re both showing hearts</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">showing hearts</span><span class="font1"> is a partial idiom since the word ‘showing’ in this term has usual meaning while the word ‘hearts’ has the meaning that is peculiar to a particular sequence of idiom.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before my head explodes</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiomatic expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">head explodes</span><span class="font1"> is considered a partial idiom. It also seems hard to decide which word has the usual meaning and which has the meaning that has is peculiar to a particular sequence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meaning of Idiom.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Connotative meaning is the communicative value which an expression has by virtue of what it </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">refers to, over</span><span class="font1"> and above purely conceptual content.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">To a large extent, the notion of ‘reference’ overlaps with the conceptual meaning</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Light it up, tell me more, explore</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In verse 2 </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Who Do We Think We Are</span><span class="font1"> song there is the phrasal verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">light it up</span><span class="font1">. The verb ‘light’ means the energy from something which shines, the adverb ‘up’ means to or in a higher place or position, condition, degree etc. The meaning ‘light it up’ related to the song lyric is to make it more hilarious; crowded. Therefore, the phrasal verb ‘light it up’ can be categorized as connotative meaning, since the meaning does not have its literal meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So we can set the mood right off</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">right off</span><span class="font1"> is considered a prepositional verb. It consists of the verb ‘right’ which means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">to return something to the normal</span><span class="font1">; </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">vertical position</span><span class="font1"> and the preposition ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off’</span><span class="font1"> means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">down or away from a place</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">at a distance in space or time</span><span class="font1">. Related to the lyric, we can set the mood ‘right off’, which in this case means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">immediately</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">quickly</span><span class="font1">. The idiom ‘right off’ can be considered having connotative meaning since it uses the word which its meaning is above its literal meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a) Stylistic meaning is that a piece language conveys about the social circumstances of its use. We ‘decode’ the stylistic meaning of a text through our recognition of different dimensions and levels of usage within the same language.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I wanna run to the light</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">run to</span><span class="font1"> is a phrasal verb, the meaning of ‘run’ as a verb is to move using legs or going faster than, and the adverb ‘to’ means in or into a close position. The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">runs to</span><span class="font1"> means searching for or looking for something better. This idiom is related to getting better of life and go away from the sadness or worst thing. This phrasal verb ‘run to’ has the connotative</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">and stylistic meaning since its meaning is not literal and the usage of this idiom is about the social circumstance.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">- &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I’m covered in you</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The phrasal verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">covered in</span><span class="font1"> consists of the verb ‘covered’ which has the meaning </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">to place</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">something over</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">in front of something</span><span class="font1"> in order to hide or protect it and the adverb ‘in’ means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">to a position in</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">inside something</span><span class="font1">. This idiom clearly tells that someone is falling in love and feels so covered with love. This idiom emphasizes style in the sentence and makes this idiom categorized as having the stylistic meaning, according to Leech types of meaning. It also considered having connotative meaning, since its meaning does not have its literal meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the types of idioms, from the 15 songs of John Legend and Coldplay albums, 27 idioms were collected; they are 15 phrasal verbs, 5 prepositional verbs and 7 partial idioms. After passing the whole analysis, the song lyrics of John Legend and Coldplay contain three types of idioms, in which the phrasal verb and the partial idiom are the types of idioms with the most frequent occurrences, while the prepositional verb, as the type of idioms, occur for the least. The three types of idioms are presented as the data for finding out the meaning of idioms.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The meaning of idioms in the song lyrics of John Legend and Coldplay can be classified into connotative meaning and stylistic meaning, and the connotative meaning is the most frequent occurrences and the least is stylistic meaning. By viewing the types and meaning of idioms in the song lyrics of John Legend and Coldplay, people can easily understand the idioms.</span></p>
