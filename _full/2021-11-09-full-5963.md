<h2><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font1">Makalah ini menganalisa dan membahas komponen-komponen dari kompleks frase nomina yang dapat ditemukan di novel yang berjudul </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Edensor.</span><span class="font1"> Novel ini dipilih karena ada banyak terdapat frase nomina. Selain itu, novelis juga telah menulis sebuah novel yang berjudul </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Laskar Pelangi”</span><span class="font1">. Melalui serangkaian tahapan analisis, dapat ditemukan bahwa kompleks frase nomina terdiri dari 3 komponen; </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the head, the pre-modification</span><span class="font1">, dan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the post modification</span><span class="font1">. Dan tipe-tipe dari kompleks frase nomina adalah </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">pre-modification, post-modification,</span><span class="font1"> dan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">multiple modification</span><span class="font1">. Untuk mengetahui data, sumber data dibaca secara hati-hati, kemudian digarisbawahi dan dicatat. Data dianalisa secara deskriptif kualitatif, dengan maksud data dianalisa berkaitan denga masalah yang didasarkan pada teori.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kunci: frase nomina, compleks frase nomina, bahasa</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">A phrase might consist of a head one modifier or a head and more than one modifier. There are nine different types of phrases in English. There are noun phrase, prepositional phrase, adjective phrase, adverb phrase, verb phrase, infinitive phrase, gerund phrase, participle phrase, absolute phrase. A noun phrase is a phrase that has a </span><a href="http://www.sil.org/linguistics/GlossaryOfLinguisticTerms/WhatIsANoun.htm"><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">noun</span><span class="font1"> </span></a><span class="font1">as its</span><a href="http://www.sil.org/linguistics/GlossaryOfLinguisticTerms/WhatIsAHead.htm"><span class="font1"> </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">head</span><span class="font1">.</span></a><span class="font1"> In addition, modifiers are the words that modify the head noun. There are two types of modification as stated by Quirk (1985:376). First, restrictive modification, if the head can be viewed as a member of a class which can be linguistically identified only through the modification that has been supplied. Second, non-restrictive modification, if the head viewed as unique or a member of class that has been independently identified, any modification given such a head is additional information which is not essential for identifying the head can be called non-restrictive modification.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of English Noun Phrase Structure in the novel “Edensor”</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pre-modification Noun Phrase</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">It means that the head noun is modified by various pre-modification, adjective, participle, noun, genitive or adverb and placed before the head other than determinatives.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">I was too young for such </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">a severe punishment</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(Edensor Novel, Mosaic 4, page 26)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the data above, the components of complex noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">a severe punishment </span><span class="font1">can be described as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">a &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;severe &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;punishment</span></p><a name="caption2"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font0"><a name="bookmark11"></a>T &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Determiner Pre-modification (adjective) Head Noun</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">This complex noun phrase “a severe punishment” can be included into premodification noun phrase because the modification occurs before the head noun “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">punishment”</span><span class="font1">. The adjective “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">severe”</span><span class="font1"> modified the head noun and completed with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“a”</span><span class="font1"> as determiner.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The use of article </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“a”</span><span class="font1"> as determiner in this complex noun phrase is to express the reference. The modification that occurs is suitable with the function of adjective that explains the head noun.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post-modification Noun Phrase</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">It means that the head noun is modified by many kinds of post-modification and positioned after the head noun.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In situation like this, my father would often hoist me up on </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">the backseat of his Forever bicycle</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(Edensor Novel, Mosaic 2, page 21)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the data above, the components of complex noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">the backseat of his Forever bicycle </span><span class="font1">can described down follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">the &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;backseat of his Forever bicycle</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">I &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Determiner Head Noun &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post-modification</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The post-modification above is a post-modification by prepositional phrase signed by the preposition </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">of</span><span class="font1"> after the head noun. The post-modification is achieved using restrictive finite clause. This head noun is pre-modified by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the.</span><span class="font1"> In addition, it is post-modified by the prepositional phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">of his Forever bicycle</span><span class="font1">. It is a simple matter to determine if this complex structure is functioning as a complete noun phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Multi-modification Noun Phrase</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This type includes the noun phrase having more than a single pre-</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">modification or post-modification. This type usually consists of the head noun, the determiner, the pre-modification and the post-modification. The modification could be more than one as well as the post-modification.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">A strange sort of happiness came over me</span><span class="font1">, a happiness that would materialize magically with the arrival of Ramadan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(Edensor Novel, Mosaic 5, page 30)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the data above, the components of complex noun phrase can be describe as follows:</span></p>
<div>
<p><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">a</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">T</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">D</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<div>
<p><span class="font1">strange</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Pre-modification</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<p><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">sort &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;of happiness came over me</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Head Noun &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post-modification</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The complex noun phrase is formed by adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“strange”</span><span class="font1"> as the premodification and prepositional phrase as the post-modification. The prepositional phrase that signed by using </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“of”</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The complex noun phrase consists of some components; the determiner (using articles an, a, the), the head, the pre-modification and the post-modification. The types of complex noun phrase are divided into three categories depending on the components used in forming the complex noun phrase. The types of complex noun phrases are pre-modification non phrase, post-modification noun phrase, and multiple-modification noun phrase.</span></p>
