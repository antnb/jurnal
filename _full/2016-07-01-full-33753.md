<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Structural Ambiguity Found In Reader’s Digest Usa Magazine</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Ni Putu Vera Eryantini<sup>1*</sup>, I Nyoman Udayana<sup>2</sup>, I Nyoman Aryawibawa<sup>3 </sup></span><span class="font3"><sup>123</sup>English Department Faculty Of Letters And Culture Udayana University <sup>1</sup></span><a href="mailto:ganesh_vs2@yahoo.com"><span class="font3">[ganesh_vs2@yahoo.com]</span></a><span class="font3"> <sup>2</sup></span><a href="mailto:udayana2011@gmail.com"><span class="font3">[udayana2011@gmail.com]</span></a><span class="font3"> <sup>3</sup></span><a href="mailto:inyoman.aryawibawa@gmail.com"><span class="font3">[inyoman.aryawibawa@gmail.com]</span></a></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">*</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Corresponding Author</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah menganalisis makna-makna yang muncul dari kalimat ambigu secara struktural yang ditemukan di majalah berbahasa Inggris, Reader’s Digest USA edisi Agustus 2014- Desember/Januari 2015. Dalam penelitian ini ada tiga teori yang digunakan untuk menjawab 2 pokok permasalahan. Adapun teori yang dimaksud, diantaranya teori tentang kalimat ambigu secara struktural oleh Lyons (1977), teori tentang struktur kalimat dan frasa oleh Fromkin(1990) dan teori tentang eksperimnen sederhana untuk mengetahui keakuratan kalimat ambigu secara structural oleh Baker(1995). Penelitian ini menemukan 22 kalimat ambigu secara structural yang terbagi menjadi 8 kategori sesuai dengan faktor-faktor penyebab. Di dalam penelitian ini terbukti bahwa kalimat/frasa bahasa inggris yang tatabahasanya benar dengan struktur tertentu bisa memunculkan makna berbeda.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: ambigu secara structural, factor-faktor penyebab, kalimat/frasa</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">In written language ambiguity can happen. Kreidler (1998:156) states the structure of sentences may lead to different possible interpretations which are generally known as structural ambiguity. Structural ambiguity is an interesting phenomenon, and natural in a language. Sentence structure obviously can lead to be ambiguous even though they are grammatically correct.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In the case of structural ambiguity, the grammar of a sentence can be interpreted in more than one way. For example the sentence </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">They are killing animals</span><span class="font3">. In the first case, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">killing animals</span><span class="font3"> is a unit and in the second, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">are killing</span><span class="font3"> is a unit. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Killing animals</span><span class="font3"> is a noun phrase that functions as a complement, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">are killing</span><span class="font3"> is a verb phrase and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">animals</span><span class="font3"> is an object. The first sentence means </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">they are animals that like to kill</span><span class="font3"> and the second means the animals are being killed by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">they</span><span class="font3"> (the subject).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader Digest USA</span><span class="font3"> magazine is one of famous trusted magazines all over the world. It is recommended as one of ten best English magazines for learning English which means the writing is grammatically good.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the background, this study focuses on finding the answer to the following questions:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What structural ambiguities are found in the articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader’s Digest USA</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">magazines?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of the ambiguous phrases/sentences found in the articles</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader’s Digest USA</span><span class="font3"> magazines?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">To respond the problems, the aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">a. To find out structural ambiguities in the articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader’s Digest USA</span><span class="font3"> magazines. b. To analyze the meanings of ambiguous phrases/sentences of the articles.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data source in this study was taken from the articles of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader’s Digest USA </span><span class="font3">magazines from August 2014 to December 2014. This magazine was chosen as data source because it is one of famous trusted magazines all over the world. It is also recommended as one of ten best English magazines for learning English by fluentu.com which means its writing is grammatically good.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data was collected by applying the documentary method. In order to get the data, all the articles of the selected magazines were read carefully. By using note taking technique, the sentences containing structural ambiguity noted. And all the required data found in the selected magazines were grouped into different kind of structural ambiguity according to the factors which cause structural ambiguity.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data are analyzed by using the descriptive qualitative method. All the data were then grouped into different kinds of structural ambiguities according to the causal</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">factors. One of data was chosen as representative to be analyzed. The structural ambiguity was shown by means of tree diagram and analyzed with the theory of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Phrasestructure explications</span><span class="font3">: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">grouping</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">categorization</span><span class="font3"> according to Lyons (1977). The simple experiment proposed by Baker (1995) was also applied in order to make sure the accuracy of the structural ambiguity.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There were seven different kinds of structural ambiguity in terms of surface structure found in the Reader’s Digest Magazine according to their causal factors.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are 22 structurally ambiguous phrases/sentences in 8 different types according to their causal factors found in the 5 editions of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Reader’s Digest USA </span><span class="font3">magazines, August 2014 to December/January2015.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This study shows English sentences which grammatically correct can be ambiguous structurally. A sentence which has differences in which the forms are grouped can be ambiguous structurally. A constituent in a sentence which has different functions to the sentence also leads the sentence to be ambiguous structurally.</span></p>
