<p><span class="font3">Sea grape (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp.) is one of the potential types of seaweed. Sea grape potential as a natural ingredient for making medicine. Balinese people use sea grape for consumption. The content of bioactive compounds in sea grape is suspected to be toxic at certain doses. The purpose of this study was to determine the compound content of ethyl acetate extract of sea grape (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp.) and the toxicity on </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Artemia salina</span><span class="font3"> Leach. The identification of the bioactive compounds was carried out by Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrophotometry and the toxicity test of extracts was carried out using the Brine Shrimp Lethality Test (BSLT) method. The results showed that the ethyl acetate extract of sea grape (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp.) contained 27 chemical compounds, 7 of which had a quality value &gt;&nbsp;70%, namely </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Propanoic acid, ethyl ester</span><span class="font3">; </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n-Propyl acetate</span><span class="font3">; </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">sec-Butyl acetate</span><span class="font3">; </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Toluene</span><span class="font3">; </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Acetic acid, butyl ester</span><span class="font3">; </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bicyclo [4.2.0] octa-1, 3, 5-triene</span><span class="font3">; and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Styrene</span><span class="font3">. The results of the toxicity test of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Artemia salina</span><span class="font3"> Leach are toxic which had an LC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font3">value of 44,070 ppm so that they can be used as a larvicide.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font3">: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Artemia salina</span><span class="font3"> Leach, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp., GC-MS, Toxicity</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Indonesia is known as a country which has a sea area with a high biodiversity county. Seaweed is one of biological resources there are available abudant in Indonesian waters. In generally, seaweed well-known source of important food hydrocolloids, such as agar, alginates, and carrageenan. In addition,</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">secondary metabolites found in seaweed potential for activity antimicrobial such as antiviral, antibacterial, and antifungal (Suptijah, 2002). Several species of seaweed is potentially developed as a medicine. Sea grape (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp.), locally known as bulung anggur in Bali has potensial nutrient such as</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">carbohydrates, crude fiber, high ash, and low fat content (Tapotubun, 2018). Balinese people have been consuming sea grape as a fresh vegetable and salad.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The previous study reported several beneficial phytochemicals components in the ethyl acetate extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp., which exhibited strong antibacterial activity and antioxidant (Marraskuranto </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al,</span><span class="font3"> 2021). According to Anggadiredja (2006), sea grape has been widely used as traditional medicine because it has plant chemical compound that are active substances. The content of bioactive compounds in sea grape is suspected to be toxic at certain doses. Therefore, it is necessary to study the identification of the bioactive components in the ethyl acetate extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp. through gas chromatography to determine the chemical compositions, especially the biological activity with toxicity test use Brine Shrimp Lethality Test (BSLT) method. Plant bioactive components can be obtained by extraction using solvent. Ethyl acetate solvent is reffered to as semipolar solvent.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Toxicity testing of the extract can be perfomed through </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Brine Shrimp Lethality Test </span><span class="font3">(BSLT) using larvae </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. Salina</span><span class="font3">. The BSLT test has a spectrum of pharmacological activity that is easy to perform, simple, fast, and does not require large costs with a 95% confidence level. This method can identify the toxicity of natural ingredients and be seen from the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">eISSN: 2655-9994 pISSN: 2303-3371 </span><a href="https://doi.org/10.24843/IJBB.2023.v10.i02.p05"><span class="font2">https://doi.org/10.24843/IJBB.2023.v10.i02.p05</span></a></p>
<p><span class="font3">number of dead larvae </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. Salina</span><span class="font3"> with observed after 24 hours. The toxicity test was assessed by determining the LC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font3">score. The LC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font3">score is defined as the concentration of compound causing 50% mortality of shrimp larva with using the solvent of ethyl acetate.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The objective of this study was to analyzed the bioactive components in the ethyl acetate extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> seaweeds using GC-MS analysis and the toxicity test with BSLT method to determine the biological activity using larvae </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. Salina</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS Preparation of Sample</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Samples of fresh bulung anggur (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span><span class="font3"> sp.) macroalgae used in this study was collected at Serangan, Bali. Bulung anggur was cleaned from impurities and washed under running water. The sample bulung anggur is thoroughly rinsed with water, dried with air at room temperature for 4 days, then oven-dried at a temperature of 45<sup>0 </sup>C for 3 days until a constant weight was obtained.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>Extraction with Macerated Method</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The dried sample was blended and sieved resulting in powder. 250 g of sample powder was macerated by 3 L of ethyl acetate for a period of three days with regular shaking. The extract is roughly filtered with filter paper and funnel. The total filtrate with extraction</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">carried out in double, then evaporated at 35<sup>0</sup> C until the viscous extract obtained.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Identification Bioactive Compounds Using </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">(GC-MS)</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Analysis of sample bulung anggur was performed by using GC-MS machine in Denpasar Police Forensic Denpasar. A volume of 1µl was injected in injector with temperature was set at 290<sup>0</sup> C for 27 minutes. Nitrogen was used as a carrier gas at a constant flow of 1.0 ml/min.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Preparation of Larvae </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">A. Salina</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Synthetic sea water was prepared by dissolving 40 g of salt without iodine in 2000 mL of water which was irradiated with 25 watt fluorescent lamp. At the same time, aeration is regulated by utilizing an aerator. Then enter the amount of approximately 1 mg of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A.salina </span><span class="font3">eggs into the vessel that already contains seawater. The eggs hatch after 24-36 hours. A period of 2 to 3 days, larvae into instar level 2 and larvae can be used as test animals.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Toxicity Test</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The test solution is made with a concentration of 500 ppm, 250 ppm, 125 ppm, 50 ppm, 10 ppm, and 0 ppm as a control or without added extract. The sampel was added Dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) as much 10 µl to dissolve in 5 mL of a suitable solvent into each test tube. Put 10 larvae of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. salina</span><span class="font3"> into the test tube and add seawater to a final volume of 5 ml so that the final results of the test solution are obtained with concentrations of 500 ppm, 250 ppm, 125 ppm, 50 ppm, and 10 ppm. The control group was only given 5 ml of seawater and added DMSO without added extract. Each concentration had five replications. The total number of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. salina</span><span class="font3"> was used 300 larvae. The percentage of dead </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. Salina</span><span class="font3"> observed after 24 hours an was calculated. The standard criteria for assessing the mortality of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">A. salina </span><span class="font3">larvae is that the larvae do not show movement of observation. The toxicity test was assessed by determining the LC</span><span class="font1">50 </span><span class="font3">score. Eq. 1 shows the % larvae mortality equation. (Nurhayati et al. 2006)</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></h1>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Identification of the bioactive compounds in the ethyl acetate extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Caulerpa</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The chromatogram of gas chromatography analysis is shown in Figure 1 Information on compound names, retention time (Rt) and the area under the curve (AUC) is shown in Table 1, sorted from the highest to the lowest quality. Bulung anggur extract contains </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Propanoic acid, ethyl ester</span><span class="font3"> as the dominant compound. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Propanoic acid, ethyl ester</span><span class="font3"> shows the activity as antimicrobial (Wang </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al., </span><span class="font3">2014) and food flavouring (Vidya, 2010).
