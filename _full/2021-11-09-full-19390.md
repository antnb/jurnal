<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini fokus pada terjemahan kata depan di, ke, dan untuk di dalam novel Fisherman Daughther. Tujuan utama dari penelitian ini untuk mengidentifikasi makna preposisi di, ke, dan untuk dan untuk menganalisis fungsi kata depan di, ke, dan untuk. Masalah yang diangkat dalam penelitian ini adalah: apa makna dari kata depan di, ke, dan untuk, ke dalam bahasa Indonesia dan apa fungsi dari kata depan di, ke dan untuk, ke dalam bahasa Indonesia.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Data ini diambil dengan cara membaca, mengidentifikasi dan mengklasifikasikan kata depan di, ke, dan untuk, dengan menggunakan metode kualitatif dan deskriptif data setelah dikatagorikan berdasarkan makna dan fungsi dari kata depan tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Teori utama tata bahasa dan terjemahan diambil dari buku A Comprehensive Grammar oleh Quirk dan Arti Terjemahan Berdasarkan Larson. Data ini diambil dari dua novel, Fisherman Daughter dalam novel bahasa inggris dan Putri Nelayan dalam novel bahasa Indonesia.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Setelah menganalisis data, para ahli menunjukkan bahwa kata depan di, ke, dan untuk memiliki beberapa makna dan fungsi ke dalam bahasa Indonesia. Kata depan di memiliki banyak makna diantaranya: di, dalam, ke, dengan, di dalam, datang, masuk, dan tidak memiliki arti terjemahan. Kata depan ke memiliki banyak makna diantaranya ke, untuk, kepada, menuju, di, sampai, dengan, dan tidak memiliki arti terjemahan. Kata depan untuk memiliki makna diantaranya untuk, selama, karena, bagi, demi, atas, terhadap dan tidak memiliki arti terjemahan. Kata depan di, ke, dan untuk memiliki fungsi sebagai penanda waktu, sebagai penanda tempat, sebagai penanda sebab akibat, sebagai penanda posisi, sebagai penanda penerima, dan sebagai penanda tujuan.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">The Functions and Meanings of English Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">In</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> have equivalent with </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam, ke</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dengan</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di dalam</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">datang</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">masuk</span><span class="font2">, and sometimes </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">zero translation</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2">” can have the equivalent of the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font2"> into Indonesian</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> It can be seen in the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The room was bright. He was alone. The sharp smell of</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">medicinal alcohol hung </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> the air, and somewhere a machine pinged </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> quiet rhythm with his heart. (Arleen, 2008: 10 )</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ruangan itu terang. Dia sendirian. Bau tajam alkohol medis</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">mengapung </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> udara dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> suatu tempat, sebuah mesin berbunyi pelan seirama jantungnya. (Cecilia, 2009: 22 )</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, the source language’s sentence, ”</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The room was bright. He was alone. The sharp of medical alchohol hung </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the air, and somewhere a machine pinged in quiet rhythm with his heart</span><span class="font2">”. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> is followed by noun phrase the air and somewhere. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> into Indonesia has function as a marker of place, it describes that phrase in the air and somewhere. In the target language’s sentence, ”</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Ruangan itu terang”. Dia sendirian. Bau tajam alkohol medis mengapung </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">udara dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">suatu tempat, sebuah mesin berbunyi pelan seirama jantungnya</span><span class="font2">”. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> is also followed by noun phrase udara dan suatu tempat. From the case, the translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> is equivalent and they have the same function as a marker of place.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2">” can have the equivalent of the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">” </span><span class="font2">into Indonesian</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> It can be seen in the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">In</span><span class="font2"> the darkness, Langdon felt the drugs washing through his</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">systema lmost instantly, dragging his body back down into that deep well from which he had emerged. (Arleen, 2008: 13)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Dalam</span><span class="font2"> Kegelapan, Langdon merasakan obat-obatan itu mengaliri</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">tubuhnya nyaris seketika, menyeretnya kembali memasuki sumur dalam, tempatnya keluar tadi. (Amidjaja, 2009: 27)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, the source language’s sentence,” </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">In </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the darkness, Langdon felt the drugs washing through his system almost instantly, dragging his body back down into that deep well from which he had emerged</span><span class="font2">”. Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> is followed by noun phrase the darkness. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> indicates a function as position, it describes that phrase in the darkness</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> In target language, the sentence is translated as “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Dalam </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kegelapan, Langdon merasakan obat obat itu mengaliri tubuhnya nyaris seketika, menyeretnya kembali memasuki sumur dalam tempatnya keluar tadi</span><span class="font2">”. Dalam is also followed by noun phrase kegelapan. Both preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam</span><span class="font2"> have a same function as a marker of position. Based on that fact, the translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam</span><span class="font2"> is equivalent.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">The Functions and Meanings of English Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">To</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> have equivalent with </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kepada</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">menuju</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sampai</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dengan</span><span class="font2"> and sometimes </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">zero translation</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> can be translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian. It can be seen in the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I’d never come close to being a murder victim until after I moved</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> his safe little town (Arleen, 2008:16).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aku baru terancam menjadi korban pembunuhan setelah pindah</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2"> kota kecilnya yang aman (Amidjaja, 2009:30).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, in source language sentence, ” </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I’d never come close to being a murder victim until after I moved </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">his safe little town</span><span class="font2">”. Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> is followed by noun phrase his safe</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> The function of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> in the sentence as a marker of place. It describes that phrase little town. In target language’s sentence, “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aku baru terancam menjadi korban pembunuhan setelah pindah </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kota kecilnya yang aman</span><span class="font2">”. Preposition</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> is also followed by noun phrase kota kecilnya</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian and has a function as a marker of place. The translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to </span><span class="font2">into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2"> is equivalent.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2">” is equivalent to “</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2">”. It can be seen from the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What facts!?” Langdon demanded, struggling </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> prop himself</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">higher, the IV in his arm pinched, and his body felt like it weighed several hundred pounds”(Arleen, 2008:17)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fakta apa!?” desak Langdon, sambil berjuang </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2"> semakin</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">menegakkan tubuh, infus di lengannya terasa menusuk, dan bobot tubuhnya seakan beberapa ratus kilogram. (Amidjaja, 2009:32)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, from the source language’s sentence, “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">What facts!?” Langdon demanded, struggling </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">prop himself higher</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The IV in his arm pinched, and his body felt like it weighed several hundred pounds</span><span class="font2">”. Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> has a function as marker of goal and target. It can be seen because preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> followed by noun phrase pop himself higher. In target language, it is translated as “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fakta apa!?” desak Langdon, sambil berjuang </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">semakin menegakkan tubuh, infus di lengannya terasa menusuk, dan bobot tubuhnya seakan beberapa ratus kilogram</span><span class="font2">”. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> in the sentence is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> In Indonesian untuk also has a function to express goal and target. The translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">untuk </span><span class="font2">is equivalence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">The Functions and Meanings of English Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">For</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> have equivalent with </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk, selama, karena, bagi, demi, atas, terhadap</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font2"> and sometimes </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">zero translation</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> can have the equivalent of the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2"> into Indonesian. It can be seen in the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“Okay, Mr Langdon, “ she said, still writing, “a couple of routine</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">questions </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> you, what day of the week is it”? (Arleen, 2008:12).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“Okay, Mr. Langdon,” kata perempuan itu, masih sambil menulis,</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">“beberapa pertanyaan rutin </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2"> anda, ini hari apa?” (Amidjaja, 2009:25).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, the use of the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> and the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian has a function as a marker of recipient. In the source language’s sentence, “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Okay, Mr Langdon, “ she said, still writing, “a couple of routine questions </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> you, what day of the week is it”. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> followed by pronoun you</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> In the target language’s sentence, “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Okay, Mr. Langdon,” kata perempuan itu, masih sambil menulis, beberapa pertanyaan rutin </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">anda, ini hari apa</span><span class="font2"> ?” the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font2"> is also followed by pronoun anda</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> From the case, the translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">untuk </span><span class="font2">is equivalent and they have the same function to state recipient.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> is equivalent to </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> could be translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian which has a meaning to state duration of time. It can be seen in the following examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“I Have to get dressed now. Can you hold yourself together </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">two minutes?” (Arleen, 2008:18).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“Aku harus berpakaian sekarang. Bisakah kamu menahan diri</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama</span><span class="font2"> dua menit?” (Amidjaja, 2009:32).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example a, the use of prepositin </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> indicates the duration of time. In the source language’s sentence, “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I Have to get dressed now. Can you hold yourself together </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">two minutes</span><span class="font2">?”. Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> is followed by noun phrase two minutes. The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> that found in the phrase describe the time that Langdon needs to wait Alice, while Alice get dressed. In the target language’s sentence, it is translated as selama dua menit. It indicates by preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> In target language, the sentence translated as “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aku harus berpakaian sekarang”. Bisakah kau menahan diri </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dua menit</span><span class="font2">?”. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Selama</span><span class="font2"> is also followed noun phrase dua menit</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> Both preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama</span><span class="font2"> have a same function to state duration of time. Based on that fact, the translation of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">selama</span><span class="font2"> is equivalent.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conslusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">After doing the analysis of English preposition “in, to, and for” from novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Fisherman Daughter</span><span class="font2"> (2008) by Arleen into novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Putri Nelayan</span><span class="font2"> (2009) by Amidjaja, it can be concluded that:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There are some functions of preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in, to</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> that found in the novel, they are: preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> has a function as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">place</span><span class="font2">, indicates function as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">position</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">recipient</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">instrument</span><span class="font2">. Preposition to as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">place</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">position</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">goal and target</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">recipient</span><span class="font2">. And preposition for has a function to indicates </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">position</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">recipient</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">purpose</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">time</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">cause</span><span class="font2">, as a marker of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">goal and target</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The meaning of the English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in, to,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> have a number of varieties in the target language (Indonesian). In the target language, preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di, dalam, ke, dengan, di dalam, datang, masuk,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">zero tran-slation. </span><span class="font2">Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ke, untuk, kepada, menuju, di, sampai, dengan</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">zero translation</span><span class="font2">. And preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">untuk, selama, karena, bagi, demi, atas, terhadap,</span><span class="font2"> and sometimes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">zero translation</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
