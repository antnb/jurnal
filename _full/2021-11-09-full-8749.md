<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Katy Perry adalah seorang penyanyi pop yang berasal dari Amerika. Salah satu albumnya yang terkenal adalah Teenage dream. Di dalam Album ini ada tiga belas lagu di antaranya: Teenage Dream, California Gurls, Last Friday Night, Firework, The One That Got Away, Hummingbird Heart Beat, Peacock, Not Like The Movies, Pearl, Who Am I Living For?, E.T, If We Ever Meet Again, Circle The Drain. Dari ketiga belas lagu tersebut, lagu hanya delapan lagu saja yang liriknya ada mengandung makna idiom. Dalam analisis ini menggunakan teori yang diprakarsai oleh Palmer. Menurut teori Palmer, ada tiga jenis idiom yaitu: Phrasal Verb, Prepositional Verb dan Partial Idiom. Dari hasil analisis yang telah dilakukan didaptakn hasil bahwa dalam Pharsal Verb ditemukan tujuh idiom, dalam Prepositional Verb ditemukan dua idiom dan yang terakhir di dalam Partial Idiom ditemukan satu idiom saja.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Katy Perry, Idiom</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Song seems interesting to be analyzed since it is a unique set of wordings that has its own function. The language of the song is built in a so specific way that it becomes different from ordinary language. Beside ordinary meaning, songs also use an idioms. Almost all of the songs may also be composed by using a language which expresses idiomatic meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">An idiom is an expression, word, or phrase that has a figurative meaning that is comprehended in regard to a common use of that expression that is separated from the literal meaning or definition of the words of which it is made (Hornby, 2007). In linguistics, idioms are usually presumed to be figures of speech contradicting the principle of compositionality; yet the matter remains debated. Idiom is usually not well translated. When an idiom is translated into another language, its meaning may be either changed or meaningless.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types and Meaning Of Idioms In Katy Perry “Teenage Dream” Album</span><br><br><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phrasal Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The phrasal verb formed by verb and the adverb. There are seven idioms that included to phrasal verb:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ I know you get me so I let my walls </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">come down</span><span class="font0">…”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">In this expression, the idiom </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">come down</span><span class="font0"> consist of two words. The two words which construct this idiom are the verb </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">come</span><span class="font0"> and the adverb (down).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ Pictures of last night </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ended up</span><span class="font0"> online.”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Then the second phrasal verb used for expressing idiomatic meaning found in the album of Katy Perry’s “Teenage Dream” is the phrasal verb formed by using the verb ‘end’ and the adverb ‘up’</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Turn</span><span class="font0"> it </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">up</span><span class="font0"> cause its getting’heavy.”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The phrasal verb which is also used to compose the song lyrics in Katy Perry’s album entitled “Teenage Dream” (2010) is the phrasal verb </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">turn up</span><span class="font0">. In this phrasal verb, the word ‘turn’ is a verb, and the word ‘up’ is an adverb, separated with direct object </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">it</span><span class="font0"> (something).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ Come on boys, </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">hanging out</span><span class="font0">”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Hereafter, the next phrasal verb which is found in Katy Perry’s album entitled “Teenage Dream” (2010) is the phrasal verb </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">hanging out</span><span class="font0">. This phrasal verb is formed by the use of the verb ‘hang’ and the adverb ‘out’, with the verb in present tense (-ing).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ We’d </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">make out</span><span class="font0"> in your mustang to radiohead”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Beside the phrasal verbs which are shown in the expression in the data above, Katy Perry has also expressed an idiomatic meaning in his album entitled “Teenage Dream” (2010) by using a phrasal verb formed by the verb ‘make’ and the adverb ‘out.’</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(6) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“ But I still hear the flames </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Calling out</span><span class="font0"> my name”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The phrasal verb which is used in Katy Perry’s “Teenage Dream” to express idiomatic meaning is a phrasal verb formed by using the verb ‘</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">call’</span><span class="font0"> and followed by an adverb ‘out’, with the verb in present tense (-ing) as its base.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(7) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Squeezing out</span><span class="font0"> the life that should be let in”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Squeezing out found in Katy Perry’s “Teenage Dream” (2010), especially in the song entitled “Pearl” is the phrasal verb formed by using the verb ‘</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">squeeze</span><span class="font0">’ and an adverb ‘</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">out</span><span class="font0">’, with the verb placed in present tense (-ing).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.2 Prepositional Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">According to Palmer (1976: 99), prepositional verb is a sequence of verb and preposition and of verb, adverb and preposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(8) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“He put it on me, I </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">put </span><span class="font0">it </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">on</span><span class="font0">”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The expression </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">put on </span><span class="font0">belongs to prepositional verb since it is a sequence of verbs plus preposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(9) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“The way you </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">turn </span><span class="font0">me </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">on</span><span class="font0">. I can’t sleep.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The expression </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">turn </span><span class="font0">me </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">on </span><span class="font0">belongs to prepositional verb since it is a sequence of verbs plus preposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.3 Partial Idiom</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The third type of idiom proposed by Palmer is partial idioms. The partial idiom is where one of the words has its usual meaning, the other has a meaning that is peculiar to the particular sequence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">(10) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;“Like a house of cards, one blow from caving in?”</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The House of Cards (10) is also found to have been used in Katy expression </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">house of cards </span><span class="font0">which belong to partial idiom since the word </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">house </span><span class="font0">has its usual meaning, while the word </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">cards </span><span class="font0">has different meaning from what it is usually found and the meaning that is peculiar to a particular sequence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Based on the analysis that has been described above, it can be concluded that Katy Perry’s songs that are packed in her album entitled “Teenage Dream” (2010) have been composed by using some idioms. However, among the thirteen songs at the album, only eight songs were contained idioms. Her eight songs which are composed by using idioms are: teenage dream, last Friday night, california gurls, firework, the one that got away, who am I living for?, pearl and not like the movies.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The types of idioms that are used to compose the lyrics are: (a) phrasal verbs, (b) prepositional verbs, and (c) partial idioms. However, the types of the idiom is still limited in number. The number of the phrasal verbs found in the song lyrics is seven phrasal verbs, the prepositional verb found in the song is two prepositional verb and the partial idioms found from the song is only one.</span></p>
