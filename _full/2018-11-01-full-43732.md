<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstract</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">This paper entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Declarative and Interrogative Sentences in the Film “American Sniper”</span><span class="font2"> is mainly aimed to identify the kinds and functions of declarative and interrogative sentences used in this film directed by Clint Eastwood. The data of this study is declarative and interrogative sentences taken from film entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">American Sniper</span><span class="font2">. This study was using qualitative and documentary methods. The data were analyzed qualitatively in accordance with some related theories to define the kinds and functions of declarative and interrogative sentences. The theories used in this study are declarative sentence theory </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2">Declarative sentence is a sentence that used to expressing of the statement, it completely formed sentence elements such a subject, verb, object, and another element.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font2"> by Quirk (1985) and interrogative sentence theory </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2">Interrogative is a term used in grammar to refer to features that form questions.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font2"> by John Eastwood (1994). Then the data were presented descriptively by explaining the process and the result of discussion. The finding in the analysis shows that declarative and interrogative sentences was used in the film </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">American Sniper.</span><span class="font2"> The result is: two kinds of declarative sentences, positive and negative declarative sentence, and eight kinds of interrogative sentences: (a) yes-no question; (b) w-h question; (c) suggestion; (d) command; (e) request; (f) greeting; (g) confirmation; (h) offering.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keyword: declarative, interrogative, yes-no question, w-h question, sentences.</span></p>

Teori yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini adalah teori kalimat deklaratif </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2">Kalimat deklaratif adalah kalimat yang digunakan untuk mengekspresikan pernyataan, itu sepenuhnya membentuk elemen kalimat seperti subjek, kata kerja, objek, dan elemen lainnya.&quot; oleh Quirk (1985) dan teori kalimat interogatif &quot;Interogatif adalah istilah yang digunakan dalam tatabahasa untuk merujuk pada fitur yang membentuk pertanyaan.&quot; oleh John Eastwood (1994). <h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language is the method of human communication, either spoken or written, consisting of the use of words in a structured and conventional way. Language is used in almost every daily activity. When described as a system of symbolic communication, language is traditionally seen as consisting of three parts: signs, meanings, and a code connecting signs with their meanings. A sentence can be classified into two types based on function and structure. The sentences that are based on function are; declarative sentence, question or interrogative sentence, imperative sentence, and exclamation sentence. Declarative Sentence is a statement, and Interrogative Sentence is a question. The topic about declarative and interrogative sentences is interesting to be studied. There are many sentences used in conversation every day, and some of those sentences definitely have declarative or interrogative sentences. And sometimes declarative and interrogative sentences have different meaning if the punctuation marks are different too.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Declarative sentence</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Declarative sentence in the conversation is used to express a statement for someone.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “Hold on. I got a woman and a kid, 200 yards out. Moving towards the convoy. Her arms aren’t swinging. She’s carrying something. Yes, she’s got grenade. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She’s got an RKG Russian grenade she just handed to the kid</span><span class="font2">.” Soldier &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “You say a woman and a</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">kid?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “You got eyes on this? Can you confirm?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Soldier : “Negative, you know the R.O.E. your call.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form </span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She’s got an RKG Russian grenade she just handed to the kid</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context </span><span class="font2">: The sentence starts with a subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She,</span><span class="font2"> followed by the verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">got</span><span class="font2">, after that inserting an object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">RKG Russian grenade,</span><span class="font2"> and ends with a period mark (.)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function </span><span class="font2">: To give information for someone. In the film: Chris Kyle tries to give information to his friends in the army camp.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">An interrogative sentence is a type of sentence that asks a question. The interrogative sentence ends with a question mark (?).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya : “What do you do for work? You’re obviously military.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “I polish dolphins. They’re in captivity. What I do is just sort of scrape off the crustaceans.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Do I look stupid to you?” </span><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “No, honestly, you look a little sad.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form </span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Do I look stupid to you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context </span><span class="font2">: Starting with the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Do,</span><span class="font2"> followed by the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I,</span><span class="font2"> after that inserting an object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2"> and ends with a question mark (?).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function </span><span class="font2">: Functioning to ask a question for a confirmation. In the film: Taya seriously asked a question about Chris Kyle job, but he answered the question with a joking answer. Taya felt she was fooled by him, so she wanted a confirmation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>Functioning as a Suggestion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The sentence is used to give a suggestion to someone.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “So, you’re from Texas.” Chris Kyle &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Yes, sir.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “You’re a patriot.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Yes, sir.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “And you’re pissed off.” Chris Kyle : “I’m looking to be of service.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “You like to fight?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “Yes, sir.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “You like to fight. Okay. Well… </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Why don’t you meet the warrior elite?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle &nbsp;&nbsp;: “SEALs.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Navy Recruiter: “Yep. Sea, air, and land.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Why don’t you meet the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">warrior elite?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: Starting with a w-h</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">question words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Why</span><span class="font2">, followed by the verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">do +</span><span class="font2"> inserting negative affixes contraction </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">n-t</span><span class="font2">. After that the sentence is continued with the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you,</span><span class="font2"> And then the object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the warrior elite</span><span class="font2"> is added</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> And in the end of sentence a question mark is used (?)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: &nbsp;&nbsp;Functioning as an</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">interrogative sentence to give a suggestion to someone. In the film: Chris Kyle watched news how terrorist exploded the U.S. embassies, and then he decided to come to a career centre. In the career centre office, Navy recruiter suggested him to join Navy SEALs, after he asked some questions to Chris Kyle.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>Functioning as a Command</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Interrogative sentence functions to give an instruction to someone.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya : “Kenna’s gonna lose that tooth, and I’m so worried she’s gonna swallow it. Like, in her sleep or something. And she said she’d let you pull it, but only you. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Would you do that?</span><span class="font2">”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form </span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Would you do that?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context </span><span class="font2">: Starting with the question word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Would</span><span class="font2">, after that inserting the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2">, then followed by the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">do</span><span class="font2">,the object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font2"> is added</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> And in the end of sentence there is a question mark (?).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function </span><span class="font2">: Functioning as an interrogative sentence to give a command to someone. In the film: Taya daughter would lose her first tooth. So, she gave a command to Chris Kyle to pull it, before it swallowed or something bad happened to her children.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>5.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence Functioning</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>as a Request</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Interrogative sentence has a function to gives an order or make a request to someone.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “Hey, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">can you wait a second?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Martin : “All right. Hurry up.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form </span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Can you wait a second?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context </span><span class="font2">: Starting with the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">can</span><span class="font2">, and then followed by the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2">. After that the object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a second</span><span class="font2"> is added</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> And in the end of the sentence there is a question mark (?)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function </span><span class="font2">: Functioning as an interrogative sentence to make a request to someone. In the film: Chris Kyle looked at the soldiers who were walking going to the plane. And in that team, he saw his brother. So, Chris Kyle made a request to his</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">captain for catching his brother before he headed back to U.S.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence Functioning as a Greeting</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Interrogative sentence has a function to greet people when meeting at the first time or whom we have already known before and to ask someone’s feeling.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Man &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Hello.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Hi.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Man &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“How are you doing?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Good. Thank you.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">How are you doing?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: Starting with the question</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">How</span><span class="font2">, and then followed by the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">are</span><span class="font2">, after that the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2"> is inserted, and in the end there is a question mark (?).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: &nbsp;&nbsp;Functioning as an</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">interrogative sentence to ask a person how his/her feeling is or how the status of their health is. In the film: Taya sat alone at the bar. And then a stranger came and wanted to start a conversation with her. In this situation, the stranger wanted to know how Taya felt.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>5.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence Functioning as a Confirmation</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Interrogative sentence has a function to make sure something or confirmation. It is like a tag-question.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Goat Winston : “It’s a f**king hotbox. Man, the f**king dirt here tastes like dog sh*t.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Chris Kyle : “Oh, well. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">You’d know, wouldn’t you?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Goat Winston : “Shut the f**k up.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">You’d know, wouldn’t</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: Starting with the subject</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">You</span><span class="font2">, and then followed by the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">would</span><span class="font2">, and in the end of the sentence a question mark (?) is added.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: &nbsp;&nbsp;Functioning as an</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">interrogative sentence to ask a person to get a confirmation. In the film: Goat Winston grumbling about weather in Iraq. With the joke intonation, Chris Kyle asked a question to get confirmation of what Goat Winston had said before.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>5.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Sentence Functioning as an Offering</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Interrogative sentence has a function to offer something to someone.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Man &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Can I get you a drink?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Will a drink make you six</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">inches taller and charming? Will it make you not married?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Man &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “I’m not….”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Taya &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Yeah. I just watched you</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">take your ring off. Okay? Don’t be a scumbag. Go home”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Form &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Can I get you a drink?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Context &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: Starting with the modal</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">can</span><span class="font2">, after that inserting the subject </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font2">, and then followed by the object </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">drink</span><span class="font2">, and in the end a question mark (?) is added.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Function &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font2">: &nbsp;&nbsp;Functioning as an</span></p>
