<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Syntactic Structure of Ditransitive Verbs Found in the Sentences Used in Eugene O’neill’s One-Act Play: </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">A Wife For A Life</span></h1>

<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini berjudul Struktur Sintaksis dari Kata Kerja Ditransitif yang Ditemukan di Kalimat-Kalimat Yang Digunakan Dalam Naskah Lakon Satu Babak Oleh Eugene O'neill: Seorang Istri Untuk Satu Kehidupan. Penelitian ini dilakukan karena menganalisis kata kerja adalah hal yang menarik, oleh karena setiap kalimat memiliki predikat, dan setiap predikat adalah frase verba, maka setiap kalimat akhirnya disusun oleh kata kerja. Verba atau kata kerja adalah kelas kata yang penting dan menjadi bagian-bagian dasar dari kalimat bahasa Inggris yang biasanya menunjukkan tindakan, kejadian, atau keadaan. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengidentifikasi kata kerja yang diklasifikasikan ke dalam kata kerja ditransitif dan juga menganalisis struktur sintaksis kalimat-kalimat yang mengandung kata kerja ditransitif dalam data.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sumber data dari penelitian ini diambil dari naskah lakon satu babak oleh Eugene O'Neill yang berjudul Seorang Istri Untuk Satu Kehidupan. Metode yang digunakan untuk mengumpulkan data adalah metode dokumentasi melalui teknik pencatatan. Metode deskriptif kualitatif digunakan untuk menganalisis dan menyajikan data. Penelitian ini menggunakan teori yang dikemukakan oleh Quirk (1973) untuk menganalisis konstruksi verba ditransitif dalam kalimat dan struktur sintaksis kalimatkalimat yang mengandung verba ditransitif berdasarkan teori yang diusulkan oleh Brown dan Miller (1991).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa dua jenis verba ditransitif ditemukan dalam data, yaitu verba ditransitif yang diikuti oleh frasa benda, (seperti: bercerita, menuangkan, memberikan, menulis, menelgram, dan berjanji) dan kata kerja ditransitif yang memungkinkan objek tidak langsung dihilangkan dan diganti dengan frasa preposisi; (memberikan kepada, membawa untuk, menemukan untuk, mengirim ke, dan mewakili untuk). Kedua jenis verba ditransitif tersebut juga disajikan dalam analisis sintaksis menggunakan diagram pohon untuk menggambarkan jenis verba ditransitif dengan rumus; (1) S [NP VP]; VP [V NP NP]; NP [Det N]; (2) S [NP VP]; VP [V NP PP]; NP [Det N]; PP [P N]. Kesimpulannya, hanya ada dua jenis verba ditransitive yang ditemukan dalam sumber data sementara verba ditransitif yang memungkinkan objek lain untuk dihilangkan tidak ditemukan dalam sumber data.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: struktur sintaksis, verba, diagram pohon, naskah lakon satu babak</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language linguistically deals with word, clause, and sentence. According to Agnes (2005) we order the words into well-structured unit before we utter the expressions that</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">our mind speak firstly in order to have a clear meaning and to make it easier for listener to interpret what we have said. A sentence is a linguistic unit consisting of group of words which is usually a grammatically complete statement. In the book entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">An Introduction to English Syntax</span><span class="font2"> (2002) Miiler states that the traditional definition of sentence talk of grammatical unit is built up from smaller units. The network of relation among the words in a sentence is called structure. In linguistics, the study about the sentence structure of languages is syntax. Latief (1995:1) stated that syntax is the system of pattern by which words are used to make the expression of the meaning into phrases and sentences. There are a large number of lexical items in every language. In English, these lexical items can be classified based on their categories. However, these categorical classifications are limited such as noun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, etc. This study analyzed one type of verb that exists in the linguistic system. Verb becomes the reason why this study was conducted because every sentence has a predicate, and every predicate is a verb phrase, meaning that every sentence is ultimately structured around a verb. Verb is an important class and it belongs to the part of speech that usually denotes an action, an occurrence, or state of being. Ditransitive verbs complementation proposed by Quirk and syntactical structure proposed by Brown and Miller were used to analyze the data taken from one-act play script entitled “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Wife for a Life</span><span class="font2">” by Eugene O’Neill.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data for this study were taken from the sentences in Eugene O’Neill’s One Act Play Script: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">A Wife for A Life</span><span class="font2">. In collecting the data, the documentation method was applied. The method was applied to obtain the data of the one-act play script. The documentary method which was divided into finding and underlining techniques was applied to collect the data from one-act play script. The collected data were analyzed and presented using the qualitative and descriptive method.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of the Types of Ditransitive Verbs in the Data</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This analysis presents two types of ditransitive verbs, (1) ditransitive verb immediately followed by NPs and (2) ditransitive verb allowing the indirect object to be omitted and replaced by a prepositional phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data 1</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He hands Jack the letter.</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/29514-1.jpg" alt="" style="width:323pt;height:164pt;">
<p><span class="font2">The sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He hands Jack the letter</span><span class="font2"> is categorized into a sentence that contains ditransitive verbs followed by NPs. In the sentence above, the node S dominates two daughter nodes labeled NP and VP. The NP consists of terminal node Pron (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font2">) and VP expands into; V (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hands</span><span class="font2">), NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Jack</span><span class="font2">), and NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">). The NP </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2"> consists of terminal nodes Det (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2">) and N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">letter</span><span class="font2">). The verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">handed</span><span class="font2"> acts as a ditransitive verb followed by NPs (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Jack</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">) since its verb has the structure rule VP [V NP NP]. The phrasal structure is defined below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">S -----* NP VP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------* Pron</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">VP -----► V NP NP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP -----► PN</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Det N</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data 2</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She did give me an address of person she might meets</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">Dinsjrani</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/29514-2.jpg" alt="" style="width:349pt;height:201pt;">
<p><span class="font2">The sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She did give me an address of person she might meets</span><span class="font2"> is categorized into a sentence that contains ditransitive verbs followed by NPs. This sentence contains three major constituents; NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2">), Aux (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">did</span><span class="font2">), and VP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">give me an address of person she might meets</span><span class="font2">). The node S has two branches and expands as two daughter nodes labeled NP and VP. This sentence also contains two intermediate level categories of NP and VP. The NP consists of terminal node Pron (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2">) and the VP dominates V (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">give</span><span class="font2">), NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">me</span><span class="font2">), NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an address of person she might meet</span><span class="font2">). The article </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an </span><span class="font2">combines with the topmost NP projection to form the maximal projection NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an address of person she might calls)</span><span class="font2">. The NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an address of person she might calls) </span><span class="font2">dominates N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">address</span><span class="font2">), PP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">of person</span><span class="font2">) and NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">person she might meets</span><span class="font2">) which consist of S (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she might meets</span><span class="font2">) as adjunct that projects into NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">she</span><span class="font2">) and VP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">might meets</span><span class="font2">). The VP consists of terminal node Aux (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">might</span><span class="font2">) and V (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">meets</span><span class="font2">).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">give</span><span class="font2"> acts as ditransitive verb followed by NPs (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">me</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">an address of person she might meets</span><span class="font2">) since its verb has the structure rule VP [V NP NP]. The phrasal structure is defined as follows:</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data 3</span></h2>
<div>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I’ll bring the letter for you</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/29514-3.jpg" alt="" style="width:302pt;height:217pt;">
</div><br clear="all"></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I’ll bring the letter for you</span><span class="font2"> is categorized into a sentence that contains ditransitive verbs which allow the indirect object to be omitted and replaced by a prepositional phrase. The sentence has three constituents; NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font2">), Aux (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">will</span><span class="font2">), and VP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bring the letter for you</span><span class="font2">). The node S has two branches and expands as two daughter nodes labeled NP and VP. The auxiliary has only one constituent </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">will</span><span class="font2">. The VP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bring the letter for you</span><span class="font2">) has three constituents V (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bring</span><span class="font2">), NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">), and PP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for you</span><span class="font2">). The NP contains terminal nodes Det (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2">) and N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">letter</span><span class="font2">). The PP dominates P (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to</span><span class="font2">) and N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">you</span><span class="font2">) as the terminal nodes. The verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">gives</span><span class="font2"> acts as a ditransitive verb followed by NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">) and PP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for you</span><span class="font2">), where PP was omitted and allowed to replace the indirect</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">object. The ditransitive verb has the structure rule VP [V NP PP]. The phrasal structure</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">is defined as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">S -----► NP Aux VP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Pron</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">VP -----► V NP PP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------* Det N</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">PP -----*P N</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data 4</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Sentence: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He hands the letter to the Older Man</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/29514-4.jpg" alt="" style="width:305pt;height:198pt;">
<p><span class="font2">The sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He hands the letter to the Older Man</span><span class="font2"> is categorized into a sentence that contains ditransitive verbs which allow the indirect object to be omitted and replaced by a prepositional phrase. In the sentence above, the node S dominates two daughter nodes labeled NP and VP. The NP consists of terminal node Pron (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font2">) and VP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hands the letter to the Older Man</span><span class="font2">) expands into; V (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hands</span><span class="font2">), NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">), and NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to the Older Man</span><span class="font2">). The NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter)</span><span class="font2"> consists of terminal nodes Det (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2">) and N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">letter</span><span class="font2">) followed by PP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to the Older Man)</span><span class="font2"> that consists of P (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to)</span><span class="font2"> and NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the Older Man</span><span class="font2">). The lowest NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the Older Man</span><span class="font2">) consists of terminal nodes Det (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the)</span><span class="font2">, A (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">older</span><span class="font2">), and N (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">man).</span><span class="font2"> The verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hands</span><span class="font2"> acts as a ditransitive verb followed by NP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the letter</span><span class="font2">) and PP, where PP (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to the Older Man</span><span class="font2">) was omitted and allowed to replace the indirect object</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">. </span><span class="font2">The ditransitive verb has the structure rule VP [V NP PP]. The phrasal structure is defined as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">S -----► NP VP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Pron</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">VP -----► V NP PP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Det N</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">PP ------► P NP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP -----► Det A N</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the discussion, there were two types of ditransitive verbs found in the data source; (1) the ditransitive verb is immediately followed by NPs with previous example in the data: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hand</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">give</span><span class="font2">, and (2) ditransitive verb which allows the indirect object to be omitted and replaced by a prepositional phrase; the preposition concerned being indicated, with previous example in the data: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bring for</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">hand to</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In addition, it was found that the sentences bearing ditransitive verbs have the structure for each type:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ditansitive verbs are immediately followed by NPs</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">S -----► NP VP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">VP -----► V NP NP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Det N</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ditransitive verb which allows the indirect object to be omitted and replaced by a prepositional phrase</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">S -----► NP VP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">VP -----► V NP PP</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">NP ------► Det N</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">PP ------►P N</span></p>
