<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>EVALUATION OF LAND SUITABILITY FOR INCREASING PRODUCTIVITY IN DEGRADED UNDA WATERSHED, DISTRICT OF KARANGASEM, BALI</span></h1>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Ni Made Trigunasih*, I Nyoman Merit, Wiyanti, I Wayan Narka, and I Nyoman Dibia</span></h2>

<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Evaluation of land suitability to increase the productivity of degraded lands in the Unda watershed aims: providing a database of land suitability for land use planning in support of sustainable agricultural development, providing spatial information, and recommendations in accordance with the land use plan of inhibiting factors that exist. Identification of characteristic land quality evaluation survey carried out by the method of land with exploration area of research followed by soil sampling at some sample of land units. Land suitability classification using the criteria of the Technical Guidelines for Evaluation of Land For Agricultural Commodities of Soil Research Institute (2003) is by matching between the haracteristics of the land with the requirements of growing plants was evaluated. Types of agricultural crops that are evaluated include plantation crops and food crops hotikultura fruits. Suitability actual land (land suitability based on survey data) are mostly classified as unsuitable (N), and marginally suitable (S3). Qualities/characteristics as the land that is the main limiting factor, among others: quality of land erosion, slope, water availability, temperature, rooting media (soil texture and coarse material). Sustainable land use planning can be recommended with agroforestry, accompanied by vegetative conservation measures because it is cheaper and easier to be carried out by farmers. Recommended cultivation effort is a combination of timber plants with horticultural crops of fruits with the provisions of at least 40% must be enclosed timber. Plant timber that can be developed between the other plants that are not too heavy canopy.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Land suitability, land productivity, land degradation, Unda watershed</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Unda watershed is the largest watershed in Bali Province with 91,585 ha of land dominated by volcanic formation. Land use is 79.59% including dry land; 68.41% of the area consist of Regosol soils that are highly susceptible to erosion; and</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">45.64% of its territory are hilly to mountainous, and there are development of illegally C digging sites in some places. The results of monitoring conducted by Unda Anyar Watershed Management Center in cooperation with Faculty of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Agriculture Udayana University (2013), stated that the Unda watershed belongs to the first priority that must be immediately restored.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The selection of this research area is based on the physical condition of the Unda watershed as mentioned above. Land productivity to support optimal production has been degraded / decreased. . In addition, information on of land resource at the micro-scale level is limited, and still low level of land management / management applied. The reason for choosing food crops and plantations to be evaluated is that most of the watershed area of Unda is dominated by dryland agriculture in mixed and cultivated fields.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The specific objectives of this research were (1) Increase the productivity and quality of degraded agricultural land to support national food security, (2) Establish formulation of land-based area-specific land-use management practices, (3) Develope a watershed-based land suitability database for urban planning (4) Establish</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">criteria and guidelines for controlling and conserving agricultural land based on sitespecific watersheds, as well as technical guidance, (5) Generate policies, programs and activities that can be offered to local governments related to the control of land damage and Increased productivity of agricultural land, and (6) Provide spatial information in the form of land suitability maps, erosion hazard maps, and land use planning maps.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Adequate databases on semidetail / detail scale in land use planning based on Watershed in Bali Province is hardly available.. Currently the database on potential and land management is mostly available on a scale of scale (scale 1: 250,000) so it is still difficult to apply in the field. Therefore, the provision of a database on the potential and pattern of land management in a larger scale is absolutely necessary as a reference in land management in a watershed, especially in the Unda watershed which is highly vulnerable to land degradation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This land suitability evaluation study is very urgent to be done in relation to the sustainability of land resources in supporting sustainable agriculture and food security at the regional and national levels. The existence of a sustainable agriculture sustainable food protection law (Law No. 41 of 2007) is one of the answers, which needs to be supplemented by various government regulations, ministerial regulations and regional head regulations.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the national strategic issues related to recent disasters such as landslide, erosion, flood, drought, volcanic disaster, etc., a study titled land suitability evaluation for increasing degraded land productivity is a very urgent study to undertake . The results of this study are expected to provide solutions on rehabilitation and conservation of land resources, increase productivity with input in accordance with existing land boundaries.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The aim of the project are : (1) the compilation of land suitability maps; (2) the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">finding of limiting factors that must be addressed in sustainable land management; (3) the formulation of recomendations in the use of land based on the potential/suitability of land and constraints owned by the land concerned.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">The Unda Watershed Territory Management Unit (SWP) is astronomically located on 08<sup>o</sup>09'36 &quot;LS to 08<sup>o</sup>34.'48&quot; LS and 115<sup>o</sup>23'24 &quot;BT to 115<sup>o</sup>42'36&quot; BT. Administratively includes Buleleng District (Tejakula District), Bangli District (Kintamani Sub-district), Klungkung Regency (Dawan and Klungkung Subdistricts) and Karangasem District (Bebandem, Rendang, Selat, Manggis, Sidemen, Karangasem, Abang, Kubu Subdistricts) is 91,585 ha (BP DAS Unda Anyar, 2010).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Land and water resource management planning activities at SWP DAS Unda are conducted through the approach of land survey unit (the smallest</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">unit having the same/similar characteristics that can still be limited in accordance with the scale of the map created). Further more, conformity evaluation activities for various food crops and plantation commodities as well as soil and water conservation measures/land management are required to be conducted on each surveyed land unit for the concerned.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The series of activities in this study include: collection and evaluation of secondary data (literature study, traceability of previous documents/reports) as well as primary data (through field survey activities, soil sampling, identification of environmental conditions and current land management, and Conducting interviews with local communities/farmers on issues, and constraints faced, in current resource management.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Evaluation of land suitability is done by matching between the requirements of growing plants with the characteristics/quality of existing land. The land suitability classification criteria used is</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">the land suitability classification system compiled by Puslittanak (1993).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Land use plans for each unit of land are prepared based on the evaluation of land suitability, prediction of erosion hazard and the amount of sedimentation occurring. The basic principle of drafting the land use plan used is increasing agricultural production both physically and economically, sustainably, without damaging the environment. In other words land use in accordance with the ability/ suitability of land.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Characteristics / Land Quality of Research Area</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the analysis of soil samples, conducted in Soil and Environmental Science Laboratory Faculty of Agriculture Udayana University, soil</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">physical properties such as soil texture is dominated by slightly coarse texture (sandy loam) with sand content 40-70%, medium texture (clay and clay dust) and slightly smooth texture (clay); Crude material ranges from 10 - 60%; Soil permeability ranges from very fast to moderately fast; Soil erodibility (soil sensitivity to erosion) ranges from moderate to somewhat high; And the erosion hazard rate of 87% is heavy to very severe.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The chemical properties and soil fertility covering soil CEC (cation exange capasity) varies from 36.92 me 100 g-1 (low) to 84.3 me 100 g-1 (high); base saturation are &gt;&nbsp;150 % (highly), soil pH classified are 6.1-7.1 (neutral), salt content are 0.85 mmhos (very low) to 3.49 mmhos (high), C- organic are 5.70 % (low) to 37.21 % (high), Total-N are 0.50 % (very low) to 0.18 % (low), P-available are 1.51 ppm (very low) to 160.17 ppm (high); And K-available are 80.90 ppm (low) to 290.78 ppm (high).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on agroclimate data including annual mean temperature, rainfall and dry month taken from several nearby climatological stations, the average annual temperature ranges from 19.5-260C, dry month (&lt;75 mm) ranges from 0-5 months, mean rainfall Annual range from 1555 mm - 3328 mm.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Land Suitability</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">The determination of the actual land suitability class/subclass using a matching system is to combine and compare the characteristics/quality of the land with the crop requirements of the evaluated plant commodities. Land suitability criteria use technical land evaluation guidelines for agricultural commodities from Soil Research Institute (2003). In this matching system, the minimum law of land suitability is determined by the smallest value of the land characteristics/qualities as the heaviest or hardest to overcome compared to other limiting factors.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The type of plant commodity evaluated is the commodity crop that has been developed/cultivated on each unit of land concerned. Plantation commodities are widely developed in research areas sch as: coconut, cocoa, clove, and robusta coffee. Fruit horticulture plants include: banana, orange, durian, jackfruit, salak, rambutan, mangosteen and mango. The result of actual and potential land suitability evaluation based on the result of matching the quality/characteristics of the land with some commodities evaluated on each unit of land can be seen in Table 1.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Actual Land Suitability</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the result of matching between the quality/characteristics of the land and the evaluated plant commodities, actual land suitability is largely classified as unsuitable (N), and marginally appropriate (S3). The quality/characteristics of the land that are the main obstacle factors are: the quality of land erosion hazard (slope and erosion hazard), water availability (rainfall</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">and dry moon), temperature, rooting medium (soil texture and coarse material).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">As previously mentioned, the research area, especially dry land, is dominated by wavy area form up to mountain with slope range of 15 -&gt; 65% with erosion hazard level is heavy to very heavy. Besides, there is no adequate soil conservation measures either mechanically or vegetatively.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The quality of land availability of water (rainfall) is a limiting factor, especially in the upstream region due to high rainfall exceeding that required for horticultural crops of fruits. This proved to be very rare to find the type of fruit plants that developed in the study area. The quality of water availability (duration of dry months) is a limiting factor due to the long dry months (5 months) for the lower research area). For forest timber species the quality of land availability is not too problematic.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>Potential Land Suitability</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the assumption of the type of improvement effort that can be done on the quality/characteristics of the land that is an inhibiting factor, potential land suitability can be grouped to:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plantation crops (coconut, cocoa, clove, and robusta coffee) range from unsuitable (N), quite suitable (S2), marginally appropriate (S3). Land quality as a limiting factor is: quality of land erosion hazard (slope and erosion hazard), temperature, water availability (rainfall and dry moon), and rooting medium &nbsp;(soil &nbsp;texture &nbsp;and crude</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">material).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The horticultural crops of fruits (bananas, oranges, durian, jackfruit, rambutan, mangosteen, and mango) range from unsuitable (N), quite suitable (S2), marginally suitable (S3), and highly sitable (S1). Land quality that is an inhibiting factor for the development of fruits horticultural crops are: quality of land erosion hazard (slope and erosion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">hazard), quality of water availability (rainfall, dry moon), temperature, root medium (soil texture and crude material)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the assumption of the level of improvement and the type of improvement effort on the actual quality/characteristics of the land to be potential according to the level of management, the suitability of potential land for evaluated commodities (plantation crops and horticulture fruits) can generally be raised one to two levels.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Heavy to very severe erosion hazard limits in terrain conditions are not too steep can be overcome by conservation actions either by mechanical or vegetative means by planting plant species that can withstand erosion and core reinforcement plants. However, on steep slopes to very steep terrain with very severe erosion hazards, it is very difficult to repair so that potentially remains a limitation in the development of the land concerned.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Limitations of water availability (rainfall) that is too high in the upper region, still remains a limiting factor in the development of several commodities of plantation crops and horticultural crops of fruits. Similarly, on the barrier duration of dry months in the lower region. Low temperature land quality in some land units in the upper region is potentially still a limiting factor in the development of some plantation commodities and horticulture of fruits.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The quality of rooted soil media (coarse soil texture and high coarse material) is still a constraint for several types of plantation commodities and horticulture of fruits. The rather coarse soil texture with high sand content still remains a barrier for some agricultural commodities because the grasp of soil to water is very low. The high content of crude materials in some units of land is still a limiting factor because it can interfere with the development of plant roots. Rough ground texture with high sand content on slopes</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">that are not too steep can still be overcome with the addition of organic material. Map of Potential Land Suitability in Unda Watershed is presented of Figure 1.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Land Management Directions/ Recommendations</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">As it is known that land suitability evaluation not only produces land suitability classes, it should also be able to demonstrate further land management options. The assumption used is that the land suitability class as described above is grouped based on the type and number of limiting factors.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Establishment of alternative land use/management must be based on the results of land suitability evaluation, namely: description of land characteristics / qualities, land suitability evaluation results, species description and intensity of inhibiting factors, easy assumptions of possible improvements to its inhibiting factors (land productivity test) . However, this research did not reach the land</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">productivity test due to time and budget constraints.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The need to design alternative land use in the research area is for improvement (sustainability of the land), in addition to increased productivity of land /plants. In accordance with the results of field identification, that dry areas in the research area are mostly very vulnerable to erosion and landslide hazards so that in their management all their rules must be fully understood and adhered to, in accordance</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">with the essence of land suitability is sustainability, terms of use, social culture and economy.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The high level of erosion hazard, vulnerability to landslide, and lack of conservation measures in the research area require first priority on erosion and landslide prevention actions through soil and water conservation measures either by mechanical methods or by vegetative methods.
