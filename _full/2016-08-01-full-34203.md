<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstract</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Judul dari skripsi ini adalah idiom yang berkaitan dengan tubuh yang ditemukan pada tiga novel terlaris. Tujuan dari skripsi ini untuk menemukan jenis dan arti idiom yang berkaitan dengan tubuh dan juga tipe yang paling sering muncul di novel.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Teori yang digunakan pada skripsi ini yaitu Teori dari Palmer (1976) tentang jenis idiom dan Teori dari Leech (1974) tentang arti idiom. Tiga novel terlaris dipilih sebagai data pada skrips ini. Dari data tersebut ditemukan idiom yang berkaitan dengan tubuh. Idiom yang berkaitan dengan tubuh ditentukan dengan melihat arti dari setiap kata pada idiom tersebut bukan pada arti yang berkaitan dengan keadaan yang ada. Metode yang dipakai dalam skripsi ini adalah penelitian pustaka. Data yang terkumpul di baca dengan hati – hati, lalu ditentukan apakah idiom tersebut berkaitan dengan tubuhatau tidak, kemudian diidentifikasi jenis dan arti dari idiom tersebut berdasarkan pada teori.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Idiom yang paling sering ditemukan adalah </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">phrasal verb. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Arti idiom yang ditemukan pada skripsi ini adalah arti konotatif, arti konseptual, dan arti afektif.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Kata kunci: idiom, arti idiom, jenis idiom</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">The idioms were analyzed in this study because idioms still exist in our daily life and many people do not really understand their meanings. Three best seller novels were chosen; they are romantic and classic novels which have more expressions of idiom than others. Furthermore, this study is useful to many people who read the novels and do not really understand their meanings. We can find idioms in our daily life because they are a kind of idiomatic expressions which make such novels enjoyable to read. The purpose of this study is to know what types of idiomatic expressions which mostly appear in such novels. Many idioms are chosen by the writer in a novel as the writer wants to construct and convey the connotative meaning; the idiomatic expressions make the readers feel enjoy and more imaginative when they read the novel. This study can</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">enrich the result of research in universities and increase the reader’s knowledge of idiomatic expressions. Most previous studies have analyzed idioms but they have used songs as the data source. This study used several novels as data source. The idioms which are discussed are those which are related to the body. An example of an idiom of the body is </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kick the bucket</span><span class="font2">, meaning committing suicide. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kick</span><span class="font2"> is a verb which is done using so it is called the idiom which is related to the body. This study used three best seller novels as the data source.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data were taken directly from the primary data source; they are romantic and adventure novels entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> by Roth </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">(2012), Twilight</span><span class="font2"> by Meyer (2003) and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Frankenstein</span><span class="font2"> by Shelley (2008). Every idiom which is related to the body was found in the novels</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> The method which was used in this study to collect data is the documentation method. The method and technique of collecting can be described in three steps they are reading the novel carefully, finding the idioms related to the body by asking questions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">what you use to</span><span class="font2"> … or directly noting the idioms made up of parts of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">the body, and noting down every phrase which was identified to be an idiom related to the body found in the novels.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The collected data were checked using the sources providing the information and the idioms were listed. The sources are the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Oxford Advanced Learnerʼs Dictionary <sup>7th </sup></span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the handbook of English Idioms</span><span class="font2"> by Lou</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Method and technique of analyzing data were qualitatively method in accordance with the theories. The process of analyzing data is as follows first, The collected data were categorized based on the theories proposed by Palmer (1976), After finding the types of idioms, the meaning of each idiom was described based on the three types of meaning adopted from the theory proposed by Geoffrey Leech (1974), then relating the meaning of each idiom to the story to make it easily understandable, after that finding the types of idioms which mostly in the best seller novels. Finally, the problems of the study were solved and the result of the research can be shared and learnt.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Phrasal Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“I </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">breathe out</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, and the pressure is still there.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The idiom ‘breathe out’ was found in the novel entitled Insurgent. The combination of the words ‘breathe’ and ‘out’ has a new meaning; therefore, it is called a idiom. ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Breathe out</span><span class="font2">’ is related to the body based on the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">breathe</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Breathe</span><span class="font2"> is the action of human to inhale and exhale by using the nose; that is why breathe is stated to be related to the body. Based on the theory proposed by Palmer (1976) concerning the types of idiom, ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">breathe outʼ</span><span class="font2"> is a phrasal verb because it consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻbreatheʼ</span><span class="font2"> as verb and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻoutʼ </span><span class="font2">as adverb. The meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">breathe out</span><span class="font2"> is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">exhale</span><span class="font2">. Based on the theory by Leech (1974) about meaning of idiom, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻbreathe outʼ</span><span class="font2"> has conceptual meaning because </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">breathe out</span><span class="font2"> is the central meaning of the expression</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Partial Idiom</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">“</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I kept </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">my eyes down </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on the reading list the teacher had given me.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">An idiom ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my eyes downʼ</span><span class="font2"> was found. The combination of the meaning of individual words ‘my eyes’ and ‘down’ has new meaning which is different from the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">meaning of ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my eyes down</span><span class="font2">’; therefore, it is called idiom. The ideom ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">My eyes down</span><span class="font2">’ is related to the body because there is the word ‘eyes’ in the idiom. Based on the theory proposed by Palmer (1976) concerning the types of idiom, ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my eyes downʼ</span><span class="font2"> is a partial idiom, because it consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my eyes</span><span class="font2"> which has its usual meaning of the ‘eye’ and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">down</span><span class="font2"> which means looking something behind. So if the meaning is joined together it becomes looking something behind with the eyes or it can be concluded that the meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my eyes down</span><span class="font2"> is reading something. Based on the theory proposed by Leech (1974) concerning meaning, the idiom </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻmy eyes downʼ</span><span class="font2"> contains conceptual meaning because it refers to its purely conceptual content</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prepositional Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“She replied that she had been </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">looking for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the child and demanded earnestly if anything had been heard concerning him.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">An idiom ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking forʼ o</span><span class="font2">n page 91 of the novel entitled Frankeinstein was found. The individual meaning of ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking for</span><span class="font2">’ is different from that of collective meaning, that is, why </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking for</span><span class="font2"> is stated to be an idiom. The ideom ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking for</span><span class="font2">’ is related to the body by looking at the word ‘looking’. The eyes are used for looking at something, so </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking</span><span class="font2"> is related to the body. Based on the theory proposed by Palmer (1976) about the types of idiom, ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking forʼ</span><span class="font2"> is a prepositional verb because consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻlookingʼ</span><span class="font2"> as a verb and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻforʼ</span><span class="font2"> as a preposition. The meaning of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">looking for</span><span class="font2"> is searching. Based on the theory by Leech (1974) about meaning of idioms, the idiom </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ʻlooking forʼ</span><span class="font2"> contains the connotative meaning because it refers to its purely conceptual content</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This study has analyzed three best seller novels. They are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgen</span><span class="font2">t by Roth (2012), </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Twilight</span><span class="font2"> by Meyer (2003) and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Frankenstein</span><span class="font2"> by Shelley (2008). A number of idioms were found in such novels. Such idioms are categorized based on the Palmer’s theory about types of idiom. The types of idioms, based on Palmer (1976), which mostly appear are phrasal verbs. All types were found in such three best seller novels.</span></p>
