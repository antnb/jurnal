<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi ini membahas tentang jenis-jenis asal mula kata sifat dan fungsi kata sifat. Sumber data yang digunakan dalam skripsi ini adalah sebuah majalah yang berjudul ‘Now Bali’. Data dikumpulkan dengan beberapa tahap dan dianalisa secara rinci menggunakan kajian teori dari Quirk et.al (1985) dalam buku yang berjudul A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language dan Quirk dan Greenbaum (1973) dalam buku yang berjudul A University Grammar of English.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Analisa dalam skripsi ini menemukan bahwa ada 10 jenis akhiran yang membentuk kata sifat yang ditemukan pada skripsi ini, akhiran-akhiran tersebut adalah </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">– </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">full, -less, -able, -ous, -ive, -al, -y, -ic, -ly, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan –</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ish. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Akhiran-akhiran tersebut mempunyai definisi masing-masing dan bisa dipasangkan dengan kata benda atau kata kerja untuk membentuk kata sifat. Pada skripsi ini ditemukan 2 fungsi kata sifat, yaitu atributip dan predikatip. Terdapat 2 jenis kata sifat yang berfungsi sebagai predikatip, yaitu pelengkap subyek dan pelengkap objek.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: kata sifat, fungsi, majalah</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language consists of sentences that bring information or message. A sentence is the largest unit of language structure treated in traditional grammar having a subject and predicate. A sentence is influenced by some factors. One of them is grammar. Grammar is the entire system of language, including its syntax, morphology, semantic and phonology.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A sentence consists of words. Word is a meaningful unit of speech. A word can be divided into some classes contributing to the meaning of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">sentence. Adjective is one of the word classes that is analyzed in this study. It is a category of lexemes characteristically denoting properties of person or objects</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This topic is chosen as adjective has some characteristics which distinguish it from other word classes (noun, adverb, pronoun, preposition, conjunction). It can modifies a noun and gives more information about the noun that goes with it. This study is different from other theses because this study gives more priority to find out and to identify the derivational adjectives and functions of adjectives. All of the students need to understand more specifically about adjective because it has an important role in English. The purpose of this study is to give a deeper analysis of the functions of derivational adjectives.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Research method is a system or procedure that is used in order to examine certain objects. It includes the sources of the data, the method and technique of collecting data which explains how the data will be taken from the sources and how that data will be analyzed. The research method in this study consists of three components, they are data source, data collection and data analysis. As described as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font2">The functions of adjective were analyzed through two steps. The first one was checking the functions of adjectives. The second one was classifying the adjectives according to their functions.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Derivational Adjectives and Their Functions in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Now Bali</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> Magazine.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Formation of Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Adjectives can be formed from word bases with some suffixes. Derivational process occurs when the process results in the formation of adjectives from base of a different word class.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Derivational Process</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This section discusses adjectives that are formed from word class noun (N) and verb (V)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Noun or Verb + Suffixes</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There were 10 derivational suffixes found in Now Bali magazine</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">edition September 2012, those suffixes are discussed as follow:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark19"></a>a.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">–ful</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex: I’m very </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grateful</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grateful</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grate</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark21"></a>b.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-less</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex: The sheer size of this outdoor playground provides a true sense of place and a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">timeless</span><span class="font2"> ambience that welcomes one and all.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">timeless</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">time</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark23"></a>c.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-able</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex: This </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">affordable</span><span class="font2">, luxury offer is valid until December 31, 2012.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">affordable</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from verb word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">afford</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark25"></a>d.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-ous</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex : Summer Syndrome II will be Potato Head’s biggest and most </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">outrageous</span><span class="font2"> celebration of the year.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">outrageous</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">outrage</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark27"></a>e.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-ive</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex : Meals at Jimbaran aren’t only mouth-watering; they’re also educational and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">interactive</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">interactive</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from verb word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">interact</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark29"></a>f.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-al</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex: Balidiscovery.com installed a Quick Response Code (QR</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">additional</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">addition</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark31"></a>g.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-y</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex: I am also </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lucky</span><span class="font2"> to have as part of our team here at NOW! Bali a young Balinese lady named Kartika Dewi Suardana.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lucky</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">luck</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark33"></a>h.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-ic</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>

<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">iconic</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">icon</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark35"></a>i.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suffix (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-ly</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">ex : It is a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">homely</span><span class="font2"> and beautiful resort, with separate villas and the Nyoman Sumerta Art Gallery.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">homely</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">home</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">ex </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">: </span><span class="font2">Sushi Tei Japanese Restaurant whose building bears the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">modish</span><span class="font2"> look punctuated with different themes and concepts unique to the island.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">iconic</span><span class="font2"> is adjective that is derived from noun word class which is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">icon</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Functions of Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">An adjective, when it is used in a sentence or phrase, performs a syntactic function. That is to say, it has a certain function at its place in the sentence or phrase. Furthermore, adjective can be classified according to the syntactic function they perform.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Attributive</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Adjectives are attributive when they premodify nouns, appear between the determiner and the head of noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Ex: When we were back in “lazy cow” days we only had very basic seafood restaurants (and the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wonderful</span><span class="font2"> Poppies of course).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The sentence above consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> as determiner, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wonderful</span><span class="font2"> as adjective, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Poppies</span><span class="font2"> as noun. The adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">wonderful</span><span class="font2"> appears between determiner </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> and the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Poppies</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Predicative</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Predicative adjectives are divided into two:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark45"></a>a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Subject complement</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">As subject complement, when they explain the subject of the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Ex: I’m very </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grateful.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The sentence above consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font2"> as subject, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">‘m</span><span class="font2"> as copular verb, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font2"> as intensifier, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">grateful</span><span class="font2"> as predicative adjective which functions as subject complement.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark46"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark47"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Object Complement</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">As object complement, when they explain the object of the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">ex: The journey begins with the first smelling, where you want to find out if you know the wine already and if the wine is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">fruity,</span><span class="font2"> earthy, or vegetative.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This sentence consists of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> as determiner</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, wine</span><span class="font2"> as object</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, fruity</span><span class="font2"> as predicative adjective which has object complement.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark48"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark49"></a>6) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">After discussing and analyzing the data from the data source in the previous chapter, there are some significant points that can be concluded from the analysis on the Functions of Derivational Adjectives in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Now Bali</span><span class="font2"> magazine.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There are 10 types of derivational adjectives, those suffixes are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–full, -less, -able, -ous, -ive, -al, -y, -ic, -ly, and -ish</span><span class="font2"> that were found in the magazine entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Now Bali</span><span class="font2"> edition September 2012. They have their own meanings and can be attached to noun or verb bases in order to produce adjectives. There are 2 functions of adjectives found in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Now Bali</span><span class="font2"> magazine, those are: attributive and predicative (subject complement and object complement). Adjectives are attributive when they premodify nouns; appear between the determiner and the head of noun phrase. Predicative can be divided into two parts, there are: subject complement and object complement.</span></p>
