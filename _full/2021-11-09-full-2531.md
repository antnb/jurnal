<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Indonesia has abundant fisheries potency, one of fisheries potency in Indian Ocean is tuna fish. Primary productivity data used as indicator of tuna fisheries potency in Idian Ocean.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Research location is in Indian Ocean 07<sup>0</sup> - 21<sup>0</sup> S and 107<sup>0</sup> - 121<sup>0</sup> E. Weekly satellite data in 2007 used are chlorophyl, sea surface temperature, Photosynthetic Available Radiation (PAR) and euphotic zone (Zeu) data. Daily fisheries data is from tuna fish catching data 2007 in PT. Perikanan Samudera Besar (PT. PSB). Satellite data is processed by Vertically Generalized Production Model (VGPM) formula to obtained primary productivity. Tuna fish catching data correlated to satellite data to know correlation of primary productivity value to fish catching data.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Result of this research is there four species of tuna fish catch in Indian Ocean, that are </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Madidihang</span><span class="font7"> or Yellowfin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus albacores</span><span class="font7">), Bigeye tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus obesus</span><span class="font7">), Southtern Bluefin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus macoyii</span><span class="font7">), and Albacore (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus alalunga</span><span class="font7">). Where 73% tuna fish is Bigeye tuna. Bigeye, Albacore and Yellowfin tuna are produced annually, while Southern Bluefin tuna in northwest monsoon is no fish production. Chlorophyll-a, sea surface temperature and primary productivity value in research location are from 0,06 mg/m<sup>3</sup> to 0,38 mg/m<sup>3</sup>, from 24,64<sup>0</sup>C to 31,82<sup>0</sup>C, and from 73,22mgC/m<sup>2</sup> to 658,57 mgC/m<sup>2</sup> respectively. Coefficient correlation in primary productivity and fish catching in fishing ground area is small (r = 0.008), its explained that, fish catching number is not influenced by primary productivity. In high or low primary productivity condition tuna fish catched in Indian Ocean area. Potential fishing ground area of Bigeye tuna, Albacore, Yellowfin and Southern Bluefin tuna in Indian Ocean are in 11<sup>0</sup> – 16<sup>0</sup> S and 106<sup>0</sup> – 121<sup>0</sup> E, the primary productivity value is from 73 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day to 732 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day and differences of sea surface temperature value of tuna fish are from 24<sup>0</sup> C to 31<sup>0</sup> C, 24<sup>0</sup> C to 30<sup>0</sup> C, and 25<sup>0</sup> C to 31<sup>0</sup> C for Bigeye tuna, Albacore and Yellowfin tuna, respectively. While Southern Bluefin tuna distribution is in temperature area from 26<sup>0</sup>C to 30<sup>0</sup> C and primary productivity value is from 92 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day to 449 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords : Primary productivity, tuna, Vertically Generalized Production Model (VGPM), hook rate, satellite data.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Potensi perikanan di Indonesia masih sangat berlimpah, salah satunya adalah potensi perikanan tuna yang terdapat di Samudera Hindia dan belum termanfaatkan secara optimal. Data produktivitas primer perairan dapat digunakan sebagai salah satu indikator untuk memperkirakan sumberdaya perikanan tuna.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Lokasi data penelitian berada di Samudera Hindia dengan koordinat 07<sup>0</sup> - 21<sup>0</sup> LS dan 107<sup>0</sup> - 121<sup>0</sup> BT. Data satelit yang digunakan adalah data satelit mingguan tahun 2007 meliputi data khloropil, suhu permukaan laut, </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Photosyntetic Available Radiation</span><span class="font7"> (PAR), dan data </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">euphotic zone</span><span class="font7"> (Zeu). Data perikanan diambil dari data harian hasil tangkapan perikanan tuna PT. Perikanan Samudera Besar Bali pada tahun 2007. Seluruh data satelit diolah menggunakan rumus </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Vertically Generalized Production Model</span><span class="font7"> (VGPM) untuk mendapatkan nilai produktivitas primer perairan. Data hasil tangkapan ikan tuna dikorelasikan dengan data satelit untuk mengetahui hubungan antara nilai produktivitas primer dengan ikan hasil tangkapan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Berdasarkan hasil penelitian, di Samudera Hindia tertangkap empat jenis ikan tuna yaitu Madidihang/Yellowfin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus albacores</span><span class="font7">), tuna mata besar/Bigeye tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus obesus</span><span class="font7">), Southtern Bluefin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus macoyii</span><span class="font7">), dan Albacore (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus alalunga</span><span class="font7">), dimana sekitar 73% total hasil tangkapan adalah jenis tuna mata besar. Tuna mata besar, albacore, dan yellowfin tertangkap hampir sepanjang tahun, sedangkan Southern Bluefin tuna tidak tertangkap pada saat musim timur (northwest monsoon). Nilai khloropil pada daerah penelitian yaitu 0,06 mg/m<sup>3</sup> - 0,38 mg/m<sup>3</sup>, suhu permukaan laut 24,64<sup>0</sup>C - 31,82<sup>0</sup>C, dan produktivitas primer 73,22mgC/m<sup>2</sup> - 658,57 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>. Koefisien relasi antara produktivitas primer dan ikan hasil tangkapan di daerah penelitian sangat kecil (r = 0.008), hal ini berarti bahwa jumlah ikan hasil tangkapan tidak dipengaruhi oleh nilai produktivitas primer perairan. Daerah penangkapan yang potensial untuk </span><span class="font7" style="text-decoration:underline;">ikan tuna mata besar, Albakora, Madidihang, dan Southern Bluefin tuna di Samudera Hindia pada sekitar koordinat 11<sup>0</sup> –</span><span class="font7"> </span><span class="font8">86</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">16<sup>0</sup>LS dan 106<sup>0</sup> – 121<sup>0</sup>BT dengan rentang nilai produktivitas primer yang sama yaitu 73 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day sampai 732 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day, hanya berbeda pada rentang suhu permukaan laut yaitu masing-masing pada suhu 24<sup>0</sup>C-31<sup>0</sup>C (tuna mata besar), 24<sup>0</sup>C-30<sup>0</sup>C (Albacore), dan 25<sup>0</sup>C-31<sup>0</sup>C (Madidihang). Sedangkan untuk Southern Bluefin tuna menyebar pada perairan dengan kisaran suhu 26<sup>0</sup>C-30<sup>0</sup>C dengan nilai produktivitas primer antara 92 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day sampai 449 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci : Produktivitas primer, tuna, Vertically Generalized Production Model</span><span class="font7"> (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">VGPM), hook rate, data satelit.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">INTRODUCTION</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Background</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Tuna fisheries potency is not apparent specifically. Therefore, this media differences can be anticipated by study the tuna fish behavior and application of detected device technology. In this time, this technology can identified tuna fisheries potency location by coastal zone nutrient parameter identification or its coastal zone primary productivity and resulted fishing ground tuna map location.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Pauly and Christensen (1995) describe that there is a high correlation between primary production and fish abundance and also attempt to obtain a more accurate estimate of the primary production required to sustain the world fisheries catches.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">All life weather on land or in the sea is based on chlorophyll, the only capable of synthesizing living matter. At sea, this first link in the chain of life is constituted by phytoplankton; microscopic organisms such as diatoms, flageliforms, floating free or grouped carried by the currents. This primary production from the root of the high productivity of various links in the tropic chain which the basis of fishing activities.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Primary production is the production of organic compounds from atmospheric or aquatic carbon dioxide, principally through the process of photosynthesis, with chemosynthesis being much less important. All life on earth is directly or indirectly reliant on primary production. The organisms responsible for primary production are known as primary producers or autotrophies, and form the base of the </span><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_chain"><span class="font7">food chain</span></a><span class="font7"> (Available at </span><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/"><span class="font7">http://en.wikipedia.org/</span></a><span class="font7"> wiki/Primary_production., 2007).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">This research is concerned about how to detect the primary productivity using an established mathematical model which the input parameters are derived from satellite data. This research is also concerned about estimating fish productivity using a model and it compared to the fish catch data with variability of environment parameter such as sea surface temperature and chlorophyll-a concentration.</span></p>

<p><span class="font7">Data source in this research is: satellite data that used in this research are MODIS (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer</span><span class="font7">) and SeaWifs satellite data every weeks in period 2007. <p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">Data Analysis</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Chlorophyll-a, sea surface temperature, photosynthetic available radiation, and primary productivity data supported by MODIS and SeaWifs that processed using digital image processing with ENVI software, ERMapper and Arc View GIS 3.2. Digital image processing divided into three steps there are: (1) preprocessing; (2) processing and (3) post processing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Primary productivity calculation based on the basic concept that harmonization of photosynthetic and carbon will be happened in euphotic layer. In this research, model that used is Vertically Generalized Production Model</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">(VGPM) developed by Benhrenfeld &amp;&nbsp;Falkowski (1997). The equation that used is:</span></p>
<p><span class="font6" style="font-weight:bold;">Ppeu = 0,66125 * Pbopt * (Eo/(Eo + 4.1))Csur * Deu * Dirr</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Where :</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Ppeu : bruto of daily primary productivity in eufotic layer (mg C/m<sup>2</sup> hari)</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Pbopt : index of flux photosynthesis optimum in eufotic layer (mg C/mg Chl * h)</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Eo : photo–synthetically available radiation – PAR (unit : W/m<sup>2</sup>)</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Csur : chlorophyll concentration in surface water (W/m<sup>3</sup>) Deu : depthness of euphotic layer (m)</span></p>
<p><span class="font6">Dirr : Daily photoperiod (in decimal hours) calculated for the middle of the month for each pixel</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULT</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">Primary productivity</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Primary productivity in the territorial waters are determined by some factors. In this studies, main factors that are influenced primary productivity and Vertically Generalized Production Model (VGPM) are sea surface temperature (SST), chlorophyll-a (Chl-a), euphotic zone (Zeu), photosynthetic available radiations (PAR) and solar radiation times a days (Dirr).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">From weekly primary productivity values during January to December are continuously similar, by high value range. The high value majority located in seashore area (between 400 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day and over 1000 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day). While in fishing ground area with significant fish catching, primary productivity is from 100 to 500 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Lowest primary productivity value in research location with fish production is 73.22 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day in March 22<sup>nd</sup> to 29<sup>th</sup> 2007 and the highest is 732.98 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day in June 2<sup>nd</sup> to 9<sup>th</sup> 2007. Lowest chlorophyll-a is 0.06 mg/m<sup>3</sup> on January 17<sup>th</sup> to 24<sup>th</sup> 2007 and the highest is 0.38 mg/m<sup>3</sup> on August 21<sup>st</sup> to 28<sup>th</sup> 2007. And lowest sea surface temperature is 24.64<sup>0</sup>C in August 13rd to 20<sup>th</sup> 2007 and the highest is 31.82<sup>0</sup>C from February 26<sup>th</sup> to March 5<sup>th </sup>2007.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">Tuna Fish Catch Production</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Four species tuna fish by long line method in Indian Ocean fishing ground are Yellowfin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus albacores</span><span class="font7">), Bigeye tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus obesus</span><span class="font7">), Southtern Bluefin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus macoyii</span><span class="font7">), and Albacore (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus alalunga</span><span class="font7">).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">From weekly fish catch data, hook rate means is from 0.002% to 0.007%, and the highest is from 0.003%. There is no fish catch in March 6<sup>th</sup> to 21<sup>st</sup> and September 14<sup>th</sup> to 21<sup>st</sup> 2007. Highest Bluefin tuna and Bigeye tuna fish production is in December 11st to 18<sup>th</sup> 2007, 17 and 363 heads fish, respectively. Highest Yellowfin tuna fish production is in May 17<sup>th</sup> to 24<sup>th</sup> 2007 (98 heads fish), and</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Albacore fish production is relatively high in February 10<sup>th </sup>to 17<sup>th</sup> 2007 (59 heads fish).</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Variability of Primary Productivity</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Figure 2 is explain weekly Indian Ocean primary productivity in 20007. Constant minimum value of primary productivity is not shows significant number and tends to increased in 15<sup>th</sup> weeks (April) and reached its maximum value in 31<sup>st</sup> weeks (August)</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/2531-2.jpg" alt="" style="width:261pt;height:79pt;">
<p><span class="font6">Figure 2 Fluctuation of Primary Productivity in 2007</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Meanwhile, high maximum primary productivity value is present significant value weekly. Maximum high primary productivity value is in weeks of 20<sup>th</sup> (June) or in southeast monsoon, sst and chl-a value are 28<sup>0</sup> - 29<sup>0</sup>C, and chl-a 0.1 – 0.2 mg/m<sup>3</sup> respectively, and presumed there is an upwelling process.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Primary productivity value higher in June to September (southeast monsoon) than in December to March (northwest monsoon). Some factors that caused high primary productivity value in southeast monsoon are air temperature in southeast monsoon is colder than</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">northwest monsoon, Chlorophyll-a value is high (&gt; 0.1) and produced upwelling process. Hendiarti </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., (1995) conclude that, there is upweilling in southeast monsoon on south of Java-Bali Islands, chlorophyll mean concentration is 0.39 mgC/m<sup>3</sup> and during northwest monsoon is 0.18 mgC/m<sup>3</sup>.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Susanto </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., (2001) explained an upwelling process in coastal west area of Sumatra and south of Java is response of wind during southeast monsoon. Different condition occurs during northwest monsoon. In transitional period (April to May and October to November) water mass stream in equator area influenced downwelling on coastal area of west Sumatra and south of Java (Clarke and Liu, 1993; Sprintall </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2000). According to Wyrtki (1961), upwelling in south of Java is temporally and placed in boundary zone of coastal Java current and south of equator current.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">There is not available primary productivity on 10<sup>th</sup> to 17<sup>th</sup> March, because satellite data is cloudy and can not be processed. While, in 6<sup>th</sup> – 12<sup>nd</sup> March, 14<sup>th</sup> -2<sup>st</sup> September and 27<sup>th</sup> -3<sup>st</sup> December there is no fish catch.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Variability of Fishery Data</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Bigeye tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus obesus</span><span class="font7">) is the biggest catch than others tuna fish in research location in Indian Ocean, that are Yellowfin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus albacores</span><span class="font7">), Southtern Bluefin tuna (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus macoyii</span><span class="font7">), and Albacore (</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Thunnus alalunga</span><span class="font7">).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Bigeye tuna dominated 73% of fish catch, the highest of fish catch is in 11<sup>th</sup> to 18<sup>th</sup> of December 2007. Chlorophyll-a, SST and primary productivity value are from 0.07 to 0.11 mg/m<sup>3</sup>, from 25.63 to 30.33<sup>0</sup>C, and from 127.26 to 482.20 mgC/m<sup>2</sup>day, respectively. Uda (1952) in Hela and Laevastu (1970) show species of Bigeye tuna is high tolerance to high temperature (11<sup>0</sup> C – 28<sup>0</sup> C), and temperature of fish catch is about 18<sup>0</sup>C - 23<sup>0</sup> C.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Small fish catching in September, although primary productivity and chlorophyll-a are high. This would be a small number of vessels of PT. Perikanan Samudra Besar (PSB) are fishing. High fish catch will achieved if</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">fisherman are fished, according to high number of fish catch by operation of other vessels that are fishing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Meanwhile, Bigeye tuna is epipelagic, mesopelagic and oceanic and live in 250 m depth water. Temperature and termoclyn layer depth is environment main factor influence the distribution of the fish, vertically and horizontally. Water temperature of Bigeye tuna is in 13<sup>0</sup> C to 29<sup>0</sup> C and optimum temperature is 17<sup>0</sup> C to 22<sup>0</sup> C (Collette and Nauen, 1983).</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">A number of fish catch of PT. PSB show the tends to have been decrease annually. Since 1973, the fishing fish in Indian Ocean area are decrease (Batubara, 2003). A mean number of fish catch in each of a hundreds of hook rate in 1997 to 1999 are decrease, respectively are 0.84, 0.78, 0.57 (Gaol </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et. al</span><span class="font7">., 2002). In 2007, weekly hook rate means is 0.003 and 0.415 monthly.</span></p>

In that primary productivity value condition, tuna are constantly catch.</span></p>
