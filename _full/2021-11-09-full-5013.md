<h2><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">This study was aimed at describing Google translations by comparing them with those which were carried out by humans. The human translations used in this study were done by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This study used qualitative-descriptive method. The sample was purposively selected and consisted of 24 sentences from Chris Barker, Cultural Studies and their two translated versions. The evaluation was done by using Sical’s approach.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The results showed that Google translations contained more errors than those made by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center. Google made errors that fell into the category of violations of linguistic and sociocultural rules or norms. This was caused by the design of Google Translate that was not based on linguistic and sociocultural analyses. Meanwhile </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center made errors that indicated reasoning by guessing meanings from context, as what humans are doing in communication.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Google translations needed revising in the forms of translating words which had been left untranslated, retranslating words which had been wrongly translated because of not considering context, retranslating phrases which had been wrongly translated because of not analyzing them into their constituents and propositions, retranslating words which had been wrongly translated because of not considering sociocultural norms. In doing the revisions the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">editor had to read the original text because the translations tended not to give clues to the original patterns of the sentences for their highly varied forms. Meanwhile, the translations of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center needed minor editing to make them more effective. There was no need for an intensive reading of the original for their more systematic characteristics.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Keywords: Google, human, translation</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This study compared </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google</span><span class="font4"> translations and human translations. Today, translating activities are no longer solely carried out by humans and it seems that machine translation has increasingly become more popular with the consequence of making translating a much easier activity. However, this can threaten professional translators. They are facing a stronger competitor since translational technology has come to a stage in which translational profession has become obsolete. The public will switch to translation machines such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> which does translating process far much faster than a human translator. Why bother yourself waiting for a professional translator who charges you at a higher rate and who does the job at a slower rate than </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">However, this opinion is not completely correct. Another thing that highly determines the choice is quality. If the quality of Google translations is the same as that of professional translator’s translations, people will choose </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google.</span><span class="font4"> Some translators state that Google translations can be used for finding information or browsing while for getting a more accurate result one needs the help of a professional translator. Vitek and Cavelier have come to this conclusion when they say that machine translations are a very good tool that a researcher can use when searching relevant patent documents. However, a professional translator is needed to get an official translation of a document </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">(the-future-of-patent translations-human-or-machine</span><span class="font4">).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> does not use context, grammatical and literal models. Instead, it uses a statistics-based approach. Statistical Analysis is applied to a bilingual parallel text with at least one million words in each of the respective languages and 2 sets of monolingual texts with at least one billion words. Statistical models and allogarithms that are derived from the data are used to compile translations of new texts between the two languages without understanding each language or its unique rules (Och, 2006). The probability of an equivalent is calculated by considering thousands of words in the parallel text and the statistical probability is used to determine the choice of lexical equivalents (Baker, 1997:148). Semantic learning technology is used to enhance machine translation by detecting patterns in the documents that have been translated by translators </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">(the-future-of-patent translations-human-or-machine).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This study compared the quality of Google Translate’s translations and that of those done by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center. The questions posed in this study are (1) What do the errors in Google Translate’s translations look like?</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">;</span><span class="font4"> (2) Can we, after considering the errors, conclude that Google Translate’s translations are unpublishable? (3) If they are unpublishable, what an editor has to do to improve the translations. 4) How Google Translate’s translations differ from those performed by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center who translated Chris Barker’s Cultural Studies professionally in terms of the errors found in both translations?; and (5) why do they differ?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">This study used qualitative descriptive method. </span><span class="font4">The data were drawn from Chris Barker’s Cultural Studies textbook which was published by Saga Publications in London in 2000 and consisted of 24 sentences. The sentences were translated by using Google Translate on October 14, 2012. As a comparison, the translations of the same sentences were taken from a translated version of Chris Barker’s textbook by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center, Yogyakarta which was published in 2005. To evaluate the translations, attention was focused on the errors in the translated sentences which were then grouped into major (grave or serious) and minor (slight) errors by referring to the Third–generation Sical ( Language Quality Measuring System in Canada) (Williams, 2001: 331).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Sical gives the definition of a serious error as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Translation: complete failure to render the meaning of a word or passage that contains an essential element of the message: also mistranslation resulting in a contradiction of or significant departure from the meaning of an essential element of the message.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Language: incomprehensible, grossly incorrect language or rudimentary error in an essential element of the message.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">An essential element is related to the conscequence of an error for a client. If the translation is erroneous, this will have a serious effect on the client. Therefore, the translation cannot be used without a revision. As a conscequence, a decision will be made on the acceptability of the translations being compared. If a revision is needed, then it should be specified what types of editing job will be needed to improve the quality of the translations. In brief, the procedures that were followed can be shown in the following figure.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Study Results and Discussion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Errors found in the Translations performed by using </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> (on October14, 2012) and errors made by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center can be described as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Translations Performed by Using Google Translate Viewed from the types of errors found in them</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Error in identifying a dependency relationship (Nababan, 1991:1991-142)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Examples of this type of errors are</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English Expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Centralized economic forms and determinations</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Google:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bentuk ekonomi terpusat dan penentuan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Its correct translation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bentuk ekonomi dan penentuan ekonomi terpusat</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The noun phrase above can be analyzed in terms of its modification structure as follows: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Centralized / economic forms and determinations </span><span class="font4">M / H</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Economic / forms and determinations</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">M / H</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Note:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">M = Modifier, H = Head</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The analysis above shows that </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">economic forms and determinations</span><span class="font4"> is modified by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">centralized </span><span class="font4">and in its turn, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">forms and determinations</span><span class="font4"> is modified by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">economic.</span><span class="font4"> In Indonesian the nominal phrase can be translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bentuk-bentuk dan determinasi-determinasi ekonomi terpusat</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This error is a serious one since it can cause misunderstanding. This error must have been caused by inability to identify immediate constituents.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Another example of the same type of error is</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Pregiven fixed aim or object</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Google:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan tetap pra-diberikan atau objek</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">A correct translation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan atau sasaran tetap yang diasumsikan sudah ada sebelumnya</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Error due to Missidentification of Figurative Meanings</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Sweeping political and economic changes</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Google:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Perubahan politik dan ekonomi yang menyapu</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">A correct translation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Perubahan politik dan ekonomi yang berlangsung dengan cepat dan memberi dampak yang luas.(</span><span class="font4"> two among the meanings of the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping</span><span class="font4"> according to Webster’s Ninth Collogiate Dictionary are </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">to cover the entire range of</span><span class="font4"> dan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">to move across or along swiftly) </span><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping</span><span class="font4"> in the expression above is used with its figurative meaning. Hence, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping</span><span class="font4"> should not be translated literally into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">menyapu.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Error due to missidentication of word meanings in context</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Argue against</span><span class="font4"> in the context</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Foucault argues against the repressive hypothesis</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Google:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Foucault berpendapat terhadap</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">A correct translation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Foucault memberikan argumen yang menentang hipotesis represif </span><span class="font4">Google mistranslated the preposision </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">against</span><span class="font4"> in the above context. Other examples of the error that belongs to this type are</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(1)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">For Foucault, power is….</span><span class="font4"> translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Untuk Foucault….</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The correct translations are </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bagi Foucault</span><span class="font4"> and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Menurut Foucault</span><span class="font4">….</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(2)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ordering</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">making them grow and ordering them,</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">membuat mereka tumbuh dan memerintahkan mereka.</span><span class="font4">The correct translation is membuat </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">… tumbuh dan mengaturnya.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(3)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Submit</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">…making them submit,</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">..membuat mereka serahkan.</span><span class="font4">The correct translation is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">membuat mereka menyerah.</span><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">serahkan </span><span class="font4">is used in an imperative sentence, such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Serahkan uang tebusan itu dengan segera!</span><span class="font4"> while --- </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">membuat mereka---</span><span class="font4"> is a declarative sentence that uses a verb with the prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">meN</span><span class="font4">- instead of a verb with suffix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">–kan</span><span class="font4">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(4)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Medicine</span><span class="font4"> in …</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">discourses about medicine,</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">obat-obatan.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The correct translation is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kedokteran.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(5)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perversity</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polymorphous perversity,</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan polymorphous.</span><span class="font4">The correct translation is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">penyimpangan berbagai bentuk.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(6)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bite</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sound-bite</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">suara gigitan.</span><span class="font4"> The correct translation is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">efek suara yang tajam.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaving the English words untranslated as the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polymorphous</span><span class="font4"> above.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Other examples are</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(1)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Determinate,</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(2)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reader,</span><span class="font4"> and</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(3)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lacanian.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The use of two words that refer to the same concept without putting them side by side and inserting one of them in a bracket to show that the two words are interchangeable.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">An example is the use of the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kekuasaan</span><span class="font4"> and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">power</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">From the description above the following can be summed up: Google translations contained many weaknesses and the serious ones among them were (1) leaving English words untranslated (lexical interference), (2) selecting the meanings of words that did not fit into the context, and (3) errors in identifying a dependency relationship.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Errors that belong to the first category caused the translations to leave essential meanings that needed to be made explixit to the reader to help him or her understand the source language text, errors of the second category caused inaccuracy since the translations did not communicate the correct meanings of the polysemous words according to the context, and errors of the third category caused misunderstandings of the propositional contents of the source language text that resulted in errors in understanding the text. In addition, there were errors in selecting the lexical meanings such as in the translation of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">perversity</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polymorphous perversity</span><span class="font4">, which is unacceptable to the reader as a member of a community who is fully aware of the norms and law. The reader will not accept such a type of translation since he or she is aware that a baby cannot be blamed for what it does. A criminal is usually a person who is regarded as having been able to differentiate good deeds from bad ones that he or she deserves to get a reward or penalty for his or her action.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>3.2 Translations Performed by </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> Cultural Studies Center Viewed from Errors Found in them</span></h2></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Errors that result in the use of reasoning to determine meanings in context</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Sweeping political and economic changes</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center (henceforth called </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim</span><span class="font4">):</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Perubahan politik dan ekonomi penting.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping</span><span class="font4"> in the nominal phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping political and economic changes</span><span class="font4"> was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">penting.</span><span class="font4"> This translation is wrong since the meaning of the word in the above context is ‘extensive’, which is ‘ekstensif’ or </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">‘</span><span class="font4">meluas’ in Indonesian. The translator might have inferred the meaning in the context. Another example of the errors of this category is as follows.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">that subjectivity is a discursive construction</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bahwa subjektivitas adalah konstruksi sosial</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">discursive</span><span class="font4"> has been wrongly understood to have the same meaning as the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">social</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Partial translation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">The pioneering work of Saussure</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Karya Saussure</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaving English words untranslated (lexical interference)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">In Lacanian term</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim</span><span class="font4"> :</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Dalam istilah Lacanian</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lacanian</span><span class="font4"> in the English expression above has been maintained in the translation above. The word should be translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lacan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Another example of error of this type is:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Sexed subjects</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Subjek –subjek terkelaminkan</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The correct translation is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">topik-topik yang digolongkan menurut jenis kelamin.</span><span class="font4"> Here the translator overgeneralized the equivalents of loan words without looking at the context in which they occur. The English word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">subject</span><span class="font4"> has many meanings and for most of them the translator has to find other words that fit in the context in Indonesian. The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Subject</span><span class="font4"> can mean course (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mata pelajaran</span><span class="font4">), citizen (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">warga negara</span><span class="font4">), subject of a sentence (subjek), subject of study (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">subjek penelitian</span><span class="font4">), etc.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wrongly applied Literal translation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">English expression:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">British Asian teenage viewers of soap opera in the UK Tim:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Remaja Asia Inggris penonton soap opera sabun di Inggris</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The translator could have explicitly specified the relationship between the noun </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Asia </span><span class="font4">and English (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Inggris</span><span class="font4">) above. Thus, the translation will read: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Remaja Inggris yang berasal dari Asia</span><span class="font4"> in which it is clear that the English teenagers come from Asia. In addition, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">penonton</span><span class="font4"> can be replaced by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">yang menonton</span><span class="font4"> to make it sound natural to the Indonesian reader</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font4"> The cause of the error was a wrong application of literal translation and the deviation from the convention or common collocational patterns in the target language. Other examples of this type of error are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(1)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Carpet bombing</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Pengeboman karpet,</span><span class="font4"> which should be </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pengeboman intensif</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(2)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The message of the Author-God</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pesan –Pengarang – Tuhan,</span><span class="font4"> which should be </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">wahyu</span><span class="font4"> atau </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">firman Tuhan</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">(5)</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Human animal</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Tim:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Hewan manusia,</span><span class="font4"> which should be </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">manusia dari segi sifat hewaniahnya</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>3.3 Differences between Errors Found in Translations Performed by Using </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> and Those Made by Tim Kunci </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Cultural Studies Center</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The errors found in the translations performed by using </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> were serious while those made by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center were not</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font4"> Many words were left untranslated such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polymorphous, determinate, reader</span><span class="font4"> and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lacanian.</span><span class="font4"> In the translations carried out by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Center</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font4"> there was only one word left untranslated, namely, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Lacanian.</span><span class="font4"> The translations peformed by using </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> tended to contain word meanings that did not fit in the context, such as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">argue against,</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">berpendapat terhadap</span><span class="font4">, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">untuk</span><span class="font4"> in the context </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">for Foucault, power is… ordering</span><span class="font4"> that is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">memerintahkan</span><span class="font4"> in the context </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">making them grow and ordering them, medicine </span><span class="font4">which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">obat-obatan</span><span class="font4"> in the context </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">discourses about medicine, perversity</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan</span><span class="font4"> in the context </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">polymorphous perversity </span><span class="font4">and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bite</span><span class="font4"> which was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">gigitan</span><span class="font4"> in the context s</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ound bite</span><span class="font4">. Furthermore, Google Translate produced translations containing errors indicating the wrong analyses of phrases into their immediate constituents that caused errors in understanding the propositional contents. As an example, Google translated </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">centralized economic forms and determinations</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">bentuk ekonomi terpusat dan penentuan. Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center did not make such types of errors. On the other hand, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center made errors indicating that the translator guessed the meanings of words in context through reasoning. Two examples of this type of error are the translation of the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping</span><span class="font0"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sweeping economic and political changes</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">penting</span><span class="font4"> and the</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">translation of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">discourse</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">discourse construction</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sosial</span><span class="font4">. In addition to the types of errors that have been described so far, Google Translate also made errors that are related to the violation of a grammatical rule in Indonesian. An example of this type of error is the translation of the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">submit</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">serahkan</span><span class="font4"> in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">making them submit.</span><span class="font4"> Such a type of error was not found in the translations made by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font4"> Nor was the error made by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google</span><span class="font4"> in translating the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">perversity</span><span class="font4"> into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan. Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center translated it into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kenakalan.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion and Suggestions</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Viewed from the types of errors, the translations performed by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate </span><span class="font4">contained far more errors than those by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> made errors related to the violation of grammatical rules and norms in the community since </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> was not designed based on a linguistic analysis and analysis of sociocultural aspects of the language. Meanwhile, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center made errors indicating the use of reasoning by guessing word meanings in context, as what is commonly done by a human being in communication. This is done by the human when he or she does not know the meaning of a word. There are two things that he or she may do in such a situation. First, he or she may analyze the word into its morphemes and secondly, he or she may relate the word to other words found together with the new word in the context.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The translations produced by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> need revising, by translating English words that were left untranslated, retranslating the words wrongly translated since they were translated without considering the context, translating phrases by analyzing them first into their constituents to get the propositional contents before finding their equivalents to maintain their propositions in the target language and retranslating the words wrongly translated since they were translated without considering the sociocultural norms in the community. All of these jobs need to be done by reading the original text intensively due to the varied and unsystematic patterns of the sentences. Meanwhile, the translations done by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center only need to be edited in one place or another to get a more effective result. Unlike the translations performed by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4">, the translations done by </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center can be edited without reading the original text intensively due to their more systematic characteristics.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Google Translate</span><span class="font4"> made errors related to grammatical rules since it was not based on a linguistic analysis but on statistical analysis applied to a parallel bilingual text and two sets of monolingual texts. The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">submit</span><span class="font4"> was translated into the Indonesian word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">serahkan</span><span class="font4"> by referring to a parallel English-Indonesian text, rather than by looking at the linguistic context in which the word occurs. Google Translate is not a human being that can differentiate the context in which the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan</span><span class="font4"> (crime) was used from the context in which the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">penyimpangan</span><span class="font4"> (deviation) was used. Thus </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">perversity</span><span class="font4"> was translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kejahatan</span><span class="font4"> in the context of a baby prior to the resolution of the Oedipus Complex. On the other hand, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci Cultural Studies Center</span><span class="font4"> made errors indicating the use of reasoning by guessing word meanings from context, a practice that is</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">commonly done by a human being in communication. The guess can be right and it can be wrong. Thus it shows that the translation has been done by a human being rather than a machine.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suggestions</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">From the comparison between the translations performed by using Google Translate and the ones performed by professional translators (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tim Kunci</span><span class="font4"> Cultural Studies Center), Google Translate can be used for searching information and in using the results of such browsing process the user of Google Translate should be careful of its contents. He or she should be aware that they have been produced by a machine rather by a human being. Thus the results tend to contain errors which a human editor can correct. After finding the information, the user needs a professional translator to do the translation of the document(s) that he or she has selected from the translations performed by Google Translate. In this manner, the time and cost can be used more economically. A professional translator can use the service given by Google Translate in finding the meaning of a word faster than by looking it up in a manual dictionary.</span></p>
