<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini berjudul “Transitivity of English Verb in Brave A Motther’s Love Movie Storybook”. Tujuan penelitian dalam jurnal ini berfokus pada transitivitas kata kerja yang ditemukan dalam buku cerita. Data yang terkumpul dianalisis dengan menggunakan teori transitivitas oleh Hopper and Thompson (1980), sebagai teori utama dalam penelitian ini. Dikarenakan penelitian sangat erat berhubungan dengan jenis kata kerja, analisis transitivitas dimulai dengan memilih kata kerja yg ditemukan dalam cerita. Teori yang digunakan menganalisa jenis kata kerja disini menggunakan teori oleh Talmy Givon (1984). Teori ini didasarkan pada skala waktu-stabilitas.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Berdasarkan hasil analisis, telah dikumpulkan dua puluh dua(22) kata kerja, empat diantaranya (4) kata kerja yang dikategorikan sebagai kata kerja keterangan. Enam (6)Kata kerja kategori proses. Serta dua belas (12) kata kerja aksi. Tingkat parameter transitivitas ternedah hanya ditemukan satu(1), diwakili kata kerja menunggu, yg mana termasuk kata kerja keterangan, dan tiga(3) kata kerja aksi yang memiliki nilai tertinggi dalam tingkatan parameter transitivitas adalah kata kerja, makan, mengejar, dan melindungi.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata kunci: Transitivitas, Jenis kata kerja, Tingkatan parameter.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word text means a pre-theoretical term used in linguistics and phonetics to refer to a stretch of language recorded for the purpose of analysis and description. Text may refer to collection of written or spoken material. e.g conversation, monologues, and so on (Crystal, 1985:307).</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Considering the explanation above, it is interesting to analyze the parameters of transitivity of the verbs, in regard to cognitive development; the various mental processes used in thinking, remembering, perceiving, recognizing, classifying, and so on. Especially, the verbs which occurred in the movie storybook. The movie storybook is chosen because it uses simple sentence to tell the story according to what happens by following the movie story itself and to understand the story more specifically.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are some problems that need to be discussed in the transitivity of English verb in the movie storybook </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span><span class="font1"> and they are formulated as follow:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of transitive verbs are found in the movie storybook </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What verbs are the lowest and the highest in parameters of transitivity contained in the movie storybook </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The aims of this are as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To classify the types of transitive verbs found in the story entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To classify and analyze the lowest and highest parameters of transitivity of the verbs in the story.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The research method covers three points; how to obtain, how to collect and how to analyze the data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data used in this study were directly taken from the movie storybook entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span><span class="font1">. This book was published by PT. Gramedia Pustaka Utama (2012) by license of the Disney Enterprise, Inc. This movie storybook also contains various types of verbs that were relevant for this study. </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">4.2 Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The method in collecting the data in this study is documentary method, using the following techniques:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is read and jot down the sentence or clause which consist of verbs which are appropriated and relevant with the scope of discussion of this study</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is note take using whenever the sentence are containing the transitivity of verb.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Is identify and classify the clause structure of text. In this study, the clause or sentence which contains passive verbs is ignored because according to Givon (1984), those kinds of verb tend to have low transitivity.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>4.3 Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The method used in this research is qualitative and quantitative method. Some steps in analyzing the data of this study are presented as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The data from the movie storybook were analyzed based on Hopper and Thomson’s theory of parameters. The explanation was presented in order to know whether a verb within a sentence or clause consists of one or two participants, action or non-action, telic or atelic, etc.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The index parameter of each verb within a sentence was counted.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then, the lowest and the highest index of transitivity among those types of verb found in the movie storybook </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love</span><span class="font1"> were identified.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Verbs Transitivity in Brave A Mother’s Love Movie Storybook</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This section shows the selected verbs form </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Brave A Mother’s Love </span><span class="font1">Movie Storybook by Melissa Legonegro (2012). A complex sentence in the story is split in one meaning clause. The sentences that are classified into three types based on the theory of verb proposed by Givon (1984), these are: state, process and action. And the analysis described:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;State Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to the data obtained, from twenty two verbs four state verbs are found, those are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">waits</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wears</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">joins</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">knows</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Process Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are six process verbs found in the story. Those verbs are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">teach</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">make</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">return</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">change</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">look for</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fix.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Action Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are twelve action verbs found in the story. Those verbs are, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">show</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tell</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cut</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">run</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">meet</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ask</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">eat</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">chase</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">protects</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fight</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">covers</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">break.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitivity</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This is the core of this study. In this part, the parameter of transitivity of verb in each selected sentence is classified and analyzed. In analyzing the transitivity of verb, each verb is classified based on its type of verb; state, action and process.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitivity of State Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to the story, four state verbs are obtained, they are: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wait</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wear</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">join</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">know,</span><span class="font1"> each of these state verbs is analyzed on parameters of transitivity.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitivity of Process Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">As stated previously that there are six process verbs occurring in the story. Those verbs are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">teach</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">makes</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">return</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">change</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">look for</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fix.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitivity of Action Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the story, it is obtained twelve of action verbs, those verb are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">shows</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tells</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cuts</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">runs</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">meets</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ask</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">eats</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">chase</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">protects</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fight</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">covers</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">break</span><span class="font1">. In this part, each of these action verb is analyzed. The analysis is on the parameter of transitivity.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis and discussion of the previous chapter, it can be concluded that there are three types of verbs found in the story. Those are state verb, process verb, and action verb. Four verbs were considered as state verb. they were </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wait</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wear</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">join</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">know.</span><span class="font1"> Six process verbs were also found: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">teach</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">make</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">return</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">change</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">look for</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fix.</span><span class="font1"> And the most of verbs which occurred in the story were classified is action verbs, they were, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">show</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tell</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cut</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">run</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">meet</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ask</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">eat</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">chase</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">protect</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">fight</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cover</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">break.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The degree of transitivity of each verb definitely depends on the type of verb itself. From the previous chapter, it can be proved that the state verb has the lowest transitivity than the other types of verb since its index parameters were less than 5 (five), while process and action verbs were proved to be high in transitivity because their index parameters were more than 5 (five). From the analysis, it is shown that only one verb has the lowest degree of transitivity with the index parameters 2 (two). This verb was classified as state verb. the verbs that have the lowest transitivity are verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wait.</span><span class="font1"> The verbs that have the highest</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">degree of transitivity with the index parameters 10 (ten) are 3 (three) verbs are; </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">eat, chase, and protect</span><span class="font1">, and those verbs are action verb.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on this result, it can be concluded that the action verb is considered high in transitivity. In contrast, the index parameters of state verb in this story was less than five, thus, it is considered that state verb is low in transitivity.</span></p>
