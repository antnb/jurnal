<h1><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">The process of transliteration(Latin-Balinese script) by using a computerized program of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font2"> is a noble step to preserve the existence of Balinese script from the domination of the written tradition with Latin letters. The utilization of Bali Simbar program in transliteration process has proved to have many advantages. First, the result of transliteration is not just a paper printout but canbe stored in digital soft copy form that does not require a large space, can be duplicated, and persist for a long time.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Second, the process ofLatin-Balinese text transliteration by utilizing BaliSimbar program is much faster than manual transliteration. Third, the cost needed in the process of Latin-Balinese transliteration of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font2"> is very little because it only requires a software program of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font2"> and computer (personal computer/PC, laptop, or notebook). This study was focused on the transliteration of ten satua text from Gedong Kirtya Singaraja Museum collection performed by 4th semester students of Balinese Language Education Department of Undiksha.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Key words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">:Transliteration, Latin letters, Balinese letters, computerized program of Bali</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Simbar</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1.Introduction</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The development of written tradition in Bali has been a very long process. In the beginning, the media inscribed with Balinese script is in the form of stone, bamboo, wood, clay, metal, up tothe introduction of literary tradition on palm leaves and paper (Dinas Kebudayaan, 2005:30).The process of writing the written media is still done manually, which relies on writing skills of Balinese script by hand (with the help of writing tools such aschisels, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">pangrupak</span><span class="font3"> (small knife), pencil and pen). Along with the development of information and communication technology, in 1996 writing medium of Balinese script was made into computer technology with the creation of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3"> software program. Through the creation of software program of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3">, Balinese literary tradition which at first was still done manually, can now be adapted to computer technology. Each character of Balinese letters is no longer be written by hand, but simply by pressing one of the keys of computer keyboard that will automatically display a variety of Balinese characters desired.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Utilization of Bali Simbar program in transliteration process has a lot of advantages. First, the results of transliteration is not just a paper printou tbut can be stored in digital softcopy form that does not require a large space, can be duplicated, and persist for a long time. Second, the process of Latin-Balinese text transliteration by utilizing Bali Simbar program is much faster than manual transliteration. Third, the cost in the process of Latin-Balinese transliteration of the text is very little because it only requires the software of Bali Simbar program and computer (personal computer/PC, laptop, ornotebook). The process of transliteration by utilizing Bali Simbar program is a noble step to preserve the existence of Balinese script from the domination of the written tradition with Latin letters.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Based on thedescription above, the focus of this study is whether the use of a computerized program of Balinese script (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3">) is capable of facilitating the text (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3">) transliteration from Latin letters into Balinese conducted by the 4A semester students of Jurusan Pendidikan Bahasa Bali Universitas Pendidikan Ganesha Singaraja.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2.Concept and Theoretical Framework</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Concept</span><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>2.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transliteration</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Kamus Besar Bahasa Indonesia</span><span class="font3"> (Indonesian dictionary) puts transliteration as a replacement of letters from one system of alphabet to another. Transliteration is defined as the transfer of one writing to another writing (Robson, 1994:24).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>2.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Computerization of Balinese script (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">)</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Computerization of Balinese script is a typing program of Balinese letters with the use of computer software. In 1996I Made Suatjana created </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3"> fonts or better known as </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar version B</span><span class="font3"> to simplify the process of typing of Balinese script by using computer software.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>2.2 . Theoretical Basis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The theory used as a basis or foundation of this research is the theory of philology, that is, text transliteration. Transliteration is very important to introduce the old texts written using the local scripts. Not many Indonesian paeople know even familiar with the existence of this local scripts. Baried (1994: 67) introduces two kinds of transliteration methods as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Diplomatic transliteration method is a transliteration of scripts as it is in accordance with the original text.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Standard transliteration method is a transliteration of scripts according to EYD or inaccordance with the rules of writing in the alphabets used for transliteration.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">This study uses a standard transliteration method, namely the transfer of the Latin alphabet to the Balinesei n accordance with the rules of writing using Balinese scripts (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">uger-uger pasang Aksara Bali</span><span class="font3">). According to Baried (1994: 64) in the text transliteration there are three things that must be considered, namely (1) decoding of words, (2) spelling, and (3) punctuation or sign graphics conventionally used to separate the various parts of a written language; punctuation</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">3.ResearchMethod</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This research applied a qualitative approach with descriptive qualitative research design. The study focused on the process of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text transliteration from Latin letters into Balinese with the use of computerized programs of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3">. It was conducted through three stages, namely (1) the preparation and manuscript inventory, (2) text transliteration, and (3) text editing. The instruments used in this study were divided into three. First, the instrument is in the form of tests, in which students were assigned to transliterate </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> texts using a Balinese script computerized program of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3">. The second instrument was in the form of a questionnaire that was used to determine in more detai lthe response of students to the activities of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text transliteration into Balinese with </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3"> program utilization. Thirdly ,timer (stopwatch) was used to record the time students needed in every process of transliteration. Data on the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text transliteration and stretch of time required were obtained through the implementation of test. The assessment of test results was obtained through editing stages of transliterated text. The data were analyzed descriptively. The data relating to test results were analyzed based on the number of errors of every aspect of the transliteration assessed. The results are then interpreted qualitatively. The data regarding the average speed per syllable of transliteration were analyzed by the following formula:</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">span oftime spent(sec) </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">= &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;number ofsyllablestransliterated</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>Specification:</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">= the average speed for the transliteration of the syllable (sec /syllable)</span></p>

<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Findings and Discussions</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">4.1 Average Speed forTransliteration of One Syllable</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the calculations by the specified formula, in 7seconds, every student was succeeded in transliterating one syllable of the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text from Latin letters into the Balinese with the probability of error rate percentage of 1.6%.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4.1</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ability to transliterate Balinese Text from Latin letters into Balinese script </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Utilizing Balinese script computerized program of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">BaliSimbar</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The test results indicate that the students’ ability to transliterate </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text of the Latin alphabet into Balinese script is excellent Hal ini terlihat dari rerata jumlah kesalahan hasil transliterasi yang hanya 1,6% dari 3.584 silabel yang berhasil ditransliterasikan. This is evident from the average number of errors that is only 1.6% of the 3,584 syllables successfully transliterated. Of the three aspects assessed, spelling ability is the most problematic aspect. It can be seen from the mean acquisition of transliteration error (1.2%) in that aspect. Kesalahan ini merupakan yangterbanyak jika dibandingkan dengan perolehan rerata di aspek lain, yaitu aspek pemenggalan kata hanya (0,34%), dan aspek pungtuasi hanya (0,1%). This error was the highest when compared with the average acquisition of other aspects, the aspect of decoding of words only (0.34%), and aspects of punctuation only (0.1%).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Of the 28 students who became subjects in this study, the test results indicate that the skills of transliterating Balinese text from Latin alphabet into Balinese script throughthe use of Balinese scrip tcomputerized programs of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3"> is excellent. This is evident from the average number of transliteration errors (based on three aspects) conducted by the students, which is only 1.6%. The rest, 98.4% are correct transliteration. However, the students still have trouble with spelling aspects (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Uger-uger Pasang Aksara Bali</span><span class="font3">), where 1.2% transliteration errors were found in that aspect.</span></p>

Errors in applying spelling rules resulted in awkward words, unacceptable, and meaning change. <h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the results of research and discussion that have been presented in the previous section, and in line with the objectives of the research the following conclusions could be made:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ability of students to transliterate Balinese </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text of Latin letters into the Balinese letters with the use of computerized programs of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar version B</span><span class="font3"> showed excellent</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Acreditation:--</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">results, since out of 3,584 syllables successfully transliterated identified only 59 syllables (1, 6%) rate of errors.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The students’ ability to transliterate Balinese </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text of Latin letters into the Balinese letters can be seen from the three aspects of the assessment criteria, such as the decoding of words, spelling, and punctuation/sign. Based on these three aspects, the average number of students’ errors in transliterating are respectively 0.34%; 1.2%; and 0.1%.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Based on the calculation by formula that has been set, within 7 seconds, every student succeeded in transliterating one syllable of the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">satua</span><span class="font3"> text from Latin letters into Balinese script. If one hour lesson(55 minutes=3300seconds) is used to transliterate Balinese language text from the Latin alphabet to the Balinese script by using the computerized program of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Bali Simbar</span><span class="font3">, the number of syllables that can be transliterated is approximately 471 syllables.</span></p></li></ul>
