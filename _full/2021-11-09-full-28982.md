<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk membuka makna dari frasa nomina dengan mengidentifikasi struktur frasa nomina, struktrur sintaksis, dan struktur semantiknya berdasarkan kepala frasa yang berasal dari nomina deverbal. Nomina deverbal dapat diidentifikasi berdasarkan teori dari Carstairs-McCarthy (2002), yang kemudian frasa nomina teridentifikasi nomina deverbal nya dikelompokkan berdasarkan jenis modifikasi frasa nomina berdasarkan teori dari Quirk et al. (1985) dan struktrur sintaksis dari frasa nomina dapat digambarkan menggunakan teori dari Wekker dan Haegeman (1985). Setelah itu, makna frasa nomina dibuka berdasarkan teori dari Larson (1998) beserta teori Van Valin dan LaPolla (1997). Berdasarkan hasil penelitian, terdapat delapan jenis modifikasi nomina deverbal dan tiga jenis modifikasi frasa nomina dari data yang diidentifikasi; juga, makna dari frasa nomina dengan nomina deverbal sebagai kepala frasa dapat dibuka menjadi dua hingga empat klausa dan dapat disatukan menjadi sebuah kalimat.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;INTRODUCTION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Words are different each other that there are varieties of word in this world where verb noun hold the position as the most-frequently renewed and modified. Noun can be modified as verb and also verb; it can also be modified into noun. As noun is modified noun phrase, the phrase may be derived from a structure of clause in which it is modified in order to avoid sentence fragments or repetition in a sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Since noun phrase varies, depending on what modifies head, the modification of head from verb can be said as one of the ways to determine whether a phrase is categorized as noun phrase or not. This modification where verb is transformed into head of noun phrase, noun, is called nomina deverbal.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">By transforming into different word class, nomina deverbals derived from verb can still bring along its previous structure, and then modifies it to match its current structure in which it is already in form of noun; however, the modification itself means it has to be rearranged. The rearrangement of the sentence structure into phrase structure from nomina deverbals may omit some words. When a structure of words is rearranged, there is no way the meaning is changed. According to Larson (1998:9), one form may express a variety of meanings and single meaning may be expressed in a variety of forms. By opening the meaning of the noun phrase, therefore it can be more useful to find out the function of every noun phrase that it later can be paraphrased to avoid ambiguity between structured words and its meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The problems of this study can be formulated as follow: What types of modifications in the noun phrase are found in Marya Holcombe's book </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">The Best Letter Book Ever</span><span class="font4">? And what types of meaning of the NP were found in Marya Holcombe's book </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">The Best Letter Book Ever</span><span class="font4">? This study aims to classify types of noun phrase found in the book and to analyze the NP in form of nomina deverbals found in the book.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MATERIALS AND METHOD</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2.1 . MATERIALS</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The data are obtained through note-taking from the book, entitled &quot;The Best Letter Book Ever&quot; written by Marya Holcombe, a 542 pages book which consists of various types of how to make business correspondence and also there are various NP found in this book. Each letter provides example of how the letter is used to write a formal letter either for a customer or firm which is addressed to.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;METHOD</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The procedure of collecting data was firstly conducted by reading the whole book of Marya Holcombe entitled &quot;The Best Letter Book Ever&quot;, and then the necessary data were noted in order to conduct further analysis.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The collected data were analyzed by conducting descriptive-qualitative method, applying theory of Carstairs - McCarthy (2002), Quirk et al. theory (1985), and Wekker and Haegeman theory (1985) to find out kinds of verb to noun derivational suffix, types of noun phrase modification, and syntactic structure, and theory of Van Valin and LaPolla (1997), and theory of Larson (1998) are applied to identify the meaning within the noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Descriptive method was applied in presenting the analyzed data. For the first problem, the data was classified into types of noun phrase based on its verb to noun derivational suffix. Then the classified data was taken to be represented in form of Haegeman's tree diagram to prove the head of the noun phrase validity. For the second problem, the analyzed data in form of noun phrase were breakdown into several clauses and were identified to find out how the forms of noun phrase changed and how the function of subject and object were related each other to the head clause or verb.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unfolding the Meaning of Noun Phrase Structures</span><br><br><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>3.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Derivational Suffix -ation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Data 1</span><span class="font4">: Because of </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">the rapid appreciation of housing (and replacement costs) here in Southern California</span><span class="font4">, we need to make sure you current homeowner’s policy of $300,00 provides sufficient protection before we renew the policy on May 30... (Holcombe, 2002: 9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Semantically, the data represents logical structure of meaning that the meanings of the noun phrase </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">the rapid appreciation of housing (and replacement costs) here in Southern California</span><span class="font4"> can also be breakdown into several parts that also implies sentence:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1.</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the rapid appreciation</span><span class="font4">: someone appreciates something rapidly</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">2.</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;appreciation of housing</span><span class="font4">: someone appreciates housing</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">3.</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;appreciation of replacement costs</span><span class="font4">: someone appreciates replacement costs</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">4.</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;appreciation in Southern California</span><span class="font4">: someone appreciates something in Southern California</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The example above implicates there are four messages which can be described as a sentence as the primary meaning of the noun phrase that is </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone appreciates housing and replacement costs rapidly in Southern California</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font4"> The phrase and sentence indicate the same meaning that the action of housing and replacement costs is highly appreciated by people in South California.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">A change to the structure shift occurs between the two phrases and sentence where the NP </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">the rapid appreciation of housing (and replacement costs) here in Southern California</span><span class="font4"> is shifted to be a sentence </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone appreciates housing (and replacement costs) rapidly in Southern California</span><span class="font4">. It is shown the two phrase and sentence have similar H that is </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">appreciation </span><span class="font4">and </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">appreciates </span><span class="font4">in which the V </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">appreciates </span><span class="font4">is derived from a root V </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">appreciate </span><span class="font4">which gains an inflectional suffix </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">-s </span><span class="font4">to explain the action is done by the third person </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">someone</span><span class="font4">, then the root is modified by adding a derivational suffix </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">-ation </span><span class="font4">to form N word class, to show a concept of </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">appreciate</span><span class="font4">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">As the clause structure is obtained, semantic roles can be identified. The underlined clause </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone appreciates housing (and replacement costs) rapidly in Southern California</span><span class="font4"> indicates that it is included in </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">action</span><span class="font4"> type of state of affairs since the participant </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone</span><span class="font4"> does something which does not involve any change to </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">housing (and replacement costs)</span><span class="font4">. Then, the word </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone</span><span class="font4"> acts as doer of the action, to appreciate, and </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">housing (and replacement costs)</span><span class="font4"> becomes the affected by the action, to be appreciated; in addition to it, </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">Southern California</span><span class="font4"> indicates where the accident occurs. As a result, </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">someone</span><span class="font4"> which refers to the doer can be termed as 'agent', </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">housing (and replacement costs)</span><span class="font4"> as the entity being appreciated can be termed as 'theme', and </span><span class="font4" style="text-decoration:underline;">Southern California</span><span class="font4"> which shows the location can be termed as 'location' It can be drawn as follow:</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">There are eight derivational suffixes to create deverbal nouns found from the data; those are -ance, -ence, -ment, -ing, -ation, -tion, -ion, and -al. All modifications of noun phrase structure are found, that is postmodification, premodification, and multimodification.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">It is identified based on semantic analysis that a phrase is derived from a clause which may consist of more than two or four arguments. This analysis shows the forms and meanings within the sentences and it is found that the described data consists of processes and action states of affairs. The analysis of semantic roles of the data also shows the use of agent, theme, recipient and patient. Logical structure identified on the sentences also results in three indications of structure: activity, achievement, and accomplishment. The identification of the three structures results in the unfolding of the sentences that conveys the meaning of the identified data.</span></p>
