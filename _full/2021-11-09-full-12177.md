<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">BORROWINGS IN </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">THE FIVE PEOPLE YOU MEET IN HEAVEN</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"> INTO</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstract</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Judul tulisan ini adalah kata-kata serapan bahasa inggris yang digunakan dalam novel terjemahan karya Mitch Albom yang berjudul “The Five People You Meet in Heaven”. Mengingat adanya perbedaan budaya dan bahasa antara penulis novel dan pembaca, kata serapan umumnya digunakan ketika sang penerjemah tidak bisa menemukan kata atau istilah yang tepat dalam bahasa Indonesia. Oleh sebab itu, kata serapan menjadi topik utama dalam penelitian ini.Penelitian ini dilakukan untuk mengetahui jenis kata serapan yang ditemukan dalam novel. Data diambil dari novel “The Five People You Meet in Heaven” dan terjemahannya yang berjudul “Meniti Bianglala”. Data dikumpulkan dengan menggunakan metode observasi lalu dianalisis dengan penjelasan deskriptif. Teori yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini adalah teori types of borrowing oleh Molina dan Albir (2002), Larson (1984) dan teori borrowing procedure oleh Vinay dan Darbelnet (2000).</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: terjemahan, kata serapan, jenis</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">In translation, the translator does not only deal with transferring words from the source language into the target language. The translator should comprehend the culture of both source and target language to transferring the message or meaning. Therefore, a translator should also master the culture, social values and words structure of both source and target languages.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">After observing Mitch Albom’s </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">The Five People You Meet in Heaven</span><span class="font0"> and its translation in Indonesian </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Meniti Bianglala</span><span class="font0">, the problems of this research are the types of borrowing and the procedures of borrowing.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Based on the problems, the aims of the study is to find out the types of borrowing and the procedures of borrowing in the novel.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The data source of this study was written by Mitch Albom entitled “</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">The Five People You Meet in Heaven</span><span class="font0">”. The novel published by Hyperion in 2003. Since this study was about translation research, the data is also taken from its translation in Indonesian. Mitch Albom’s </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">The Five People You Meet in Heaven </span><span class="font0">was translated into Indonesian by Andang H. Sutopo. The translation version was published by PT. Gramedia Pustaka Utama in 2005.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">To collect the data, the method of doing library research was applied and technique of note taking is important to support the method. The first step was reading the whole story starting from the beginning until the end. After figured out what the story about, borrowing words that occured in the SL and TL were underlined to mark them. The next step was listing the borrowing words found in SL and TL. After listing the borrowing words, they were classified based on their constituent.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">This study belongs to descriptive qualitative research. Therefore the collected data were analyzed qualitatively and comparatively. The data was analyzed qualitatively means that the data was analyzed by applying descriptive technique. The collected data were analyzed comparatively, it means that the words in the source language and target language are compared to comprehend and to figure out which words belongs to which kind of borrowing types and procedure.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Types of Borrowing and Procedures of Borrowing in the Novel </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Five</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">People You Meet in Heaven</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"> into </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Meniti Bianglala</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">This study focused on three types: </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Pure</span><span class="font0">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Naturalized</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Cultural Borrowing</span><span class="font0">, as supported by the standard integration of absorption of word-element that legalized by Indonesian Government as stated in PUEBIYD.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font0">The word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">maintenance</span><span class="font0"> in the SL means </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">the act of keeping something in a good condition by checking or repairing it regularly</span><span class="font0"> (Oxford, 1995:891) should be translated into </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">teknisi</span><span class="font0"> in the TL text which seem the similar concept in BI. In this case, as a pure borrowing, the translator uses the specific word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">maintenance </span><span class="font0">with spelling system. The translation is acceptable in the TL, the word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">maintenance</span><span class="font0"> itself is common nowadays in the TL culture, it has been well known in the TL.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">2. &nbsp;SL: Eddie was a block away, &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;TL: Eddie sedang berada satu</span></p></li></ul>

<p><span class="font0">2005:10)</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">sandwich</span><span class="font0"> in the SL text means two slices of bread, often spread with butter, with a layer of meat, cheese, etc (Oxford, 1995:1294) should be translated into </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">roti lapis</span><span class="font0"> in the TL text which seem to refer to similar concept of BI. Here, the translator still using the borrowing word (</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">sandwich</span><span class="font0">) in the TL and didn’t change it into Indonesian. The spelling and pronounciation of it word are absorbed in target language.</span></p>

<div>

<p><span class="font0">TL: Mereka didorong masuk ke dalam barak bambu. (Sutopo, 2005:71)</span></p>

<p><span class="font0">TL: …, pengawal-pengawal tak berkeprimanusiaan itu, dengan gigi-gigi merka yang gingsul dan tulang-tulang wajah menonjol dan tawon mati di dalam sup. (Sutopo, 2005:85)</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<p><span class="font0">The word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">bamboo</span><span class="font0"> (no. 1) in the SL text is translated into the word </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">bamb</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">u</span><span class="font0"> in the TL text and both of them have the same meaning that is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">a tall tropical plant that is a member of the grass family and has hard hollow stems that are used for making furniture</span><span class="font0"> (Oxford, 1995:100). In this case, the translator translated the word by taking the sound /ˌbæmˈbuː/ from SL and modifying spelling</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">system. Consequently, the adjusment occurs by changing the vowel combination –</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">oo</span><span class="font0"> into –</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">u</span><span class="font0"> in BI. Consequently, the adjusment occurs by changing the vowel combination –</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">oo</span><span class="font0"> into –</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">u</span><span class="font0"> in BI. This case is also happened in other words such as: </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">b</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ee</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">r</span><span class="font0"> becomes </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">b</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">i</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">r</span><span class="font0">, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">s</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ou</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">p</span><span class="font0"> becomes </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">s</span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">u</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">p</span><span class="font0">. Those words also change the spelling of vowel combination without taking the sound.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">From the analysis, we have seen the borrowings in the translation divided into three, those are (1) Pure Borrowing is an expression or a word is taken over purely into TL, (2) Naturalized Borrowing is transferred and consists of adapting a source language to the target language and (3) Cultural Borrowing, has the meaning source language cultural words is translated by using the word of target language for thing or event, which is not exactly the same but occurs in the target language.</span></p>
