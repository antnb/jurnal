<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Studi ini di buat dengan tujuan untuk mengidentifikasi fungsi dan menganalisa kesepadanan preposisi </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">at, on </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">yang ditemukan pada novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">oleh John Green. Data studi ini diperoleh dengan menggunakan metode kepustakaan dan dianalisa dengan metode kualitatif. Teori yang digunakan adalah teori yang dikeluarkan oleh Larson dan Quirk. Sedangkan temuan dalam studi ini adalah preposisi </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">at, on </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">berfungsi untuk menyatakan, tempat, posisi, waktu, kondisi, stimulus, target/tujuan, alat, dan agen. Selain itu juga ditemukan padanan terjemahan preposisi </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">at </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">adalah </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di, pada</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dalam, atas </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kepada</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">On </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">diterjemahkan menjadi </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di, pada, di atas, dengan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">By </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">diterjemahkan menjadi </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dengan, oleh </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata Kunci: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">terjemahan, preposisi, dan kesepadanan.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In translating a language, there are a large number of difficulties found such as how to translate English language as source language (SL) into Indonesian as target language (TL) since three words in English such as the prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> can have many translation equivalents in Indonesian. Thus, it is very interesting to analyze the function and translation equivalents of the English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The problems that the writer would like to discuss in this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the functions of the English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> found in</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font2"> by John Green?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the translation equivalents of the English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian based on the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font2"> by John Green?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">General Objectives:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the final paper (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">skripsi</span><span class="font2">) to obtain S1 degree in English Department Faculty of Arts, Udayana University.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To make the readers understand the English language preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> better.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Specific Objectives:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the functions of the English language prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the translation equivalents of the English language prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data of this study include the English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> which were taken from a novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font2"> by John Green and translated by Dwijani Nimpoeno.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The study used library research method in collecting the data. The method was done by reading both English and Indonesian novels. Then, the writer was finding out the sentences that contain the prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by.</span><span class="font2"> After that, those prepositions were marked, underlined and retyped based on both in English and Indonesian.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The collected data were analyzed using the qualitative method. They are described in detail based on theories related to the topic of discussion. The selected data were identified and analyzed to determine the functions and the translation equivalents of the English language prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> based on the concept and theoretical framework.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the result of the study, there are some equivalents of the prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian such as;</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font2"> translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di, pada, dalam, atas</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kepada</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">One example of the English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font2"> which is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: …taking the elevator is a Last Days kind of activity </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">at </span><span class="font2">Support Group, …(Green., 2012: 8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: …memakai lift adalah sejenis aktivitas Hari Terakhir </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">di </span><span class="font2">pertemuan Kelompok Pendukung,.. (Nimpoeno., 2014: 16)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at support group</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di pertemuan Kelompok Pendukung.</span><span class="font2"> The functions of the preposition in SL and TL are used to denote a place since both prepositions describe a particular area where someone is available.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font2"> translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di, pada, di atas, dengan</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font2">.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">One example of the English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font2"> which is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: I was standing with my chuck Taylors </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">on </span><span class="font2">the very edge of the curb. (Green., 2012: 20)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: Aku berdiri dengan sepatu kets Chuck Taylorku berada persis </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">di </span><span class="font2">pinggir jalan. (Nimpoeno., 2014: 32)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on the very edge of the curb</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di pinggir jalan.</span><span class="font2"> The function of the prepositional phrase in SL and TL are used to denote a place. They are used to describe a particular area where someone is available.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dengan, oleh</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">One example of the English preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dengan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">SL: They didn’t seem too surprised </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">by </span><span class="font2">my arrival, which made sense: The fact that Augustus made me feel special did not necessarily indicate that I was special. (Green., 2012: 27)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL: Mereka seakan tidak begitu terkejut </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">dengan </span><span class="font2">kedatanganku, dan itu masuk di akal: Fakta bahwa Augustus membuatku merasa istimewa tidak selalu menunjukan bahwa aku memang istimewa. (Nimpoeno., 2014: 41)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The prepositional phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by my arrival</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dengan kedatanganku</span><span class="font2">. The function of the preposition in SL and TL are used to denote means, since it answers the question starting with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">how</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded that there are many English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by </span><span class="font2">found in the novel. Their functions are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to denote place, to denote position, to denote time, to denote condition, to denote stimulus, to denote goal or target, to denote means</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to denote agentive.</span><span class="font2"> Meanwhile, the preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di, pada, dalam, atas</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">kepada.</span><span class="font2"> The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di, pada, di atas, dengan,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ke.</span><span class="font2"> The preposition </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dengan, oleh </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">di.</span><span class="font2"> The result of this study shows that there are many translation equivalents of the English prepositions </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">at, on</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> in Indonesian.</span></p>
