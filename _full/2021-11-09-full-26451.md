<p><span class="font1">A.A Arista Adnya Pramita<sup>1*</sup>, I Gede Sadia<sup>2</sup>, Yana Qomariana<sup>3 [123]</sup>English Department Faculty of Arts UdayanaUniversity <sup>1</sup></span><a href="mailto:aadnyapramita@ymail.com"><span class="font1">[aadnyapramita@ymail.com]</span></a><span class="font1"> <sup>2</sup></span><a href="mailto:yqomariana@gmail.com"><span class="font1">[yqomariana@gmail.com]</span></a><span class="font1"> </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><sup>*</sup></span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Corresponding Author</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0">Penelitian ini berjudul</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">“The Language Function Used by Receptionist in C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland”</span><span class="font0">. Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk mengindetifikasi jenis fungsi bahasa dan untuk menganalisis kesopanan bahasa receptionist di C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland. Teori yang digunakan untuk menganlisis data diambil dari teori fungsi bahasa oleh M.A.K Halliday (1973) dan teori kesopanan dalam berbicara oleh Ronald Wardhaugh. Data dalam penelitian ini diambil dari 10 percapakapan yang diperoleh dengan metode observasi dan merekam percakapan. Hasil dari analisis adalah percakapan antara receptionist dan tamu hanya menggunakan empat fungsi bahasa dari tujuh fungsi bahasa yang disebutkan oleh Halliday. Empat fungsi bahasa itu adalah </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">fungsi instrumental, fungsi interaksi, fungsi heuristic, dan fungsi representasional.</span><span class="font0"> Dalam penelitian ini juga ditemukan bahwa 10 percakapan tersebut terdapat 1 struktur yang sering digunakan yaitu, penyebutan title seseorang.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: FungsiBahasa, Kesopanan, Ekspresi</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">English is an international language. Everyone wants to be able to speak English because English is an International language. Therefore, people who come to other countries must be able to speak the language of that country; at least they should be able to speak English. According to Halliday (1973:7), in the most general terms, people do differently with their language, that is; they expect a large number of different purposes by talking, writing, listening and reading. Especially for talking, Halliday determinesseven classifications of language function. They are Instrumental function, Regulatory function, Representational function, Interaction function, Personal function, Heuristic function, and Imaginative function.While we are talking with someone, we have to be polite. Politeness is best expressed as the practical application of good manners or etiquette, and it is also one way of showing appreciations of one person to</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">another.This study analyzes conversation between the receptionist and the guest in order to see how politeness is involved in the conversation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font0">1.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What kind of language functions found in English used in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What politeness expressions are used in each function?</span></p></li>
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the problems stated above, the purposes of the present study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the kinds of language functions used by Receptionist in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze politeness used by Receptionist in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The research method of this study is related to the problems mentioned above; the method used in this study consists of data source, method and technique of collecting data and method and technique of analyzing data, as presented as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data of this study were collected from 5 receptionistsin </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1"> as the respondents.“</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1">” waschosen with consideration that </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151</span><span class="font1"> is five star villa and all of the Receptionist in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1"> have basic tourism skill, including English.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were collected in two ways. The first was done by interviewing the receptionistsin order to receive their personal data. The second methodwas done through observations. It was done by recording and taking note during the conversations between the staff and the guest.Finally, identifying and classifying the data to see the kinds of language functions.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">After completing the data,it was continued with the method and technique of analyzing data. The qualitative method is the method used to find out the answer to the problem. First of all, the data were collected by recording the conversations between the staff and the guest at the villa. After that, the data weretranscribed on paper.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of the Language Function.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">After collecting the conversations between the receptionist and the guest in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 luxury villas dreamland,</span><span class="font1"> the conversations were divided into seven functions of language by Halliday. From all of the language function, there were four functions occurring. A more detailed explanation can be seen below.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Instrumental Function.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The example of this function can be seen in the conversations below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Conversation:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Good evening</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Good evening, sir. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Can I help you</span><span class="font1">?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">May you call taxi for us?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : All right sir, where would you have to go?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : I’m going to Jimbaran bay to have a dinner.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Could you have a sit first and the taxi will coming.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Ok, thank you</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The conversation above refers to the instrumental function because in the conversation there is a sentence that shows a guest requests a taxi to the receptionist. In this case, the guest wants to the receptionist to call the taxi for him. Another sentence is when the receptionist said “Can I help you?” the receptionist offers help to the guest.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Representational Function.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The example of this function can be seen in the conversations below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Conversation II:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Good afternoon. May I help you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Yes. We have a booking for tonight. The name is Jane Merrick</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Just a moment, please. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Yes, that was 1 villa with 2 bedroom, living room, and swimming pool.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Yes, that’s right.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Would you like to register, please? How are you going to pay, madam? By cash or by credit card?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : In Credit card.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : May I see your passport, please? Thank you, madam. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Here’s your key card. Your villa number is 7. The butler will show you up.</span><span class="font1"> Have a nice day, madam.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Thank you</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The conversation above has the representational function because the receptionist in this conversation describes the facilities in the villa’s room.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interaction Function</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Conversation II shows that there is an interaction between the receptionist and the guest. The receptionist asks the guest about the payment and the guest answers the receptionist’s question. The conversation can be seen as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Conversation II:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Good afternoon. May I help you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Yes. We have a booking for tonight. The name is Jane Merrick</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Just a moment, please. Yes, that was 1 villa with 2 bedroom, living room, and swimming pool.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Yes, that’s right.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Would you like to register, please? How are you going to pay, madam? By cash or by credit card?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">In Credit card.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : May I see your passport, please? Thank you, madam. Here’s your key card. Your villa number is 7. The butler will show you up. Have a nice day, madam.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Thank you</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the example above, in conversation II when the receptionist said “: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">would you like to register, please? How are you going to pay, madam? By cash or by credit card?”</span><span class="font1"> and the guest answer “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">In Credit card”</span><span class="font1">. The receptionist wants to know about the payment. She used interaction function in this case.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Heuristic Function</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this case, the guest wanted to know about the information in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1">. She used heuristic function, as can be seen when the guest came to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury Villas Dreamland.</span><span class="font1"> The conversation can be seen in the following example below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Conversation I</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : Good morning madam, welcome to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury villas Dreamland</span><span class="font1">, may I help you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Good morning, it’s nice place, I need some information about your room rate. Can I looking your brochure?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Reception : Yes please madam.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Halliday (1973:14-15) concludes that the young child is really aware of how to use language to learn. Based on the utterance above, the guest often conveys a question in order to get more information about </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">C151 Luxury villas dreamland</span><span class="font1"> in brochure.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The analysis of Politeness</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.2.1</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Politeness in Conversation I Conversation I:</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Receptionist : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Good morning madam</span><span class="font1">, welcome to C151 Luxury villas Dreamland, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">may I help you?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Guest : Good morning, it’s nice place, I need some information about your room rate. Can I looking your brochure?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Reception : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Yes please madam.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Based on conversation I, the utterance </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Good morning madam”</span><span class="font1">, from the receptionist is polite in the situation where the conversation took place. It was a formal language to greet someone; it was also used to greet someone else when they arrive at their destination and the first time when the receptionist met them. The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Madam” </span><span class="font1">was a polite way of greeting or addressing them since the receptionist didn’t know them well. The questions and the answer made by the receptionist and guest are polite such as: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“May I…, Can I…</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Yes please madam”</span><span class="font1">. Form the social status, the language used was also polite because in the language used by the receptionist to the guest is a formal language which is always used in the conversations at the Villa and the language from the guest is also polite because it gives good answers to the receptionist’s question.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>6.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis, it can be concluded that there are seven types of function. However, in this study only four functions were found from those seven functions. Those four functions are: instrumental function, interactional function, heuristic function and representational function. The analysis shows that the function which is frequently used is instrumental function. The conversation in the data shows that the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">sentence used as the means of communication is the characteristic of instrumental function.From the finding it was also found that the structure of politenessinfluenced the conversation between receptionist and guest. From those structures, it was found that term of address was the dominant structure found in the conversation that influenced politeness in the conversation between the receptionist and guest.</span></p>
