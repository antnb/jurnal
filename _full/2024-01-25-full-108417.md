The research was conducted employing a normative legal research methodology with a statutory approach and a conceptual approach. LPDs are subject to two types of legal regulations in Bali: state law and customary law. In granting credit"

  - "LPDs must foster client confidence by providing protection and legal certainty to ensure the fulfillment of their clients' rights and obligations. In the event of client default"
  - "LPDs may impose customary sanctions that can be resolved within the village. The ability to impose customary punishments is an integral component of the authority to resolve any offenses or disputes that may arise within a territory due to its genuine autonomy.
