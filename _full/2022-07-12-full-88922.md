<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRAK</span><span class="font3">: Saat ini minat terhadap penggunaan tanaman aromatik di berbagai bidang kehidupan berdasarkan sifatnya makin meningkat. Oleh karena itu, pada penelitian ini akan dilakukan uji komposisi kimia daun kayu putih (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melalueca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn.) dari Pulau Lombok dengan menggunakan uji fitokimia dan teknik GC-MS (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Gas Chromatography -Mass Spectrometry</span><span class="font3">). Hasil uji fitokimia yang teridentifikasi adalah terpenoid, flavonoid, alkaloid, triterpenoid, tanin, dan saponin. Komposisi kimia dari daun kayu putih (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melalueca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn.) ditentukan oleh GC-MS dari ketiga fraksi yaitu etanol, DCM, dan heksana. Komposisi utama dalam ekstrak etanol adalah asam heksadekanoat (26,69%) dan asam heptadekana-8-karbonat (31,19%), pada DCM yaitu 1,8-cineole (2,31%), asam heksaadekanoik (25.88%), heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (28,75%) Sementara senyawa kimia utama yang ada pada heksana adalah 1,8-cineole (23,31%), 1-phellandren (1,33%), terpineol (1,16%), spathulenol (0,85%), ethanone (3,4-epoxy-2-heksanon) (0.72%), asam heksadekanoat (16,06%), dan heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (19,92%).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Kata kunci</span><span class="font3">: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn; 1,8-Cineole; Fitokimia; GC-MS.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRACT : </span><span class="font3">Recently, there has been increasing interest in the use of aromatic plants, which are used in the different areas of life based on various of the plant properties. The aim of this study was to investigate the chemical composition of Eucalyptus leaves (Melalueca leucadendron Linn.) from Lombok Island by phytochemical screening and GC-MS (Gas Chromatography - Mass Spectrometry) technique. The identified phytochemical screening of the leaves was terpenoid, flavonoid, alkaloid, triterpenoid, tannin and saponin. The chemical composition of the leaves was determined by GC-MS (Gas Chromatography - Mass Spectrometry) at three fractions, such as ethanol, DCM, and hexane. The major compounds in ethanol extract are hexadecanoic acid (26.69%) and heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (31,19%) while in the DCM are 1,8-cineole (2.31%), hexadecanoic acid (25.88%), heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (28.75%). Meanwhile, the major compounds in the hexane are 1,8-cineole (23.31%), 1-phellandren (1.33%), terpineol (1.16%), spathulenol (0.85%), ethanone (3,4-epoxy-2-heksanon) (0.72%), hexadecanoic acid (16.06%), and heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (19.92%).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn; 1.8-Cineole; Phytochemical; GC-MS.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;INTRODUCTION</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The aromatic medicinal plant </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus </span><span class="font3">and its essential oils have been used since long time ago in folk medicine. Chemical composition by GC-MS presented the abundance major compound as of 1.8-cineole. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus camaldulensis</span><span class="font3"> had the strongest bioactivities despite its major compound was spatulenol [1]. Essential oils from four species of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus</span><span class="font3"> plantation in northern Thailand namely (E</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">. deglupata, E.urophylla, E. citriodora,</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">E. camaldulensis</span><span class="font3">) were analyzed using GC-MS and 63 compounds were identified, the highest concentration from those was citronellal. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">E. citriodora</span><span class="font3"> oil showed the strongest antioxidant and antibacterial activity and can be a good candidate for the development of product for antioxidant and antibacterial activity [2].</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Historically, various preparation from the bark, leaves, and fruits of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus </span><span class="font3">species have been used in folk medicine tranquilizers, sedatives, painkillers and in the treatment of various skin disorder. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> flower extract inhibits UVB-induced oxidative damage of HaCaT, reduces pro-inflammatory cell mediators, ensures protection of DNA-damage, and acts as photoprotective agent [3]. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> leaves showed a series of biological activities of interest, including antimicrobial [4], antitumor, and anti inflamatory properties [5], meanwhile </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> flower has application as an adjuvant in sunscreen formulations as a strategy to reduce risk of sunburn and prevent skin diseases associated with UV-induced inflammation and cancer [6]. More than 20 species of Eucalyptus have ever been exploited commercially to produce 1.8-cineol (more than 70 %) by cosmetics industries and pharmaceutical. Eucalyptus has many of secondary metabolites as terpenoid [7]. For a few years, essential oils from eucalyptus have</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">involved interest as the source of natural product. They have presented for their uses as alternative remedies for the treatment of many infections and food preservative. Herein, we conducted study to determine phytochemical screening and GC-MS (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Gas Chromatography - Mass Spectrometry) </span><span class="font3">analysis of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melalueca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn which grows in Lombok since no report found about the chemical composition of the plant planted in this island.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Phytochemical test</span></h2>
<p><span class="font3">Phytochemical test used to screen the secondary metabolites contained in the ethanol extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">M. leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn was Harborne procedures [8].</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>GC-MS analysis of three extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">M.leucadendron</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"> Linn</span></h2>
<p><span class="font3">Analysis of three extracts were carried out by </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Gas Chromatography - Mass Spectrometry</span><span class="font3"> (GCMS). GC analysis was performed using a Varian Star 3400 Cx chromatograph fitted with a DB-5MS fused silica capillary column. The column temperature was increase from 60 to 260 ℃ at 5 min <sup>-1.</sup> The total analysis time was 150 min. For GC-MS analysis, the extract was dissolved in ethanol. A 1 μL sample was injected in the split mode ratio 1:10. Helium was used as carried gas at a flow rate of 1 mLmin<sup>-1</sup>. The injector temperature was</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">200℃. The mass spectrometer was operated at an emission current of 10 μA and an electron multiplier voltage between 1400 and 1500 V. The percentage compound of the essential oil and extract were computed by normalization method from the GC peak areas, assuming an identical mass response factor for all compounds.</span></p>

<p><span class="font3">Table 1 shows that the content of secondary metabolites identified in the ethanol extract of the eucalyptus leaves are terpenoids, flavonoids, alkaloids, triterpenoids, tannins and saponins group. As comparison, it was found a hydrolysable tannin which is isolated from dried fruits of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Melaleuca leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> and characterized as 1,2-di-O-galloyl-3-O-digalloyl-4,6-O-(S)-hexahydroxydiphenoyl-β-D-glucose as well as known stilbene glycosides and triterpenes[9]. Secondary metabolites from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus camaldulensis</span><span class="font3"> were identified</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">flavonoids, pigments, phenolics, terpenes, starches, steroids, and essential oils [10]. Phytochemical investigation of leaves of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus globules</span><span class="font3"> Labill extracts showed the presence of alkaloids, phenolic compounds, steroids, cardiac glycosides, and terpenes [11].</span></p>

<p><span class="font3">The chemical compositions in ethanol fraction were dominated by hexadecanoic acid (26.69%) and heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (31,19%) while in the DCM were 1,8-cineole (2.31%), hexadecanoic acid (25.88%), heptadecene-8-carbonic acid (28.75%). <h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Bioactivities of Identified Chemical Constituents</span></h2>
<p><span class="font3">The 1.8-cineole is the most significant compositions of Eucalyptus essential oil [12]. This research showed 1.8 Cineole as of 23.31% in n-hexane phase and 2.31 % in ethanol phase. The current study focused on the inhibitory activity of eucalyptol (1.8 cineole) against Mpro/3CLprofrom SARS-CoV-2 [13]. The 1.8-cineole was found in Eucalyptus alba as the major component (76.5-88.1%). In addition to this compound, others that were found including limonene</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">(3.8–8.6%), α-terpineol (1.4–2.8%), globulol (1.3–6.3%) and α-pinene (1.5–1.8%) [14].</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Eucalyptus intertexta</span><span class="font3"> has 19 components which were identified in the oils and 1,8-cineole (67.2–83.1 %) also globulol (1.9–8.9 %) act as main constituents. [15]. Three different species of eucalyptus from various locations in Indonesia were studied particularly, such as </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">E. citriodora, E. urophylla, and E. viminalis.</span><span class="font3"> The major components of eucalyptus oil were determined to be 1,8-cineole (18.2%– 45.5%) and citronellal (69.27%–82.81%). The highest composition of 1,8-cineole was obtained from the eucalyptus oil extracted from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">E. urophyllawhereas</span><span class="font3"> the highest composition of citronellal was obtained from eucalyptus oil extracted from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">E. citriodora</span><span class="font3"> [16].</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Hexadecanoic acid and Heptadecene-8-carbonic acid was found in all fractions of eucalyptus leaves. Both compounds are fatty acids. <h2><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSION</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on phytochemical test, the content of secondary metabolites in the ethanol extract of eucalyptus leaves were the terpenoids, flavonoids, alkaloids, triterpenoids, tannins and saponin group. The compounds found in ethanol extract of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">M. leucadendron</span><span class="font3"> Linn were hexadecanoic acid and heptadecene-8-carbonic acid while in the DCM were 1.8-cineole, hexadecanoic acid, heptadecene-8-carbonic acid, in the hexane were 1.8-cineole, 1-phellandren, terpineol,</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">spathulenol, ethanone (3.4-epoxy-2-heksanon). The potential compound for the leaves based on review bioactivities the major chemical components were 1.8-cineole (23.31%). Meanwhile the major compounds found in hexane extract was hexadecanoic acid (26.69%) and in ethanol extract was eptadecene-8-carbonic acid (31.19%).</span></p>
