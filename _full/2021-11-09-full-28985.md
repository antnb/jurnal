<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Gusti Ayu Marcela Dewi<sup>1*</sup>, I Nengah Sudipa<sup>2</sup>, Sang Ayu Isnu Maharani<sup>3 </sup></span><span class="font1"><sup>123</sup>English Department, Faculty of Arts, Udayana University <sup>1</sup></span><a href="mailto:ela_gek@ymail.com"><span class="font1">[ela_gek@ymail.com]</span></a><span class="font1"> <sup>2</sup></span><a href="mailto:nengah_sudipa@yahoo.co.id"><span class="font1">[nengah_sudipa@yahoo.co.id]</span></a><span class="font1"> <sup>3</sup></span><a href="mailto:isnu.maharani@yahoo.com"><span class="font1">[isnu.maharani@yahoo.com]</span></a><span class="font1"> </span><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">*</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Judul dari studi ini adalah “Figurative Language in The Fault in Our Star Movie Script”. Tujuan dari penelitian adalah untuk mengidentifikasi jenis bahasa kiasan dan untuk menganalisis makna yang direpresentasikan dari bahasa kiasan yang terdapat dalam naskah film berjudul The Fault in Our Star.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Data penelitian diambil dari naskah film berjudul The Fault in Our Star. Pengumpulan data dilakukan melalui metode observasi. Data yang dikumpulkan dan diklasifikasikan secara kualitatif analisis berdasarkan teori dari Knickerbocker dan Reninger (1963) dalam buku “Interpreting Literature” dan juga didukung oleh teori dari Leech (1979) dalam buku “Semantic”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukan terdapat 38 contoh bahasa kiasan yang ditemukan seperti; 9 contoh simile, 3 contoh metafora, 2 contoh personifikasi, 1 contoh metonimi, 15 contoh hiperbola, 3 contoh ironi, 1 contoh paradok dan 4 contoh ironi.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords: bahasa kiasan, jenis makna, naskah film</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Movie is a medium that disseminates moving pictures, that is, the series of images projected on a screen in order to create the illusion (deception) motion of the living. Nowadays movie becomes a literary work because literature doesn’t have limitation on how you should do it; although many people think that literature must be expressed through standard literary work e.g. prose or poetry. Literature is one way through which human beings express their feeling, thoughts, experiences, emotions, expectations, and faith.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Figurative Language is one type of literary work, which puts an emphasis on connotation rather than denotation meaning. Generally, figurative language is language that uses words or expressions with a different meaning. Since there are many figurative languages in the movie that commonly have different sense from the literal meaning, the writer is interested in analyzing and classifying the communication used in a movie</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">into the kinds of figurative language itself. One of movies that contain figurative language is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault In Our Star</span><span class="font1"> directed by Josh Boon</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font1"> which was adopted from a novel by John Green.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font1">Based on the background of the study, the writer formulates the following problems:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of figurative language are used in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star</span><span class="font1"> movie script?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How meanings are represented of figurative language in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star </span><span class="font1">movie script?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This research has some proposes:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify types of figurative language used in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star</span><span class="font1"> movie script.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the meanings of figurative language in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star</span><span class="font1"> movie script.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The main data were taken from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star</span><span class="font1"> movie script, which is a 2014 American</span><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romance_film"><span class="font1"> romantic</span></a><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comedy-drama_film"><span class="font1"> comedy-drama film </span></a><span class="font1">directed by</span><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josh_Boone_(director)"><span class="font1"> Josh Boone.</span></a><span class="font1"> The data were analyzed using the qualitative method based on the theory of figurative language proposed by Knickerbocker and Reninger (1963:367) who state that figurative language is sometimes called ‘metaphorical language’. Theory of meaning proposed by Leech (1979) is also used.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Figurative Language in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star</span><span class="font1"> Movie Script</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simile</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">...she eats like a bird</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> She barely leaves the house</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> (p. 2)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The above sentence directly compared two ideas and allowed them to remain distinct in spite of similarities by employing the words “like”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> It compared the way in which she (Hazel) eats, that is, like a how bird eats.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metaphor</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I’m a grenade.</span><span class="font1"> And at some point I’m gonna blow up, and everything in my wake will</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">be obliterated and I really... (p. 65)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">“I’m a grenade” could be categorized into metaphor because the statement compares different two things; between a human and a grenade. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Grenade</span><span class="font1"> is a small bomb thrown by hand or launched mechanically. The word grenade makes similar character to the speaker that could be blown up and obliterate everything to people around her.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Personification</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">“Pain demands to be felt.” (p.32)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The above quotation belongs to personification because the word “pain” is portrayed as human being that demands to be felt. Whereas </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">pain</span><span class="font1"> is a noun, which is highly unpleasant, physical sensation caused by illness or injury and the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">demands</span><span class="font1"> is a verb that means an insistent and peremptory request.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metonymy</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">..some days she won’t even </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">get out of bed.</span><span class="font1"> (p. 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The characteristic of metonymy in the above sentence is showed by “get out of bad” because the term is used for another thing closely associated with it. This metonymic target forms part of an idealized script in culture, when someone wakes up, the first thing to do is getting out of bed.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyperbole</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Gus has </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">one eye on the road, the other on Hazel.</span><span class="font1"> He was impressed with her before.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(p. 23)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The sentence “one eye on the road, the other on Hazel” is actually an impossible thing. Human eyes are always aligned and cannot be turned toward the opposite direction simultaneously. The exaggeration in the above sentence is used to create a strong impression of an action.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">f. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Irony</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">And</span><span class="font1"> yours is - even though you had freaking cancer </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">you give money to a</span><span class="font1"> corporation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">for the chance to acquire even more cancer!? Ugh. And you were doing so well.</span><span class="font1"> (p. 19)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The above sentence belongs to irony because it is used an opposite statement between what appears to happen and what is apparently being said and the actual truth or reality. One side says that “you give money to a corporation for the chance to acquire even more cancer” as a bad perspective, and the other side is “And you were doing so well” means positive response, however, it is totally a sarcasm expression. Nonetheless, giving chance to acquire more cancer is an adverse action.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">g. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paradox</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">They don’t hurt you unless you light them.</span><span class="font1"> (They refers to cigarettes) (p. 19)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The above sentence belongs to paradox because it seems absurd, however, it’s possibly true. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Cigarette</span><span class="font1"> is a thin cylinder of finely cut tobacco</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">rolled in paper for smoking. If the cigarette is not lighted then the cigarette will not be harmful.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">h. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Allusion</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I’m a bit of a Victorian lady</span><span class="font1">, fainting-wise. (p. 25)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The sentence “I’m a bit of a Victorian lady” is kind of allusion because it shows reference to something from another piece of literature or to a famous person from history. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Victorian lady</span><span class="font1"> is epithet of hard working women and has disadvantages both financially and sexually, enduring inequalities within their marriages and social statuses, distinct differences in men and women’s rights which takes place during this era; therefore, men are provided with more stability, financial status and power over their homes and women.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meanings Represented by the Figurative language in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Star </span><span class="font1">Movie Script</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Connotative Meaning</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Gus has one eye on the road, the other on Hazel</span><span class="font1">. He was impressed with her before. (p. 23)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The meaning of the above sentence “one eye on the road, the other on Hazel” is that Augustus tries to focus with Hazel’s story and gives attention; however, the condition is that they are on the road and Augustus is driving and hazel is sitting on the passenger’s seat; therefore, Augustus should focus on looking forward even if he wants to pay attention to Hazel.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Affective Meaning</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The instructor looks at me and goes, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">your driving, while pleasant...is not technically unsafe.</span><span class="font1"> (p. 20)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The statement above shows that the instructor wants to say if Augustus’s way of driving is not safety; however, in order to say good words to Augustus, the instructor says “pleasant” first and then “unsafe”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reflected Meaning</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Hazel and the BOY we will come to know as AUGUSTUS “GUS” WATERS (17) at out door restaurant in some magical place. [</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Though we DONOTSEEHISFACE, the impression we get is that the two of them look very much the perfect Hollywood couple.]</span><span class="font1"> (p.1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Hollywood</span><span class="font1"> is literally a district in Los Angeles because it has come to be linked to the entertainment business, celebrities, and movie making. Hazel and August look very much like perfect Hollywood couple means they look very harmonious who enjoy their holiday.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Collocative Meaning</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">..some days she won’t even </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">get out of bed</span><span class="font1">. (p. 4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Get out of bed</span><span class="font1"> is one of familiar grouping of words, especially words that habitually appear together and, thereby, convey meaning by association. The meaning represented by the above sentence is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">she got offher bed of lying there for some time and won’t make friends</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Thematic Meaning</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Hazel, listen to me... </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">it’s gonna hurt like hell to lose you but</span><span class="font1"> ...you of all people know it’s possible to live with pain. (p. 118)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The statement above shows an abstract illustration to show the feelings expressed by an equation situation. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">It’s gonna hurt like hell to lose you</span><span class="font1"> means it is very painful if she has to lose her daughter symbolized with the word “hell”</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the above analysis in the movie script </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font1"> directed by Josh Boone 38 figurative languages were found; the writer found 8 kinds of figurative languages consisting of 9 similes, 3 metaphors, 2personifications, 1 metonymy, 3 ironies, 1 paradox and 4 allusions and 15 hyperboles; they were used to illustrate the strong feelings of the characters like the feeling of happiness, pain and love as the theme of this movie is one kind of dramatic movie about wishes of two lovers who want to live in a normal life without cancer. All of the figurative languages that are used in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font1"> are made to be descriptive, understandable and imaginable. Some figurative expressions in the conversation and narration were used to describe something funny, serious, or unpredictable.</span></p>
