<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Translation Techniques Used in Monster University Movie</span></h2>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract*</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">This article discussed the translation techniques that is used in the subtitle of the movie “Monster University”. This article used ten sentences that are found in the movie script of “Monster University” as the data source. Qualitative and descriptive methods were used to analyze the data. This article used the theory by Molina and Albir (2002) to find out the translation techniques used by the translator to translate the English movie script. The result of the analysis showed that there were eight translation techniques found in the data; adaptation (2 data), borrowing (1 data), established equivalent (2 data), generalization (1 data), linguistic amplification (1 data), linguistic compression (1 data), literal (1 data), and transposition (1 data).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">theory of translation techniques by Molina and Albir (2002), there are 18 kinds of translation techniques, namely:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adaptation</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Amplification</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borrowing</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">4) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calque</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">5) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compensation</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">6) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Description</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">7) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discursive Creation</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">8) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Established Equivalence</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">9) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Generalization</span></p></li>
<li>

<h3><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This study uses the theory of translation techniques proposed by Molina and Albir (2002). The 18 translation techniques that proposed by Molina and Albir (2002) are literal, generalization, discursive creation, amplification, established equivalence, calque, particularization, linguistic compression, borrowing, description, adaptation, transposition, modulation, linguistic amplification, reduction, compensation, variation, and substitution.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data for this article was taken from the movie script of Monster University movie, specifically the utterances of the main character, Mike Wazowski, and the Indonesian subtitle which were taken from the website subtitlist.com.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data is collected with the following steps:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Choosing the movie.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Buying the movie DVD.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Downloading the movie script and the Indonesian subtitle from the internet.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Watching the movie for several times.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Note down the utterances found in the movie.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">After collecting the data, it is analyzed with the following step:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finding out the techniques used in translating the source text of the movie.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Classifying the technique of translation.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font4">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Writing a conclusion.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discussion</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The discussion explains about the classification of the utterances into its translation techniques with further explanation on why the utterance is classified as the following techniques.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adaptation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">It is accomplished by simply replacing a cultural element from the target text's culture for the source text's cultural element.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 1</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “I found a </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">nickel</span><span class="font4">!”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Aku menemukan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">koin</span><span class="font4">!”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The translator is using adaptation technique for the data above. As we can see in the sentence above, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">nickel</span><span class="font4"> is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">koin</span><span class="font4"> in the target language because the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">nickel</span><span class="font4"> in Indonesian means </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">money-coin</span><span class="font4">. As it cannot be translated in the same position with the source language, the translator has made the correct decision by translating it into the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">koin</span><span class="font4">. The information above comes from a scene in which Mike is getting off a bus and claiming to have found a nickel while holding it in his hand.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 2</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Cool”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Keren”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data above is another example of adaptation, the meaning of the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">cool</span><span class="font4"> in the target language is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dingin</span><span class="font4"> if translated literally. Meanwhile, on sentence above, the word refers to something that interesting. Therefore, it is classified as an adaptation technique.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borrowing</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This technique takes word or expression directly from the SL to the TL. It could be pure or naturalized.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 3</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “How do I become a </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Scarer</span><span class="font4">?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Bagaimana aku bisa jadi </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Scarer</span><span class="font4">?”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data above is translated using the borrowing technique. The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Scarer</span><span class="font4"> is translated directly to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Scarer</span><span class="font4"> which is a name of a job on this movie. It is where the monsters are collecting children’s scream by sneaking into their room and scaring them. As a result, the term Scarer is left untranslated because translating it into the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">lebih menakutkan</span><span class="font4"> would alter the intended context. When Mike is asked a question after seeing a Scarer do their work, he says this.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Established Equivalent</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This translation technique uses a term or expression that is recognized as an equivalent in the target language (by dictionaries or the language in use) (TL).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 4</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Hey there, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">freshmen</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Hey </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mahasiswa baru</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Using the established equivalent technique, the data above is translated into the target language. As can be seen from the preceding sentence, the phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mahasiswa baru</span><span class="font4"> in Indonesian has the same meaning as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">freshmen</span><span class="font4">, and is a slang term for a first-year student at a university or high school (The Oxford Paperback Dictionary, 1988). As a result, the words </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">freshmen</span><span class="font4"> and the translation </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mahasiswa baru</span><span class="font4"> have the same meaning. Mike says the words above the first time he sets foot on Monster University.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 5</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Just wait, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">hotshot</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Tunggu saja, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">jagoan</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The data presented above is another example of the established equivalent technique. The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">hotshot</span><span class="font4"> is translated as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">jagoan</span><span class="font4"> in the target language. According to the </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">An English-Indonesian Dictionary</span><span class="font4"> (1975), the proper translation for the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">hotshot</span><span class="font4"> is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">jagoan</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font4">The word hotshot means </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tembakan panas</span><span class="font4"> if it is translated literally. It will not convey the same meaning that the source language (SL) intended.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Generalization</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Generalization is done by using a more general or neutral word in the target language (TL).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 6</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Well, everyone, I don't mean to get </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">emotional</span><span class="font4">…”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Semuanya, aku tak bermaksud membuat kalian </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sedih</span><span class="font4">…”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Using the generalization method, the data above is translated into the target language. As the generalization form of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">emotional</span><span class="font4">, the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">emotional</span><span class="font4"> is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sedih</span><span class="font4"> in the target language (TL). This sentence is spoken by Mike as he exits the bus and give his friends a farewell.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linguistic Amplification</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">When translating the source language (SL), this strategy involves adding new linguistic elements to the target language (TL).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 7</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Okay. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Good catching up</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Ya. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Pasangan yang bagus</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">catching up</span><span class="font4"> is translated as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pasangan bagus</span><span class="font4"> in the target language (TL), regardless of the fact that it means </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mengejar</span><span class="font4"> in Indonesian. Mike says this when he asks his cousin, Russel, to be his partner, but Russel has already chosen another monster as his partner.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">f. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Linguistic Compression</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This technique of translation is done by synthesize the linguistics elements in the target language (TL).</span></p>
<div>
<p><span class="font4">Data 8</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<p><span class="font4">: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Listen, it was quite delightful meeting you and whatever that is</span><span class="font4">,”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Senang bertemu denganmu</span><span class="font4">,”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The expression above is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">senang bertemu denganmu</span><span class="font4"> which literally means </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">nice to meet you.</span><span class="font4"> Although the expression in the target language has been synthesized, it still conveys the similar meaning as the source language. The above sentence is spoken by Sullivan as he enters Mike's room for the first time, mistaking it for his own.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">g. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Literal</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This technique of translation is done by literally translating SL words by words to TL.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 9</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Thanks Joe</span><span class="font4">.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Terimakasih Joe.”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The literal translation method was used to translate the phrase above. The translator is translating the sentence word by word in the results. The word thanks are translated into the Indonesian phrase </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">terima kasih</span><span class="font4">, which is the proper translation for the word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">thanks</span><span class="font4">. Mike says this sentence after he gets off the bus and thanks the bus driver.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">h. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transposition</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This translation technique involves changing a grammatical category (word class) from the source language (SL) to the target language (TL).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data 10</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Source Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Okay! First thing on my list, get registered”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Target Language &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: “Baik! Pertama, mendaftar dulu”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The transposition technique is used to translate the data above. The word </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">registered</span><span class="font4">, which is directly translatable as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">terdaftar</span><span class="font4">, is translated as </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">mendaftar dulu</span><span class="font4">. On the text above, there is a grammatical change. Mike, one of the two major protagonists, said this phrase when he first arrived to Monster University.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Novelties</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The translation strategies found in the movie script of “Monster University” movie can be used as a source of a better understanding of the translation process of a movie script into Indonesian. There are ten different data sets that have been analyzed in the previous chapter.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Based on the data analysis in the previous chapter, it can be concluded that ten different data sets were collected from the Indonesian subtitle of the movie Monster University contain eight different translation techniques. The conclusion can be described as follows using Molina and Albir's (2002) theory to analyze the translation techniques: The </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Monster University</span><span class="font4"> movie’s subtitle translator used eight different translation techniques. There are 2 data that were translated using adaptation technique; 1 data were translated using borrowing technique; 2 data were translated using established equivalent technique; 1 data were translated using generalization</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">technique; 1 data were translated using linguistic amplification technique; 1 data were translated using linguistic compression technique; 1 data were translated using literal technique and; 1 data were translated using transposition technique. The researcher hopes that those who read this journal will gain a better understanding of translation techniques. In response to the last sentence, researchers encourage people who will translate books or films to read more and understand more about the language they will be dealing with, including its structure, grammar, slang, and so on.</span></p>
