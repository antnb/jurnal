<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>*Corresponding Author: </span><a href="mailto:manikseptianiari@gmail.com"><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;">manikseptianiari@gmail.com</span></a><br><br><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Received Date: 31-05-2019 Accepted Date: 31-05-2019 Published Date: 31.-07.-2019</span></h2>
<p><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstract</span><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;">— </span><span class="font8">Various languages in the world have their own systems, especially in terms of verb classification. It can determine argument in the structure. This paper is intended to analyze the interaction between syntax and semantics in terms of the argument structure of the </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> verb in English. The main theory used in this study was the theory of argument structure proposed by Kim &amp;&nbsp;Sells (2008). The result of analysis showed that two kinds of </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> verb: </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce</span><span class="font8"> and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide </span><span class="font8">are intransitive and transitive verbs. The specifier (SPR) of the sentence structure functions as the agent; the Complement (COMPS) of the verb </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce</span><span class="font8"> serves as the patient and the COMPS of the verb </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> functions as the theme.</span></p>
<p><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords</span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">: </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Slide Verb, Argument Structure, English</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font8">Language is a system of communication in speech and writing used by people of a particular country or area (Hornby, 2010:834). According to Crystal (2003:1) English is the global language, meaning that most people use English as their language. The English signs and advertisements in a hotel or restaurant in a foreign city, for example, will be understood by people.</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">From the linguistic point of view, English can be analyzed in terms of phonology, morphology, syntax, and semantics. This paper focuses on syntax and semantics. Syntax is the study of sentence structure (Varga, 2010:56). A sentence consists of words from different word classes such as noun, verb, adjective, and adverb. Verb is one of the most important elements in a sentence, especially in English. According to Richards </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font8"> (1985:305), verb refers to an action or state. Generally, verb can be divided into two types; they are intransitive and transitive verbs (Dixon, 2010). A transitive verb is a verb which occurs in a transitive clause. Intransitive verb is a verb which occurs in an intransitive clause (Utami </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font8">. 2018). The sentence with an</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">333 e III ⅛llll</span><span class="font3">∣</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">intransitive verb only has one argument. Meanwhile, the sentence with a transitive verb has two or more arguments. Syntactically, a sentence structure has its core and noncore arguments (Masreng </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font8">. 2019).</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">Semantic roles are the ways of classifying the arguments of predicators (Kim and Sells, 2008:43). They are agent, patient, experience, theme, benefactive, source, goal, location, and instrument. These roles are used to capture the relationship between two related sentences, for example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">1a. The cat chased the mouse</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">The cat &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;chased &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the mouse</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">agent &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;patient</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">1b. The mouse was chased by the cat</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">The mouse &nbsp;was chased by &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the cat</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">patient &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;agent</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">(Kim and Sells, 2008:45)</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">The structure of sentence (1a) is different from that of sentence (1b). Sentence (1a) is an active sentence and sentence (1b) is a passive sentence proved by the preposition </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font8">. Those sentences have the same semantic roles assigned to the Noun Phrase (NP). It can be seen that the NP </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">the cat</span><span class="font8"> is the agent and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">the mouse</span><span class="font8"> is the patient. Agent is a participant that performs something. Meanwhile, patient is a participant that is being affected by what happens to it (Kim and Sells, 2008: 44). The slide verb was chosen in this study because it belongs to intransitive and transitive verbs. It consists of the verbs </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce, float, roll,</span><span class="font8"> and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> (Levin, 1955:133)</span></p>
<p><span class="font8">Based on the explanation above, this study is attempted to examine the argument structure of the slide verb in English. The data in this study were taken from a British Corpus, that is, the British National Corpus (BNC), which can be accessed from </span><a href="https://corpus.byu.edu/bnc/"><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">https://corpus.byu.edu/bnc/</span><span class="font8">.</span></a><span class="font8"> BNC consists of a 100 million word collection of written and spoken British language.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Theoretical Framework</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font8">The Theory of Argument Structure is used in this study. The theory is proposed by Kim and Sells (2008). Argument Structure (ARG-ST) has the elements realized by Specifier (SPR) and Complements (COMPS). Complement is a phrasal element in which a head must combine with or a head includes direct object, indirect object, predicative complement, and oblique complement (Kim and Sells, 2008:51). SPR is the first element which is realized by a subject and the rest are realized by COMPS.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font8">This study is qualitative descriptive research using syntax and semantic approach. The data were collected using observation method and obtained from one of many online corpuses, the British National Corpus (BNC), which can be accessed from </span><a href="https://corpus.byu.edu/bnc/"><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">https://corpus.byu.edu/bnc/</span><span class="font8">.</span></a><span class="font8"> This corpus contains 100 million words especially in British English language. This corpus is free to access. This study focuses on types of </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> verb such as </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce, float, roll,</span><span class="font8"> and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> (Levin , 1955). The data were classified based on the types of verb in order to know the argument structure in a sentence. The theory proposed by Kim and Sells (2008) was used to analyze the data.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font8" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font8">Based on the explanation above, it can be concluded that </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide verbs</span><span class="font8"> belong to intransitive and transitive verbs. The verbs </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce</span><span class="font8"> and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> can be used as intransitive and transitive verbs. Meanwhile, the verbs </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">float</span><span class="font8"> and </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">roll</span><span class="font8"> are used as intransitive verbs. Furthermore, SPR in those sentences functions as the agent. The COMPS of the verb </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">bounce</span><span class="font8"> serve as the patient and the COMPS of the sentence structure with the verb </span><span class="font8" style="font-style:italic;">slide</span><span class="font8"> serve as the theme.</span></p>
