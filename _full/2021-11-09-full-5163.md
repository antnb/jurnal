<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ENGLISH PRESENT AND PAST PARTICIPLES IN PSALM OF BIBLICAL TEXT AND THEIR TRANSLATIONS IN INDONESIAN</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Enda Dwi Karina P.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">English Department, Faculty of Letters Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Tujuan utama dari penelitian ini adalah menjawab ketiga rumusan permasalahan, yakni: (1) Apakah bentuk present dan past participle yang ditemukan dalam ‘psalm’ Holy Bible? (2) Apakah fungsi present dan past participle tersebut dalam kalimat yang dikutip dari ‘psalm’ Holy Bible? (3) Translation shift apakah yang muncul dalam proses penerjemahan present dan past participle ke dalam bahasa Indonesia?</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Data diperoleh dari ‘psalm’ Holy Bible NIV dan ‘Mazmur’ Alkitab dengan menggunakan metode dokumentasi. Data yang terkumpul kemudian dianalisis secara deskriptif berdasarkan desain kualitatif dengan menerapkan teori Quirk dan Catford.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil analisis data menunjukkan bahwa present participle dapat diidentifikasikan sebagai bentuk –ing dari kata kerja dasar dan past participle ditandai dengan akhiran –d, -ed, -ied, dan –n pada kata kerja beraturan. Present participle dapat berfungsi sebagai kata sifat, kata keterangan, pelengkap, dan kata kerja. Sedangkan past participle dapat berfungsi sebagai kata kerja, khususnya untuk tensis perfect (have+ past participle), kalimat pasif (be + past participle) dan klausa non-finite –ed participle; dan juga sebagai kata sifat. Selain itu, dalam menerjemahkan present dan past participle ke dalam bahasa Indonesia perubahan bentuk tak dapat dihindari. Perubahan tersebut merupakan translation shifts yang dikategorikan sebagai level shift dan category shift. Category shift dapat diklasifikasikan menjadi structure shift, class shift, unit shift, dan intra-system shift.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: terjemahan, present participle, past participle, translation shifts</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Introduction</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Sometimes the translators find difficulties in determining the translation of present and past participle in a sentence. The difference in function is also characterized by the different meanings. Present participles can serve as adjective, adverb, object complement, and verb. Past participle, on the other hand, has functions as verb and adjective. Therefore, it is important to analyze the grammatical system of the English participles to discover their proper translation in Indonesian Language. This study was concerned with the forms and functions of English present and past participles in constructing sentences found in chapter </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Holy Bible and also their translation in Indonesian.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The challenging problem in translation from English into Indonesian is grammatical structure. It is because English and Indonesian have different</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">structure, for instance the structure deals with present and past participles. English has grammatical structure of present and past participles, while Indonesian language has no both present and past participles. The translation of English present and past participle into Indonesian can change the structure of the sentences, the element of the sentences, or can change the position of that element. This phenomenon is commonly known as shift.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Problems of the Study</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">From the ideas presented in the background of the study above, there are three problems analyzed in this study, those are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of form of English present and past participles are found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psalm</span><span class="font1"> of biblical text?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the functions of English present and past participle found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Biblical text?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What translation shifts are applied in the process of translating English present and past participle into Indonesian?</span></p></li></ul>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>Aims of the Study</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the formulation of problems above, this study is investigated in order to meet the following purposes:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify forms of English present and past participles occurring in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Biblical text.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the functions of English present and past participle in the sentences found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Biblical text.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe types of translation shifts applied in the process of translating English present and past participle into Indonesian.</span></p></li></ul>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>Scope of Discussion</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Due to the massiveness and complexity of the topic, the present study confined and focused its investigation on the form and functions of English present and past participle found in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Biblical text. Furthermore,</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">translation shifts were discussed since it was also applied in the process of translating English present and past participle into Indonesian.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Research Method</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">The method of this research includes three parts: data source, method and technique of collecting data, and method and technique of analyzing data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were gained from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Psalm</span><span class="font1"> in ‘The Holy Bible, New International Version’, published by International Bible Society, East Brunswick, New Jersey (1978), as the Source Language and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Mazmur</span><span class="font1"> in ‘Alkitab’ published by </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lembaga Alkitab Indonesia</span><span class="font1">, Jakarta (28<sup>th</sup> edition, 2007), as the Target Language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were collected through library research by documentation method and techniques of close reading and note-taking.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The collected data were analysed through descriptive qualitative method. In this case, theories of Quirk (1985) are applied to answer the first and second research problems, and theory of Catford (1965) is applied to answer the third problem.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Analysis</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">After the data were collected, they are tabulated and classified into some groups according to their kinds and functions. The tabulated data were analyzed by considering their meanings in Indonesian language so as to verify and justify their function in the sentences. Furthermore, the tabulated data were analyzed in terms of the occurrence of translation shifts based on the theory of translation shift as proposed by Catford (1965).</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>Forms of Present and Past Participles</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Form of Present Participles</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Basically, present participle is formed by adding suffix –</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ing</span><span class="font1"> to the base form of a verb. Quirk (1985) states that the present participle is often referred to</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">the –</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ing</span><span class="font1"> form of the verb. For example: Many are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">asking</span><span class="font1">, &quot;Who can show us any good?&quot; (Psalm 4. 6: 401). The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">asking</span><span class="font1"> is a kind of present participle which is derived from the verb base </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ask</span><span class="font1"> by adding suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ing.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Forms of Past Participles</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Mostly, the past participle is the same as the past tense and is created by adding a </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">d</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ed</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">-</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ied</span><span class="font1"> to the regular verb stem. However, the past participles of irregular verbs do not follow any pattern. For example: He is like a tree </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">planted </span><span class="font1">by streams of water. (Psalm 1. 3: 400). The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">planted</span><span class="font1"> is a kind of past participle which is formed by adding suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ed</span><span class="font1"> to the verb base </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">plant</span><span class="font1">. The following example shows the form of past participle in irregular patter: The Lord is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">known</span><span class="font1"> by his justice (Psalm 9.16: 403). Different from the first example, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">known</span><span class="font1"> is conjugated irregularly from the verb stem </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">know</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>Functions of Present and Past Participles</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Functions of Present Participles</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Present Participle as Adjective</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: A </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">scorching</span><span class="font1"> wind will be their lot. (Psalm 11. 6: 404)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Angin </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang menghanguskan</span><span class="font1">, itulah isi piala mereka. (Mazmur 11. 6: 584) Analysis: The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">scorching</span><span class="font1"> fills the grammatical function as the adjective explaining the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wind</span><span class="font1">. It serves as pre-modifier describing the head noun.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Present Participle as Adverb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: ..they come </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">trembling</span><span class="font1"> from their strongholds. (Psalm 18. 45: 407)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: dan keluar dari kota kubunya </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dengan gemetar</span><span class="font1">. (Mazmur 18. 46:589)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">trembling</span><span class="font1"> is a kind of present participle functioning adverb of manner. Notice how the present participle is preceded with “by” to give the same meaning (They come </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">trembling</span><span class="font1"> from their strongholds → They come (by) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">trembling</span><span class="font1"> from their strongholds).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Present Participle as Object Complement</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: I have seen a wicked and ruthless man </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">flourishing</span><span class="font1"> … (Psalm 37. 35: 417)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Aku melihat seorang fasik yang gagah sombong, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang tumbuh mekar</span><span class="font1"> ... (Mazmur 37. 35: 603)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The present participle indicates an object complement since it comes after the object of verb of sensation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">see</span><span class="font1">. In this case, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I</span><span class="font1"> is the subject of the sentence which possesses verb of sensation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">see</span><span class="font1"> and object of verb of sensation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a wicked and ruthless man</span><span class="font1">. The present participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">flourishing</span><span class="font1"> completes the sense of the object </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ruthless man</span><span class="font1">. In that sentence, the present participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">flourishing</span><span class="font1"> is what </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the ruthless man</span><span class="font1"> does.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Present Participle as Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: Many are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">saying</span><span class="font1"> of me, (Psalm 3. 2: 401)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Banyak orang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang berkata</span><span class="font1"> tentang aku, (Mazmur 3. 2: 580)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">saying</span><span class="font1"> represents present participle functioned as a main verb. Grammatically, the present participle was used as a finite verb performing present continuous tense which has basic form Subject + to be (is/am/are) + present participle</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Functions of Past Participles</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Past Participle as Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: I have </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">installed</span><span class="font1"> my King. (Psalm 2. 6: 401)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Akulah yang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">telah melantik</span><span class="font1"> raja-Ku (Mazmur 2. 6: 579)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The identified past participles </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">installed</span><span class="font1"> on the first example can be classified as the main verb. The participial verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">have installed</span><span class="font1"> indicates that the sentence explicitly contains present perfect tense. Coupled with the auxiliary verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">have,</span><span class="font1"> the past participle is used to express perfect tense.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Past Participle as Adjective</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL: it is like a </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sharpened</span><span class="font1"> razor (Psalm 52. 2: 425)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: lidahmu seperti pisau cukur </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang diasah</span><span class="font1"> (Mazmur 52. 4: 613)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The past participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sharpened</span><span class="font1"> functions as a means of expanding ideas and creating textual unity and cohesion in elaborating and describing the noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">razor</span><span class="font1">. Notice if the noun phrase is added by relative pronoun and auxiliary verb (a </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sharpened</span><span class="font1"> razor → a razor </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">which is</span><span class="font1"> </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sharpened</span><span class="font1">).</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>Translation Shifts</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Translation Shift means departures from formal correspondence in the process of going from the source language to the target language (Catford. 1965).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Level Shift</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The commonest level shift in translation is shift from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">grammar</span><span class="font1"> to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lexis</span><span class="font1"> and vice-versa, especially shifts on the surface structure. For Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: A band of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">destroy</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">ing</span><span class="font1"> angels. (Psalm 78. 49: 438)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Suatu pasukan malaikat </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">yang membawa malapetaka</span><span class="font1">; (Mazmur 78. 49: 632) Analysis: The example shows that SL grammatical item can exist in the TL form as a compound of words. The present participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">destroying</span><span class="font1"> was translated into Indonesian Language as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang membawa malapetaka.</span><span class="font1"> The compound words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang membawa malapetaka</span><span class="font1"> is clearly the full semantic equivalent translation of the present participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">destroying.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Category Shift</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Category shift is departures from formal correspondence in translation. It can be categorized into four types; those are structure shifts, class shifts, unit shifts, and intra-system shifts.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Structure Shift</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Structure shifts involve a grammatical change between the structures of both the Source Language and the Target Language. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: Let their </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">lying lips</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">be silenced… (Psalm 31: 18, page 413)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">M H</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/5163-1.png" alt="" style="width:28pt;height:28pt;">
<p><span class="font1">TL: Biarlah </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">bibir dusta</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">menjadi kelu... (Mazmur 31: 19, page 597) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">H M</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The example above is occurrence of structure shift at word group level. Those sequences of both elements are different, in the Source Language; the present participle modifier (M) precedes the head noun (H). Conversely in the Target Language the head noun (H) precedes the modifier (M).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Class Shift</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Class shift occurs when the translation equivalent of a SL item is a member of a different class from the original item. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: repay them for what their hands have </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">done</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">(Psalm 28. 6: 412)</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>V</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">TL: ganjarilah mereka setimpal dengan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">perbuatan</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">tangan mereka. (Mazmur 28.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">6: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;595) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">N</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: Viewed from example above, it can be seen that there is a class shift occurring in the process of translating English past participles. The translation equivalent of English </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">verb</span><span class="font1"> ‘done’ is the Indonesian </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">noun</span><span class="font1"> ‘perbuatan’. The translator considered that the noun is the most appropriate word class to represent the meaning of the past participle.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unit Shift</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">A unit shift occurs when translation equivalent of a unit at one rank in the SL is a unit at a different rank in the TL. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: They behaved like men </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">wielding</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">axes to cut through..(Psalm 74. 5: 435)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Kelihatannya seperti orang </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">mengayunkan tinggi-tinggi</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">sebuah kapak kepada..(Mazmur 74. 5: 628)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: The translation equivalent of the present participle </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wielding</span><span class="font1"> is a group of words or phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mengayunkan tinggi-tinggi</span><span class="font1"> which contains a verb and a reduplicated word</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> This strategy comprises a change at the level of the phrase, that is, modification in the adverbial phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Intra System Shift</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Intra-system shift occurs when two languages have systems that correspond formally, but where the formally equivalent terms in the systems do not function in the same context. For Example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL: I will not fear the tens of thousands </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">drawn up</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">against me on every side.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Psalm 3. 6: 401)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL: Aku tidak takut kepada puluhan ribu orang yang siap </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">mengepung</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> </span><span class="font1">aku. (Mazmur 3. 6: 580)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis: In these structures, it can be seen that there are shifts which occur internally within the system. In this case, the shifts occur in grammatical point of view, specifically in passive-active construction. The passive construction in SL has translation equivalent active construction in TL.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>Conclusion</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Firstly, there are many English participles, both in present and past forms which can be identified in SL, from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Psalm</span><span class="font1"> of Biblical text. Present participles are marked by the attachment of suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ing</span><span class="font1"> to the verb base. Meanwhile, past</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">participles which in regular pattern, are marked by suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–d, -ed, -ied,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–n</span><span class="font1">, also in various other ways for irregular verbs</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Secondly, English present and past participle have various functions in the view functional aspect. Present participle can be used as an adjective, adverb, complement and main verb (to form continuous tenses). Past participle can function as a verb, specifically to form perfect tense (</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">have</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">past participle</span><span class="font1">), passive voice (</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">be</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">past</span><span class="font1"> participle) and a non-finite </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ed</span><span class="font1"> participle clauses; and also as an adjective.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Thirdly, in the process of translating English present and past participle into Indonesian, shifts are tangibly inevitable since both of those languages possess different cultural features and values.</span></p>
