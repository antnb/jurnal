<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">BORROWING IN </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">DIARY OF A WIMPY KID</span></p><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>AND ITS TRANSLATION INTO </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">DIARY SI BOCAH TENGIL</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Junita RS</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">English Departement, Faculty of Letters</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini bertujuan untuk menjawab permasalahan utama, yakni tentang tipe peminjaman apa saja yang ditemukan pada novel ‘Diary of A Wimpy Kid’ dan terjemahannya dalam ‘Si Bocah Tengil’.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Data diperolah dari novel ‘Diary of A Wimpy Kid’ karya Jeff Kinney dan terjemahannya ‘Si Bocah Tengil’ karya Ferri Halim menggunakan metode dokumentasi dan dianalisis dengan deskriptif kualitatif menggunakan gabungan teori Molina &amp;&nbsp;Albir (2002) dan Larson (1984) yang disesuaikan dengan Tata Baku Bahasa Indonesia dan Pedoman Umum Pembentukan Istilah dari Pusat Bahasa Departemen Pendidikan Nasional.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil analisis data menunjukkan bahwa, ditemukan tiga tipe peminjaman, yakni; (1) peminjaman murni, (2)peminjaman alamiah, dan (3) peminjaman budaya yang sudah desesuaikan dengan kaidah dan norma bahasa target.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: terjemahan, peminjaman, teknik peminjaman</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Larson (1984; 3) stated that translation is transferring the meaning of the source language into the receptor language. This is done by transferring form of the first language to the form of the second language using of semantic structure. It is meaning which is being transferred and must be held constant and the form may change. But translation activities do not only deal with transferring language from one language to another language, but also deal with the most important thing, such as; message and meaning from source language to target language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As we know, each language has vocabulary and variation of words based on society experience and the development of the culture where that language grows and develops and sometimes the speakers may have to refer to some unfamiliar object or</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">concept for which they have no word in their own language. To interpret or express the right expression from source language into target language, borrowing method is needed to deal with it.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Related to the background of the study above, the problems of the study are formulated as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of borrowing are found in the translation of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid </span><span class="font1">into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary si Bocah Tengil</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How is the borrowing technique applied in the translation of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid</span><span class="font1"> into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary Si Bocah Tengil?</span></p></li>
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the problems formulated above, the aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the types of borrowing in the translation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid </span><span class="font1">into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary si Bocah Tengil.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe the process of borrowing applied to the word elements of the source language (English) into the target language (Indonesian) in the translation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid</span><span class="font1"> into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary si Bocah Tengil.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scope of Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In order to make the research more specific, the scope of the discussion of this study is focused on the words or phrases which convey meaning of borrowing in the translation of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid</span><span class="font1"> into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary si Bocah Tengil.</span><span class="font1"> The analysis covered the types of borrowing in order to get the result of the analysis.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The method of this research includes three parts: data source, method and technique of collecting data, and method and technique of analyzing data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were taken from the novel entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary of A Wimpy Kid</span><span class="font1"> by Jeff Kinney (2007) published by Amulet Books in New York and its translation entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Diary si Bocah Tengil</span><span class="font1"> by Ferry Halim (2009) published by Atria in Jakarta. The English version consists of 217 pages meanwhile the Indonesian version consists of 216 pages.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and technique of collecting data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were collected through library research by documentation method and techniques of close reading and note-taking.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and technique of analyzing data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The collected data were analysed through descriptive qualitative method using combined theories proposed Molina &amp;&nbsp;Albir (2002) and Larson (1984), and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Tata Baku Bahasa Indonesia dan Pedoman Umum Pembentukan Istilah</span><span class="font1"> from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pusat Bahasa Departemen Pendidikan Nasional.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The analysis below is based on some theories of some linguistics; the types of borrowing proposed Molina &amp;&nbsp;Albir (2002) and Larson (1984) and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pedoman Umum</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pembentukan Istilah</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pedoman Umum Ejaan Bahasa Indonesia yang Disempurnakan</span><span class="font1"> from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pusat Bahasa Departemen Pendidikan Nasional</span><span class="font1"> as the standard integration of absorbing word elements in Indonesian system.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>Types of Borrowing</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pure borrowing</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Pure borrowing is taking the full absorption of the word from source language without any modification of the spelling and/or pronunciation. For example:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Today, I probably would have gone up to Rowley’s on my own anyway, because my brother Rodrick and his </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">band </span><span class="font1">were practising down in the basement. (Kenny, 2007:29)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Hari ini aku mungnkin akan pergi ke rumah Rowley atas keinginan sendiri karena kakakku, Rodrick, bersama </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">band </span><span class="font1">nya sedang berlatih di ruang bawah tanah, (Halim, (2009:29)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">band</span><span class="font1"> in the source language text was translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">band</span><span class="font1"> in target language, totally the same as the SL spelling system and sound. As we can see the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">band</span><span class="font1"> means a group of musicians who play popular music (Oxford, 2000). It was absorbed into TL as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">band</span><span class="font1"> meaning orkes (KBBI, 2008). This technique was used by the translator because the readers themselves have understood the meaning of the word in the target language and the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">band</span><span class="font1"> more suitable then the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">orkes</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Naturalized borrowing</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Naturalized borrowing is transferring the source language by adapting from a source language (SL) word to the phonetic and morphological norm of the target language (TL).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">Example 1:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : If there’s one thing most boys in my school are into, it’s </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">professional </span><span class="font1">wrestling. (Kenney, 2007:77)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Kalau ada satu hal yang digemari sebagian besar anak laki laki di sekolahku, hal itu adalah gulat </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">profesional</span><span class="font1">. (Halim, 2009: 77)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">professiona</span><span class="font1">l in the SL text was translated into the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">professional </span><span class="font1">in the TL text by modifying the spelling system. Consequently, the adjustment occurs by changing double consonant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ss</span><span class="font1"> into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">s</span><span class="font1"> in the TL. This word is shared in Indonesian system and has been familiar to the readers as the kind of absorbing process.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">Example 2:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Mom picked out a red </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">wool </span><span class="font1">sweater for our Giving Tree guy. (Kenney, 2007:119)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Mom memilihkan sehelai sweter berbahan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">wol </span><span class="font1">merah untuk salah seorang peserta penerima Pohon Rezeki. (Halim, 2009:119)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wool</span><span class="font1"> in the source language text was translated into the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wol</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. Consequently, the adjustment occurs by changing the combination of double vowel </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">oo</span><span class="font1"> from source language into the pronunciation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">o</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. This translation is acceptable in Indonesian system and has been common to in the RL readers.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">Example 3:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Manny was circling every single toy in the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">catalogue. </span><span class="font1">(Kenney, 2007:115)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Manny melingkari setiap mainan dalam </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">katalog. </span><span class="font1">(Halim, 2009:115)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the example above, the translator used a style of borrowing by modifying the spelling in which the consonant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">c</span><span class="font1"> in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">catalogue</span><span class="font1"> was changed into the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">pronunciation </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">k</span><span class="font1"> in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">katalog</span><span class="font1"> and the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–ue</span><span class="font1"> was omitted. In the target language word above, the procedure applied is borrowing.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">Example 4:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Sure enough, Rowley totally lost his </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">concentration</span><span class="font1">. (Kenney, 2007:92)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Tentu saja, Rowley benar benar kehilangan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">konsentrasi</span><span class="font1">nya. (Halim, 2009:92)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In this case, the translator translated the word by modifying the spelling system, the adjustment occurs by changing the consonant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">c</span><span class="font1"> before </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a, u, o</span><span class="font1"> which changes into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">k</span><span class="font1"> in the target language, and the suffix </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(t)-ion</span><span class="font1"> becomes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">–si</span><span class="font1"> in the target language (PUEBIYD, 2009).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">Example 5:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : People started coming up with these crazy </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">theories </span><span class="font1">about what happen to it. (Kenney, 2007:213)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Orang orang mulai menciptakan berbagai </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">teori </span><span class="font1">gila tentang apa yang terjadi pada keju. (Halim, 2009:212)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Therefore, the TL takes the sound and modifies the spelling system by adjusting the TL word. The example above showed that the consonant </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">th</span><span class="font1"> was changed into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">t.</span><span class="font1"> In the example above the procedure applied is borrowing. The same case is also found in theory which was translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">teori.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cultural borrowing</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Cultural borrowing is the borrowing when the words of source language are translated using the words in target language indicating things or event, which is not</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">exactly the same but occurs in the target language because of lexical equivalent in the target language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">SL : We cheerleaders are tired of riding to games in the same bus as the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">nerds </span><span class="font1">in the band. (Kenney, 2007:44)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : Kami para pemandu sorak sudah capek naik bis yang sama dengan anggota kelompok music yang </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">norak </span><span class="font1">ke tempat pertandingan. (halim, 2009:44)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nerd</span><span class="font1"> in the source language becomes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">norak</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nerd</span><span class="font1"> means a person who is boring, stupid, and not fashionable (Oxford, 2000) and it is not known in Indoneasia. The culture substituted the word using the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">norak</span><span class="font1"> which means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sangat berlebih-lebihan; kurang serasi (tt dandanan dsb); kampungan</span><span class="font1"> because both words have the same meaning</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font1"> so it can be understood by the readers.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>7. Conclusion</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">There were three types of borrowing found in the data, those are; (1) taking the full absorption of the word from SL without any modification of the spelling and/or pronunciation (pure borrowing), (2 transferring SL to TL with modification of the spelling and/or pronunciation based on norms of the target language (naturalized borrowing), (3) translating the source language into the target language using the words of target language for things or event, which are not exactly the same but occurs in the target language because the lexical equivalent is not available in the target language (cultural borrowing).</span></p>
