<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">THE ANALYSIS OF PREPOSITION IN THE NOVEL “THE PERKS OF BEING A</span></p><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>WALLFLOWER” BY STEPHEN CHBOSKY</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0">I Kadek Yudi Adi Saputra</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">English Department-Non Regular Program, Faculty of Letter and Culture,</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;">2014</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Makalah ini berjudul The Analysis of Preposition in the Novel “The Perks of Being A Wallflower”. Makalah ini bertujuan untuk menganalisa fungsi sintaksis dari kata depan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">at</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">in</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">on</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">from</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">for</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;"> serta makna dari kata depan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">at</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">in</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">on</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">from</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">for</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Semua data merupakan kutipan kalimat-kalimat dari novel The Perks of Being A Wallflower yang digolongkan berdasarkan jenis-jenis kata depan yang ditentukan serta dikembangkan menurut teori yang diterapkan dalam makalah ini.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Ada dua teori grammar yang diaplikasikan untuk menganalisa data, berdasarkan teori pokok dari Randolph Quirk, et. al. (1985) serta berdasarkan teori dari George Yule (1998) sebagai teori pendukung untuk menganalisa makna kata depan tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil dari analisa makalah ini yaitu ditemukannya fungsi sintaksis dari frase kata depan yang diawali </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">at, in, on, from,</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;"> dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">for</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;"> sebagai kata tambahan (adjunct, disjunct, and conjunct), tapi hampir semua frase kata depan sebagai adjunct yang menentukan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">place adjunct</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">time adjunct</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">source adjunct</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">target adjunct</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">. Beberapa frase kata depan juga sebagai pengubah frase benda dan pelengkap. Berdasarkan makna, kata depan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">at, in</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">on</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">from</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">for </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">menunjukkan tempat, waktu, sebab-akibat, dan lokasi dalam kiasan/metafora. Kata depan yang menunjukkan tempat dapat menentukan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">point in space with no dimensions</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">line or surface with one or two dimensions,</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;"> dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">area or volume with two or three dimensions</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">. Serta kata depan yang menunjukkan waktu dapat menentukan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">point of time</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;"> dan </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">period of time</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Kata depan, fungsi sintaksis, dan makna.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">In Indonesia, English has been taught since junior high school even from elementary school up to universities. One of the problems in learning English is about how discriminates situating of prepositions. Prepositions are quite short and significant looking, but they have very important functions. Preposition is one of the language devices that build up a sentence. In English this component is called a connective word or function word, and also part of speech. Preposition expresses a relation between two entities, one being that represented by the prepositional complement (Quirk, et. al, 1985). The prepositions are normally placed before noun or pronouns, but the position itself could change depend on their functions.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Prepositions are classified into two. They are simple prepositions and complex prepositions. Simple prepositions are prepositions, which consist of one word such as</span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">: at, in, on, for, from, to,</span><span class="font0"> etc. And complex prepositions consisting of more than one word such as: </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">aboard, about, because of, against, behind below, </span><span class="font0">etc.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Based on the background above, the problems discussed in this paper are formulated as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the syntactic functions of prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from, for </span><span class="font0">found in the novel?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from, for</span><span class="font0"> found in the novel?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the syntactic functions of prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from, for</span><span class="font0"> found in the novel.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font0">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the meanings of prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from, for</span><span class="font0"> found in the novel.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis and Result</span></h1>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font0">4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> is denoting place</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">It can be proved by the explanation in example as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">a) My brother is busy being a football player </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">at Penn State</span><span class="font0">. (Chbosky, 2012:8)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Prepositional phrase which appears in sentence (a) is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Penn State. </span><span class="font0">Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Penn State</span><span class="font0"> is constructed by preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> and adverb of place </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Penn State</span><span class="font0"> which has a function as position adjunct, which can be evoked as a response to a </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">where</span><span class="font0"> question: “Where was Charlie’s brother being a football player?” and the answer is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Penn State.</span><span class="font0"> In sentence (a), prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Penn State</span><span class="font0"> is denoting place especially positive position because it determines the position where Charlie’s brother was being a football player, and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at Penn State</span><span class="font0"> as adverbial of place. Preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at</span><span class="font0"> is used because the noun </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Penn State</span><span class="font0"> is viewed as a place which has no dimension and it shows a mere point of location.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0"> is denoting time</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">It can be proved by the explanation in example as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">b) </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">In middle school</span><span class="font0" style="text-decoration:underline;">,</span><span class="font0"> Susan was very fun to be around. (Chbosky, 2012:6)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The prepositional phrase in sentence (b) is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in middle school</span><span class="font0">. Prepositional phase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in middle school</span><span class="font0"> is constructed by preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in</span><span class="font0"> and adverb of noun </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">middle school</span><span class="font0">. The function of prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in middle school</span><span class="font0"> as adjunct of time because it describes the period of time when Susan was very fun in school around. Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in middle school</span><span class="font0"> is denoting of time because it associates with an event. Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">in middle school</span><span class="font0"> expressed the context situation when Susan began the study process in English class, then she was very fun.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> is denoting goal</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">It can be explained in the example as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, I get perfect scores </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">on all my tests</span><span class="font0">. (Chbosky, 2012:165)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The prepositional phrase found in sentence (c) is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on all my tests</span><span class="font0">. Prepositional phase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on all my tests</span><span class="font0"> is constructed by preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on</span><span class="font0"> and noun </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">all my tests</span><span class="font0">. The function of prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on all my tests </span><span class="font0">serves as post modifier of noun phrase. This prepositional phrase is modifying Charlie (I) as the head of the sentence who has all tests. And this prepositional phrase indicates the meaning of goal. The meaning of prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on all my tests</span><span class="font0"> in sentence (c) is that she had got perfect scores </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">on all my tests</span><span class="font0">. </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">All my tests</span><span class="font0"> are viewed as the noun which has two dimensions as a surface.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">4.4 Prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from</span><span class="font0"> is denoting source</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">It can be explained in the example as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">d) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I watched the end of the game when Dave caught a touchdown thrown </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">from Brad</span><span class="font0">. (Chbosky, 2012:32)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">The prepositional phrase in sentence (d) is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from Brad</span><span class="font0">. Prepositional phase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from Brad</span><span class="font0"> is constructed by preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from</span><span class="font0"> and noun </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Brad</span><span class="font0">. The function of the prepositional phrase is as source adjunct, it can be proved by using question like “from whom did a touchdown thrown?” the answer is </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Brad. </span><span class="font0">Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">from Brad</span><span class="font0"> is denoting source. The meaning of prepositional phrase in sentence (d) is that the source of a touchdown thrown was coming.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">4.5 Preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font0"> is denoting recipient</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">It can be proved by the explanation in example as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font0">e) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I like eating with my fingers and off napkins, but my sister says that doing so is bad </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">for the environment</span><span class="font0">. (Chbosky, 2012:10)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">In sentence (e), preposition </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font0"> that appears in the prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for the environment</span><span class="font0"> is constructed by adverb of place </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">the environment</span><span class="font0">. Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for the environment</span><span class="font0"> serves as complementation of an adjective because the preceding words of prepositional phrase is a word bad as an adjective, and prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for the environment</span><span class="font0"> has a function to complete the structure. Prepositional phrase </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for the environment</span><span class="font0"> is denoting recipient. The meaning of sentence (e) is that the environment as the recipient from her bad behavior impact.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font0">Based on the classified and analysis of prepositional phrase in the previous chapter, the conclusions can be made as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">The syntactic function that prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from,</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font0"> which appears in prepositional phrases found in the sentences serves as adverbial (adjunct), complementation, and post-modifier in noun phrase. As adjunct, the preposition relates the location or the time of an event or a happening, they are position adjunct, time when adjunct and time duration adjunct, indicating purpose and target adjuncts. As post-modifier, that the prepositional phrase modifies a certain noun is by placing it after the Head noun and has the meaning of “having”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Prepositions </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">at, in, on, from,</span><span class="font0"> and </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">for</span><span class="font0"> which appear in prepositional phrases can denote place, time, and cause-purpose. The meaning of each preposition is based on the noun phrase that follows the preposition in forming prepositional phrase. If the prepositional phrase serves as adverbial of place, so the preposition will indicate place. Prepositional phrase serves as adverbial of time, so the preposition will indicate time. Prepositional phrase which means the causepurpose is related to the goal or target of the subject of the sentence. Prepositions that are denoting place can indicate point in space with no dimensions, line or surface with one or two dimensions, and area or volume with two or three dimensions. And prepositions that are denoting time can imply point of time and period of time.</span></p>
