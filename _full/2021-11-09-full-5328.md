<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>LEXICAL CHOICES OF POLITICAL NEWS HEADLINE</span><br><br><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark2"></a>IN THE JAKARTA POST CYBER-NEWS</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Ni Putu Ayu Kartika Sari Dewi</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Headline (judul berita) merupakan hal yang pertama dilihat oleh pembaca ketika membaca sebuah berita. Pilihan kata yang menarik pada judul berita diharapkan mampu menarik minat pembaca untuk melanjutkan membaca keseluruhan isi berita. Berdasarkan teori yang dikemukanan oleh M.A.K Halliday ‘Cohesion in English’ (1995), pilihan kata yang singkat, padat serta menarik perhatian sering kali digunakan pada judul berita. Pilihan kata tersebut tidaklah semata-mata untuk menarik perhatian pembaca, namun memiliki makna yang merefleksikan keseluruhan isi dari berita tersebut. Pada tulisan ini dapat ditemukan bahwa pilihan kata yang digunakan dalam judul berita bersifat unik. Hal tersebut dibuktikan dengan adanya penyingkatan nama maupun penggunaan istilah yang merujuk pada julukan seseorang. Selain itu, hubungan antara judul dan isi berita (lead) bersifat lexical cohesion.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: Pilihan kata, judul berita, kohesi</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of The Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The news is one of the press media which has means of communication. It influences in creation of new words and expression in the language used. Many new words, especially slang and acronyms, are used by society because they are read in the news and then used, though sometimes people do not really know the exact meaning of the new expressions.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In reporting the news, journalists are free to use words and expressions, language style and linguistic structure. It is quite hard to understand the news headline, due to the fact that they are displayed in such a ‘different’ construction of English language. Many reductions occur in terms of grammar and the choice of special words. The aim of such a noticeable construction is to attract people to read the news.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">As one of news component to attract reader’s attention, Headline must be arranged in short, attractive and can reflect the summary of the news. In this case the interrelatedness between headline and lead of news is looked by means of cohesion.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Halliday and Hasan (1995) Cohesion divides into two types, those are grammatical cohesion and lexical cohesion. Each item of grammatical and lexical cohesion called as the cohesive devices. The cohesive devices act as a signal for giving the interrelatedness between the sentences.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Related to this study, it is focus on one aspect of cohesion that is lexical cohesion. This topic is chosen because of the phenomenon of the headline that arranged from any uniqueness lexical are choice to attract reader’s attention.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of The Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Referring to the background above, the research problems can be formulated as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How are the lexical items presented in news headline?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is the relation between the news headline and lead of the news?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of The Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Every scientific paper has scientific aims, in this case to activate knowledge in English language. The purposes of this paper are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To know the lexical items which are presented in news headline in the English language newspaper The Jakarta Post.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To investigate the relation between news headline and the lead of the news.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Research method the main requirement in research; it is about what data will be collected and how they are analyzed. It consists of three elements; data source; data collection and data analysis.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The English language data sources used in this study were online news media items on </span><a href="http://www.thejakartapost.com"><span class="font3">www.thejakartapost.com</span></a><span class="font3">, Focused on the political news topic that Rhoma Irama (‘dangdut’ singer) was running for president in the 2014 election.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data in this research were collected by purposive sampling, in order to get political headlines to be used as data. All selected were read carefully, lexical choices used in news headline was found and were identified the relation between the news headline and the lead of the news.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The data found through data collections were analyzed qualitatively and descriptively based on the theory of Cohesion in English proposed by M.A.K Halliday and Ruqaiya Hasan (1995) and also supported by the theories proposed by Nunan (1993). The collected data were analyzed based on the lexical choice occurring in headlines. Then, data analysis was presented descriptively to describe lexical cohesion and grammatical cohesion that occurred in the relation between the headline and lead of the news.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Lexical Choices in Political News Headlines in The Jakarta Post Cyber-news</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lexical Choices</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1.</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;‘Dangdut’ king ready for presidency</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">(</span><a href="http://www.thejakartapost.com"><span class="font3">www.thejakartapost.com</span></a><span class="font3">, Wednesday, November 14 2012, 9:35 AM)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The headline of news has a different structure from the Standard English. News headlines are often written in a special style, which is different from ordinary English. In headline (1) above, the writer used quotation marks on the word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">dangdut</span><span class="font3">, because that word does not have a suitable match in English, as the word has a special meaning. The writer used quotation marks because he wanted to show the singers character as a </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">dangdut</span><span class="font3"> singer’s in Indonesia. The quotation marks were used to emphasize the famous figure and that he was well known as the ‘king of dangdut’. The phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">dangdut king</span><span class="font3"> was used because Rhoma Irama has been more popular as a singer than as the politician. Lexical choice used in this headline was a part of making or creating an image of someone in the public eyes.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Headlines often leave out articles and it is reflected in the headline (1) </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">‘Dangdut’ king ready for presidency</span><span class="font3">. This headline could be changed into </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The Dangdut King ready for presidency</span><span class="font3"> in order to be a Standard English sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the way…The King and the president</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">(</span><a href="http://www.thejakartapost.com"><span class="font3">www.thejakartapost.com</span></a><span class="font3">, Sunday, November 25 2012, 10.08 AM)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">News headline are very often written in a special style to express messages to the reader. This special style of headline too exaggerates from what news content actually is. The use of the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">by the way</span><span class="font3"> in headline (2) has the meaning as the question phrase or emphases something uncertain.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Sometimes, the choice of word in the headline contains ambiguous meaning for the reader. This headline (2) has the ambiguous meaning in the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">The king and the president</span><span class="font3">. This phrases has two different meanings. First, both of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the king </span><span class="font3">and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the president</span><span class="font3"> are both of phrases with the meaning that ‘the president and the king of dangdut’ are different persons in the different duties’. Therefore the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> between those two phrases was used as the linking word. The second meaning, the context of the news is used to create the meaning </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the king and the president</span><span class="font3">. Based on the content of news the meaning is that ‘the king of dangdut would run to the president’ (becoming president), therefore </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the king and the president</span><span class="font3"> are the one person who has his ambition to be president. That means that the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> does not refer to the linking word, but it is also as the comparison between two phrases the word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">or</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;PKB in talks with Rhoma Irama</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">(</span><a href="http://www.thejakartapost.com"><span class="font3">www.thejakartapost.com</span></a><span class="font3">, Monday, December 03 2012, 7.16 AM)</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">News headline are often written as short as possible. The news reporters and editors tend to use abbreviations or acronym to correspond to originally longer words. The shortening was used to economize space without changing the original meaning. From the headline structure above, the writer used the shortened word by taking first letter of each word. The shortened PKB in both of the headline above has meaning The National Awakening Party (PKB). The shortening of ‘PKB’ in this headline, includes as well metonymy news headline. Metonymy appeared because PKB the party supported by many politicians. However, when individual members of PKB were in talks with Rhoma, it was metonymy to say PKB in the headlines news, as the individual talking with Rhoma Irama.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In this case, the verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">talks</span><span class="font3"> in headline (3) was used in simple present tense. Therefore, this headline shows the time when the subject (PKB ) was talking with Rhoma Irama. The transitive verb ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">talks</span><span class="font3">’ was explained clearly by using the prepositional phrase to explain the object of the headline. In headline (3) the</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">preposition </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">‘in’</span><span class="font3"> is found, which is based on grammatical rule, is incorrect. Based on the grammatical rule, the proposition ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in’</span><span class="font3">must be followed by a gerund, so if the writer wants to preserve the verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">talks,</span><span class="font3"> the preposition keep it concise ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in’</span><span class="font3"> can be omitted. On the other hand, the preposition ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">in’</span><span class="font3"> in headline (3) has the function to emphases the action that has been done by the subject.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Relation between Headline and The Lead of News</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The most important part of the story is exposed in the first paragraph of the news known as the lead of news. Therefore, the headlines represent what is said within the lead by using shorter construction</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;text-decoration:underline;">Dangdut’ king ready for presidency</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Believing in the democratic value that everyone has the same right to be a presidential candidate, </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">Rhoma Irama,</span><span class="font3"> the country’s king of dangdut music, says that </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> is </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">ready to go the distance in the 2014 presidential election</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>

The phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Dangdut king</span><span class="font3"> in the headline and its reference </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Rhoma Irama</span><span class="font3"> in lead of news is categorized into </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">collocation term</span><span class="font3">. This link is called </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">collocation</span><span class="font3"> term because of the lexical reference to the identity of specific person in the lead of news.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Then, the verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ready</span><span class="font3"> is linked in the same word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ready</span><span class="font3"> in the lead of news. This verb </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ready</span><span class="font3"> in the headline belongs to ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">reiteration’</span><span class="font3"> term focused in the ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">repetition’</span><span class="font3"> type, because the word </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ready</span><span class="font3"> in the headline is repeated in the lead of news without changing the meaning. However, the adverbial phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">for presidency </span><span class="font3">has reflected the meaning </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to go the distance in the 2012 presidential election</span><span class="font3">. This link between headline and lead is that the headline is the summary of the meaning in the lead of news. The lexical cohesion ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">collocation’</span><span class="font3"> is also shown in the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">for presidency</span><span class="font3"> in the headline, and is fully described as </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to go the distance in the 2012 presidential election</span><span class="font3"> in the lead of news.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the way… The King and The president</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Everyone’s having a grand time lampooning </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">dangdut King Rhoma Irama </span><span class="font3">after </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> announced </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> was heeding </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">his</span><span class="font3"> “calling” </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">to run for president</span><span class="font3"> in 2014. In the name of good journalism, </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> has been given ample space and time to share </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">his</span><span class="font3"> reasons. “</span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">I</span><span class="font3"> rejected requests </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">to run for president</span><span class="font3"> in 2009,” </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> said, adding that </span><span class="font3" style="text-decoration:underline;">he</span><span class="font3"> initially rejected renewed requests for the next election.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">This headline and lead of news above are related by lexical cohesion and grammatical cohesion. The lexical cohesion between the headline and lead of news is shown below.</span></p>

<p><span class="font3">Rhoma Irama is well known as the king of dangdut singer therefore the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the king</span><span class="font3"> in the headline refers to the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">dangdut king Rhoma Irama</span><span class="font3">. This type of lexical cohesion is called the ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">reiteration’</span><span class="font3"> because one word refers to another word which is repeated in the lead of news. Then the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the president</span><span class="font3"> in the headline is the simple way to express that Rhoma is running for president. The headline is written in a simple a way, and therefore the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the president</span><span class="font3"> in the headline refers to the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to run for president</span><span class="font3"> in the lead of news. Based on this data, the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the president</span><span class="font3"> and its reference, in the lead of news are categorized into type of lexical cohesion called ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">collocation’</span><span class="font3"> because the phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">the president</span><span class="font3"> contains the implicit meaning based on the context of the news </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to run for president</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The phrase </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">by the way</span><span class="font3"> in the headline has meaning </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to emphasis the confusion of the writer about the news</span><span class="font3">, meanwhile this phrase also </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">attracts the curiosity of reader to read the news</span><span class="font3">. Additionally, the conjunction </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font3"> which is used in the headline, besides its use as a link between two phrases in the same level, also has function to </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">compare two phrases</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font3">The type of grammatical cohesion occurring between headline and the lead of news in termed as </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">personal reference</span><span class="font3">. The pronoun used in the lead function as the ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">subject</span><span class="font3">’ in the news, the pronoun </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">his</span><span class="font3"> as ‘t</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">he possessive pronoun</span><span class="font3">’ of Rhoma Irama’s statements, and the pronoun I are used in the ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">direct statement (direct speech’)</span><span class="font3"> of Rhoma Irama who said that he rejected requests to run for president in 2009.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Headlines were written as short as possible, because the news reporter and editor tend to create abbreviation to correspond to originally longer words. Besides, there are common situations for the news media to omit ‘</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">to-be’</span><span class="font3"> in news headline. It is obvious that the point of the omission is to attract people to read the news.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The relation between headline and lead of the news are proved with the lexical cohesion occurr between headline and lead of news. Lexical cohesion is the shortest ways to find out the relation between headline and lead of the news, because headlines consist of uniqueness lexical were chosen and that lexical choices reflect the message of the news.</span></p>
