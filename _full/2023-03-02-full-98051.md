<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Zoonotic diseases can be transmitted through close interactions between long-tailed monkeys. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> is one of the parasites that can attack mammals and is most commonly found in the intestinal tract. This study aims to analyze the presence of infection and the phylogenetic tree of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">. in long-tailed monkeys in Alas Purwo National Park, Banyuwangi, East Java. Identification of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">. in this study using morphological and molecular methods. A total of 100 stools were examined microscopically using the floating method, showing that 61 samples were positive, followed by a PCR test with a target of 600bp. PCR results obtained three positive samples followed by squencing. Sequences processed in BLAST isolate samples TNAP2 and TNAP9 having homology with </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">. Subtype 3 was 98-99%, while the TNAP7 isolate had a lower homology level of 78-79% and the level of phylogenetic analysis of the TNAP2 and TNAP9 isolates was related to </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> from the Philippines (KY610153.1) and Egypt (OP942294.1) and the TNAP7 isolate is related to </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">. from Thailand (MH197670.1, MH197668.1). Isolate </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">. from Alas Purwo National Park has high homology analysis results to </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis </span><span class="font3">type hominis from Rep. Czech and Chinese by 80-99%, it is possible to have a connection with the zoonotic problem.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Keywords: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font3">.; long-tailed monkeys; Alas Purwo National Park; zoonotic diseases.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Penyakit zoonosis dapat ditularkan melalui Interaksi yang dekat antara monyet ekor panjang. </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> merupakan salah satu parasit yang dapat menyerang mamalia dan paling banyak ditemukan di saluran usus. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk menganalisis adanya infeksi dan pohon filogenetik dari </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> pada monyet ekor panjang di Taman Nasional Alas Purwo, Banyuwangi, Jawa Timur. Identifikasi </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> pada penelitian ini menggunakan metode morfologis dan molekuler. Sebanyak 100 feses dilakukan pemeriksaan secara mikroskopis dengan metode apung menunjukkan bahwa ada 61 sampel yang positif, dilanjutkan dengan uji PCR dengan target 600bp. Hasil PCR didapatkan tiga sampel positif dilanjutkan dengan squencing. Hasil sekuens diproses dalam BLAST isolate sampel TNAP2 dan TNAP9 memiliki tingkat homologi dengan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> Subtype 3 sebesar 98-99% sedangan isolate sampel TNAP7 memiliki tingkat homologi yang lebih rendah yaitu 78-79% dan tingkat analisis filogenetik isolat TNAP2 dan TNAP9 memiliki kekerabatan dengan isolat </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> dari Filipina ( KY610153.1) dan Mesir (OP942294.1) serta isolat TNAP7 memiliki kekerabatan dengan isolat </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> dari Thailand (MH197670.1, MH197668.1). Isolat </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font3"> asal Taman Nasional Alas Purwo mempunyai hasil analisis homologi yang tinggi terhadap </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font3"> type hominis asal Rep. Ceko dan China sebesar 80-99%, hal itu memungkinkan mempunyai keterkaitan dalam masalah zoonosis.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.;</span><span class="font3"> monyet ekor panjang; Taman Nasional Alas Purwo; penyakit zoonosis.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">INTRODUCTION</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> is a parasite that can attack mammals and is most commonly found in the intestinal tract in various vertebrae including humans (Zhu et al., 2017). Transmission of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font4"> through direct contact with infected animals is a zoonotic risk that can occur (Osman et al., 2015). The interaction between visitors and long-tailed monkeys can cause changes in behavior to become aggressive. This causes the risk of disease transmission between long-tailed monkeys and humans to increase (Friishansen et al., 2015). In developing countries the prevalence distribution of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">blastocystis </span><span class="font4">infection is up to 100%, while in developed countries it is up to 56% (El Safadi et al., 2014; Scanlan et al., 2014). </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> found in children and monkeys in an area in Khatmandu India (Yoshikawa et al. (2009). </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp. </span><span class="font4">identified based on analysis of the small subunit ribosomal RNA (SSU rRNA) gene has 17 subtypes (Alfellani et al., 2013). In research on </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4">, there are similar subtypes between humans and Non-Human Primates (NHP), ST1, ST2, ST3, and ST4 (Yoshikawa et al., 2009; Alfellani et al., 2013; Ramírez et al., 2016) In determining subtypes and phylogenetic trees to analyze the presence of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font4">. infection in long-tailed macaques in Alas Purwo National Park, Banyuwangi, East Java, it is necessary to carry out morphological and molecular examinations.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">RESEARCH METHODS</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Sample Collection</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">100 samples of long-tailed monkey feces in Alas Purwo National Park, Banyuwangi, East Java were taken 5 grams and then collected and put in pots. sample containing 2% potassium dichromate. Each pot was given a sample code, then put in an ice box and then stored at -4OC until DNA extraction was carried out.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Sample Inspection</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Microscopic &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Examination &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;of</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> Stool samples were examined at the Laboratory of the Institute of Tropical Diseases, Airlangga University, Surabaya. Samples were examined to observe each phase of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> namely vacuolar, granular and cyst. Identification for this protozoa using native and floating methods and observed using a microscope with a magnification of 400 and 1000 times. Microscopic identification of Blastocystis is based on vacuolar and granular forms. The vacuolar form has two nuclei located at opposite poles, while the granular form shows small granules that fill the cytoplasm (Arpitha et al., 2018).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Molecular Examination by PCR</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Stool samples that have been confirmed through morphological examination are then followed by microscopic examination. There were three samples which were continued at the stage using the PCR method. A sample of 100 µl was put in a 1.5 ml microtube and DNA was extracted using the QIAamp DNA stool mini kit (QIAGEN, Hilden, Germany) according to the protocol in the kit. Furthermore, the samples were stored at -20°C until further examination. This research was conducted at the Institute of Tropical Disease (ITD) Airlangga University, Surabaya. PCR amplification used &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;the &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;primers &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;BhRDr</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(GAGCTTTTTAACTGCA-ACAACG) and &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;RD5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(ATCTGGTTGATC-</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">CTGCCAGT) (Kurniawati et al., 2020). Initial denaturation was carried out at 94°C for 5 minutes, followed by 35 cycles of denaturation at 94°C for 1 minute, annealing at 56°C for &nbsp;1 &nbsp;minute and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">elongation at 72°C for 1 minute, and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">additional elongation for 5 minutes. The PCR product was then read on 2% agarose gel electrophoresis and showed the band at the 600 bp position using Ultraviolet light (Mahendra et al., 2020). Samples that have been confirmed through morphological</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">and molecular examinations, samples that are positive will continue sequencing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Data analysis</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Data from the sequencing results from the samples obtained will be processed in the BLAST NCBI Genbank database and forwarded using a phylogenetic tree with the genetyx ver.10 application.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Results of Morphological Examination of </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Microscopic morphological analysis of 100 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;stool &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;samples &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;examined</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">microscopically showed that 61 samples (61%) were positive for </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp. </span><span class="font4">The results of microscopic examination using the floating method showed that the parasite was morphologically similar to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> (Figure 1). According to Ahmed and Karanis (2018) the morphological form of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font4"> has characteristics, namely the nucleus is located on the edge of the cytoplasm, there is sizable cytoplasm inside the cell, has 1 central vacuole or there is a vacuole in the middle (vacuolar) (Figure 1 A), No there are vacuoles or vacuoles are replaced by granules (granular) (Figure 1 B) and there is no central vacuole (cyst) (Figure 1 C).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) Examination Results</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">PCR results with primer </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp. </span><span class="font4">BhRDr (5'-GAG CTT TTT AAC TCC AAC AAA CG-3') and RD5 (5'-ATC TGG TTG ATC CTG CCA GT-3') (Kurniawati et al., 2020) which produced a band of 600 bp in amplification. The results of PCR product visualization using electrophoresis can be seen in Figure 2.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">PCR results obtained three positive samples followed by squencing. RD5 is a primer that amplifies eukaryotic species widely and BhRDr is a primer with high </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> specificity. False positives can occur, for that confirmation through sequencing is done to confirm the results are positive (Stensvold et al., 2013). Sequences processed in BLAST isolate</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">samples TNAP2 and TNAP9 having homology with </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> Subtype 3 was 98-99%, while the TNAP7 isolate had a lower homology level of 78-79% and the level of phylogenetic analysis of the TNAP2 and TNAP9 isolates was related to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> from the Philippines (KY610153.1) and Egypt (OP942294.1) and the TNAP7 isolate is related to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> from Thailand (MH197670.1, MH197668.1). Subtypes 1 and 3 are subtypes that are commonly found in humans and non-human primates (NHP) in the world (Ramírez et al., 2016). Isolate </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> from Alas Purwo National Park has high homology analysis results to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> type hominis from Rep. Czech and Chinese by 80-99% which can be seen in the phylogenetic tree (Figure 3).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">CONCLUSION AND SUGGESTION</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Conclusion</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Long-tailed macaques in Alas Purwo National Park, Banyuwangi, East Java, as many as 61 samples (61%) found </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> sp on microscopic examination, while molecularly there were three samples that were positive for </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> Long-tailed macaques in Alas Purwo National Park were infected with </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> Subtype 3, at the level of phylogenetic analysis the isolates TNAP2 and TNAP9 were related to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp</span><span class="font4">. from the Philippines and Egypt and the TNAP7 isolate is related to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp. </span><span class="font4">from Thailand. High homology analysis to </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis</span><span class="font4"> type hominis from Rep. Czech and Chinese by 80-99%, it is possible to have a connection with the zoonotic problem.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Suggestion</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Further research is needed regarding the molecular characters and phylogenetic analysis of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Blastocystis sp.</span><span class="font4"> on other hosts in order to know the zoonotic status in other areas of Indonesia.</span></p>
