<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">THE TRANSLATION OF BALINESE MATERIAL CULTURAL TERMS IN ENGLISH WITH REFERENCE TO</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">BALINESE FOLKTALES</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">By:</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">NI KADEK DESIANI</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ENGLISH DEPARTMENT</span></p>

<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi dengan judul ‘The Translation of Balinese Material Cultural Terms in English with Reference to Balinese Folktales’ bertujuan untuk menjawab dua pertanyaan pokok: Apa jenis istilah budaya materi yang ditemukan dan jenis prosedur penerjemahan yang digunakan dalam penerjemahan dongeng-dongeng berbahasa Bali ke dalam Bahasa Inggris. Pembahasan ini berhubungan dengan peristiwa sosial sehingga dapat dikategorikan dalam penelitian kualitatif yang dilakukan melalui penelitian kepustakaan dan wawancara dengan para informan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Data dalam penelitian ini diambil dari beberapa dongeng dalam buku yang berjudul Satua Bali Tales from Bali oleh Sutjaja dalam edisi kedua. Data dikumpulkan melalui penelitian kepustakaan dan metode dokumentasi kemudian diklasifikasi menggunakan teori jenis-jenis budaya materi oleh Newmark (1988) dan dianalisis menggunakan teori prosedur penerjemahan oleh Vinay dan Darbelnet dalam Venuti (2000).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Berdasarkan analisis dan pemaparan ditemukan bahwa terdapat lima jenis istilah budaya materi yang dikemukakan oleh Newmark dalam penerjemahan dongeng-dongeng berbahasa Bali ke dalam Bahasa Inggris, dan terdapat enam jenis prosedur penerjemahan oleh Vinay dan Dalbernet ditemukan dalam dongeng-dongeng tersebut yang diklasifikasikan menjadi dua metode penerjemahan dasar.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata Kunci: Penerjemahan, istilah budaya materi, prosedur penerjemahan.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Cultural knowledge and differences have represented a major focus of translation scholars. Since the translation is not only transferring the meaning linguistically but culture is also important, the study of cultural terms translation plays important role in transferring the source language into the target language. Against the difficulties of translation, many kinds of texts have been translated including folktale. In Bali, there are many folktales with their own characteristics, and the important character they contain a lot of Balinese cultural terms. On one hand, translating those terms is very difficult, moreover, it is also communicating</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">the message from Balinese into English, and should be attended with the meaning. On the other hand, the meaning should be maintained and the form may change.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Facing the problem of translating cultural terms, this study is concerned with the translation of cultural terms in a Balinese folktale into English. These stories were taken because they are interesting and have several cultural terms especially material culture that can be analyzed.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of material cultural terms are applied in the translation of the Balinese material cultural terms in English with reference to the Balinese folktales?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What translation procedures are applied in the translation of the Balinese material cultural terms in English with reference to the Balinese folktales?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To investigate the types of material cultural terms applied in the translation of the Balinese material cultural terms in English with reference to the Balinese folktales.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To describe and explain the translation procedures applied in the translation of the Balinese material cultural terms in English with reference to the Balinese folktales.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Scientific writing is a writing that needs a scientific procedure of creating a success research. Research method has an influential role to determine the success of the report and it is very important to know a valid research. The data collection was dominated with library research and qualitative method was used in presenting the analysis.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">In this study, the source of data was mainly written data taken from some of Balinese folktales in a book entitled </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Satua Bali Tales from Bali</span><span class="font3"> by Sutjaja in</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">the second edition. This book was published by Lotus Widya Suari in 2005 (Denpasar). This analysis is primary research because it is taken from the original source that is folktale. The stories as the data analysis contain two languages: Balinese as the source language, and English as the target language which have several cultural terms that can be classified clearly. Therefore, it will be easier when we are concerned with the analysis of material cultural terms.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Library research was used in this study such as reading or noting down the sources which are related with the topic. The documentation method is also used so that some pictures related to material cultural terms were collected to complete the description. Some steps for collecting the data were done as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First, the folktales were read carefully to find words or phrases indicated as material cultures,</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Second, they were observed intensively to identify the material culture appropriate to the requirement.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Third, note taking technique was applied to complete the process. It was done by taking notes and continued by the classification of material cultures based on their types.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">The results of the analysis were presented using qualitative method or descriptive-narrative method which describes the content of the study. In brief, the analysis of Balinese material culture can be seen as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;First, the note-taking data was checked.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Next, the required material cultural terms based on the theory of Newmark (1988) were explained and continued by the theory of translation procedures proposed by Vinay and Darbelnet in Venuti (2000).</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The description was also presented by adding some pictures and additional information to describe the SL term from the informant as the native speaker.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font3">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finally, the analysis was closed by the conclusion.</span></p></li>
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Translation of Balinese Material Cultural Terms in English with Reference to Balinese Folktales</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">There are eight folktales used in the examples of analysis namely </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Anak Sugih teken Tiwas (AST)</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I Belog Kelangan (IBK)</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I Juragan Anom (IJA)</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I Siap Badeng (ISB)</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I Tuung Kuning (ITK)</span><span class="font3">, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Pan Ketumpit teken I Lutung (PKIL), Men Leket (ML),</span><span class="font3"> and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">I Rare Angon (IRA)</span><span class="font3">. Two languages are used as the data, they are: (1) Balinese as the SL, and (2) English translation as the TL.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The first analysis uses the theory of material culture proposed by Newmark (1988). It defines 5 types of material culture with first four classifications (material culture related to foods, clothes, houses and town, and transport). The fifth type (material culture related to equipments) is added depend by some dominated data applied in the stories. The second analysis uses the theory of translation procedures proposed by Vinay and Darbelnet in Venuti (2000). It defines 7 translation procedures which belong to two basic translation methods they are: 1. Direct translation (Borrowing, Calque, and Literal translation), 2. Oblique translation (Transposition, Modulation, Equivalence, and Adaptation).</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">From the analysis of this study, it can be concluded that the cultural translation needs to consider the cultures of both SL and TL because the message in the target language should be equivalent as it is in the source language and it should also make sense and easily understood. There are 22 times of material cultural terms appearing from 5 classification of the theory, those are the terms related to foods (3 times), clothes (2 times), houses and towns (7 times), and equipments (10 times).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Six procedures applied to translate the material cultural terms and divided into two basic translation methods. From the direct translation method, there are two translation procedures are mostly used among the three parts of direct translation; they are borrowing (2 times) and literal translation (1 times). From the oblique translation method, all translation procedures are applied among the four parts of oblique translation, those are transposition (6 times), modulation (2 times), equivalence (9 times), and adaptation (2 times). Based on the finding described above, it can be seen that the translation procedures used in the Balinese folktales into English were dominantly oriented toward the oblique translation and implied that there was a quite big cultural gap between the SL and TL with regard to the material cultural terms.</span></p>
