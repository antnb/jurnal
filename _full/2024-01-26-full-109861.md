<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Noor Reza Indah Lestari<sup>1</sup>, Sintha Nugrahini<sup>2</sup>, Dian Mutiasari<sup>3</sup>, Abi Bakring Balyas<sup>4</sup>, Nisa Kartika Komara<sup>5</sup></span></p>

<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRAK</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Sebagian besar wanita di Desa Muara Untu masih mencuci pakaian menggunakan tangan hampir setiap hari. Kecepatan tangan, posisi tangan yang ekstrim, gerakan berulang dengan tekanan dalam waktu yang lama dan genggaman pada saat mengucek pakaian yang menggunakan kekuatan adalah salah satu faktor resiko terjadinya </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3">. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui hubungan usia, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3">, dan frekuensi gerakan repetitif dengan kejadian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> pada wanita yang mencuci pakaian menggunakan tangan di Desa Muara Untu Kabupaten Murung Raya Provinsi Kalimantan Tengah. Penelitian ini merupakan studi korelasi yang menggunakan desain Cross Sectional. Instrumen dalam penelitian ini adalah kuesioner </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Boston-Carpal Tunnel Questionare (BCTQ)</span><span class="font3"> dan kuesioner pemeriksaan fisik </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Phalen’s test.</span><span class="font3"> Berdasarkan analisis bivariat ditemukan bahwa hubungan usia dengan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrom</span><span class="font3"> menghasilkan koefisien korelasi 0,318 dengan probabilitas 0,001, hubungan </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3"> dengan kejadian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> menghasilkan koefisien korelasi 0,111 dengan probabilitas 0,210, dan hubungan frekuensi gerakan repetitif dengan kejadian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> menghasilkan koefisien korelasi 0,077 dengan probabilitas 0,385. Kesimpulannya adalah terdapat hubungan yang signifikan antara usia dengan kejadian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> dan tidak terdapat hubungan yang signifikan antara </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3"> dan frekuensi gerakan repetitif dengan kejadian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Kata kunci : </span><span class="font3">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome., Usia., Body Mass Index., Gerakan Repetitif.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRACT</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Most women in Muara Untu Village still wash clothes by hand almost every day. Hand speed, extreme hand positions, repetitive movements with pressure for a long time and grip when rubbing clothes using force are one of the risk factors for </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3">. This study aims to determine the relationship between age, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3">, and frequency of repetitive movements with the incidence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> in women who wash clothes by hand in Muara Untu Village, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan Province. This research is a correlation study using a cross sectional design. The instruments in this study were the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Boston-Carpal Tunnel Questionnaire (BCTQ)</span><span class="font3"> and the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Phalen's test</span><span class="font3"> physical examination questionnaire. Based on bivariate analysis, it was found that the relationship between age and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> produced a correlation coefficient of 0.318 with a probability of 0.001, the relationship between </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3"> and the incidence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> produced a correlation coefficient of 0.111 with a probability of 0.210, and the relationship between the frequency of repetitive movements and the incidence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome </span><span class="font3">produced a correlation coefficient of 0.077 with a probability of 0.385. The conclusion is that there is a significant relationship between age and the incidence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3"> and there is an insignificant relationship between </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font3"> and frequency of repetitive movements and the incidence of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font3">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords : </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.</span><span class="font3">, Age, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index.</span><span class="font3">, Repetitive Movements.</span></p><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">Indonesia is a maritime country and the largest archipelagic country in the world, so of course activities carried out at sea and in rivers have become part of people's daily lives. If we explore further, many villages in Indonesia are built along rivers. Kalimantan Island itself has 63 rivers covering 73% of the total island. The longest rivers in Indonesia are also found on Kalimantan Island, namely the Kapuas River, Mahakam River and Barito River. Muara Untu Village is a village located in Murung District, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan Province. Muara Untu Village is one of the villages built along the river, namely the Barito River. Most women in this village still wash clothes by hand almost every day, both morning and evening. Hand speed, extreme hand positions, repetitive movements with pressure for a long time and grip when rubbing clothes using force are one of the risk factors for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> is a condition where pressure occurs when the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">median nerve</span><span class="font2"> passes through the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">carpal tunnel</span><span class="font2"> in the wrist. Pressure on the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">median nerve</span><span class="font2"> causes motor and sensory disorders in the hand and finger area. Motor disorders that can occur include weakening of the muscles at the base of the thumb, resulting in loss of grip strength and hand skills. Sensory disorders that occur include tingling and decreased touch sensitivity on the sides of fingers I, II, III and IV. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> can occur in all circles, both among the general public and among workers. According to the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">National Health Interview Study (NHIS),</span><span class="font2"> the prevalence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> is 1.55%, this is what makes it one of the three most common groups of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cumulative Trauma Disorder (CTD)</span><span class="font2"> in the upper limbs. According to the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">International Labor Organization (ILO), </span><span class="font2">it shows that in every case of occupational disease, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> is almost always found in several countries. In 2010 the number of cases of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> due to work increased by around 30% compared to 2001. The prevalence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> due to work in Indonesia is still unknown because not much research has been conducted on this topic in Indonesia. However, studies showing high risk wrist and hand occupations report a prevalence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome </span><span class="font2">ranging from 5.6% to 15%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> occurs more often in women than men with an age range of 25-64 years. The highest age for this syndrome to occur is &gt;55 years, with the most susceptible age being 40-60 years. Women who do not have symptoms of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> at a vulnerable age are less likely to have symptoms of this syndrome at an older age. The prevalence of this syndrome among the general population is 5% of women and 0.6% of men. A study shows </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">that Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> is closely related to a person's </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2">, which is said to mean that </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">, which is caused by compression of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">median nerve</span><span class="font2"> under the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">transverse carpal ligament</span><span class="font2">, is related to an increase in a person's weight. This research also shows that someone with an abnormal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> is at greater risk of experiencing symptoms of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> than someone with a normal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index </span><span class="font2">with a ratio of 5:1. Repetitive movement frequency is a risk factor</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. Repetitive movements or repeated movements carried out for a long period of time without rest can cause fatigue and tension in the muscle tendons, this reduces blood flow in the peripheral blood vessels and reduces the function of the blood vessels in the wrist, causing symptoms of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study aims to determine the relationship between age, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2">, and frequency of repetitive movements with the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> in women who wash clothes by hand in Muara Untu Village, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan Province.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">The research was conducted in Muara Untu Village, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan Province in June-July 2023 using an observational method with a quantitative approach and a </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cross Sectional</span><span class="font2"> design, namely looking at the relationship between age, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2">, and frequency of repetitive movements with the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.</span><span class="font2"> The population of this study were all women with the habit of washing clothes by hand in Muara Untu Village, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan. The sample for this study was all women with the habit of washing clothes by hand who had symptoms of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> during the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phalen's test</span><span class="font2"> in Muara Untu Village, Murung Raya Regency, Central Kalimantan. The sample was taken using the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Purposive Sampling Technique</span><span class="font2"> and calculated using the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Slovin</span><span class="font2"> formula which makes the total sample in the study This numbered 130 people. The independent variables in this study were age, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2">, and frequency of repetitive movements. Meanwhile, the dependent variable is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The materials and tools in this research are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index </span><span class="font2">measuring instruments (</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">microtoise, bathroom scale</span><span class="font2">, and calculator), and a stopwatch. Meanwhile, the research instruments used in this study were questionnaires (respondent characteristics questionnaire and the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Boston-Carpal Tunnel Questionnaire (BCTQ)),</span><span class="font2"> and the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phalen's test</span><span class="font2"> physical examination questionnaire.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Respondents will be asked to sign </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Informed Consent</span><span class="font2"> after listening to the explanation, then respondents will carry out a physical examination of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phalen's test</span><span class="font2"> for a maximum of 1 minute. Respondents who had symptoms of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> then filled out a respondent characteristics questionnaire, took </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> measurements, filled out the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Boston-Carpal Tunnel Questionnaire (BCTQ),</span><span class="font2"> and finally measured the frequency of repetitive movements. Data analysis was carried out using a computer and the SPSS for Windows version 26 application, after which the researcher carried out hypothesis testing and independence testing using the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Spearman Rank</span><span class="font2"> test.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This research was approved by the Research Ethics Committee of the Faculty of Medicine, Palangka Raya University No. 77/UN24.9/LL/2023</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;DISCUSSION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This study shows that there is a significant relationship between age and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> with a weak level of relationship. This means that increasing age is followed by an increase in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">, conversely, decreasing age is followed by a decrease in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Syndrome.</span><span class="font2"> This is because starting from the age of 30 years, degeneration occurs such as tissue damage, tissue changes to scar tissue, fluid loss, bones and muscles become less stable, thereby increasing the risk factors for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. Research by Richard says that the older a person is, the higher the risk of experiencing </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. The average age for this syndrome to occur is 25-64 years, with the most susceptible age being 40-60 years. This is because age directly affects a person's physical capacity starting from the age of 25 years, which is proven by the age of 50 to 60 years, muscle strength decreases by 25% and sensory and motor abilities decrease by 60%. Research by Dede et al. and Moutasem said that </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome </span><span class="font2">can also occur because starting from the age of 30 there is an increase in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lysyl oxidase</span><span class="font2"> activity which results in thickening and stiffness of the collagen fibers so that the elasticity of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">flexor retinaculum</span><span class="font2"> decreases and makes it unable to withstand changes in the pressure it receives.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This research shows that there is no significant relationship between </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> with a very weak level of relationship. The results of this study are inversely proportional to research by Fanny et al. which states that there is a significant relationship between </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> and the incidence of Carpal Tunnel Syndrome with a significance value of &lt;0.05, this is caused by excessive </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> causing a buildup of fluid so that the contents of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">carpal tunnel</span><span class="font2"> increase which then increases the risk factor for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome.</span><span class="font2"> Other research was conducted by Faisal et al. stated that there is a significant relationship between </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome </span><span class="font2">with a significance value of 0.018, this is because an abnormal </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Body Mass Index</span><span class="font2"> is a risk factor for the occurrence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> in the general population. Based on research conducted by Nadhifah et al. said that the more a person's weight increases, the more pressure the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">median nerve</span><span class="font2"> under the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">transverse carpal ligament</span><span class="font2"> will have, thereby increasing the risk factor for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> so that obese people have a 50% higher risk of developing Carpal Tunnel Syndrome than thin people.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study shows that there is no significant relationship between the frequency of repetitive movements and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> with a very weak level of relationship. The results of this research are inversely proportional to research by Ahmad which was conducted on stone crushing workers who worked for 7-8 hours/day, stating that there was a significant relationship between the frequency of repetitive movements and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> with a significance value of 0.001 and a correlation the strong one. Subsequent research conducted by Indriani on workers in the crab production section who worked for 8-10 hours/day also stated that there were 80.6% of respondents who were at risk of experiencing </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. Subsequent research also conducted by Cindy on </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">repair veneer</span><span class="font2"> workers who worked for 10-12 hours/day stated that there was a relationship between the frequency of repetitive movements and the incidence of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2"> with the probability that the frequency of repetitive movements on the right hand was 0.003 and on the left hand was 0.005. Based on the results of research conducted by Ahmad, Indriani, and Cindy, it appears that there is a strong relationship between the duration of a person's repetitive movements and the </span><a href="http://ojs.unud.ac.id/index.php/eum"><span class="font0">http://ojs.unud.ac.id/index.php/eum</span></a><span class="font0"> doi:10.24843.MU.2024.V13.i01.P18</span></p>

Research by Richard and Moore states that the frequency of repetitive movements is a risk factor for </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Carpal Tunnel Syndrome</span><span class="font2">. <h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSIONS AND SUGGESTIONS</span></h1></li></ul>
