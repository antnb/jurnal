<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">ANALYSIS OF ADJECTIVES IN NOUN PHRASE IN ONLINE NEWSPAPER “THE JAKARTA GLOBE”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">By</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">NI WAYAN MIKA CAHYANI</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata sifat dalam bahasa Inggris merupakan kata yang sangat berperan penting dalam membentuk sebuah frase kata benda. Kata sifat berfungsi sebagai kata yang dipergunakan untuk menjelaskan atau memberikan informasi lebih dari objek yang dimodifikasi. Tujuan dari artikel ini adalah untuk menganalisa jenis-jenis kata sifat serta fungsi kata sifat dalam sebuah frase kata benda yang ditemukan di sebuah situs berita online “The Jakarta Globe”. Berita online sangat dikenal baik di seluruh kalangan, baik di kalangan anak muda, maupun orang dewasa, karena beritanya sangat mudah untuk di akses. Informasi serta berita terbaru yang disediakan oleh situs situs berita online bisa terbitkan dalam waktu yang sangat cepat, tidak perlu menunggu sampai satu hari, hanya dengan beberapa menit saja berita serta informasinya sudah bisa dibaca oleh berbagai kalangan. Studi ini menggunakan metode kualitatif serta motode kepustakaan. Teori yang digunakan sebagai acuan dalam studi ini adalah teori tentang jenis-jenis kata sifat oleh A.J. Thomson dan A.V. Martinet dalam bukunya yang berjudul A Practical English Grammar dan teori tentang fungsi-fungsi kata sifat oleh Quirk dalam bukunya yang berjudul A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language. Hasil dari studi ini menunjukkan bahwa ada enam jenis kata sifat dalam bahasa Inggris: demonstrative adjectives, distributive adjectives, quantitative adjectives, interrogative adjectives, possessive adjectives, dan adjectives of quality serta tiga fungsi kata sifat dalam sebuah frase kata benda: attributive functions, postpositive functions, dan adjectives as head of noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Kata kunci: kata sifat, jenis-jenis kata sifat, fungsi-fungsi kata sifat</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Noun phrase is a group of word with a noun or pronoun as its head. There are some possible elements to form a noun phrase such as determiner, adjective, complement, etc. The one that is concerned in this study is adjective because adjective has an important role in forming a noun phrase. It is a describing word which gives more information about the object they modified. This study is focused</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">on the kinds of adjectives in noun phrase, and the functions of adjectives used in noun phrase.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The data sources of this study were taken from articles on online newspaper site “The Jakarta Globe” (</span><a href="http://www.thejakartaglobe.com"><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">www.thejakartaglobe.com</span></a><span class="font1">) which is concerned with Asia -Pacific category. This data were chosen because those articles contain many English adjectives. This site also can increase our knowledge of what is happening nowadays. Besides, online newspaper is really popular nowadays, because the updating information is really quick. The information or the news will be posted on the site just in a few moment.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are two problems that are discussed in this study. They are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What kinds of English adjectives in noun phrase were found in an online newspaper site </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What were the functions of English adjectives in noun phrases found in an online newspaper site </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To know the kinds of English adjective in noun phrase found in an online newspaper site entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1">.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explore the functions of English adjective in noun phrase found in an online newspaper site entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1">.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data were taken from articles on online newspaper site </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1"> which is concerned with Asia - Pacific category. There were five articles taken from October 09, 2012. The Jakarta Globe daily newspaper serves unrivaled information written in English on Indonesia, Asia, and the wider world. It is</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">published six days a week from Monday to Sunday. It was launched in November 2008. This site provides the newspaper with the latest stories, a daily e-mail, newsletter, breaking news around the clock and a searchable archive stories. The Jakarta Globe (</span><a href="http://www.thejakartaglobe.com"><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">www.thejakartaglobe.com</span></a><span class="font1">) published in Jakarta by PT Jakarta Globe Media, located at Citra Graha Building 11<sup>th</sup> Floor, Suite 1102, Jl. Jend. Gatot Subroto Kav. 35 – 36, Jakarta 12650, Indonesia.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Documentation method was used in this study by reading the data intensively and note taking to find relevant data to complete this study. First, the daily online newspaper entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Jakarta Globe</span><span class="font1"> which is concerned with Asia - Pacific category were read intensively to find the data of adjectives used in noun phrases. There were 286 data of noun phrases with adjectives. Then, Those data were chosen randomly to get the sample data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data of noun phrases with adjectives from the data source were analyzed using qualitative method based on the related problems and theory. Then the data were classified based on six main kinds of adjectives’ theory proposed by A.J. Thomson and A.V. Martinet in their book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">A Practical English Grammar </span><span class="font1">which was published on 1986 to find out kinds of English adjectives. Then the data were classified and analyzed based on syntactic functions of adjectives’ using the theory proposed by Quirk, Greenbaum, Leech, and Svartvik in their book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language</span><span class="font1"> which was published on 1985.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kinds of Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There were six kinds of adjectives used in noun phrase found in the data source. They are demonstrative adjectives, distributive adjectives, quantitative adjectives, interrogative adjectives, possessive adjectives, and adjectives of quality.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Demonstrative Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Demonstrative adjectives include </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">this</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">these</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">those</span><span class="font1">. They are used to point out the noun. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">That</span><span class="font1"> contrast (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1"> contrast in the sentence “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">That</span><span class="font1"> contrast, between Pyongyang and every other city in the country, reflects an ever-growing chasm between North Korea’s elite and the daily struggles of everyone else”, consists of demonstrative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1"> and noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">contrast</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">That</span><span class="font1"> was used to point out something that was farther away. Demonstrative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">that</span><span class="font1"> modifies the singular noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">contrast</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Distributive Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Distributive adjectives actually include ”</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">each</span><span class="font1">”, “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">every</span><span class="font1">”, “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">either</span><span class="font1">”, and “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neither</span><span class="font1">”. They refer to a number of people or things. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Every</span><span class="font1"> other city (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">every other city</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “That contrast, between Pyongyang and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">every</span><span class="font1"> other city in the country, reflects an ever-growing chasm between North Korea’s elite and the daily struggles of everyone else” consists of distributive adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">every</span><span class="font1">, adjective of quality </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">other</span><span class="font1"> and noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">city</span><span class="font1">. Distributive adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">every</span><span class="font1"> is used with singular noun and is placed before the noun. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Every</span><span class="font1"> which is followed by adjective of quality </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">other</span><span class="font1"> is used to modify the noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">city</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quantitative Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Quantitative adjectives such as some, any, no, many, much, little, few, cardinal number (one, two, etc), and ordinal number (second, fifth, seventh, etc) are used to show number of people or things. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">No</span><span class="font1"> choice (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th </sup>October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">no choice</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “Koh Yu Hwan, a North Korean studies professor based in Seoul, said the North had </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">no</span><span class="font1"> choice but to respond to South Korea’s extended missile range but it won’t likely launch a provocation as it is waiting for the results of US and South Korean presidential elections.”, consists of quantitative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">no</span><span class="font1"> and noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">choice</span><span class="font1">. Quantitative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">no</span><span class="font1"> expresses certain number of amount of the noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">choice</span><span class="font1"> with expressing a negative.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Interrogative adjectives include “what”, “which”, and “whose” which are used to make questions related to person or things. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">What</span><span class="font1"> misogyny (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">what misogyny</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “&quot;If he wants to know </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">what </span><span class="font1">misogyny looks like in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">modern</span><span class="font1"> Australia, he doesn't need a motion in the House of Representatives, he needs a mirror,&quot; she added.”, consists of interrogative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">what</span><span class="font1"> and noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">misogyny</span><span class="font1">. The interrogative adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">what</span><span class="font1"> is used to point out the singular noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">misogyny</span><span class="font1">. It occurs in the form of indirect question.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Possessive Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Possessive adjective includes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">my</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">your</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">its</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">her</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">his</span><span class="font1">, our and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">their</span><span class="font1">. They are used to show possession. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Their</span><span class="font1"> dollars (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">their dollar</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “Today, the Pyongyang rich, spending their dollars, euros and Chinese yuan, can buy everything from high heels to imported watches.”, consists of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">their</span><span class="font1"> as possessive adjective, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dollars</span><span class="font1"> as noun. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Their is</span><span class="font1"> a possessive adjective form of the subject pronoun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">they</span><span class="font1">. Possessive adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">their</span><span class="font1"> shows the possession of plural noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dollars</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>5.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjectives of Quality</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adjectives of quality show the quality or characteristic of noun or pronoun, such as big, black, old, handsome, etc. Based on the data, adjectives of quality were mostly found in the data sources. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Desperate</span><span class="font1"> doctors (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th </sup>October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">desperate doctors</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “They say </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">desperate </span><span class="font1">doctors struggle to treat patients with almost no medicine, using equipment that can be decades old.”, consists of adjective of quality </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">desperate</span><span class="font1"> and noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">doctors</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Desperate</span><span class="font1"> as an adjective of quality expresses a general description. It describes plural noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">doctors</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Functions of Adjectives</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are three functions of adjectives used in noun phrase found in the data source. They are attributive functions, postpositive functions, and adjectives as head of noun phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Attributive Functions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adjectives mostly appear in attributive functions. It is located before the noun. Example: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">New</span><span class="font1"> schools (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Adjective of quality </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">new</span><span class="font1"> of the noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">new schools</span><span class="font1"> in the sentence “There is little overt sign of those protests today in Right Ujumchin. Government officials proudly point out </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">new</span><span class="font1"> schools and hospitals.”, occurs in attributive function. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">New</span><span class="font1"> is adjective of quality expressing a general description, which is placed before plural noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">schools</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Postpositive Functions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adjective sometimes appears in postpositive function. Postpositive function occurs when the adjective comes after the noun. Example: Decades </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">old</span><span class="font1"> (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Decades old</span><span class="font1"> as a noun phrase in the sentence “They say desperate doctors struggle to treat patients with almost no medicine, using equipment that can be decades </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">old</span><span class="font1">.”, occurs in postpositive function. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Old</span><span class="font1"> is an adjective of quality showing a general description which comes after plural noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">decades</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjectives as Head of Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adjectives often have a function as adjectives as a head of noun phrase. It occurs when there is only determiner and adjective in a noun phrase; therefore, adjective can replace the noun function in the noun phrase. Example: The Pyongyang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rich</span><span class="font1"> (The Jakarta Globe, 9<sup>th</sup> October 2012)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The Pyongyang rich</span><span class="font1"> as a noun phrase in the sentence “Today, the Pyongyang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rich</span><span class="font1">, spending their dollars, euros and Chinese yuan, can buy everything from high heels to imported watches.”, has a function as adjective which occurs as head of noun phrase. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Rich</span><span class="font1"> is an adjective of quality showing a positive degree. It is preceded by article </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1"> and proper noun </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pyongyang</span><span class="font1"> as pre-modification. It appears as</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">head of noun phrase denoting class of people. The noun phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the Pyongyang rich </span><span class="font1">may refers to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the Pyongyang rich (people)</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This study has discussed about kinds of adjectives and functions of adjectives used in noun phrase. There are six kinds of English adjectives in noun phrase in the data source. They are demonstrative adjectives, distributive adjectives, quantitative adjectives interrogative adjectives, possessive adjectives and adjectives of quality.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">According to the syntactic functions theory, adjectives in the noun phrases found in the data source are divided into three functions. They are attributive functions, postpositive functions, and adjectives as a head of noun phrases. Attributive functions occur when the adjectives are placed before a noun. Postpositive functions occur when the adjectives come after the noun. Adjectives as head of noun phrases occur when they are only determiner and adjective in a noun phrase; therefore, adjective can replace the noun function in the noun phrase.</span></p>
