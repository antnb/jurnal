<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">The development of strawberry farming in Bali experiencing some obstacles that cause a decline in production, such as wilting disease. The disease was reported caused by the fungi base on morphological recognition. There are two fungi were recognized caused the strawberry wilt disease in Bali, they are from genus </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Verticillium</span><span class="font7"> and </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium.</span><span class="font7"> More specific information about causal agent of wilt disease in strawberry especially in Bali is needed. The one accurate identification is done through the molecular approach by analyzing DNA that encode the ribosomal DNA (rDNA). The 18S rDNA, including the internal areas of transcribed spacers (ITS), ITS1 and ITS4 have been widely used in phylogenetic studies. The amplification results of this area produce bands in different sizes that can be used to identify fungal species. Based on that the identification of strawberry wilt disease using molecular analysis was conducted. The 542 bp of Internal Transcribed Spacer (ITS) DNA was successfully amplified using PCR with pairing primers ITS 1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(5-</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">TCCGTAGGTGAACCTGCGG-3’), and ITS 4 (5’-TCCTCCGCTTATTGATATGC-3’). The sequences of three isolates were successfully obtained through sequencing. Homology levels were tested between sequences and showed that Candi Kuning sequence and Gobleg sequence had 95% similarity with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font6"> NRRL 13307 (U34571) from </span><span class="font7">America. While Pancasari sequence have 94% similarity with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font6"> NRRL 13307 (U34571) from </span><span class="font7">America. Candi Kuning, Gobleg, and Pancasari sequences had the same 86% with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> isolate C34-294 Brazil (</span><span class="font6">KJ439088) </span><span class="font7">and had 89% similarity with sequence </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> f.sp</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">. fragariae</span><span class="font7"> China (</span><span class="font6">KT833080)</span><span class="font7">. Homology levels were tested between sequences and showed that Candi Kuning sequence and Gobleg sequence had 95% similarity with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum </span><span class="font6">NRRL 13307 (U34571) from </span><span class="font7">America. While Pancasari sequence have 94% similarity with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font6"> NRRL 13307 (U34571) from </span><span class="font7">America. Candi Kuning, Gobleg, and Pancasari sequences had the same 86% with sequence of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum </span><span class="font7">isolate C34-294 Brazil (</span><span class="font6">KJ439088) </span><span class="font7">and had 89% similarity with sequence </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> f.sp</span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">. fragariae</span><span class="font7"> China (</span><span class="font6">KT833080)</span><span class="font7">. Based on phylogeny analysis of Pancasari, Gobleg and Candi Kuning isolates were obtained in one group with </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum </span><span class="font7">identified in America and Brazil, and also in one group with </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> f. sp. </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">fragariae</span><span class="font7"> that identified in China.</span></p>

<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">INTRODUCTION &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font7">introduced in 1983 precisely in Candi</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Strawberries entered Indonesia in the &nbsp;Kuning Village, Tabanan Regency, Bali and</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">1980s. Strawberries in Bali were first &nbsp;were developed to Bukit Catu Village,</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Pemuteran Village and Batu Sesa Village Tabanan Regency, Bali. Strawberry is one of potential fruit because it contains many phytochemicals mainly phenolic compounds that are beneficial to human health (Hannum, 2004). Strawberries in Indonesia within a year can produce up to five times and peak production occurs from July to August depending on environmental conditions (Sukumalanandana and Verheij, 1997). However recently the production of strawberry fruit in Bali has been decreased, and one of the contributing factors is strawberry wilt diseases. The strawberry wilt diseases was reported caused by the fungi from the genus </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Verticillium</span><span class="font7"> (Sari </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2018). In contras some researcher reported that the strawberry wilt disease caused by </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> in many countries such as Carolina (Williamson </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font7">, 2012), Australia (Golzar </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font7">, 2007), Serbia (Stankovic </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font7">, 2014), and Spain (Arroyo </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font7">, 2009). Based on the data there are two fungi were reported cased the strawberry wilt disease in Bali, they are from genus </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Verticillium</span><span class="font7"> and </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium.</span><span class="font7"> More specific information about causal agent of wilt disease in strawberry especially in Bali is needed. The one accurate identification method is done through the molecular approach by analyzing DNA that encode the ribosomal DNA (rDNA). Therefore on this study the identification of strawberry wilt</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">disease using molecular analysis was conducted.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS Sample collection and fungi isolation</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">This research was started from April 2018 to June 2018. The wilting strawberry plants samples were collected at the center of strawberry plantations in Bali (Pancasari Village, Sukasada District, Buleleng Regency; Gobleg Village, Banjar District, Buleleng Regency and Candikuning Village, Baturiti District, Tabanan Regency) and Plant Disease Laboratory, Faculty of Agriculture, Udayana University. The isolations have done by cutting the infected parts (leaf, trunk or root) with the size about (1x1) cm<sup>2</sup>, then dipped it in alcohol for 2 minutes to remove contamination outside. Then, the pieces of plant were washed off by dipping it into the sterile water for 3 times, and then put it on Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA) medium which already contains chloramphenicol antibiotics (100mg/L) and grow on PDA medium which contained chloramphenicol antibiotics (100mg/L) (Ando </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font7">, 2003).</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>Molecular identification of fungi on strawberry that infected wilt disease</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Pure cultures were rejuvenated to the PDA medium. A disc (4 mm diameters) of mycelium was put at the middle of petri dish</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">that already contains PDA medium. After that, the cultures wereincubated for 24 hours and then used for DNA extraction.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>DNA extraction and PCR</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">DNA extraction was done following a protocol based on ZR Fungal DNA Kit™ Catalog No. D6005 by ZYMO. The Genomic DNA extraction was conducted using ZR Fungal Bacteria DNA Kit (Zymo Research). The amplification odf DNA was conducted by PCR using MyTaq HS Red Mix that started by preparing the PCR master mix (9.5 µL ddH20, 12.5 µL MyTaq Red Mix [2x], 20 µmol primer ITS 1 &nbsp;[5-</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">TCCGTAGGTGAACCTGCGG-3’], &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;20</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">µmol &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;primer &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ITS &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;[5’-</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">TCCTCCGCTTATTGATATGC-3’] (Singha </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2016), and I µL DNA template. The following cycling condition was used for PCR reaction: Initial denaturation at 94ºC for 60 s, then 35 cycles of 95ºC for 15 s, 52ºC for 15 s, 72ºC for 45 s, and 75ºC for 5 minutes. The PCR product was purified with ZymocleanÔ Gel DNA Recovery Kit (Zymo Research). Finally, bi-directional sequencing was conducted at Laboratory of Genetika Science in Kembangan Jakarta Barat.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>Electrophoresis and sequencing</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">As much as 1 µL of PCR products were assessed by electrophoresis with 1%</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">TBE agarose gel. Electrophoresis was done for 30 minutes at 100 Volt. The electrophoresed DNA then was visualized with a UV transiluminator.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Sequence Analysis</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">DNA sequences were analyzed to make the alignment which then used to determine the level of homology or alignment with the sequence of ITS gene that has been published in GenBank within Program Basic Local Alignment Tool (BLAST) (NCBI 2014).</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Phylogenetic Analysis</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">The analysis was continued with the phylogenetic analysis by using software: ChromasPro, Molecular Evolutionary Genetics Analysis (MEGA 6.06), PAUP 4.0, and TreeGraph2.0.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>RESULT AND DISCUSSION</span></h1>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>Molecular Identification of Fungi on Strawberry that Infected Wilt Disease</span></h1>
<p><span class="font7">Fungal cultures from Pancasari Village, Gobleg Village, and Candikuning Village, were successfully obtained, reproduced and identified morphologically based on macroscopic character (Fig. 1). Therefore the culture of the fungi can be used in the molecular identification phase.</span></p>
<div><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/60843-1.jpg" alt="" style="width:451pt;height:141pt;">
<p><span class="font7" style="font-weight:bold;">Fig. 1. </span><span class="font7">Pure culture 7 days-old on PDA medium of A) Pancasari isolate, B) Gobleg isolate, and C) Candi Kuning isolate that isolated from strawberry plant on pathogenicity test</span></p>
</div><br clear="all">
<p><span class="font7">According to Sari </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., (2018), the decrease in strawberry production due to infection by fungi was very high. The decrease in strawberry production that occurred in Pancasari Village reached 95% and in Candi Kuning Village it reached 85%. The decrease in plant production due to the attack of </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font7"> fungus is very dependent on the attack intensity, so the decline in production in each region will be different. This is because, </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font7"> fungus is strongly influenced by environmental conditions. When the environmental conditions are suitable, the fungus will be able to reduce very high plant production. Environmental conditions that are not suitable for the development of Fusarium fungi, cause a decrease in plant production also decreases (Nagy </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2006). It was supported by Melysa </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">. (2013), that plant infections carried out by </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font7"> sp. affected by humid weather conditions and plant age.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Younger plant ages tend to be more susceptible to disease than adults plant. Disease caused by </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium</span><span class="font7"> sp. easier to attack plants in the high rainy season.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">In addition to environmental factors and plant age, the factor of varieties resistance may also affect the decrease in strawberry plant production. Considering, the development of strawberries in Bali is still depend on strawberry seedlings that taken from the former brood-stock. Broodstock varieties of developed in strawberry production centers in Bali, especially in Pancasari Village, Gobleg Village and Candi Kuning Village are mixed varieties. Each variety for strawberry seedlings that taken from the previous brood-stock is certainly unknown. Therefore, resistant varieties and non-resistant varieties were mixed at a strawberry plantation that were mainly developed. The number of resistant and nonresistant varieties in each region certainly</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">will not be the same, so the decreasing in strawberry production in each region will possibly different. It was compatible with the statement by Melysa </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">. (2013), that strawberry plants of California varieties were more sensitive to disease. The results of the analysis of the disease intensity of the in vivo test on California varieties and Santung varieties can be seen that California varieties were more sensitive than Santung varieties. The intensity of pathogen attacks on California varieties were higher than Santung varieties.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Another research such as in Australia (Golzar </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2007; Fang </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2012), United States (Koike </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2009, 2013), Spain (Arroyo </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2009), Korea (Nagarajan </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2004) and China (Zhao </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">., 2009) stated that </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">F. oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> f. sp. </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">fragariae</span><span class="font7"> has recently been considered as one of the most serious fungal threats to strawberry cultivation and it can also</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">threaten strawberry fruit yield and transplant production in Bali.</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">Identification of pathogenic fungi that cause strawberry wilt disease was carried out molecularly by amplifying the Internal Transcribed Spacer (ITS) area by using two primers, namely ITS 1 and ITS 4 rDNA (ribosomal DNA). One of the molecular analysis that have been developed is using ribosomal DNA sequences in the Internal Transcribed Spacer area (Druzhirina and Kubicek, 2005). DNA amplification in this molecular analysis was carried out to multiply gene fragments located in the Internal Transcribed Spacer area. The DNS fragment size of the amplification target with the primer used was in accordance with the findings stated by White </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">. (1990)The PCR product obtained and electrophoresed, showed the DNA band that appeared. The amplified DNA band showed the base length are 542 bp (Gobleg), 542 bp (Candi Kuning), and 544 bp (Pancasari) (Fig. 2).</span></p>

<p><span class="font7">The appearance of DNA bands is an important information that shows that PCR has been successfully carried out on the DNA sample that was believed as </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7">. However, these results have not been able to ascertain the truth about whether the amplified is truly DNA from </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> because the primer used is a</span></p>
<p><span class="font7">universal primer. Although, in accordance with the statement from Singha </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font7">. (2016), the </span><span class="font7" style="font-style:italic;">Fusarium oxysporum</span><span class="font7"> species that amplified using ITS1 and ITS4 primers produced 380-620 bp sized bands. Therefore, sequencing was carried out to ensure the correctness of the information needed in this study.</span></p>
