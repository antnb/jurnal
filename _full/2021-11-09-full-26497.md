<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Artikel ini berjudul “Akhiran derivasional Bahasa Inggris yang dipakai dalam pembentukan nomina pada majalah elektronik Condé Nast Traveller UK”. Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk mengidentifikasi jenis-jenis akhiran derivasional bahasa Inggris dalam pembentukan nomina pada majalah Condé Nast Traveller UK dan untuk menjelaskan makna dari bentuk derivasi nomina. Sumber data diambil dari majalah Condé Nast Traveller UK edisi Mei 2016.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini menerapkan metode dokumentasi dalam mengumpulkan data. Data-data yang telah terkumpul kemudian dianalisis dengan metode kualitatif. Penelitian ini menerapkan teori yang dipaparkan oleh McCharty (2002) untuk mengidentifikasi jenis akhiran derivasional bahasa Inggris dan juga menerapkan teori yang dipaparkan oleh Haspelmath and Sims (2010) untuk menganalisis struktur dan makna dari bentuk derivasi nomina. Selain itu, penelitian ini juga menerapkan teori lain yang relevan yaitu teori oleh Carnie (2013).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil dari penelitian ini adalah sebagai berikut: akhiran yang membentuk nomina dari nomina adalah akhiran –ess, -er, -ian, -ship, -hood, and –ist; akhiran yang membentuk nomina dari adjektif adalah akhiran –ity, -ness, dan –ism; akhiran yang membentuk nomina dari verba adalah akhiran –ance/ence, -ment, -ing, -ation, -al, and –er/-or. Makna derivasi yang ditunjukkan oleh nomina denomina adalah female noun, status noun, dan inhabitant noun; makna derivasi yang ditunjukkan oleh nomina deadjektiva adalah quality noun; dan makna derivasi yang ditunjukkan oleh nomina deverba adalah agent noun, instrument noun, dan action noun.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: derivasional, akhiran pembentuk nomina, derivasi nomina</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Morphology is defined as the branch of linguistics that deals with word forms, their internal structure, component of grammar that includes word formation and how words are formed. Morphology can be divided into inflectional morphology and derivational morphology. Derivation is the morphological process in which a derivational affix is attached to a base form to create a new word.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The focus of this study is on derivational morphology, particularly on the types of nominal suffixes. This topic was chosen because the noun class has the largest members in English word classes and it is frequently used in written text. It is very interesting to note that through a derivational process one word can gain many other words with different parts of speech. For examples, the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">simplify</span><span class="font2"> (verb), </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">simply</span><span class="font2"> (adverb), and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">simplicity</span><span class="font2"> (noun) are derived from the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">simple</span><span class="font2"> (adjective). Based on the phenomena mentioned above, this study is focused on the description of the process of derivational suffixes forming nouns in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Condé Nast Traveller UK</span><span class="font2"> e-Magazine May 2016 edition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the background described above, there are two problems formulated:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What kinds of derivational suffixes forming nouns are found in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Condé Nast Traveller UK</span><span class="font2"> e-Magazine?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of the derived nouns?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The aims of this study are determined as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify the kinds of derivational English suffixes forming nouns found in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Condé Nast Traveller</span><span class="font2"> UK e-Magazine.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the meanings of the derived nouns.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data of this study were taken from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Condé Nast Traveller UK</span><span class="font2"> e-Magazine May 2016 edition. This study was a library research, which applied the documentation method. The data were collected from 17 article texts on e-magazine. Collecting the data from the article texts in e-magazine was done by taking note of the derived words including their original sentences and classifying them into kinds of derivational suffixes. The data of this study were analyzed using the qualitative method. Analyzing the data was done by classifying the suffixes taken from the e-magazine based on the kinds of derivational suffixes forming nouns based on McCarthy (2002), analyzing the meanings of derived nouns based on meanings proposed by Haspelmath and Sims</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(2010), and presenting the result of the analysis in the form of narrative text, tree diagram and table.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<h2><a name="bookmark37"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark38"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the discussion, the derivational suffixes forming nouns found in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Condé Nast Traveller UK</span><span class="font2"> e-Magazine are suffixes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ess, -er, -ian, -ship, -hood,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ist </span><span class="font2">which are attached to noun bases; the suffixes</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ity, -ness,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ism</span><span class="font2"> which are attached to adjective bases; and suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ance/-ence, -ment, -ing, -ation, -al, -er/-or</span><span class="font2"> which are attached to base verb</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The derivational meanings of denominal nouns are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">female noun</span><span class="font2"> which denotes nouns ‘female’ with the use of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ess</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">status noun</span><span class="font2"> which denotes a state of being X (base) with the use of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ship</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-hood,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">inhabitant noun</span><span class="font2"> which denotes inhabitant of being X (base) with the use of suffixes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ian</span><span class="font2">. The derivational meanings of deadjectival nouns found in data source is only </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">quality noun</span><span class="font2"> which refers to the state or quality of being X (base) with the use of suffixes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ity, -ness</span><span class="font2">, and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-ism</span><span class="font2">. Last, the derivational meanings of deverbal nouns are </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">agent noun</span><span class="font2"> which refers to the agent or participant of the action with the use of suffixes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–er</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-or, instrument noun </span><span class="font2">which indicates a tool of an action with the use of suffix </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-er,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">action noun</span><span class="font2"> which refers to the event or action itself with the use of suffixes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">–ance, -ment, -ing, -((a)t)ion, </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">-al</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
