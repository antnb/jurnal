<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Ada banyak istilah dalam bahasa Inggris yang muncul dalam sepuluh tahun terakhir ini. Hal ini tidak dapat dipisahkan dari kontribusi hobi atau profesi tertentu. Hobi dan profesi tersebut dengan cepatnya memproduksi jargon untuk menamai konsep-konsep baru dalam bidangnya. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui proses pembentukan kata apa saja yang berkontribusi dalam pembentukan jargon, dan menjelaskan makna istilah berdasarkan konteksnya. Jenis-jenis proses pembentukan kata dikelompokkan berdasarkan teori dari Yule (2006), sedangkan teori dari Louw (1991) digunakan dalam menemukan makna-makna dari jargon. Hasil penelitian menunjukkan bahwa ada 107 jargon ditemukan. Dalam penelitian ini juga ditemukan ada 8 proses pembentukan kata yang berkontribusi dalam pembentukan jargon, sedangkan makna kontekstual jargon dalam penelitian ini cenderung lebih spesifik bahkan berbeda dari makna leksikalnya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Kata Kunci: jargon, komputer, pembuatan film</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">We live in the time where we can </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">email</span><span class="font2"> people anywhere and anytime. We need to stay </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">online</span><span class="font2"> to be able to watch </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Youtube</span><span class="font2">. Sometimes, we decide to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">uninstall</span><span class="font2"> a program in our computer to remove them, and even casual computer users use impressive lexicon of new technical terms like </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">software, digital</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">gigabyte.</span><span class="font2"> Yet, none of those terms existed a few years ago, at least before the invention of computer.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">New terms emerged rapidly in the last ten years and it cannot be separated from the contribution of certain hobby or profession. Those hobbies and professions quickly develop jargon to name a concept that people need to talk about. Akmajian (2001:302) states that jargon is special or technical vocabulary that evolves to meet the particular needs of the profession. Since a new jargon emerges whenever there is an invention of technique or technology in certain field, it makes jargon as one of the most productive language variety.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In the other side, there is nothing new in the creation of most jargon that exists today. Some jargon sound familiar since it was adopted from familiar terms and gone through certain formation process, forming a new term. However their meaning could be slightly or completely different. Therefore, it is a good idea to find the formation process that contributes in the creation of jargon and its meaning based on the context where it is being used.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study is very important since it reveals the process of jargon creation used in computing and filmmaking. It will be useful for those who like to learn about it, especially for students. As for teachers, this research is expected to add a new teaching material.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the word formation processes found in jargon used in Andrew Kramer’s keynote speech at After Effect World Conference 2014?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is the meaning of the jargon found in Andrew Kramer’s keynote speech at After Effect World Conference 2014?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The aims of this study are to find out the formation process found in jargon used in Andrew Kramer’s keynote speech at After Effect World Conference 2014 and to find out the meaning of jargon found in Andrew Kramer’s keynote speech at After Effect World Conference 2014.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Material and Methods</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Material</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data that support this writing was taken from youtube.com, in form of video by videocopilot.net entitled “AE WORLD Andrew Kramer Keynote Speech (FULL)”. The AE World Conference itself was held on 2014 in Seattle, US. There were many jargon that was used referring to some acts, things, or conditions in computing and filmmaking. Most of those terms are well known by common people, since it use common English terms, but some of those are unknown.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Methods</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The method used in collecting the data in this research is documentation method. The data of this study was collected through some steps as follows; first in order to identify jargon in this study, observation in language used by Andrew Kramer in the keynote speech video at AE Conference 2014 is need to be done. Then, the next step is transcribing the speech by Andrew Kramer in his keynote speech video at AE Conference 2014, therefore the data were available in the written form.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The method used in data analysis in this research is descriptive qualitative method. In doing this analysis, the data were processed through the procedures as follows, started by extracting jargon in collected data, which in form of transcription. After the data was collected, it was analyzed based on the theory of word formation proposed by Yule (2006) in order to know what formation processes are contributing in the creation of those terms. Then, in order to discover its meaning, it was analyzed by the theory proposed by Louw (1991) to find out its meaning in context and its comparison with their lexical meaning if it possible.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Yule (2006) states that there are some words formations, there are: coinage, borrowing, compounding, clipping, back formation, conversion, acronym, derivation, and blending. According to Louw (1991:137), there are two distinct meaning in proper semantic analysis, they are: lexical meaning and contextual meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Results and Discussion</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Process of Word Formation</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Coinage</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">One of the least common processes of word formation in English is coinage, that is, the invention of totally new terms.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-1) …looking at someone’s </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">Tumblr</span><span class="font2"> pictures…</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">It may be that there is an obscure technical origin of this invented term, for instance it derives from the term “tumblelogs”. However, it still can be considered as coinage because of there is no regulation in forming the term itself.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borrowing</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">One of the most common sources of new words in English is the process simply labeled borrowing, that is, the taking over of words from other language (5-2) Montage</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The word montage is a word from French that means “mounting”</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compounding</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Compounding is a joining of two or more separate words to produce a single form. Compounds may be written as one word (closed form), as a hyphenated word (hyphenated form), or as two words (open form).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-3) Closed form compounding: Hyper+Card --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Hypercard</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-4) Hyphenated form compounding: Built+in --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Built-in</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-5) Open form compounding: After+Effect --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">After Effect</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clipping</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This occurs when a word of more than one syllable is reduced to a shorter form, often in casual speech. Clipping means cutting off the beginning or the end of a word, or both, leaving a part to stand for the whole</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-6) Composition --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Comp</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">comp</span><span class="font2"> is considered as clipping because it is a shorthand of </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">comp</span><span class="font2">osition</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-7) Panorama --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Pan</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">pan</span><span class="font2"> is considered as clipping because it is a shorthand of </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">pan</span><span class="font2">orama</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-8) Moving Pictures --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Movie</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">movie,</span><span class="font2"> according to Yule (2006:55) is considered as special type of clipping known as hypocorism clipping, which is done by reducing the term moving pictures into one syllable “mov”, then –y or –ie is added to the end (movie)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Backformation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">A very specialized type of reduction process is known as backformation. Typically, a word of one type (usually a noun) reduced to form another word of a different type (usually a verb).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-9) Emulation --&gt; </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Emulate</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">emulate</span><span class="font2"> is a verb that derives from the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">emulation</span><span class="font2">. The word emulation first came into use around 1550s and then verb </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">emulate</span><span class="font2"> was created from it around 1580s.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conversion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Conversion is a change in the function of a word, as for example, when a noun comes to be used as a verb (without any reduction).</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-10) …go out and </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">film</span><span class="font2"> something…</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term film originally is a noun denotes a series of moving pictures that tells a story. However here it is used as a verb that denotes an act of recording a video.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.1.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Acronym</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Acronyms is the process whereby a word is formed from the initials or beginning segments of a succession of words.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-11) …like the </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">MIDI</span><span class="font2"> guys in the back…</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The term MIDI stands for Musical Instrument Digital Interface</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.1.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Derivation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Derivation is the forming of new words by combining derivational affixes or bound bases with existing words. Derivation is achieved by adding affixes; there are prefix, suffix, and infix.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-12) Beyond </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">digital</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Digital is formed by adding derivational suffix –al into existing noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">digit</span><span class="font2">, which denotes any of the numeral from 0 to 9. This term is an adjective that literary means something to do or related to those number.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Meaning of Jargon</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">According to Louw (1991:137), there are 2 different kind of meaning, they are; lexical meaning and contextual meaning. Lexical meaning is the meaning that is what the word itself, on its own, contributes to the understanding to an utterance, while contextual meaning is what features of meaning derives from the context, enable one to define the event precisely by adding particular context features. The comparison of both kind of meaning in this study can be seen as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-13) …I’m learning </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">3D</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Table 1: Jargon 3D</span></p>
<table border="1">
<tr><td style="vertical-align:middle;">
<p><span class="font2">Lexical Meaning</span></p></td><td style="vertical-align:middle;">
<p><span class="font2">Contextual Meaning</span></p></td></tr>
<tr><td style="vertical-align:middle;">
<p><span class="font2">Quality of appearing to have three dimensions</span></p></td><td style="vertical-align:middle;">
<p><span class="font2">Emulated three dimensional object (not real three dimensional object)</span></p></td></tr>
</table>
<p><span class="font2">3D is a quality of appearing to have three dimensions, like any object in the real world. However, in computing, what is meant by 3D object is not the same with the real world. When we try to touch what is 3D in computing, it would feel flat just like 2 dimensional object. That happens because we are touching a flat lcd panel that displays those 3D-like images.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-14)…I can’t find a higher </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">resolution</span><span class="font2">, I’m sorry</span></p>

<tr><td style="vertical-align:top;">
<p><span class="font2">Act of solving a problem</span></p></td><td style="vertical-align:middle;">
<p><span class="font2">A degree of sharpness of displayed or printed image</span></p></td></tr>
</table>
<p><span class="font2">In common usage, the term resolution means the act of solving or settling a problem, disagreement, etc., and apparently this term has existed long before the computer was invented. However, the term was later adopted in computing to describe the degree of sharpness of a displayed or printed image. As we can see, this term has completely different meaning with its lexical meaning.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">(5-15) …Youtube videos, not the greatest </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">effect</span><span class="font2"> I’ve seen..</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">The term effect generally means a change that something causes in something else. However, in this context, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">effect</span><span class="font2"> or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">special effect</span><span class="font2"> or simply </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">SFX</span><span class="font2"> is the illusions or tricks of the eye used in the film, television, theatre, video game, and simulator industries to simulate the imagined events in a story or virtual world. Therefore it can be concluded that the term </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">effect</span><span class="font2"> has different meaning in this context.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded that there are eight word formation processes found in the formation of jargon in this study, they are coinage, borrowing, compounding, clipping, back formation, conversion, acronym, and derivation. The entire word formation</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">processes excluding blending were found in every jargon in this study. However, the term that is resulted by blending only found in the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bit</span><span class="font2"> in hyphenated-compounding </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">4-bit.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There are also two kinds of meaning found in those jargon, they are lexical and contextual meaning. Based on the comparison of these meanings, it is found that contextual meaning is usually have more specific sense, even completely different meaning from its lexical meaning.</span></p>
