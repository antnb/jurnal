<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">ABSTRACT</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">This study aimed to determine the genetic diversity and fruits quality of the &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; cultivars grown in Bali. This research was conducted in all regencies and city in Bali, during 2016. Furthermore, several cultivars of “Jeruk Bali” were genetically analyzed based on RAPD markers using 10 primers. Analysis of the quality of fruit is based on physical properties and chemical content. Eighteen cultivars of &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; obtained have a fruit morphological character with round, short round, and piriform fruit shapes, which are red, pink, cream and white flesh color. RAPD analysis results at 53% similarity level are grouped into 5 groups. The first group was only one cultivar, the second group consisted of 13 cultivars, the third and fourth groups were only one cultivar, while the fifth group consisted of two cultivars. The analysis of the diversity between cultivars based on the combination of physical and chemical properties of the fruit with hierarchy method on similarity level about 85% in a group is obtained by 4 (four) groups. Groupings by combination of physical and chemical properties of the fruit are not synchronized in their entirety with dendograms based on their genetic diversity. This illustrates the physicochemical properties of “Jeruk Bali” fruit in general is not fully genetical expressed, but also influenced by conditions of environmental growth.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Keywords: “Jeruk Bali”, genetic diversity, fruit quality</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">&quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; or pummelo (shaddock) (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Citrus grandis</span><span class="font4"> L. Osbeck) is native to Indonesia, widely cultivated in India, China, Thailand and other East Asian</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">regions (Davies and Albrigo, 1994), needs to be handled seriously in cultivation and utilization in Indonesia (Setiawan and Sunarjono, 2003). “Jeruk Bali” has another name </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">C. maxima</span><span class="font4"> (Burm.) Merr., </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">C. decumana</span><span class="font4"> L., </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">C. aurantium</span><span class="font4"> var. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Grandis</span><span class="font4"> L.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">C. aurantium</span><span class="font4"> var. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Decumana</span><span class="font4"> L. (Niyomdham, 1992; Setiawan, 1999; Hamilton, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">. 2008). “Jeruk Bali” is a good source of vitamin C and antioxidants (Rahman </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2003; Pichaiyongvongdee and Haruenkit, 2009).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Identification of genetic diversity of crop cultivars needs to be done for use in their development and cultivation. In addition, the evaluation of fruit quality also needs to be done to identify the nutritional compound of fruit as well as to know the response of plant varieties to the changing of the growing environment (Somantri </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2008). Biochemical analysis with isoenzymes in citrus plants, among others, is also used to distinguish the acidity level of citrus cultivars (Rahman </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2001). Excellence pomelo cultivars are generally determined based on the delicious fruit flavors (sweet and sour taste balance) as well as the water content of the juice (juicy)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(Rahman </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2003; Paudyal and Haq 2007).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">&quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; fruit are generally considered to be ripe when the fruit skin color, the content of the juice and the ratio of total soluble solids with acidity and other internal components have reached the level of acceptance of visual minimum or palatability of consumers (Paudyal and Haq, 2007; Rukmana, 2009; Lado, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2014; Riaz, M. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">, 2015 ). &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; fruits are a source of calories, minerals and excellent for relieving thirst due to high water content (up to 86 g per 100 g of fruit flesh). The leaves can be used for epilepsy, chorea, and cough seizures (Niyomdham, 1992; Direktorat Tanaman Buah, 2012).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">RAPD molecular markers are PCR applications used to detect the presence of a DNA polymorphism in a population or interpopulation. Polymorphisms produced by PCR RAPD techniques are due to changes in nucleotide bases, deletions, and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">insertions (William </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 1990). The advantage of the RAPD PCR technique is that it takes only a small quantity of DNA samples, is cost effective, easy to learn, and easy to obtain primers (Azrai, 2005). The use of molecular markers with RAPD PCR techniques has been widely used for early plant breeding activities, such as analyzing the genetic diversity of wani Bali (Rai, I N., </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2008), identifying genetic diversity among barley cultivars (Fernández </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2002), and detecting polymorphisms in distance plants due to gamma-ray radiation (Dhakshanamoorthy, D. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2010). Evaluation of fruit quality including total soluble solids content (TSS), total titrated acids (TA), vitamin C, and flavonoid compounds (Pichaiyongvongdee and Haurenkit, 2009), are required to supplement the description of the &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; that were grown in Bali.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>MATERIALS AND METHODS</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">This research was conducted in all regencies/cities in Bali province. Molecular analysis was conducted in the laboratory of Genetics and Plant Breeding of Faculty of Agriculture UGM, and fruit quality observation was done at Agricultural Technology Technology Laboratory of Faculty of Agricultural Technology, Udayana University, Denpasar.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The method of genetic diversity testing using Random Amplified Polymorphic DNA (RAPD) method, while observation of fruit quality evaluation is done by sensory analysis (organoleptic) and analysis of chemical compound (nutrient) content according to the method of observation of each specification of the compound. DNA extraction methods (Doyle and Doyle, 1990) use the materials used are fresh &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; leaves. The purified DNA is then quantified by using</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Gene Quant to determine the concentration of DNA obtained.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">DNA amplification is done by PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) reaction which aims to multiply DNA sequences based on the primary used. The PCR reaction was performed at a total volume of</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">10 μl for each PCR tube. Each PCR reaction consisted of 5 μl Go Taq® Green (Promega) PCR mixtures, 0.25 μl 100 μM primers (Sigma-Proligo), 2.5 μl sample DNA (template) and 2.25 sterile aquabidest. DNA amplification is done with PCR System BOECO tool. The DNA of PCR was then electrophoresed using 1.0% (w/v) agarose which has been added florosafe DNA stain as dye, in TBE buffer with 100 volt for 45 min. The results were then visualized with UV light. The DNA data obtained was analyzed using binary data by scoring, ie score 1 for the emerging band and score 0 for the band that did not appear (Williams, JGK., </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 1990). The data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">obtained by scoring the electrophoresis results for each individual on a certain size, and the binary data is then analyzed using the help of software Genalex 6.1 and Ntsys pc 2.2.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Observation of fruit quality evaluation used three pieces of ripe and healthy fruit in accordance with the criteria and characteristics, namely: smooth shiny smooth fruit skin, the colors are brighter and feels heavy, with PTT content around 7 - 10 <sup>o</sup>Brix, generally between 24 - 30 weeks after flowering (Setiawan, 1999; Mahardika and Susanto, 2003; Lado, J. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2014). Sensory analysis in this study was conducted to identify consumer preferences and match the acceptability level of citrus fruits from various cultivars. The variables observed in fruit quality evaluation are: (a) percentage of fruit parts, including edible portion; (b) the vitamin C content of the fruit was measured using iodometric titration method (Cahyadi, 2006); (c) fruit</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">juice pH measured with Jenway 3010 digital pH meter; (d) total titrated acid content (TA) was measured by titration using 0.1 N NaOH until the pH of fruit juice reached 7 (AOAC, 2005); (e) total soluble solids (TSS) content, calculated as</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">the degree of Brix (<sup>o</sup>Brix) measured using a refractometer; (f) flavonoid levels were performed by spectrophotometry using aluminum chloride reagents (Chang and Wen, 2002); (g) sensory analysis performed ie. hedonic test and scalar quality test. The test was conducted by 10 semi-trained panelists, consisting of students and</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">laboratory technicians of the Faculty of Agricultural Technology Udayana University (Wagiyono, 2003).</span></p>

<p><span class="font4">Stages of RAPD analysis is started from DNA extraction, DNA quantification, DNA dilution, DNA amplification and electrophoresis. DNA amplification is done by PCR reaction. Ten selected primers were applied the DNA obtained from the extraction of the &quot;Jeruk Bali&quot; samples of Bali, in the order of nucleotide bases as shown in Table 2. DNA concentrations in this study were obtained from 400 ng / μl in the 'Juuk Saba' Denpasar (DEN-03) and 'Jerungga' Klungkung (KLU-01) cultivars, up to 3996 ng / μl in the 'Juuk Saba' Badung (BAD- 02), while the purity of DNA obtained from 1.33 to 2.00. DNA purity is determined by the ratio of A260 / A280 (Sambrook </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 1989; Chen, H. </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 2010). The number of amplified DNA</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">fragments obtained in this study ranged from 5 to 15 bands depending on the type of primer and the genotype being analyzed. The smallest amount of DNA fragment (5) was obtained on the primary use of OPA-8, while the highest number of DNA fragments (15) was obtained on primary use of OPD-8 (Table 2). The ribbon pattern on agarose gel which is the result of genomic DNA amplification can be grouped into two categories: polymorphic band and monomorphic band. Polymorphic bands are a picture of DNA bands that appear at certain sizes, but in other samples no DNA bands are found in these sizes. The monomorphic band is a band in some samples that has no variation (Williams </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font4">., 1990).</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>CONCLUSIONS</span></h2>
<p><span class="font4">RAPD analysis results at 53% similarity level are grouped into 5 groups. The first group was only one cultivar, the second group consisted of 13 cultivars, the third and fourth groups were only one cultivar, while the fifth group consisted of two cultivars. The analysis of the diversity between cultivars based on the combination of physical and chemical properties of the fruit with hierarchy method on similarity level about 85% in a group is obtained by 4 (four) groups. Groupings by combination of physical and chemical properties of the fruit are not synchronized in their entirety with dendograms based on their genetic diversity. This illustrates the physicochemical properties of “Jeruk Bali”fruit in general is not fully genetical expressed, but also influenced by conditions of environmental growth. Most cultivars were observed highly preferred by consumers,</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">but three less preferred cultivars namely “Jerungga” Bangli (BAN-03), “Juuk Bali”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Jembrana (JEM-03) and “Jeruti”</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Karangasem (KAR-02).</span></p>
