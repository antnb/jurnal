<p><span class="font4">In order to translate cookbook from source language into target language is not easy, because the wrong translation can make the result of the food different from the original one. Translation, sometimes, is not an easy task to do because there are many aspects that should be determined in order to maintain the message conveyed. The different grammatical features and culture are two of them (Widya &amp;&nbsp;Dewi, 2015). Proper grammar provides a vital role for understanding language. One of the main components in any grammar is the verb. Thus, the translator has to give the most attention to the translation of verbs. One of the possible problems a translator may face is related to passive voice. Because of the diverse functions and usage of passive voice in a language, the translator should know how to translate it with appropriate function and acceptable usage (Abbasi &amp;&nbsp;Arjenan, 2014). Baker (1991, 103-109) said that one must weigh this potential change in content and focus against the benefits of the rendering a smooth, natural translation in context where the use of the passive, for instance, would be statistically less acceptable than the use of the active or an alternative structure in the target language, their respective stylistic value in different text types, and the most important of all the functions of the passive and the similar structure in each language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The linguistic features of cookbook commonly use imperative structures and the verbs are in active forms. Sometimes in the English cookbook the use of passive voices can be seen in the process of making food. This research will focus the analysis on the passive sentences occur in English cookbook and its translation into Indonesian. This research can help students in food production major understand the structure of English passive sentences and its translation so they can make the food similar with the original one.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The previous researches that having similar focus with this research are Abbasi &amp;&nbsp;Arjenan (2014), Widya &amp;&nbsp;Dewi (2015), Suastini (2019). From those three researchers found that in translation study, the understanding of passive structure is really important for the translator, so the target reader can have the meaning equivalent with the source language.</span></p>

<h3><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Methods</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">This research uses qualitative method. The data used in this research is English cookbook entitled &quot;The Essential Book of Sauces &amp;&nbsp;Dressings&quot; from Lowery Barbara, published by Periplus, Singapore and its translation into Indonesian titled </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Saus dan Dressing yang Esensial</span><span class="font4"> by Hadyana P. published by Periplus, Indonesia. The method used in preparing the data related to the subject of this research is documentation method. Arikunto (2002 : 206) says that</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">documentation method is a method used to look for the data about things or variables which are in the form of notes, transcriptions, books, newspaper, magazines, leafs, etc. The technique data collections of this study were collected by using the following steps: the first is reading the English cookbook and its translation version. Then, select the sentences which contain English passive voice in the English cookbook and its translation. The technique and method of analyzing the collected data are: the first is classifying the data. After collecting the data, the researcher will divide the data collection into each classification based on the type of passive in English and its translation. Second is analyzing the passive voice in the English cookbook and its translation.</span></p>

<h3><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Theoretical Framework</span></h3>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font4">3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;English passive voice</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Passive voice is formed by putting “to be” followed by past participle (V3). In forming the passive sentence, the learners have to pay attention to the tense. English has many tenses</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">which have the different use and changing. Voice in English grammar refers to an active or passive use of verb. The active is the voice that is used most commonly. The active and passive have different verb phrases in that the passive has an additional auxiliary: a form of the auxiliary be followed by an -ed participle. Here are examples of corresponding active and passive verb phrases:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Active &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Passive</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">loves &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;is loved</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">sold &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;was sold</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">is fighting &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;is being fought</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">has reconstructed will proclaim &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;has been reconstructed will be proclaimed</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">may have asserted should be purifying may have been asserted should be being purified</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(Greenbaum, 2002:57)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The “by-phrase” is used in passive sentences when it is important to know who performs an action (Azar, 1993: 283). Azar (1993: 282) said only transitive verb can be used in the passive. A transitive verb is a verb that is followed by an object. The opposite of transitive verb is intransitive verb. An intransitive verb is a verb that is not followed by an object. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitive Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Active : Bob mailed the letter.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Passive : The letter was mailed by Bob.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Intransitive Verb</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Active : I slept well last night.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Passive : (not possible).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Indonesian passive voice</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">In Indonesian, there are also two type of sentences based on the nature of the relationship of actor-action, they are active and passive sentences. According to Anbiya (2010: 77) passive sentence is sentence that the subject is subjected to a thing or action, whether intentional or not.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Indonesia dijajah Belanda selama 350 tahun.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">On the sentence above, the word “Indonesia” serves as a subject which shows unintentional action. In other words, subjects of passive have a role to do the action. The verb in passive voice usually have affix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">-, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font4">-, and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The form of prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- does not have the other variation and also it never does the change. Prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- just has one function to form the passive verb (Ramlan: 1997: 116).</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;text-decoration:underline;">Buku itu diambil Fuad</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">S &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;P &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;O</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">From the example above, the writer concludes that in passive sentence, the object is behind of the verb and the subject is affected the action. Prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- is an affix which is productive and has the meaning as “the situation happened”. Affix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- has the function to form the passive verb (Ramlan, 1997: 117). The sentence in which the predicate is a passive form of verb using prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- shows that the subject is as receiver of the action and which is not intentional. For examples: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Jariku </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">teriris </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">pisau</span><span class="font4">. The meaning of prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font4">-, is in the state of the purpose of suffer of the doing action. For examples: </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Dia </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kesakitan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">oleh gigitan anjing itu</span></p>

<h3><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discussions</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">The following are data analysis of English passive sentences and their translations.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-1) SL : Dressing up simple desserts such as ice cream, fresh or poached fruit, pancakes and steamed puddings </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">is made </span><span class="font4">very easy with the addition of these luscious sweet sauces. (p.52)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Menambah citarasa hidangan penutup sederhana seperti es krim, buah segar/setup buah, panekuk, dan puding dapat </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dilakukan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dengan mudah; cukup dengan menambahkan saus manis dan lezat</span><span class="font4">. (p.52)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">From the text above, the translator translated the SL passive voice is made into dilakukan in TL. The passive construction in SL is formed by auxiliary verb be + past participle (is + made), and in TL is formed by prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- + verb base + -</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kan</span><span class="font4"> (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dilakukan</span><span class="font4">). The tense in SL is present tense and its passive characteristic is be (is/am/are) + past participle. The doer in the sentence above is unknown, but the sentence is still acceptable.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-2) SL : The egg yolks </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">are whisked </span><span class="font4">with the water in a pan over very low heat until thick and foamy. . . (p.58)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kocok </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">kuning telur dalam wadah yang ditaruh di atas panci berisi air mendidih lembut</span><span class="font4">. . . (p.58)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In the data above the SL passive voice, </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">are whisked </span><span class="font4">is translated into active voice </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">kocok</span><span class="font4"> in TL. The tense in SL is also present tense. In procedure text the sentences are mostly in the form of active sentences in order to explain the process of making the food. The translator in TL choose to translate the SL passive voice into the TL active voice to transfer the message easily to the target reader specially for the Indonesian people because the instruction of making the food is clear and to the point.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">(4-3) SL : The sauce </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">can be prepared </span><span class="font4">ahead and </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">refrigerated </span><span class="font4">for up to 24 hours. (p.9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Saus </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dapat dipersiapkan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sebelumnya dan </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">didinginkan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">sampai 24 jam</span><span class="font4">. (p.9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">There are two passive voices in SL, they are </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">can be prepared </span><span class="font4">and </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">refrigerated</span><span class="font4">. Both passive voices can be classified as modal passive with the characteristic is modal (can) + be + V past participle (prepared and refrigerated). The SL passive voices are translated into modal (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dapat</span><span class="font4">) + </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- verb (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dipersiapkan</span><span class="font4"> and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">didinginkan</span><span class="font4">) in TL. The doer in this sentence is unknown, but the sentence is still acceptable, because it is implicitly understood that the doer is the chef or cook or the person who wants to do the job of cooking in that particular situation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-4) SL : Plain milk </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">may be used</span><span class="font4">, particularly if the sauce is to have other flavourings. (p.24)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Gunakan </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">susu biasa tanpa bumbu tambahan bila saus akan diberi aroma lain</span><span class="font4">. (p.24)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In the data above, SL passive voice is </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">may be used</span><span class="font4">. It is also classified as modal passive with its characteristic is is modal (may) + be + V past participle (used). Its translation in TL becomes active voice (</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">gunakan</span><span class="font4">). The linguistic structures of cookbook commonly used imperative sentence with active verb. So the structure of SL passive that is translated into TL active is still acceptable.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-5) &nbsp;SL : Other fresh herbs such as chives, dill or tarragon </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">may be added</span><span class="font4">, or try a</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">combination of your favourite herbs. (p.25)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Rempah daun, seperti kucai, dill atau tarragon </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dapat dipakai</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">, atau kombinasikan beberapa rempah daun yang Anda sukai</span><span class="font4">. (p.25)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">In the data above, SL passive voice is may be added. It is also classified as modal passive with its characteristic is is modal (may) + be + V past participle (added). The SL passive voice is translated into modal (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dapat</span><span class="font4">) + </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- verb (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dipakai</span><span class="font4">) in TL. The usage of prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- instead of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- in the translation indicates that the verb in the sentence intends to perform the action.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-6) SL : When all the oil </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has been added</span><span class="font4">, stir in an extra 2 teaspoons of lemon juice and season to taste with salt and white pepper. (p.34)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Bila semua minyak </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">telah dituang</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">, tambahkan 2 sendok teh sari jeruk lemon tambahan, garam dan lada putih</span><span class="font4">. (p.34)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The SL passive voice, </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has been added </span><span class="font4">is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">telah dituang</span><span class="font4">. The most equivalent word to translate had been added is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dituang</span><span class="font4"> not </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tertuang</span><span class="font4">. The usage of prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- instead of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter-</span><span class="font4"> is the meaning contained by the verb intends to perform the action. The passive voice in this sentence can be classified as present perfect tense with its characteristic is auxiliary (has) + been + V past participle (added).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font4">(4-7) &nbsp;SL : When all the milk </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has been added</span><span class="font4">, keep stirring over medium heat until the</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">mixture boils and thickens. (p.24)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">TL : </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Setelah semua susu </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">, masak sambil diaduk di atas api sedang hingga mendidih dan kental</span><span class="font4">. (p.24)</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">The SL passive voice, </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">has been added </span><span class="font4">is translated into </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4">. The prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- has the function to form the passive verb in TL. When comparing Indonesian passive voice with prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">- and prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-, it can be seen that there is such an unintended factor implied in the usage of prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-. Commonly, verb with prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- is the predicate of the sentence which is used to express the sudden and unexpected action. Otherwise, in the data passive above, the prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">identified that the process of verb </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4"> express an expected action. The verb </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tercampur </span><span class="font4">means to add something to combine two or more things to form one substance. The doer is the person who does the job of cooking in the particular situation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Novelties</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">Research on passive voice is commonly found in novel or narrative text meanwhile the procedure text especially cookbook commonly use active verbs in the process of making food based on the structure of imperative sentence. There is one novelty of this study that was found in the analysis which presented as in below.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4">Commonly, in the English passive voice the structure is be + past participle, meanwhile in the Indonesian passive voice the verb in passive voice usually have affix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">-, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ke</span><span class="font4">-, and </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-. From the analysis of passive voice found that in TL the passive construction with the prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-(</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4">) expressed an expected action. It is different from the theory that the verb with prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">- is used to express the sudden and unexpected action. The example of the analysis is </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Setelah semua susu </span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">, masak sambil diaduk di atas api sedang hingga mendidih dan kental</span><span class="font4">. The Indonesian passive (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4">) expressed an expected action. It is also emphasized from the conjunction (</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">setelah</span><span class="font4">) which means the process of </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">tercampur</span><span class="font4"> has really expected to be happened. In this study the form of the passive voice shows that the receiver of the action which is expected thing in the context of the sentence</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h3><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h3></li></ul>
<p><span class="font4">From the analysis above, it can be concluded that both English and Indonesian have passive constructions. The usage of passive constructions in English cookbook is also found meanwhile the characteristic of cookbook commonly used active construction. The passive construction in English cookbook are in the form of present tense (is/are + V past participle), modal passive (modal + be + V past participle), present perfect tense (has + been + V past participle). The translation in Indonesian are translated into passive construction by using verbs added by prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">di</span><span class="font4">-, active construction with active verbs, and verbs added by prefix </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font4">-.</span></p>
