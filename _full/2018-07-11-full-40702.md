<p><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstract—</span><span class="font5">Based on the typology of the morphological point of view, Indonesian is an agglutinative language. Affixation plays an important role in its morphological processes, meaning that prefixes, suffixes and affixes are added to the word stems to form new words. From the syntactical point of view, its basic word order adheres to SVO. The predicate, which is in the form of verb, has the ability to bind arguments to create a clausal structure. Therefore, it is possible that one verb binds one argument, two arguments and three arguments, highly depending on the type of the verb. As a case study of typological linguistics, this present study is an initial one which explores the Indonesian derivative verbs. The theory of typological linguistics is used to analyze the system through which how the Indonesian derivative verbs are morphologically formed is analyzed. In this case, the verb functioning as the predicate, can bind arguments in order to create a clausal structure. The result of the analysis shows that, from the perspective of the typological linguistics, the Indonesian derivative verbs can be derived from the stems under the categories of 1) noun, 2) adjective, 3) precategory, and 4) verb. It also shows that there are six types of derivational affixes which can be used to form derivative verbs from the four stems mentioned above; they are (1) </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber</span><span class="font5">-, (2) </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber</span><span class="font5">-an, (3) </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font5">-, (4) </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng</span><span class="font5">-, (5) –</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kan</span><span class="font5">, and (6) </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5">. The ones which can be attached to nouns to form derivative verbs are </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-, ter</span><span class="font5">-, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng</span><span class="font5">-, -</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kan</span><span class="font5"> , and –</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">i</span><span class="font5"> ; the ones which can be attached to adjectives to form derivative verbs are </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-, ter-, meng-, -kan</span><span class="font5"> and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5">; the ones which can be attached to precategory to form derivative verbs are </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-</span><span class="font5">, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-an</span><span class="font5">, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ter-</span><span class="font5">, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng-</span><span class="font5">, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">-kan</span><span class="font5">, and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5">; and the ones which can be attached to verbs are </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">– kan</span><span class="font5"> and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5">. The derivational affixes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-, ter-,</span><span class="font5"> and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng-</span><span class="font5"> are used to form intransitive derivative verbs, and the derivational affixes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng-</span><span class="font5">, -</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kan</span><span class="font5">, and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5"> are used to form transitive derivative verbs.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The study of the Indonesian derivative verb typological linguistics is one of the ways in which the Indonesian morphology and syntax can be analyzed from the typological perspective. It can present the universal conception of a language. It is intended to describe the universality of a language so its findings can be easily understood by both Indonesians and foreigners. In the current globalized era, many foreigners intend to learn Indonesian language; therefore, many universities have created the Indonesian Program Designed for Foreigners ‘Bahasa Indonesia Untuk Penutur Asing (BIPA)’.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">The Indonesian derivative verbs can be derived from noun (N), precatetory (P), and verb (V). As an illustration, </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">batu</span><span class="font5"> ‘tone’ (N) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">mem</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">batu</span><span class="font5"> ‘getting petrified’ (V), </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">darat</span><span class="font5"> ‘shore’ (N) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">men</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">darat</span><span class="font5"> ‘landing’ (V), </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">baik</span><span class="font5"> ‘good’ (A) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">per</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">baik</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">i ‘</span><span class="font5">repairing/revising’ (V), </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">alir</span><span class="font5"> (P) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">alir</span><span class="font5"> ‘flowing’, </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">duduk </span><span class="font5">(V) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">duduk</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">i</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> ‘</span><span class="font5">sitting on/occupying’. From the affixes used to form the Indonesian derivative verbs described above, several unique things can be observed. </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">First</span><span class="font5">, the Indonesian derivative verbs can be formed from four word roots; namely, noun, adjective, precategory and verb. Therefore, it is assumed that the derivative verbs produced highly vary. </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Second</span><span class="font5">, the derivational affixes can produce 1) intransitive derivative verbs, as exemplified by the word root ‘batu’ (stone) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">membatu</span><span class="font5"> after the prefix </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">mem-</span><span class="font5"> is added to it; 2) transitive derivative verbs, as exemplified by the word root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">duduk</span><span class="font5"> (sitting) which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">duduki</span><span class="font5"> (sitting on or occupying), </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">dudukan</span><span class="font5"> (seating); 3) both transitive and intransitive verbs, as illustrated by the word root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">alir</span><span class="font5"> which becomes </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">alirkan</span><span class="font5"> (making something to flow) as a transitive verb and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">mengalir</span><span class="font5"> (flowing) as an intransitive verb. </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Third</span><span class="font5">, the arguments needed in a clause differ, as illustrated by the verb root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">duduk</span><span class="font5"> (sitting) in the clause </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Ibu duduk</span><span class="font5"> (the mother is sitting), there is one argument, namely </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ibu</span><span class="font5">; however, in the clause </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Ibu menduduki kursi</span><span class="font5"> (the mother is occupying the chair), there are two arguments, namely </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ibu</span><span class="font5"> (mother) and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kursi</span><span class="font5"> (chair). Furthermore, in the clause </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">Ibu mendudukan adik di kursi</span><span class="font5"> (mother is causing younger sibling to sit on the chair), there are three arguments; they are </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ibu</span><span class="font5"> (mother), </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">adek</span><span class="font5"> (younger sibling), and </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kursi</span><span class="font5"> (chair).</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">136</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">The other reason why it is necessary to explore the Indonesian derivative verbs is the fact that no research has been conducted to analyze the Indonesian derivative verbs as stated above. Moreover, only a few Indonesian grammar books discuss the morphological formation of the Indonesian derivative verbs from the derivational perspective. Apart from that, on the one hand, none has analyzed the Indonesian derivative verbs using the theory of typology; on the other hand, the formation of the Indonesian derivative verbs from the typological perspective is worth exploring. It is expected the findings of the present study may contribute to the typology of the Indonesian verbs. As well, it is expected that the findings of the present study can give a new paradigm of the conception of the affixation used to form the Indonesian derivative verb as the core predicate which binds the arguments needed to construct a clausal structure.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">This current study is an initial study conducted to investigate the Indonesian derivative verbs. As a study of typological linguistics, it only focuses on the formation of the derivative verb and the structure of its arguments. Based on what is described above, the writer feels interested and finds it necessary to further investigate the Indonesian derivative verbs from the typological perspective. The problems of the study can be formulated in three questions. They are 1) how the Indonesian derivative verbs are formed, 2) what affixes can be used to form the Indonesian derivative verbs; 3) what affixes can be used to form the Indonesian transitive and intransitive verbs.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The method used to analyze the formation of the Indonesian derivative verbs is the descriptive qualitative method. This method can systematically, factually, and accurately give the data needed and the nature of and relationship among the phenomena investigated so that the scientific picture of the data can be obtained. The theory used to analyze the formation of the Indonesian derivative verbs is the Theory of Typology, which is basically intended to answer the question of what language X is like. As far as this current study is concerned, the steps taken from collecting to analyzing the data are as follows. First, the novel “Sang Pemimpi”, as the data source, was scanned using the </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">pdf</span><span class="font5"> format. After that, it</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">137</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">was converted into the word format. The data were identified from the converted file using the </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">software antcount</span><span class="font5">. The data were processed first by opening the </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">software antcount</span><span class="font5"> from which the file of the data source (the converted file) was opened. After the file was opened, the data were collected by typing the derivational affixes. Before pressing </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">start</span><span class="font5"> the column </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">search</span><span class="font5"> was pressed, then the affix </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng</span><span class="font5">-, for example, was typed. The corpus program as the data provider showed all the verbs with the prefix {</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">meng</span><span class="font5">-} on the computer screen, which were then saved on a separate file. Similar steps were taken to find the other derivational affixes such as {</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">-i</span><span class="font5">}, {</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ter</span><span class="font5">-}, {</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber</span><span class="font5">-}, {</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">ber-an</span><span class="font5">}, and {-</span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">kan</span><span class="font5">}. The derived verbs saved on the separate file were then processed based on the problems of the study, namely the formation of derivative verbs. They were classified based on the classes of the word roots, namely noun, adjective, precategory, and verb. Then what affixes could be attached to noun, what affixes could be added to adjective, what affixes could be attached to precategory and which ones could be attached to verb were classified. Finally, what affixes could be used to form intransitive derivative verbs and which ones could be used to form transitive derivative verbs were grouped as well.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Theoretical Framework</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The main theory used to analyze the Indonesian derivative verbs is the Theory of Typological Linguistics. It is commonly used to describe the universality of a language. The basic principle in the typological linguistic study includes scientific-descriptive and cross languages. It is basically intended to answer the question of what language X is like. It can explain that (1) the structure of one language can be compared with that of another, and (2) one language is different from another (Mallinson and Blake, 1991; Dixon, 2010; Comrie, 1988, and Artawa, 1988).</span></p>

They were used in 182 clauses. The second derivative verbs mostly used in the data source are derived from the word root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">verb</span><span class="font5">; it was found that they were used in 104 clauses. The third derivative verbs mostly used in the data source are derived from the word root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">noun</span><span class="font5">; it was found that they were used in 53 clauses, and the derivative verbs derived from the word root </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">adjective</span><span class="font5"> were the least of all. It was found that they were used in 39 clauses. The affixes which productively formed the derivative verbs were the affix </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–kan</span><span class="font5">. It was found that it was used in 130 clauses, followed by the affix </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–i</span><span class="font5"> used in 77 clauses, the affix </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">–meng </span><span class="font5">141</span></p>
