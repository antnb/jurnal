<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Backgruond</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Syntax is a part of grammar that is described in terms of taxonomy (i.e. the classificatory list) of the range of different types in syntactic structure found in the language which serve as grammatical function. Syntax has its goal of grammar construction of producing the language sentences.(Brown and Miller; 1991).In linguistics, an adjunct is an optional or structurally dispensable part of a sentence, clause, or phrase, when removed, will not affect the remainder of the sentence being an element of clause structure with adverbial function. An adjunct is not an argument (nor is it a predicative expression), and an argument is not an adjunct. The argument-adjunct distinction is central in most theories of syntax and semantics. In English, there are</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">some forms of adverbial. They could be taken in the form of adverb, adverb phrase, temporal noun phrase, and prepositional phrase. There are some words can be changed in the other spelling when an adverbs are formed from an adjective.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;CONCLUSION</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font3">Based on the previous discussion and analysis abovean adverb of manner is usually considered as the optional constituent of the sentence, because without the presence of</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">this adverb, the sentence is still well formed. The positions of manner adjunct which can be filled by the form of manner adverb can occurs in the initial position (before the subject), medial position, and final position. The position of manner adjunct which can be filled by the form of prepositional phrase can occurs in the initial position and end position of the sentence. But it does not occur in the medial position. The function of manner adjunct as the modifier to the nucleus sentence. The adjunct of manner can be filled by form of adverb of manner which can be formed by adding – </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ly</span><span class="font3"> to corresponding adjectives. Such as: </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">furious</span><span class="font3"> + (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">ly) suffix,</span><span class="font3"> therefore it becomes </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">furiously</span><span class="font3">. This word can be formed by an adjective to be an adverb by the process of suffixation in which the world class is changed.</span></p>
