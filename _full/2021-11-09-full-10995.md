<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal berjudul The Analysis of Syntactic Structures in The Relative Clause With Reference To Steinbeck’s The Pearl difokuskan pada analisis Klausa Relatif, terutama dalam pola, struktur, dan jenis klausa relatif. Tujuan dari penelitian adalah untuk mengetahui dan mengidentifikasi pola-pola, struktur, dan jenis klausa relatif yang ditemukan dalam novel berjudul The Pearl oleh Steinbeck pada tahun 1947. Analisis ini berdasarkan pada teori Generative Transformation oleh Chomsky dalam bukunya Syntactic Structure (1971) dan didukung oleh Radford dalam bukunya Transformational Syntax: A Student Guide to Chomsky’s Extended Theory (1988). Temuan penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa ada tiga pola yang menonjol dari klausa relatif dan struktur klausa relatif adalah klausa tertanam yang dimodifikasi kata benda dalam frasa kata benda. Hal ini dapat tertanam subjek atau objek. Klausa relatif ditandai dengan kata ganti relatif atau kata kerja relatif. Ada tiga jenis klausa relatif yang ditemukan dalam penelitian ini, seperti: restrictive, nonrestrictive, dan free. Analisis dalam penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa semua jenis klausa relatif utama yang ditemukan dalam data. Mereka adalah klausa relatif restrictive, nonrestrictive, dan free yang diperkenalkan oleh kata ganti relatif </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">who, whom, whose, which, or that, </span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">dan dengan kata kerja bantu relatif, seperti </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">when, where, or why</span><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">. Dalam hal fungsi mereka, tergantung pada jenis mereka dapat berfungsi sebagai subjek dan objek yang menyediakan informasi dari kata benda sebelumnya sebagai anteseden atau memberikan informasi tambahan dari kata benda sebelumnya.</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci:</span></p>
<p><span class="font4" style="font-style:italic;">Struktur Sintaksis, Klausa Relatif, Generative Transformation</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>Types of Relative Clause</span></h1>
<p><span class="font5">This analysis adopted three types of relative clause, in accordance with the Radford’s theory (1988) such as; Restrictive, Nonrestrictive, and Free.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Restrictive</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">This type was the prominent one used in novel </span><span class="font5" style="font-style:italic;">The Pearl</span><span class="font5"> by Steinbeck in 1947. For examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The best and happiest pearl buyer was he [who bought for the lowest prices] (Steinbeck, 1947: 23)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A country [where there was little game] managed to live (Steinbeck, 1947: 42)</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nonrestrictive</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The feature of nonrestrictive is not restricted to the reference of a noun phrase but provides the extra information of the major of a noun phrase. For examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The little tree frogs, [that lived near the stream] twittered like birds (Steinbeck, 1947: 48)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The birds, [which spent the day in the brushland] came at night to the little pools (Steinbeck, 1947: 45)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He lighted a candle and came to them, [where they crouched in a corner] (Steinbeck, 1947: 36)</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Free</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The last type of relative clause used in this research is free; it refers to the primary of the noun phrase which is vanished (headless), mainly in a surface structure (Radford, 1981: 358).</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">For examples;</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They would find it </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">where</span><span class="font5"> Kino and Juana had rested (Steinbeck, 1947: 43)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His hand strayed limply to the place </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">where</span><span class="font5"> the pearl was hidden under his clothes (Steinbeck, 1947: 43)</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">A relative clause is a subordinate clause that modifies a noun phrase, generally a noun. It starts with a relative pronoun, such as [who, whom, whose, which, or that] or a relative adverb, such as [when, where, or why].</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">The patterns of the relative clause could be:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Relative Clause = Relative Pronoun or adverb + subject + verb</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Relative Clause = Relative Pronoun as a Subject + verb</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">c) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clause + Relative Pronoun + Clause = Relative Clause</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">Based on the structure, a relative clause is embedded clause that is added to a free clause or a sentence. It can be embedded in the subject or the object.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">For examples:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The relative clause is embedded the subject. For example:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">A country [where there was little game] managed to live (Steinbeck, 1947: 42)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The relative clause is embedded the object. For example:</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font5">The best and happiest pearl buyer was he [who bought for the lowest prices] (Steinbeck, 1947: 23)</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">Most of the relative clauses used in the novel entitled “The Pearl” by Steinbeck are headless clause, especially in second clause. The purpose of relative clause itself is to change the noun phrase. Mostly, the Noun Phrase position is an antecedent of relative clause as a subject and object.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5">There are three types of relative clause found in the novel entitled “The Pearl” by Steinbeck in 1947, such as; </span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">Restrictive, Nonrestrictive, and Free.</span></p>
