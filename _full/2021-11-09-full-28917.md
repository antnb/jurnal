<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">The title of this paper is “Grammatical Adjustment in Novel Kaleidoscope”. In this paper I try to analyze and discuss the adjustment of grammar in the translation taken from novel Kaleidoscope by Daniele Steel. In analyzing the grammatical adjustment in translation, I applied the theory of Mildered L.Larson in her book entitled Meaning Based Translation as my main theory. I also applied the theory of Catford, in his book entitled A linguistic Theory of translation and supported by other theories that are considered relevant to the topic of discussion. The data of this paper is taken from English novel Kaleidoscope by Daniele Steel and its translation by Therss Susilastuti with the same title. Based on the result of the research and analysis it is found that the grammatical adjustment can be classified into six namely : adjustment from clause into phrase, adjustment from pronoun into noun, adjustment from adjective into noun, adjustment from active into passive, adjustment from passive into active and sentence structure adjustment in translation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Key word : Grammatical, Adjustment</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In this era, language plays as important role, either is written or spoken forms. Language is also used to convey ideas, thoughts, experiences and others that speaker of writer has in their mind.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">English language is considered to be an international language which is used by the people all over the world and Indonesia too. But to understand English well needs a lot of time and it is not an easy task.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">There are many kind of sciences written in English or maybe in another foreign language. Of course it is difficult to understand. We need to translate the text into Indonesian so we can find the meaning of the text easily.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Translation is transfer of meaning from one form of language to the other language. Translation is not an easy task. The translator must keep the meaning of the source language text in the form of target language or receptor language, so translation is not an easy thing to do.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The meaning must constantly be carried out in the target language and the form can be adopted. We have seen that each language has its own distinctive form for representing the meaning. Therefore in translation the same meaning has to be expressed in another language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Translation has often been defined with reference to meaning, a translation is said to have the same meaning as original or the source language. Its mean that the translator translates a word of source language and then uses the receptor language form in natural way in order to reproduce or express the same meaning as intended by the source language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">But sometimes discovering the meaning with a natural way in the target language is a problem because one word of source language can be used to represent several alternative meaning and so it depends on the context. Once again, the meaning must have the secondary meaning as long as the meaning conforms with the context. We can say that meaning is one important thing in translation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Very often that a single word in the source language will need to be translated by several words. This principal is not limited to lexical items that it is also true that the same grammatical pattern may express several quite different meanings. For example, the English possessive phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">my house</span><span class="font2">may mean </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the house I own, the house I rent, the house I live in, the house I built, or the house for which I drew up the plants</span><span class="font2"> (Larson, 1984:7-8)</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Grammatical Adjustment In Novel Kaleidoscope</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1. Grammatical Adjustment From Clause Into Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1.</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I don’t care</span><span class="font2">, you’re the best. I want you. (page 75)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">TL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tidak peduli</span><span class="font2">, kau yang terbaik. Aku mau kau. (page 99)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example 1 the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I don’t care</span><span class="font2"> is a clause in the source language text. This sentence is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tidak peduli</span><span class="font2"> in target language text. As we can see </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tidak peduli</span><span class="font2"> is a phrase. But it doesn’t change the meaning of the sentence, although the subject (I) was not translated in the target language text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2.</span><span class="font2"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I don’t want</span><span class="font2"> anyone else. I want you. (page 75)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">TL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tidak mau</span><span class="font2"> orang lain. Aku mau kau. (page 99)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example 2 there is a clause </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I don’t want</span><span class="font2"> in the source language text. This clause is translated into a phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">tidak mau</span><span class="font2"> in the target language text. The subject (I) is not translated but the readers understand the meaning of this sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.2. Grammatical Adjustment From Pronoun Into Noun</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SL : Have you lost someone in the war ?</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font2"> hated to ask……. your husband? (page 32)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">TL : Anda kehilangan seseorang dalam perang ?</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sam</span><span class="font2"> tidak suka harus menanyakan…… suami anda ? (page 47)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example 1 there is a sentence in the source language text </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He hated to ask. He</span><span class="font2"> is a pronoun. But </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sam</span><span class="font2"> in the target language text, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sam</span><span class="font2"> is a profer noun. The translator changes </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He</span><span class="font2"> by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sam</span><span class="font2"> because she did not want the readers to be confused in reading the sentence. But the changing doesn’t produce a new meaning or message.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2"> shook her head, her eyes serious (page 32)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">TL : </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Solange</span><span class="font2"> menggelengkan kepalanya, sorot matanya bersungguh –</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">sungguh (page 48)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In example 2 there is word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2"> in the source language text then </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2"> is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Solange</span><span class="font2"> in the target language text. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Solange</span><span class="font2"> is a proper noun, by translating </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Solange</span><span class="font2"> the sentence in the target language is more easily understood by the readers.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>6.</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Grammatical adjustment in translation means the translator change the form of the text when he translate the text. For example a pronoun in SL changed into noun in TL. We see that the form changed but the meaning produce in TL is not different from SL. The aim of grammatical adjustment in translation is to make the sentence more easily to understand by the readers.because we know that between English and Indonesia is grammatically different. We need to adjust the foem but we didn’t have to change the meaning. The meaning must be the same as the source language text.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Grammatical adjustment in translation is belong to idiomatic translation. Idiomatic translation use the natural forms of the receptor language, both in the grammatical constructions and the choice of lexical item. A truly idiomatic translation does not sound like a translation. It sounds like like it was written originally in the receptor language. Therefore, a good translator will try to translate idiomatically.</span></p>
