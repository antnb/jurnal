<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">TRANSLATION OF IDIOMATIC EXPRESSIONS IN </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ECLIPSE</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> AND IN</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">GERHANA</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">By:</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">Skripsi yang berjudul </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“The Translation of Idiomatic Expressions in Eclipse and in Gerhana”</span><span class="font1"> bertujuan untuk menjelaskan tipe-tipe idiomatic expressions dan cara dari makna </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">idiomatic expressions</span><span class="font1"> yang berasal dari novel Eclipse diterjemahkan ke Gerhana. Data penelitian ini diambil dari novel Eclipse oleh Stephenie Meyer dan novel terjemahannya Gerhana oleh Monica Dwi Chresnayani. Novel ini dipilih untuk menjadi sumber data karena mengandung banyak </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">idiomatic expressions</span><span class="font1"> yang berhubungan dengan penelitian ini. Teori utama yang digunakan untuk mengklasifikasikan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">idiomatic expressions</span><span class="font1"> dan cara dari makna </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">idiomatic expressions</span><span class="font1"> diterjemahkan ke bahasa tujuan adalah teori </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">idiomatic expressions</span><span class="font1"> dan teori </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lexical equivalent</span><span class="font1"> yang diusulkan oleh Larson (1998).</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Ada tiga jenis idiomatic expressions yang ditemukan di dalam penelitian ini: figurative language, proverb dan saying. Makna dari idiomatic expressions yang terdapat di novel dapat di terjemahkan melalui dua cara: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lexical equivalence when the concept is known</span><span class="font1"> dan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lexical equivalence when the concept is unknown</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lexical equivalence when the concept is known</span><span class="font1"> dibagi menjadi lima jenis yaitu: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">non literal translation</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">descriptive phrase</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">related word as equivalent</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">generic specific words</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">secondary and figurative senses</span><span class="font1">. Sedangkan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Lexical equivalence when the concept is unknown</span><span class="font1"> dibagi menjadi tiga jenis: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Modifying generic words</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">modifying a loan words and cultural substitutes</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci : idiomatic expressions, ways, lexical equivalence, translation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Each language has its own linguistic rule and system. English rules are different from Indonesian language. Consequently, a word may be translated into word, phrase, clause, or even sentence. There are some words or phrases that cannot be translated literally. One of them is idiomatic expressions. This study was focused on the types idiomatic expressions found in the novel and the way used to transfer the meaning in the target language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font0">2.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Words and phrases in the sentence that contained of idiomatic expressions were marked and recorded.</span></p></li></ul>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The selected data were analyzed to describe the ways used to transfer the meaning of idiomatic expressions in the source language novel into the target language novel.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;THE IDIOMATIC EXPRESSIONS IN THE NOVEL ECLIPSE AND IN GERHANA AND THE WAYS USED TO TRANSFER THE MEANING IN THE TARGET LANGUAGE</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on Larson (1998) figurative languages are devided into seven types: metonymy, synecdoche, idiom, euphemism, hyperbole, metaphor, and simile, proverb and saying.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metonymy</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : You’ve been very quiet,” Edward observed. “Did the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">plane </span><span class="font1">make you sick?” (65)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL :</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Sejak tadi kau diam saja,” komentar Edward. “Apakah </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">terbang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">membuatmu mual?</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> (81)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the sentence above, it can be concluded that the thing which makes someone sick is not </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the plane</span><span class="font1"> itself but </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the flight</span><span class="font1">. The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">plane</span><span class="font1"> refers to the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">flight</span><span class="font1"> since those words have a close relationship.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Synecdoche</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Though the afternoon was the only time I spent away from Edward, it was enough to make me restless, and the </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">hours </span><span class="font1">always dragged. (10)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL :</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Walaupun aku hanya tidak bertemu Edward pada sore hari, itu sudah cukup membuatku gelisah, dan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">waktu </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rasanya selalu berjalan lambat</span><span class="font1"> (20)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The bold word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hours</span><span class="font1"> refers to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">time</span><span class="font1">. The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hour</span><span class="font1"> if it translated literally means sixty minutes and it is part of time. In this case, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hours</span><span class="font1"> substitutes for the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">time</span><span class="font1"> since it is part of the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hours</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>5.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Idiom</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : “I would always be </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">waiting in the wings</span><span class="font1">, hopping for that to happen.” (502)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aku akan selalu </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">menunggu di dekat kalian</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, berharap itu terjadi. (549)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The idiom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">waiting in the wings</span><span class="font1"> means being ready to take over a particular job or being used in a particular situation when needed. The idiom above cannot be translated literally because it will make no sense, the translator need to use idiomatic translation in order to maintain the meaning in the source language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>5.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Euphemism</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Sam’s grandfather </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">had passed away </span><span class="font1">before he was born, and his father has never been around. (116)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kakek Sam </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">meninggal </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sebelum ia lahir, dan ayahnya tak pernah ada.</span><span class="font1"> (135)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">passed away</span><span class="font1"> means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">die.</span><span class="font1"> The author did not use the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">die</span><span class="font1"> to avoid something unpleasant and made it more polite and acceptable. In this case the translator translated the expression </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">had passed away</span><span class="font1"> into Indonesian </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">meninggal</span><span class="font1">, the translator translated it literally.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark24"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark25"></a>5.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyperbole</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">: </span><span class="font1">Jacob’s pain </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">cut me deeper </span><span class="font1">than my own. (8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">:</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kepedihan Jacob </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">mengoyak-ngoyak hatiku </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lebih dalam daripada kepedihanku sendiri.</span><span class="font1"> (18)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The meaning of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jacob’s pain </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">cut me deeper </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">than my own</span><span class="font1"> is not about the sharpness of someone pain, since pain is an abstract thing and it is a feeling, therefore, it is impossible to use it as a cutting tool. The phrase that is used refer to sadness. The implicit meaning is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jacob’s pain made me very sad more than my pain</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark26"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark27"></a>5.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metaphor</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : He dropped it by the door. “You said she needed a </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">space heater</span><span class="font1">, and here </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I am</span><span class="font1">.” (490)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Jacob menjatuhkannya di dekat pintu. “Kaubilang tadi dia butuh </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pemanas</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, jadi inilah </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">aku</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.”</span><span class="font1"> (535)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In this context, things that are compared are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">space heater</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I am</span><span class="font1">. The meaning of word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">space heater</span><span class="font1"> in this context refers to something or someone that can make you warm. In this case, it can be concluded that “I am” is someone that can make and keep somebody or her warm.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>5.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simile</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : He was hiding inside a windowless room behind a door </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">as thick as bank vault’s, </span><span class="font1">guarded outside by armed men,.. (163)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Royce bersembunyi di dalam ruangan tak berjendela, dibalik pintu </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">setebal pintu ruang brangkas di bank</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, dijaga pengawal bersenjata,..</span><span class="font1">(186)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The things that are being compared are door and bank’s vault. The meaning of word bank’s vault refer to something that is hard to enter and really thick. It can be concluded that the door has the same quality, in this case thick, with the bank vault.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark30"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>5.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Proverb and Saying</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : “You know how it is, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">out of sight, out of mind.” </span><span class="font1">(375)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Kau tahu kan kata orang, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">jauh dimata jauh pula di hati.</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">”</span><span class="font1"> (409)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">out of sight, out of mind</span><span class="font1"> in this context means it is hard to get closer to the person when she is not near you, it is really hard to make a person fall in love with you when you are far away from her.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>5.9 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Non Literal Lexical Equivalents</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : He smiled at me, and I could see no trace of apprehension in </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">his jewel like eyes. </span><span class="font1">(232)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Edward tersenyum padaku, dan aku tidak melihat setitik pun kecemasan di </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">matanya yang bagaikan permata itu.</span><span class="font1"> (256)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">his jewel like eyes</span><span class="font1"> in the source language, if it translated literally, will be </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">permatanya bagaikan mata</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. The translator modifies and changes the order to use acceptable sentence structure in the target language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark34"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark35"></a>5.10 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Descriptive Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : That was...”</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">words failed me</span><span class="font1">. “Wow, thats something else.” (261)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Acara tadi..</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">aku tidak bisa mengungkapkannya dengan kata-kata</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">. Wow, lain dari pada yang lain.</span><span class="font1"> (288)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">failed</span><span class="font1"> in the source language is a single word but it is translated into several words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tidak bisa mengungkapkan</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. The words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tidak bisa mengungkapkan</span><span class="font1"> in the target language is used as an equivalent in order to make the meaning clearer.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark36"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark37"></a>5.11 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Using Related words as Equivalents</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Edward said little; he just held me on the bed and let me ruin his shirt, staining it with </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">salt water</span><span class="font1">. (609)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Edward tidak banyak bicara; ia hanya memelukku di tempat tidur dan membiarkan aku menghancurkan kemejanya, menodainya dengan </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">air mata. </span><span class="font1">(661)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Literally, the translation of the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">salt water</span><span class="font1"> is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">air garam</span><span class="font1"> in Indonesian and the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">air mata</span><span class="font1"> is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tears</span><span class="font1"> in English. In this case, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">salt water</span><span class="font1"> in the source language is closely related or has a close meaning to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">air mata</span><span class="font1"> in the target language.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark38"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark39"></a>5.12 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Generic-specific words</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : Alice, don’t you think this is just a little bit controlling? Just a tiny bit </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">psychotic</span><span class="font1">, maybe? (146)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Alice, apa menurutmu ini tidak sedikit sok mengatur? Agak sedikit </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sakit </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mungkin?</span><span class="font1"> (168)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The translator did not translate the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psychotic</span><span class="font1"> literally, but used generic word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sakit</span><span class="font1"> in the target language, to make it more natural in the target language. In this case, the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">psychotic</span><span class="font1"> in the source language takes a role as a more specific term, while </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sakit</span><span class="font1"> in the target language takes a role as more generic term.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark40"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark41"></a>5.13 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Secondary and Figurative senses</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : I </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">racked my brains </span><span class="font1">in panic, trying to remember what i’d ever decided to get her for graduation that she might have seen. (268)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Aku </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">memutar otak </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">panik, berusaha mengingat aku pernah memutuskan member hadiah kelulusan apa untuk Alice, yang mungkin dilihatnya.</span><span class="font1"> (296)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">racked my brain</span><span class="font1"> in the target language is an idiom. The phrase in the target language is translated into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">memutar otak</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. In English it means </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">spin your brain</span><span class="font1">, if it is translated literally it will make no sense.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark42"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark43"></a>5.14 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Equivalence by Modifying a Generic Words</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : They all were curious, and then overwhelmed as they took in the huge room decked out like a chic </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">rave. </span><span class="font1">(368)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Mereka semua penasaran, dan langsung ternganga takjub melihat ruangan besar yang ditata menyerupai </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">tempat nongkrong </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yang chic.</span><span class="font1"> (402)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rave</span><span class="font1"> means a place where people hold a large party in which people dance to fast electronic music and often take illegal drugs. The translator translated the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rave</span><span class="font1"> in the source language into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tempat nongkrong</span><span class="font1"> in the target language since both of words have same function.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.15 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Equivalence by Modifying a Loan Words</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">: </span><span class="font1">He was nervous </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">as a turkey on Thanksgiving all morning</span><span class="font1">. (587)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sepagian dia gelisah </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sepeti kalkun yang akan disembelih untuk perayaan Thanksgiving.</span><span class="font1"> (638)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The translator modified the loan words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Thanksgiving</span><span class="font1"> from the target language into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">perayaan Thanksgiving</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. The translator tried to give clear explanation that thanksgiving is some kind of a party or celebration.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark44"></a><span class="font0" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark45"></a>5.16</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Equivalence by Cultural Substitute</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">SL : I noticed how their eyes widened as they took in all </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">six foot seven inches of</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Jacob’s long body</span><span class="font1">,.. .(77)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">TL : </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kulihat mata mereka membelalak saat melihat </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sosok Jacob yang tinggi menjulang, hamper dua meter,...</span><span class="font1"> (93)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The translator translated the phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">six foot seven inches</span><span class="font1"> in the source language into </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hampir dua meter</span><span class="font1"> in the target language. The translator used cultural substitute in order to make the reader understand the meaning that tried to describe by the author.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark46"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark47"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the analysis in chapter three, it can be concluded that there are three types of idiomatic expressions found in the novel Eclipse by Stephenie Meyer; figurative, proverb and saying. There are seven types of figurative languages found in the novel; metonymy, synecdoche, idiom, euphemism, hyperbole, metaphor, and simile.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the process of translation of figurative languages from English novel into Gerhana in Indonesia version, there are some methods which were applied. It can be translated by changing the form or structure and meaning of the word, phrase or sentence. Regarding equivalent, there are two concepts found when analyzing the data; Lexical equivalent when concepts are known (Shared) and Lexical equivalent when concepts are unknown. Lexical equivalent when concepts are known (Shared) can be divided into five types; non literal equivalents, descriptive phrase, the use of related words as equivalents, genericspecific words, secondary and figurative senses. Lexical equivalent when concepts are unknown are divided into three types; equivalence by modifying a generic word, equivalence by modifying a loan words and equivalence by cultural substitute.</span></p>
