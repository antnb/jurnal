<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>TYPES, FUNCTION AND POSITION OF ADVERBS IN THE YAK MAGAZINE</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">BY</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I GUSTI AYU DEWI KURNIAWATI</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">ENGLISH DEPARTMENT, FACULTY OF LETTERS UDAYANA UNIVERSITY</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>2013</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Jurnal ini berjudul “Types, Function and Position of Adverb In The Yak Magazine”. Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk menemukan jenis-jenis, fungsi dan posisi dari kata keterangan yang ada di dalam artikel “Interview” yang terdapat di majalah The Yak. Teori yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini adalah teori grammar oleh Huddleston (1984), Quirk (1985) dan Swan (1996). Teori Huddleston khususnya mengenai klasifikasi kata keterangan didukung oleh teori pendukung dari C.E. Eckersley dan J.M Eckersley (1965). Dalam teorinya Huddleston (1985) membagi kata keterangan menjadi dua jenis yaitu secara morpologi dan secara sintaksis didalam bahasa Inggris. Secara morpologi kata keterangan dibagi atas tiga bagian yaitu simple adverb, compound adverb and complex adverb. Kemudian secara sintaksis kata keterangan dibagi menjadi sepuluh bagian yaitu adverb of degree, adverb of manner, adverb of time, adverb of frequency, adverb of place, adverb of affirmative, adverb of probability, adverb of duration, relative adverb and interrogative adverb. Selanjutnya teori dari Quirk (1985) khususnya mengenai fungsi kata keterangan didukung pula teori oleh Curme (1966). Quirk (1985) menyatakan bahwa kata keterangan mempunyai tiga fungsi yaitu adverb as adverbial, adverb as modifier dan adverb as complement of preposition. Teori dari Swan (1995) khususnya mengenai posisi kata keterangan dengan teori pendukung oleh Eastwood (2002). Dalam teorinya Swan menyatakan bahwa posisi kata keterangan yaitu front position, mid position dan end position. Metode yang digunakan dalam menganalisis penelitian ini adalah metode deskriptif qualitatif. Pembahasan data diawali dengan membaca dan memilih artikel dalam majalah, memberi tanda dan mencatat kalimat-kalimat yang mengandung adverb, kemudian menganalisa jenis, fungsi dan posisi kata keterangan.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Kata kunci : </span><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">types, function, position</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverb as one of the elements of grammar takes an important role in structure.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Since adverbs can influence the expression of English grammatically, they various in types some morphologically and syntactically. Morphologically adverb can be classified into three parts, they are simple adverb, compound adverb and complex adverb. Syntactically, adverb can be classified into ten parts, they are adverb of</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">degree, adverb of manner, adverb of time, adverb of place, adverb of frequency, adverb duration, adverb of affirmative, adverb of probability, relative adverb and interrogative adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problem of the study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on the current study, this writing is focused on some problems as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of adverb occurs in The Yak Magazine?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the functions of adverbs occurring in The Yak Magazine?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where are their positions in The Yak Magazine?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are three main aims of doing this study related to the problems proposed above; those are, to identify types of adverb, the function of adverb and the position of adverb taken from “Interview” article in The Yak Magazine.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>

Research method used in this writing comprise three steps; they are data source, method and technique of collecting data and method and technique of analyzing data.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>4.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>

All data collected were identified, classified and finally analyzed based on the theory is used. The theory of grammar proposed by Huddleston in his book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Introduction to the Grammar of English</span><span class="font1"> (1984) was used to analyze the types of adverb. The theory of grammar proposed by Quirk in his book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">A Comprehensive Grammar of the English</span><span class="font1"> (1985) was used to analyze the function of adverb. The theory of grammar proposed by Swan in his book entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Practical English Usage</span><span class="font1"> (1995) was used to analyze the position of adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Types, Function and Position of Adverbs in “The Yak” Magazine</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The analysis of adverbs in The Yak magazine are divided into three parts of explanation; they are types of adverb, the function of adverb and position of adverb. </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 Based on morphological analysis</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Morphologically according to their lexical stems, adverb can be divided into three parts are simple adverb, compound adverb and complex adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simple adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverbs are categorized into simple adverb, that is the ones with simple lexical stems. The primary function is to qualify verbs and other parts of speech. Many simple adverbs denote position and direction such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">back, down, near, out, just, only, etc</span><span class="font1">. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Paris at that time </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">only</span><span class="font1"> showed MTV. (YM,2010 : 70)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It’s true I’ve shared of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ups</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">downs.</span><span class="font1"> (YM, 2008 : 35)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The italic words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">only, ups</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">downs</span><span class="font1"> are kinds of simple adverb because they</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">consist of one lexical stem; besides, these adverbs also give a qualify meaning to the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Compound Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Compound adverb are words formed by combining or joining two words into one word. The number of compound adverb is quite small, for example, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">downstairs, sometimes, furthermore, hereby, somewhere, somewhat, somehow, anywhere, something</span><span class="font1">. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sometimes</span><span class="font1"> he makes it happen. (YM, 2008:68)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Wherever</span><span class="font1"> I am I hope it’s still green and lush. (YM, 200</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">8</span><span class="font1">:44)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The italic words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sometimes</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wherever</span><span class="font1"> are kinds of compound adverbs because they are formed by two words into one word. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sometimes</span><span class="font1"> comes from words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">some (adverb)</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">time (noun</span><span class="font1">) which denote adverb of frequency of an event. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Wherever </span><span class="font1">comes from the words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">where (adverb)</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ever (adverb)</span><span class="font1"> which is denote adverb of place.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Complex Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The great majority of adverbs have complex stems and the most of are formed by highly productive process of suffixing –ly to an adjective. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…so many </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">completely</span><span class="font1"> different countries are nested on the continent. (YM, 2008:35)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…and for me it’s more </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">personally</span><span class="font1"> satisfying. (YM, 2008:76)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The italic words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">completely</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">personally</span><span class="font1"> are kinds of complex adverbs because they are formed by adding –ly to the end of the word. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Completely</span><span class="font1"> comes from words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">complete (adjective)</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ly (suffix)</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Personally</span><span class="font1"> comes from words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">personal (adjective) </span><span class="font1">+ </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ly (suffix)</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.2 Based on Syntactical Analysis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Syntactically, analysis of adverb can be classified into ten parts; they are adverb of degree, adverb of manner, adverb of time, adverb of frequency, adverb of place, adverb of affirmative, adverb of probability, adverb of duration, relative adverb and interrogative adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Degree</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverb of degree is used to explain degree or to what extent an action is done. Adverb of degree includes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nearly, completely, barely, quite, just, too, enough, very, all, rather, so, almost</span><span class="font1">, etc. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I suppose I was closer to my mother, but we had a </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font1"> happy family. (YM, 2008:40)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font1"> is modifies the adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">happy</span><span class="font1"> and makes another word stronger.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Manner</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Generally this kind of adverb is formed by adding –ly and can describe how someone does something or how something happens or is done. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;We feel </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">gracefully</span><span class="font1"> with the music. (YM, 2010:76)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Gracefully is derived from </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">graceful (adjective)</span><span class="font1"> + </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">-ly (suffix)</span><span class="font1"> then becomes an adverb. Adverb of manner normally modifies verb or placed before or after the verb and adjective.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Time</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverb of time may express of present time </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(now, immediately, today</span><span class="font1">), </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">past time (ago, before, yesterday,)</span><span class="font1"> and the future </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(afterward, soon, tomorrow)</span><span class="font1">. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">now</span><span class="font1"> we have international standard musician. (YM, 2010:76).</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">now</span><span class="font1"> denote an adverb of time in the present time.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark35"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark36"></a>5.2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Frequency</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of adverb express how often an event is happen. The words belong to the adverb frequency are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">seldom, rarely, always, often, never, sometimes, usually,</span><span class="font1"> etc. For Example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;My good feeling </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">usually</span><span class="font1"> dictate my decision. (YM, 2010:80).</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The adverb of frequency in the data above is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">usually</span><span class="font1"> placed in the middle of the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark37"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark38"></a>5.2.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Duration</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of adverb is used to explain how long an activity is done. The words belong to the adverb of duration are always, while, never, still, ever, etc. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;..and we’re </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">still</span><span class="font1"> developing the product. (YM, 2010:70)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverb of duration in the data above is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">still</span><span class="font1"> which expresses a length of time in</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">“developing the product”.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark39"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark40"></a>5.2.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Place</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Adverb of place is used to answer question may denote place, denote motion to (forward, onward), motion from (away, hence). Adverbs of place usually modifies verbs. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Take what we can from them move </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">forward</span><span class="font1"> with the lesson. (YM 2008:43)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">forward</span><span class="font1"> is kind of adverb of place may denote motion to and modify the verb moved.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark41"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark42"></a>5.2.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interrogative Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Most common interrogative adverb is used to inquire concerning time, place, manner, cause and degree. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where</span><span class="font1"> did you grow up? (YM, 2008:43)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">where</span><span class="font1"> is a kinds of interrogative adverb and is used to ask about information.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark43"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark44"></a>5.2.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Relative Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of adverb is more complex in their function to modify verb, adjective and adverb. Additionally they relate, subordinate and join clause. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Everything is good </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">until</span><span class="font1"> the money run out. (YM, 2010:68)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">until</span><span class="font1"> has function as adverb which modifies the adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">good</span><span class="font1"> and is used to related two clauses into one long sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark45"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark46"></a>5.2.9 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb of Probability</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of adverb is used to explain what is said likely to happen or to prove the truth. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;That’s a typical day for me </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">might be</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">. </span><span class="font1">(YM, 2008:40)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">might be</span><span class="font1"> used to say that something is possibly true or uncertain prediction.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark47"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark48"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Function of Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are three functions of adverb:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark49"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark50"></a>6.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb as Adverbial</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of adverb usually expresses about time, degree and manner. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…it was moved so </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">slowly</span><span class="font1">. (YM, 2008:35)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">slowly</span><span class="font1"> is formed by a formulated words </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">slow (adjective)</span><span class="font1"> + -</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ly (suffix</span><span class="font1">).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark51"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark52"></a>6.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb as Modifier</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Modifier of Verb is kind of modifier is used to explain the activity done by the subject. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…if they clean </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">up</span><span class="font1"> their act. (YM, 2010:72)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">up</span><span class="font1"> is a kind of adverb which modifies verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cleaned</span><span class="font1"> and belongs to the simple adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark53"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark54"></a>6.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of adjective</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">An adverb may pre modifies an adjective and is used as an intensifier </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">very.</span><span class="font1"> For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The D’or label has been </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font1"> successful. (YM 2008:35)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above the word </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">very</span><span class="font1"> modifies the adjective </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">successful</span><span class="font1"> and has meaning how the label develops.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark55"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark56"></a>6.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">An adverb may pre modifies another adverb and functions as an intensifier. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Life has become </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">so much</span><span class="font1"> more interesting. (YM, 2010:76)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above the adverb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">so</span><span class="font1"> modifies another adverb that is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">much.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark57"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark58"></a>6.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of Pronoun, Pre-determiner and Numeral</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Intensifying adverbs can pre-modify indefinite pronouns, pre-determiner and cardinal numeral. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A wedding ring worn for over 40 years by the same man… (YM, 2008:44)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the data above adverb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">over</span><span class="font1"> pre-modifies the cardinal numeral 40, which is the determiner in this sentence is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">40 years.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark59"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark60"></a>6.2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of Prepositional Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">This kind of function adverb is commonly placed in the medial of the sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I’m coming </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">through</span><span class="font1"> that at that moment. (YM, 2008:35)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word t</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">hrough</span><span class="font1"> modifies the prepositional phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">at that moment</span><span class="font1"> and gives more meaning (intensifier) to the verb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">coming.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark61"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark62"></a>6.2.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of Noun Phrase</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">A few intensifier may pre-modifies noun phrase; the noun phrase is normally indefinite noun. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The island has </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">such</span><span class="font1"> a versatile range of independent designers. (YM, 2008:44)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The word </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">such</span><span class="font1"> is modify the noun phrase “a versatile range” which </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">such</span><span class="font1"> function as intensifier.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark63"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark64"></a>6.2.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modifier of Clause</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">As a clause modifier, adverb is used to modify the whole clause. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1.</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Maybe</span><span class="font1">, in a few more years. (YM, 2008:43)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">As the explanation above, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">maybe</span><span class="font1"> modifies the whole clause. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Maybe</span><span class="font1"> has function as subjunctive which expresses about possibility.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark65"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark66"></a>6.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adverb as Complement of Preposition</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">A number of adverb, signifying of this kind of adverbs are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">through, from, under, here</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">there, along</span><span class="font1">, etc. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I had a friend who sailed from </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1"> to Australia. (YM, 2008:72)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the data above here as the place adverb and has function as complement of preposition.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark67"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark68"></a>7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Position of Adverb</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark69"></a>7.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Front Position</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The adverb which has the front position is usually placed before the subject of the sentence. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Actually I wanted to be a genetic engineer. (YM, 2010:76)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From the data above is a kind of adverb of manner placed in the beginning of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">sentence which modifies the whole clause in the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark70"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark71"></a>7.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Mid Position of Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The term mid position is used here for those adverbs which are normally placed with the verb or after the unstressed finites of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">be, have, do,</span><span class="font1"> and the other auxiliary and modal finites. For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">really</span><span class="font1"> had to sell him idea….(YM, 2010:72)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The adverb in the data above is placed between subject and verb which expresses mid position of adverb.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark72"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark73"></a>7.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;End Position of Adverb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">All example of adverb can come in his term position especially with prepositional phrase</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There’s one of the best British international school </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">here</span><span class="font1">. (YM, 2010: 72)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The adverb in the data above is relevant to theory concerned that the adverb comes after the long prepositional phrase </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the best British international school</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark74"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark75"></a>8. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Dealing with the types of adverbs, it was found that adverb in “The Yak” can be classified morphologically and syntactically. Morphologically, adverb can be classified into three types: simple, compound and complex adverb. Syntactically, adverb can be classified into: adverb of degree, adverb of manner, adverb of time, adverb of frequency, adverb of duration, adverb of place, adverb of interrogative, adverb of relative, adverb affirmative and adverb of probability. The functions of adverbs in this writing are as adverbial which usually expresses about manner, action, etc. There were found seven types of adverb as modifier in the data such as : modifier of verb, modifier of adjectives, modifier of prepositional phrase, modifier of noun phrase, modifier of clause and modifier of pronoun, pre-determiner and numeral and adverbs may function as complement of preposition.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The positions of adverbs found in this writing are front position, mid position and end position. Types of adverbs appear in the front position such as adverb of time, place, manner and adverbs of interrogative which regularly appear in this position. The position of this adverb is usually put before the subject or at the beginning of the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">clause. Types of adverbs which appear in the mid positions are adverbs of frequency and adverbs of degree. The term of mid position is placed between subject and verb or after auxiliary verbs. Usually an adverb is put in the mid position because it is not the main focus of the sentence. In the term of end position adverb is used for the adverb placed after the verb or after other complement of the sentence. The kinds of adverb that appear in the end position are adverb of manner and adverb of place. Usually these adverbs are put in the end position because there is the main focus of the sentence.</span></p>
