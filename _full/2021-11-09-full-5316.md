<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak:</span></h1>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Banyak kata kerja dalam bahasa inggris yang bisa dikombinasikan dengan kata keterangan dan kata depan atau yang biasa disebut dengan particle. Kombinasi ini menciptakan arti baru yang berbeda dari arti komponen penyusunnya atau biasa disebut frase verba. Tujuan dari artikel ini adalah untuk menganalisis tipe dan structure yang membangun frase verba yang ditemukan di teen-lit “Understudy to Miss Perfect”. Studi ini menggunakan teknik kualitatif dan data dikumpulkan melalui studi pustaka. Teori Quirk (1985) di A Comprehensive Grammar of The English Language menganalisis bahwa frase verba dapat dibagi menjadi 2 yaitu frase verba transitif (diikuti objek) dan frase verba intransitif (tidak diikuti objek). Studi ini menggunakan diagram pohon dan pelabelan dalam kurung untuk merepresentasikan struktur yang membangun frase verba.</span></p>
<p><span class="font0">Kata kunci: kata kerja, frase verba, struktur pembangun</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Particularly, a phrasal verb is a phrase consisting of a verb followed by one or more prepositions which all together are treated as a single verb. For example the word “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">get</span><span class="font1">” and “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">get up</span><span class="font1">” are verbs however they have different meaning. It has idiomatic meaning and if the people use it frequently their English will be more natural. For example: verbs with particles </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">up, down, on, off, over, away, in</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">out</span><span class="font1">. Phrasal verb is quite difficult because its meaning cannot be predicted from each part individually. In order to avoid misunderstanding in speaking or writing something, it is very important to understand phrasal verbs. Besides, people can not speak or understand English well without knowing phrasal verbs. The increase of using phrasal verbs will obviously enrich the vocabularies of learners. Therefore, it is very interesting to analyze the phrasal verb especially its constituent structure in the teen-lit “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Understudy to Miss Perfec</span><span class="font1">t”.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Types of Phrasal Verb</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transitive Phrasal Verb</span></p></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">One type of phrasal verb is transitive phrasal verb where is not followed by object. Some transitive phrasal verbs are separable. The object is placed between the verb and the particle. Besides, some phrasal verbs are inseparable and the object is placed after the particle. The data of transitive phrasal verbs are explained as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">She </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched</span><span class="font1"> the phone </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1"> and placed it in her bag. (Deckert: 8)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Analysis:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">According to Quirk (1985), the particle of phrasal verb can stand either before or after the noun phrase following the verb, but that of the prepositional verb must (unless deferred) precede the noun phrase. In simple way, the object of prepositional verb can not be placed between the verb and the particle. From that statement, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched off</span><span class="font1"> in the data above belongs to the transitive phrasal verb which is formed by two words “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched</span><span class="font1">” and particle “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1">”. Besides, it has an object “the phone” as the sign of the transitive phrasal verb. Even it is formulated of 2 words, it has one meaning. The phrase “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched off”</span><span class="font1"> equals with “to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">put or turn off an electric current”</span><span class="font1">. The sentence above can be checked by syntactic criteria below to prove whether it is transitive phrasal verb or not:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">a) Passivization.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The acceptance of passivization of the sentence can be used to check the syntactic similarity of the phrasal verbs, as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched</span><span class="font1"> the phone </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1"> and placed it in her bag. (Deckert: 8) (active)</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The phone was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched off</span><span class="font1"> by her and it was placed in her bag. (Passive) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">b) Pronominal question form.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The questions of phrasal verbs are built with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">who(m)</span><span class="font1"> for personal or people and with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">what</span><span class="font1"> for non-personal objects or things:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Question : Who </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched</span><span class="font1"> the phone </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1"> and placed it in her bag?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Answer &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: She did (Louise Eary’s mother).</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Question : What did she </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switch off</span><span class="font1"> and placed in her bag?</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Answer &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;: The phone</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">c) The position of Particle</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In Phrasal verb, according to Quirk “The particle of a phrasal verb can stand either before or after the noun phrase following the verb, but that of the prepositional verb must (unless deferred) precede the noun phrase”. Therefore, the particle of</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">phrasal verb can be often placed either before or after a noun. But it is only placed after a personal pronoun.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched</span><span class="font1"> the phone </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1"> and placed it in her bag. (Deckert: 8)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">t</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">switched off</span><span class="font1"> the phone and placed it in her bag. (Deckert: 8)</span></p></li></ul>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5.1.2 Intransitive Phrasal Verb</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Besides transitive phrasal verb, another type of phrasal verb is intransitive phrasal verb which is not followed by object. Phrasal verb is similar to from free combination but they are quite different in term of meaning. Phrasal verb has idiomatic meaning. Meanwhile the verb and the adverb of free combination have distinct meaning on the other. In this part is explained the distinction between intransitive phrasal verbs and free combinations using some syntactic criteria, below:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">I knew exactly what Kelly was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">getting at</span><span class="font1">. (Deckert: 94)</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Substitution</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In free combinations, the separability of verb and adverb in terms of meaning is shown by possible substitutions. For example in “</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wade across</span><span class="font1">” the verb “wade” can be substituted with “walk”, “jump”, “fly”, etc ; and for particle like “across” can be substituted with “through”, “over”, “in”, etc. As discussed previously, phrasal verbs are idiomatic expressions, combining verbs and prepositions or adverbs to make new verbs whose meaning cannot be predicted from the meaning of the verb and the particle in isolation. So, the word cannot be substituted with another word individually or word by word easily because it can change the meaning of the phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. I knew exactly what Kelly was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">getting at</span><span class="font1">. (Deckert: 94)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">* b. I knew exactly what Kelly was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">receiving on</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Insertion of a Modifying Adverb Right or Straight</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There are also syntactic signs of cohesion. In free combinations, it is often possible to place a modifying adverb </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">right</span><span class="font1"> (or sometimes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">straight</span><span class="font1">) between the</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">adverb particle and the verb. This insertion is to differentiate prepositional verbs with phrasal verbs:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">a. I knew exactly what Kelly was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">getting at</span><span class="font1">. (Deckert: 94)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">* b. I knew exactly what Kelly was </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">getting right at</span><span class="font1">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">S &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</span><span class="font1">→ NP VP &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;NP → Pr. N</span></p>

<p><span class="font1">The constituent structure of the sentence above in the form of labeled and</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">bracketed strings would look like this:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(S(NP(N(Kelly)) VP(V(put up)) NP(Pro.(her)N(hand))))</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">As discussed before, there are various forms of constituent structure in the data. For example, In the constituent structure of inseparable phrasal verb like in the sentence “Kelly </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">put up</span><span class="font1"> her hand.” , the verb and the particle are string together functioning as a single verb (V), so V dominates two words (verb +adv) and forming a triangle, however in the separable phrasal verb like “I began but shelley </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">cut</span><span class="font1"> me </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">off</span><span class="font1">” the verb and the adverb are separated so, the branch of the verb (V) and the particle (Adv) are separated by (NP). The particle in this phrasal verb functions as an adverb. The constituent structure of tree diagram and labeled and bracketed string of the data is similar, however they have different appearance where the tree diagram in tree form and labeled and bracketed string in horizontal form.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>6. Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">1.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Syntactically, there are 2 types of phrasal verbs: transitive phrasal verb which needs an object and intransitive phrasal verb which does not need an object. Some transitive phrasal verbs are separable. The object is placed between the verb and the particle. Besides, some phrasal verbs are inseparable where the object is placed after the particle. Phrasal verb is resembled but different with free combination in which the verb and the adverb have distinct meaning on the other.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">2.</span><span class="font1"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After making the constituent structures of phrasal verbs in the teen-lit </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">“Understudy to Miss Perfect”</span><span class="font1">, there are various forms of constituent structure of the data in this study. The ways of representing the information about constituent structure which are used in this study are tree diagram and labeled and bracketed strings. Related to the type of phrasal verb, in transitive phrasal verb, some transitive phrasal verbs are separable. The object is placed between the verb and the particle. Besides, some phrasal verbs are inseparable where the object is placed after the particle. In the constituent structure of inseparable phrasal verb, the verb and the particle are string together functioning as a single verb (V), so V dominates two words (verb +adv) and forming a triangle, however in the separable phrasal verb, the verb and the adverb are separated so, the branch of the verb (V) and the particle (Adv) are separated by (NP).</span></p></li></ul>
