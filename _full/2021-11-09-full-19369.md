<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penelitian ini berjudul “The Analysis of Slangs Used in “Bad Boys” Movie Script. Tujuan dari penelitian ini adalah untuk mengidentifikasi jenis, fungsi, dan arti dari bahasa popular yang di temukan dalam naskah film tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Data yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini diambil dari naskah film yang berjudul “Bad Boys” yang di tulis oleh Doug Richardson (1995). Metode yang di gunakan adalah metode dokumentasi dengan teknik mencatat. Teori yang di gunakan dalam penelitian ini berdasarkan teori Allan dan Burridge (2006) yang menjelaskan jenis dan fungsi dari bahasa popular tersebut dan arti dari bahasa popular tersebut.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Hasil penelitian tersebut menyebutkan bahwa terdapat lima kata bahasa popular dalam jenis yang pertama, enam kata pada jenis yang kedua, empat kata pada jenis yang ketiga, dua kata pada jenis yang ke empat dan empat kata pada jenis yang kelima yang di utarakan oleh para karakter pada naskah film tersebut. Dan jenis – jenis kata tersebut memiliki fungsi dan arti yang berbeda-beda. Arti dari kata-kata tersebut sebagian besar mengandung makna mengenai kekerasan, kriminal dan obat-obatan terlarang.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: bahasa popular, jenis, fungsi, makna</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Slang is a kind of jargon marked by its rejection of formal rules, its comparative freshness and its common ephemerally, and its marked use to claim solidarity in a group membership, it is an important social force that has a major impact on language.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Slang is primarily speech claiming group membership; slang is often associated with peer group and gang speech, intentionally used to obtain some degrees of secrecy. It may become a secret language; slang regularly transgresses other social norms, making free use of taboo expressions (Spolsky:35).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The problem related to the background can be formulated into three, as the following questions.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What types of slang are used by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the functions of slang used by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie script?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is the meaning of each slang used by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys” </span><span class="font2">movie script?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the formulated problems above, the aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To find out types of slang used by the characters in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie script.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To observe the functions of each slang type used by the characters in movie script.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">3) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze meaning of slang used in the movie script.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The primary data in this undergraduate paper were taken from a movie script entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> written by Doug Richardson (1995). The method and technique used in collecting data is documentation method and note taking technique. The data in</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">this study were analyzed qualitatively based on the theory of Allan and Burridge (2006).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This part deals with the result and discussion of the data. The data analysis is divided into three parts: analysis of types of slang, function of slang, and meaning of slang.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The type of slang</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">According to Allan and Burridge (2006), There are five types of slang: Fresh and Creative, Flippant, Imitative, Acronym, Clipping.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Imitative Type</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Imitative means that slang language imitated or derived from Standard English (SE) word using SE in different meaning or combined two different words. The data categorized into imitative type are the slang word such as </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">yeah, pussy, honey, and dope.</span><span class="font2"> The data are as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Imitative Type</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Slang Words</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yeah</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pussy</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Honey</span></p></li>
<li>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The function of slang</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">According to Allan and Burridge (2006), There are seven different functions of slang: to address, to form intimate atmosphere, to initiate relax conversation, to express impression, to reveal anger, to humiliate, and to show intimacy.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Initate Relax Conversation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Another function is to initiate relax conversation. Sometimes the speakers prefer to use slang word rather than standard variant of vocabulary to create relax conversation. They have tendency to use slang word to initiate relax conversation in purpose of having smooth conversation in formal situation. Someone who has close relationship with other tends to use certain vocabulary in order to make the conversation run more relaxed so that they can feel more comfortable. The data are as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fouchet: Haul Ass!</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fouchet Man: </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Yeah!</span><span class="font2"> Let’s move it!</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the conversation on movie script, Fouchet man has close relationship with his boss Fouchet and they prefer to use slang word to create relax conversation, when they speak to his boss.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burnett: There's gonna be a lotta cash when the </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dope</span><span class="font2"> hits the street.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lowrey: I'm tellin' you there's a buttload of H on the move and someone's wanna stick their dick in it.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the conversation on movie script, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dope</span><span class="font2"> means Heroin and burnett used slang word </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">dop</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">e</span><span class="font2"> to Lowrey, to tell lowrey that the heroin will hit the street on Miami</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark20"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark21"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To humiliate</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The speakers has the tendency to express unpleasent or dislike feeling of the speakers towards someone or something by mocking them. The conversation data that have function to humiliate are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lowrey: okay, so we talk to Max.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burnett: Huh uh. Not this time. I'm tired of you working </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pussy</span><span class="font2"> into everything we do.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the conversation on movie script, Burnett used the slang word </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">pussy</span><span class="font2"> to mock Lowrey’s girl, because burnett is tired and bored, when Lowrey wants to do something, he always fix it with his girl.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark22"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark23"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To Address</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The first function of the use of slang words is to address. The Speaker uses slang word to address another speaker in order to maintain their close relationship. Someone who has close relationship usually tends to use informal kind of address because using formal address indicats to the speaker has distant relationship or has specific purpose with other speaker. The conversation data that have function to address are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Burnett: </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Honey.</span><span class="font2"> I wouldn't go if I didn't have the most trustworthy man I know to look after my family...</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">(2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Theresa: Oh, no, Not Lowrey. Not in my house.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In this conversation the slang word </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">honey</span><span class="font2"> is used to address to beloved person. Burnett used a slang word </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">honey</span><span class="font2"> to address Theresa because she is the special person for him and she is Burnett`s wife.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark33"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark34"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Considering the first problem about the type of slang used by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie script written by Doug Ricahardson (1995), there are five types of slang employed by the characters in this movie. They are fresh and creative, flippant,</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">imitative, acronym, and clipping. The occurance of flippant type dominated the slang type used by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie script. Meanwhile, acronym type is the fewest slang type in this movie script. The speakers require significant knowledge of slang vocabulary to use it thus acronym type become the fewest slang type.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In reffering to the next problem about the function of each slang type employed by the characters in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys”</span><span class="font2"> movie script, there are two slang functions discovered in fresh and creative type, they are to address, and to form intimate atmosphere. Meanwhile in the flippant type, there are five different functions, those functions are to address, to initiate relax conversation, to humiliate, to reveal anger, and to form intimate atmosphere. In the imitative type, there are three different functions of slang, they are to initiate relax conversation, to humiliate, and to address. In the meantime, there is one function to initate relax conversation in acronym type. In addition I found three different functions in clipping type, they are to address, to initiate relax conversation, and to express impresion. The meaning of American slang used by the character in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">“Bad Boys</span><span class="font2">” movie script mostly consists of drugs, crimes and violence issues, because this movie is about drug dealers, criminal and drug users in the Miami.</span></p>
