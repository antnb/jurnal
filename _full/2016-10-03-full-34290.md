<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Verbal And Nonverbal Signs In Early Pages Of </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Putu Cahya Jaya Dirga<sup>1*</sup>, Ni Wayan Sukarini<sup>2</sup>, I Gede Budiasa<sup>3 </sup></span><span class="font2"><sup>123</sup>English Department Faculty Of Arts, Udayana University <sup>1</sup>[</span><a href="mailto:cahyaj@gmail.com"><span class="font2">cahyaj@gmail.com</span></a><span class="font2">] 2[</span><a href="mailto:wayansukarini@yahoo.co.id"><span class="font2">wayansukarini@yahoo.co.id</span></a><span class="font2">] <sup>3</sup>[</span><a href="mailto:budiasa.igede@yahoo.com"><span class="font2">budiasa.igede@yahoo.com</span></a><span class="font2">] </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><sup>*</sup></span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Corresponding Author</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Tanda dapat dibagi menjadi tanda verbal dan tanda non verbal. Tanda nonverbal meliputi gambar dan tanda verbal meliputi kata. Penerapan kedua tanda ini dapat ditemukan dalam manga atau komik. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">singifier </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">signified </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dari tanda verbal dan tanda nonverbal dan hubungan kedua tanda tersebut dalam membentuk rangkaian cerita dalam manga. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Signifier </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">signified </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">dari tanda verbal dan nonverbal dianalisa dengan menggunakan teori dari Saussure yang dikutip dari Chandler (2001), dan hubungan kedua tanda tersebut dalam membentuk cerita dianalisa menggunakan teori dari McCloud (1993). Hasil penelitian menunjukkan bahwa tanda nonverbal mengindikasikan latar dan tindakan karakter dan tanda verbal mengindikasikan aspek yang tidak dicakup oleh tanda nonverbal. Kedua tanda dalam membentuk rangkaian cerita menerapkan lima jenis hubungan,yaitu </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">wordspecific</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">picture-specific</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">additive</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">parallel</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">interdependent</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: tanda verbal, tanda nonverbal, manga, semiotics</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Signs can be found in many aspects of life that represent certain concept or meaning. Sign is not limited only to alphabet, but also symbol and picture. The study about sign and its meaning is known as Semiotics. Saussure (as cited in Chandler, 2001) defines semiotics as the science of signs in society. Semiotics focuses on something that can be considered as a sign. The sign can also be divided into verbal and nonverbal sign. Verbal sign involves words while nonverbal sign includes pictures. Therefore, sign</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">covers a wide scope of category ranging from a simple sign (symbol) to a more complicated one such as picture.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The application of verbal and nonverbal signs is also found in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga</span><span class="font2"> is a type comic book originally developed in Japan that is characterized by stylized art. The picture in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> is developed into more complex one and closely resemble the features of real world, including the illustration of the human (shape, gesture, expression), and the environment (landscape, architecture, animal, plant). The non verbal sign is represented by the picture or the drawing, and the verbal sign is indicated by the textual dialog or description of the setting. The meaning in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> focuses on how the verbal and non verbal signs create an event within the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> because the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> is a story told in picture sequence of events. Therefore, it is important to understand the event to obtain the meaning.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font2">The problems can be formulated as follows:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the signifier and the signified of non verbal signs and verbal signs in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote</span><span class="font2">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What is the relation between the verbal and non verbal signs to create meaningful event in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>

<p><span class="font2">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To explain the signifier and the signified of the non verbal signs and verbal signs depicted in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the relation between verbal and non verbal signs to create meaningful event in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote.</span></p></li></ul>

<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data used in this study was taken from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Deathnote</span><span class="font2"> written by Tsugumi Ohba and published in 2004. The </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> was used because the study about</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">nonverbal and verbal signs in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> has not been conducted before. It consists of simple and interesting combination of verbal and nonverbal signs to create events in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study is library research. The technique of collecting data is taking notes. The technique followed several steps. First, the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote</span><span class="font2"> was downloaded to obtain the softcopy data from the internet. Second, reading the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Manga</span><span class="font2"> to know the general story of the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2">. Then, a chapter was chosen. This study used five pages from chapter one because the data is homogeny, consisting of text and pictures. Therefore, five pages are considered sufficient to represent the whole chapters. Then, taking notes of the verbal and non verbal signs found in the chosen chapter.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">This study used qualitative descriptive method to analyze the data. The theories used are theory of semiotics from Saussure and theory of relationships in comic from McCloud. The verbal and nonverbal signs were analyzed by Saussureâ€™s semiotic theory to find out the signifiers and signified of the verbal and non verbal signs. Then, theory of relationship in comic proposed by McCloud was applied to analyze the relation between the nonverbal and verbal signs in creating a meaningful event in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark13"></a>1.1</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Signifier and Signified of Verbal and Nonverbal Signs in Early Pages of </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The nonverbal signs depicted in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> are primarily signifying characterâ€™s motion and settings, mainly setting of place. These signs display how the charactersâ€™ actions are done, including sitting, walking, and reading, standing up, and resting. The actions are also supported by other nonverbal signs in order to make a comprehensible activity. Other nonverbal signs signify the environment by presenting some pictures of the corresponding setting, thus creating different settings of place such as classroom, house, and bedroom. Verbal signs support the nonverbal signs by signifying some aspects which are not covered by the nonverbal signs, such as characterâ€™s identity and the setting of time. The characterâ€™s identity is not illustrated by the images, but is described by the words. The identity includes the characterâ€™s name and age. This applies to the setting of time, which is also described by the words, not the pictures.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2"><a name="bookmark15"></a>1.2</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Relationship between Verbal and Nonverbal Signs to Create Meaningful Event in </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Manga Deathnote.</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Verbal and nonverbal signs have various types of relationship to coexist within </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2">. The types of relationships used in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> are as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Word-Specific</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Word-specific combination is the combination between words and pictures in which the pictures do not depict the whole aspect to the readersâ€™ comprehension. The word tells the illustration more specifically than the picture does (McCloud, 1993:153). The example is the presence of the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Yagami Raito, 19 years old</span><span class="font2"> (Ohba, 2004:9) which explains specifically about the characterâ€™s identity. Thus, it explains more than the picture does.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Picture Specific</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Picture-specific combination can be considered the opposite of the word-specific combination. Picture specific is the combination in which the words have fewer roles than the pictures. Words are not completely necessary because the pictures have clearly told the idea or event in comic (McCloud, 1993:153). The example is the presence of some panels which have no word or verbal sign, only showing the picture to tell the idea without involving any words.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Additive</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Additive combination is the combination in which the words have the function to amplify or elaborate the meaning pictures and also vice versa (McCloud, 1993:154). The example is the presence of the words </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Deathnote</span><span class="font2"> followed by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">meaning book of death </span><span class="font2">(Ohba, 2004:10) which gives more elaborate meaning about the illustration of the notebook.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Parallel</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In parallel combination, the words and pictures do not have any correlation between each other. They run their course simultaneously without intersection (McCloud, 1993:154). The example is the illustration of a yard along with the presence of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">huh?</span><span class="font2"> (Ohba, 2004:9). These two aspects stand separately to give two different ideas. Thus, they are not necessarily related.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Interdependent</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In interdependent combination, the words and pictures have a balanced role in conveying an idea. In this combination, they are mutually dependent because the words explain the pictures and the pictures describe the words (McCloud, 1993:155). The example is the depiction of the main characterâ€™s greeting </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Iâ€™m home</span><span class="font2"> which is replied by someone saying </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">welcome back</span><span class="font2"> (Ohba, 2004:13). Even though the one who gives replies is not shown, the reader can understand that there exists another character aside the main character by looking at the pictures and the words. Thus, both words and pictures are dependent.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded the nonverbal signs depicted in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2"> are primarily signifying characterâ€™s motion and settings and the verbal signs support the nonverbal signs by signifying some aspects which are not covered by the nonverbal signs. The relationship between verbal and nonverbal signs is an important factor to create a meaningful event in the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">manga</span><span class="font2">. Both verbal and nonverbal signs have various types of relationship; Words-specific, Picture-specific, Additive, Parallel, and Interdependent combination.</span></p>
