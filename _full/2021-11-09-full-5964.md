<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">FORMAL LINKS APPROACH TO GRAMMAR AND LEXICON USED IN BARACK OBAMA’S SPEECH</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">“PULANG KAMPUNG NIH”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">By:</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Risna Cahyani</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">English Department</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Faculty of Letters, Udayana University</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font0" style="font-style:italic;">Kata penghubung formal merupakan bagian dari analisis sebuah wacana yang berfungsi menghubungkan kalimat dan kalimat juga klausa dan klausa. Hubungan tersebut juga dikenal sebagai keterpaduan yang menunjukan satu kesatuan makna antar konteks dan isi dari suatu wacana. Dalam hal ini, terdapat dua pendekatan yang digunakan untuk mengetahui seberapa jauh kesesuaian dan kelogisan dari suatu wacana agar makna dapat tersampaikan dengan baik, yaitu pendekatan grammatikal dan pendekatan leksikon. Dalam sebuah pidato presiden Barak Obama yang di sampaikan di Universitas Indonesia pada tanggal 9November 2010, terdapat kata penghubung formal yang dianalisis melalui teori dari Guy Cook (1989). Adapun tujuan dari penelitian ini yaitu untuk mengidentifikasi kata penghubung sebagai pendekatan grammatikal dan leksikon yang terdapat pada pidato “Pulang Kampung Nih”. Sehingga dari penelitian ini dapat diketahui adanya kesesuaian dan kelogisan konteks dan isi dari pidato presiden agar makna yang ingin disampaikan oleh pembicara dapat tersampaikan dengan baik.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: kata penghubung formal, grammatikal, leksikon</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Speech as public address is a type of spoken discourse. Therefore discourse analysis is required in speech. Analysis of discourse examines how stretches of language, considered in their full textual, social and psychological context become meaningful and unified for their users (Guy Cook, 1989: ix). Because of that, there is an approach to show the stretches of language in speech text named Formal Links. . Formal links between sentences and between clauses are known as cohesive device.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The formality of speech can be found in presidential speech, especially in Obama’s speech entitled “Pulang Kampung Nih” which delivered in University of Indonesia on November 9, 2010. This speech is to express his gratitude in relation to his childhood in Indonesia. Formal Link as an approach to grammar and lexicon will influence the content of speech can be clearly understood by the audience.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li><a name="caption1"></a>
<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">To what extent do the Formal Links are used in Barack Obama’s speech “Pulang Kampung Nih” as an approach to grammar and lexicon?</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">To describe to what extent formal links are used in Barack Obama’s speech “Pulang Kampung Nih” as an approach to grammar</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> In addition, to find out what formal links are used in the speech as an approach to lexicon.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Source</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The data of this study were taken from the text of Barrack Obama’s speech addressed to Indonesian people on November 9, 2010 at the University of Indonesia as a sign of good relation between Indonesia and America. This speech is for expressing his gratitude to Indonesia because of his bright childhood in Indonesia. The reason for choosing this text speech of Barack Obama as the data source of this study is because this speech used formal English.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Method and Technique of Collecting Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The method applied in this study was documentation method. This was done by downloading the speech of President Barack Obama “Pulang Kampung Nih” from the internet.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>a. Method and Technique of Analyzing Data</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In analyzing the data, the methods used were qualitative. After getting the valid and relevant data, the data were descriptively analyzed based on the theory of Formal Links taken from the discourse theory by Guy Cook (1989) and the grammar theory by Thomson and Martinet (1986). The data was analyzed descriptively using the theory of formal links as an approach to grammar and lexicon. Each data was analyzed from its form. Then, all of selected data in the sentences consisting of formal English in the speech text and their approaches to discourse and grammar were concluded to emphasize the formal links in the text of speech.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Formal links approach to grammar and lexicon used in Barack Obama’ s speech “Pulang Kampung Nih”</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Analysis of Grammar</span></h1></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The grammar of formal links that consists of verb form, referring expressions, substitution, ellipsis, and conjunction were analyzed from each paragraph of the text of Barrack Obama’s speech.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>5.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Verb Form</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The first analysis based on the use of correct tenses according to the times of events being spoken and some impressions appear.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">3<sup>rd</sup> paragraph</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Pulang kampung nih </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">(1)</span><span class="font1">. I </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">am</span><span class="font1"> so glad that I </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">made</span><span class="font1"> it back to Indonesia and that Michelle </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">was able to</span><span class="font1"> join me </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">(2)</span><span class="font1">. We </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">had</span><span class="font1"> a couple of false starts this year, but I </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">was</span><span class="font1"> determined to visit a country that’</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">s mean</span><span class="font1">t so much to me </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">(3)</span><span class="font1">. And unfortunately, this visit </span><span class="font1" style="text-decoration:underline;">is</span><span class="font1"> too short, but I look forward to coming back a year from now when Indonesia hosts the East Asia Summit.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bahasa Indonesia was used in first sentence, in the second sentence there is a combination of simple present and simple past tense</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">I am so glad (simple present) that I made (simple past) it back to Indonesia and that Michelle was able to join me</span><span class="font1"> (2<sup>nd</sup> sentence). </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Was able to join me</span><span class="font1"> indicates the past ability. The relation of those tenses, can be expressed Obama’s happiness to visit Indonesia with his wife Michelle and they wanted to come back again to Indonesia in East Asia Summit.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Referring Expression</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Referring expression are the words whose meaning can only be discovered by referring to other words or to elements of the context which clear to both sender and receiver.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(1)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;…..But </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">was determined to visit a country that’s meant so much to </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">me</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">. (Data 3:3)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I </span><span class="font1">refers to the speaker, Barack Obama. The usage of </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I </span><span class="font1">has the purpose that the speaker wants to declare himself as someone who delivered the speech. </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Me </span><span class="font1">is object pronoun which is related to the subject. Both subject and object pronouns belong to personal reference.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark17"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Substitution</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Another kind of formal link is Substitution. Whereas reference is a relation between meanings, substitution is defined as grammatically relationship. In English, the substitution functions as a noun, a verb, and a clause.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(1)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">…those who loves </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ones </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(data 4:1)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Ones </span><span class="font1">is indicated to substitute for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Indonesian who are affected by the recent tsunami and the volcanic eruption</span><span class="font1"> related to the previous sentence. Ones is to substitute the plural nouns.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>5.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Ellipsis is the omitted part of sentences on the assumption that an earlier sentence or the context will make the meaning clear (cook, 1989:20).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(1)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now, I stayed here for </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">four years</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">, a time that helped shape my childhood; a time that saw the birth of my wonderful sister, Maya; a time that made such an impression on my mother that she kept returning to Indonesia over the next 20 years to live and to work and to travel.</span><span class="font1">(data 9:1)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">There is an omitted part in this paragraph after the semicolon, </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">four years as </span><span class="font1">a subject is not mentioned anymore. The purpose is to make the meaning clearer.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>5.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conjunction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Conjunctions are the words to join sentence elements such as words, phrases, or clauses, or even sentences.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(1)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The story of Indonesia </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">America should make us optimistic,..(data 49:2)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In this paragraph, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and</span><span class="font1"> is used to join words Indonesia and America. It is also used for adding some information or words in the speech text.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis of Lexicon</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The lexicon of formal links that consist of parallelism, repetition, and lexical chains were analyzed from each paragraph of the text of Barack Obama’s speech.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Parallelism</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Parallelism is a balance of two or more similar words, phrases, or clauses. It is divided into three categories: parallelism of words, parallelism of phrases and parallelism of clauses.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Symbolized in </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">mosques </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">churches </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">temples </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">standing along each other;…(data 15:1)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The application of parallelism of words in this paragraph construction improves writing style of the speech text clearness and readability. The parallelism was found as the object of a sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>5.2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Repetition</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Repetition is the simple repeated word, within the sentence or a poetical line, with no particular placement of the words, in order to emphasize. There were some repetitions of words found in the speech of Barack Obama such as </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">America, Indonesia, government, change, prosperity, responsibility, power, effort and country.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>5.2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lexical Chains</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Lexical chains mean the use of Antonym (the words that are opposite in meanings) and Super ordinate (the various words that represent narrower categories).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">(1) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The old </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">men and women </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">who welcomed us….(data 7:8)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Men and women are antonyms. Both of them contrast to express gender of human and in plural form. At that time Obama delivered his gratitude to Indonesian who welcomed him at that time.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark31"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark32"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">According to the analysis of Obama’s speech “Pulang Kampung Nih” , some points can be presented as conclusion. All of the tenses used in the text of Barack Obama’s speech are appropriate with the time of the events and actions being spoken, because all the formal links that indicate formal connection as formal English are found in the speech text. The most tenses used in the text are simple present, simple past and simple future because Obama tells about his childhood in Indonesia. And also simple future tense is used to indicate the speaker’s hope and what he is going to do. The additive</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">conjunction “and” is used in most paragraphs and there is only causal conjunction found in the text. The referring expressions consist of personal reference, comparative reference, and demonstrative reference. In the personal references, the first person (I, me, my, mine) only refer to one person, the President of America, Barack Hussein Obama. On the other hand, the second person plural (you, we, our, us, ours) refer to the American and Indonesian, meanwhile the third person plural (they, them, their, and theirs) refer to every child born in Indonesia and also Southeast Asia People. The third person singular refers to a word or phrase depending on the content. There were also some repetitions of words (America, Indonesia, government, change, prosperity, responsibility, power, effort and country) found in speech. All of the formal links were found in the text, except the super ordinate (the various words that represent narrower categories).</span></p>
