<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">This study was aimed at identifying the types of constructions in which ellipsis of subject took place in the Balinese language, the meaning of conjunction, and the syntactic category of the omitted subject in Subordinative construction.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Descriptive-qualitative method was used, and the ellipsis of subject in the Balinese Subordinative construction was investigated in the present study. The data were collected using triangulation system, that is, the combined method of observation, interview, and documentation. From the process of observation, the researchers noted down the Subordinative constructions in which the ellipsis of subject took place. Then the data were selected based on the meaning of the conjunction used. The data were taken from a Balinese story. The data which could not be obtained from the written source were replaced by the intuitive data as the researcher is a Balinese native speaker. To make the intuitive data more accurate, the informants who were knowledgeable of the structures of the written and spoken Balinese language were used.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The result of the study showed that the conjunctions used in the Subordinative constructions indicated temporal, expectation, concession, cause, result, conditional, and unison meaning relations. Furthermore, it was identified that the constituents in which the ellipsis of subject took place in the Subordinative constructions were noun, noun phrase, and pronoun (the first and third pronouns).</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: Ellipsis of subject, meaning of conjunction, and syntactic category</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Quirk et al (1985: 536) stated that ellipsis refers to any grammatical unit which is omitted for economic purpose. The omitted words can be made to reappear in the structure in which they are used to make it perfect. The reason is that the construction in which ellipsis takes place is not perfect.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Verhaar (1981: 70) stated that there are three levels of syntactic constructions; they are function, category, and role. The subject, as one of the syntactic functions, plays a role in the system of ellipsis. If compared to the other syntactic functions such as predicate, object, and adverb, the subject is more dominantly omitted in the Subordinative construction. Similarly, in the syntactic category, the omitted subject can fill in the elements of the syntactic category level such as noun, pronoun, verb, adjective, adverb, and preposition.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The problems in the present study are formulated as follows. (1) What is the meaning of conjunction in the Balinese Subordinative clause? (2) What syntactic category could be omitted in the ellipsis of subject in the Balinese Subordinative construction? Based on the two problems mentioned above, this article is focused on the meaning of conjunction and the syntactic category which can be omitted in the Balinese Subordinative construction.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Descriptive-qualitative method was used in the present study. The data were obtained from a Balinese story ‘satua Bali’ (abbreviated to SB) written by I.N.K. Supatra (2006) published by CV. Kayumas Agung, Denpasar. The data were focused on the Subordinative construction using conjunction.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The data were collected using triangulation method; a combined method of observation, interview, and documentation. It was believed that using the triangulation method could overcome the limitation and weakness of every method. The observation method was used to obtain the written data from SB. Then, the informants were interviewed in order to obtain the Subordinative constructions which could not be written. The interview was held repeatedly in order to obtain accurate data. Elicitation technique was used in the interview.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The data were also collected using documentation method to obtain the written data from SB. The collected data were then analyzed using direct element dividing technique. This technique was used to select the data in such a way that the types of conjunction, the syntactic</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">category, and the meaning of conjunction in the Balinese Subordinative construction could be identified.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Discussion and Result of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Based on what was described above, the result of the present study related to the ellipsis of subject in the Balinese Subordinative construction can be presented as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject, Category, and Meaning of Conjunction</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Subject, as one of the syntactic elements, can be omitted in the level of its category. In the present study, the Subordinative construction using conjunction is discussed. The Subordinative conjunction links the nucleus clause and the non nucleus one; in other words, the Subordinative conjunction links the clauses whose syntactic statuses are not the same. The meaning of the conjunction in the Subordinative clause indicates (1) temporal meaning relation, (2) requisite meaning relation, (3) expectation meaning relation, (4) result meaning relation, (5) unison meaning relation, (6) concessive meaning relation, (7) cause meaning relation, and (8) conditional meaning relation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>3.1.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Conjunction Using Conjunction Indicating Temporal Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative construction with the conjunction indicating temporal relation, the subject can be omitted. The conjunctions used include </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dugas</span><span class="font1"> ‘when’, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rikala</span><span class="font1"> ‘when’, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">uling </span><span class="font1">‘since’ as exemplified below.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-1) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Kedis </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">paekin-a teken I Bojog rikala </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">waiwasan</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 11)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art burung dekat-Pas oleh Art kera Konj menoleh</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Burung didekati oleh Si Kera pada waktu menoleh’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The bird was approached by the monkey when turning its head]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-2</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Macan </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ketemu I Bojog dugas </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">melaib ngawag ulian takut-ne</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006:4)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Harimau bertemu Art Ker Konj lari tanpa arah karena takut-Pos</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Harimau bertemu dengan Si Kera ketika berlari tanpa arah karena takut’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The tiger met the monkeu when running aimlessly because of being scared]</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">(3-3) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Wayan Suta </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">suba nongos di Gianyar uling Ø enu cerik</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;sudah tinggal Prep Gianyar Konj masih kecil</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Wayan Suta sudah tinggal di Gianyar sejak masih kecil’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Wayan Suta has been staying in Gianyar since he was a child]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-4) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pan Balang Tamak tonden bangun dugas Ø delokin-a teken timpal-e</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:7)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;belum bangun Konj tengok-Pas oleh teman-Pos</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Pan Balang Tamak belum bangun ketika ditengok oleh temannya”</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Pan Balang Tamak has not woken up when he was visited by his friend]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From examples (3-1) to (3-4) it can be identified that </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">rikala</span><span class="font1"> ‘when’, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dugas</span><span class="font1"> ‘when’, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">uling</span><span class="font1"> ‘since’ indicate temporal relation. The subjects </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I Kedis </span><span class="font1">‘Si Burung’ (the bird), </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I Macan </span><span class="font1">‘Si Harimau’ (the tiger), </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Wayan Suta</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Pan Balang Tamak </span><span class="font1">were omitted in the second clauses. From the level of category</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">, I Kedis </span><span class="font1">‘Si Burung’ (the bird), </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">I Macan </span><span class="font1">‘Si Harimau’ (the tiger), </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Wayan Suta</span><span class="font1">, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Pan Balang Tamak </span><span class="font1">are noun phrases.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>3.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with Conjunction Indicating Requisite Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative clause with the conjunction indicating conditional relation, the subject can be omitted. The conjunctions indicating conditional relation include yen </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kalau</span><span class="font1"> (if) and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yening</span><span class="font1"> ‘jika’ (if) as illustrated below.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-5) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Tiang suba mati amah macan yen </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing malaib uli alas- e</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 43)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">1T sudah mati dimakan harimau Konj Neg lari dari hutan-Def</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Saya sudah mati dimakan harimau kalau tidak lari dari hutan itu’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[I would have been dead eaten by the tiger if I had not fled from the forest]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-6) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Iraga sepatutne mineh-ang malu yening </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar melaksana</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 10)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Ijm sebenarny pikir-Apl dulu Konj akan melakukan sesuatu</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Kita sebenarnya pikirkan dulu jika akan melakukan sesuatu’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[We should have thought about when we would do something]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-7</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">) I Belog tusing ngelah pretisentana yening </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing nganten</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006:59)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Nama Neg &nbsp;&nbsp;punya keturunan &nbsp;&nbsp;Konj Neg kawin</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘I Belog tidak punya keturunan jika tidak kawin’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[I Belog would not have any descendant if he were not married]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative constructions (3-5) and (3-7) above, it can be identified that the subjects were omitted in the second clauses. The subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tiang</span><span class="font1"> ‘I’ is a noun, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Iraga</span><span class="font1"> ‘</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Kita</span><span class="font1">’ (we) is a pronoun, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Belog</span><span class="font1"> is a noun phrase. The conjunctions yen ‘kalau’ (if) and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yening</span><span class="font1"> ‘jika’ (if) indicate conditional meaning relation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>3.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with the Conjunction Indicating</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark18"></a>Meaning Expectation Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative clause with the conjunction indicating expectation relation, the subject can be omitted. The conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">apang, mangda</span><span class="font1"> ‘supaya’ (so that) as exemplified by the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-8) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Iraga </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sepatutne malajah apang Ø mani puan tusing kena</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">1Jm sebenarnya belajar Konj dikemudian hari Neg kena</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">uluk-uluk</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 10)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">olok-olok</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Kita sebenarnya belajar supaya dikemudian hari tidak kena olok-olok’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[We should learn so that we will not be deceived in the future]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-9) </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pan Angklung Gadang matur ring Jero Kelian mangda Ø polih matanggeh</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;berbicara dengan Jero Kelian Konj dapat menunda</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">utang malih abulan</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 51)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">hutang lagi satu bulan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Pan Angklung Gadang berbicara dengan Jero Kelian supaya dapat menunda</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Hutang lagi satu bulan’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Pan Angklung proposed to Jero Kelian that he could postpone paying for the debt until next month’]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-10</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">) I Cicing nunasica ring Ida Sang Hyang Widhi apang Ø</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Anjing berdoa Prep Tuhan &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Konj</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">setata rahayu</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006:27)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">selalu selamat</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Anjing berdoa kepada Tuhan supaya selalu selamat’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The dog prayed to God so that it would always be healthy]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From examples (3-8) – (3-10), the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Iraga</span><span class="font1"> ‘Kita’ omitted in the second clause is a noun, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pan Angklung Gadang</span><span class="font1"> is a noun phrase, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Cicing</span><span class="font1"> ‘Si Anjing’ [the dog] is noun phrase as well. The conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">apang</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mangda</span><span class="font1"> [so that] indicating expectation meaning relation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>3.1.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with the Conjunction Indicating Result Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The subject in the Subordinative clause with the conjunction indicating result relation can also be omitted. The conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nganti, ngatos</span><span class="font1"> ‘hingga’ (until) as exemplified by the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-11) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tiang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tiwas kaliwat nganti Ø tusing ngidang mayah utang di</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1T miskin sekali Konj Neg bisa membayar hutang Prep</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">banjar</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006:49)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">banjar</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Saya miskin sekali hingga tidak bisa membayar hutang di banjar’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[I was so poor that I could not pay my debt at </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">banjar</span><span class="font1"> (hamlet)]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-12) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Luh Sari </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nimbus sela nganti Ø puun</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama menambus ketela Konj hangus</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Luh Sari menambus ketela mingga hangus’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Luh Sari roasted in a fire the sweet potato until it was burnt]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-13) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ida Bagus Anom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">makarya ring gria ngantos Ø leleh</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;work Pref rumah Konj lelah</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Ida Bagus Anom bekerja di rumah hingga lelah’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Ida Bagus Anom worked at home so he was tired]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From examples (3-11) – (3-13) it can be identified that the conjunctions used in the second clauses are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nganti, ngatos</span><span class="font1"> ‘hingga’ [so] indicating result meaning relation. The subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tiang</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> ‘Saya</span><span class="font1">’ is a pronoun, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">sela</span><span class="font1"> ‘ketela’ [sweet-potato] is a noun, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Ida Bagus Anom </span><span class="font1">is a noun phrase too.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>3.1.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with the Conjunction Indicating Conditional Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The subject could also be omitted in the Subordinative clause with the conjunctions indicating conditional meaning relation. The conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dipete, dipradene </span><span class="font1">‘seandainya’ [if] as described in the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-14) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Bojog </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar ngewantu I Kambing dipete </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sengkala</span><span class="font1"> (SB, 2006: 26)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Kera akan membantu Art Kambing Konj bahaya</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Kera akan membantu Si Kambing seandainya bahaya’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The monkey would help the goat if there were a danger]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-15) </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tiang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar ngemang adin tiang-e pipis dipete</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">maan gaji dipegaen &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;tiang-e</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1T akan memberi adik 1T- Pos uang Konj &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;dapat gaji di tempat kerja 1T - Pos</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">‘Saya akan memberi adik saya uang seandainya dapat gaji di tempat kerja saya’ [I will give my younger sibling some money if I receive salary where I work]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-16)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Wayan Sudi </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar megae di kapal pesiar dipradene</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">bisa ngomong</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama akan bekerja Prep kapal pesiar Konj bisa bicara</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">basa Inggris</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">bahasa Inggris</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Wayan Sudi akan bekerja di kapal pesiar seandainya bisa bicara bahasa Inggris’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Wayan Sudi will work on a cruise if he can speak English]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The conjunctions used in the Subordinative clauses (3-14) – (3-16) are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dipete</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">dipradene </span><span class="font1">‘seandainya’ [if] indicating conditional meaning relation. The subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Kambing</span><span class="font1"> ‘Si Kambing’ is a noun phrase, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Tiang</span><span class="font1"> ‘Saya’ [I] is a pronoun, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Wayan Suandi</span><span class="font1"> is a noun phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark23"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark24"></a>3.1.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with the Conjunction Indicating Unison Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The subject could also be omitted in the Subordinative clause with the conjunction indicating unison meaning relation. The conjunction used is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sambilanga</span><span class="font1"> ‘sambil’ [while] indicating unison as illustrated in the following example.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-17)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I &nbsp;&nbsp;Belog </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nganggur kema ka umah-ne Luh Sari sambilanga</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngaba</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Nama melancong kesana Prep rumah-Pos Nama Konj &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;membawa</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">bakul misi tabia</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">keranjang berisi cabai</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘I Belog melancong kesana ke rumah Luh Sari sambil membawa keranjang berisi cabai’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[I Belog came to Luh Sari’s house while brought basket white red pepper]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-18)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Bojog </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">masaut sambilanga</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngamah biu</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:11)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Kera menjawab Konj &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;makan pisang</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Kera menjawab sambil makan pisang’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[ The monkey answered while ate banana]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-19)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Bojog </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">suir – suir ngamah sela sambilanga</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngurut – ngurut</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Kera bersiul makan ketela Konj &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;mengelus – elus</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">basang-ne</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:35)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">perut-Pos</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Kera bersiul makan ketela sambil mengelus – elus perutnya’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The monkey whistled sweet potato while stroked its stomach]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative clauses above, it can be identified that the conjunction used is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sambilanga</span><span class="font1"> ‘sambil’ [while] indicating unison meaning relation. The subjects </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Belog</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Bojog</span><span class="font1"> were omitted and are noun phrases.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark25"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark26"></a>3.1.7 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject with the Conjunction Indicating Concessive Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">In the Subordinative clause using the conjunction indicating concessive meaning relation, the subject could also be omitted. The conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">yadiastun, yadian</span><span class="font1">, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">diastu, yadiapin, wiadin</span><span class="font1"> ‘walaupun’ [although] as illustrated by the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-20)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Cai </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nyuang upon – upon carik -e makejang diastu</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing tuyuh</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">2T ngambil hasil sawah-Def semua Konj Neg payah</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">megae</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:13)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">bekerja</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Kamu mengambil hasil sawah itu semua walaupun tidak payah bekerja’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[You took all of the rice field crops although you were tired to work]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-21)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Iba </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing bisa ngae &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;umah yadian</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">suba kelih</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:15)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">2T Neg bisa membuat rumah Konj sudah dewasa</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Kamu tidak bisa membuat rumah walaupun sudah dewasa’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[You could not make house although you were adult]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-22)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ni Luh Sari </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing kapok-kapok yadiastun</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">suba uluk-uluk –a teken timpal-ne</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama Neg jera-jera Konj sudah olok-olok-Pas oleh teman-Pos</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(SB,2006:23)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Ni Luh Sari tidak jera – jera walaupun sudah diolok – olok oleh temannya’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Ni Luh Sari undaunted although she had been joked by her friend]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-23)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Iraga </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sepatutne mapineh malu yadiapin</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar mapitulung anak lenan</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:20)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">1Jm sebenarnya berpikir dulu Konj akan menolong orang lain</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Kita sebenarnya berpikir dulu walaupun akan menolong orang lain’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[We ought to think first although we will help some one]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-24)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ketut Anom </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">demit gati wiadin</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">liu ngelah kasugihan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama pelit sekali Konj banyak punya kekayaan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Ketut Anom pelit sekali walaupun banyak punya kekayaan’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Ketut Anom was very stingy although he had wealth]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the examples above, it can be identified that the subject can be omitted in the Subordinative clause using the conjunction indicating concessive meaning relation. The conjunctions include </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">diastu, yadian, yadiastun, yadiapin,</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">wiadin</span><span class="font1"> ‘walaupun’ [although]. The subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Cai </span><span class="font1">‘Kamu’ [you], </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Iba </span><span class="font1">‘Kamu’ [you] are nouns, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Ni Luh Sari </span><span class="font1">is a noun phrase, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Iraga </span><span class="font1">‘Kita’ [we] is a pronoun, and the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Ketut Anom </span><span class="font1">is a noun phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark27"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark28"></a>3.1.8 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ellipsis of Subject in the Subordinative Clause with the Conjunction Indicating Cause Meaning Relation</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The subject can also be omitted in the Subordinative clause using the conjunction indicating cause meaning relation which includes </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mawinan, dening</span><span class="font1"> ‘oleh karena’ [therefore], sawireh, mapan, krana, sangkaning, santukan ‘karena’ [therefore], as illustrated by the following examples.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">3-25)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ia </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngenah enggal tua sawireh</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sing suud – suud makeneh ane</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">3T kelihatan cepat tua Konj Neg henti – henti berpikir yang</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tidong – tidong</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:5)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">bukan – bukan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Dia kelihatan cepat tua karena tidak henti – hentinya berpikir yang bukan – bukan’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[He seemed to be getting older quickly as he kept thinking about what was not necessary]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-26)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pan Balang Tamak </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sing nuutin &nbsp;&nbsp;awig – awig banjar mawinan</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kena</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neg mematuhi peraturan banjar Konj kena</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">danda</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:9)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">denda</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Pan Balang Tamak tidak mematuhi peraturan banjar oleh karena itu kena denda’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Pan Balang Tamak broke the regulation issued by the banjar (hamlet); therefore, he was fined]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-27)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pan Balang Tamak </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngeka &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;daya krana</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tusing demen megae</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:53)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;membuat upaya Konj &nbsp;&nbsp;Neg suka bekerja</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Pan Balang Tamak membuat upaya karena tidak suka bekerja’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Pan Balang Tamak made attempted to do something as he did not like working]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-28)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Kedis </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ngajang sebun mapan</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lakar ngawit mataluh</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:11)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Art Burung membawa sarang Konj akan mulai bertelur</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Si Burung membawa sarang karena akan mulai bertelur’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[The bird brought nest as it would lay eggs soon]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-39)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Dewa Agung </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kituk – kituk santukan</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nenten resep teken munyin</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama geleng – geleng Konj Neg mengerti dengan pembicaraan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Pan Angklung Gadang-e</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:41)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;-Pos</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Dewa Agung geleng – geleng kepala karena tidak mengerti dengan pembicaraan</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Pan Angklung Gadang’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Dewa Agung shook their heads as he did not understand what was said by Pan Angklung Gadang]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-30)</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pan Angklung Gadang </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">tan presida naur &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;utang ring banjar sangkaning</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Nama &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Neg bisa &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;membayar hutang Prep Banjar Konj</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">kaliwat tiwas</span><span class="font1"> (SB,2006:49)</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">sangat miskin</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Pan Angklung Gadang tidak bisa membayar hutang di banjar karena sangat miskin’</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">[Pan Angklung Gadang could not pay his debt at the banjar (hamlet) as he was too poor]</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">(3-31)</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Sabilang minggu </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Wayan Suarta </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">luas ka pasih Sanur dening</span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"> Ø </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mandus di </span><span class="font1">setiap minggu Nama pergi Prep pantai Sanur Konj mandi Prep </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">pasih -e </span><span class="font1">pantai-Def</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">‘Setiap minggu Wayan Suarta pergi ke pantai Sanur oleh karena mandi di pantai itu’</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">[ Every Sunday Wayan Suarta went to Sanur Beach to bath]</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">From examples (3-25) – (3-31) it can be identified that the conjunctions used are </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">mawinan, dening</span><span class="font1"> ‘oleh karena’ [therefore], </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">sawireh, mapan, krana, sangkaning, santukan </span><span class="font1">‘karena’ [therefore], which indicate cause meaning relation. The subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Ia</span><span class="font1"> ‘Dia’ is a pronoun, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Pan Balang Tamak</span><span class="font1"> is noun phrase, the subject </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">I Kedis</span><span class="font1"> ‘Si Burung’ [the bird] is a noun phrase; the subjects </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Dewa Agung, Pan Angklung Gadang</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Wayan Suarta</span><span class="font1"> are too.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark29"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark30"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusions and Suggestions</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark31"></a>4.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">If observed from the level of syntactic function, the subjects from (3-1) to (3-31) are omitted for economic and practical purposes, although, if observed from the grammatical structures of the Subordinative constructions, they are not perfect. To make them perfect, the omitted elements are mentioned again or replaced with nouns, pronouns and so forth.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the level of category, the subjects omitted from (3-1) to (3-31) are nouns, noun phrases, and pronouns. Neither verb nor adjective and adverb are found, as far as the examples above are concerned.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">From the meanings of the conjunctions used in the Subordinative constructions (3-1) to (3-31), they are identified to indicate temporal, requisite, unison, result, expectation, conditional, concessive, and cause meaning relations.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark32"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark33"></a>4.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Suggestions</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">It is suggested to other researchers that they should explore the ellipsis of the the other syntactic functions such as the ellipsis of predicate, object and adverb. It is also suggested that they should compare the Subordinative clauses with conjunctions and those without</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">conjunctions, and the difference in meaning between the nucleus clauses and the non nucleus ones.</span></p>
