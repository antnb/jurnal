<h2><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Background: </span><span class="font2">Monitoring drug therapy based on Permenkes RI No. 73 of 2016 is one of the pharmaceutical care standards in pharmacy practice. Monitoring drug therapy is a process that includes activities to ensure safe, effective and rational drug therapy for patients. Patients who receive drug therapy are at risk of experiencing drug-related problems. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Objective: </span><span class="font2">This study aims to determine the implementation of monitoring drug therapy in one pharmacy in Badung Regency. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Methods: </span><span class="font2">The type of research is a descriptive observational study conducted by interview session one pharmacist in X Pharmacy, then analyzed descriptively according to Permenkes RI No 73 of 2016. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Results: </span><span class="font2">The results showed that in January-June 2020 there were 34 patients with disease categories requiring monitoring drug therapy: 27 patients with asthma (79%), six patients with diabetes (18%), and one patient with tuberculosis (3%). Patients with the largest visits were geriatric, which were 19 out of 34 patients. </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Conclusion</span><span class="font2">: The monitoring of drug therapy at Pharmacy X has fulfilled all the parameters in the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for the implementation of in Pharmacy based on Permenkes RI No.73 of 2016.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font2">Monitoring drug therapy, Implementation, Pharmacy, Pharmacist</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">Every pharmacist profession is obliged to practice pharmacy according to the legal requirements that apply and comply with professional and ethical standards. Based on the science of forensic pharmacy, pharmacists are involved in professional work related to litigation, regulatory processes, or the criminal justice system<sup>[2]</sup> In government legislation number 51 of 2009<sup>[9]</sup>, explained that in practising pharmacy care facilities, pharmacists must apply the pharmaceutical care standards of pharmacy. The referred service facilities can be in the form of a pharmacy, health center, or hospital that has been arranged in each Permenkes. The current pharmaceutical service orientation has shifted from drug oriented to patient-</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">oriented. Services that initially only focused on drug management must shift to a comprehensive service, both drug management and service to the community. It aims to improve the quality of people life.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Several studies related to the evaluation of pharmaceutical care standards in pharmacies based on legislation have been carried out<sup>[1,6,11]</sup>. Based on existing research mentioned that there are few pharmacists who do not apply it in maximum, so that needs to be reviewed more specific. One of the pharmaceutical service standards in pharmacies is monitoring drug therapy. Monitoring drug therapy is a process that includes activities to ensure safe, effective and rational drug therapy for</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">patients. In legislation in force in Indonesian is regulated in Permenkes RI No. 73 of 2016 concerning Pharmaceutical Services Standards in Pharmacies<sup>[8]</sup>. Monitoring drug therapy as standard pharmacy service aimed at preventing the occurrence of unwanted events to improve the quality of pharmaceutical care, ensure legal certainty for workers and protecting the patient’s pharmacy or community of irrational drug use. It has been stipulated in the constitution of Indonesian Republic number 11 of 2017 about Patient Safety<sup>[13]</sup>, which states that patient safety aims to improve the service quality of health care facilities through the implementation of risk management in all aspects of the services provided by the health care facility.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Implementation in pharmacies is based on Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) to improve the quality of pharmaceutical services provided to patients. Management has been standardized in its implementation at the Pharmacy. Those are selecting patients, collecting patient data, identifying problems, recommending therapy, monitoring, following up and documenting<sup>[3]</sup>.</span></p>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">S: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Subjective</span></p></li></ul>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">P: &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Plan</span></p></li></ul>

Based on Permenkes No.73 in 2016, the pharmacist is responsible for conducting the prescription review provided by the doctor, which includes administration studies, pharmaceutical suitability, and clinical considerations such as drug interactions.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Monitoring the success of therapy to patients is done verbally when patients come to Pharmacy X and by utilizing social media such as Whatsapp conducted by pharmacists and doctors for evaluation. Systematic documentation is in the renewal stage to adjust to the existing condition.</span></p>

<p><span class="font2">The stages of PTO in X’s Pharmacy are adjusted based on laws and regulations which have been outlined in Table 1. The Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) of the implementation of in Indonesian Pharmacy based on Permenkes RI No.73 of 2016 is implemented as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Patients selection by criteria</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Patients selection is the stage of beginning that is required to determine patients who require priority in monitoring. Patient selection can be based on the patient's condition or the drug the patient’s consuming<sup>[3]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taking the data that is needed is a history of the treatment of patients which consists of a history of the disease, a history of use of Drugs and history of allergies</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Retrieval of data of patients can be through medical records. The medical record is a draft of patient data, including history, current problems statement, diagnosis and treatment procedures<sup>[10]</sup>. Data that not yet included in the medical records can be obtained by other ways such as interviews with patients or their</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">family or force the health of others. These data are summarized and reviewed to be identified.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doing the identification of problems related to medicine.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Drug-related problems are events or circumstances that involve drug treatment that really or has the potential to interfere with the optimal results of medical care in patients<sup>[4]</sup>. Here are eight categories of problems related to drugs<sup>[12]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The patient has a medical condition that requires drug therapy, but the patient is not receiving a drug for that indication.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a medical condition with the wrong drug.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">c. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a medical condition for which too little of the correct drug is being taken.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">d. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The patient has a medical condition for which too much of the correct drug is being taken. ·</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">e. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The patient has a medical condition resulting from an adverse drug reaction.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">f. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a medical condition like drug interaction.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">g. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a medical condition because of not receiving the prescribed drug.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">h. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a medical condition because of taking a drug for which there is no valid medical indication.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pharmacist determines the priority of the problem</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the problem issues that have been identified, patients who require settlement soon need to be a priority.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Providing &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;therapeutic</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">recommendations</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Therapeutic recommendations or plan to follow up includes a plan of monitoring with the purpose of ensuring the achievement of the effects of therapy and minimize the effects which are not desirable on therapy were given based on efficacy, safety, cost, and the regimen that acceptable for a patient<sup>[3]</sup>.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The results of the identification of problems related to medicine and the recommendation that has been made by pharmacists must be communicated to other workers health optimize the purpose of therapy.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Make documentation about the implementation of monitoring of therapeutic drugs</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Documentation in aims as authentic evidence of pharmaceutical service implementation that can be used for accountability, service evaluation, education and research.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the results of observations in X’s Pharmacy in the Badung Regency, according to the seven points of SOP in Permenkes RI 73 of 2016, has been fulfilled all the parameters are accordance with the requirements for monitoring drug therapy based on regulations.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>CONCLUSION</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2">The &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;implementation &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;of</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">pharmaceutical services in X’s Pharmacy in Badung Regency showed that in January-June 2020 there were 34 patients</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">with disease categories requiring monitoring drug therapy: 27 patients with asthma (79%), 6 patients with diabetes (18%), and 1 patient with tuberculosis (3%). Patients with the largest visits were geriatric, which were 19 out of 34 patients. The monitoring of drug therapy at Pharmacy X has fulfilled all the parameters in the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for the implementation of in Pharmacy based on Permenkes RI No.73 of 2016.</span></p>
