<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>The Analysis Of Semantic Types Of Adjective Classes In Adjective Noun Collocation With References To The Novel </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"> And </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Fault In Our Stars</span></h1>

<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>Abstrak</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Penlitian yang berjudul </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">The Analysis of Semantic Type of Adjective Classes in Adjective Noun Collocation with References to the Novel Insurgent and The Fault in Our Stars </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ini bertujuan untuk meneliti dan menganalisis jenis dari kelas kata sifat yang ada dalam kolokasi kata sifat dan kata benda. Penelitian ini bermaksud menganalisis kolokasi kata sifat dan kata benda serta menganalisa makna dari kolokasi tersebut secara rinci. Data dari penelitian ini diambil dari dua novel yang berjudul “insurgent”dan “The Fault in Our Stars” yang diterbitkan pada tahun 2012. Data dikumpulkan dengan menggunakan metode dokumentasi dan dianalisa dengan menggunakan metode kualitatif. Teori yang digunakan dalam penelitian ini dikemukakan oleh Quirk (1973) tentang “semantic types of adjective”. Berdasarkan analisa, ditemukan delapan jenis dari kata sifat. Yaitu “intensifying adjective”, “post determiners and limiter adjective”, “subjective measure”, “objective measure”, “denoting age”, “denoting color”, “denoting material”, and “denoting provenance or style”. Dari analisa juga ditemukan hampir semua contoh termasuk kedalam jenis “collocation based on the item”. empat contoh lainnya termasuk kedalam jenis “collocation strictest sense”.</span><span class="font2"> Dan semua contoh dari kolokasi kata sifat- kata benda termasuk kedalam jenis “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">collocation based on the range”.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">semantic types of adjective classes, kata sifat, kolokasi kata sifat- kata benda.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">A word cannot stand by itself because it does not have meaning. A word can have meaning when it entries a sentence or a clause. The combination of word is a word related with another word. As we know that, there are so many kinds of word combinations. However, this writing focused on collocation. Collocation is the way</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">words combine in a language to produce natural-sounding speech and writing,” which emphasizes production in the target language rather than only comprehension.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In collocation, a word has its own restriction, such as some are based wholly on the meaning of the item, some are based on the range, and some combinations are collocation in the restriction sense, involving neither meaning nor range.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Based on the phenomena in the background above, some problems can be discussed clearly in this study. The problems are formulated as follow:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the semantic types of adjective in adjective noun collocation that found in the novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font2">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What are the meanings of adjective noun collocation that found in the novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars</span><span class="font2">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In this writing, there are two kinds of aims, they are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To find out the semantic types of adjective in adjective noun collocation are found in the novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">b) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze meanings of the adjective noun collocation found in the novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The methodology includes the data source, the method and technique of collecting data, which includes some steps in analyzing the data. In this writing, the data sources are novels entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Insurgent</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Fault in Our Stars.</span><span class="font2"> These novels were used as data source because provide many adjective noun collocations which support this writing.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The study used documentation method in collecting the data. The method was done by reviewing the literature mentioned above. Second, the texts in detail are found to find out the collocation written down in the texts. The collocation was only limited to adjective noun. Third, the words were noted and quoted on the texts that show the adjective noun collocation. Fourth, data were identified, grouped and classified</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">according to its kinds. Finally, the meaning inside was found and explanation or reason was given based on the theory.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The collected data was analyzed by qualitative method. The collected data analyzed based on explanation of the theory in theoretical framework. The theory of semantic type of adjective in adjective noun collocation is analyzed by theory of Quirk (1973) and their meanings are explained. Each part of the data is given the same set of analysis in accordance with the general overview in order to draw a conclusion.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span></h2>
<ul style="list-style:none;">
<li>
<p><span class="font2">3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;Intensifying Adjectives</span></p></li></ul></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Intensifying adjectives have a heightening or lowering effect on the noun they modify. Some adjectives are considered as intensifying adjectives, such as </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">real</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">perfect </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">poor</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. but that my headache was caused by </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">poor oxygenation</span><span class="font2"> (Green, 2012: 34)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">poor oxygenation</span><span class="font2"> is considered as collocation based on the meaning of the item. Poor oxygenation means the process of oxygenation which has a low or inferior standard or quality (Macmillan: 2010). The adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">poor</span><span class="font2"> is lowering effect of the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">oxygenation.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Post Determiners and Limiter Adjectives</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Post determiners and limiter adjectives are used to particularize the reference of nouns. This class commonly co-occurs with nouns to specify the noun itself.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) I am not the </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">only person</span><span class="font2"> with bathing in mind (Roth, 2012:43)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only person</span><span class="font2"> is considered as collocation based on the meaning of the item. This collocation is predicted from its individual words. The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only person</span><span class="font2"> means limitation of human being as individual. The meaning of the adjective </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only</span><span class="font2"> is combined with the meaning of noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">person.</span><span class="font2"> The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only person,</span><span class="font2"> the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only</span><span class="font2"> is used specify the noun </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">person</span><span class="font2">. In contrast, the combination </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lone person</span><span class="font2"> is</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">not easily understood and not heard naturally. Although the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">only</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">lone</span><span class="font2"> are synonyms.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;General Adjective Susceptible to the Subjective Measures</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This kind of adjective is commonly used to denote feelings, ideas and experiences. It is used based on personal taste or opinions</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. You’re giving us a </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">bad reputation</span><span class="font2"> among the Candor (Roth,2012:58)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">bad reputation</span><span class="font2"> is considered as collocation based on the meaning of the item. Bad reputation means the loss of other people’s respect because of something bad that you have done (Macmillan:2010).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;General Adjective Susceptible to Objective Measure (Size or Shape)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">This kind of adjectives is denoting size or shape. It is susceptible which is commonly used to denote size of an object.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a) It’s no </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">big deal</span><span class="font2">.” (Green, 2012:67)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">big deal</span><span class="font2"> is considered as collocation based on the strictest sense. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Big deal</span><span class="font2"> is</span><a href="http://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/used"><span class="font2"> used </span></a><span class="font2">for</span><a href="http://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/showing"><span class="font2"> showing </span></a><span class="font2">that you do not</span><a href="http://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/think_1"><span class="font2"> think </span></a><span class="font2">that something is very</span><a href="http://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/good_1"><span class="font2"> good </span></a><span class="font2">or </span><a href="http://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/impressive"><span class="font2">impressive </span></a><span class="font2">(Macmillan: 2010). </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Big deal</span><span class="font2"> here are not words that occur in neutral context but words that have a single meaning, and the meaning is determined by their context of the sentence. The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">big deal is</span><span class="font2"> easily understood. In contrast, the combination </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">large deal</span><span class="font2"> not easily understood and not heard naturally. Although the word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">big</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">large</span><span class="font2"> are synonyms.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.5 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjectives Denoting Age</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adjectives that are commonly used to denote age such as </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">young, new</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">old</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. My </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">old man</span><span class="font2">. He always knew just what to say (Green, 2012:78)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The collocation </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">old man</span><span class="font2"> is considered as collocation based on the strictest sense. Old man used for talking to a male friend or name used for talking to someone in a friendly way (Macmillan:2010). In this case, someone talk about her father and she calls her father “old man”. Here the meaning of this collocation is determined by the context of the sentence.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3.6 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjective Denoting Color</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Color is determined as a visible quality that objects have, produced by the way they reflect light. Here, by using the words of color, a person can specify something in term of its color.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">For example:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">a. I would wade in the </span><span class="font2" style="text-decoration:underline;">White River</span><span class="font2"> (Green, 2012:21)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The adjective white is combined together with the noun river. This collocation is considered as collocation based on the strictest sense. White river is a river that flows in two main branches through Indiana, past Indianapolis, to the</span><a href="http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/wabash-river%23wabash-river__4"><span class="font2"> Wabash River.</span></a><span class="font2"> White river here are not words that occur in the neutral context but words that have a single meaning and the meaning is determined by their context of the sentence. In this context someone talks about the place, White river is the name of river not river in white color.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">After analyzing all the data which are taken from the novels, it can be concluded that all kinds of semantic type of adjective noun adjective collocation can be found in the novels like intensifying adjective, post determiners and limiter adjectives, subjective measure, object measure (size and shape), adjectives age, denoting color, denoting material and denoting provenance or style.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">All examples of noun adjective collocation are included into collocation based on the meaning of the item, except: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">big bang, big deal, old man,</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">white river</span><span class="font2">. They are collocation based on strictest sense or have secondary meaning in certain context of situation.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The type of collocational is based on the range. All examples of noun adjectives collocation can be classified into this type because they can go together with a set of words that have some semantic features in common. These words collocate with an adjective to form collocational ranges which have the semantic features.</span></p>
