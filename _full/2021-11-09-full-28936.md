<h1><a name="bookmark3"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark4"></a>Abstrak</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Makna kias bisa ditemukan di dalam bentuk – bentuk ujaran dan biasanya digunakan pada karya fiksi, seperti novel. Dalam penerjemahan makna kias tersebut terdapat prosedur – prosedur yang diterapkan. Penelitian ini bertujuan untuk mengetahui makna kias dalam novel ʻThe Giverʼ dan bagaimana prosedur penerjemahan dipakai untuk menerjemahkan makna kias tersebut dalam novel ʻSang Pemberiʼ. Makna kias dalam bentuk ujaran tersebut diidentifikasi dengan menggunakan teori dari Larson (1998), dan prosedur penerjemahannya dianalisis dengan menggunakan teori dari Vinay dan Darbelnet (1995). Hasil penelitian menunjukkan bahwa ada makna kias yang digunakan dalam tujuh bentuk ujaran. Dalam penerjemahan makna kias tersebut, ada dua prosedur yang diterapkan, yaitu terjemahan langsung dan terjemahan tak langsung.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Kata kunci: makna kias, prosedur penerjemahan, terjemahan langsung, terjemahan tak langsung</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark5"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">To change the form of one language into another language is sometimes difficult for some translators. The reason is that translation involves the change in the form of the source language (SL) into the target language (TL) (Larson, 1998:3). It is like serving the same dish with different plates, a translator tries to transfer the same meaning but in different styles based on the target language into which it is translated.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Nowadays, especially in Indonesia, there are many translated novels. The Indonesian translators have succeeded in changing the form of one language (SL),</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">especially that written in English into Indonesian (TL) so that the readers in the receptor language can enjoy the same literature as the readers of the source language. Figurative senses are mostly found in literary work such as fiction, prose, and novel. The biggest challenge is to find the equivalent meaning to make it acceptable to the TL readers. Figurative senses cannot usually be translated literally (Larson 1998:121). Translating a text which contains figurative senses might be difficult as the translators need to find the closest translation equivalent in the TL. Thus, the translated text will sound acceptable and does not sound strange.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Problems of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are two problems regarding translation to discuss. They are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What figurative senses occur in the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Giver</span><span class="font2">?</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What translation procedures are applied to the translation of the figurative senses in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sang Pemberi</span><span class="font2">?</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Aims of the Study</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The aims of this study are:</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To identify and classify the figurative senses occurring in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Giver</span><span class="font2"> as the SL.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To analyze the translation procedures applied to the translation of figurative senses in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sang Pemberi</span><span class="font2"> as the TL</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Method</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The data were taken from two novels. The Giver, written in English by Lowry in 1993, was selected as the SL; while its translation in Bahasa Indonesia by Tarman (published in 2014) was selected as the TL. The novels, both the original and the translated ones, were chosen as data sources because they are interesting and contain the data needed in this study. As translation needs to find the closest equivalent meaning, this novel contains some figurative senses, such as social, sex and death.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">In composing this study, library research method was applied as the method of collecting data since the data source is novel. It was done using several techniques. First, reading the English version novel as the SL to get a better understanding of the story. The next step was taking note of any words or phrases containing figurative senses in SL novel. Subsequently, their translations in the TL novel were noted to find out the translation procedure. Ultimately, the data were classified to be analyzed using the theories concerning this topic.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Qualitative method was applied in this study to give an actual explanation descriptively. The analysis was done by identifying and classifying words and phrases where the figurative senses occurred by applying the theory proposed by Larson in his book entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Meaning-Based Translation</span><span class="font2"> (1998) about figurative senses in figures of speech. Furthermore, the translation procedures in the translation of figurative senses were analyzed by applying the theory proposed by Vinay and Darbelnet in their books entitled A Methodology for Translation (1995).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Result and Discussion</span><br><br><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>5.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Figurative Senses Occurring in The Giver</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The figurative senses in novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">The Giver</span><span class="font2"> were classified into figures of speech, namely: metonymy, synecdoche, euphemism, idioms, hyperbole, metaphor and simile based on the types of figure of speech proposed by Larson (1998).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metonymy</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Metonymy is the use of words in a figurative sense involving association. It means that one word substitutes another word but they are not synonymous. In sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">He had been permitted an additional month in which to complete </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the hours</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">…(Lowry, 1993:28),</span><span class="font2"> the noun phrase </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the hour</span><span class="font2"> is used in a figurative way. It is used to show certain amount of time in the story, a time for doing volunteer work, as well as showing temporal relationship.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Synecdoche</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Synecdoche is figurative senses based on part-whole relationships (a part of something which is being substituted for the whole part). It can be described as using part of something to mean the whole of it. In sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">NEEDLESS TO SAY, HE WILL BE RELEASED, </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">the voice </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">had said…(Lowry, 1993:8)</span><span class="font2">, the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the voice</span><span class="font2"> refers to </span><span class="font1">17</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the speakerʼs voice</span><span class="font2">, a person who was in charge of giving information. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Voice</span><span class="font2"> is a part of human’s whole body and it is used in a figurative way to refer to a person in charge.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Idioms</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Idioms are expressions of ‘at least two words which cannot be understood literally and which function as a unit semantically’ (Beekman and Callow, 1974:121). In the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">I </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">made friends </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">on a new level, friends of all ages. (Lowry, 1993:18)</span><span class="font2">, the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">made friends</span><span class="font2"> means </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">to become a friend</span><span class="font2"> (Hornby, 2010:177). The sentence means that Jonas’s mother became friends with people of all ages in her new office.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Euphemism</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Euphemism it is used to avoid an offensive expression or one that is socially unacceptable or unpleasant (Beekman and Callow, 1974:119). In sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">a time of celebration for a life well and fully lived; and release of a </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">new child </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">(Lowry, 1993:7)</span><span class="font2">, the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">new child</span><span class="font2"> is considered acceptable in the story to avoid the use of word like </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">baby</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Hyperbole</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Hyperbole is a metonymy or synecdoche used by the author intended to understand Beekman and Callow (1974:118). The exaggeration words or phrases are used to emphasize the sense of a sentence. In the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Frightened meant that </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">deep, sickening feeling</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">…(Lowry, 1993:6)</span><span class="font2">, the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">deep, sickening feeling</span><span class="font2"> is exaggerated to emphasize the meaning of word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">frightening</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Metaphor</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Metaphor is an analogy between two objects or ideas. It means that one object is compared to another object that has similarity; usually in terms of characteristic. In the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">it was a disaster </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">for them (Lowry, 1993:144),</span><span class="font2"> the sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it was a disaster </span><span class="font2">means that the situation (it) is compared with a disaster, a state of sudden misfortune (Hornby, 2010:126).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Simile</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Simile is a figure of speech that compares two things using the conjunction such as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">like</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">as</span><span class="font2"> or </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">than</span><span class="font2">. In sentence </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">They acted like </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">... l</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">ike animals.</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">&quot; (Lowry, 1993:11)</span><span class="font2">, the expression means that the other group (they) acted without thinking, because animal means a living creature that can feel and move (Hornby, 2010:14).</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>5.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Translation Procedures Used to Translate Figurative Senses</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">There are seven procedures divided into two, namely direct translation and oblique translation. The translation procedures applied in the translation of figurative senses are as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Direct Translation</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Borrowing</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Borrowing means a word taken directly from another language. In other words, it is considered the simplest procedure of translation. In the sentences above where some figurative senses were classified, there are no examples of borrowing found in the translation into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sang Pemberi</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Calque</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Calque is a special kind of borrowing whereby a language borrows an expression from another language and each of its elements is translated literally. The result could be a lexical calque, a calque which respects the syntactic structure of the TL while introducing a new mode of expression, or a structural calque that introduces a new construction into the language (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:32). The example is the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">new child</span><span class="font2"> which is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">anak baru</span><span class="font2"> (Tarman, 2014:19) in TL. The expression is translated literally in TL that is considered equivalent. Subsequently, it introduces a new expression in TL</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font2"> Thus, it is categorized as a lexical calque.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Literal Translation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Literal translation is the transfer of SL to TL literally or word-for-word translation, the direct transfer of a SL text into a grammatically and idiomatically appropriate TL text (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:33). The example is the translation of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">it was a disaster </span><span class="font2">into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">itu menjadi bencana</span><span class="font2"> in TL (Tarman 2014:172). The translation is grammatically correct.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5.2.2 Oblique Translation</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Transposition</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Transposition is a method of translation that includes the change of word class without changing the actual meaning of the message (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:36). It also involves grammatical change that occurs in the translation, such as: position of adjective, changing the word class or part of speech. It can be seen in the translation </span><span class="font1">19</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">deep, sickening feeling</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">perasaan memualkan jauh di dalam tubuh</span><span class="font2"> in TL (Tarman, 2014:11). The word </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">deep</span><span class="font2"> is a word, but it is translated into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">jauh di dalam tubuh</span><span class="font2"> which is a phrase.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Modulation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In modulation, there is a change in point of view when the SL is translated into the TL. This change can be justified when translation results in a grammatically correct utterance but it is considered unsuitable, unidiomatic or awkward in the TL (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:36). The example is the translation of the expression </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the hour</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">waktu kerja sukarelanya</span><span class="font2"> in TL (Tarman, 2014:41). The equivalent is based on the context in the story.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Equivalence</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Equivalence as a translation procedure is used to describe the same situation using different style or structural methods. The striking cases of equivalences are the onomatopoeia of animal sounds. The method of creating equivalences is also frequently applied to idioms (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:38). The example is the translation of the idiom </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">made friends</span><span class="font2"> into </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">memiliki pertemanan</span><span class="font2"> in TL (Tarman, 2014:11). The translation undergoes equivalence as friendship cannot be ‘made’ like when someone ‘makes a pancake’ or something else. The meaning of the idioms in the sentence above is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">having friends</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font2">7. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adaptation</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Adaptation is the final procedure in which the translators have to create a new situation in the TL that can be considered being equivalent. It is used in cases where the type of situation being referred to by the SL message is unknown in TL culture (Vinay and Darbelnet, 1995:39). This translation procedure cannot be found in any of the translation of figurative senses in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Sang Pemberi.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h1><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h1></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">It can be concluded that the figurative senses occurring in TL novel are: metonymy, synecdoche, idioms, euphemism, hyperbole, metaphor and simile. In analyzing the translation procedures of the figurative senses, there are five translation procedures applied, namely: calque, literal translation, transposition, modulation and equivalence. The translation procedure is dominated by the direct translation (calque and literal </span><span class="font1">20</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">translation). This is done to make the reader understand the situation in the SL better. One method of direct translation and one method of oblique translation are absent in the translation of figurative senses, namely, borrowing and adaptation.</span></p>
