<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>Abstract</span></h2>
<p><span class="font1">This undergraduate thesis entitled </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Types of Passive Construction in the Novel Say You’re Sorry by Michael Robotham</span><span class="font1"> is aimed at analyzing the types of passive voice found in the novel and figuring out which one among them is the most dominant used in the novel. The data source of this study was taken from Michael Robotham’s </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Say You’re Sorry</span><span class="font1"> which was published by Sphere in 2012. This study is a descriptive qualitative and quantitative research and the method used to collect the data was the documentation method using the note taking technique. The result of this study shows that there are only three types of passive voice found in the novel: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">be </span><span class="font1">passive, </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">get</span><span class="font1"> passive, and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">bare</span><span class="font1"> passive. From 219 passive sentences taken from the novel, 207 of them is </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">be</span><span class="font1"> passive with the percentages of 95% while there are only 7 sentences of get passive with percentage of 3% and 5 sentences of bare passive type with the percentage of only 2%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Key words: passive voice, be passive, get passive, bare passive</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstrak</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Skripsi berjudul “Types of Passive Construction in the Novel Say You’re Sorry by Michael Robotham” ini bertujuan untuk menganalisa bentuk-bentuk kalimat pasif di dalam novel serta mencari tahu bentuk pasif manakah yang paling sering digunakan. Sumber data dalam penelitian ini diambil dari novel berjudul “Say You’re Sorry”karya Michael Robotham yang dipublikasikan oleh Sphere pada tahun 2012. Studi ini merupakan penelitian deskriptif kualitatif dan kuantitatif dengan metode pegumpulan data yaitu metode dokumentasi yang menggunakna tekhnik mencatat. Hasil dari penelitian ini menunjukkan bahwa hanya ada tiga betuk kalimat pasif yag ditemukan di dalam novel: pasif be, pasif get, dan pasif bare. Dari 219 kalimat pasif yang diperoleh dalam novel, pasif be terdiri dari 207 kalimat dengan persentase sebesar 95%, sementara hanya ada 7 kalimat yang menggunakan pasif get dengan persentase 3% dan 5 kalimat yang meggunaka pasif bare dengan persentase sebesar 2%.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Kata kunci:Kalimat Pasif, pasif be, pasif get, pasif bare</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Background of the Study</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">Language (spoken or written) is a ‘tool’ that people use to communicate with others. The form, meaning, and context of language could be analyzed by learning the linguistic study. It incorporates a number of aspects which often include phonetics, phonology, morphology, pragmatics, semantics, and syntax. Syntax is a component of the language that interconnects words into larger units, one of which is a sentence.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A sentence could be written in active or passive voice.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">A sentence is written in active. The sentence is performing the action while it could also be written in passive voice when the subject of the sentence has something done to it by someone or something</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Passive sentence could be found in many literary works including novels. In this study, a thriller novel entitled </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Say You’re Sorry</span><span class="font2"> by Michael Robotham was used as the source of data to be analyzed</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">in order to find more about passive voice and its types used in the novel. The novel tells a story about two friends Tash and Piper who got kidnapped and locked up for about three years by anonymous.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The topic in this study was chosen because the passive analysis on novel is rarely found and even though the use of active voice in literary works is still dominant compared with the passive voice, there are still many sentences of passive voice found in novels with different types. The study focuses on the types of passive voice clauses or sentences found in the novel </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Say You’re Sorry</span><span class="font2"> by Michael Robotham and compared them to find out which types are more dominant to be found in the novel.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark62"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark63"></a>5.1.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Get Passive</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">In the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">get-passive,</span><span class="font2"> the subject receives the action of the verb in the form of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">get </span><span class="font2">plus a past participle.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark64"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark65"></a>Data 10</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Aiden got wasted at the party</span><span class="font2"> (Pg.188)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Seeing the sentences above, the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">get passive</span><span class="font2"> is normally constructed with human subject that is non-agentive, affected. It emphasizes more on the subject and on what happens to the subject as a result of the event rather than the agent. It emphasizes the subject referent’s condition which is usually on unfavorable conditions just like </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">got sent to see the headmistress</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">got wasted at the party.</span><span class="font2"> The events that are perceived are either having fortunate or unfortunate consequences for the subjects.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark66"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark67"></a>5.1.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Bare Passive</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">We can have passive with no auxiliary verb at all. They are called bare passives because they are kinds of naked without their auxiliary verb and cannot be put into tenses form.</span></p>
<h2><a name="bookmark68"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark69"></a>Data 11</span></h2>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">She leads me upstairs, pausing at the open door to Emma’s room where we watch our youngest sleeping, surrounded by stuffed animals and her gloriously imaginative paintings. (Pg. 434)</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The passive clause above only consists of subject preceded by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font2"> past participle of a verb. The subject of the clause is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">our youngest</span><span class="font2"> followed by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">by phrase</span><span class="font2"> and past participle </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">surrounded.</span><span class="font2"> It doesn’t contain </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">be</span><span class="font2"> (was, were) as it does in passive with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">be</span><span class="font2"> but we know that the</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">clause implies the same meaning as it has in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">our youngest are surrounded by.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark70"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark71"></a>6. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">According to Pullum, there are seven types of passive voice. They are passive with be, prepositional passive, bare passive, embedded passive, adjectival passive, get passive, and concealed passive. However, there are only three types of passive voice found in the study, namely passive with </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">be (</span><span class="font2">Long) and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">(</span><span class="font2">Short), Get passive and Bare passive.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">From 219 passive sentences found in the novel, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">be</span><span class="font2"> passive is more dominant used in the novel with a percentage of 95% while bare passive type is the least used among others with only a percentage of 2%.</span></p>
