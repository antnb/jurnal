<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>Nowcasting</span><span class="font4" style="font-weight:bold;"> the Number of Airplane Passengers at Ngurah Rai Airport Using Google Trends Data</span></h1>
<p><span class="font1">I Putu Juni Adi Widianata<sup>a1</sup></span><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;">, </span><span class="font1">Nori Wilantika<sup>a2</sup></span></p>

<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstract</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Data on the number of aircraft passengers is essential to airport managers and the government's policies. The policy relates to improving the facilities and capacity of airports and other affected sectors, such as the transportation and tourism industries. A policy taken will be better if the data used is very close to the time of policy decision-making. Therefore, a technique is needed to forecast very close to the current condition of the number of aircraft passengers, namely nowcasting. One of the data sources that can be used for nowcasting is Google Trends data. In this study, the identification of relevant keywords used for nowcasting, the formation of nowcasting models, and the search for the best model for nowcasting the number of aircraft passengers was carried out. The nowcasting methods used are SARIMAX and multilayer perceptron. In this study, five relevant keywords were generated for domestic departures and two for international departures. In the nowcasting modeling, the best model for nowcasting domestic departures is produced, namely the multilayer perceptron with MAPE and MAE values of 11.194% and 28.048 respectively, while for departures Internationally, the best model was produced, namely SARIMAX with MAPE and MAE values of 8,641% and 50,205 respectively.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Nowcasting,</span><span class="font1"> Google Trends</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">,</span><span class="font1"> SARIMAX, airplane passengers, multilayer perceptron</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Data on the number of aircraft passengers is one of the air transportation statistical data released by Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS) every month through the Official Statistical Statistics (BRS), divided into two types: domestic departure aircraft passengers and international departure aircraft passengers. The development of the number of aircraft passengers at an airport is essential regarding the policies that airport managers and the government will carry out. High-flight activity must be balanced with the airport's safety, facilities, and capacity factors. For example, such as the addition of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a runway</span><span class="font1"> at Soekarno Hatta Airport by airport managers due to high flight activities at Soekarno Hatta Airport [1]. For the government, the increase in airplane passengers can be used to take a policy because high aviation activities will impact the economy, especially in the transportation and tourism industries.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Taking a policy requires data that is very close to the time of policy decision-making. Data on the number of aircraft passengers issued by BPS has </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a</span><span class="font1"> lag time of approximately two months from the reported data. With the time </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">lag</span><span class="font1">, data is not suitable to be used as a basis for policy decision-making. The closer the data period is to the decision-making time, the better the policies will be generated. Therefore, techniques are needed to carry out forecasting that is very close to the current conditions of the number of aircraft passengers.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Forecasting related to air passenger demand is essential to developing a proper airport operation plan in the aviation industry. The airport operation requires long-term demand forecasting to draw up a long-term plan and short-term demand forecasting to draw up more urgent plans, such as short-term maintenance plans, airport schedules, and monthly operation plans [2].</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">With the importance of short-term demand forecasting for airport managers and government policies, techniques are needed to make current predictions </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">or nowcasting</span><span class="font1">. </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Nowcasting</span><span class="font1"> is very</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">close to forecasting the present and the future. The process </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">of nowcasting</span><span class="font1"> requires an indicator that can come from various sources. The development of technology and internet use in this digital era is developing very rapidly. In line with that, more and more new data can be used for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nowcasting</span><span class="font1">. One of the data sources that can be used for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nowcasting</span><span class="font1"> is Google Trends data, which is available in real time.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Google is a search engine that is quite widely used. In Indonesia, from 2009 to October 2021, the average user who used Google as a search engine was 96.87%, with the highest reaching 98.78% occurring in February 2018 [3]. The percentage is calculated by comparing search engine users using Google against all search engine users. With such a large number of Google search engine users in Indonesia, the Google search index data can be used to represent the behavior of internet users in Indonesia. This is supported by research [4], which states that internet search data can provide an overview of the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">micro-behavior</span><span class="font3"> of </span><span class="font1">internet users so that it can be used in predicting socioeconomic indicators.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Ngurah Rai Airport is one of the main airports in Indonesia. In addition, the airport is ranked in the top 100 best airports in the world [5]. Based on publications issued by BPS [6], the transportation and warehousing sector is the third largest contributor to the GRDP of Bali Province. In 2020, this sector generated an added value of only 15.66 trillion rupiah, a decrease of 36.24% compared to the previous year. The main cause of the decline in air transportation is the restrictions applied during the pandemic. The decline in air transportation impacts the decrease in flight activities at Ngurah Rai Airport, starting in the early quarter of the second quarter of 2020.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Bali Province is a province with a high level of internet penetration. Based on the publication of BPS [7], the percentage of people who have accessed the Internet aged five years and over is 61.06%. This percentage is higher than the national internet penetration rate of 53.73%. The significant level of internet penetration makes Bali Province the province with the fifth highest internet penetration rate in Indonesia in 2020. The high internet penetration rate in Bali Province can be used to see an overview of air transportation conditions through searches conducted on the Internet related to flight information.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Modeling nowcasting the</span><span class="font1"> number of airplane passengers at Ngurah Rai Airport with Google Trends requires keywords relevant to the data on the number of aircraft passengers. The search keywords obtained must be able to describe the indicators of the number of airplane passengers at Ngurah Rai Airport well. Therefore, the identification process is necessary for generating keywords suitable for delineating the indicator of the number of aircraft passengers at Ngurah Rai Airport.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In addition, models built for </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nowcasting</span><span class="font1"> should be appropriate for predicting the number of passengers in the aircraft. The model was created using statistically time-guided methods and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">machine learning,</span><span class="font1"> such as research [8], which uses SARIMAX and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Neural Network</span><span class="font1"> (NN). Specifically, this study used one of the NN models with a </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">perceptron multilayer</span><span class="font1"> architecture recommended in the study [9].</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Methods</span><br><br><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark6"></a>2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Google Trends</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Google Trends is a service from Google that provides internet search information based on queries. Google Trends generates an index of search activity ranging from 0 to 100, measured based on comparing a particular query in the selected region and time against the total number of queries for the same period. Before making a comparison, Google Trends normalizes search data to facilitate comparison. The data on Google Trends shows the user's daily search, but there are unnatural searches as it is. Automated searches can be interpreted as spam attempts on search results[10]. For this reason, based on Google support, Google Trends filters several searches such as[10] :</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Few people conduct searches: Google Trends only shows data for popular terms. Search terms with a low number will be displayed with a value of &quot;0&quot;.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Duplicate searches: Google Trends removes repeated inquiries by the same user shortly.</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Special characters: Google Trends filter queries related to punctuation and other special characters.</span></p></li></ul>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark7"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark8"></a>2.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Data Collection Methods</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Data on the number of airplane passengers departing at Ngurah Rai Airport was obtained through the BPS website, and Google Trends index data was obtained from the Google Trends application using Python version 3.8.3 with an API for Google Trends, namely &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">pytrends</span><span class="font1">.&quot; The keywords used for the index data collection process are relevant to </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nowcasting</span><span class="font1"> the number of passengers on domestic and international departure aircraft at Ngurah Rai Airport.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The stages of collecting Google Trends Index data in this study are as follows.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Define a list of basic </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">keywords (seed queries</span><span class="font1">)</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Basic search keywords are relevant to air transportation, especially the number of domestic and international departing passengers at Ngurah Rai Airport. The basic search keywords are determined by researchers through theoretical studies related to air transportation demand and adding keywords derived from related research.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Download </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">related queries</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Related keywords are keywords that users also use when searching. Related keywords are obtained from basic keywords whose process has been provided by Google Trends with its features. Related keyword results from each base keyword are added to the base keyword list, increasing the number of keywords used.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Eliminate duplicate keywords</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The results of related keywords added to the list of basic keywords are then eliminated. The elimination process to avoid duplicating related keywords is generated from each base keyword so that the resulting list of keywords is unique.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Download Google Trends Index data.</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The list of keywords that have been obtained is then used to download Google Trends search index data. Downloading data is adjusted to the location of Ngurah Rai Airport, namely Bali Province.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">5. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Perform </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">feature selection</span><span class="font1">. This section consists of two stages: Pearson correlation with values over 0.7 and selection of LASSO variables. These two stages refer to research [11]</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The research data period for Google Trends index data related to domestic departures and data on the number of domestic departure aircraft passengers is January 2016 – December 2021, while Google Trends index data related to international departures and data on the number of international departure aircraft passengers is January 2016 – February 2020.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark9"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark10"></a>2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Analysis Methods</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The modeling method used for nowcasting in this study is SARIMAX and multilayer perceptron, whose process stages can be seen in Figure 1. Both methods are implemented in Rstudio. Data for modeling is divided into training data for the period January 2016–December 2020, with a total of 60 data for domestic departures and January 2016–February 2019 for international departures with 38 data, while data testing each with 12 testing data for domestic departures for the January 2021 period – December 2021 and international departures for the period March 2019 – February 2020.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark11"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark12"></a>2.3.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SARIMAX</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Time series data can have seasonal movement patterns seen from the repeated increase or decrease in specific periods influenced by seasonal factors. The seasonal ARIMA model (SARIMA) is used if the data contains seasonal patterns. SARIMAX is a time series model called an extension of the SARIMA model. The extension contained in the SARIMAX model is the presence of additional or exogenous variables. The formation of the SARIMAX model begins with establishing the SARIMA model, which has fulfilled the parameter significance test and diagnostic test.</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/93169-1.jpg" alt="" style="width:368pt;height:383pt;">

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark13"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark14"></a>2.3.2 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Multilayer Perceptron (MLP)</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The MLP method is one of the network architectures in ANN that has one or more </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layers </span><span class="font1">between the input and output layers. In MLP, data </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">training</span><span class="font1"> is carried out through </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a backpropagation</span><span class="font1"> algorithm process. This algorithm can solve real problems with skilled architectural models by performing well for various data and recognizing complex patterns [12]. The </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">backpropagation</span><span class="font1"> algorithm is carried out in two stages: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">feed-forward</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">backward</span><span class="font1">. The data will be entered into the input layer in the forward feed</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">.</span><span class="font1"> At this stage, there is a calculation process from the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">input layer</span><span class="font1"> stage through </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the hidden layer</span><span class="font1"> to reach </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the output layer</span><span class="font1"> that produces the predicted value. The resulting prediction </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">error</span><span class="font1"> value can be known because the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">real</span><span class="font1"> value in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">training</span><span class="font1"> is known. Therefore, in the second stage, backward are carried out, and the error value generated from the previous process will be used to adjust the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">weight</span><span class="font1"> in each </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neuron</span><span class="font1"> on the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">output layer</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layer.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark15"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark16"></a>2.4 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Evaluation Methods</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">nowcasting modeling</span><span class="font1"> results of the SARIMAX method and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">perceptron multilayer</span><span class="font1"> were then compared with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">error</span><span class="font1"> values. This study used several evaluation methods: </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Mean Absolute Percentage Error</span><span class="font1"> (MAPE) and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">Mean Absolute Error</span><span class="font1"> (MAE). For MAPE, there are criteria, namely [13] :</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font1">1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MAPE value is below 10%; then forecasting is excellent,</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MAPE values are in the range of 10% - 20%, then forecasting is good,</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MAPE values are in the range of 20% - 50%, then forecasting is quite good, and</span></p></li>
<li>
<p><span class="font1">4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;MAPE value is greater than 50%; hence, the forecasting is poor.</span></p></li></ul>

<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark19"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark20"></a>3.1.3. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elimination of Duplicate Keywords</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The elimination is done on the total domestic and international departure search keywords list. From the process, 315 unique keywords out of 613 search keywords for domestic departures. It has generated 426 unique keywords for international departures out of 867 search keywords. <h2><a name="bookmark21"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark22"></a>3.1.4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Correlated Search Keyword List</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">The downloaded Google Trends Index data is then selected by looking at Pearson's correlation value with data on the number of passengers departing domestic and international departures. But among these keywords, there are irrelevant keywords related to domestic and international departures. Therefore, researchers manually eliminate keywords based on searching the meaning of each keyword on the Internet.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">The elimination process obtained 40 relevant search keywords for domestic departures. However, no relevant keywords correlated more than 0.7; therefore, researchers lowered the minimum correlation limit to more than 0.6 for international departures to obtain two relevant search keywords. If </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the p-value</span><span class="font1"> of the ADF test is smaller than the value of α = 5%, then it can be said that the data is already stationary, and if the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">p-value</span><span class="font1"> of the KPSS test is greater than the value of α = 5%, then the data can be declared stationary [18]. The test results at the level showed that the data was not stationary, so </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a differencing</span><span class="font1"> process was carried out.</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark28"></a><span class="font1" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark29"></a>3.2.2. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Multilayer Perceptron</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font1">Modeling with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">multilayer perceptron</span><span class="font1"> using the help of R </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">software</span><span class="font1"> using </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the neuralnet()</span><span class="font1"> function in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the</span><span class="font1"> neuralnet package. In this modeling, determining the number of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neurons</span><span class="font1"> on the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layer</span><span class="font1"> considers the rule suggested in the book by Heaton: the number of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden neurons</span><span class="font1"> must be within the size range </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">of the input layer</span><span class="font1"> and </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">output layer</span><span class="font1"> [20]. For the number of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layers</span><span class="font1">, use a maximum of two </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layers</span><span class="font1"> because the book written by Laurene Fausett explains that one </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layer</span><span class="font1"> is enough to solve the problem. Still, some problems are easier with two </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden layers</span><span class="font1"> [21]. Therefore, in this modeling, the number of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neurons</span><span class="font1"> in </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the first hidden layer is</span><span class="font1"> 1 to n, with n being the size </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">of the input layer</span><span class="font1">. Based on research recommendations [9], the number of </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">hidden second layers</span><span class="font1"> is a maximum of two </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neurons.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">In modeling with </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">a perceptron multilayer,</span><span class="font1"> researchers used two scenarios. For the first scenario, not applying the activation function on the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neuron output</span><span class="font1"> or the </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">neuralnet()</span><span class="font1"> function sets </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">linear.output =</span><span class="font1"> TRUE, while the second scenario applies the activation function on </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">the neuron output</span><span class="font1"> or </span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">linear.output =</span><span class="font1"> FALSE. The activation function used in this study is the logistic sigmoid activation function. The model naming for the first scenario is the MLP-sigmoid model, and the second is the MLP+sigmoid model.</span></p>
<p><span class="font1">Before modeling, data as input variables are standardized data. Data standardization aims to avoid data with different scales to cause all inputs to be in the same range, namely 0-1.</span></p>

<p><span class="font1">Based on the results of the research that has been carried out, it can be concluded that several things: Search keywords are obtained that are relevant to the data on the number of airplane passengers for domestic departures, namely &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">ticket</span><span class="font1">,&quot; &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">web check in</span><span class="font1">,&quot; &quot;trip,&quot; &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">booking</span><span class="font1">,&quot; and &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">online check</span><span class="font1"> &quot;&nbsp;for international departures, namely &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">batik air check in</span><span class="font1">&quot; and &quot;</span><span class="font1" style="font-style:italic;">scoot</span><span class="font1">.&quot; The SARIMAX method and multilayer perceptron can be used to nowcast the number of passengers on domestic and international departure flights with good results. The best model for nowcasting the number of passengers on domestic departure flights is a multilayer perceptron neural network architecture with MLP-sigmoid, which does not apply the sigmoid logistic activation function to the output neurons, one hidden layer with three neurons. It uses three GT variables: &quot;booking&quot; and &quot;check-in.&quot; “online,” and “web check-in”. This model has a MAPE and MAE evaluation value of 11,194% and 28,048, respectively. Based on the MAPE value, which is in the range of 10% - 20%, the forecasting results can be said to be good. The forecasting results are excellent based on the MAPE value below 10%. Based on the results of this study, it is known that Google trends have the potential to be used in nowcasting the number of passengers departing domestically and internationally. The nowcasting results can be used to see the growth rate in the number of aircraft passengers as seen from the nowcasting results data compared to the last period of the actual data to be used as information and recommendations for airport management and government policies.</span></p>
