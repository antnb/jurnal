<h1><a name="bookmark0"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark1"></a>ABSTRAK</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">Dua senyawa triterpenoid tipe damaran, cabraleadiol (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font2">) dan cabraleahidrosilakton (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2</span><span class="font2">), telah diisolasi dari ekstrak </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font2">-heksana kulit batang </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font2"> (Meliaceae). Struktur kimia senyawa </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font2">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font2">diidentifikasi berdasarkan data-data spektroskopi terutama, NMR dan massa serta perbandingan data spektra dari laporan sebelumnya. Senyawa </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font2">dan </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font2">pertama kali dilaporkan pada tumbuhan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Kata kunci: </span><span class="font2">Cabraleadiol, Cabraleahidrosilakton, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font2">, Meliaceae.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>ABSTRACT</span></h1>
<p><span class="font2">Two dammarane-type triterpenoids, cabraleadiol (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font2">) and cabraleahydroxylactone (</span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2</span><span class="font2">), have been isolated from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font2">-hexane extract of the stembark of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font2"> (Meliaceae). The structure of compounds </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font2">were determined by spectroscopic data mainly NMR and mass as well as by comparing with previously reported spectral data. Compounds </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font2">and </span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font2">were reported for the first time from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font2">.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">Keywords: </span><span class="font2">Cabraleadiol, Cabraleahydroxylactone, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font2">, Meliaceae.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>INTRODUCTION</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Triterpenoids are the most important group of terpenoids because they exhibit a great diversity of biological activities and they are the major constituents of tropical higher plants. Recently, research of new biologically active compounds from plants used in traditional medicine has led to the isolation of numerous triterpenoids with important biological activities (Fu </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2015; Nguyen </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2015). Higher plants are major source of triterpenoids with several biological activities and numerous reports have shown that family of Meliaceae, Rhamnaceae, Cucurbitaceae, Ganodermataceae and Apocynaceae produce a wide variety of tetracyclic triterpenoids, whereas, family of Ranunculaceae, Burseraceae, Capparidaceae, Celastraceae and Lamiaceae families are recognized to contain</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">active pentacyclic triterpenoids (Harneti </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2012; Farabi </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3"> 2017; Tian </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2005; Zhang </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2005).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton</span><span class="font3"> genera is belong to the Meliaceae family, which consist of more than 50 plant species distributed mainly in tropical countries (Yang </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2009; Heyne, 1982). Previous phytochemical studies on </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton</span><span class="font3"> species have yielded a number of interesting compounds, including limonoids (Supratman </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2019; Supriatno </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2018; Katja </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2016) and triterpenoids (Inada </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al.</span><span class="font3">, 1993; Katja </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2017 ).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In our continous search for novel constituents from </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font3">, we isolated new limonoids, pentandricin A from the stembark of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">C. pentandrus</span><span class="font3"> (</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Suprianto et al</span><span class="font3">., 2018). In the further investigation for novel compounds from non polar fraction of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">C. pentandrus</span><span class="font3">, we found two dammarane-type</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">triterpenoids from the </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane extract. In this paper, we report the isolation and structural determination of two dammarane-type triterpenoids, cabraleadiol (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font3">) and cabraleahydroxylactone (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2</span><span class="font3">).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">MATERIAL AND METHODS</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark6"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark7"></a>Plant material</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The stem bark of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">C. pentandrus</span><span class="font3"> were collected in Bogor Botanical Garden, Bogor, West Java Province, Indonesia in June 2016. The plant was identified by Mr. Ismail, the staff of the Herbarium and a voucher specimen (No. Bo-104) was deposited at the Herbarium.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark8"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark9"></a>Extraction and isolation</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">The dried ground stembark (1.8 kg) of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">C. pentandrus</span><span class="font3"> was extracted with methanol (3 x 4 L) at room temperature for 6 days. After removal of the solvent under vacuum, the viscous concentrate of methanol extract (340 g) was first suspended in water and then partitioned with </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane, ethyl acetate and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-butanol, successively. Evaporation resulted in the crude extracts of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane (10.9 g), ethyl acetate (25.2 g) and </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-butanol (228.6 g), respectively. The </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane extract (10.9 g) was fractionated by column chromatography on silica gel using a gradient </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane-ethyl acetate-methanol to give eight fractions (I– VIII). Fraction II (5.4 g) was subjected to column chromatography over silica gel using a gradient mixture of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane-dichloromethane-ethyl acetate (5% stepwise) as eluting solvents to afford thirteen subfractions (II<sub>1</sub>-II<sub>13</sub>). Subfraction II<sub>9</sub> (912.2 mg) was column chromatographed on silica gel, eluted with </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane:dichloromethane:ethyl acetate (5:4:1), to give six subfractions (II</span><span class="font1">9-1</span><span class="font3">-II</span><span class="font1">9-6</span><span class="font3">). subfraction II</span><span class="font1">9-3 </span><span class="font3">(62.2 mg) was further subjected to column chromatography over silica gel using a gradient mixture of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">n</span><span class="font3">-hexane-dichloromethane-ethyl acetate (7:2:1) to give </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">(12.2 mg) and </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font3">(8.3 mg) .</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark10"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark11"></a>General Experimental Procedure</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Melting points were determined on an electrothermal melting point apparatus. The IR spectra were recorded on a Perkin-Elmer spectrum-100 FT-IR in KBr. Mass spectra were obtained with a Synapt G2 mass spectrometer instrument. NMR data were recorded on a JEOL-ECZ-600 spectrometer at</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">600 MHz for <sup>1</sup>H and 150 MHz for <sup>13</sup>C. Chemical shifts are given on a </span><span class="font0">δ </span><span class="font3">(ppm) scale with tetramethylsilane (TMS) as an internal standard. Column chromatography was conducted on silica gel 60 (Merck). TLC plates were precoated with silica gel GF</span><span class="font1">254 </span><span class="font3">(Merck, 0.25 mm) and detection was achieved by spraying with 10% H<sub>2</sub>SO<sub>4</sub> in ethanol, followed by heating.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">RESULTS AND DISCUSSION</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark12"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark13"></a>Cabraleadiol (1)</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Physical properties: colourless needle crystal, m.p. 170-172<sup>o</sup>C. IR </span><span class="font0">λ</span><span class="font1">max </span><span class="font3">(KBr) cm<sup>-1</sup>: 3457, 2948, 2861, 1461, 1380. HR-TOFI-MS </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">m/z</span><span class="font3">: 461.3879 [M+H]<sup>+</sup>. <sup>1</sup>H-NMR (600 MHz, CDCl</span><span class="font1">3</span><span class="font3">) and <sup>13</sup>C-NMR (125 MHz, CDCl3) see Table 1.</span></p>
<h1><a name="bookmark14"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark15"></a>Cabraleahydroxylactone (2)</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Physical properties: white amorphous powder, m.p. 240-242 <sup>o</sup>C, IR </span><span class="font0">λ</span><span class="font1">max </span><span class="font3">(KBr) cm<sup>-1</sup>: 3550, 2900, 2810, 1760, 1457, 1386, 1249, 1196. HR-TOFI-MS </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">m/z</span><span class="font3">: 416 [M+H]<sup>+</sup>; <sup>1</sup>H-NMR (600 MHz, CDCl</span><span class="font1">3</span><span class="font3">) and <sup>13</sup>C-NMR (125 MHz, CDCl3) see Table 1.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Compound </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was isolated as a colourless needle crystal. The molecular formula of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was determined to be C</span><span class="font1">30</span><span class="font3">H</span><span class="font1">52</span><span class="font3">O</span><span class="font1">3 </span><span class="font3">from its molecular ion peak [M+H]<sup>+</sup> at </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">m/z </span><span class="font3">460.3916 (calcd. for C</span><span class="font1">30</span><span class="font3">H</span><span class="font1">52</span><span class="font3">O</span><span class="font1">3</span><span class="font3">, 460.3920) in the LC-TOFMS. The IR spectrum showed absorption peaks due to hydroxyl (3457 cm<sup>-1</sup>), C-C aliphatic (2948 and 2861 cm<sup>-1</sup> ) and ether functional groups (1380 cm<sup>-1</sup>).The <sup>1</sup>H NMR spectrum (Table 1) displayed eight tertiary methyl singlets [δ</span><span class="font1">H</span><span class="font3">0.87, 0.82, 0.92, 1.09, 1.17, 1.13, 0.95 and 0.84 (each 3H, s)], two oxymethine protons [δ<sub>H</sub> 3.38 (1H, t, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">J</span><span class="font3">=3.0 Hz, H-3 and 3.62 (1H, dd, </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">J</span><span class="font3">=4.8, 10.2 Hz, H-24)], and some aliphatic protons in the up field region. The<sup>13</sup>CNMR and DEPT-135 spectra (Table 1), exhibited the presence of eight methyl signals [δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">15.6 (Me-30), 22.2 (Me-29), 28.4 (Me-28), 24.1 (Me-27), 27.9 (Me-26), 27.3 (Me-21), 16.6 (Me-18) and 16.2 (Me-19)], ten methylenes [δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">33.7 (C-1), 25.4 (C-2), 18.3 (C-6), 34.8 (C-7), 21.7 (C-11), 27.1 (C-12), 31.5 (C-15), 25.9 (C-16), 35.3 (C-22), 26.4 (C-23)], six methines including two oxygenated methines [δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">76.4 (C-3), 49.6 (C-5), 50.7 (C-9), 42.8 (C-13), 49.8 (C-17), and</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">86.3 (C-24)] and eight quartenary carbon signals [δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">37.3 (C-4), 40.7 (C-8), 37.7 (C-10) and 50.2 (C-14). The NMR data suggested that </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">had a triterpenoid tetracyclic skeleton similar to the dammarane-type triterpenoid (Harneti et al., 2014). The structure of the tetracyclic system in </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was determined by analysis of COSY and HMBC spectra (Figure 2). Key HMBC spectra were the <sup>2</sup></span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">J</span><span class="font3"> correlations from the eight methyl groups (Me-18, Me-19, Me-21, Me-14, Me-26, Me-27, Me-29 and Me-30) to their attached carbons enabled the assignment of the eight singlet methyls. A secondary alcohol was assigned at C-3 by the HMBC correlations from H-1 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.42), H-2 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.55), and H-5 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.24) to C-3 (δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">76.4), whereas a tertiary alcohol was located at C-25 by the HMBC correlation from Me-26 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.17), Me-27 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.09), H-24 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">3.62) to C-25 (</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">70.3).</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/57171-1.png" alt="" style="width:210pt;height:266pt;">
<p><span class="font3">Figure 1. Chemical Structure of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">and </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The relative stereochemistry of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was identified based on coupling constants in the <sup>1</sup>H-NMR and biogenetic point of view occurrence of dammarane-type triterpenoid in </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton</span><span class="font3"> genus (Supratman </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2019).</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">In comparison of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">with literature data of a cabraleadiol (Phongmaykin </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">et al</span><span class="font3">., 2008), showed good agreement, therefore compound </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was identified as a cabraleadiol, which shown in this plant for the first time.</span></p><img src="https://jurnal.harianregional.com/media/57171-2.png" alt="" style="width:216pt;height:153pt;">
<p><span class="font3">Figure 2. <sup>1</sup>H-<sup>1</sup>H COSY and HMBC for </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">Compound </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font3">was obtained as a white amorphous powder. The IR spectra showed absorption peaks at 3550 cm<sup>-1</sup> (OH), 2960 and 2865 cm<sup>-1</sup> (aliphatic), 1382 and 1238 cm<sup>-1 </sup>(</span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">gem</span><span class="font3">-dimethyl groups), and 1040 cm<sup>-1</sup> (C-O). The NMR spectra of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font3">was very similar with </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font3">. The main differences was the absence of an isopropyl alcohol group at [(</span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">H </span><span class="font3">1.17 (3H, s) and 1.09 (3H, s), </span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">24.1 (Me-27), 27.9 (Me-26) and 70.3 (H-25)] and the presence of carbonyl lactone signal at </span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">176.9, suggested that compound </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font3">was a lactone derivative of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font3">. In the HMBC spectrum, methylene signal at </span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font3"><sub>H</sub> 2.52 was correlated to carbonyl lactone at </span><span class="font0">δ</span><span class="font1">C </span><span class="font3">176.9, indicated that a carbonyl lactone was located at C-24 to make a lactone ring as a side chain ring.</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">The relative stereochemistry of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2 </span><span class="font3">was identified based on coupling constants in the <sup>1</sup>H-NMR and biogenetic point of view occurrence of dammarane-type triterpenoid in </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton</span><span class="font3"> genus (Supratman et al., 2019). In comparison of </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">with literature data of a cabraleahydroxylactone (Phongmaykin et al., 2008), showed good agreement, therefore compound </span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1 </span><span class="font3">was identified as a cabraleahydroxylactone, which shown in this plant for the first time.</span></p>

<h1><a name="bookmark16"></a><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark17"></a>CONCLUSIONS</span></h1>
<p><span class="font3">Two dammarane-type triterpenoids have been isolated from the the stembark of </span><span class="font3" style="font-style:italic;">Chisocheton pentandrus</span><span class="font3"> belong to Meliaceae family and identified by spectroscopic data as cabraleadiol (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">1</span><span class="font3">) and cabraleahydroxylactone (</span><span class="font3" style="font-weight:bold;">2</span><span class="font3">). The investigation of these dammarane-type triterpenoids were shown in this species</span></p>
<p><span class="font3">for the first time and strenghten the occurance of this compounds in this genera.</span></p>
