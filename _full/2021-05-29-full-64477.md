<p><span class="font2">Gede Agus Surya Atmaja<sup>a1</sup></span><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;">, </span><span class="font2">I Komang Ari Mogi<sup>a2</sup></span></p>

<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Abstract</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cybercrime usually happen on internet. There are many type of cybercrime such as Cyberbullying, Online Scams, Malware, etc. WhatsApp is a chatting application that is easy to use and many of its users. Because of many users that used WhatsApp chatting application not infrequently many crimes occur there. Especially Online Scams, the perpetrator usually sell something online and make their target use WhatsApp to contact them and their transaction begin there. In this research using NIST steps, those steps are Identification, collect data, analyze data and report the result. This research also focusing on WhatsApp data extraction to obtain evidence from perpetrator Smartphone. The end of the research</span></p>
<p><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;font-style:italic;">Keywords: </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">CyberCrime, WhatsApp, Forensic, Online Scam, NIST</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark2"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark3"></a>1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Introduction</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The Internet is a place to find all things that user want and need. There is benefit user can get from internet such as search for information, news, communicate with others and shop online. The Internet can be access from computer and smartphone. With smartphone the user can access the internet and make their work easier. According to We Are Social in 2018 the internet users pass more than 4 billion people around the world. Smartphone can be use for communication with social media application such as </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2">, LINE, Instagram, Telegram, etc. Although there is so much good things that user get from the internet. The internet can be a tool for evil or bad things such as Cybercrime. Cybercrime is rife on internet these days such as Online Scams, Cyberbullying, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Phising,</span><span class="font2"> Malware etc. One of the cybercrimes that often occur is Online scam. In Indonesia, Online Scam occupy the top position in 2019. Indonesia Police received 1.617 reports of online scam. Judging from the disadvantages, online scam via website reached 73 billion in this case especially in the case of online shopping and this mode was mostly reported as many as 351 reports [1].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Online Scam usually take place on online shop or an email or short message that the target win prize, the perpetrator pretend sale things and looks convincing so that the victim believes. One of the popular chat applications is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Application</span><span class="font2">. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Application</span><span class="font2"> is easier to use and user-friendly. According to We Are Social </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> reach 1.6 billion users active in 2019. Because of its popularity </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> can be used for Online scam. Usually after the perpetrator get the money they will disappear and can’t be contacted. Therefore to prevent this happening requires caution so as not to get caught up in the perpetrator’s trick. The police will do an investigation, track the perpetrator down and chase the perpetrator. So begin the examination and collecting the evidence from the suspect. One of the things that can be done in solving the digital crime problem is digital forensics.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Digital Forensics is an appropriate step for investigating digital evidence. The main purpose of this research is to extract </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> database to get evidence from perpetrator’s </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Smartphone.</span><span class="font2"> In</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">this research, the researchers will conduct forensics on </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> application which uses forensics steps from NIST.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<h2><a name="bookmark4"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark5"></a>1.1. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Literature Review</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">The previous research related to this research is as follows:</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">The research conducted by Ayubi Wirara et al (2020) is about “Identifikasi Bukti Digital pada Akuisisi Perangkat Mobile dari Aplikasi Pesan Instan ‘</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2">’ ”. The research discusses crimes on the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> application. In their research they acquisition the digital evidence and they analyzed it. The tool that they used are XRY version 8.0.0 and Encase Mobile Forensic version 9.09.00.192. The Result they got from their research are the digital evidence that they extracted from the smartphone [2].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Research conducted by Hussein Abed Ghannam (2020) is about “Forensic Anaysis of Artifacts of Giants Instant Messaging “</span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2">” in Android Smarphone”.his research discusses about how the researcher acquire data from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> from it database. Decrypt and extract the database without rooting the smartphone and collect digital evidence as much as possible. The tools used in this research are Whatcrypt to decrypt the database, SQLite to view the database, UFED cellbrite to recover the deleted message. In the end of the research, the researcher succeeded in getting data from extracting and analyzing these data [3].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Research conducted by Muhammad Irwan Syahib et al (2018) entitled &quot;&nbsp;Analisis Forensic Digital Aplikasi </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">BEETALK</span><span class="font2"> untuk Pengamanan </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Cybercrime</span><span class="font2"> Menggunakan Metode NIST &quot;. Their research discusses the analysis of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">BEETALK</span><span class="font2"> applications using the NIST method. In the data acquisition process, the beetalk smartphone application must be rooted first using the Kingroot tools. After that, take data from the smartphone, but the data from the smartphone must be backed up first using the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">MOBILedit</span><span class="font2"> Forensic tool, then Examination using OXYGEN Forensic which is used to acquire data that has been backed up by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">MOBILedit</span><span class="font2"> [4].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Research conducted by Muhammad Iqbal Ramadhan and Imam Riadi (2019) entitled “Forensic </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> based Andorid using National Institute of Standard Technology (NIST) Method”. Their research discusses about acquisition the data from rooted and non-rooted smartphones. The researchers using following tools </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">FTK Imager, ProDiscover Basic</span><span class="font2">, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> DB Extractor. </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">TWRP</span><span class="font2"> and </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Viewer</span><span class="font2">. The result from the research are they successfully raised evidence from the smartphones [5].</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Research conducted by Dr. Iyobor Egho-Promise et al (2020) entitled “A Forensic Analysis of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> on Andorid Smart phone”. Their research is about how to extract valuable information from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> and from similar mobile applications installed on the Android platform and focus on extraction and analysis data user from volatile and non-volatile memory of an Android Device [6].</span></p>

There is two ways to decrypt the database, first we need to find the key encryption.</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">Actually after </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> encrypt the database it also generate the key encryption and store it in the smartphone, but it is not easy to find because we need to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ROOT</span><span class="font2"> the smartphone to gain access to the folder where the key stored. The second way is to downgrade the </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> application and backup all the data, because the older version of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> not using encryption to the databases. Because the recent smartphone is too hard to </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">ROOT,</span><span class="font2"> the second way is the method chosen by the researchers. There is a</span></p>
<p><span class="font2">tool used for this method, the tools is </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Smartphone Forensic System</span><span class="font2">. <p><span class="font2">The last step is make report, the report contain all information from the previous steps. The database was encrypted, so need to decrypt it using tool </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Smartphone Forensic System </span><span class="font2">with built-in </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp</span><span class="font2"> Message Extractor and Decryption. After decryption, begin analysis the database from the database that decrypted open it through </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Viewer</span><span class="font2"> and it will shows all messages stored in the database. From the conversation between the victim and the perpetrator that viewed by </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Viewer</span><span class="font2">, The perpetrator talking about selling smartphone after the victim sure and send the money the perpetrator</span></p>

<h2><a name="bookmark18"></a><span class="font2" style="font-weight:bold;"><a name="bookmark19"></a>4. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Conclusion</span></h2></li></ul>
<p><span class="font2">From the research that we have done, there so much we can explore in forensics especially in mobile forensics. There are many tools we can use depending on our needs. In this research we are using </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">MOBILedit</span><span class="font2"> to acquisition the smartphone mobile data, </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">Smartphone Forensic System</span><span class="font2"> to decrypt the databases and using </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Viewer</span><span class="font2"> to view all message that stored in the database. The latest version of </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp,</span><span class="font2"> the database always encrypted so we must decrypt or extract it first before we can use </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp Viewer</span><span class="font2"> to shows all the data from it. This research use forensic method from NIST where the steps are identify or identification, collect data, analyze data and report the result. The evidence that obtain from those steps. The steps can be applied to any forensic for mobile to obtaining data from </span><span class="font2" style="font-style:italic;">WhatsApp.</span></p>
