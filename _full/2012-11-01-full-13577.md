<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">2.1 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Location</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font13">The research location was located between 10</span><span class="font4">°</span><span class="font13">S-18</span><span class="font4">°</span><span class="font13">S and 110 </span><span class="font4">°</span><span class="font13">E - u8</span><span class="font4">°</span><span class="font13">E in the Indian Ocean. <ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">2.2</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Research Data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font13">In this paper there are two types of data sets; fisheries data and remote sensing data.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">Fisheries data</span></p>
<p><span class="font13">Fisheries data was obtained from PT. Perikanan Nusantara log book data, Benoa, Bali. The type of this data is daily data. The data consist of time, location and number of tuna in each species. The data stored directly by the ship through the radio and was noted by the company staff in the office.</span></p>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">Remote sensing data</span></p>
<p><span class="font13">Monthly remotely sensed oceanographic data, specifically, sea surface temperature (SST) was used. The data was derived from level 3 ocean color data of Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) Aqua standard mapped images (SMI) on 2010 with the spatial resolution is 4 km.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;">2.3 &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fisheries data Classification</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font13">Andreade (1999) divided fishery data sets into 3 groups: (a) null catches-cases with CPUE equal to zero ; (b) positive catches - cases with CPUE greater than o but lower than 17.5 tons and (c) high catches - cases with CPUE equal or greater than 17.5. The value of 17.5 is equal to the lower limit of the upper quartile of CPUE. The lower limit of the upper quartile has the same meaning with Quartile 3 (Q3). I will use number of big eye tuna to replace CPUE. Based on the data value of Q3 is 3.</span></p>
<ul style="list-style:none;"><li>
<p><span class="font3">2.4</span><span class="font5" style="font-weight:bold;"> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Adjustment of Fishery Data To Remotely Sensed SST data</span></p></li></ul>
<p><span class="font13">To Match fisheries data and remote sensed data, I use ArGIS 9.3 software. In this step, we can extract values of each SST parameter corresponding to latitude and longitude positions of the fishery dataset.</span></p>
